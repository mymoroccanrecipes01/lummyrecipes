Cherry Martini: Refreshing Cocktail Recipe for Any Occasion

Lummy Hayes Published on January 11, 2026
10 min prep 5 min cook 2 servings

Chill out with this refreshing cherry martini, ready in just 15 minutes! Bursting with fresh cherries, smooth vodka, and a hint of citrus, it's a fun drink for any gathering. Try this recipe and impress your friends!

Alright, so let’s talk about this cherry martini. It’s like summer in a glass! Seriously, the sweet, juicy cherries paired with the crisp vodka and a zing of fresh lemon juice? So good! This drink isn’t just pretty; it’s packed with flavor and is super easy to whip up. Perfect for a chill night with friends or when you're just feeling fancy. You’ll want to keep this recipe handy for gatherings or just because!

The first time I made this? Total disaster. I used way too much lemon juice and it was so sour, I couldn’t even drink it. Now, I know to start with a little and adjust to taste. It’s all about balance, you know? Don’t be like me - taste as you go!

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

  • Main ingredient: Fresh cherries are key here. Look for ones that are plump and dark red. They add sweetness and a vibrant color!
  • Key seasonings: Fresh lemon juice enhances the cherry flavor. Trust me, it’s way better than bottled!
  • Supporting ingredients: Simple syrup helps balance the tartness. You can make it easily by dissolving sugar in water.
  • Smart substitutions: Don’t have vodka? Try gin or even white rum for a twist. And if cherries aren't in season, frozen ones work in a pinch!
  • Equipment needed: A cocktail shaker is a must for mixing. I use my trusty KitchenAid shaker.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase:
Start by pitting the cherries. Takes about 5 minutes. Muddle them in the shaker - that’s where the flavor magic happens!
Cooking process:
Mix and shake for about 20 seconds. You want it to be really cold, but not too long or it’ll get watery.
Finishing touches:
Strain into a glass and garnish with cherries. Presentation is everything!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh cherries, pitted
  • 2 ounces vodka (I recommend Tito's)
  • 1 ounce fresh lemon juice
  • 1 ounce simple syrup (you can make this by dissolving sugar in equal parts water)
  • Ice cubes
  • Extra cherries for garnish

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Start by muddling the fresh cherries in a cocktail shaker. You want to break them down a bit to release their juices, so go for about 30 seconds.

  2. Step 2

    Add the vodka, fresh lemon juice, and simple syrup to the shaker. Fill it with ice cubes, but don't overdo it - just enough to chill the mixture.

  3. Step 3

    Shake vigorously for about 15-20 seconds until it's nice and cold. You’ll see the shaker get frosty - that's when you know it’s ready.

  4. Step 4

    Strain the mix into a chilled cocktail glass. If you want to be fancy, you can use a fine mesh strainer to catch any cherry bits.

  5. Step 5

    Garnish with a few cherries on top for that visual pop. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips & Notes

Here are some pro tips to make your cherry martini even better! - Use fresh cherries when they’re in season for the best flavor. Trust me on this - frozen ones just don’t cut it. - If you're feeling adventurous, try adding a splash of orange liqueur for a twist. It brings out the cherry flavor nicely. - Don't have simple syrup? No problem! Just dissolve some sugar in warm water and let it cool before using. - Always chill your glass ahead of time. A cold martini glass makes a HUGE difference in the drinking experience. - If you want a sweeter drink, just add a bit more simple syrup - adjust to your taste!
