Moist Banana Bread with Optional Chocolate Chips

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient (bananas): Use ripe bananas for the best flavor and sweetness. Look for ones that are heavily speckled. They mash easily and mix well into the batter.

  • Key seasonings/aromatics: Vanilla extract really enhances the overall flavor. I use pure vanilla for a richer taste.
  • Supporting ingredients: The all-purpose flour gives the bread its structure. Sift it for a lighter loaf if you want.
  • Smart substitutions: You can swap in whole wheat flour for a denser texture. Applesauce can replace some butter for a lower-fat version.
  • Equipment needed: A good mixing bowl and a whisk are essential for this. My KitchenAid mixer makes it super easy, but a simple fork works too!

    • Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

    Preparation phase: Start by gathering everything you need. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease your loaf pan. This is a good time to mash your bananas and measure out your ingredients.

    Cooking process: Mix everything together until combined, but don’t overdo it! Pour the batter into your prepared pan and pop it in the oven. You’ll know it’s done when a toothpick comes out clean, about 50-60 minutes.

    Finishing touches: Once it’s baked, let it cool in the pan for a bit before transferring it to a wire rack. Slice and serve warm with a pat of butter!

    Ingredients

    • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
    • 1/3 cup melted Kerrygold butter
    • 1 teaspoon baking soda
    • Pinch of salt
    • 3/4 cup brown sugar
    • 1 large egg, beaten
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour from Bob's Red Mill
    • Optional: 1/2 cup chocolate chips

    Instructions

    1. Step 1

      Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 4x8 inch loaf pan, or line it with parchment paper.

    2. Step 2

      In a mixing bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until smooth. Stir in the melted Kerrygold butter.

    3. Step 3

      Add the baking soda and salt to the banana mixture. Then mix in the brown sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract until combined.

    4. Step 4

      Gradually add the flour, mixing just until everything comes together. If you wanna, fold in the chocolate chips for an extra treat.

    5. Step 5

      Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth out the top. Bake for about 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.

    6. Step 6

      Let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

    Tips & Notes

    Look, here are some pro tips to help you nail this banana bread: 1. Use overripe bananas - the darker, the better. They’re sweeter and mash easier. 2. Don't overmix the batter! A few lumps are totally fine. You want it to be tender, not tough. 3. If you’re worried about it sticking, I recommend using a non-stick spray or a good layer of butter. 4. To store leftovers, wrap it in plastic wrap and keep it at room temp for a few days. It also freezes really well - just slice it up first! 5. Feel free to experiment with add-ins like nuts or dried fruits. Just keep the proportions the same for best results.
