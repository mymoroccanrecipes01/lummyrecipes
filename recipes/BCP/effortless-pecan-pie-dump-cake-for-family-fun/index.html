Easy Pecan Pie Dump Cake Recipe for Cozy Gatherings

Lummy Hayes Published on January 14, 2026
15 min prep 50 min cook 8 servings

Cozy up with this easy pecan pie dump cake! Loaded with rich pecans, buttery goodness, and a hint of sweetness, it’s a slice of home. Great for family gatherings or a quick dessert. Save this recipe for your next get-together!

First time I made this? Complete disaster. I forgot to grease the pan and it stuck like glue. I had to scrape it out with a spatula, and let me tell you, it was a mess! Now I know greasing is non-negotiable, or you’ll be cleaning up a sticky situation.

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

  • Pecans: I always use fresh pecans; they add a rich, buttery flavor. Look for ones that are plump and not too dry.
  • Cake mix: A yellow cake mix works best for this recipe. If you want to make it from scratch, go for a basic vanilla cake recipe.
  • Sweetened condensed milk: This adds creaminess and sweetness. You can use a dairy-free alternative if needed.
  • Smart substitutions: If you’re out of pecans, walnuts work too. They won't taste quite the same but still delicious!
  • Equipment needed: A mixing bowl and 9x13 baking dish are essential. My KitchenAid really speeds up the mixing!

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase:
Gather your ingredients and preheat the oven. Make sure to chop those pecans; about 1 cup should do the trick. You can measure out everything ahead of time to keep things smooth.
Cooking process:
Mix the ingredients as directed, layering them in the pan. Bake until you see bubbling edges and golden tops. You’ll know it’s ready when the kitchen starts smelling good.
Finishing touches:
Let it cool slightly before serving. I like to serve it warm, maybe with ice cream on top – just saying!

Creative Variations to Try

  • Gluten-Free: Use a gluten-free cake mix instead. It works surprisingly well!
  • Vegan: Substitute the butter with coconut oil and use almond milk instead of sweetened condensed milk.
  • Spicy Kick: Add a dash of cayenne pepper for a surprising twist.
  • Fruit Addition: Toss in some chopped apples or pears for a fruity version.

Pro Chef Secrets

  • Layering technique: Always layer your ingredients; it keeps the top crunchy and the bottom gooey.
  • Pecan prep secret: Toasting pecans before adding intensifies their flavor.
  • Timing strategy: Monitor closely towards the end of baking; ovens vary, and you don’t want to overdo it.
  • Presentation hack: Drizzle some caramel before serving for a restaurant-style finish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 box yellow cake mix (like Bob's Red Mill)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted (I use Kerrygold)
  • 1 cup light brown sugar, packed
  • 1 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish, so it’s ready to go.

  2. Step 2

    In a bowl, mix together the chopped pecans, brown sugar, and cinnamon. Set this aside.

  3. Step 3

    In another bowl, whisk together the melted butter, sweetened condensed milk, and vanilla extract. It’s gonna smell amazing!

  4. Step 4

    Spread the cake mix evenly over the greased dish, then pour the butter mixture on top. Don’t mix it; just let it layer.

  5. Step 5

    Sprinkle the pecan mixture over the top of everything. It’s like creating a cozy blanket for your cake!

  6. Step 6

    Bake for 45-50 minutes. The edges should be golden and bubbling. Let it cool for a bit before digging in...

Tips & Notes

Alright, listen up! Here are some tips to nail this pecan pie dump cake: 1. **Layering:** Don't stir the layers together; the top should stay crunchy while the bottom gets all gooey. 2. **Pecan choice:** Use fresh pecans for the best flavor. If you can, toast them lightly before adding – it brings out their nuttiness! 3. **Timing:** Bake until the top is golden brown. If it’s not bubbling, give it a few more minutes. 4. **Storage:** This cake keeps well covered at room temperature for up to 3 days. You can also freeze slices – just wrap them well to avoid freezer burn. 5. **Serving idea:** Serve warm, topped with vanilla ice cream, for an extra treat! Trust me, it’s a hit.
