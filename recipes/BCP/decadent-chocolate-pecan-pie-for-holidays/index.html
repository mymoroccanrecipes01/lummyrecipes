Chocolate Pecan Pie That's So Easy to Make

Lummy Hayes Published on January 14, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

Rich, chocolate pecan pie ready in just 45 minutes! With a flaky crust and a gooey, chocolaty filling, this dessert is a total hit at every gathering. Great for family dinners or holiday celebrations. Save this recipe for your next sweet craving!

That gooey filling with the crunchy pecans and a flaky crust? It's a combination that just hits right. Whether you’re hosting a holiday dinner or just want to treat yourself, this pie is super easy to whip up, and it looks so good!

What I Learned The Hard Way

My first attempt was a disaster. I accidentally grabbed the salt instead of sugar and... yeah, total fail. The pie tasted like a salt lick. Now I double-check my ingredients before mixing. It’s a good reminder that attention to detail matters, especially when baking!

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

  • Main ingredient (protein/base): The pie crust is crucial. I prefer pre-made for ease, but homemade can be stunning—just make sure it's chilled before use.
  • Key seasonings/aromatics: Vanilla extract adds depth to the flavor, so don’t skimp on quality. A dash of cinnamon could also give it a warm twist!
  • Supporting ingredients: Pecans contribute that crunchy texture, and chocolate chips provide sweetness. Try to chop the pecans roughly for a better bite.
  • Smart substitutions: You can use walnuts instead of pecans for a different flavor. Coconut milk can replace corn syrup for a dairy-free option.
  • Equipment needed: A good pie dish is essential. I love my KitchenAid mixer for mixing things – it makes the process so much smoother!

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase:
Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). While it heats, get your pie crust ready in the dish. You can chop the pecans and measure out the chocolate chips in advance to save some time.
Cooking process:
Once mixed, pour the filling into your prepared crust. Bake until the center is just set – it’ll keep cooking as it cools. This is key for that gooey consistency.
Finishing touches:
After cooling, slice it up and serve! Garnish with whipped cream or a scoop of ice cream if you're feeling fancy!

Flavor Boosters & Customization Ideas

  • Try adding a pinch of sea salt on top before serving to enhance the flavor (trust me, it’s a game changer).
  • For a spicy kick, consider a hint of cayenne in the filling. It’s subtle but makes a difference.
  • Swapping dark chocolate for semi-sweet can give a richer flavor—just adjust sugar to taste.
  • Add a layer of caramel sauce between the crust and filling for an extra trynt treat.

  • Presentation tips: Serve on a rustic wooden board and garnish with extra pecans for that wow factor.
  • Temperature serving note: Best served slightly warm, but it’s also great cold!

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips (I like Ghirardelli)
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup corn syrup
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (Kerrygold works great), melted

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). This is key for getting that crust nice and golden.

  2. Step 2

    In a mixing bowl, combine the melted butter, brown sugar, and corn syrup. Whisk it all together until smooth.

  3. Step 3

    Add in the eggs, vanilla extract, and salt. Mix until everything is well combined. Trust me, it's gonna smell so good!

  4. Step 4

    Fold in the chopped pecans and chocolate chips, making sure they get evenly distributed.

  5. Step 5

    Pour the filling into the pie crust, spreading it out evenly. Bake for about 30 minutes, or until the center is set but still slightly jiggly.

  6. Step 6

    Let it cool for at least 15 minutes before slicing. It's gonna be hard to resist, but trust me, it’s worth the wait.

Tips & Notes

Here are some tips to help you nail this pie: 1. If you want an extra flaky crust, chill it for 30 minutes before baking. It helps a lot! 2. Don't skip the cooling time! It allows the filling to set properly, so the slices hold up beautifully. 3. Use a glass pie dish if you can; it helps with even baking. 4. Got leftovers? Store them in the fridge for up to 3 days. Just reheat slices in the microwave for a few seconds. 5. Feel free to switch up the nuts! Walnuts or even a mix could work well.
