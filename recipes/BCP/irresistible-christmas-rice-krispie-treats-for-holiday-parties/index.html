Festive Rice Krispie Treats for Your Holiday Celebrations

Lummy Hayes Published on January 14, 2026
10 min prep 10 min cook 12 servings

This holiday season, you gotta try these easy Rice Krispie Treats! With gooey marshmallows and a dash of festive cheer, they’re a hit for any gathering. Whether for a party or a cozy night, save this recipe for all your holiday fun!

So, these Rice Krispie Treats are like the ultimate holiday treat. Honestly, they’re super easy to whip up and everyone loves them! The gooey marshmallows mixed with crispy cereal create this amazing texture that’s just... well, you gotta try it. It’s become a fun tradition for us!

What I Learned The Hard Way

First time? Total disaster. I thought I could skip greasing the pan. Nope! They stuck like crazy. Now I know that buttering the pan really matters because it makes removing the treats so much easier. Don’t skip this step, alright?

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient: Rice Krispies are super light and crunchy. They hold up well with the marshmallows. Look for the original version; it works best for texture.

  • Key seasonings/aromatics: Vanilla extract adds a warm flavor. Always use pure vanilla for the best taste.
  • Supporting ingredients: The mini marshmallows are key for that gooey texture. I find that the brand matters (try Kraft!).
  • Smart substitutions: You can swap the butter for coconut oil for a dairy-free option, or add in some chocolate chips for a different twist!
  • Equipment needed: Grab a large pot and a spatula. Trust me, having a good spatula makes mixing way easier!

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Prepping is super simple. Measure out your Rice Krispies and butter beforehand. Melt the butter first, then mix in the marshmallows. You can do this part ahead of time if you want! When cooking, keep an eye on the marshmallows; they can burn easily. Once everything is mixed, spread into your greased pan and let it cool down. Seriously, that’s all there is to it!

Critical Tips You Must Know

  • Temperature precision: Keep the heat low while melting marshmallows. Too hot, and they’ll burn. Stir gently to help them melt evenly.
  • Common mistake to avoid: Not greasing the pan! It leads to sticking. Always butter or spray your pan.
  • Perfect doneness indicator: You'll know they're ready when they’re just set but still soft to the touch.
  • Storage & reheating: Store in an airtight container for about 3 days. They won’t need reheating, just enjoy at room temperature!

Ingredients

  • 6 cups Rice Krispies cereal
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter (I use Kerrygold for the best flavor)
  • 1 package (10 oz) mini marshmallows
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Sprinkles for decoration (optional)

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    In a large pot, melt 4 tablespoons of butter over low heat. Keep it low to avoid burning, okay?

  2. Step 2

    Once melted, stir in the mini marshmallows and mix until they’re all gooey and melted. This should take about 2-3 minutes.

  3. Step 3

    Remove the pot from heat and add in the vanilla extract. This part? Totally essential for flavor!

  4. Step 4

    Now, add the Rice Krispies cereal to the marshmallow mixture. Stir it up until everything is well-coated. Use a spatula for this - trust me.

  5. Step 5

    Spread the mixture into a greased 9x13 inch pan. Press it down firmly (but don’t crush it). Let it cool for about 30 minutes before cutting into squares.

Tips & Notes

OK, here are some pro tips to make your Rice Krispie Treats even better: - **Temperature control:** When melting the marshmallows, keep the heat low. If it’s too high, they’ll scorch. You want them gooey, not burnt! - **Mixing:** Stir quickly but gently as you add the cereal. You don’t wanna break them up too much. - **Storage:** Store in an airtight container. They’ll stay fresh for about 3 days, but I doubt they’ll last that long! - **Decorating:** Sprinkles not only make them pretty, but they add a little crunch. My kids love them! - **Batch size:** If you find they disappear faster than expected, double the recipe! Seriously, they go quick.
