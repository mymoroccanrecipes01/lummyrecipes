Iced Gingerbread Chai Latte Recipe for Cozy Days

Lummy Hayes Published on January 14, 2026
10 min prep 5 min cook 2 servings

Cozy up with this iced gingerbread chai latte that blends creamy oat milk and a medley of warm spices. Ideal for chilly gatherings or a treat for yourself. Save this recipe and warm up your winter days!

So I've made this iced gingerbread chai latte a few times and honestly? It's a total winner for cozy winter days. Imagine a creamy blend of oat milk and aromatic spices that just hits the spot. The gingerbread syrup brings that festive vibe, making it an awesome treat for chilly weather or holiday gatherings. You can sip this by the fire or while binge-watching your favorite show on Netflix. Trust me, you won’t regret it!

  • 10 minutes. That’s it. Seriously.
  • All the cozy vibes without the fuss.

My Story With This Recipe

I first tried this iced gingerbread chai latte at my friend Anna's house last December. It was a snowy Sunday afternoon, and the aroma of spices filled the air. Anna said, "You HAVE to try this! It's my favorite holiday drink!" One sip and I was hooked. Creamy, warm spices, and just the right sweetness. Now I make it every winter for my family, and my husband Dave always says, "This is the best drink on a cold day!"

What I Learned The Hard Way

My first attempt? Total disaster. I didn’t let the chai cool and added the oat milk too soon. It ended up lukewarm and watery. Now I know that letting the tea chill is crucial. If you skip that step, you’ll end up with a sad, diluted drink.

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient (chai tea): I recommend using a strong chai blend for that robust flavor. Look for brands like Twinings or Tazo for a good balance of spices. Steep it strong so it holds up against the oat milk.

  • Key seasonings/aromatics: Ground cinnamon is essential. I usually go for McCormick’s brand because it’s super aromatic. A little goes a long way in enhancing that cozy vibe.
  • Supporting ingredients: Oat milk adds creaminess, but if you’re looking for a richer flavor, try using Kerrygold butter instead of syrup for a twist.
  • Smart substitutions: If you can't find gingerbread syrup, you can use molasses or honey mixed with some spices for that warm flavor.
  • Equipment needed: A good blender helps mix everything evenly, but honestly, a whisk works just fine too!

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase:
Start by brewing the chai tea. Let it steep while you gather your ingredients. Aim for about 5 minutes. Once brewed, let it cool for at least 15 minutes. You can prep the oat milk and syrup in the meantime.
Cooking process:
Combine the cooled chai with oat milk and syrup in a large glass. Stir well until combined. You want the flavors to meld perfectly.
Finishing touches:
Pour over ice, add whipped cream, and a sprinkle of cinnamon on top. It’s all about that final touch!

Flavor Boosters & Customization Ideas

Want to kick it up a notch? Try adding a splash of vanilla extract or a hint of nutmeg for some extra warmth. For those who love a spicy kick, a dash of cayenne pepper can really wake it up. Thinking about a dairy-free version? Swap the oat milk for almond or soy milk. Feeling adventurous? Add a shot of espresso for a gingerbread latte twist!

Perfect Pairings & Serving Suggestions

For sides, consider pairing this latte with some gingerbread cookies or a slice of pumpkin bread for that classic holiday vibe. As for drinks, a light sparkling water can cleanse your palate. If you're feeling fancy, you could even serve it alongside some eggnog. This drink shines at holiday gatherings or cozy evenings with family. Serve it in clear glasses to show off those layers, and it looks super inviting!

Creative Variations to Try

If you want a gluten-free version, just make sure your gingerbread syrup is gluten-free. For a vegan approach, stick to oat milk and check your syrup ingredients. Feel like getting spicy? Add some chili flakes for a surprising kick. Want a sweeter version? Try adding extra gingerbread syrup or even a touch of maple syrup for a different flavor profile.

Pro Chef Secrets

  • Restaurant technique: Use cold brew chai for an even smoother latte; it’s less bitter and more aromatic.
  • Ingredient prep secret: Chill your glasses before serving to keep the drink cold longer.
  • Timing strategy: Brew your tea ahead of time and chill it overnight for the best flavor.
  • Presentation hack: Drizzle some gingerbread syrup on the inside of the glass before pouring in the latte for a beautiful effect.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups brewed chai tea, cooled
  • 1 cup oat milk (I use Oatly)
  • 2 tablespoons gingerbread syrup (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Whipped cream for topping
  • Ice cubes

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Brew your chai tea and let it cool down completely. This is key, trust me.

  2. Step 2

    In a large glass, mix the cooled chai with oat milk and gingerbread syrup. Stir it like you mean it!

  3. Step 3

    Add a sprinkle of ground cinnamon, but you might wanna go heavy if you're feeling spicy.

  4. Step 4

    Fill another glass with ice cubes, then pour that delicious mixture over the ice.

  5. Step 5

    Top it off with whipped cream and another sprinkle of cinnamon for flair. It's all about that presentation!

Tips & Notes

1. Use a KitchenAid frother for creamy oat milk - it makes a world of difference. 2. Want more spice? Consider adding a pinch of nutmeg or cardamom. 3. If you’re short on time, use store-bought gingerbread syrup, but homemade is always a plus. 4. My first attempt was too sweet because I added way too much syrup - now I measure it out! 5. This drink keeps well in the fridge for a day, but the ice will dilute it, so maybe skip the ice if you're prepping ahead.
