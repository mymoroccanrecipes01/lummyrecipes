Delicious Apple Fritter Waffle Donuts for Fall Mornings

Lummy Hayes Published on January 11, 2026
20 min prep 15 min cook 6 servings

Warm and crispy apple fritter waffle donuts that’ll make your mornings cozy. Loaded with tart apples and sweet glaze, they’re a fantastic way to start your day. Enjoy them fresh with a cup of coffee. Save this recipe for your next fall brunch!

The smell of cinnamon and baked apples wafted through my kitchen, and honestly, it was magical. These donuts, with their crispy edges and tender centers, are like fall on a plate. Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient: Apples are key here. I recommend using tart varieties like Granny Smith. They give a nice contrast to the sweetness of the glaze. Make sure to chop them small so they cook through easily.
Key seasonings: Cinnamon and nutmeg add that warm, cozy flavor. I always use freshly grated nutmeg for an extra kick!
Supporting ingredients: The flour and eggs help bind everything together, creating that fluffy texture we want. Don't skimp on the butter—it adds richness!
Smart substitutions: You can swap out the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend if needed. Also, almond milk works just fine instead of regular milk if you're looking for a lactose-free option.
Equipment needed: A good quality waffle maker is a must. My KitchenAid mixer really helps get the batter nice and smooth without lumps.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase: Gather your ingredients and start by measuring everything out. It really helps to have everything ready. You can even chop the apples the night before to save time. Cooking process: Once the batter is mixed, it’s all about timing. Keep an eye on the waffles; they’re done when they’re golden brown and crispy on the outside. Finishing touches: Drizzle the glaze right before serving. It makes them look extra tempting and keeps that freshness!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (I use Bob's Red Mill)
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup milk (I prefer whole milk for richness)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup diced apples (Granny Smith works great)
  • 1/4 cup melted Kerrygold butter
  • 1 cup powdered sugar (for glaze)
  • 2 tbsp milk (for glaze)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract (for glaze)

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    OK, so first thing, preheat your waffle maker. I usually set it to medium-high. While that's heating up, mix the dry stuff together in a bowl: flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.

  2. Step 2

    In another bowl, whisk the egg, then add the milk and melted butter. Combine these wet ingredients with the dry ones until it’s all just blended. It’s OK if there are a few lumps – don’t overmix!

  3. Step 3

    Now fold in those diced apples. You want them evenly distributed, but don’t go crazy – just a gentle mix. Trust me, the chunks will be delicious!

  4. Step 4

    Once your waffle maker is ready, scoop some batter onto it. I usually use about 1/3 cup per donut. Close the lid and let them cook for about 3-5 minutes. You want them golden brown – like, really golden.

  5. Step 5

    While those are cooking, mix up your glaze. Just whisk together the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla until smooth. It should be drizzly but thick enough to coat. Adjust with more milk or sugar as needed.

  6. Step 6

    When the donuts are done, remove them carefully and drizzle that sweet glaze on top. Serve warm because they taste best fresh out of the waffle maker!

Tips & Notes

Alright, here are some tips that’ll help you nail these donuts: 1. **Don’t skip the resting time** - let the batter sit for 5 minutes before cooking. It helps the texture! 2. **Check your waffle maker’s heat** - if they’re browning too fast, lower the heat a bit. My old one likes to run hot. 3. **Use fresh apples** - they give the best flavor. I usually go for tart varieties like Granny Smith. 4. **Make it fun** - try adding some chopped nuts or even a sprinkle of chocolate chips for a twist! 5. **Storage tip** - these are best eaten fresh, but if you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Reheat in the toaster for a quick warm-up!
