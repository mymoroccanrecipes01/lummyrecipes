Bright Winter Salad with Oranges and Pecans

Lummy Hayes Published on January 14, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

A versatile winter salad that balances citrus brightness with creamy feta and toasted pecans. This simple combination demonstrates how quality ingredients and proper technique can create depth in minimal-ingredient cooking. Naturally gluten-free and adaptable to dietary needs.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups mixed greens (spinach and arugula, or similar)
  • 2 medium navel or Valencia oranges, peeled and segmented
  • ½ cup pecans, roughly chopped
  • ¼ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • ¼ teaspoon fine sea salt
  • Fresh ground black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Pat the mixed greens dry using paper towels or a salad spinner. Moisture is the enemy of crispness—any excess water will dilute the dressing and cause wilting. This step takes just 2-3 minutes and makes a measurable difference in texture. If your greens are especially wet, lay them on clean towels for a few minutes.

  2. Step 2

    Toast the pecans in a dry skillet over medium heat for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until fragrant. You'll notice the kitchen fills with a warm, nutty aroma—that's when you know they're ready. This optional step concentrates their flavor and deepens the sweetness, but if you're short on time, skip it without penalty. Remove from heat immediately to prevent burning.

  3. Step 3

    Peel your oranges by cutting off the top and bottom, then slicing away the white pith in vertical strips, following the curve of the fruit. Over a small bowl to catch juice, segment the orange by slicing between the membranes. This takes about 3 minutes per orange. Reserve any juice that collects.

  4. Step 4

    Pour the olive oil and balsamic vinegar into a small bowl. Add ¼ teaspoon salt and a few grinds of black pepper. Whisk for about 30 seconds until combined. The dressing should emulsify slightly and appear darker. Taste and adjust seasoning—the acidity should feel bright without overwhelming. The vinegar-to-oil ratio is intentionally lower than classic vinaigrettes because the citrus juice provides additional acid.

  5. Step 5

    Place the dried greens in a large bowl. Add the orange segments (and any reserved juice), toasted pecans, and crumbled feta. At this stage, don't add dressing yet. This prevents premature wilting and keeps you in control of flavor intensity.

  6. Step 6

    Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently but thoroughly, using two forks or salad servers. You want every leaf coated and flavors distributed, but handle the orange segments carefully so they remain intact rather than breaking apart. The residual citrus juice and oil create a natural secondary dressing.

  7. Step 7

    Taste a bite. If it needs more salt, add a pinch—seasoning greens properly is essential since they don't naturally contain sodium. Adjust pepper as desired. Serve immediately while the greens are crisp and cold.
