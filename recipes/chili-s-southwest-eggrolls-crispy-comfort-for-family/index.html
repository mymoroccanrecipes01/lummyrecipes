Crispy Southwest Eggrolls

Lummy Hayes Published on January 14, 2026
15 min prep 15 min cook 12 servings

Homemade southwest eggrolls with seasoned chicken, black beans, corn, and melted cheese in a shatteringly crisp wrapper. Ready in 30 minutes with frying or baking options.

I started making these southwest eggrolls about three years ago when I needed a substantial appetizer for a football watch party. Store-bought frozen versions never had enough filling, and the wrappers always turned soggy before halftime. This homemade version solves both problems with a generous spiced chicken filling and a frying technique that keeps the exterior shatteringly crisp for hours.

The combination of smoky cumin, black beans, and sharp cheddar creates layers of flavor that work whether you're feeding a crowd or packing lunches for the week. I've made these at least forty times now, adjusting the spice levels and testing both frying and baking methods to find what actually works in a home kitchen.

Crispy Southwest Eggrolls

The key to a crispy eggroll that stays crispy lies in moisture control. Wet fillings create steam inside the wrapper during cooking, which softens the exterior from within. Rinsing and thoroughly draining both the black beans and corn removes excess liquid that would otherwise turn your wrappers soggy.

Using cooled chicken rather than warm prevents the cheese from melting prematurely, which would release fat into the filling and create greasiness. The cheese should melt during frying, not before, so it becomes stretchy and helps bind the filling together.

Frying at 350°F hits the sweet spot where the wrapper crisps quickly enough to form a barrier before oil can penetrate, but slowly enough that the filling heats through completely. Too hot and you get a burnt exterior with cold filling. Too cool and oil seeps into the wrapper, making everything greasy.

Why This Recipe Works

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup (140g) cooked chicken, shredded — rotisserie chicken works well, or use leftover roasted chicken
  • 1/2 cup (85g) black beans, rinsed and drained — canned is fine; pat dry with paper towels
  • 1/2 cup (75g) corn kernels — frozen and thawed, or canned and drained
  • 1/2 cup (55g) sharp cheddar cheese, shredded — pre-shredded melts less smoothly due to anti-caking agents
  • 1/4 cup (40g) red bell pepper, finely diced — about 1/4 of a medium pepper
  • 2 tablespoons green onions, thinly sliced — white and light green parts only
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon black pepper

For Assembly and Cooking:

  • 12 eggroll wrappers — found in the refrigerated produce section near tofu, not frozen
  • 2 cups (480ml) vegetable or canola oil — neutral oils with high smoke points work best

Ingredients

  1. Prepare the filling (2 minutes): Combine shredded chicken, black beans, corn, cheddar cheese, red bell pepper, and green onions in a large mixing bowl. The ingredients should be evenly distributed throughout.
  2. Season the mixture (1 minute): Add chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir thoroughly until spices coat all ingredients. Look for a uniform reddish-brown color with no visible patches of unseasoned chicken.
  3. Position the wrapper (30 seconds): Place one eggroll wrapper on a clean, dry surface with one corner pointing toward you like a diamond. Spoon 2 heaping tablespoons of filling onto the lower third, shaping it into a compact 4-inch log.
  4. Roll and seal (45 seconds): Fold the bottom corner up and over the filling, pressing gently to remove air. Fold both side corners toward the center. Roll tightly toward the top corner. Wet the top corner with your finger and press to seal. The wrapper should feel taut without air pockets.
  5. Repeat assembly (8-10 minutes): Continue with remaining wrappers. Place finished eggrolls seam-side down on parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  6. Heat the oil (5-7 minutes): Pour oil into a heavy pot to 2 inches deep. Heat over medium-high until a thermometer reads 350°F (175°C). Test readiness by dropping a small wrapper piece into the oil—it should sizzle immediately and rise to the surface.
  7. Fry in batches (3-4 minutes per batch): Lower 3-4 eggrolls into the oil using a slotted spoon. Fry without moving for 90 seconds, then turn gently. Continue frying until deep golden brown on all sides. The bubbling will slow as moisture escapes from the filling.
  8. Drain and rest (2 minutes): Transfer to a wire rack over a sheet pan. Let excess oil drip off before serving. The surface should be dry and uniformly golden.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  • Dry your ingredients completely. Spread rinsed beans and thawed corn on paper towels and pat dry before adding to the filling. Surface moisture is the primary cause of soggy wrappers.
  • Keep the filling cool. If your chicken is still warm from cooking, spread it on a plate and refrigerate for 10 minutes. Cool filling makes the wrappers easier to handle and prevents premature cheese melting.
  • Don't overfill. Two heaping tablespoons is the maximum. Overstuffed eggrolls burst at the seams during frying, releasing filling into the oil and creating a mess.
  • Maintain oil temperature. Adding cold eggrolls drops the oil temperature significantly. Wait 30 seconds between batches for the oil to recover to 350°F. A thermometer removes all guesswork.
  • Use a wire rack, not paper towels. Paper towels trap steam against the bottom of the eggroll, softening the crisp exterior. A wire rack allows air circulation on all sides.
  • Serve within 2 hours. These are best fresh. After 2 hours at room temperature, the filling moisture begins migrating into the wrapper regardless of how well you dried the ingredients.

Pro Tips for Best Results

  • Wrapping with wet hands. Eggroll wrappers become sticky and tear when exposed to water anywhere except the sealing edge. Keep a dry towel nearby and wipe your hands between each eggroll. The only moisture should be the small amount used to seal the top corner.
  • Crowding the frying pot. Adding too many eggrolls at once drops oil temperature below 325°F, causing the wrappers to absorb oil instead of crisping. Fry no more than 4 at a time in a standard pot, fewer if your pot is small.
  • Moving eggrolls too soon. Wrappers need 90 seconds of undisturbed frying to develop a set crust. Turning them earlier causes the coating to tear and allows oil to seep into the filling.
  • Using cold oil. Starting with oil below 340°F results in greasy, heavy eggrolls. The initial high temperature creates a seal that prevents oil absorption. Always verify temperature with a thermometer before adding the first batch.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Baked version: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Arrange eggrolls on a wire rack over a sheet pan and spray lightly with cooking oil. Bake 18-22 minutes, turning halfway through, until golden and crisp. The texture differs from fried—more crackly than shatteringly crisp—but significantly reduces oil consumption.
  • Vegetarian filling: Replace chicken with an additional 1/2 cup black beans plus 1/4 cup cooked quinoa. Add 2 tablespoons cream cheese to help bind the filling. Increase cumin to 3/4 teaspoon for depth.
  • Spicy variation: Add 1 minced jalapeño (seeds removed for moderate heat, included for intense heat) and 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper to the filling. The heat builds as the eggroll cools slightly.
  • Air fryer method: Spray eggrolls with cooking oil and arrange in a single layer. Air fry at 375°F (190°C) for 8-10 minutes, turning halfway through. Results fall between baked and fried in terms of crispness.
  • Gluten-free adaptation: Substitute rice paper wrappers. These require a different technique: briefly dip in warm water to soften, fill, and roll. Pan-fry in 1/4 inch of oil rather than deep frying for best results.

Nutrition Information

Refrigerator storage: Place cooled eggrolls in a single layer in an airtight container with paper towels between layers to absorb moisture. Store up to 3 days.

Freezer storage: Freeze uncooked, assembled eggrolls on a parchment-lined sheet until solid (about 2 hours), then transfer to freezer bags. Store up to 2 months. Fry directly from frozen, adding 1-2 minutes to cooking time.

Reheating: Oven reheating produces the best results. Place eggrolls on a wire rack over a sheet pan and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 8-10 minutes until heated through and crisp. Avoid microwaving, which turns the wrapper rubbery.

Make-ahead strategy: Prepare and refrigerate the filling up to 2 days in advance. Assemble eggrolls the day of serving for best texture. Wrapped, uncooked eggrolls can sit refrigerated for up to 4 hours before frying.

Storage, Reheating, and Make-Ahead Tips

Can I make southwest eggrolls ahead of time?
You can assemble them up to 4 hours ahead and refrigerate, covered. For longer storage, freeze uncooked eggrolls and fry directly from frozen when ready to serve.

Why are my eggrolls soggy?
Soggy wrappers result from excess moisture in the filling or oil that wasn't hot enough. Drain and pat dry all canned or frozen ingredients, and verify your oil reaches 350°F before frying.

Can I use wonton wrappers instead of eggroll wrappers?
Yes, but results differ significantly. Wonton wrappers are thinner and produce bite-sized appetizers rather than substantial eggrolls. Reduce filling to 1 tablespoon per wrapper and fry for 2 minutes total.

How long do southwest eggrolls last in the fridge?
Cooked eggrolls keep 3 days refrigerated. The wrapper texture softens over time, so plan to reheat in the oven to restore crispness before serving.

What dipping sauce goes best with southwest eggrolls?
Avocado ranch, chipotle mayo, or cilantro-lime crema all complement the filling well. A simple combination of sour cream with lime juice and a pinch of cumin works in a pinch.

Can I bake these instead of frying?
Yes. Bake at 400°F for 18-22 minutes on a wire rack, turning halfway through. Spray lightly with oil before baking. The texture is crackly rather than shatteringly crisp, but still satisfying.
