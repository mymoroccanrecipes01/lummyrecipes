Southern Thanksgiving Feast: Cornbread, Turkey & Greens

Lummy Hayes Published on January 11, 2026
30 min prep 90 min cook 8 servings

This Southern Thanksgiving feast is all about comfort and tradition. Enjoy homemade cornbread, tender turkey, and flavorful collard greens. A great way to bring family together for the holidays! Make sure to save this recipe for your next family gathering.

So I've made this Southern Thanksgiving feast a few times now, and every time, the aroma of roasted turkey fills my kitchen, making it feel like a warm hug. The cornbread is golden and fluffy, and the collard greens add a perfect touch of spice and color to the plate. Honestly, there's something about this combination that just makes my heart happy.

What I Learned The Hard Way

Listen, my first attempt at this was a disaster. I overcooked the turkey by about 30 minutes. It was dry and sad. Now I know to check the temperature early. It'll save you from turkey tragedy.

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient (turkey): I recommend using a fresh, organic turkey for the best flavor. Look for one that's free-range if you can. It really makes a difference in taste and texture when cooked. Key seasonings: Salt and pepper are essential, but smoked paprika adds that depth you're gonna love. Brands like McCormick work great! Supporting ingredients: Collard greens add nutrients and a slightly bitter taste that balances the meal. When prepping, make sure to rinse them thoroughly. Smart substitutions: If you're short on time, you can use frozen collard greens, but fresh is always better. And for cornbread, any quality cornmeal will do, but I personally love Bob's Red Mill for its texture.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase: Start by prepping your ingredients: chop the collard greens, and season the turkey. This takes about 20 minutes. You can even make the cornbread batter ahead of time. Cooking process: Roast the turkey for about 2 hours total, checking the temperature. You want it to reach 165°F. For the collards, sauté them for about 10 minutes until they're tender. Finishing touches: Once everything's cooked, serve the turkey sliced, with collards on the side and cornbread for scooping. It’s a feast!

Flavor Boosters & Customization Ideas

  • Herbs: Add fresh thyme or rosemary to the turkey for a fragrant twist. Just throw them in the cavity before roasting.
  • Spices: Paprika adds warmth; use about 1 teaspoon for that perfect kick.
  • Upgrades: Try adding bacon to the collard greens for extra flavor; it’s a game changer.
  • Technique variations: Smoked turkey instead of roasted can give you a unique flavor profile; just adjust the cooking times.

Perfect Pairings & Serving Suggestions

  • Side dishes: Mashed potatoes are a classic. They soak up all those turkey drippings, yum!
  • Beverage pairings: A nice chilled cider pairs perfectly with this meal.
  • Bread options: Serve some crusty rolls for those who love to mop up juices.
  • Occasion ideas: Thanksgiving dinner is a must, but it also works great for family reunions.
  • Presentation tips: Arrange the turkey on a big platter with greens and cornbread artfully placed around.
  • Temperature serving note: Make sure to serve everything warm, especially the turkey.

Creative Variations to Try

  • Dietary adaptation 1: For gluten-free, use a gluten-free cornbread mix instead of regular flour. It still tastes delicious!
  • Dietary adaptation 2: If you're vegan, try using jackfruit for a meaty texture and sautéed greens without any meat.
  • Flavor variation: Add a dash of hot sauce to the collards for a spicy kick!
  • Protein swap: You can also use chicken instead of turkey if that's what you have on hand.

Ingredients

  • 1 batch of cornbread (made with Bob's Red Mill cornmeal)
  • 1 whole turkey (about 12-14 lbs)
  • 2 tablespoons Kerrygold butter
  • 4 cups collard greens, washed and chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 cups chicken broth

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    OK, so first things first, preheat your oven to 325°F. While that's heating up, let's prep the turkey. Rinse it under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels. Season it generously with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.

  2. Step 2

    Melt that Kerrygold butter in a roasting pan over medium heat. Sear the turkey breast side down for about 30 minutes. This gives it a nice golden color and locks in those juices.

  3. Step 3

    Flip the turkey and pour in 2 cups of chicken broth. Cover with foil and roast for about 2 hours. Check for doneness; it should reach 165°F in the thickest part. Trust me, you want to avoid undercooked turkey.

  4. Step 4

    While the turkey is roasting, heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until translucent, then throw in the garlic and collard greens. Cook until the greens are wilted, about 10 minutes.

  5. Step 5

    Season the collards with salt and pepper, then add a splash of chicken broth if they seem dry. Let them simmer while the turkey finishes up. And don't forget to whip up that cornbread - easy peasy with Bob's Red Mill cornmeal.

Tips & Notes

Listen, cooking a turkey can be a bit daunting, but here’s what I’ve learned: 1) Always let your turkey rest for at least 20 minutes before carving. This helps keep it juicy. 2) If you want crispy skin, dry the turkey thoroughly before seasoning. 3) Collard greens can be prepped the day before; just reheat them in the pan. 4) For cornbread, I recommend using Kerrygold butter for richer flavor. 5) If you’re feeling adventurous, add some red pepper flakes to the collards for a spicy kick!
