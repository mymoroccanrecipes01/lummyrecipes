Cheesy Baked Mac and Cheese That'll Make You Smile

Lummy Hayes Published on January 11, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This cheesy baked mac and cheese is comfort food at its finest! Loaded with gooey cheese and topped with a crispy crust, it's a dish your family will love. Great for cozy nights or gatherings. Seriously, you gotta try it!

So, I gotta tell you about this baked mac and cheese. It's like a warm hug on a plate! Seriously, when you pull it out of the oven, the aroma of melting cheese fills the air. The texture? Creamy, with that crispy top that everyone loves. It’s a dish that brings people together, and trust me, it’s super easy to whip up. You'll be low-key obsessed after just one bite!

What I Learned The Hard Way

Honestly, my first try was a disaster. I thought I could be a hero and double the recipe. Big mistake! The sauce was a mess and didn’t thicken properly. Now I know that keeping the proportions right is key - too much milk and it just won’t set. Lesson learned!

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient (macaroni): I always use elbow macaroni because it holds the sauce well. Look for the kind that’s sturdy and not too soft. Cook it al dente so it doesn’t get mushy in the oven.

Key seasonings/aromatics: Garlic and onion powders really amp up the flavor. I prefer using fresh garlic sometimes, but the powders are super convenient.

Supporting ingredients: Cream and cheese contribute to that rich, creamy texture. I recommend using sharp cheddar for a stronger flavor profile. Mix in some Gruyère if you’re feeling fancy!

Smart substitutions: If you don’t have heavy cream, half-and-half works too. Want a lighter version? Use low-fat milk, though it won’t be as rich. And for a gluten-free option, swap the elbow macaroni for quinoa or gluten-free pasta.

Equipment needed: A sturdy pot is essential for making the cheese sauce, and a good baking dish like my trusty Pyrex makes all the difference when it comes to even cooking.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase: Start by cooking the macaroni according to package directions. Drain and set aside. While that’s cooking, prep your cheese and measure out the cream and milk. You can do all this ahead of time if you want to make it easier!

Cooking process: After making the roux with the butter and flour, slowly whisk in the milk and cream until it thickens. This usually takes about 5 minutes over medium heat, and you should see it bubble slightly.

Finishing touches: Once it’s all combined, transfer to your baking dish, sprinkle with cheese and breadcrumbs, then pop it in the oven. It should be golden and bubbly in about 20-25 minutes. Just watch for that crispy top that’ll have everyone excited!

Flavor Boosters & Customization Ideas

Wanna kick it up a notch? Try adding some spices like smoked paprika or a pinch of cayenne for a little heat. Fresh herbs like thyme or parsley can also add a nice contrast. And if you’re feeling adventurous, roasted veggies like broccoli or spinach can be mixed in too!

Beverage pairings: A chilled glass of white wine, like Chardonnay, complements the creaminess beautifully. Bread/starch options: Garlic bread is always a hit, but a nice baguette works too! Occasion ideas: This dish is fantastic for family gatherings or cozy movie nights. Presentation tips: Serve in a rustic bowl for that homey feel. And don’t forget to garnish with chopped parsley for a pop of color!

Creative Variations to Try

Dietary adaptation 1 (Gluten-Free): Use gluten-free pasta made from rice or quinoa. It may change the texture slightly, but it's super yummy! Dietary adaptation 2 (Vegan/Dairy-Free): Swap cheese with a nut-based cheese or nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor. Use almond milk instead of dairy. Flavor variation (Spicy): Mix in some jalapeños or a bit of Sriracha for a spicy kick! Protein/ingredient swap: For a protein boost, add shredded chicken or cooked sausage. Just mix it in before baking for an extra hearty dish!

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces elbow macaroni, cooked and drained
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese, divided
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup unsalted Kerrygold butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour, Bob's Red Mill recommended
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs for topping

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). While that's happening, grab a large pot and melt the Kerrygold butter over medium heat.

  2. Step 2

    Whisk in the Bob's Red Mill flour to create a roux, stirring for about 2 minutes until it’s bubbling. You want it to be a light golden color, kinda like a tan.

  3. Step 3

    Slowly pour in the milk and heavy cream while whisking continuously. Bring it to a gentle simmer and watch for it to thicken, which takes about 5 minutes.

  4. Step 4

    Stir in 1 1/2 cups of the cheddar cheese along with the garlic powder and onion powder. Keep stirring until everything is melted and creamy. Then, mix in the cooked macaroni and season with salt and pepper.

  5. Step 5

    Transfer the mac and cheese to a greased baking dish. Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top, then add breadcrumbs. Bake for 20-25 minutes until it’s all bubbly and golden on top.

Tips & Notes

First off, make sure your cheese is freshly shredded. Pre-packaged stuff? Nah, it doesn’t melt the same way. If you want a bit of spice, add some cayenne pepper to the mix. And honestly, if you’re feeling fancy, toss in some cooked bacon bits or even lobster for a treat! I always make extra because leftovers taste even better the next day. If you're reheating, just add a splash of milk to keep it creamy.
