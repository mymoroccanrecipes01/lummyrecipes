Fudgy Black Bean Brownies with Crunchy Walnuts and Dark Chocolate Chunks

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Medium Difficulty

These brownies are a delightful surprise—super fudgy thanks to black beans instead of flour, with crunchy walnuts and rich dark chocolate chunks. Perfect for a guilt-free treat anytime you crave chocolatey goodness!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon black bean brownies years ago when I was looking for a way to satisfy my chocolate cravings without the usual guilt. Honestly, I was skeptical at first — beans in brownies? But after a few tries, I found a version that’s fudgy, rich, and surprisingly delicious. What sets this recipe apart is using black beans instead of flour, which not only adds moisture but also ups the protein and fiber. Plus, I toss in walnuts for a satisfying crunch and dark chocolate chunks for that luscious melty goodness in every bite. The trick I learned is to blend the beans really well so the batter is smooth — no one wants a gritty texture! Baking time is crucial too; you want it just set but still fudgy inside. These brownies come out dense and chewy, not cakey, which is exactly how I like them. They’re perfect for an afternoon snack, a quick dessert, or even a lunchbox surprise. I love keeping a batch in the fridge for when I need a little chocolate fix that doesn’t derail my healthy eating. I think you’ll love how rich they feel without the heaviness of traditional brownies.

What You'll Need

8 brownies, approximately 9x9 inch pan

  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, chopped
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chunks

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease or line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper. This ensures your brownies won’t stick and makes cleanup easier. Getting the oven hot before you start mixing helps the brownies bake evenly and develop a nice crust.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Oven preheated to 350°F with pan prepared and ready
Step 2
In a food processor or blender, combine the rinsed black beans, eggs, melted coconut oil, cocoa powder, brown sugar, and vanilla extract. Blend until completely smooth and creamy. This step is key because any bean chunks can ruin the brownie’s texture. Smooth batter means fudgy brownies!
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no visible bean pieces
Step 3
Add baking powder and salt to the batter and pulse a few times just to combine. Overmixing here isn’t necessary and can make brownies tougher. The baking powder helps give a slight lift, so you get a tender crumb instead of a dense brick.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly mixed with baking powder and salt incorporated
Step 4
Fold in the chopped walnuts and dark chocolate chunks gently using a spatula. This preserves the chunks’ shape and distributes them evenly throughout the batter. Walnuts add a wonderful crunch, and the chocolate chunks melt into pockets of gooey richness during baking.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Nuts and chocolate chunks are evenly dispersed without breaking down
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly with your spatula. Make sure the batter reaches all corners so the brownies bake uniformly. Uneven batter can cause some parts to overcook while others stay too wet.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Batter surface is smooth and evenly spread in the pan
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center. It should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking dries them out, so watch carefully!
⏱ Time: 25
✓ Doneness: Toothpick has moist crumbs but no wet batter
Step 7
Remove from oven and let cool in the pan for at least 15 minutes before cutting. This resting time lets the brownies set up so they don’t crumble when sliced. Cooling also helps the flavors meld and intensify.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Brownies are firm to the touch and no longer steaming
Step 8
Cut into 8 squares with a sharp knife. For clean edges, wipe the knife between cuts. Serve at room temperature or slightly chilled. These brownies taste amazing either way but chilling intensifies the fudgy texture.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Clean brownie squares with defined edges
Step 9
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge to keep them fresh. They’ll stay moist and fudgy for several days. You can warm them slightly before serving if you like melty chocolate again.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Brownies stored properly, maintaining texture and flavor

Pro Tips

Make sure to blend the beans until completely smooth.
Any bean chunks left in the batter will create an unpleasant gritty texture. A smooth puree ensures the brownies have a uniform, fudgy consistency that's more like traditional brownies.
Don’t overbake the brownies.
Overbaking dries out the brownies and ruins their fudgy texture. Check them around 25 minutes with a toothpick; you want moist crumbs, not wet batter or a dry crumb.
Use parchment paper to line your baking pan.
Parchment makes removing the brownies easier and prevents sticking. It also helps maintain the edges’ shape when cutting.
Toast walnuts before folding them in.
Toasting enhances the walnut flavor and crunch while reducing any bitterness. It adds depth to the brownies’ overall taste.
Chill brownies before cutting.
Letting brownies cool and chill firms them up, making cutting cleaner and preventing crumbling.
Use dark chocolate chunks instead of chips.
Chunks melt into pockets of gooey chocolate, adding texture variation. Chips often hold their shape and don’t create the same melty effect.
Store brownies in an airtight container in the fridge.
The fridge preserves freshness and maintains the fudgy texture longer. Room temperature storage can dry them out quickly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not rinsing canned black beans thoroughly.

Why it happens People often skip rinsing to save time, unaware of the metallic or canned flavor in the liquid.

Fix Rinse beans under cold water in a fine mesh strainer until the water runs clear. This removes excess sodium and canning liquid taste, improving the final flavor.

Blending beans insufficiently.

Why it happens Some assume a quick pulse is enough and don’t realize texture matters here.

Fix Blend until completely smooth and creamy. Scrape down the sides and blend again if needed to avoid any bean chunks.

Overbaking the brownies.

Why it happens It’s tempting to bake longer to be sure they’re done, but this dries them out.

Fix Start checking at 25 minutes with a toothpick. Remove when it comes out with moist crumbs; residual heat finishes the bake.

Skipping the parchment paper or greasing the pan insufficiently.

Why it happens Sometimes people underestimate how sticky the batter can be.

Fix Line the pan with parchment paper and grease lightly. This prevents sticking and helps with clean removal.

Adding nuts or chocolate chunks too early in the blending process.

Why it happens Some add everything at once, which can break up chunks or nuts too much.

Fix Fold walnuts and chocolate chunks in after blending the batter to keep their texture intact.

Variations

Peanut Butter Swirl Brownies

Add dollops of peanut butter on top of the batter before baking and swirl gently with a knife. This adds a creamy, nutty flavor that pairs perfectly with chocolate and walnuts. Expect a richer, slightly creamier texture with a salty-sweet twist.

Note: Swirling peanut butter on top requires gentle mixing to avoid deflating the batter. Bake as usual but watch for slightly longer baking time if adding more topping.

Vegan Black Bean Brownies

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg). Use maple syrup instead of brown sugar and vegan dark chocolate chunks. The texture will be slightly denser but still fudgy and delicious.

Note: Flax eggs need a few minutes to gel before mixing. Baking time may vary by a few minutes; check doneness carefully.

Spiced Brownies with Cinnamon and Chili

Add 1 tsp ground cinnamon and a pinch of chili powder to the batter for a warm, spicy twist. This variation adds depth and a subtle kick that contrasts nicely with the chocolate and walnuts.

Note: Spices integrate well during blending. No change in baking time needed.

Mint Chocolate Black Bean Brownies

Add 1/2 tsp peppermint extract to the batter and use mint-flavored dark chocolate chunks. The refreshing mint flavor makes these brownies perfect for holiday treats or a fresh twist on the classic.

Note: Peppermint extract is strong; start with a small amount and adjust to taste.

Walnut-Free Brownies with Sunflower Seeds

Substitute walnuts with toasted sunflower seeds for a nut-free option. Sunflower seeds add a similar crunch and nutty flavor but are allergy-friendly. The texture remains crunchy and satisfying.

Note: Toast seeds lightly to enhance flavor. Fold in after blending like walnuts.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Brownies can safely sit out covered at room temperature for up to 24 hours. Cover tightly with plastic wrap or a cake dome to prevent drying out and keep them moist.

Refrigerator

Store brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Chilling intensifies the fudgy texture and flavor, but some prefer to warm them slightly before eating to melt the chocolate chunks again.

Freezer

Freeze brownies wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then foil or in a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. To thaw, place in the fridge overnight and warm slightly before serving to restore their fudginess.

Reheating

Reheat brownies gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for about 5 minutes. Adding a tiny splash of water or milk before microwaving can help keep them moist. Avoid overheating which can make them dry.

Make Ahead

You can blend the batter and fold in nuts and chocolate chunks 1 day ahead, storing it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh on the day you want to serve for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I substitute black beans with another type of bean?
While black beans work best for their mild flavor and creamy texture, you can try pinto or kidney beans as substitutes. However, the taste and color may change, and you’ll want to rinse and blend thoroughly to avoid a gritty texture.
What if I don’t have a food processor?
You can use a high-speed blender to puree the beans until smooth. If neither is available, mash the beans very well with a fork or potato masher, but the texture might be less smooth, affecting the fudginess.
Are these brownies gluten-free?
Yes! Since these brownies don’t use any flour, they’re naturally gluten-free, making them a great option for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease.
Can I make these brownies nut-free?
Absolutely! Simply omit the walnuts or substitute with seeds like sunflower or pumpkin seeds for a similar crunch without nuts.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Insert a toothpick in the center around 25 minutes. It should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter. This ensures the brownies are fudgy but fully cooked.
Can I use regular sugar instead of brown sugar?
You can, but brown sugar adds moisture and a slight caramel flavor that helps keep the brownies chewy. If using white sugar, consider adding a tablespoon of molasses or honey to compensate.
What’s the best way to store leftover brownies?
Store them in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. This keeps them moist and fresh. You can also freeze for longer storage.
Can I make these brownies ahead and freeze them?
Yes, you can freeze baked brownies wrapped tightly for up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight and warm before serving for best texture.
Why are my brownies dry or crumbly?
This usually happens if they’re overbaked or the beans weren’t blended smooth enough. Make sure to check doneness early and blend the beans fully to avoid dryness.
Can I add other mix-ins like dried fruit or seeds?
Definitely! Feel free to fold in dried cherries, pumpkin seeds, or even shredded coconut to customize your brownies. Just fold them in gently after blending the batter.

Final Thoughts

Give these fudgy black bean brownies a try—you might just be surprised how delicious and healthy they are. Don’t forget to share how they turn out!
