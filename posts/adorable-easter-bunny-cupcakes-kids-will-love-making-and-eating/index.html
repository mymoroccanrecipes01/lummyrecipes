Easter Bunny Cupcakes: Soft Vanilla Cupcakes with Buttercream and Marshmallow Bunny Faces

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
25 min prep 20 min cook 12 servings

These Easter Bunny Cupcakes combine tender vanilla cake with smooth, creamy buttercream frosting. The cute bunny faces made from marshmallows and candies bring a playful touch to spring gatherings. This recipe is approachable for home cooks and a fun activity to share with children during Easter or spring celebrations.

Introduction

Every spring, I look forward to making these Easter Bunny Cupcakes with my family. Baking them has become a ritual around Easter brunch, and I enjoy how the soft vanilla cupcakes fill the kitchen with a sweet, comforting aroma that's both nostalgic and inviting. What makes this version stand out is the way the marshmallow bunny faces add a playful dimension—soft, pillowy marshmallows contrast with the creamy buttercream, while the candy eyes and noses bring the bunnies to life visually. These cupcakes are simple enough to bake on a busy weekend yet charming enough to impress guests at spring gatherings or kid's parties. With a few straightforward steps, you can create a batch that not only tastes balanced—with tender crumb and just-sweet-enough frosting—but also brings a touch of seasonal whimsy to any table.

Why This Works

The success of these cupcakes lies in balancing texture and flavor through both cake and decoration. The cake uses a combination of creamed butter and sugar to create a light, tender crumb that feels soft but holds its shape well under frosting. The inclusion of baking powder ensures a gentle rise without over-expanding, which keeps the structure even and prevents dryness.

The buttercream is intentionally lightened with milk, creating a smooth, spreadable consistency that complements the cupcakes without overpowering their vanilla flavor. Marrying the sweetness of the frosting with the subtle vanilla in the cake establishes a harmonious flavor profile.

Decorating with marshmallows offers a soft, chewy texture that contrasts the smooth frosting and moist cake. The mini chocolate chips and candies add small bursts of texture and visual appeal. The simplicity of these elements makes the decoration approachable while still creating a convincing and charming bunny face.

  • Using all-purpose flour provides enough protein to give the cupcakes structure without making them dense. Avoid substituting with cake flour unless you adjust liquids, as cake flour’s lower protein content can result in a more fragile crumb that may not support the decoration well.
  • Butter at room temperature allows for proper creaming with sugar, trapping air that helps leaven the cake. Using unsalted butter gives control over salt levels, especially since the recipe includes a small amount of added salt to enhance flavor.
  • This leavening agent reacts with moisture and heat to create carbon dioxide bubbles, giving the cupcakes their rise. Using the right amount ensures an even crumb and prevents the cupcakes from collapsing or becoming gummy.
  • Sifting the powdered sugar before making buttercream avoids lumps and creates a smooth texture. The fine sugar dissolves easily, allowing the frosting to be creamy without graininess.
  • Large marshmallows provide a soft, chewy texture that contrasts the smooth frosting and tender cake. Use fresh marshmallows for best texture; older marshmallows can be dry or hard, which affects the bunny faces’ appearance and mouthfeel.

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F). Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners and set aside.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Oven reaches target temperature; muffin tin is prepared
  2. Step 2

    In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. This ensures even distribution of leavening and prevents pockets of salt.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Dry ingredients combined and aerated, fine powdery mixture
  3. Step 3

    In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar with an electric mixer on medium speed until pale and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. This step incorporates air, contributing to a light crumb.

    ⏱ 3-4 minutes✓ Mixture light in color and texture, increased volume
  4. Step 4

    Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. This helps emulsify the batter and ensures even texture.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Batter smooth with no streaks of egg visible
  5. Step 5

    Mix in the vanilla extract.

    ⏱ 30 seconds✓ Batter evenly scented with vanilla
  6. Step 6

    Alternately add the dry ingredients and milk in three parts, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix on low speed until just combined after each addition to prevent overmixing, which can develop gluten and toughen the cupcakes.

    ⏱ 3-4 minutes✓ Batter smooth, no visible flour streaks, slightly thick but pourable
  7. Step 7

    Divide the batter evenly among the 12 cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full to allow room for rising.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Even layer of batter in each cup, smooth surface
  8. Step 8

    Bake for 18-20 minutes, rotating the pan halfway through. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs.

    ⏱ 18-20 minutes✓ Cupcakes risen and golden on top, toothpick test passes
  9. Step 9

    Remove cupcakes from oven and let cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Frosting warm cupcakes will cause melting.

    ⏱ 30-40 minutes cooling✓ Cupcakes fully cooled to touch, no warmth
  10. Step 10

    Prepare the buttercream by beating the softened butter until creamy. Gradually add powdered sugar, then vanilla extract and 2 tablespoons of milk or cream. Beat on medium speed until smooth and spreadable. Add additional milk if needed to reach desired consistency.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Buttercream smooth, glossy, holds shape but spreads easily
  11. Step 11

    Frost each cupcake generously with buttercream using a spatula or piping bag, creating a smooth or swirled surface.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Evenly coated cupcakes with visible creamy texture
  12. Step 12

    Decorate each cupcake with two marshmallows positioned vertically to form bunny ears. Use mini chocolate chips for eyes, and place a small pink candy for the nose. If desired, add small touches of pink food coloring to the marshmallows for ear detail or cheeks.

    ⏱ 15-20 minutes✓ Cute bunny faces formed with neatly placed decorations

Pro Tips

  • Ensure butter and eggs are at room temperature before starting. — Room temperature ingredients emulsify more easily, creating a smoother batter with better rise and crumb.
  • Don't overmix the batter after adding flour. — Overmixing develops gluten, which results in tougher cupcakes rather than tender ones.
  • Allow cupcakes to cool completely before frosting. — Frosting warm cupcakes causes it to melt and slide off, ruining the decoration and texture.
  • Use fresh marshmallows for decorating bunny ears. — Fresh marshmallows are soft and pliable, making them easier to position and less likely to dry out or crack.
  • Rotate the baking pan halfway through baking. — Ovens often have hot spots; rotating ensures even baking and consistent cupcake texture and color.
  • Sift powdered sugar before making buttercream. — Sifting prevents lumps and gives the frosting a smooth, creamy finish.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold butter or eggs directly from the fridge.
    Why: Cold ingredients don't blend smoothly, leading to a dense batter and uneven crumb.
    Fix: Take ingredients out 30-60 minutes before baking to reach room temperature.
  • Overmixing the batter once flour is added.
    Why: Mixing too long develops gluten, which toughens the cupcakes.
    Fix: Mix just until the dry ingredients are incorporated and no streaks remain.
  • Frosting cupcakes before they are fully cooled.
    Why: Warm cupcakes cause buttercream to melt and slide off, ruining appearance.
    Fix: Wait until cupcakes are completely cool before applying frosting.
  • Using stale or dry marshmallows for decoration.
    Why: Old marshmallows become hard and brittle, making decoration difficult and less visually appealing.
    Fix: Use fresh marshmallows and store unused ones in an airtight container.

Variations

  • Vegan Easter Bunny Cupcakes: Replace butter with plant-based margarine, eggs with flaxseed eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg), and use a dairy-free milk alternative. Use vegan powdered sugar and marshmallows that are gelatin-free.
    Flax eggs provide binding but produce a denser crumb. The dairy-free substitutes may slightly alter texture, but the cupcakes remain moist and flavorful.
  • Gluten-Free Vanilla Cupcakes: Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend that contains xanthan gum. Ensure baking powder is gluten-free. Follow the recipe as usual.
    Gluten-free flours often require slight adjustments in liquid; monitor batter consistency to avoid dryness. Texture may be slightly more crumbly but still tender.
  • Lemon-Infused Easter Bunny Cupcakes: Add 1 tbsp finely grated lemon zest to the batter and replace 1 tbsp of milk with fresh lemon juice. Add a teaspoon of lemon juice to the buttercream for a subtle citrus note.
    The acidity in lemon juice can react with baking powder to enhance rise, but too much lemon juice may affect texture. This version offers a fresh spring flavor.
  • Chocolate Easter Bunny Cupcakes: Add 1/4 cup (25g) unsweetened cocoa powder to the dry ingredients and reduce flour by the same amount. Use chocolate chips for decoration instead of candy noses for a chocolate twist.
    Cocoa powder absorbs moisture; ensure batter is not overly thick. The chocolate flavor pairs well with vanilla buttercream or a chocolate buttercream alternative.
  • Natural Food Coloring Bunny Faces: Use natural coloring agents such as beet juice powder for pink accents or spirulina powder for green. Mix into small portions of frosting or marshmallow coating for subtle colors.
    Natural colors are less vibrant than synthetic but add an earthy, authentic touch. Test small amounts to avoid altering flavor significantly.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store cupcakes in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days to maintain freshness and softness.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Cupcakes can be refrigerated for up to 4 days in an airtight container. Bring to room temperature before serving for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Wrap cupcakes individually in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container or bag. Freeze for up to 1 month. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Avoid microwaving as this can melt the frosting and soften decorations too much. Instead, allow cupcakes to come to room temperature naturally.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Bake cupcakes and prepare buttercream a day ahead. Store cupcakes and frosting separately. Assemble and decorate just before serving to keep marshmallow decorations fresh and intact.

FAQ

Can I make Easter Bunny Cupcakes ahead of time?
Yes, you can bake the cupcakes and prepare the buttercream a day in advance. Store them separately and decorate just before serving to keep decorations fresh.
Why are my cupcakes dense instead of light and fluffy?
This often happens if butter and eggs were too cold or if the batter was overmixed after adding flour. Use room temperature ingredients and mix just until combined.
Can I substitute marshmallows with something else for the bunny ears?
Marshmallows provide a soft, chewy texture that's hard to replicate. You could try sliced almond slivers or fondant shaped into ears, but texture and appearance will differ.
How long do these cupcakes last in the fridge?
They will keep in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Bringing them back to room temperature before serving improves the frosting's texture.
Can I use store-bought frosting instead of homemade buttercream?
Yes, store-bought frosting works, but homemade buttercream tends to have better texture and flavor balance, especially for spreading and decorating.
Is it possible to make these cupcakes dairy-free?
Yes, substitute butter with a plant-based margarine and use dairy-free milk. Ensure marshmallows and candy decorations are also vegan.
