Easter Bunny Cupcakes: Soft Vanilla Cupcakes with Buttercream and Marshmallow Bunny Faces

Lummy Hayes
Published on March 21, 2026
25 min prep
20 min cook
45 min total
12 servings
Easy

These Easter Bunny Cupcakes combine tender vanilla cake with smooth, creamy buttercream frosting. The cute bunny faces made from marshmallows and candies bring a playful touch to spring gatherings. This recipe is approachable for home cooks and a fun activity to share with children during Easter or spring celebrations.

Pro Tips

Ensure butter and eggs are at room temperature before starting.
Room temperature ingredients emulsify more easily, creating a smoother batter with better rise and crumb.
Don't overmix the batter after adding flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, which results in tougher cupcakes rather than tender ones.
Allow cupcakes to cool completely before frosting.
Frosting warm cupcakes causes it to melt and slide off, ruining the decoration and texture.
Use fresh marshmallows for decorating bunny ears.
Fresh marshmallows are soft and pliable, making them easier to position and less likely to dry out or crack.
Rotate the baking pan halfway through baking.
Ovens often have hot spots; rotating ensures even baking and consistent cupcake texture and color.
Sift powdered sugar before making buttercream.
Sifting prevents lumps and gives the frosting a smooth, creamy finish.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold butter or eggs directly from the fridge.

Why it happens Cold ingredients don't blend smoothly, leading to a dense batter and uneven crumb.

Fix Take ingredients out 30-60 minutes before baking to reach room temperature.

Overmixing the batter once flour is added.

Why it happens Mixing too long develops gluten, which toughens the cupcakes.

Fix Mix just until the dry ingredients are incorporated and no streaks remain.

Frosting cupcakes before they are fully cooled.

Why it happens Warm cupcakes cause buttercream to melt and slide off, ruining appearance.

Fix Wait until cupcakes are completely cool before applying frosting.

Using stale or dry marshmallows for decoration.

Why it happens Old marshmallows become hard and brittle, making decoration difficult and less visually appealing.

Fix Use fresh marshmallows and store unused ones in an airtight container.

Variations

Vegan Easter Bunny Cupcakes

Replace butter with plant-based margarine, eggs with flaxseed eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg), and use a dairy-free milk alternative. Use vegan powdered sugar and marshmallows that are gelatin-free.

Note: Flax eggs provide binding but produce a denser crumb. The dairy-free substitutes may slightly alter texture, but the cupcakes remain moist and flavorful.

Gluten-Free Vanilla Cupcakes

Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend that contains xanthan gum. Ensure baking powder is gluten-free. Follow the recipe as usual.

Note: Gluten-free flours often require slight adjustments in liquid; monitor batter consistency to avoid dryness. Texture may be slightly more crumbly but still tender.

Lemon-Infused Easter Bunny Cupcakes

Add 1 tbsp finely grated lemon zest to the batter and replace 1 tbsp of milk with fresh lemon juice. Add a teaspoon of lemon juice to the buttercream for a subtle citrus note.

Note: The acidity in lemon juice can react with baking powder to enhance rise, but too much lemon juice may affect texture. This version offers a fresh spring flavor.

Chocolate Easter Bunny Cupcakes

Add 1/4 cup (25g) unsweetened cocoa powder to the dry ingredients and reduce flour by the same amount. Use chocolate chips for decoration instead of candy noses for a chocolate twist.

Note: Cocoa powder absorbs moisture; ensure batter is not overly thick. The chocolate flavor pairs well with vanilla buttercream or a chocolate buttercream alternative.

Natural Food Coloring Bunny Faces

Use natural coloring agents such as beet juice powder for pink accents or spirulina powder for green. Mix into small portions of frosting or marshmallow coating for subtle colors.

Note: Natural colors are less vibrant than synthetic but add an earthy, authentic touch. Test small amounts to avoid altering flavor significantly.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Store cupcakes in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days to maintain freshness and softness.

Refrigerator

Cupcakes can be refrigerated for up to 4 days in an airtight container. Bring to room temperature before serving for best texture.

Freezer

Wrap cupcakes individually in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container or bag. Freeze for up to 1 month. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator.

Reheating

Avoid microwaving as this can melt the frosting and soften decorations too much. Instead, allow cupcakes to come to room temperature naturally.

Make Ahead

Bake cupcakes and prepare buttercream a day ahead. Store cupcakes and frosting separately. Assemble and decorate just before serving to keep marshmallow decorations fresh and intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make Easter Bunny Cupcakes ahead of time?
Yes, you can bake the cupcakes and prepare the buttercream a day in advance. Store them separately and decorate just before serving to keep decorations fresh.
Why are my cupcakes dense instead of light and fluffy?
This often happens if butter and eggs were too cold or if the batter was overmixed after adding flour. Use room temperature ingredients and mix just until combined.
Can I substitute marshmallows with something else for the bunny ears?
Marshmallows provide a soft, chewy texture that's hard to replicate. You could try sliced almond slivers or fondant shaped into ears, but texture and appearance will differ.
How long do these cupcakes last in the fridge?
They will keep in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Bringing them back to room temperature before serving improves the frosting's texture.
Can I use store-bought frosting instead of homemade buttercream?
Yes, store-bought frosting works, but homemade buttercream tends to have better texture and flavor balance, especially for spreading and decorating.
Is it possible to make these cupcakes dairy-free?
Yes, substitute butter with a plant-based margarine and use dairy-free milk. Ensure marshmallows and candy decorations are also vegan.

Ingredients

12 cupcakes

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F). Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners and set aside.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches target temperature; muffin tin is prepared
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. This ensures even distribution of leavening and prevents pockets of salt.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients combined and aerated, fine powdery mixture
Step 3
In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar with an electric mixer on medium speed until pale and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. This step incorporates air, contributing to a light crumb.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture light in color and texture, increased volume
Step 4
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. This helps emulsify the batter and ensures even texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter smooth with no streaks of egg visible
Step 5
Mix in the vanilla extract.
⏱ Time: 30 seconds
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly scented with vanilla
Step 6
Alternately add the dry ingredients and milk in three parts, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix on low speed until just combined after each addition to prevent overmixing, which can develop gluten and toughen the cupcakes.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter smooth, no visible flour streaks, slightly thick but pourable
Step 7
Divide the batter evenly among the 12 cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full to allow room for rising.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even layer of batter in each cup, smooth surface
Step 8
Bake for 18-20 minutes, rotating the pan halfway through. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs.
⏱ Time: 18-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cupcakes risen and golden on top, toothpick test passes
Step 9
Remove cupcakes from oven and let cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Frosting warm cupcakes will cause melting.
⏱ Time: 30-40 minutes cooling
✓ Doneness: Cupcakes fully cooled to touch, no warmth
Step 10
Prepare the buttercream by beating the softened butter until creamy. Gradually add powdered sugar, then vanilla extract and 2 tablespoons of milk or cream. Beat on medium speed until smooth and spreadable. Add additional milk if needed to reach desired consistency.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Buttercream smooth, glossy, holds shape but spreads easily
Step 11
Frost each cupcake generously with buttercream using a spatula or piping bag, creating a smooth or swirled surface.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly coated cupcakes with visible creamy texture
Step 12
Decorate each cupcake with two marshmallows positioned vertically to form bunny ears. Use mini chocolate chips for eyes, and place a small pink candy for the nose. If desired, add small touches of pink food coloring to the marshmallows for ear detail or cheeks.
⏱ Time: 15-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cute bunny faces formed with neatly placed decorations
