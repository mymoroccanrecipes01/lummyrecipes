Fresh Spring Pasta with Peas and Lemon Cream Sauce

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 7, 2026
15 min Prep Time
20 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This spring-inspired pasta combines tender peas and zesty lemon cream for a light yet satisfying dish. The secret? A splash of fresh lemon juice that brightens every bite. Great for a quick, refreshing dinner.

Pro Tips

Reserve pasta water before draining.
The starchy pasta water helps loosen the sauce and allows it to cling better to the noodles, creating a silky texture. Without it, the sauce can feel clumpy or separate.
Don’t overcook the peas.
Overcooked peas lose their bright color and sweetness, turning mushy. Cooking them just until tender preserves their fresh flavor and adds a pleasant pop in texture.
Use fresh lemon zest and juice.
Fresh lemon provides vibrant acidity and aromatic oils that brighten the sauce. Bottled lemon juice lacks this freshness and can dull the flavor.
Sauté garlic gently.
Burnt garlic tastes bitter and harsh. Cooking it slowly over medium heat releases its flavor without darkening, ensuring a mellow, fragrant base for the sauce.
Add Parmesan off heat.
Stirring in cheese off the heat prevents it from clumping or separating, allowing it to melt smoothly into the sauce and enhance the flavor evenly.
Season gradually.
Adding salt and pepper in small increments helps balance the dish without over-seasoning. Tasting as you go ensures the flavors are just right.
Serve immediately.
This pasta is best enjoyed fresh. The sauce can thicken and lose its creamy texture upon standing, so serve right after tossing with pasta.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the pasta

Why it happens Many cooks assume softer pasta is more tender and enjoyable, so they cook it past al dente.

Fix Cook pasta just until al dente according to package instructions. Taste early and often to catch the perfect firmness, then drain promptly to avoid mushiness.

Burning the garlic

Why it happens Garlic cooks quickly and can burn if left unattended or cooked on high heat.

Fix Sauté garlic over medium heat while stirring frequently. Remove from heat as soon as it turns golden and fragrant to prevent bitterness.

Skipping the reserved pasta water

Why it happens People often forget to save pasta water or underestimate its importance in sauce texture.

Fix Always reserve at least 1 cup of pasta water before draining. Use it to loosen and emulsify the sauce, making it silky and cohesive.

Using bottled lemon juice

Why it happens Bottled juice is more convenient and often used without considering flavor impact.

Fix Use fresh lemon juice and zest for bright, fresh flavor. The oils in fresh zest add aroma that bottled juice lacks.

Adding cheese on high heat

Why it happens Some add cheese directly to hot pan, causing it to clump or separate.

Fix Remove pan from heat before stirring in Parmesan. This ensures smooth melting and a creamy sauce texture.

Variations

Add Grilled Chicken

For a heartier meal, add sliced grilled chicken breast on top. The smoky flavor complements the creamy lemon sauce and adds protein for a satisfying dinner.

Note: Cook chicken separately and add just before serving to keep textures distinct and flavors fresh.

Swap Peas for Asparagus

Try tender asparagus tips instead of peas for a different spring vegetable note. It adds a slightly earthy flavor and a crisp bite that pairs well with lemon.

Note: Blanch asparagus briefly before adding to the sauce to maintain vibrant color and texture.

Use Half-and-Half Instead of Heavy Cream

For a lighter sauce, substitute half-and-half for heavy cream. The sauce will be less rich but still creamy and flavorful.

Note: Simmer gently to avoid curdling, and don’t boil vigorously after adding half-and-half.

Make It Vegan

Replace cream with coconut milk and Parmesan with nutritional yeast. Use olive oil instead of butter for sautéing to keep it plant-based without sacrificing creaminess.

Note: Adjust seasoning and lemon juice to balance the richer coconut flavor.

Add Red Pepper Flakes

For a spicy kick, sprinkle red pepper flakes into the sauce while simmering. It adds warmth that contrasts nicely with the lemon brightness.

Note: Add flakes early to infuse the sauce, but adjust quantity to your heat preference.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This pasta should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours as dairy-based sauces can spoil quickly. Cover it loosely if you need to hold it briefly before serving.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken when cold; reheat gently with a splash of water or broth to restore creaminess.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this dish as the cream sauce can separate and peas become mushy. If needed, freeze in airtight containers for up to 1 month and thaw overnight in the fridge.

Reheating

Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat, stirring frequently and adding a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce. Avoid microwaving at high power to prevent curdling.

Make Ahead

You can cook the pasta and sauce separately up to 1 day ahead. Store pasta and sauce in separate airtight containers. Combine and reheat just before serving for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peas instead of fresh?
Absolutely! Frozen peas work great and are convenient. Just add them straight to the skillet without thawing. They’ll cook perfectly in a few minutes, keeping their sweetness and color.
What if I don’t have heavy cream?
You can substitute with half-and-half or a mix of milk and butter, but the sauce will be less rich and creamy. Simmer gently to prevent curdling and consider adding a bit more cheese for richness.
How do I prevent the sauce from separating?
Avoid boiling the cream sauce after adding lemon juice and cheese. Gentle simmering and stirring off heat when adding cheese help maintain a smooth, emulsified sauce.
Can I make this gluten-free?
Yes! Use your favorite gluten-free pasta. Just watch cooking times as they can vary. The sauce works the same and is naturally gluten-free.
Is it okay to prepare this recipe ahead of time?
You can prepare the sauce and pasta separately a day ahead. Store them in airtight containers and combine just before serving to keep textures fresh and sauce creamy.
Can I add other vegetables?
Definitely! Asparagus, spinach, or zucchini work beautifully. Add them during the sauce simmering step and cook until tender but not mushy.
How do I make the sauce thicker?
Simmer the cream longer to reduce and thicken. Adding Parmesan cheese also helps thicken and enrich the sauce. Just be careful not to overcook or boil vigorously.
What wine pairs well with this pasta?
A crisp white like Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio complements the lemony brightness and creamy sauce perfectly. It keeps the meal light and refreshing.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 12 ounces dried fettuccine or linguine pasta
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente. This step is crucial because slightly firm pasta holds the sauce better and provides the perfect texture. Avoid overcooking, as mushy pasta won’t absorb the flavors well. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and drain, reserving about 1 cup of the pasta water before draining for later use.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be tender with a slight bite when tasted.
Step 2
While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté gently for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant but not browned. Browning garlic can make it bitter, so keep an eye on the color and stir frequently to release the flavor without burning.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Garlic should be golden and aromatic without dark spots.
Step 3
Add the peas to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes until they’re tender but still bright green. Cooking peas just right preserves their sweetness and texture. If using frozen peas, no need to thaw first; just add them straight from the freezer to the pan.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Peas should be vibrant and tender but not mushy.
Step 4
Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Let it cook for about 3-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened. This step creates the luscious base for the sauce. Be patient here — rushing can result in a thin sauce that won’t cling to the pasta well.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Sauce should coat the back of a spoon and have a creamy consistency.
Step 5
Stir in the lemon zest and fresh lemon juice. The acidity brightens the cream sauce and balances its richness perfectly. Add salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Season gradually and taste often to get the balance just right.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Sauce should smell fresh with a hint of citrus and taste balanced.
Step 6
Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to combine with the sauce. If the sauce feels too thick, add reserved pasta water a little at a time to loosen it and help the sauce cling beautifully. This starchy water is a secret weapon for silky sauce texture.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be evenly coated with a creamy sauce that isn’t dry or watery.
Step 7
Remove from heat and stir in the grated Parmesan cheese. The cheese melts into the sauce, adding a nutty depth and helping to thicken it slightly. Stir quickly so the cheese blends smoothly without clumping.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Sauce should be creamy and enriched with melted cheese.
Step 8
Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt, pepper, or lemon juice if needed. This final adjustment tailors the flavor to your preference. Remember, a little extra lemon can brighten the dish, while more cheese deepens the richness.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Flavor should be balanced with creaminess, brightness, and seasoning.
Step 9
Serve immediately, garnished with chopped fresh basil or parsley if you like. The herbs add a fresh, herbal note and make the dish look extra inviting. Pair with a crisp white wine or a simple green salad for a complete spring meal.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Dish should look colorful and fresh, ready to enjoy.

Final Thoughts
