Roasted Cabbage Steaks with Herb Lemon Drizzle

Lummy Hayes
Published on March 24, 2026
10 min prep
25 min cook
35 min total
4 servings
Easy

This recipe for roasted cabbage steaks highlights the simple transformation of cabbage when caramelized in the oven and finished with a bright herb and lemon drizzle. It’s an easy vegetable dish that offers a satisfying combination of texture and flavor, suitable for weeknight dinners or light lunches.

Pro Tips

Use a sharp knife to slice cabbage steaks evenly to ensure uniform cooking.
Uneven thickness causes thinner parts to overcook and burn while thicker parts remain undercooked.
Pat the cabbage dry thoroughly before oiling to encourage browning instead of steaming.
Excess moisture creates steam in the oven, preventing caramelization and resulting in soggy texture.
Flip the cabbage steaks halfway through roasting for even color and texture on both sides.
Roasting one side only can lead to uneven cooking and a less appealing texture.
Add the herb lemon drizzle while the cabbage is still warm, not hot or cold.
Warm cabbage helps the drizzle absorb better without wilting the fresh herbs or muting the lemon flavor.
If you want extra crispness, roast the cabbage on a wire rack set over the baking sheet.
Elevating the cabbage allows air circulation beneath, promoting more even roasting and crisper edges.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Cutting cabbage steaks too thin, causing them to dry out and burn.

Why it happens Thin slices roast very quickly and lose moisture, resulting in bitter charred edges and a dry center.

Fix Aim for slices about 1.5 to 2 cm thick to balance tenderness and caramelization.

Not drying the cabbage before oiling and roasting.

Why it happens Water on the surface creates steam and inhibits browning, leading to soggy texture.

Fix Pat dry carefully using kitchen towels before oiling.

Using bottled lemon juice instead of fresh.

Why it happens Bottled juice often lacks brightness and can taste flat or overly acidic.

Fix Always use freshly squeezed lemon juice for the best flavor balance.

Skipping the flip during roasting and only cooking one side.

Why it happens One-sided roasting leads to uneven texture and color.

Fix Flip the cabbage steaks halfway through cooking time for even caramelization.

Variations

Vegan Garlic Tahini Drizzle

Replace the herb lemon drizzle with a sauce made from tahini, garlic, lemon juice, and a touch of water to thin. This adds a creamy, nutty dimension while keeping the dish vegan and flavorful.

Note: Tahini emulsifies with lemon juice similarly to olive oil and adds richness without overpowering the roasted cabbage.

Spiced Roasted Cabbage

Add ground cumin, smoked paprika, and a pinch of cayenne to the olive oil before brushing on the cabbage. Finish with a drizzle of yogurt and chopped cilantro after roasting.

Note: The warm spices complement the natural sweetness of roasted cabbage and introduce a Middle Eastern flavor profile.

Roasted Red Cabbage Steaks

Use red cabbage instead of green. Roast similarly but add a splash of balsamic vinegar to the herb lemon drizzle for a tangy twist that enhances the cabbage's natural color and sweetness.

Note: Red cabbage holds its color well under roasting and pairs nicely with acidic finishes like balsamic vinegar.

Winter Root Vegetable Medley

Add thick slices of parsnips and beets alongside the cabbage steaks on the baking sheet. Roast together, then drizzle with the herb lemon sauce for a hearty seasonal side.

Note: Root vegetables require similar roasting times to cabbage at this thickness, making them ideal companions.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Do not leave roasted cabbage at room temperature for more than 2 hours to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftover roasted cabbage in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The texture may soften after refrigeration.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as cabbage texture deteriorates significantly upon thawing.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a 180°C (350°F) oven for 5-7 minutes to recover some crispness. Avoid microwaving as it can make cabbage soggy.

Make Ahead

Prepare the herb lemon drizzle in advance and store separately. Roast cabbage fresh just before serving to maintain texture, then drizzle immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make roasted cabbage steaks ahead of time?
You can roast the cabbage ahead and refrigerate it, but the texture will soften. For best results, roast fresh and prepare the drizzle separately in advance.
Why is my roasted cabbage soggy instead of crispy?
It likely had excess moisture or was sliced too thick or thin. Patting dry before oiling and ensuring even thickness helps achieve crisp edges.
Can I substitute lemon juice with vinegar in the drizzle?
You can use mild vinegar like apple cider or white wine vinegar, but lemon juice provides a brighter, more balanced acidity for this recipe.
How long does roasted cabbage last in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, it keeps well for up to 3 days but may lose crispness over time.
Is it better to roast cabbage steaks on a baking sheet or a wire rack?
A wire rack promotes air circulation and crisper edges but a baking sheet works well if you flip the steaks halfway.

Ingredients

4 servings of roasted cabbage steaks

  • 1 large green cabbage (about 1.5 kg / 3.3 lbs)
  • 3 tbsp (45 ml) olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp (8 g) fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp (4 g) fresh thyme leaves, chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon (about 30 ml)
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Optional: pinch of red pepper flakes

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it to prevent sticking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully heated; baking sheet prepared
Step 2
Remove the outer leaves of the cabbage and rinse it under cold water. Pat dry with a kitchen towel.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cabbage is clean and dry, ready for slicing
Step 3
Using a sharp knife, cut the cabbage into 1.5–2 cm (about ¾ inch) thick steaks starting from the core. You should get about 4-6 steaks depending on cabbage size. Some leaves may fall off; keep them to roast separately or discard.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even cabbage rounds sliced with visible layered structure
Step 4
Arrange the cabbage steaks on the baking sheet in a single layer. Brush both sides generously with olive oil. Sprinkle salt and freshly ground black pepper evenly over each steak.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Steaks are coated and seasoned uniformly
Step 5
Place the baking sheet in the oven on the middle rack. Roast the cabbage steaks for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through. Look for edges to turn golden brown and slightly crisp. The centers should soften but still hold shape.
⏱ Time: 20-25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden-browned edges, softened centers, slight caramel aroma
Step 6
While the cabbage roasts, prepare the herb lemon drizzle by whisking together the olive oil, lemon juice and zest, minced garlic, chopped parsley, thyme, and optional red pepper flakes in a small bowl. Season lightly with salt and pepper to taste.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Drizzle is emulsified, aromatic, and balanced
Step 7
Once the cabbage steaks are roasted, transfer them to a serving platter. Drizzle the herb lemon mixture evenly over the top while the cabbage is still warm.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cabbage glistens with fresh herb and lemon dressing
Step 8
Serve immediately to enjoy the contrast of warm, caramelized cabbage with the bright, fresh drizzle.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is visually appealing with vibrant colors and aromatic finish
