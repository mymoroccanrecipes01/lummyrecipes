Creamy Tuscan Chicken with Sun-Dried Tomatoes and Spinach

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 3, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy Tuscan chicken hits all the right notes—rich sauce, tangy sun-dried tomatoes, and fresh spinach. It’s a quick, cozy dinner that feels fancy but comes together in under 45 minutes. Perfect for busy weeknights!

Introduction

I first made this creamy Tuscan chicken on a hectic weeknight when I wanted something quick but still special. The combination of sun-dried tomatoes and spinach in a creamy sauce felt like a little escape to Italy right in my own kitchen. What sets this version apart is pounding the chicken breasts to an even thickness—it helps them cook evenly and stay juicy. Plus, I always add a splash of chicken broth to lighten the sauce just a bit without losing that rich, comforting feel. The secret is letting the sauce simmer gently until it thickens and coats the chicken perfectly. When it’s done, you get tender chicken with a luscious, slightly tangy sauce dotted with vibrant spinach and sun-dried tomatoes. It looks fancy but is totally fuss-free, making it my go-to when I want to impress my family or just treat myself after a long day. I think you’ll love how fresh yet indulgent it tastes!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by pounding the chicken breasts to an even thickness, about 1/2 inch thick. This helps them cook evenly and prevents drying out. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper. Heating the olive oil over medium-high heat, sear the chicken for about 4-5 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Don’t rush this step—it’s key for developing flavor through the Maillard reaction, which creates that beautiful crust and deep flavor. Avoid overcrowding the pan to keep the chicken from steaming. Once cooked, set the chicken aside on a plate.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken is golden brown on both sides and registers 165°F internally or no longer pink inside.
Step 2
In the same pan, reduce heat to medium and add the minced garlic. Sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant but not browned to avoid bitterness. Add the chopped sun-dried tomatoes and stir for another minute to release their tangy flavor into the oil. Pour in the chicken broth to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits—these add depth to the sauce. Then stir in the heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, Italian seasoning, and crushed red pepper flakes if using. Let the sauce simmer gently, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly, about 5-7 minutes. This slow simmer allows the cheese to melt fully and the flavors to meld beautifully.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Sauce is creamy, slightly thickened, and coats the back of a spoon.
Step 3
Add the fresh spinach leaves to the sauce, stirring gently just until wilted, about 1-2 minutes. This keeps the spinach bright and fresh instead of soggy. Return the chicken breasts to the pan, spooning some sauce over them. Let everything cook together for another 3-4 minutes on low heat so the chicken absorbs the sauce flavors and stays warm. This final step ensures each bite is juicy and flavorful, with the spinach adding a fresh contrast to the creamy sauce.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Spinach is wilted but still vibrant green; chicken is heated through and coated with sauce.
Step 4
Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. The balance of salty Parmesan and tangy tomatoes should be just right. If the sauce feels too thick, add a splash more chicken broth to loosen it up. If too thin, simmer a minute longer to reduce. This step is important to tailor the sauce exactly to your taste and desired consistency.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Sauce tastes balanced, neither too salty nor bland, and coats the back of a spoon smoothly.
Step 5
Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a burst of herbal brightness and serve immediately. This dish pairs wonderfully with crusty bread, mashed potatoes, or pasta to soak up all that creamy sauce. Serving it hot ensures the sauce stays luscious and the chicken juicy.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Fresh basil added and dish plated attractively, ready to serve.
Step 6
For an extra touch, you can sprinkle additional Parmesan cheese on top just before serving. This adds a lovely salty bite and an extra layer of richness. Just be sure not to add too much at this stage to keep the sauce’s delicate balance.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Light dusting of Parmesan visibly melts slightly on hot sauce.
Step 7
If you want to add a bit of heat, crushed red pepper flakes can be sprinkled over the finished dish. This adds a subtle spicy kick that complements the creamy sauce perfectly. Start with a small amount and adjust according to your heat preference.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Red pepper flakes are evenly distributed without overpowering the dish.
Step 8
Make sure to use fresh garlic rather than pre-minced for the best flavor. Garlic loses its punch quickly when pre-minced and jarred. Fresh garlic gives that vibrant aroma and depth that really shines in this sauce.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Garlic aroma is fresh and fragrant during cooking.
Step 9
Finally, let the sauce rest for a minute off the heat before serving. This lets it thicken slightly and the flavors meld even more, making every bite rich and satisfying. Resist the urge to serve immediately off the heat for the best texture.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Sauce thickens slightly and flavors taste more rounded.

Pro Tips

Pound the chicken breasts evenly before cooking.
An even thickness ensures the chicken cooks uniformly, preventing dry edges and raw centers. This technique helps the chicken stay juicy and makes the cooking time predictable.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing the chicken.
Crowding traps steam, which prevents the chicken from browning properly. Browning creates complex flavors through the Maillard reaction, which is key for a tasty dish.
Deglaze the pan with chicken broth after sautéing garlic and tomatoes.
This lifts the flavorful browned bits stuck to the pan, infusing the sauce with rich, savory depth that would otherwise be lost.
Simmer the cream and Parmesan gently without boiling.
High heat can cause the dairy to curdle. A gentle simmer allows the sauce to thicken smoothly and the flavors to meld perfectly.
Add fresh spinach at the end to avoid overcooking.
Spinach wilts quickly; adding it last preserves its bright color, fresh flavor, and slight crunch, balancing the richness of the sauce.
Use oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes for best flavor and texture.
They are softer and more flavorful, contributing a tangy sweetness that brightens the creamy sauce.
Let the sauce rest off heat for a minute before serving.
This thickens the sauce slightly and allows flavors to meld, enhancing the overall taste and texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the step of pounding chicken breasts.

Why it happens People often skip this because it feels like extra work or they think it’s unnecessary.

Fix Take a few minutes to pound the chicken evenly using a meat mallet or rolling pin. It ensures even cooking and juicier meat, preventing dry or undercooked spots.

Cooking chicken on too high heat causing burning.

Why it happens Rushing to cook the chicken quickly can cause the outside to burn before the inside cooks through.

Fix Use medium-high heat and watch carefully. Adjust the heat if the chicken browns too fast. Proper searing builds flavor without burning.

Boiling the cream sauce.

Why it happens Some assume higher heat speeds cooking and thickening.

Fix Simmer gently on low to medium heat. Boiling can cause curdling and separate the sauce, ruining texture.

Adding spinach too early.

Why it happens Inexperience or rushing can lead to overcooked, mushy spinach.

Fix Add spinach at the very end and cook just until wilted for vibrant color and fresh flavor.

Using pre-grated Parmesan cheese.

Why it happens Convenience often wins over quality.

Fix Grate fresh Parmesan for a smoother, richer sauce. Pre-grated cheese contains anti-caking agents that affect melting.

Variations

Creamy Tuscan Chicken with Mushrooms

Add sliced cremini or button mushrooms when sautéing garlic for an earthy twist. The mushrooms soak up the sauce beautifully, adding texture and umami depth.

Note: Sauté mushrooms until golden before adding liquids to avoid excess moisture diluting the sauce.

Spicy Creamy Tuscan Chicken

Incorporate cayenne pepper or more crushed red pepper flakes for extra heat. This variation gives the creamy sauce a lively kick without overpowering the flavors.

Note: Add spices gradually and taste to balance heat with creaminess.

Creamy Tuscan Chicken with Zucchini Noodles

Serve the chicken over sautéed zucchini noodles instead of pasta for a low-carb option. The light, fresh noodles complement the rich sauce perfectly.

Note: Cook zucchini noodles briefly to avoid sogginess and drain excess moisture.

Dairy-Free Creamy Tuscan Chicken

Use coconut cream and nutritional yeast in place of heavy cream and Parmesan for a dairy-free version. The sauce stays creamy with a slightly different but delicious flavor.

Note: Simmer gently and taste often to adjust seasoning with dairy substitutes.

Creamy Tuscan Chicken with White Beans

Stir in drained canned white beans near the end for added protein and a hearty texture. This makes the dish more filling and adds a lovely creaminess from the beans.

Note: Warm beans through gently to avoid breaking them apart.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover loosely if you need to keep it out briefly but refrigerate promptly to maintain freshness and safety.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and the spinach will soften, so gently reheat with a splash of chicken broth to restore creaminess before serving.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. Avoid freezing with fresh spinach as it can become mushy. To freeze, omit spinach and add fresh when reheating. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator.

Reheating

Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat or in a microwave at medium power. Add a little chicken broth or cream to loosen the sauce and stir frequently to prevent curdling or drying out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the sauce and sear the chicken up to 1 day ahead. Store separately and combine when reheating to keep the chicken juicy and the sauce fresh. Add fresh spinach just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Boneless skinless thighs work well and stay juicy. They may take a few minutes longer to cook, so check for doneness with a thermometer or cut to ensure no pink remains.
What can I substitute for sun-dried tomatoes?
If you don’t have sun-dried tomatoes, chopped roasted red peppers or cherry tomatoes can add a similar sweet and tangy flavor. Fresh tomatoes will make the sauce lighter and less intense.
How do I prevent the cream sauce from curdling?
Simmer the sauce gently over low to medium heat and avoid boiling. Adding cheese slowly and stirring continuously helps emulsify the sauce and keeps it smooth.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free chicken broth and check that your Parmesan cheese is not processed with additives containing gluten.
What sides pair best with this dish?
Crusty bread, mashed potatoes, pasta, or even cauliflower rice are all great options to soak up the creamy sauce and complement the flavors.
Is there a way to make this recipe lighter?
You can substitute half-and-half for heavy cream to reduce fat or use Greek yogurt stirred in at the end off heat for creaminess without heaviness. Just be careful not to boil yogurt to prevent curdling.
How do I store leftovers to keep the spinach fresh?
Spinach tends to lose texture when stored. For best results, store spinach separately and add fresh when reheating. If mixed in, expect softer spinach after refrigeration.
Can I prepare this recipe in advance for a dinner party?
Yes! You can sear the chicken and prepare the sauce a day ahead, then reheat gently and add fresh spinach just before serving to keep everything vibrant and fresh.
