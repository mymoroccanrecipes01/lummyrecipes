Garlic Butter Shrimp with Fresh Spring Peas: Quick & Flavorful Dinner

Lummy Hayes Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 15 min cook 4 servings

This shrimp recipe is a buttery, garlicky treat with vibrant spring peas for a fresh pop. It’s super quick to make, so perfect when you want something tasty without the fuss. Great for busy weeknights or casual dinners!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this garlic butter shrimp recipe when I was desperate for a quick dinner after a hectic day. What I love about this dish is how it comes together in under 30 minutes but still feels special and fresh. The combination of garlic-infused butter and bright spring peas adds a lovely balance—rich yet vibrant. I’ve tweaked it over time to get the peas perfectly tender but still crisp, and the shrimp cooked just right—juicy and flavorful without getting rubbery. The trick I learned is to add the lemon zest and juice at the end, which really brightens the whole dish and lifts the buttery richness. It’s become a staple for weeknights and casual dinners with friends because it’s so easy but never boring. Plus, it looks beautiful on the plate with those poppin’ green peas. If you want a fuss-free meal that doesn’t skimp on flavor, this one’s a winner every time!

Why This Works

This recipe succeeds because it balances rich, buttery flavors with bright, fresh ingredients like spring peas and lemon. The garlic-infused butter creates a luscious sauce that coats the shrimp perfectly, while the peas add a sweet, crisp contrast that keeps the dish from feeling heavy. Cooking the shrimp just until opaque ensures they stay tender and juicy rather than rubbery, which is a common pitfall.

The timing of adding ingredients is also key: garlic is gently cooked first to release flavor without burning, shrimp are seared quickly to lock in moisture, and peas are added last to maintain their vibrant color and snap. Finishing with lemon juice and zest brightens the dish and cuts through the richness, providing a fresh balance.

The technique of tossing the shrimp back into the buttered peas melds the flavors and gives the sauce its silky texture. Using fresh parsley at the end adds a final herbal note and visual appeal. Altogether, these carefully timed steps and ingredient choices create a dish that feels both indulgent and light, perfect for a quick yet impressive meal.

  • Choose fresh or thawed large shrimp that are peeled and deveined for the best texture and flavor. Fresh shrimp provide a sweet, briny taste, while frozen can work if thawed properly. Avoid pre-seasoned or pre-cooked shrimp to maintain control over flavor and cooking time.
  • Use unsalted butter so you can control the saltiness. Butter adds richness and helps carry the garlic flavor throughout the dish. High-quality butter with a creamy texture will melt smoothly and create a glossy sauce.
  • Fresh spring peas are preferred for their sweetness and crisp texture. If unavailable, frozen peas can be used but add them later and cook briefly to avoid mushiness. Shelled peas add freshness and a pop of color.
  • Fresh garlic cloves provide the best pungent, aromatic flavor. Mince finely to release oils quickly during cooking. Avoid pre-minced garlic as it can be less flavorful and sometimes bitter when cooked.
  • Fresh lemon zest and juice brighten the rich butter and shrimp, adding acidity that balances the dish. Avoid bottled lemon juice for best freshness and flavor impact.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup fresh spring peas, shelled
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by prepping your shrimp—make sure they’re peeled and deveined for the best texture. Heat the olive oil and 1 tablespoon of butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the butter melts and starts to foam, add the minced garlic. Cook it gently for about 1 minute until fragrant but not browned; this step infuses the fat with garlic flavor without bitterness, setting the base for the dish.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Garlic is fragrant and slightly softened but not browned or burnt
  2. Step 2

    Toss in the shrimp, spreading them out in a single layer. Cook for about 2-3 minutes per side, depending on size, until they turn pink and opaque. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure even cooking and prevent steaming. If you rush or cook on too high heat, shrimp can become tough and rubbery, so maintain medium-high heat and watch closely.

    ⏱ 5-6 minutes✓ Shrimp are opaque pink with curled tails but not shriveled
  3. Step 3

    Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside. In the same pan, add the fresh spring peas and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring often. The goal is to soften them slightly while keeping that fresh snap. Adding them after the shrimp ensures they don’t overcook and lose their vibrant color and sweet flavor.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Peas are bright green and tender-crisp
  4. Step 4

    Lower the heat to medium and add the remaining 1 tablespoon of butter to the peas. Once melted, return the shrimp to the pan and toss everything together gently to coat in the garlic butter. This step melds the flavors and gives the dish that luscious, silky finish.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Shrimp and peas are evenly coated with glossy butter sauce
  5. Step 5

    Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Then add the lemon zest and fresh lemon juice. Stir quickly and remove from heat. The lemon adds a bright, fresh contrast that cuts through the richness of the butter, balancing the dish beautifully.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Dish smells bright and fresh with a hint of citrus
  6. Step 6

    Sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the top for a burst of color and herbaceous flavor. Give the dish one last gentle toss before serving. The parsley adds a fresh, slightly peppery note that complements the shrimp perfectly.

    ⏱ 30 seconds✓ Parsley looks vibrant and evenly distributed
  7. Step 7

    Serve immediately while warm. This dish goes great with crusty bread to soak up the buttery sauce or over a bed of rice or pasta for a fuller meal. The key is to enjoy it fresh so the shrimp stay tender and the peas remain crisp.

    ⏱ Serve immediately✓ Meal is served hot with vibrant colors and glossy sauce
  8. Step 8

    If you want to prep ahead, you can shell the peas and clean the shrimp in advance. Keep them refrigerated separately and combine just before cooking to keep everything fresh and vibrant.

    ⏱ Prep ahead✓ Ingredients are fresh and ready to cook
  9. Step 9

    For an extra touch, try adding a pinch of red pepper flakes while cooking the garlic for a subtle heat that pairs beautifully with the buttery shrimp. It’s an easy way to add a little kick without overpowering the dish.

    ⏱ Optional✓ Garlic butter has a gentle spicy aroma

Pro Tips

  • Don’t overcrowd the pan when cooking shrimp. — Shrimp release moisture as they cook, and overcrowding traps steam, leading to soggy, rubbery shrimp rather than nicely seared ones. Cooking in batches if needed ensures a better texture and browning.
  • Add lemon zest and juice off the heat. — Adding lemon while the pan is hot can cause bitterness and diminish the fresh aroma. Adding it at the end preserves brightness and ensures the acidity balances the butter without cooking off.
  • Use fresh peas and cook them briefly. — Fresh peas add a sweet crunch and vibrant color that frozen peas can’t fully replicate. Cooking them just until tender-crisp preserves their natural sweetness and prevents mushiness.
  • Mince garlic finely and cook gently. — Garlic burns quickly and turns bitter if cooked on high heat or too long. Cooking it gently in butter releases its flavor without bitterness, creating a smooth, fragrant base for the sauce.
  • Serve immediately after cooking. — Shrimp can overcook and peas can lose their snap if left to sit. Serving right away ensures optimal texture and flavor, with warm butter sauce coating the ingredients perfectly.
  • Use a wide skillet for even cooking. — A wide pan allows shrimp to cook in a single layer, ensuring even heat distribution and proper searing. Crowded pans lead to uneven cooking and steaming.
  • Fresh parsley adds a bright finish. — Adding fresh herbs at the end preserves their flavor and color, giving the dish a fresh herbal note that complements the richness of the butter and garlic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overcooking the shrimp
    Why: Shrimp cook very quickly, and it’s easy to leave them on heat too long, especially if you’re multitasking or not watching closely.
    Fix: Cook shrimp just until they turn opaque and pink, usually 2-3 minutes per side. Remove them promptly from the heat to avoid toughness.
  • Burning the garlic
    Why: Garlic cooks fast and can burn if cooked over high heat or for too long before adding other ingredients.
    Fix: Cook garlic gently over medium to medium-low heat until fragrant, about 1 minute. If it starts to brown, reduce heat or remove from pan briefly.
  • Using frozen peas without adjusting cooking time
    Why: Frozen peas can become mushy if cooked too long or added too early.
    Fix: Add frozen peas at the very end and cook just until heated through to keep texture intact.
  • Skipping lemon juice or zest
    Why: Some cooks overlook the finishing step, missing out on the bright contrast that balances the dish.
    Fix: Always add fresh lemon zest and juice off the heat to enhance flavor and freshness.
  • Overcrowding the pan
    Why: Trying to cook all shrimp at once traps steam and prevents proper searing.
    Fix: Cook shrimp in batches if needed, giving them space to brown evenly.

Variations

  • Spicy Garlic Butter Shrimp: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of cayenne pepper with the garlic to give this dish a gentle heat. It pairs wonderfully with the buttery sauce and fresh peas for a slightly spicy kick.
    Be careful adding spice early to avoid burning; add with garlic on medium heat and adjust amount to taste.
  • Creamy Lemon Garlic Shrimp: Stir in a splash of heavy cream or crème fraîche after adding lemon juice to create a rich, creamy sauce. This variation is indulgent and perfect over pasta or rice.
    Reduce butter slightly to balance cream, and cook gently to prevent cream from curdling.
  • Shrimp and Asparagus with Garlic Butter: Swap out spring peas for tender asparagus tips for a different fresh green vegetable. Asparagus adds a nice earthy note that complements shrimp beautifully.
    Add asparagus earlier than peas to ensure it cooks through but remains crisp.
  • Garlic Butter Shrimp with Mushrooms: Add sliced cremini or button mushrooms sautéed with the garlic for an earthier, heartier version. Mushrooms soak up the buttery garlic sauce, making each bite savory and satisfying.
    Cook mushrooms first until browned and their moisture evaporates before adding shrimp.
  • Herb-Infused Garlic Butter Shrimp: Add fresh thyme or tarragon with the parsley for a more complex herbal flavor profile. These herbs add subtle aromatic notes that elevate the dish.
    Add delicate herbs at the end to preserve their fresh flavor.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit out for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to maintain freshness and safety.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days. The shrimp can become a bit firmer when chilled, so reheat gently to restore tenderness. Peas may lose some crispness but remain tasty.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freezing is not recommended for best texture, as shrimp can become rubbery and peas mushy after thawing. If freezing, use airtight containers or freezer bags and consume within 1 month.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce. Avoid microwaving at high power as it can overcook shrimp and dry them out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can shell peas and prep shrimp 1-2 days ahead, storing separately in the fridge. Combine and cook just before serving to keep everything fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use frozen shrimp for this recipe?
Yes, frozen shrimp work well if thawed completely before cooking. Thaw them in the fridge overnight or under cold running water. Pat dry to avoid excess moisture that can prevent proper searing.
What can I substitute if I don’t have fresh peas?
Frozen peas are a good alternative; just add them at the end of cooking and heat through briefly to avoid mushiness. You could also use snap peas or green beans for a different texture.
How do I avoid rubbery shrimp?
Shrimp cook very quickly—usually 2-3 minutes per side. Overcooking causes toughness. Watch for the shrimp turning pink and opaque and remove promptly from heat.
Can I make this dish spicy?
Absolutely! Add red pepper flakes or a dash of cayenne with the garlic for a gentle heat that complements the buttery sauce without overpowering it.
Is it better to use lemon juice or zest?
Both are important. Zest adds aromatic oils and brightness, while juice adds acidity to balance richness. Use fresh lemon for best flavor.
Can I prepare this dish ahead of time?
You can prep ingredients like peeling shrimp and shelling peas in advance, but cook everything just before serving to keep textures fresh and flavors vibrant.
What sides go well with this shrimp dish?
This shrimp is delicious with crusty bread, over rice, pasta, or even a simple green salad. The buttery garlic sauce pairs perfectly with these accompaniments.
How do I store leftovers?
Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Reheat gently to avoid toughening the shrimp. Avoid freezing if possible as texture degrades.
