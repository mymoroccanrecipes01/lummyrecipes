Crispy Airfryer Chicken Wings Tossed in Garlicky Parmesan Goodness

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

These airfryer chicken wings are my favorite game night snack—crispy without the mess of frying and coated in a flavorful garlic parmesan mix. They’re quick to make and perfect for sharing with friends!

Introduction

I’ve been making these garlicky parmesan airfryer chicken wings for a few years now, and they always steal the show at game nights or casual hangouts. The first time I tried airfrying wings, I was skeptical about getting that crispy skin without deep frying. But the trick I learned—drying the wings thoroughly and tossing them in a little oil before airfrying—makes all the difference. Plus, coating them in a buttery garlic parmesan mix right after cooking adds so much flavor without overpowering the natural chicken taste. What I love most is how they come out perfectly crunchy on the outside, juicy inside, and ready in about 45 minutes total. No mess, no fuss, just delicious wings you’ll want to make again and again. The parmesan and garlic combo is a classic, but the airfryer method keeps things lighter and easier to clean up. If you’ve struggled with soggy wings or greasy fryers, this recipe is a game changer!

Why This Works

The secret to these crispy airfryer chicken wings lies in moisture control and the magic of hot circulating air. Patting the wings dry before cooking removes surface water that would otherwise steam the skin, preventing it from crisping. The olive oil coats the skin and promotes the Maillard reaction—the chemical browning that creates those irresistible golden, crunchy textures.

Airfrying is a wonderful technique because it mimics deep frying by circulating hot air at high speed, cooking the wings evenly without submerging them in oil. This method reduces grease and mess while still achieving a crisp exterior. The seasoning blend of garlic powder and smoked paprika adds depth without overpowering, setting the stage for the final parmesan coating.

The garlic butter and parmesan mixture is added immediately after cooking, using the residual heat to gently melt the cheese onto the wings without turning it into a clumpy mess. This balance keeps the cheese flavorful and well distributed, enhancing every bite. Tossing the wings in sauce off heat also preserves the crisp skin, which can get soggy if coated too early.

Finally, optional steps like air-drying the wings in the fridge before cooking or finishing with a short blast of higher heat help intensify the crispiness. These techniques work together to deliver wings that are crunchy, juicy, and bursting with garlicky parmesan flavor every time.

  • Choose fresh, whole chicken wings with the tips removed for the best cooking results. Fresh wings have a better texture and cook more evenly than frozen, though you can use frozen wings if thawed thoroughly. Pat them dry well before seasoning to ensure crisp skin. Avoid overcrowding when cooking to allow air circulation.
  • A light coating of olive oil helps the wings brown and crisp in the airfryer by promoting the Maillard reaction. Use extra virgin olive oil for flavor or a neutral oil if preferred. Too much oil can cause sogginess, so just a tablespoon for 2 pounds of wings is ideal.
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese works best for coating the wings, offering a sharp, nutty flavor and dry texture that clings well. Pre-grated cheeses often contain anti-caking agents that can affect melting and texture. If unavailable, Pecorino Romano makes a good substitute but has a saltier bite.
  • Melting unsalted butter with fresh garlic creates a rich, flavorful sauce that coats the wings. Using unsalted butter lets you control the salt level better. Be careful not to brown or burn the garlic during melting, as it can turn bitter and overpower the flavor.
  • Fresh minced garlic infuses the butter with bright, pungent flavor that’s essential to the sauce. Garlic powder in the seasoning adds background depth, but fresh garlic in the sauce provides that unmistakable punch. If garlic is too strong for you, reduce the amount or substitute with garlic-infused oil.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken wings, split and tips removed, patted dry
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley (optional)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by patting your chicken wings very dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture on the skin will steam the wings instead of crisping them up. Once dry, place the wings in a large bowl and drizzle with olive oil, tossing to coat evenly. The oil helps promote browning and crispiness in the airfryer. Season the wings with garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper, making sure each piece is well coated. This seasoning mix gives the wings a subtle smoky depth and garlic flavor that pairs beautifully with the parmesan later.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Wings are evenly coated and dry to the touch before cooking
  2. Step 2

    Preheat your airfryer to 380°F (193°C) for about 5 minutes. Arrange the wings in a single layer in the basket without overcrowding to allow air to circulate freely. Cook for 25 to 30 minutes, shaking the basket or turning the wings halfway through to ensure even cooking. The key here is the hot circulating air which crisps the skin beautifully without frying. Rushing this step or stacking the wings will cause uneven cooking and less crispiness. The wings should be golden brown and cooked through with an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ Golden, crispy skin with no pink meat inside
  3. Step 3

    While the wings are cooking, prepare the garlic parmesan sauce. In a small bowl, melt the butter and stir in the minced garlic. Let it sit for a minute so the garlic infuses the butter without burning. This buttery garlic sauce will coat the wings after airfrying, adding richness and a punch of flavor that complements the crisp skin perfectly.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Butter is melted and garlic is fragrant but not browned
  4. Step 4

    Once the wings are done, transfer them to a large mixing bowl. Pour the garlic butter over the hot wings and toss gently but thoroughly to coat each piece. Immediately sprinkle the grated Parmesan over the wings and toss again. The residual heat helps the cheese stick without melting into a clump. This step ensures every bite is covered in that irresistible garlicky, cheesy goodness.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wings are evenly coated with garlic butter and Parmesan
  5. Step 5

    If you like, sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the wings for a pop of color and a hint of freshness. Serve immediately while the wings are still warm and crispy. Pair them with your favorite dipping sauces or enjoy as is—the garlicky parmesan flavor is enough to satisfy on its own.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Wings look glossy with cheese and butter, garnished with parsley
  6. Step 6

    To get even crispier wings, you can increase the airfryer temperature to 400°F (204°C) for the last 3-5 minutes of cooking. Just watch closely to avoid burning, especially since wings vary in size. This extra step intensifies the Maillard reaction, giving you that golden crunch everyone loves.

    ⏱ 3-5 minutes✓ Skin is deeply golden and crackly without charred spots
  7. Step 7

    If you want to prep ahead, season and oil the wings, then refrigerate them uncovered on a wire rack for a couple of hours or overnight. This air-drying step helps draw out moisture and leads to even crispier results when you cook them later. Just bring the wings back to room temperature before airfrying to ensure even cooking.

    ⏱ Varies✓ Wings feel dry and slightly tacky before cooking
  8. Step 8

    Avoid overcrowding the airfryer basket by cooking wings in batches if necessary. Crowding traps steam and prevents the skin from crisping. Taking the extra time to cook in smaller batches ensures you get that perfect crunch every time without soggy spots.

    ⏱ Varies✓ Wings cook evenly and crisp all around
  9. Step 9

    After tossing in the garlic parmesan sauce, let the wings rest for a minute or two before serving. This allows the flavors to meld and the cheese to set slightly, so it sticks better and doesn’t fall off when you pick up a wing. It also cools them to a perfect eating temperature.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Wings are flavorful, cheese coating is stable

Pro Tips

  • Dry wings thoroughly before seasoning. — Removing surface moisture prevents steaming and helps the skin crisp up in the airfryer. If the wings are even slightly damp, they release steam during cooking, resulting in soggy skin instead of that coveted crunch.
  • Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket. — Air needs to circulate freely around each wing to cook evenly and crisp properly. Overcrowding traps moisture and causes uneven cooking. Cook in batches if necessary for best results.
  • Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese. — Freshly grated Parmesan melts beautifully and sticks to the wings without clumping. Pre-grated cheese often has additives that can affect texture and flavor, resulting in a less satisfying coating.
  • Toss wings in sauce immediately after cooking. — The residual heat helps melt the butter and cheese, creating a cohesive coating. Waiting too long cools the wings, making the cheese stick poorly and the sauce less flavorful.
  • Try air-drying wings in the fridge before cooking. — Leaving seasoned wings uncovered on a rack for a few hours or overnight draws out moisture from the skin, leading to even crispier results when cooked.
  • Finish with a blast of high heat. — Increasing the airfryer temperature at the end intensifies the Maillard reaction, deepening the golden color and crunch without drying out the meat.
  • Rest wings briefly before serving. — Letting the wings sit for a minute after tossing in sauce allows the flavors to meld and the cheese coating to set, improving texture and taste.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Cooking wings straight from the fridge without drying.
    Why: People often underestimate how much moisture clings to wings after refrigeration, leading to steaming instead of crisping.
    Fix: Pat wings dry thoroughly with paper towels before cooking. For best results, air-dry them uncovered on a rack in the fridge for a couple of hours to remove excess moisture.
  • Overcrowding the airfryer basket.
    Why: Trying to cook all wings at once to save time is tempting, but it traps steam and prevents a crispy crust.
    Fix: Cook wings in batches if needed, leaving space between pieces to allow hot air circulation for even crisping.
  • Skipping the oil or using too little.
    Why: Some believe airfryer cooking requires no oil, but skin needs a light coating to brown properly.
    Fix: Toss wings in about 1 tablespoon of oil to encourage browning and prevent sticking. Too much oil can cause sogginess, so measure carefully.
  • Adding the parmesan too early.
    Why: Coating wings with cheese before cooking leads to burnt or clumpy cheese instead of a smooth crust.
    Fix: Add the parmesan after wings come out of the airfryer while still hot, tossing gently to coat evenly.
  • Burning garlic in the butter sauce.
    Why: Melting butter over high heat with garlic can quickly burn the garlic, making the sauce bitter.
    Fix: Melt butter over low heat and add garlic, letting it infuse gently without browning. Remove from heat as soon as garlic is fragrant.

Variations

  • Spicy Buffalo Parmesan Wings: Add 1-2 teaspoons of cayenne pepper or hot sauce to the garlic butter before tossing the wings for a spicy kick. This variation combines classic buffalo heat with the cheesy parmesan coating for a flavorful twist. Expect a slightly tangy, spicy flavor balanced by the richness of butter and cheese.
    Adjust the amount of cayenne or hot sauce to control heat level. Toss wings quickly after cooking to keep skin crispy.
  • Lemon Herb Parmesan Wings: Mix in zest of one lemon and 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme or rosemary into the garlic butter sauce. This brightens the flavor and adds herbal notes that complement the parmesan. The wings taste fresher and less heavy, perfect for spring or summer meals.
    Add lemon zest and herbs to sauce off heat to preserve fresh flavors.
  • Honey Garlic Parmesan Wings: Stir 1-2 tablespoons honey into the melted butter with garlic for a sweet-savory glaze. These wings have a sticky, caramelized finish with a gentle garlic bite, great for those who like a touch of sweetness.
    Toss wings immediately after cooking to allow honey to coat evenly without burning.
  • Smoky BBQ Parmesan Wings: Add 1 teaspoon smoked paprika and 1 tablespoon BBQ sauce to the butter-garlic mixture. This gives the wings a smoky, tangy flavor that pairs well with the parmesan crust. Expect a smoky sweetness with a savory finish.
    Use a mild BBQ sauce to avoid overpowering the parmesan flavor.
  • Parmesan Ranch Wings: Mix 2 tablespoons ranch seasoning into the parmesan cheese before tossing. This adds creamy herb notes that make the wings taste like a crispy ranch-flavored snack. Great for fans of ranch dressing.
    Use a dry ranch seasoning mix for best texture and flavor.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These wings can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. To keep them from drying out, cover with foil or place in a shallow dish but avoid airtight containers at room temp to prevent sogginess.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. They may lose some crispiness when cold but reheat well in the airfryer or oven to restore crunch.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cooked wings in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw in the fridge overnight before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat wings in the airfryer at 375°F (190°C) for 5-7 minutes to crisp them back up. Avoid microwaving as it makes the skin soggy. Add a splash of melted butter or garlic sauce after reheating if needed.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can season and oil wings 1-2 days ahead and keep them uncovered on a wire rack in the fridge to air-dry. Prepare the garlic parmesan sauce fresh on the day of cooking for best flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but make sure to fully thaw and pat the wings dry before seasoning. Cooking frozen wings directly will result in uneven cooking and less crispy skin because excess moisture creates steam.
What if I don’t have an airfryer?
You can bake the wings in a preheated oven at 425°F (220°C) on a wire rack over a baking sheet for about 40-45 minutes, flipping halfway. The wire rack helps air circulate and crisp the skin.
How do I make the wings extra crispy?
Patting dry, air-drying in the fridge, and not overcrowding the basket are key. Also, finishing with a few minutes at a higher temperature in the airfryer intensifies crispiness.
Can I use pre-grated Parmesan cheese?
You can, but freshly grated cheese melts better and clings nicely to the wings. Pre-grated often contains anti-caking agents that affect texture and flavor.
What dipping sauces go well with these wings?
Ranch, blue cheese dressing, or a simple garlic aioli complement the garlicky parmesan flavor beautifully. You can also serve them plain since they’re flavorful on their own.
How do I store leftover wings?
Keep them in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in the airfryer or oven to restore crispiness before serving.
Can I make the garlic parmesan sauce ahead of time?
It’s best to prepare the sauce fresh to keep the garlic flavor bright and the butter from solidifying. However, you can melt the butter and add garlic a few hours ahead, then rewarm gently before tossing.
What if I want to make these wings spicier?
Add cayenne pepper or hot sauce to the garlic butter sauce before tossing. Start with small amounts and adjust to your heat preference.
