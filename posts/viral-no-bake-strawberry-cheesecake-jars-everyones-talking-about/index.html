No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Jars That Feel Like Summer in a Bite

Lummy Hayes Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

These no bake strawberry cheesecake jars are delightfully creamy and fresh, perfect for warm days when you want a simple but impressive dessert. The graham cracker base gets a quick soak for extra softness, making each spoonful melt in your mouth. Serve chilled for an easy summer treat!

Introduction

I first made these no bake strawberry cheesecake jars on a hot summer afternoon when I really didn’t want to turn on the oven. What I love about this recipe is how simple it is—just a few ingredients, no baking, and it still feels like a treat. My family especially loves the soft graham cracker base, which I soak just a bit in melted butter to keep it from being too dry or crumbly. The cream cheese filling is whipped until silky smooth with powdered sugar and vanilla, then folded into whipped cream to get that light, airy texture. It’s not dense or heavy like some cheesecakes, which makes it perfect for warm days. Plus, layering the fresh chopped strawberries inside and on top adds a burst of natural sweetness and color that’s just irresistible. I’ve tried a few different versions before, but this one strikes the perfect balance between creamy and fresh. It’s great for summer parties or just a casual dessert when you want something homemade but fuss-free. The best part? You can prep it in under 20 minutes and pop it in the fridge to chill until you’re ready to enjoy. Honestly, it’s become my go-to for impressing guests without the stress.

Why This Works

This recipe balances texture and flavor by carefully layering creamy filling with a softened graham cracker crust and fresh strawberries. The key to success is the gentle soaking of the crumbs in melted butter, which keeps the crust from being dry or crumbly but avoids sogginess. This moisture allows the crust to bind together while remaining tender.

The cream cheese filling is whipped with powdered sugar and vanilla, then lightened by folding in freshly whipped heavy cream. This creates a mousse-like texture that is airy yet rich, which makes it feel less heavy compared to traditional baked cheesecakes. The whipped cream acts as a stabilizer and adds volume, preventing the filling from becoming dense.

Chilling the jars for several hours is essential to allow the filling to set and flavors to blend. The cold temperature also helps the crust firm up slightly while keeping the strawberries fresh and juicy. The layering technique ensures that each spoonful has a balance of textures and flavors, from buttery crust to creamy filling to bright, fresh berries.

Overall, this recipe succeeds because it uses simple ingredients combined with thoughtful techniques: softening, whipping, folding, and chilling, all of which contribute to a dessert that feels indulgent but light, easy to make but impressive to serve.

  • Look for finely crushed graham cracker crumbs for an even crust texture. Avoid large chunks as they won’t bind well. The buttery flavor is crucial here, so don’t skip the melted butter; it adds richness and moisture that prevents the crust from drying out or becoming crumbly. Store crumbs in an airtight container to keep them fresh.
  • Use full-fat cream cheese that’s softened to room temperature for smooth mixing. If it’s too cold, lumps form and the filling won’t be silky. The cream cheese provides tang and body to the filling, balancing the sweetness. Lower-fat versions often lack the richness and can cause the filling to be too runny or grainy.
  • Powdered sugar dissolves easily into the cream cheese, preventing any grit in the filling. It also helps stabilize the whipped cream mixture. Avoid granulated sugar here as it won’t fully dissolve and can affect texture. Adjust sweetness to taste but don’t skimp—this balances the tang of the cheese nicely.
  • Chilled heavy cream whips into stiff peaks, adding volume and lightness to the filling. It also helps stabilize the mixture so it sets nicely without collapsing. Use fresh cream with at least 36% fat for best results. Overwhipping can turn it into butter, so watch carefully during whipping.
  • Choose ripe, fragrant strawberries for the best flavor and juiciness. Fresh berries add natural sweetness and a pleasant tartness that cuts through the richness of the filling. Avoid overly soft or mushy berries as they can make the dessert watery. Wash and dry thoroughly before chopping to keep the filling texture intact.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream, chilled
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, chopped
  • Extra strawberries for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by mixing the graham cracker crumbs with melted butter and sugar in a bowl. The butter softens the crumbs and helps them stick together, creating a base that’s moist but still holds shape. Avoid skipping the butter or using cold butter straight from the fridge because it won’t soak into the crumbs properly, resulting in a dry crust. Stir well until the mixture feels like wet sand. This step is crucial because it forms the foundation of your cheesecake jar and gives a buttery flavor that balances the sweetness.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ The crumbs should clump together when pressed but not be soggy or greasy.
  2. Step 2

    Divide the graham cracker crust evenly into the bottom of 4 jars or small glasses. Press down gently with the back of a spoon to compact the crust and create a firm layer. This compaction helps the crust hold its shape when you scoop out the dessert later. If you press too hard, the crust will be too dense and lose its delicate texture, so aim for gentle but firm pressure.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ A smooth, even crust layer that stays in place when you tilt the jar slightly.
  3. Step 3

    In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed, scraping down the sides to avoid lumps. This step ensures the filling is silky and sweet without graininess. If the cream cheese isn’t fully softened, you’ll get clumps and a less smooth texture, so plan ahead to let it come to room temperature.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ The mixture should be creamy, lump-free, and slightly thickened.
  4. Step 4

    In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk out, the peaks stand tall without collapsing. Whipping the cream properly is key to a light, airy texture in the cheesecake filling. Undermixing will make the filling too runny, while overmixing could turn the cream into butter, ruining the texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Cream holds stiff peaks and doesn’t slide around when you tilt the bowl.
  5. Step 5

    Fold the whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use a gentle folding motion to combine without deflating the whipped cream. This step creates a fluffy, mousse-like filling that feels light on the palate. If you stir too vigorously, you’ll lose the airiness and end up with a dense filling.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Filling is smooth, airy, and well combined with no streaks of cream or cheese.
  6. Step 6

    Layer a few tablespoons of the cheesecake filling over the graham cracker crust in each jar. Then sprinkle a layer of chopped fresh strawberries on top. Repeat layering with remaining filling and strawberries until jars are filled, finishing with strawberries on top for a pretty presentation. The layering gives a nice contrast between creamy and fruity textures in every bite.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Jars have distinct layers visible through the glass, with strawberries on top.
  7. Step 7

    Cover the jars tightly with lids or plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, ideally 4 hours or overnight. Chilling lets the flavors meld and the filling set properly, improving texture and taste. If you skip chilling, the filling will be too soft and the crust may crumble when you try to eat it.

    ⏱ 2-4 hours✓ Filling is firm to the touch and the jars feel cold throughout.
  8. Step 8

    Before serving, garnish each jar with a few whole strawberries or a sprig of mint for a fresh look. This final touch adds color and a hint of aroma that makes the dessert feel special. It’s a simple step that elevates presentation without extra effort.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Jars look inviting with bright, fresh garnishes on top.
  9. Step 9

    Serve chilled with a small spoon. The crust should be soft but slightly crumbly, the filling light and creamy, and the strawberries fresh and juicy. Enjoy immediately for the best texture contrast. Leftovers keep well in the fridge but the crust may soften more over time.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ A perfect bite includes all layers in balance, creamy and fresh.

Pro Tips

  • Soften cream cheese completely before mixing. — Softened cream cheese blends smoothly without lumps, resulting in a silky filling. If cold, it can cause grainy texture and uneven sweetness distribution, affecting the mouthfeel of the cheesecake.
  • Use chilled heavy cream for whipping. — Cold cream whips better and holds stiff peaks longer, which is essential for creating a light, airy filling. Warm cream won’t whip properly and can lead to a runny or dense texture.
  • Gently fold whipped cream into cream cheese mixture. — Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, maintaining a fluffy texture. Stirring vigorously deflates the mixture, resulting in a dense and heavy filling.
  • Press graham cracker crust firmly but gently in jars. — Compact crust holds together when eating but remains tender. Pressing too hard makes it dense; too loose and it crumbles apart, losing the textural contrast.
  • Chill jars for several hours before serving. — Chilling allows the filling to set, flavors to meld, and crust to firm up slightly. Skipping this step results in a runny filling and crumbly crust that won’t hold shape.
  • Use fresh, ripe strawberries for best flavor. — Ripe berries provide natural sweetness and juiciness, balancing the richness of the filling. Unripe or overripe berries can introduce unwanted tartness or sogginess.
  • Avoid overwhipping the cream. — Overwhipped cream turns grainy and eventually separates into butter and buttermilk, ruining the texture of the filling and making it less smooth.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold cream cheese straight from the fridge.
    Why: Many assume mixing cold cream cheese is fine, but it resists blending and forms lumps.
    Fix: Let cream cheese sit at room temperature for 30-60 minutes before mixing. This ensures a smooth, lump-free filling.
  • Skipping chilling time.
    Why: Impatience or last-minute preparation leads to serving before the dessert sets.
    Fix: Plan ahead to chill the jars at least 2 hours. This sets the layers and improves texture and flavor.
  • Overmixing whipped cream into the filling.
    Why: Trying to fully incorporate the cream, people stir too vigorously.
    Fix: Fold gently with a spatula to keep air bubbles intact, preserving lightness.
  • Using soggy or overripe strawberries.
    Why: Not checking berry quality or washing them too early.
    Fix: Choose firm, ripe berries and add just before layering to prevent excess moisture.
  • Pressing graham crust too hard.
    Why: Thinking a firmer crust is more stable.
    Fix: Press gently to maintain crumbly, tender texture that contrasts nicely with creamy filling.

Variations

  • Blueberry Lemon No Bake Cheesecake Jars: Swap strawberries for fresh blueberries and add a teaspoon of lemon zest to the cream cheese filling. This gives a bright, citrusy twist that pairs beautifully with the creamy base. Expect a slightly tangier and vibrant flavor profile with juicy blueberry bursts.
    Zest lemon before mixing cream cheese for even flavor distribution. Blueberries should be fresh and firm to avoid watery layers.
  • Chocolate Swirl No Bake Cheesecake Jars: Fold in 2 tablespoons of melted dark chocolate into the cream cheese filling before adding whipped cream. This creates a rich, marbled effect and adds deep cocoa flavor. Great for chocolate lovers seeking a no bake option.
    Melt chocolate gently and cool slightly to avoid curdling the cream cheese mixture.
  • Vegan No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Jars: Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped until fluffy instead of dairy. The graham crust can be made with vegan-friendly crackers and coconut oil. This version is dairy-free but still creamy and fresh with strawberry layers.
    Chill coconut cream overnight for best whipping results; substitute ingredients carefully to maintain texture.
  • Peach and Ginger No Bake Cheesecake Jars: Replace strawberries with diced peaches and add a teaspoon of grated fresh ginger to the filling. The warm spice complements the creamy layers and juicy peaches, offering a unique seasonal alternative.
    Use ripe peaches and fold ginger in gently to avoid overpowering the delicate cream cheese flavor.
  • Salted Caramel No Bake Cheesecake Jars: Drizzle salted caramel sauce between layers and swirl some into the filling for a decadent twist. The sweet-salty combo enhances the creamy texture and adds complexity to the dessert.
    Add caramel last to avoid breaking the whipped cream and gently swirl without overmixing.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Keep cheesecake jars covered and at room temperature for no more than 2 hours to avoid spoilage. Since this is a dairy-based dessert, longer time out can risk food safety and texture loss.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store jars in airtight containers or tightly covered with lids/plastic wrap for up to 3 days. The crust may soften further over time, but the filling stays creamy. Serve chilled or let sit a few minutes before eating for softer texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze jars without fresh fruit for up to 1 month. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and place in freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn. Thaw in refrigerator overnight and stir gently before serving to restore texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed or recommended. Serve chilled for best texture and flavor. If too firm from fridge, let sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before eating to soften slightly.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the crust and cream cheese filling up to 2 days ahead; store separately in airtight containers. Assemble jars with fresh strawberries just before serving to keep fruit fresh and prevent sogginess.

FAQ

Can I use regular sugar instead of powdered sugar in the filling?
Powdered sugar dissolves more easily into the cream cheese, giving a smooth texture. Using granulated sugar may result in a slightly grainy filling because it doesn’t dissolve fully. If you only have granulated sugar, consider blending it into a powder or using superfine sugar for a better result.
How long can I store these cheesecake jars in the fridge?
They keep well for up to 3 days when stored in airtight containers. After this, the crust may become too soft and the strawberries can lose freshness. For best taste and texture, enjoy within 2 days.
Can I make this recipe vegan or dairy-free?
Yes! Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped until fluffy instead of dairy ingredients. Replace butter in the crust with coconut oil and use vegan-friendly graham crackers. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and creamy.
What can I substitute for graham cracker crumbs if I can’t find them?
Digestive biscuits or vanilla wafer crumbs work well as substitutes. Make sure to crush them finely and mix with melted butter to form a cohesive crust layer. Avoid using overly sweet or flavored cookies that might overpower the dessert.
How do I prevent the crust from becoming soggy?
Mixing the crumbs with melted butter and pressing firmly helps create a moisture barrier. Also, chilling the jars allows the crust to set. Avoid adding wet fruit directly on the crust without a layer of filling in between, as this can cause sogginess.
Can I prepare these jars ahead of time for a party?
Absolutely! You can make the crust and filling up to 2 days in advance and store separately. Assemble with fresh fruit the day of the event to keep the berries fresh and the textures perfect.
Why is my cheesecake filling too runny?
This usually happens if the cream cheese was too cold or the whipped cream was under-whipped. Make sure cream cheese is softened and whip cream to stiff peaks before folding. Also, avoid over-folding which can deflate the mixture.
Can I use frozen strawberries in this recipe?
Frozen strawberries release more water when thawed, which can make the filling watery and the crust soggy. If using frozen berries, thaw and drain them well before chopping and adding to the jars.
