Fresh Mini Caprese Skewers with Sweet Balsamic Glaze for Spring

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 7, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These mini Caprese skewers are a breeze to make and bring a fresh burst of flavor with juicy tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and a drizzle of sweet balsamic glaze. Perfect for spring parties or a light snack!

Introduction

I first made these mini Caprese skewers last spring when I was invited to a friend's garden party. I wanted something light and fresh but still impressive. What makes this recipe stand out is the homemade balsamic glaze — it’s so easy to make and adds a sweet tang that really wakes up the flavors. I've tried store-bought glazes before, but they never have that perfect balance of sweet and tart. The trick I learned is simmering balsamic vinegar with a bit of honey until it thickens just right. The skewers themselves are super simple: just cherry tomatoes, fresh mozzarella balls, and basil leaves threaded onto toothpicks. The result is a colorful, bite-sized appetizer bursting with fresh, vibrant flavors. These are perfect for spring or summer gatherings, and honestly, I find myself making them even for a quick snack at home. The juicy tomatoes and creamy mozzarella contrast beautifully, and the glaze pulls it all together with a glossy finish. They look gorgeous on a platter, too — people always ask for the recipe when I bring these along!

What You'll Need

Approximately 20 mini skewers, serves 4

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, washed and dried
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • Fresh basil leaves, about 20
  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar
  • Wooden skewers or toothpicks

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the balsamic glaze. Pour the balsamic vinegar into a small saucepan and add the honey or brown sugar. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture begins to simmer. Keep it at a gentle simmer so it reduces slowly without burning. This slow reduction is key because it concentrates the flavors and thickens the vinegar into a syrupy glaze. Watch carefully as it can go from perfect to burnt quickly. Once it coats the back of a spoon and has thickened (about 10-12 minutes), remove from heat and let it cool. The glaze will thicken more as it cools.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Syrupy consistency that coats the back of a spoon without running off too quickly
Step 2
While the glaze cools, prepare the skewers. Thread one cherry tomato onto a toothpick, followed by a fresh basil leaf (folded if large), then a mozzarella ball, and finish with another cherry tomato. The order can be adjusted for presentation, but this arrangement balances the flavors in each bite. Be gentle with the mozzarella balls so they don’t tear. Using fresh basil leaves is essential for that aromatic punch. Assemble all skewers and place them on a serving platter.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly assembled skewers with vibrant colors and intact ingredients
Step 3
Once all skewers are assembled and the balsamic glaze has cooled to room temperature, drizzle it generously over the skewers just before serving. The glaze adds a glossy finish and a sweet tang that complements the creamy mozzarella and fresh tomatoes. Avoid drizzling too early as the glaze can make the basil leaves soggy. For a final touch, you can add a small pinch of flaky sea salt or freshly cracked black pepper to enhance the flavors.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shiny glaze coating the skewers without pooling on the platter
Step 4
Chill the skewers in the refrigerator if you’re not serving immediately, but bring them out about 10 minutes before to take the chill off. This helps the flavors come through better and the mozzarella soften slightly. Serving them too cold can dull the taste and texture.
⏱ Time: Optional 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers cool but not ice cold, basil leaves crisp
Step 5
If you want to add a little extra flair, lightly toast the wooden skewers over a flame for a few seconds before assembling. This adds a subtle smoky aroma and helps prevent splinters. Be careful not to burn them or leave them too long near the flame.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers slightly darkened but not charred
Step 6
For a twist, consider swapping cherry tomatoes with grape tomatoes or heirloom baby tomatoes for different textures and colors. This doesn’t change the assembly method but offers a fun variation in taste and presentation.
⏱ Time: No extra time
✓ Doneness: Varied tomato colors and sizes on skewers
Step 7
To make the glaze ahead, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. Reheat gently before drizzling to bring back that syrupy texture. Avoid boiling it again as it can become too thick or bitter.
⏱ Time: Storage step
✓ Doneness: Warm, pourable glaze after gentle reheating
Step 8
If you want to serve these skewers at a party, prepare the skewers but keep the glaze separate until just before serving. This keeps the basil fresh and prevents the mozzarella from becoming soggy. Arrange the skewers on a platter and drizzle the glaze tableside for a fresh presentation.
⏱ Time: No extra time
✓ Doneness: Fresh looking skewers with dry basil until glaze is added
Step 9
Finally, experiment with finishing touches like sprinkling chopped toasted pine nuts or a few drops of good quality extra virgin olive oil over the skewers. These add a crunchy or silky richness that pairs beautifully with the main ingredients.
⏱ Time: Optional 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Additional texture or sheen on skewers enhancing flavor

Pro Tips

Simmer balsamic vinegar slowly to avoid burning.
Balsamic vinegar can quickly go from perfect to bitter if heated too fast or at too high a temperature. Slow simmering allows it to reduce evenly, concentrating flavors and caramelizing sugars without burning, which preserves the glaze’s sweet-tart balance.
Dry ingredients thoroughly before assembling skewers.
Excess moisture on tomatoes, mozzarella, or basil can make the skewers soggy and cause the glaze to slide off. Patting ingredients dry ensures the glaze sticks well and the skewers maintain their fresh appearance and texture.
Add balsamic glaze just before serving.
Applying the glaze too early can cause the basil leaves to wilt and the mozzarella to become soggy. Drizzling right before serving keeps the basil crisp and the presentation vibrant, enhancing both taste and visual appeal.
Use toothpicks instead of long skewers for bite-sized portions.
Toothpicks make these appetizers easy to eat in one or two bites, which is ideal for parties. They also help control portion size and keep the ingredients tightly packed without overpowering the delicate flavors.
Fold larger basil leaves to fit neatly on skewers.
Folding basil leaves reduces bulk and prevents them from tearing. This technique releases more aroma and essential oils, enhancing the overall flavor with each bite while maintaining a tidy presentation.
Toast wooden skewers lightly before use.
Toasting removes any raw wood aroma and adds a subtle smoky note that complements the fresh ingredients. It also helps prevent splinters, making the skewers safer and more pleasant to handle.
Serve skewers at room temperature for best flavor.
Chilling can mute the flavors and firm up the mozzarella, making it less creamy. Bringing the skewers to room temperature allows the ingredients’ natural tastes and textures to shine, especially the basil’s aroma and the glaze’s sweetness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Burning the balsamic glaze.

Why it happens Rushing the reduction by using high heat causes the sugars in the vinegar to burn, resulting in a bitter taste.

Fix Reduce the heat to low or medium-low and simmer slowly, stirring occasionally. Watch closely once it starts thickening and remove from heat promptly when it coats the back of a spoon.

Using soggy or wet ingredients.

Why it happens Not drying tomatoes, mozzarella, or basil properly leads to excess moisture that dilutes the glaze and makes the skewers mushy.

Fix Pat all ingredients dry with paper towels before assembling. This helps the glaze stick and keeps the basil fresh and crisp.

Drizzling glaze too early.

Why it happens Applying the glaze well before serving causes basil leaves to wilt and mozzarella to soften excessively.

Fix Wait until just before serving to drizzle the glaze. Keep skewers refrigerated or covered until then to preserve freshness.

Using low-quality mozzarella.

Why it happens Pre-shredded or processed mozzarella lacks the creamy texture and fresh flavor needed for this recipe.

Fix Use fresh bocconcini or small mozzarella balls packed in water. Drain well and use within a day or two of purchase for best taste.

Overloading the skewers.

Why it happens Putting too many ingredients on each skewer makes them hard to eat and unbalanced in flavor.

Fix Stick to the recommended three to four items per skewer to keep portions bite-sized and flavors harmonized.

Variations

Spicy Caprese Skewers

Add a small slice of fresh jalapeño or a sprinkle of crushed red pepper flakes to each skewer for a spicy kick. This variation gives a nice contrast to the creamy mozzarella and sweet glaze, perfect if you like a little heat.

Note: Be cautious with jalapeño quantity to avoid overpowering the delicate flavors. Adjust glaze sweetness if desired to balance spice.

Avocado Caprese Skewers

Swap out one tomato per skewer for a small chunk of ripe avocado. It adds a buttery texture and richness that pairs beautifully with basil and mozzarella, making the appetizer a bit more filling.

Note: Use firm but ripe avocado to prevent mushiness. Assemble just before serving to avoid browning.

Pesto Drizzle Caprese Skewers

Instead of balsamic glaze, drizzle homemade or store-bought pesto over the skewers. This adds an herbaceous, nutty flavor that complements the mozzarella and tomatoes in a different but equally delicious way.

Note: Pesto is thicker, so use sparingly to avoid overpowering. Serve immediately to keep basil fresh.

Caprese Skewers with Prosciutto

Add a thin slice of prosciutto wrapped around the mozzarella ball for a salty, savory twist. This makes the skewers more substantial and perfect for guests who want a bit of protein.

Note: Use delicate slices of prosciutto to avoid overwhelming the skewer. Adjust glaze amount to balance saltiness.

Heirloom Tomato Caprese Skewers

Use a variety of colorful heirloom cherry tomatoes instead of standard red ones. This adds visual interest and subtle flavor differences, making the skewers look stunning on any platter.

Note: Heirloom tomatoes can be softer; handle gently and assemble shortly before serving.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Keep skewers covered and at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Avoid leaving in direct sunlight or warm spots to prevent basil wilting and mozzarella spoilage.

Refrigerator

Store assembled skewers without glaze in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Add glaze just before serving. Skewers may lose some freshness when cold; bring to room temperature before eating.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as mozzarella and fresh basil lose texture and flavor. Tomatoes also become watery upon thawing.

Reheating

This appetizer is best served cold or at room temperature. Avoid reheating to preserve fresh flavors and textures.

Make Ahead

Prepare the balsamic glaze and store it refrigerated up to a week. Assemble skewers without glaze 1 day ahead, cover tightly, and add glaze just before serving to maintain freshness.

FAQ

Can I use dried basil instead of fresh basil?
Dried basil won't work well here because it lacks the vibrant aroma and moisture fresh basil provides. It also won’t hold its shape on skewers. Fresh basil is essential for the authentic flavor and texture in Caprese skewers.
How do I store leftover balsamic glaze?
Store leftover glaze in a sealed container in the refrigerator for up to a week. It may thicken further when cold; gently reheat over low heat to restore pourable consistency before using again.
Can I make these skewers vegan?
Absolutely! Replace mozzarella with vegan cheese alternatives like cashew-based mozzarella or marinated tofu cubes. The balsamic glaze and fresh ingredients will still shine.
What if I don’t have honey for the glaze?
You can substitute honey with an equal amount of brown sugar or maple syrup. These sweeteners help balance the acidity and caramelize during reduction, creating that perfect glaze texture and flavor.
Is it okay to use larger tomatoes?
Using larger tomatoes means cutting them into smaller pieces so they fit nicely on skewers. Cherry or grape tomatoes work best because they’re the perfect size and have a sweeter flavor.
Can I prepare the skewers a day ahead?
You can assemble the skewers a day ahead but keep them refrigerated without the balsamic glaze. Add the glaze just before serving to keep basil fresh and avoid sogginess.
How do I prevent the basil from wilting?
Keep basil leaves dry and add the balsamic glaze right before serving. Also, store assembled skewers covered and chilled until serving to maintain freshness and prevent wilting.
Can I use bottled balsamic glaze instead of making my own?
Bottled glaze can be convenient but often contains added sugars and thickeners that alter the flavor. Homemade glaze has a fresher, more balanced taste and lets you control sweetness and thickness.
