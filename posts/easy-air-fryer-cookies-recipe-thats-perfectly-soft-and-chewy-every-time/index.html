Soft and Chewy Air Fryer Chocolate Chip Cookies You’ll Love

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 18, 2026
15 min Prep Time
12 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These air fryer cookies are soft in the middle with just a crisp edge, baked fast without warming up your kitchen. The air fryer really nails that perfect chewy texture. Great for a quick sweet treat anytime!

Introduction

I first tried making cookies in my air fryer when it was way too hot to turn on the oven during summer. Honestly, I wasn’t sure if it would work, but I was so pleasantly surprised! These cookies come out soft and chewy with just a slight crisp around the edges, and the best part is the kitchen stays cool. I think the secret is baking them in small batches at a moderate temperature to get that perfect texture. I usually use brown sugar for extra moisture and a deeper flavor, which makes these cookies taste a little richer than your standard chocolate chip. Plus, the air fryer’s circulating heat bakes them evenly without drying them out. If you want a quick homemade cookie fix without heating the whole house, this is a game changer. The finished cookies are golden brown, gooey in the middle, and melt-in-your-mouth delicious — perfect for a last-minute treat or when unexpected guests arrive.

What You'll Need

Approximately 16 cookies, 8 servings

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by creaming the softened butter with both sugars in a large bowl. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed until the mixture is light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. This step is crucial because creaming incorporates air into the butter, helping the cookies rise and giving them a tender texture. Avoid rushing here; if you don’t cream long enough, your cookies might turn out dense and flat.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks pale, fluffy, and smooth without any visible sugar granules
Step 2
Add the egg and vanilla extract to the creamed butter mixture. Beat again until everything is fully combined and glossy. The egg acts as a binder and adds moisture, while vanilla gives that classic warm flavor. Mixing just until combined prevents overworking the dough, which can toughen cookies.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, shiny, and no longer streaky
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. This ensures the leavening agent and salt are evenly distributed, preventing uneven rising or salty pockets in your cookies. Sifting is optional but recommended for a lighter dough texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined and free of clumps
Step 4
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing on low speed or folding gently with a spatula. Overmixing flour can develop gluten, making cookies tough rather than soft. Mix just until no streaks of flour remain.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is uniform with no visible flour pockets but still soft
Step 5
Fold in the chocolate chips evenly through the dough. This distributes the chocolate so every cookie gets a good amount of melty chips. Be gentle to avoid breaking the chips or overworking the dough.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are well dispersed throughout the dough
Step 6
Preheat your air fryer to 320°F (160°C) for about 3 minutes. Preheating is important because it ensures the cookies start baking immediately, helping them rise properly and develop that crisp edge.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Air fryer display shows 320°F and is ready for baking
Step 7
Scoop tablespoon-sized balls of dough onto parchment paper or a silicone mat that fits your air fryer basket. Space them about 2 inches apart to allow for spreading. If you skip spacing, cookies can merge into each other and bake unevenly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough balls are evenly spaced and slightly flattened on top
Step 8
Place the basket in the air fryer and bake at 320°F for 10-12 minutes. Check at 10 minutes; the cookies should look set around the edges but still soft in the center. The air fryer’s circulating hot air cooks quickly and evenly, so watch closely to avoid overbaking.
⏱ Time: 10-12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden brown and centers look slightly underbaked but not wet
Step 9
Remove the cookies carefully using a spatula and transfer to a wire rack to cool for 5 minutes. They’ll firm up as they cool but stay soft inside. Cooling on a rack prevents sogginess from trapped steam, keeping edges crisp.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies feel set but still soft when lightly pressed

Pro Tips

Use room temperature butter for creaming.
Butter that’s too cold won’t cream properly, resulting in dense dough and flat cookies. Softened butter traps air when mixed with sugar, which helps cookies rise and become tender. If butter is too warm or melted, cookies spread too much and lose structure.
Don’t overmix once you add flour.
Overmixing flour develops gluten, which toughens cookies and makes them chewy in an unpleasant way. Mixing just until combined ensures a soft texture. It also prevents the dough from becoming greasy or dense.
Preheat the air fryer before baking.
Preheating ensures the cookies start baking immediately at the correct temperature, helping them rise properly and creating a crisp edge. Skipping this step can cause uneven cooking and flat cookies.
Space cookie dough balls at least 2 inches apart.
Cookies spread as they bake, so giving them space prevents them from merging into one another. This spacing helps maintain individual cookie shape and ensures even baking on all sides.
Use parchment paper or a silicone mat in the air fryer basket.
This prevents sticking and makes cleanup easier. It also helps distribute heat evenly across the cookie bottoms, aiding in even browning without burning.
Check cookies a minute or two before recommended bake time.
Air fryer models vary in heat intensity, so your cookies might bake faster. Checking early prevents overbaking, which dries out cookies and ruins the soft-chewy texture.
Let cookies cool on a wire rack.
Cooling on a wire rack prevents steam from softening the cookie bottoms, keeping edges crisp. It also stops cooking from residual heat, ensuring perfect texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using melted butter instead of softened butter

Why it happens Some people assume melted butter makes cookie dough easier to mix or results in chewier cookies, but it actually changes the dough’s structure.

Fix Always use butter softened to room temperature for creaming with sugar. This traps air that helps cookies rise and creates a tender crumb. If you only have melted butter, adjust the recipe to accommodate the change in texture and spread.

Overcrowding the air fryer basket

Why it happens Trying to bake too many cookies at once to save time can seem efficient but traps steam and prevents proper airflow.

Fix Bake in small batches with enough space between dough balls. This allows hot air to circulate, producing crisp edges and even cooking.

Skipping preheating the air fryer

Why it happens It’s easy to pop in cookies without waiting, especially when in a hurry.

Fix Always preheat to the right temperature to ensure immediate baking and proper texture development.

Measuring flour incorrectly

Why it happens Scooping flour directly from the bag packs it down, leading to too much flour and dry cookies.

Fix Spoon flour into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife for accurate measurement.

Overbaking cookies

Why it happens Leaving cookies in too long to get a “done” look can dry them out.

Fix Take cookies out when edges are golden but centers still look slightly soft. They’ll firm up as they cool.

Variations

Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Cookies

Swap half the flour for rolled oats and add a pinch of cinnamon for a cozy twist. Oats add chew and a nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with chocolate. Expect a heartier texture and a bit more bite.

Note: Oats absorb more moisture, so you might need to add a tablespoon of milk or reduce baking time slightly to keep cookies soft.

Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Add 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter to the butter and sugar mixture for a rich, nutty flavor. These cookies are a bit denser and super satisfying for peanut butter lovers.

Note: Peanut butter adds fat and moisture, so reduce butter by 2 tablespoons to balance texture.

Double Chocolate Cookies

Mix 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder into the flour for a chocolate base, then add white chocolate chips instead of semi-sweet. This creates an indulgent, fudgy cookie with bursts of sweetness.

Note: Cocoa powder dries dough slightly; adding a splash of milk can help maintain softness.

Vegan Air Fryer Cookies

Use coconut oil instead of butter, flax egg (1 tbsp ground flax + 3 tbsp water) instead of egg, and dairy-free chocolate chips. These come out just as chewy and delicious with a subtle coconut flavor.

Note: Coconut oil melts faster than butter, so chill dough briefly before baking to reduce spreading.

Salted Caramel Chocolate Chip Cookies

Add small chunks of caramel candy to the dough and sprinkle flaky sea salt on top before baking. The sweet-salty combo elevates these cookies to a special treat perfect for sharing.

Note: Caramel melts quickly; watch baking time closely to avoid burning the caramel pieces.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cookies can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 days when stored in an airtight container. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat sources to prevent melting chocolate chips or drying out.

Refrigerator

Store cookies in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Refrigeration can firm up the texture, so bring cookies to room temperature or warm slightly before eating to restore softness and flavor.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature for 30 minutes before serving.

Reheating

Reheat cookies in a microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5 minutes to soften. Avoid overheating as it can dry them out. Adding a small piece of bread in the container can help retain moisture during reheating.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the cookie dough 1-2 days ahead and keep it tightly wrapped in the fridge. When ready to bake, scoop and air fry as usual. This resting time actually improves flavor and texture by allowing the flour to hydrate.

FAQ

Can I use frozen cookie dough in the air fryer?
Yes, you can bake frozen cookie dough balls directly in the air fryer, but you’ll need to add a couple of extra minutes to the baking time. Keep an eye on them as air fryer models vary. Baking from frozen helps prevent spreading for thicker cookies.
Can I make these cookies gluten-free?
Yes, swapping all-purpose flour for a gluten-free baking blend works well. Make sure the blend contains xanthan gum or another binder to maintain texture.
