No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Cups with Fresh Berries and Creamy Filling

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 6 servings

These no bake cheesecake cups are a breeze to whip up and perfect for warm days. The buttery graham crust pairs beautifully with the fluffy cream cheese filling and fresh strawberries on top. Great for casual gatherings or a quick sweet treat!

Introduction

I first made these no bake strawberry cheesecake cups during a last-minute summer picnic, and honestly, they saved the day! No oven meant less fuss and a cooler kitchen, which was a huge win on a hot afternoon. What I love about this version is the balance between the buttery graham cracker crust and the fluffy cream cheese filling—it’s light but still feels indulgent. The secret is whipping the cream cheese with powdered sugar until silky smooth before folding in whipped cream; it makes the filling airy rather than dense like traditional cheesecake. Plus, topping with fresh strawberries adds a juicy freshness that feels like summer in every bite. I’ve tried other no bake cheesecakes that ended up too sweet or heavy, but this one strikes the perfect harmony. It’s a crowd-pleaser at casual get-togethers or just when you want a quick, impressive dessert without turning on the oven. The texture is creamy and smooth, with a slight crunch from the crust. I think you’ll find it’s a fun recipe to make with friends or even kids, since it’s all about simple mixing and layering. Give it a try—you might just find your new favorite no bake treat!

Why This Works

This recipe works because it balances texture and flavor without the need for baking. The graham cracker crust binds together using melted butter, which solidifies as it chills, creating a crisp and buttery base that contrasts beautifully with the creamy filling. The key is pressing the crust firmly to avoid crumbling when serving.

The cream cheese filling achieves its light, fluffy texture by folding whipped cream into a smooth cream cheese and powdered sugar mixture. Whipping the cream separately and folding it gently preserves the air bubbles, making the filling feel airy instead of dense. This is what sets no bake cheesecakes apart from their baked counterparts, which rely on eggs for structure.

Chilling is essential to let the filling set up properly. Without baking, the cold temperature firms the cream cheese and butter in the crust, giving the dessert structure. Rushing this step results in a runny filling that won’t hold its shape. Lastly, the fresh strawberries add brightness and a juicy contrast to the creamy richness, making the dessert feel balanced and refreshing.

Every element works in harmony: the crust’s buttery crunch, the filling’s smooth fluffiness, and the fresh fruit’s natural sweetness. That’s why this no bake strawberry cheesecake is so successful and loved.

  • Look for finely crushed graham cracker crumbs for the best crust texture. Coarse crumbs won’t pack well and can cause your crust to fall apart. Using store-bought crumbs saves time, but you can crush whole crackers yourself for freshness. The buttery, slightly sweet flavor of graham crackers complements the creamy filling perfectly.
  • Unsalted butter allows you to control the salt level in your crust. Melt it gently to avoid burning, as burnt butter will affect flavor. Butter solidifies as it cools, helping the crust hold together firmly once chilled. Using salted butter can be done but reduce added sugar slightly to balance flavors.
  • Choose full-fat cream cheese for the creamiest and richest filling. It should be softened to room temperature to avoid lumps when mixing. Low-fat or whipped cream cheese varieties contain additives and less fat, which can affect texture and flavor, resulting in a less stable filling.
  • Powdered sugar dissolves easily into the cream cheese, preventing graininess. It also contains a bit of cornstarch which helps stabilize the filling. Using granulated sugar here would result in a gritty texture. Adjust sweetness according to taste, but powdered sugar provides the best smooth finish.
  • Cold heavy cream whips up best and creates stable peaks that add lightness to the filling. Avoid using half-and-half or lighter creams as they don’t hold air as well, resulting in a denser filling. Overwhipping can turn cream buttery, so watch it carefully during whipping.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, sliced

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by making the crust. Combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and granulated sugar in a medium bowl. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and resemble wet sand. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs together, giving the crust its perfect crumbly texture. If you skip thorough mixing, the crust might fall apart when you serve the cups. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of your serving cups or glasses to create an even, compact base. Using the back of a spoon or your fingers works well here. A tightly packed crust holds up better once you add the filling.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ The crust feels firm and compact when pressed; no loose crumbs should fall off.
  2. Step 2

    Next, prepare the cream cheese filling. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and creamy using an electric mixer. Gradually add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, beating until fully incorporated. This gradual mixing helps avoid lumps and ensures a silky texture. If you rush or add all the sugar at once, the filling can become grainy. Once smooth, set aside. The smoothness here is key for that fluffy, melt-in-your-mouth feel you want in cheesecake filling.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling is silky smooth with no lumps and slightly glossy in appearance.
  3. Step 3

    In a separate chilled bowl, whip the cold heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the beaters, the cream holds its shape without collapsing. Cold cream whips better and results in a lighter texture. Overwhipping can turn it grainy or into butter, so watch closely and stop at the right moment. Whipped cream is what makes the filling airy and light instead of dense.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Cream stands in stiff peaks that hold shape firmly but are still smooth.
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use a light hand to keep as much air in the mixture as possible. Folding, rather than stirring, preserves the fluffiness that makes this dessert feel so light. Be patient and fold until just combined—overmixing can deflate the cream and make the filling dense.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Filling is uniform in color and texture, light and airy with no streaks.
  5. Step 5

    Spoon or pipe the cream cheese filling over the prepared crust in each cup, filling about three-quarters full. Piping can make a prettier presentation but spooning works just fine. Level the tops gently with a spatula or back of a spoon. This layered approach keeps the crust crisp and the filling creamy, offering a nice textural contrast in every bite.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling layer is smooth, even, and reaches just below the rim of each cup.
  6. Step 6

    Chill the cups in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, preferably longer. This chilling step allows the filling to set and the flavors to meld. If you skip chilling or shorten it too much, the filling might be too soft and not hold its shape well when served. Patience here pays off with a perfectly firm yet creamy cheesecake texture.

    ⏱ 120 minutes✓ Filling feels firm to the touch and holds shape when gently pressed.
  7. Step 7

    Just before serving, top each cheesecake cup with fresh sliced strawberries. The fresh fruit adds a vibrant color and a juicy burst of flavor that balances the richness of the filling. You can also add a light dusting of powdered sugar or a drizzle of honey for extra sweetness if you like. The fresh topping makes this dessert feel light and summery.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Strawberries look fresh, bright red, and evenly distributed on top.
  8. Step 8

    Serve these cups chilled straight from the fridge. They’re perfect for warm days or casual gatherings where you want something sweet but not heavy. The single-serving cups make them easy to share and mess-free. If you’re transporting them, keep chilled until just before serving to maintain their shape and texture.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Dessert is cool and creamy with a crisp crust and fresh fruit topping.
  9. Step 9

    If you want to prepare these ahead, assemble everything except the strawberries. Cover and refrigerate for up to 24 hours, then add fresh berries just before serving. This keeps the fruit fresh and prevents it from making the crust soggy. Planning ahead makes entertaining stress-free and lets the flavors develop nicely.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Strawberries remain fresh and crust stays crisp after adding just before serving.

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature cream cheese for smooth mixing. — Cold cream cheese is stiff and difficult to blend, causing lumps in your filling. Softening it to room temperature allows it to whip smoothly with powdered sugar and vanilla, resulting in a silky texture essential for a creamy cheesecake filling.
  • Chill your mixing bowl and beaters when whipping cream. — Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold its structure better. Warm bowls or beaters can cause the cream to remain runny or take longer to reach stiff peaks, which impacts the fluffiness of your filling.
  • Press the graham cracker crust firmly into the cups. — Firm pressing compacts the crumb mixture and helps the crust hold together once chilled. A loose crust will crumble and fall apart when serving, ruining the texture contrast that makes this dessert special.
  • Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture. — Folding preserves air bubbles trapped in the whipped cream, keeping the filling light and airy. Stirring vigorously deflates these bubbles, resulting in a dense, heavy texture instead of a fluffy cheesecake.
  • Add fresh strawberries just before serving. — Adding fruit too early can release moisture and make the crust soggy. Fresh berries added last stay bright and juicy, maintaining the dessert’s appealing texture and flavor balance.
  • Use powdered sugar instead of granulated sugar in the filling. — Powdered sugar dissolves quickly and smoothly, preventing gritty texture. It also contains cornstarch that helps stabilize the filling, ensuring it sets nicely without weeping.
  • Chill the cheesecake cups for at least 2 hours. — Chilling lets the butter in the crust harden and the filling firm up. Without enough chill time, the cups will be too soft and won’t hold their shape when served.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold cream cheese without softening
    Why: Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but cold cream cheese is hard and lumpy.
    Fix: Let cream cheese sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing. This ensures smooth blending with powdered sugar and vanilla, preventing lumps in your filling.
  • Overwhipping the heavy cream
    Why: It’s easy to keep whipping cream past stiff peaks, turning it grainy or buttery.
    Fix: Watch the cream closely and stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. The cream should hold shape but still be smooth and not dry.
  • Not pressing the crust firmly
    Why: Rushing or lightly pressing the crumb mixture results in a loose crust that crumbles.
    Fix: Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to firmly pack the crust into the cups. This creates a stable base that holds up well when serving.
  • Mixing whipped cream too vigorously into filling
    Why: People often stir rather than fold, deflating the air bubbles.
    Fix: Use a spatula to gently fold whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture to keep it light and fluffy.
  • Adding fruit too early
    Why: Adding strawberries too soon releases moisture, making the crust soggy.
    Fix: Add fresh fruit just before serving to keep the crust crisp and the berries fresh.

Variations

  • Mixed Berry Cheesecake Cups: Swap strawberries for a mix of blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries for a colorful and tangy twist. The combination adds complexity and a burst of different berry flavors while keeping the dessert fresh and light.
    Use the same preparation method; just top with mixed berries before serving to maintain freshness and crust texture.
  • Chocolate Drizzle No Bake Cheesecake Cups: Add a drizzle of melted dark or milk chocolate over the finished cups for a rich, decadent touch. The chocolate pairs beautifully with the tangy cream cheese and sweet strawberries.
    Allow the chocolate to cool slightly before drizzling to avoid melting the filling; drizzle just before serving.
  • Lemon Zest Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Add a teaspoon of lemon zest to the cream cheese filling for a bright citrus note that complements the strawberries. This gives the dessert a refreshing zing that’s perfect for spring and summer.
    Incorporate lemon zest into the cream cheese mixture before folding in whipped cream to distribute flavor evenly.
  • Graham Cracker and Nut Crust: Mix finely chopped toasted nuts like pecans or almonds into the graham cracker crust for added crunch and nutty flavor. This variation adds texture and a savory balance to the sweet filling.
    Toast nuts lightly before chopping and mixing with the crust ingredients to enhance flavor.
  • Vegan No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks instead of dairy cream. The crust can be made with vegan butter or coconut oil. This version is creamy and delicious without animal products.
    Chill coconut cream overnight and whip the solid part only. Adjust sweetness to taste.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These cheesecake cups should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours, as the cream cheese filling can spoil. Cover with plastic wrap or lids if you need to keep them out briefly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The crust may soften slightly over time, but the flavor remains delicious. Best served chilled; no need to reheat.
  • 🧊 Freezer: You can freeze the assembled cups (without fresh strawberries) for up to 1 month. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container. Thaw overnight in the fridge before adding fresh fruit and serving.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed. Serve chilled for the best texture and flavor. If you prefer a softer filling, let sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the crust and filling up to 24 hours ahead and refrigerate. Add fresh strawberries just before serving to keep the fruit bright and the crust crisp.

FAQ

Can I use low-fat cream cheese for this recipe?
You can, but low-fat cream cheese tends to have more water and less fat, which can affect texture and flavor. The filling might be less creamy and stable, so I recommend full-fat cream cheese for the best results.
What if I don’t have an electric mixer?
You can mix by hand, but it will take more effort to get the cream cheese smooth and the cream whipped properly. Make sure the cream cheese is very soft, and whip the cream in a chilled bowl using a whisk to help incorporate air.
How long can I store these cheesecake cups?
They keep well in the fridge for up to 3 days. After that, the crust may start to soften and the filling texture can change. For best freshness, add fruit just before serving.
Can I substitute the graham cracker crust with cookies?
Absolutely! Crushed digestive biscuits or vanilla wafers work well too. Just make sure to melt the butter and mix thoroughly to bind the crumbs into a firm crust.
Why does the filling sometimes weep or get watery?
This can happen if the cream cheese isn’t fully softened or if the whipped cream is overmixed and starts to separate. Also, adding fruit too early can release juice. Chill the dessert well and add fruit just before serving to prevent this.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! Use gluten-free graham crackers or gluten-free cookies for the crust. The filling is naturally gluten-free, so just ensure your crust ingredients are safe for gluten-free diets.
What’s the best way to soften cream cheese quickly?
Cut it into small cubes and let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes. Alternatively, microwave in short 5-second bursts, checking often to avoid melting.
Can I add gelatin to make the filling firmer?
You can, if you want a more set filling. Dissolve gelatin in warm water and fold it into the cream cheese mixture before adding whipped cream. This helps the filling hold shape longer, especially in warm conditions.
