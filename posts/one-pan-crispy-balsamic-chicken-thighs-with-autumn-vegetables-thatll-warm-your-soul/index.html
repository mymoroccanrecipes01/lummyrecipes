One-Pan Crispy Balsamic Chicken Thighs with Autumn Veggies

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 4, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This one-pan dinner is all about crispy chicken thighs and tender fall veggies glazed in a tangy balsamic sauce. It’s cozy, easy, and perfect for chilly nights when you want something comforting but fuss-free.

Introduction

I first made this balsamic chicken dish on a chilly fall evening when I wanted a comforting meal but didn’t want to spend hours in the kitchen. What I love about this recipe is how the chicken skin crisps up beautifully in the oven while the veggies roast tender and caramelized alongside it. The balsamic and honey glaze adds just the right tangy-sweet punch that pulls everything together. I’ve tried similar recipes before but what sets this one apart is the timing — roasting everything together in one pan saves so much cleanup and the flavors really meld in the glaze. A little trick I use is to pat the chicken skin super dry before seasoning, which helps get that golden crispiness everyone loves. By the time it’s done, the chicken is juicy, the veggies are soft with a slight char, and the sauce has thickened into a sticky, irresistible coating. It’s perfect for a relaxed weeknight dinner or even a casual weekend meal when you want something that feels special but is actually easy. Trust me, once you try this, it’ll become a go-to in your rotation!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs, patted dry
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 3 cups mixed autumn vegetables (carrots, Brussels sprouts, butternut squash), chopped
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh thyme sprigs for garnish (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). While it heats, pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels — this is key for crispy skin. Then season both sides generously with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika. Dry skin browns better and the paprika adds a subtle smoky depth. Avoid skipping this step or the skin may turn out soggy.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin looks dry and evenly coated with spices
Step 2
Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat. Place chicken thighs skin side down without crowding. Sear for about 5-7 minutes until skin is golden brown and crisp. Don’t move them around too much — letting the skin sit undisturbed forms that beautiful crust. If you rush or flip early, the skin won’t crisp properly.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Skin is deep golden brown and releases easily from the pan
Step 3
Flip the chicken thighs over and sear the other side for 2 minutes just to brown slightly. Then remove the chicken from the pan and set aside. This quick sear on the second side locks in juices without overcooking before oven roasting.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Second side has light golden color
Step 4
Add the chopped autumn vegetables to the same skillet. Toss with remaining olive oil, a pinch of salt, and some pepper. The residual chicken fat will add flavor, so don’t discard it. Spread veggies evenly to roast and caramelize properly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Veggies are coated and glistening lightly with oil
Step 5
In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, honey, and minced garlic. This glaze will add tang and sweetness to balance the savory chicken and earthy veggies. Set aside until ready to use.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth and slightly thickened
Step 6
Nestle the seared chicken thighs skin side up on top of the veggies in the skillet. Pour the balsamic glaze evenly over the chicken and vegetables. This ensures every bite gets that sticky, flavorful coating during roasting.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Glaze is spread evenly and chicken skin remains exposed
Step 7
Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and roast for 20-25 minutes. The high heat finishes cooking the chicken through and allows the veggies to get tender and caramelized. Keep an eye so the glaze doesn’t burn; if it starts to darken too fast, loosely cover with foil.
⏱ Time: 25
✓ Doneness: Chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F and veggies are fork-tender
Step 8
Remove the skillet from the oven and let the chicken rest for 5 minutes. This helps redistribute juices for moist meat. Meanwhile, spoon some glaze from the pan over the chicken and veggies for extra flavor.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken juices run clear when pierced
Step 9
Garnish with fresh thyme sprigs if you like for a pop of color and herbal aroma. Serve warm straight from the pan with your favorite side or a simple green salad. The combination of crisp skin, tender veggies, and tangy glaze is pure comfort food.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Dish is visually vibrant and aromatic

Pro Tips

Pat chicken skin completely dry before seasoning.
Removing moisture from the skin is crucial for getting it crispy. Water creates steam during cooking which prevents browning and leads to soggy skin. Thorough drying ensures the Maillard reaction can occur, producing that golden, crunchy texture we all love.
Sear chicken skin side down without moving it.
Letting the chicken sit undisturbed allows the skin to render fat and brown evenly. Moving it too soon breaks the crust formation and makes the skin stick to the pan. This step locks in flavor and improves texture significantly.
Use an ovenproof skillet for seamless stove-to-oven cooking.
Using a pan that can go from stovetop to oven means you don’t lose any of the flavorful browned bits when transferring. It also allows the chicken to finish cooking gently with the veggies, blending flavors effortlessly.
Don’t overcrowd the pan with veggies or chicken.
Crowding traps moisture and causes steaming instead of roasting, which prevents caramelization. Give ingredients space to brown and crisp for better texture and flavor development.
Rest chicken after roasting before serving.
Resting lets juices redistribute inside the meat, making it juicier and more tender. Cutting in immediately causes juices to run out, resulting in dry chicken.
Add glaze towards the end to avoid burning.
Balsamic vinegar and honey can burn if exposed to high heat too long. Adding the glaze just before roasting or midway through prevents bitterness and keeps the sauce sticky and flavorful.
Use fresh thyme or herbs as garnish last minute.
Fresh herbs add brightness and aroma without overpowering the dish. Adding them at the end preserves their flavor and visual appeal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not drying the chicken skin before cooking.

Why it happens Many assume seasoning is enough, but moisture on the skin blocks crisping and browning.

Fix Pat the chicken skin with paper towels until completely dry before seasoning. This step is essential for that coveted crispy texture.

Flipping chicken too early during searing.

Why it happens Impatience or fear of sticking makes cooks move chicken prematurely.

Fix Allow the chicken to sear undisturbed until it naturally releases from the pan, usually 5-7 minutes, to form a proper crust.

Overcrowding the pan with vegetables.

Why it happens Trying to cook everything at once without enough space.

Fix Spread veggies evenly and don’t pile them on top of each other. Use a larger pan or roast in batches if needed.

Adding glaze too early and burning it.

Why it happens Not understanding balsamic and honey’s sugar content causes them to burn under high heat.

Fix Add glaze just before roasting or halfway through cooking to avoid bitterness.

Skipping resting time after roasting.

Why it happens Eagerness to eat leads to cutting chicken immediately.

Fix Set chicken aside for 5 minutes after roasting to keep it juicy and tender.

Variations

Spicy Balsamic Chicken with Roasted Veggies

Add a teaspoon of red pepper flakes to the seasoning and glaze for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and depth, balancing the sweetness of the balsamic honey glaze nicely. Perfect if you like a little heat with your comfort food.

Note: Be cautious with the amount of chili flakes to avoid overpowering the glaze’s sweetness.

Herb-Roasted Chicken with Lemon Zest

Mix fresh rosemary and thyme into the glaze and add lemon zest for a bright, herbal twist. The citrus cuts through the richness and adds freshness, making this a great spring or summer adaptation.

Note: Add lemon zest right before roasting to preserve its aroma.

Maple Balsamic Chicken with Sweet Potatoes

Swap honey for pure maple syrup and use sweet potatoes instead of butternut squash. This gives a deeper sweetness and a slightly different flavor profile that's perfect for fall and winter meals.

Note: Maple syrup caramelizes faster; watch glaze closely to prevent burning.

Garlic Parmesan Roasted Chicken and Veggies

Add minced garlic to the glaze and sprinkle grated Parmesan over the veggies before roasting. This adds a savory, cheesy layer of flavor that pairs beautifully with the crispy chicken skin.

Note: Add Parmesan halfway through roasting to prevent burning.

Mediterranean Style Balsamic Chicken

Incorporate olives, cherry tomatoes, and capers into the vegetable mix for a Mediterranean flair. The salty, briny flavors contrast nicely with the sweet balsamic glaze for a vibrant dish.

Note: Add olives and capers midway through roasting to keep their texture intact.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so refrigerate promptly to keep safe.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken skin may lose crispness and veggies soften further. Reheat gently to avoid drying out the meat.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven for about 15 minutes, adding a splash of water or broth to keep veggies moist. Avoid microwaving if possible, as it makes skin soggy.

Make Ahead

You can prep the glaze and chop veggies 1-2 days ahead. Store separately in the fridge. Sear chicken and assemble just before roasting for freshest results.

FAQ

Can I use boneless chicken thighs instead?
Yes, boneless thighs work but cook faster and may dry out more easily. Reduce oven time by about 5-7 minutes and check for doneness earlier. Skin crispiness may also be less pronounced without bone support.
What if I don’t have balsamic vinegar?
You can substitute with red wine vinegar mixed with a bit of brown sugar or maple syrup to mimic the sweetness and acidity. The flavor will differ slightly but still delicious.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Absolutely! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you ensure your balsamic vinegar and honey have no added gluten-containing ingredients. Always check labels if sensitive.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
The safest way is to use a meat thermometer. Chicken thighs are done at an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). The juices should run clear when pierced with a knife.
Can I add other vegetables to this dish?
Definitely! Root vegetables like parsnips, potatoes, or beets work well. Just adjust chopping size so everything cooks evenly and consider roasting time differences.
Why is my chicken skin not crispy?
Most often it’s due to moisture on the skin or overcrowding the pan. Make sure to pat dry thoroughly and give the chicken enough space to sear properly without steaming.
Can I prepare this recipe entirely ahead of time?
You can prep the veggies and glaze ahead, but it’s best to sear the chicken and roast everything fresh for best texture. Pre-assembled raw chicken and veggies can be stored covered in the fridge for up to 24 hours.
What sides pair well with this chicken dish?
A simple green salad, crusty bread, or creamy mashed potatoes complement this meal nicely. The chicken and veggies are flavorful enough to be the star but these sides add balance.
