Roasted Cabbage Wedges Wrapped in Crispy Bacon

Lummy Hayes
Published on March 23, 2026
10 min prep
25 min cook
35 min total
4 servings
Easy

This recipe transforms simple cabbage wedges into a satisfying side by wrapping them in smoky, crisp bacon before roasting. The slow caramelization of the cabbage edges combined with the fat rendered from the bacon creates a dish rich in aroma and texture, ideal for colder evenings or festive meals.

Why This Recipe Works

Array

Pro Tips

Pat the cabbage wedges completely dry before seasoning and wrapping.
Excess moisture causes steaming rather than roasting, which prevents caramelization and crisping. Dry surfaces brown better and develop richer flavors.
Use thick-cut bacon for wrapping instead of thin slices.
Thicker bacon renders fat slowly, allowing the cabbage to soften while the bacon crisps evenly without burning or drying out.
Turn the wedges halfway through roasting for even browning on all sides.
Roasting only one side can lead to uneven cooking and less consistent texture. Turning ensures all surfaces crisp and caramelize properly.
Space wedges apart on the baking sheet to promote hot air circulation.
Crowding traps moisture, causing sogginess and inhibiting browning. Proper spacing helps achieve crisp edges and evenly cooked cabbage.
Serve shortly after roasting to enjoy bacon at its crispiest.
Bacon wrapped around cabbage can lose crispness as it cools due to moisture from the vegetables. Serving warm preserves texture contrast.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Wrapping cabbage with wet or damp leaves.

Why it happens Not drying the cabbage thoroughly after washing.

Fix Pat the wedges dry with paper towels before seasoning and wrapping to ensure roasting rather than steaming.

Using thin bacon slices that burn before the cabbage cooks through.

Why it happens Thin bacon renders fat too quickly and crisps up too fast at high oven temperatures.

Fix Choose thick-cut bacon which will render fat gradually, aligning cooking times with the cabbage.

Overcrowding the baking sheet, leading to steaming.

Why it happens Placing wedges too close traps moisture and reduces airflow.

Fix Arrange wedges with space between each to allow hot air to circulate and brown the cabbage evenly.

Not turning wedges during roasting.

Why it happens Leaving wedges on one side results in uneven texture and color.

Fix Flip wedges halfway through cooking to crisp all sides and promote uniform doneness.

Variations

Vegan Roasted Cabbage Wedges

Skip the bacon and brush wedges generously with olive oil and smoked paprika. Roast as usual for a plant-based side with smoky, caramelized cabbage.

Note: Without bacon fat, the cabbage won’t baste itself, so oil coating is essential to prevent drying and encourage browning.

Spicy Roasted Cabbage with Bacon

Add a sprinkle of cayenne pepper or chili flakes to the seasoning before roasting to introduce heat that balances the richness of the bacon.

Note: Spices can burn if applied too thickly; mix them with oil to distribute evenly and reduce direct contact with hot pan surfaces.

Herbed Bacon-Wrapped Cabbage

Tuck fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme under the bacon slices before roasting for an aromatic note that complements the smoky flavor.

Note: Herbs release oils during roasting, infusing the cabbage and bacon but should be fresh for best effect to avoid bitterness.

Smoked Paprika and Garlic Butter Cabbage

Brush wedges with a mixture of melted garlic butter and smoked paprika before wrapping with bacon for a richer, aromatic profile.

Note: Butter has a lower smoke point than oil; watch roasting time carefully to prevent burning, or add butter after initial roasting.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Do not leave leftover cabbage and bacon at room temperature for more than 2 hours to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store cooled leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Reheat gently to preserve bacon crispness.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as bacon texture degrades and cabbage becomes watery upon thawing.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F (175°C) oven for 5-10 minutes to restore crispness. Avoid microwaving, which softens bacon and cabbage textures.

Make Ahead

You can prep the wedges wrapped in bacon up to 6 hours ahead and keep refrigerated. Roast just before serving for best texture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make roasted cabbage wedges wrapped in bacon ahead of time?
Yes, you can assemble the wrapped wedges a few hours ahead and refrigerate. Roast just before serving to maintain crispness.
Why is my bacon soggy after roasting with cabbage?
This usually happens if the wedges are overcrowded or not turned during roasting, causing trapped moisture. Space wedges and flip halfway to keep bacon crisp.
Can I use red cabbage instead of green cabbage?
Yes, red cabbage works well but may require slightly longer roasting due to its denser leaves. The flavor will be slightly sweeter and earthier.
Is it possible to roast cabbage wedges without bacon?
Absolutely, simply coat wedges with olive oil and season well. Roasting caramelizes the cabbage naturally, though the dish will lack the smoky richness bacon provides.
How long do roasted cabbage wedges last in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, they keep well for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven to restore some crispness before serving.

Ingredients

4 servings

  • 1 medium head of green cabbage (about 1.5 lbs / 680 g), cut into 8 wedges
  • 8 slices of thick-cut bacon (about 200 g), preferably smoked
  • 2 tablespoons (30 ml) olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon (5 g) kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon (1 g) freshly ground black pepper
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon (1 g) smoked paprika for extra depth

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). This temperature is high enough to promote browning without burning the bacon prematurely.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes (oven preheating)
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches steady 400°F; heating feels even throughout oven space
Step 2
Cut the cabbage into 8 wedges, keeping the core intact to hold leaves together. Rinse and pat dry thoroughly to remove any moisture that could steam the cabbage instead of roasting it.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges feel firm and dry to the touch; no visible water droplets
Step 3
Brush each wedge evenly with olive oil on all exposed surfaces. Sprinkle with kosher salt, black pepper, and smoked paprika if using. The oil and seasoning will help the cabbage caramelize and enhance flavor.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges have an even sheen of oil and visible seasoning specks
Step 4
Wrap each cabbage wedge with a slice of bacon, starting at the base near the core and spiraling up so the bacon covers as much surface as possible. Secure with a toothpick if needed to prevent unwrapping during roasting.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bacon is snug around cabbage; no loose ends, wedges look uniformly wrapped
Step 5
Place the wrapped wedges on a rimmed baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a wire rack set over a sheet pan. Arrange them so there is some space between each wedge to allow hot air circulation.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges sit evenly spaced; bacon edges do not touch each other
Step 6
Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes, turning the wedges halfway through. Look for the bacon to become crisp and the cabbage edges to brown and caramelize. The interior should become tender but still hold shape.
⏱ Time: 20-25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bacon is crisp and golden brown with some darker edges; cabbage wedges are tender when pierced with a fork, edges slightly charred
Step 7
Remove from oven and let rest for 3-5 minutes before serving. This allows residual heat to finish softening the cabbage and the bacon fat to settle.
⏱ Time: 3-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges are warm but not steaming; bacon remains crispy; cabbage feels tender without falling apart
