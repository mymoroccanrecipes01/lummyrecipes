Quick Chicken Stir Fry with Soy-Ginger Sauce

Lummy Hayes
Published on March 24, 2026
10 min prep
15 min cook
25 min total
4 servings
Easy

A fast and flavorful chicken stir fry perfect for busy weeknights. This recipe features a simple soy-ginger marinade that tenderizes the chicken while keeping the vegetables crisp and vibrant. Learn how timing and high heat combine to create ideal textures and balanced flavors in under 30 minutes.

Pro Tips

Slice chicken thinly and uniformly to ensure even cooking.
Thin slices cook quickly and prevent drying out. Uniform size avoids some pieces overcooking while others remain underdone.
Do not overcrowd the pan when cooking chicken or vegetables.
Crowding lowers pan temperature, causing steaming rather than searing, which leads to soggy texture and less flavor development.
Use fresh ginger rather than powdered for maximum aroma and flavor.
Fresh ginger contains volatile oils that dissipate in powdered form, resulting in a duller taste and less vibrant aroma.
Cook vegetables quickly over high heat to maintain crunch and color.
Extended cooking breaks down cell walls, making vegetables mushy and dull in appearance. Quick stir-frying preserves their natural texture and visual appeal.
Marinate chicken just long enough to flavor and tenderize without turning mushy.
Over-marinating, especially with acidic ingredients like vinegar, can break down proteins excessively, resulting in a mealy texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the marinade or marinating too briefly.

Why it happens Some may rush or omit marination to save time, not realizing it affects flavor and texture.

Fix Allow at least 10 minutes for the marinade to impart flavor and tenderize the chicken without compromising texture.

Cooking chicken on low heat or overcrowding the pan.

Why it happens Trying to cook faster by lowering heat or adding all ingredients at once leads to steaming instead of searing.

Fix Maintain high heat and cook in batches if necessary to achieve proper browning and texture.

Overcooking vegetables until limp.

Why it happens Vegetables cook faster than meat, so adding them too early or cooking too long softens them excessively.

Fix Add vegetables after chicken is cooked and stir-fry briefly to preserve their crispness and color.

Using low-quality soy sauce with overpowering saltiness or artificial flavor.

Why it happens Some store-bought soy sauces contain additives or excessive salt, which can unbalance the dish.

Fix Choose naturally brewed, low-sodium soy sauce for a more balanced umami flavor.

Variations

Vegan Tofu Stir Fry

Replace chicken with firm tofu, pressed and cubed. Marinate tofu in the same soy-ginger mixture and cook until golden. Increase vegetable quantity for volume.

Note: Tofu requires careful pressing to remove excess moisture; cooking time is shorter to avoid crumbling.

Spicy Szechuan Style

Add 1 tsp of chili garlic sauce or crushed red pepper flakes to the marinade. Include sliced bell peppers and diced water chestnuts for crunch.

Note: Spicy elements balance the soy and ginger, adding heat and complexity without overpowering the dish.

Gluten-Free Version

Use gluten-free tamari or coconut aminos in place of soy sauce. Double-check all other sauces and ingredients for gluten content.

Note: Tamari closely mimics soy sauce flavor but without wheat; ensure rice vinegar and other condiments are certified gluten-free.

Seasonal Vegetable Stir Fry

Swap snap peas and bell pepper for seasonal vegetables like asparagus in spring or broccoli and mushrooms in fall. Adjust cooking times slightly to suit vegetable density.

Note: Denser vegetables require longer cooking; adjust heat and timing to maintain crisp-tender textures.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Do not leave stir fry at room temperature for more than 2 hours after cooking to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store cooled stir fry in airtight containers for up to 2 days. Vegetables may soften upon standing.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as vegetables lose texture and chicken may dry out. If freezing, use freezer-safe containers and consume within 1 month.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a hot skillet over medium heat, stirring often to warm evenly. Avoid microwaving to preserve texture. Add a splash of water or broth if the dish seems dry.

Make Ahead

Marinate chicken up to 2 hours ahead and refrigerate. Prepare vegetables fresh before cooking. This saves time while keeping textures vibrant.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make chicken stir fry ahead of time?
You can marinate the chicken up to 2 hours ahead. However, cook the stir fry fresh to keep vegetables crisp and chicken tender.
Why is my chicken dry after stir frying?
Cooking at too low heat or overcooking causes moisture loss. Use high heat and cook chicken quickly in thin slices to retain juiciness.
Can I use frozen vegetables instead of fresh?
Frozen vegetables release moisture and tend to be softer when cooked. Fresh vegetables are better for maintaining crisp texture in stir fry.
Can I substitute soy sauce with another sauce?
You can use tamari for gluten-free or coconut aminos for a lower sodium option. Avoid thick sauces that may alter cooking times and flavor balance.
How long does stir fry last in the fridge?
Stored properly in airtight containers, stir fry can last 1-2 days in the refrigerator. Reheat gently to maintain texture.
Is it better to use chicken thighs or breasts?
Breasts cook faster and have a milder flavor, while thighs are more forgiving and moist due to higher fat content. Both work well with adjusted cooking times.

Ingredients

4 servings

  • 500g (1.1 lb) boneless, skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp (30ml) soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp (15ml) rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp (15ml) grated fresh ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp (5ml) sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp (15ml) vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 100g (3.5 oz) snap peas, trimmed
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • Optional: 1 tsp (5ml) honey or brown sugar (to balance acidity)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Cooked jasmine or basmati rice, for serving

Instructions

Step 1
Prepare the marinade by combining soy sauce, rice vinegar, grated ginger, minced garlic, sesame oil, and optional honey in a bowl. Stir well to blend all ingredients.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is uniform with no visible separation
Step 2
Add the thinly sliced chicken to the marinade, tossing to coat evenly. Let it sit at room temperature for 10 minutes while you prepare vegetables. This short marination allows flavors to penetrate without breaking down the meat excessively.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces are glossy and coated, beginning to soften slightly
Step 3
Heat a large wok or skillet over high heat until very hot but not smoking. Add half the vegetable oil and swirl to coat the surface.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oil shimmers and moves fluidly across the pan
Step 4
Add the marinated chicken in a single layer, spreading pieces out to avoid crowding. Let cook undisturbed for 2 minutes to develop a light golden sear, then stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until chicken is opaque and cooked through.
⏱ Time: 4-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces have golden edges and no visible pink inside
Step 5
Transfer the cooked chicken to a plate. Add the remaining oil to the pan. Toss in the bell pepper, carrot, and snap peas. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until vegetables are bright, crisp-tender, and emit a fresh, slightly sweet aroma.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables retain vibrant colors and crisp texture
Step 6
Return the chicken to the pan with the vegetables. Add sliced green onions and a pinch of black pepper. Stir everything together for 1-2 minutes to warm through and combine flavors evenly.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is steaming hot and aromas of ginger and garlic intensify
Step 7
Remove from heat and serve immediately over cooked rice. The contrast between the tender chicken, crisp vegetables, and the fragrant sauce is most pronounced when served fresh.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish emits a balanced aroma of soy, ginger, and garlic with vibrant colors
