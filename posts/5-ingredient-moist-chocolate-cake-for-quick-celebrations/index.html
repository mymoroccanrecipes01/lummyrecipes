Quick and Moist 5-Ingredient Chocolate Cake with Sour Cream

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This chocolate cake is my go-to when I need something fast, moist, and super chocolatey. The secret? Sour cream adds that perfect richness and keeps it so tender. Great for last-minute treats or casual family dessert nights.

Introduction

I stumbled on this chocolate cake recipe a few years ago when I needed a quick dessert for an unexpected guest, and it instantly became a favorite. What I love most is how incredibly moist it turns out, despite using just five main ingredients. The real game-changer here is sour cream—it adds richness and keeps the crumb tender without making the cake dense or heavy. I've tried plenty of chocolate cakes that either dry out too fast or taste too eggy, but this one strikes the perfect balance every time. Plus, it’s so simple to whip up, which is a lifesaver on busy days or last-minute celebrations. One secret I picked up is to mix the dry ingredients separately and to fold in the wet ingredients gently but thoroughly—this helps the cake rise evenly without overworking the batter. Another trick is using full-fat sour cream, which really amps up the texture and flavor. When baked, the cake has a lovely crackly top and a soft, tender crumb inside. It’s perfect on its own or with a simple dusting of powdered sugar, but I often add a scoop of vanilla ice cream or fresh berries for a little extra indulgence. Honestly, this cake has become my go-to comfort dessert, and I’m excited to share it with you!

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, approximately 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup sour cream, full fat
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan or line it with parchment paper. This prevents sticking and ensures your cake comes out cleanly. Proper pan prep is key for an even bake and easy removal, so don’t skip this step!
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan feels evenly coated and dry to the touch without excess flour clumps
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, salt, and sugar. Sifting helps remove lumps and aerates the dry ingredients, which contributes to a lighter cake texture. Take your time here to get an even mix, which ensures consistent flavor and rise.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no visible clumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk together the sour cream, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract until smooth. This wet mixture adds moisture and richness. Whisking well breaks up the eggs and incorporates air, helping the cake rise beautifully.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wet ingredients form a smooth, slightly thick batter with no streaks
Step 4
Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold together with a spatula. Avoid overmixing to prevent a tough cake. Folding just until combined keeps the crumb tender and light. Watch for any dry flour pockets and gently fold those in.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is uniform in color and texture but still slightly thick
Step 5
Pour the batter into your prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Even spreading helps the cake bake uniformly. Tap the pan lightly on the counter to release any large air bubbles, which can cause holes or uneven texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter surface is level and free of large bubbles
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes. Start checking at 28 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. Overbaking dries out the cake, so keep an eye on it toward the end.
⏱ Time: 28-32 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs
Step 7
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. This resting allows the structure to set so it won’t break apart when you remove it. Cooling partially in the pan also makes it easier to flip out without sticking.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake edges begin to pull slightly from pan sides
Step 8
Run a knife around the edges of the cake to loosen it, then invert onto a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling fully on a rack prevents moisture buildup underneath, keeping the cake from becoming soggy. Resist cutting it too soon or the crumb might crumble.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is fully cooled to room temperature and feels firm but tender
Step 9
Serve as is or with a dusting of powdered sugar, fresh berries, or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. This cake shines with simple accompaniments that complement its rich chocolate flavor and moist texture. Enjoy immediately or store properly for later.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Presentation looks inviting with optional garnishes

Pro Tips

Always sift your dry ingredients before mixing.
Sifting aerates the flour and cocoa powder, breaking up lumps and evenly distributing leavening agents. This helps the cake rise uniformly and develop a lighter, more tender crumb.
Use room temperature eggs and sour cream.
Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly, affecting texture. Room temperature ingredients blend more smoothly, resulting in a uniform batter and better rise.
Don’t overmix the batter once wet and dry ingredients combine.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making the cake tough and dense. Fold gently just until there are no dry streaks to keep the crumb soft.
Check for doneness with a toothpick starting at 28 minutes.
Oven temperatures vary, so checking early prevents overbaking. A toothpick with a few moist crumbs indicates the cake is perfectly baked and not dry.
Cool the cake in the pan briefly before inverting.
This allows the cake structure to set, reducing the risk of breaking apart when you remove it. It also helps the cake release easily from the pan.
Use a wire rack to cool the cake completely.
Cooling on a rack prevents condensation from forming underneath, which can make the bottom soggy and affect texture.
For extra flavor, add a teaspoon of espresso powder to dry ingredients.
Espresso enhances the chocolate flavor without making the cake taste like coffee. It intensifies the cocoa notes for a richer dessert.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter after adding wet to dry ingredients.

Why it happens Many cooks think thorough mixing is better, but overmixing develops gluten, making the cake dense and chewy instead of tender.

Fix Gently fold the batter just until combined. Stop when there are no visible dry flour streaks. Use a spatula instead of a mixer for this step to avoid overworking.

Using cold eggs or sour cream straight from the fridge.

Why it happens Cold ingredients don’t blend well, causing uneven batter and potential curdling.

Fix Let eggs and sour cream sit at room temperature for 20-30 minutes before mixing. This helps the batter come together smoothly and bake evenly.

Skipping pan preparation.

Why it happens Rushing or forgetting to grease and flour the pan leads to sticking and broken cakes.

Fix Always grease the pan with butter or oil, then dust with flour or line with parchment paper. This simple step ensures easy release after baking.

Baking at the wrong temperature.

Why it happens Not preheating the oven or baking too hot/cold affects cake rise and texture.

Fix Preheat the oven fully to 350°F. Use an oven thermometer if possible to ensure accuracy for consistent results.

Opening the oven door too often during baking.

Why it happens This lets heat escape and can cause the cake to sink or bake unevenly.

Fix Resist peeking until the minimum baking time has passed. If you must check, do so quickly and gently.

Variations

Chocolate Sour Cream Bundt Cake

Use the same batter and bake it in a bundt pan for a more decorative cake perfect for celebrations. The bundt shape creates more crust, adding a lovely texture contrast.

Note: Increase baking time by 5-10 minutes and test doneness carefully since bundt pans vary greatly in thickness.

Add Chocolate Chips for Extra Gooeyness

Fold in 1/2 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips to the batter before baking. This adds pockets of melted chocolate inside the cake, making it extra indulgent and rich.

Note: Toss chips in a little flour before folding in to prevent them from sinking to the bottom.

Gluten-Free Version

Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum. This makes the cake accessible for gluten-sensitive eaters without sacrificing moisture or flavor.

Note: Check your blend’s instructions for 1:1 substitution and avoid overmixing to maintain crumb tenderness.

Spiced Chocolate Cake

Add 1 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the dry ingredients for a warm, slightly spicy twist that pairs beautifully with chocolate.

Note: Adjust spices to taste and be cautious with cayenne to avoid overpowering the cake.

Frosted Chocolate Sour Cream Cake

Top the cooled cake with a simple cream cheese frosting or chocolate ganache for a richer dessert. This elevates the cake for special occasions.

Note: Ensure the cake is fully cooled before frosting to prevent melting or sliding.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cake covered loosely with foil or a cake dome at room temperature for up to 2 days. This keeps the crumb moist without drying out but avoid leaving it uncovered to prevent crust hardening.

Refrigerator

Keep the cake in an airtight container or wrapped tightly in plastic wrap for up to 5 days. Chilling can firm up the texture slightly, so bring to room temperature before serving or warm gently.

Freezer

Wrap the cooled cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil before freezing for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature to maintain moisture and texture.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 10 minutes to refresh softness. Add a small splash of milk when reheating if the cake feels dry.

Make Ahead

You can bake the cake 1-2 days ahead and store it wrapped at room temperature or refrigerated. Add any frosting or toppings just before serving to keep them fresh.

FAQ

Can I substitute sour cream with yogurt?
Yes, plain full-fat Greek yogurt works well as a substitute for sour cream. It provides similar moisture and acidity to activate the baking soda, though it might slightly change texture. Avoid low-fat or flavored yogurts.
Why is my cake dense instead of moist and tender?
Dense cake often results from overmixing the batter or using cold ingredients. Mix just until combined and use room temperature eggs and sour cream for a lighter crumb.
Can I use butter instead of vegetable oil?
Butter can be used but it will make the cake less moist and slightly heavier because it solidifies when cooled. If substituting, melt the butter and cool it before mixing to maintain moisture.
How do I store leftover cake?
Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap or store in an airtight container. It keeps well at room temperature for 2 days or refrigerated up to 5 days.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes, substitute the flour with a gluten-free baking blend that includes xanthan gum. Be sure to follow the blend’s instructions for best results.
What if I don’t have baking soda?
Baking soda is essential here because it reacts with the sour cream’s acidity to leaven the cake. Without it, the cake will be dense. You could try baking powder, but the texture and rise will differ.
Why does the cake crack on top?
A crackly top is normal and signals good oven spring and moisture escaping as the cake bakes. It adds rustic charm and doesn’t affect taste.
Can I double this recipe?
Yes, you can double the ingredients and bake in a larger pan or two pans. Adjust baking time accordingly and test for doneness with a toothpick.
