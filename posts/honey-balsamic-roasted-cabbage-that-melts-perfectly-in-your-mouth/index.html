Roasted Cabbage with Honey and Balsamic Glaze

Lummy Hayes
Published on March 23, 2026
10 min prep
30 min cook
40 min total
4 servings
Easy

A straightforward recipe for roasted cabbage that brings out natural sweetness through honey and a balanced tang from balsamic glaze. The roasting process creates crispy, caramelized edges and tender inner leaves, making this an approachable yet flavorful vegetable side dish.

Pro Tips

Cut wedges evenly to ensure consistent cooking throughout.
Uneven sizes cause some pieces to overcook while others remain underdone, affecting texture and flavor balance.
Keep core intact when cutting wedges to hold the leaves together during roasting.
Intact core prevents leaves from separating and drying out, maintaining a pleasing wedge shape.
Brush glaze generously but avoid pooling on the baking sheet.
Pooling liquid can cause steaming instead of roasting, preventing caramelization and crisping.
Turn wedges halfway through roasting using tongs gently to avoid breaking leaves.
Even browning on all sides enhances flavor and texture without damaging the delicate cabbage structure.
Adjust honey quantity based on your preference for sweetness and caramelization intensity.
More honey increases browning but can risk burning if oven temperature is too high or roasting too long.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using an oven temperature that is too low.

Why it happens A lower temperature slows caramelization and roasting, resulting in cabbage that is soft but lacks crisp edges.

Fix Roast at 220°C (425°F) or higher to encourage browning and maintain texture contrast.

Cutting wedges too thin or too thick.

Why it happens Thin wedges dry out and burn quickly; thick wedges take longer to cook and may be underdone inside.

Fix Aim for wedges about 2-3 inches (5-7.5 cm) wide to balance cooking time and texture.

Applying glaze unevenly or in excess.

Why it happens Uneven coating results in inconsistent caramelization and soggy spots from excess liquid.

Fix Use a brush or drizzle carefully to coat all exposed surfaces without pooling.

Skipping turning the wedges during roasting.

Why it happens Without turning, one side may burn while the other remains pale and undercooked.

Fix Turn wedges gently halfway through roasting for even color and texture.

Variations

Vegan Roasted Cabbage

Use maple syrup or agave nectar instead of honey for a plant-based sweetener. Keep the balsamic vinegar for acidity.

Note: Maple syrup has a thinner consistency, so apply in slightly smaller amounts to avoid excess moisture.

Spiced Roasted Cabbage

Add a sprinkle of smoked paprika or ground cumin to the glaze for a warm, earthy flavor twist.

Note: Spices should be mixed into the honey-balsamic glaze before applying to ensure even distribution.

Winter Herb Roasted Cabbage

Add fresh thyme or rosemary sprigs placed on the cabbage wedges before roasting for herbal aroma.

Note: Herbs release oils during roasting that infuse the cabbage, complementing the sweet and tangy glaze.

Asian-Inspired Roasted Cabbage

Replace balsamic vinegar with rice vinegar and add a splash of soy sauce to the glaze. Sprinkle sesame seeds before serving.

Note: Soy sauce adds saltiness and umami; reduce added salt accordingly.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Do not leave roasted cabbage at room temperature for more than 2 hours to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store cooled leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The cabbage will soften further but retains flavor.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as cabbage texture degrades significantly upon thawing.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a 180°C (350°F) oven for 10-15 minutes to restore some crispness, or warm in a skillet over medium heat.

Make Ahead

Prepare wedges and glaze up to 4 hours ahead, keep refrigerated, then roast just before serving for optimal texture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make roasted cabbage ahead of time?
You can prepare the cabbage wedges and glaze a few hours ahead, but it’s best to roast just before serving to maintain crisp edges.
Why is my roasted cabbage soggy instead of crisp?
This usually happens if the cabbage is crowded on the baking sheet or coated with too much glaze, causing steaming rather than roasting. Spread wedges apart and use glaze sparingly.
Can I use red cabbage instead of green cabbage?
Yes, red cabbage works well and adds color. It has a slightly different flavor and may require slightly less roasting time due to thinner leaves.
How long does roasted cabbage last in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, roasted cabbage keeps well for up to 3 days, although texture softens over time.
Can I substitute honey with another sweetener?
Maple syrup or agave nectar are good vegan alternatives. Adjust the amount as these liquids vary in sweetness and consistency.

Ingredients

4 servings

  • 1 medium green cabbage (about 1.5 kg / 3.3 lbs), cut into 6-8 wedges
  • 3 tablespoons (45 ml) olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons (30 ml) honey
  • 2 tablespoons (30 ml) balsamic vinegar
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly oil it to prevent sticking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated, baking sheet prepared
Step 2
Remove the outer leaves of the cabbage if they are damaged or wilted. Cut the cabbage into 6 to 8 wedges, keeping the core intact to hold the leaves together during roasting.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges are uniform in size, core holding leaves firmly
Step 3
In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, honey, and balsamic vinegar until combined.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth and slightly glossy
Step 4
Place cabbage wedges on the baking sheet, cut side up. Brush or drizzle the honey-balsamic mixture evenly over each wedge. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges are evenly coated with glaze and seasoning
Step 5
Roast in the preheated oven for 25 to 30 minutes. Halfway through, use tongs to gently turn the wedges to ensure even caramelization on all sides. Look for edges turning golden brown and a slight crispness developing. The interior should become tender when pierced with a fork.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are caramelized and crisp, interior softened but wedges hold shape
Step 6
Remove from oven and let rest briefly before serving. The aroma will be a mix of sweet honey, tangy balsamic, and roasted cabbage earthiness.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges cool slightly, glaze sets on edges
