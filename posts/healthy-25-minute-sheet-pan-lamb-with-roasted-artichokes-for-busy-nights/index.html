Sheet Pan Lamb with Roasted Artichokes: A Mediterranean Weeknight Winner

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 10, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This sheet pan lamb dinner combines tender lamb and earthy roasted artichokes for a simple yet flavorful meal. The artichokes roast up beautifully, soaking in the herbs and juices. Perfect for a healthy weeknight dinner that feels special without fuss.

Pro Tips

Let the lamb marinate at least 10 minutes before roasting.
Marinating allows the acid and herbs to penetrate the meat, breaking down proteins slightly for tenderness and infusing vibrant flavor. Skipping this step results in less flavorful and potentially tougher lamb.
Don’t overcrowd the sheet pan when roasting.
Crowding traps steam, preventing proper caramelization and causing soggy textures. Leaving space between pieces promotes even browning and crisp edges, which improves both flavor and mouthfeel.
Use a meat thermometer to check lamb doneness.
Lamb can quickly go from perfectly tender to overcooked. A thermometer ensures you hit the ideal internal temperature for juicy results, avoiding guesswork and disappointment.
Flip the lamb and artichokes halfway through cooking.
Turning the pieces promotes even roasting on all sides, preventing one-sided charring and ensuring uniform texture and color.
Let the lamb rest after roasting before slicing.
Resting lets juices redistribute throughout the meat, making it moist and tender. Cutting immediately causes juices to spill out, drying the lamb.
Trim artichokes carefully to remove tough leaves and tips.
Proper trimming removes fibrous parts that can be unpleasant to eat. It ensures the artichokes roast up tender and buttery rather than chewy or bitter.
Add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice after roasting.
A bright splash of lemon juice lifts the rich flavors and balances the dish with acidity, enhancing the overall taste experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the marinating step

Why it happens Sometimes cooks rush to get food in the oven, thinking marinating takes too long or isn’t necessary for a quick dish.

Fix Even a short 10-minute marinade makes a difference. Mix the lamb with lemon, garlic, and herbs and let it rest while prepping other ingredients, ensuring flavor and tenderness.

Overcrowding the pan

Why it happens To save time or avoid using multiple pans, people pile ingredients close together.

Fix Use a large enough pan so air circulates. If needed, roast in batches. This avoids steaming and helps achieve crisp, caramelized edges.

Not using a meat thermometer

Why it happens Some rely on guesswork or appearance alone, which can be misleading.

Fix Invest in an instant-read thermometer to check lamb temperature. Aim for 145°F for medium-rare to retain juiciness and flavor.

Cutting lamb right after roasting

Why it happens Impatience to serve often leads to slicing immediately.

Fix Let lamb rest covered loosely for 5-10 minutes. This redistributes juices so meat stays moist rather than drying out.

Improper artichoke trimming

Why it happens Artichokes can be intimidating; some skip trimming or don’t remove tough parts.

Fix Take time to trim outer leaves and snip tips carefully. It makes the difference between tender, buttery bites and tough, fibrous pieces.

Variations

Spicy Harissa Lamb and Artichokes

Add 1-2 tablespoons of harissa paste to the marinade for a smoky, spicy kick that pairs beautifully with the earthy artichokes. This variation gives the dish a vibrant North African flair with a bit of heat that’s perfect for spice lovers.

Note: Harissa’s moisture may slightly increase cooking time, so watch for caramelization and adjust roasting by 5 minutes if needed.

Lemon and Mint Lamb with Artichokes

Swap thyme and rosemary for fresh mint leaves and add extra lemon zest for a bright, refreshing twist. Mint complements lamb wonderfully and lightens the dish, making it great for spring or summer dinners.

Note: Add mint at the end of cooking or as a garnish to preserve its fresh flavor and vibrant color.

Sheet Pan Lamb with Artichokes and Baby Potatoes

Add halved baby potatoes to the pan for a heartier meal. The potatoes roast alongside the lamb and artichokes, soaking up all the delicious juices and herbs for a comforting one-pan dinner.

Note: Cut potatoes small enough to cook through in 30 minutes; parboil if unsure to ensure tenderness.

Greek-Style Lamb with Feta and Olives

After roasting, sprinkle crumbled feta cheese and pitted Kalamata olives over the lamb and artichokes for a salty, tangy finish. It adds a classic Greek flavor profile and extra texture to the dish.

Note: Add feta and olives just before serving to keep feta from melting and olives firm.

Garlic and Herb Lamb with Roasted Asparagus

Replace artichokes with asparagus spears for a quicker roasting time and a fresh, green flavor. The asparagus cooks fast and pairs well with the herbaceous lamb marinade.

Note: Add asparagus to the pan halfway through cooking to avoid overcooking and keep it crisp-tender.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit out for up to 2 hours covered loosely to prevent drying. Avoid leaving it longer to reduce risk of bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The flavors meld nicely, but the artichokes may soften further. Reheat gently to avoid drying out the lamb.

Freezer

Freeze cooked lamb and artichokes in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a 325°F oven covered with foil for 15-20 minutes to keep the lamb moist. Alternatively, microwave on medium power with a splash of water or broth, covered, to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

You can marinate the lamb and prep the artichokes 1 day in advance, storing separately in the fridge. Assemble and roast just before serving for the freshest taste.

FAQ

Can I use lamb leg instead of shoulder for this recipe?
Yes, lamb leg is a leaner cut and works well for roasting. However, it can be less tender than shoulder, so watch cooking time carefully to avoid overcooking. Marinating helps tenderize leg meat, and using a meat thermometer is especially important.
What if I can’t find fresh artichokes?
Frozen or jarred artichoke hearts can be used as a substitute. They’re softer and may roast faster, so reduce cooking time by 5-10 minutes and watch closely to prevent burning. The flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.
How do I know when the lamb is cooked perfectly?
Use an instant-read meat thermometer. For medium-rare, aim for 145°F internal temperature; for medium, 160°F. The lamb should be browned on the outside but still juicy inside. Resting after cooking is key to perfect texture.
Can I prepare this meal in advance?
You can marinate the lamb and trim artichokes up to 24 hours ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Assemble and roast just before serving to keep flavors fresh and textures ideal.
What sides go well with this dish?
Couscous, quinoa, or a crisp green salad complement the rich lamb and earthy artichokes perfectly. Roasted or steamed vegetables like green beans or asparagus also pair nicely for a balanced meal.
Is it okay to skip the lemon in the marinade?
Lemon adds brightness and helps tenderize the lamb. Omitting it won’t ruin the dish, but you’ll lose some of the fresh, zesty flavor and tenderizing effect. You can substitute with a splash of vinegar if needed.
How do I prevent the artichokes from turning brown after trimming?
Rub the cut artichokes with lemon juice immediately after trimming to prevent oxidation and browning. Keeping them submerged in lemon water until roasting also helps maintain their color.
Can I use dried herbs instead of fresh?
Fresh herbs provide brighter, more vibrant flavors, but dried herbs can be used if necessary. Use about one-third the amount of dried herbs since they’re more concentrated. Add dried herbs earlier in the marinade to allow time for flavor release.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1.5 pounds lamb shoulder, trimmed and cut into chunks
  • 2 large artichokes, trimmed and quartered
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon smoked paprika

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). This high heat is essential to get a nice caramelization on both the lamb and the artichokes, which adds depth to the flavor. While the oven heats, prepare the artichokes by trimming tough leaves and cutting them into quarters. Toss them in a bowl with 1.5 tablespoons of olive oil, half the minced garlic, salt, and pepper. This initial seasoning helps the artichokes soften and develop a rich, earthy flavor during roasting. Avoid overcrowding the pan later to ensure everything crisps up nicely.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Artichokes should look coated and glossy with oil and seasoning.
Step 2
In a separate bowl, combine the lamb chunks with the remaining olive oil, garlic, rosemary, thyme, lemon zest, lemon juice, smoked paprika if using, salt, and pepper. Massage the marinade into the meat well to ensure every piece is coated. This step not only flavors the lamb but also helps tenderize it by breaking down muscle fibers slightly. Let it sit for 10 minutes if you have time, but you can roast immediately if pressed for time.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Lamb pieces should be evenly coated and fragrant with herbs and lemon.
Step 3
Arrange the marinated lamb and artichokes in a single layer on a large rimmed baking sheet. Make sure pieces are spaced out enough to roast rather than steam. This spacing is key for getting that golden crust on the lamb and caramelized edges on the artichokes. If the pan is overcrowded, the ingredients will release moisture and become soggy instead of crispy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ingredients spread evenly without overlapping on the pan.
Step 4
Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and roast for 25-30 minutes, flipping the lamb and artichokes halfway through. Flipping encourages even browning on all sides and helps the lamb cook uniformly. Keep an eye on the artichokes to prevent burning, especially if your oven runs hot. The lamb is done when it reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C) for medium-rare or 160°F (71°C) for medium.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Lamb is browned and juicy; artichokes are tender with golden edges.
Step 5
Once cooked, remove the pan from the oven and let the lamb rest for 5-10 minutes before serving. Resting allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making each bite tender and juicy rather than dry. Cover loosely with foil to keep warm. Meanwhile, you can squeeze a little extra lemon juice over the artichokes for a fresh pop of brightness before plating.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Lamb juices settle and meat feels tender when pressed gently.
Step 6
Serve the lamb and artichokes warm, ideally with a side of couscous or a fresh green salad to balance the richness. The combination of juicy lamb, tender artichokes, and fragrant herbs makes a satisfying and wholesome meal. Leftovers reheat well and taste even better the next day as the flavors meld.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish plated attractively with vibrant colors and inviting aroma.
Step 7
For an extra touch, drizzle a little good quality olive oil or a sprinkle of fresh parsley over the dish just before serving. This adds a lovely fresh note and beautiful color contrast. It’s a simple garnish but makes the plate look restaurant-worthy.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bright green herbs and glossy oil finish on the plated dish.
Step 8
If you want to add some spice, sprinkle crushed red pepper flakes on the lamb before roasting or add a pinch to the marinade. This gives the dish a subtle kick that pairs well with the earthy artichokes. Adjust the heat to your taste to keep it balanced.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Spices evenly distributed on the meat with no clumps.
Step 9
Clean up the pan promptly after cooling to keep it in good condition. Roasting artichokes can leave some sticky bits, so soak the pan in warm soapy water for a few minutes before scrubbing gently. This helps preserve your bakeware and keeps it ready for your next delicious sheet pan meal.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan cleaned without scratches or residue.
