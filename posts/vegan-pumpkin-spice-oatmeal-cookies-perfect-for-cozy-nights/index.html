Soft Vegan Pumpkin Spice Oatmeal Cookies for Cozy Fall Evenings

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
12 min Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These vegan pumpkin spice oatmeal cookies are soft, chewy, and bursting with warm fall spices. I love how the pumpkin adds moisture and a subtle sweetness. Perfect for snuggling up with tea on a crisp evening!

Introduction

I first made these vegan pumpkin spice oatmeal cookies a few years ago when I was craving something cozy but dairy-free. The pumpkin puree keeps them super moist, and the blend of warm spices makes every bite feel like a warm hug. I’ve tried a bunch of pumpkin cookie recipes, but this one sticks because it balances chewy oats with just the right pumpkin punch and spice mix — nothing too overpowering. The trick I learned was to melt the coconut oil instead of using solid, which makes the dough easier to mix and keeps the cookies tender. Plus, using rolled oats rather than quick oats gives them a nice texture, a little more bite without being tough. When they come out of the oven, they look golden and smell like fall heaven. I love grabbing one with a cup of tea after work or packing a few for a weekend hike. Honestly, these cookies have become my go-to fall treat, and I think you’ll love how simple and satisfying they are!

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree (not pumpkin pie filling)
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Getting the oven ready first ensures your cookies bake evenly right when the dough is mixed. Using parchment helps prevent sticking and makes cleanup easier, which is a lifesaver when you’re baking a batch.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated to 350°F
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, brown sugar, melted coconut oil, almond milk, and vanilla extract until smooth. Mixing these wet ingredients well is key because it helps distribute moisture evenly, which keeps the cookies soft and prevents dry spots.
⏱ Time: 3-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth and uniform in color
Step 3
In a separate bowl, combine the rolled oats, all-purpose flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and ginger. Whisking these dry ingredients together helps spread the spices evenly throughout the dough, so every bite has that cozy fall flavor.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are fully mixed with no clumps
Step 4
Slowly add the dry mixture to the wet ingredients, folding gently with a spatula. Avoid overmixing here — just combine until no flour streaks remain. Overmixing can make cookies tough, but gentle folding keeps them tender and chewy.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is sticky and cohesive, no dry flour visible
Step 5
Scoop heaping tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. This spacing is important because the cookies will spread a little during baking, and you want them to keep their shape without merging.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough balls evenly spaced on the sheet
Step 6
Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes. Keep an eye on them around the 10-minute mark; they should look set but still soft in the center. Pulling them out too late can dry them out, but underbaking leaves them doughy.
⏱ Time: 10-12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are lightly golden, centers look set but soft
Step 7
Remove the baking sheet from the oven and let the cookies cool on the sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. This resting time lets the cookies firm up a bit, so they don’t fall apart when you move them.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies hold shape when touched gently
Step 8
Once cooled, store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. They’ll stay soft and chewy for several days, making them a perfect make-ahead treat for busy weeks or snack cravings.
⏱ Time: No bake time
✓ Doneness: Cookies remain soft and flavorful after storage
Step 9
Optional: For an extra touch, sprinkle a little cinnamon sugar on top of the dough balls before baking. It adds a subtle crunch and a pretty finish, perfect if you’re serving these to guests or want a little extra fall flair.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Lightly sparkling cinnamon sugar visible on cookie tops

Pro Tips

Use room temperature wet ingredients before mixing.
When ingredients like pumpkin puree and almond milk are at room temperature, they combine more smoothly with dry ingredients. This prevents clumping and ensures even hydration, resulting in a uniform dough and consistent cookie texture.
Don’t overmix the dough once dry ingredients are added.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make cookies tough and chewy in an undesirable way. Folding just until combined keeps the texture tender and soft, which is especially important in vegan baking where eggs aren’t used as a binder.
Chill the dough for 15-20 minutes before baking.
Chilling firms the dough, making it easier to scoop and preventing excessive spreading during baking. This results in thicker, chewier cookies with a better shape and texture. Skipping this step can cause thin, flat cookies.
Use parchment paper or silicone mats on baking sheets.
These liners prevent sticking without adding extra fat. They also promote even baking and make cleanup quick. Using unlined sheets can cause cookies to stick or brown unevenly.
Check cookies a minute or two before the timer ends.
Ovens vary in heat, so cookies may bake faster or slower. Pulling them out when edges are golden but centers still soft ensures they remain moist after cooling. Leaving them too long dries them out.
Store cookies in an airtight container with a slice of bread.
The bread releases moisture that keeps the cookies soft and chewy for days. Without this, cookies can harden as they lose moisture to the air.
Use fresh spices for best flavor.
Spices lose potency over time. Freshly ground cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves provide vibrant flavor and aroma that make these cookies truly taste like fall.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using pumpkin pie filling instead of pure pumpkin puree

Why it happens Many assume canned pumpkin and pie filling are interchangeable, but pie filling contains added sugar and spices that can throw off the recipe's balance.

Fix Always use plain canned pumpkin puree to control sweetness and spice levels. If you accidentally use pie filling, reduce added sugar and omit spices to compensate.

Overmixing the dough after adding flour

Why it happens It’s tempting to thoroughly mix for evenness, but overworking the dough develops gluten, making cookies tough.

Fix Mix just until the flour disappears. Use a gentle folding motion to preserve tenderness and the right chewy texture.

Not spacing cookies properly on the sheet

Why it happens Crowding cookies saves space but causes them to merge and bake unevenly.

Fix Leave about 2 inches between scoops so cookies spread without sticking together, promoting even baking and ideal shape.

Baking at too high a temperature

Why it happens Some ovens run hot or people set temps too high, which can brown edges too fast and dry out centers.

Fix Use an oven thermometer to ensure 350°F and watch cookies closely near the end of baking to pull them out at the right softness.

Skipping the cooling time on the baking sheet

Why it happens Rushing to move cookies while hot can cause them to break apart since they’re still fragile.

Fix Let cookies cool on the sheet for 5 minutes so they firm up and hold shape when transferred to a rack.

Variations

Add Vegan Chocolate Chips

Stir in 1/2 cup of vegan semi-sweet chocolate chips to the dough for bursts of melty chocolate in every bite. This adds richness and complements the pumpkin spice perfectly.

Note: Fold chips in gently at the end of mixing to avoid melting them into the dough before baking.

Use Gluten-Free Flour Blend

Swap the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free all-purpose blend to make the cookies safe for gluten-sensitive eaters. The texture will be just as soft but slightly more crumbly.

Note: Make sure your gluten-free blend contains xanthan gum or a binder to help with structure.

Add Chopped Pecans or Walnuts

Mix in 1/2 cup of chopped nuts for a crunchy contrast to the chewy cookies. Nuts add flavor depth and a nice texture variation.

Note: Toast nuts lightly beforehand to boost their aroma and crunch.

Make Mini Cookies

Use a teaspoon to scoop smaller dough balls for bite-sized cookies that bake faster and are perfect for parties or lunchboxes.

Note: Reduce baking time by 2-3 minutes and watch closely to prevent overbaking.

Swap Almond Milk for Oat Milk

Use oat milk instead of almond milk for a slightly creamier, naturally sweeter dough. This adds a subtle flavor twist while keeping the recipe vegan and dairy-free.

Note: No other changes needed; oat milk behaves similarly in baking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat sources to prevent drying out. Adding a slice of bread in the container helps maintain softness.

Refrigerator

Cookies can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 1 week. They may firm up when cold, so bring to room temperature or warm slightly before eating to restore softness.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet first, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Reheat gently in the oven to refresh texture if desired.

Reheating

Warm cookies in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 15-20 seconds. Adding a tiny splash of almond milk before reheating can help maintain moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough up to 24 hours ahead and refrigerate tightly covered. Scoop and bake fresh when ready to serve for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use fresh pumpkin instead of canned pumpkin puree?
Yes, but you’ll need to roast or steam the pumpkin until soft, then puree it until smooth. Fresh pumpkin contains more water, so it’s best to cook it down slightly to remove excess moisture or your dough may be too wet and spread too much during baking.
What if I don’t have coconut oil? Can I use another fat?
You can substitute melted vegan butter or neutral vegetable oil. Coconut oil adds a slight richness and helps with texture, but other fats will work. Keep in mind that flavor and texture might vary slightly depending on the substitute.
Why are my cookies too flat after baking?
Flat cookies often result from too much liquid, overmixing, or not chilling the dough. Make sure to measure ingredients accurately, fold gently, and consider chilling the dough for 15-20 minutes before baking to reduce spreading.
Can I make these cookies gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend that contains a binder like xanthan gum. This helps maintain the cookie’s structure and chewiness. The texture might be a bit more crumbly but still delicious.
How long do these cookies keep fresh?
Stored in an airtight container at room temperature, they stay soft and tasty for about 3 days. Refrigerating extends freshness up to a week but may firm them up. Freezing is best for longer storage, up to 3 months.
Can I add mix-ins like nuts or chocolate chips?
Yes! Add up to 1/2 cup of your favorite mix-ins like chopped nuts or vegan chocolate chips for extra texture and flavor. Fold them in at the end of mixing to avoid breaking them up.
What’s the best way to scoop the dough?
Use a cookie scoop or two spoons to form even-sized balls. This helps cookies bake uniformly. If the dough is sticky, lightly wet your hands or scoop before shaping.
Why do my cookies crumble when cooling?
This usually happens if the cookies are removed from the baking sheet too soon or if the dough was overmixed. Let them cool on the sheet for at least 5 minutes so they firm up and hold together better.
