Creamy Keto Chicken Pasta Bowl That’s Ready in 45 Minutes

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 20, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy chicken keto pasta bowl is perfect when you want comfort food without the carbs. It’s loaded with tender chicken and a cheesy sauce that feels indulgent but fits your keto lifestyle. Great for busy weeknights!

Pro Tips

Dry the shirataki noodles well before adding to the sauce.
Shirataki noodles come packed in water and can be quite wet, which dilutes the sauce if not properly drained and dried. Patting them dry with paper towels or lightly pan-frying helps remove excess moisture, allowing the sauce to cling better and improving texture.
Cook the chicken in batches if your pan isn’t large enough.
Overcrowding the pan causes the chicken to steam rather than brown, which results in less flavor and a rubbery texture. Cooking in batches ensures a nice golden crust and juicy interior, which adds depth to the dish.
Simmer the sauce gently over low heat rather than boiling.
High heat can cause the cream to curdle and cheese to separate, ruining the smooth texture. A gentle simmer ensures the fat emulsifies properly with the cheese, creating a silky, cohesive sauce.
Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese for best flavor and melt.
Pre-grated cheeses often contain anti-caking agents that prevent smooth melting. Freshly grated Parmesan melts better and adds more authentic flavor, improving the sauce’s texture and taste.
Let the dish rest off heat for a couple of minutes before serving.
Resting allows the sauce to thicken and flavors to meld. Serving immediately after cooking might result in a thinner sauce and less integrated taste.
Season gradually and taste often, especially after adding cheese.
Cheeses and broth can vary in saltiness, so adding salt little by little prevents over-salting. Tasting ensures the final dish is perfectly balanced.
Add fresh herbs like parsley just before serving for brightness.
Fresh herbs bring a pop of color and fresh flavor that cuts through the richness of the sauce, balancing the overall dish.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the chicken until dry.

Why it happens In a rush, many cooks keep the heat too high or cook chicken pieces too long, which dries them out.

Fix Cook chicken over medium heat until just cooked through and golden. Use a meat thermometer if unsure (165°F internal). Removing chicken as soon as it’s done keeps it juicy.

Not drying shirataki noodles before cooking.

Why it happens It’s easy to forget the noodles come packed in water, which dilutes the sauce.

Fix Drain and pat noodles dry thoroughly before adding to the pan. Optionally, pan-fry them briefly to remove excess moisture.

Cooking the sauce on too high heat causing curdling.

Why it happens High heat causes dairy proteins to separate and cheese to clump.

Fix Simmer sauce gently over low heat, stirring constantly to keep it smooth.

Skipping seasoning adjustments after adding cheese.

Why it happens Cheese adds salt, so not re-tasting leads to over- or under-seasoned sauce.

Fix Taste the sauce after cheese melts and adjust salt and pepper gradually.

Adding noodles too early and stirring vigorously.

Why it happens Shirataki noodles are delicate and can break apart with rough handling.

Fix Add noodles last and fold gently to coat without breaking.

Variations

Spicy Keto Chicken Pasta

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the sauce for a spicy kick. You can also toss in some sautéed jalapeños with the garlic. This variation gives the dish a lively heat without overwhelming the creamy base.

Note: Adjust spice levels carefully; start with small amounts to maintain balance with the creamy sauce.

Keto Chicken Alfredo with Mushrooms

Sauté sliced mushrooms with the chicken for an earthy flavor and extra texture. Mushrooms soak up the sauce beautifully and add a savory depth that pairs well with the creamy cheese base.

Note: Cook mushrooms until golden before adding garlic to prevent excess moisture in the sauce.

Low-Carb Pesto Chicken Pasta

Swap out the Italian seasoning for 1/4 cup of basil pesto stirred into the sauce just before adding noodles. This creates a fresh, herbaceous twist that brightens the rich creaminess.

Note: Add pesto off heat to preserve its vibrant flavor and color.

Dairy-Free Keto Chicken Pasta

Use coconut cream instead of heavy cream and nutritional yeast instead of cheese for a dairy-free version. The sauce will be slightly different in texture but still creamy and flavorful, perfect for those avoiding dairy.

Note: Heat coconut cream gently and whisk nutritional yeast in slowly to avoid clumping.

Keto Chicken Pasta with Spinach

Add a couple of handfuls of fresh spinach to the sauce at the end and cook until wilted. This adds color, nutrients, and a fresh flavor that complements the creamy sauce nicely.

Note: Add spinach last and cook just until wilted to maintain vibrant color and texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely stay at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and the noodles soften when cold. Reheat gently on the stovetop or microwave with a splash of broth or cream to restore creaminess.

Freezer

Freeze in a freezer-safe container for up to 1 month. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and reheat gently to maintain texture.

Reheating

Reheat over low heat on the stovetop or in short bursts in the microwave, stirring frequently. Add a little chicken broth or cream to loosen the sauce if it thickens too much. Avoid high heat to prevent curdling.

Make Ahead

You can cook the chicken and prepare the sauce 1-2 days ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Combine with noodles and heat through just before serving for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use regular pasta instead of shirataki noodles?
You can, but that will increase the carb count significantly, making it less keto-friendly. If you’re not following keto, regular pasta works fine but cook it al dente and drain well before adding to the sauce.
How do I prevent the cream sauce from curdling?
Cook your sauce gently over low heat and avoid boiling. Stir constantly when adding cheese, and use full-fat cream. High heat causes dairy proteins to separate, leading to curdling.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers without drying out the chicken?
Reheat slowly over low heat on the stove or in the microwave in short intervals. Add a splash of chicken broth or cream to keep the sauce moist. Avoid overheating, which dries out the chicken.
Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. They add richer flavor and work beautifully in this recipe. Adjust cooking time slightly as thighs may take a bit longer.
How do I get rid of the odor from shirataki noodles?
Rinse shirataki noodles thoroughly under cold water and boil them for 2-3 minutes. Dry them well by patting with paper towels or pan-frying briefly to remove excess moisture and odor.
Is this recipe suitable for dairy-free diets?
Not as is, but you can substitute heavy cream with coconut cream and use nutritional yeast or dairy-free cheese alternatives. The sauce texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
Yes, you can cook the chicken and prepare the sauce 1-2 days in advance. Store separately and combine with noodles just before reheating to keep textures fresh.
Why is my sauce too thin?
If the sauce hasn’t simmered long enough or the heat was too high causing separation, it can be thin. Let it simmer gently longer to thicken, and make sure to add cheese slowly over low heat.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups cooked shirataki noodles, drained and rinsed
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the bite-sized chicken pieces in a single layer, seasoning them lightly with salt and pepper. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is golden brown and cooked through. Avoid overcrowding the pan, as this can cause the chicken to steam rather than brown, which lessens flavor. Browning the chicken properly creates a deeper flavor and a better texture in the final dish.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces are golden on the outside and no longer pink inside
Step 2
Add the minced garlic to the skillet with the chicken and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Then pour in the heavy cream and chicken broth, stirring to combine. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat. This slow simmer allows the sauce to thicken gradually without curdling. If you rush this step by turning the heat too high, the cream might separate or burn, ruining the texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce starts to thicken and bubbles gently around the edges
Step 3
Stir in the shredded mozzarella, grated Parmesan, and Italian seasoning. Keep stirring until the cheeses melt completely into the sauce, creating a smooth, creamy texture. Cheese melts best over low heat; high heat can cause it to clump or become grainy. Stirring constantly helps emulsify the cheese and cream into a luscious sauce.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is thick, smooth, and cheesy with no lumps
Step 4
Add the drained shirataki noodles to the skillet, folding them gently into the sauce to coat evenly. Shirataki noodles have a delicate texture, so fold carefully to avoid breaking them apart. The noodles will absorb some of the sauce, making the dish creamy and satisfying without the carbs of traditional pasta.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Noodles are heated through and coated well with sauce
Step 5
Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. This step is important because the cheese and broth vary in saltiness. Season gradually and taste often to avoid over-salting. Remember, a well-balanced seasoning elevates the entire dish.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Sauce tastes rich and balanced, neither bland nor too salty
Step 6
Turn off the heat and let the dish rest for 2 minutes. This resting period allows the sauce to thicken further and the flavors to meld together. Skipping this step might result in a thinner sauce and less cohesive flavors.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce thickens slightly and clings well when stirred
Step 7
Serve the creamy chicken keto pasta bowl garnished with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and fresh flavor. The parsley brightens the rich sauce and adds a lovely herbal note that balances the dish. Presentation matters, so a sprinkle of green makes it look as good as it tastes.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dish looks vibrant and appetizing with garnished parsley
Step 8
For an extra touch, sprinkle a bit more grated Parmesan on top before serving. It adds a sharp, salty contrast to the creamy sauce. This finishing touch takes the dish from great to restaurant-worthy, and it’s a simple way to enhance the flavor profile.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Cheese melts slightly on top, adding texture and flavor
Step 9
Enjoy your creamy keto chicken pasta bowl right away while it’s warm and comforting. The sauce thickens as it cools, so eating it fresh ensures the best texture and flavor experience. If you have leftovers, reheat gently to avoid drying out the chicken or breaking the sauce.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Dish is enjoyed warm with creamy sauce and tender chicken
