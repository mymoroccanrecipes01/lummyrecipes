Classic Homemade Chocolate Berry Tart for Father’s Day Delight

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 25, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This chocolate berry tart is a perfect blend of rich chocolate and fresh berries on a buttery crust. It’s easy to make and makes any Father’s Day feel special and homemade. Trust me, Dad will love it!

Introduction

I first made this chocolate berry tart a few summers ago when I wanted something special for Father’s Day but didn’t want to fuss over complicated desserts. The mix of bittersweet chocolate and fresh berries on a tender, buttery crust instantly won over my family. What makes this version stand out is how I use cold butter in the crust and chill the dough thoroughly before baking — it creates that perfect flaky texture every time. Plus, melting the chocolate with warm cream makes the ganache silky smooth without any graininess. I remember one time I rushed the crust chilling step and ended up with a crumbly mess, so I’ve learned patience really pays off here. When it’s done, the tart looks rustic but elegant, with glossy berries nestled on top of the rich chocolate filling. It’s the kind of dessert that feels homemade and thoughtful, perfect for a relaxed Father’s Day celebration at home. You’ll love how easy it is to pull together, yet it tastes like you spent hours on it.

What You'll Need

One 9-inch tart, 8 servings

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons cold water
  • 6 ounces bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 cup fresh mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar for dusting

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the tart crust. Combine the sifted flour and sugar in a large bowl. Add the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. This step is crucial because the cold butter creates pockets in the dough that bake into a flaky crust. Avoid warming the butter with your hands to keep the texture light and tender.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks sandy with pea-sized butter bits evenly distributed
Step 2
Mix in the egg yolk and cold water, stirring gently until the dough just comes together. Don’t overwork it, or the crust will be tough. Once combined, gather the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic, and chill it in the fridge for at least 30 minutes. This chilling step lets the gluten relax and keeps the butter firm, which helps prevent shrinking during baking.
⏱ Time: 40 minutes (including chilling)
✓ Doneness: Dough is firm and smooth, not sticky
Step 3
Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Press the dough into the pan’s edges and trim the excess. Prick the bottom with a fork to prevent bubbling. Cover the crust with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans to blind bake. This technique ensures the crust cooks evenly and stays crisp once filled.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden and bottom feels firm when touched
Step 4
Bake the crust with weights for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment paper. Return the crust to the oven and bake for another 8-10 minutes until golden brown. Watch carefully to avoid burning the edges. A well-baked crust will hold the ganache filling without becoming soggy.
⏱ Time: 25 minutes total
✓ Doneness: Crust has a deep golden color and feels dry
Step 5
While the crust cools, prepare the chocolate ganache. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan just until it starts to simmer—don’t let it boil. Pour it over the chopped bittersweet chocolate in a heatproof bowl. Let it sit for a minute to soften the chocolate, then stir gently until smooth and glossy. Adding a tablespoon of butter at this stage enriches the ganache and gives it a silky texture.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ganache is shiny, smooth, and thick enough to coat the back of a spoon
Step 6
Pour the ganache into the cooled tart shell, spreading it evenly with a spatula. The ganache should fill the crust without overflowing. Let the tart sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes, then place it in the fridge to set completely for at least 2 hours. This chilling step is key to developing a firm yet creamy filling that slices beautifully.
⏱ Time: 2.5 hours including chilling
✓ Doneness: Ganache is firm to the touch but still creamy
Step 7
Just before serving, arrange the fresh mixed berries on top of the ganache. You can pile them in a pretty pattern or scatter them casually. The tartness and juiciness of the berries balance the rich chocolate perfectly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Berries look fresh and vibrant, no moisture bleeding into ganache
Step 8
Dust the tart lightly with powdered sugar for a delicate finish. This simple step adds a touch of elegance and a hint of sweetness to the berries without overpowering the chocolate.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even, light dusting of sugar on berries and crust edges
Step 9
Serve the tart chilled or at room temperature. Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water for clean slices. This dessert pairs wonderfully with a cup of coffee or a glass of red wine for a relaxed Father’s Day treat.
⏱ Time: Immediate serving
✓ Doneness: Slices hold shape cleanly with visible layers of crust, ganache, and berries

Pro Tips

Keep your butter and water cold when making the crust.
Cold ingredients help create flaky layers by preventing the butter from melting into the flour before baking. If the butter warms up, the dough can become greasy and dense, resulting in a tough crust instead of a tender, flaky one.
Don’t skip blind baking the crust.
Blind baking sets the crust so it won’t become soggy once the ganache filling is added. The weights prevent the dough from puffing up or shrinking, ensuring a flat, even base for the filling to rest on.
Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water for slicing the tart.
Heating the knife between cuts helps it glide through the firm ganache and crust cleanly, preventing messy edges and preserving the tart’s elegant appearance.
Let the ganache cool slightly before pouring into the crust.
If the ganache is too hot, it can soften or warp the crust. Cooling it just until pourable prevents heat damage and helps the filling set properly once chilled.
Chill the tart thoroughly before serving.
Chilling allows the ganache to firm up and the flavors to meld. Serving it too soon results in a runny filling that’s hard to slice and less satisfying texture.
Use fresh, firm berries for topping.
Overripe or wet berries can release moisture, making the tart soggy and less visually appealing. Drying them well ensures a fresh look and taste.
Dust powdered sugar just before serving.
Powdered sugar can dissolve or clump if applied too early. Adding it last keeps the tart looking fresh and adds a delicate sweetness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the dough chilling step

Why it happens Sometimes cooks want to rush and skip chilling to save time, thinking it won’t affect the crust much.

Fix Always chill the dough for at least 30 minutes to an hour. This relaxes gluten and firms the butter, preventing shrinkage and ensuring a tender, flaky crust. If you skip this, the crust may shrink and become tough.

Overworking the dough

Why it happens People often knead the dough too much, trying to get it perfectly smooth, not realizing this develops gluten.

Fix Mix the dough just until it comes together. Over-kneading causes toughness. Use gentle folding and minimal handling to keep the crust tender.

Using boiling cream for ganache

Why it happens Some assume boiling cream is needed to melt chocolate quickly.

Fix Heat cream just to a simmer, not a boil. Boiling can scorch cream, causing bitterness. Simmering extracts flavor without damaging the chocolate.

Not blind baking the crust

Why it happens Skipping this step to save time or assuming the crust will bake fully with filling inside.

Fix Blind bake crust with weights to set it and prevent sogginess. Without this, the crust can be undercooked and mushy.

Using overripe or wet berries

Why it happens Rushing berry prep or buying berries without checking freshness.

Fix Choose firm, ripe berries and dry them thoroughly before topping. This prevents soggy tart and preserves appearance.

Variations

Nutty Almond Chocolate Tart

Add 1/4 cup finely ground almonds to the crust for a subtle nutty flavor and crunch. You can also sprinkle slivered almonds on top of the ganache before chilling. This variation adds texture and depth without overpowering the chocolate and berries.

Note: Adding almonds to the crust slightly changes the dough’s moisture absorption, so reduce flour by a tablespoon to keep the right consistency.

Orange Zest Infused Ganache

Stir in 1 teaspoon of fresh orange zest into the warm ganache before pouring it into the crust. The citrus brightens the rich chocolate and pairs beautifully with berries, giving the tart a refreshing twist.

Note: Add zest after heating cream but before pouring over chocolate to allow oils to infuse evenly.

Vegan Chocolate Berry Tart

Use coconut oil instead of butter in the crust and replace heavy cream with full-fat coconut milk for the ganache. This creates a dairy-free tart that’s just as rich and delicious, with a subtle coconut flavor that complements the berries.

Note: Chill the crust dough a bit longer since coconut oil softens faster than butter.

Spiced Chocolate Tart

Add 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the ganache for a warm, spicy kick. This variation is great for cooler months and adds an unexpected depth to the classic chocolate and berry combo.

Note: Mix spices into the chopped chocolate before adding hot cream to ensure even distribution.

Lemon Curd and Berry Swirl Tart

Before pouring the ganache, spread a thin layer of lemon curd in the crust and swirl it slightly with the chocolate filling. This adds a tangy contrast that brightens the dessert and pairs nicely with fresh berries on top.

Note: Keep lemon curd chilled and spread evenly to avoid mixing too much with ganache.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The tart can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with a cake dome or plastic wrap. Beyond this, the ganache may soften too much and berries can start to release moisture, affecting texture.

Refrigerator

Store the tart in an airtight container or covered with plastic wrap for up to 3 days. The ganache firms up when chilled, so you may want to let it sit at room temperature for 15 minutes before serving to soften slightly. Fresh berries can lose firmness over time.

Freezer

Freeze the tart wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then foil for up to 2 months. To thaw, place it in the refrigerator overnight. Avoid thawing at room temperature to prevent condensation and soggy crust. The texture may be slightly less crisp after freezing.

Reheating

This tart is best served cold or at room temperature and is not recommended for reheating. If you prefer a softer ganache, let it sit out for 20 minutes before slicing. Avoid microwaving as it can melt the filling unevenly.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the crust and ganache up to 2 days ahead. Blind bake and chill the crust, prepare ganache and refrigerate separately. Assemble and add berries on the day of serving for the freshest presentation.

FAQ

Can I use frozen berries instead of fresh?
Frozen berries tend to release more moisture as they thaw, which can make the tart soggy and affect the ganache texture. If you must use frozen, thaw and drain them thoroughly on paper towels before topping the tart, but fresh berries are always best for this recipe.
How do I prevent the crust from shrinking during baking?
Chilling the dough before rolling and again after placing it in the tart pan helps relax the gluten and firm the butter, preventing shrinkage. Also, avoid stretching the dough when fitting it into the pan, and use pie weights during blind baking to keep it in place.
Can I make the ganache in advance?
Yes! Ganache can be made up to 2 days ahead and stored in the fridge. Reheat gently in short bursts in the microwave or over a double boiler, stirring until smooth before pouring into the crust.
What’s the best way to slice the tart cleanly?
Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water and wiped dry between cuts. This prevents the ganache from sticking to the blade and ensures neat, clean slices.
Can I substitute the bittersweet chocolate with milk chocolate?
You can, but milk chocolate is sweeter and less intense, which will change the flavor balance. The tart may taste sweeter and less rich, so you might want to reduce the sugar in the crust slightly.
Is it possible to make this tart gluten-free?
Yes, you can substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend designed for baking. Make sure it contains xanthan gum or add a small amount to help with structure. The texture might be a bit different but still delicious.
Why did my ganache turn grainy?
Grainy ganache usually happens when the chocolate isn’t melted smoothly or if it comes into contact with moisture. Make sure to chop chocolate finely and stir gently. Avoid overheating the cream or chocolate, which can cause separation.
Can I use salted butter in the crust?
Using salted butter is possible but not ideal because salt levels vary and can affect the dough’s flavor unpredictably. It’s better to use unsalted butter and add salt separately if needed for control.
