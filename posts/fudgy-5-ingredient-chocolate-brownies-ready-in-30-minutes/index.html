Insanely Fudgy 5-Ingredient Brownies That Bake in No Time

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
10 min prep 25 min cook 9 servings

These brownies are my go-to when I want something rich and fudgy without fuss. Just five simple ingredients and a quick bake time make them perfect for spontaneous cravings or cozy nights in.

Introduction

I first stumbled on this brownie recipe during a hectic week when I desperately needed a quick, satisfying dessert. Most brownie recipes I tried before either took forever or came out cakey, which wasn’t what I wanted. This version is different because it uses melted chocolate instead of cocoa powder, which gives it that insanely fudgy texture I crave. The simplicity is what won me over—just five ingredients and minimal fuss. I’ve made these brownies countless times, and the trick I learned is to melt the chocolate and butter gently together; overheating can make the batter grainy. Also, don’t overbake them! When done right, the brownies have a shiny crackly top with a gooey center that’s pure chocolate heaven. They’re perfect for cozy nights, last-minute guests, or whenever you need a chocolate hug. I love that they come together so quickly, making them my go-to when I want homemade brownies without the wait or complicated steps.

Why This Works

What sets these brownies apart is the use of melted chocolate instead of just cocoa powder. Melting chocolate with butter creates a rich, emulsified base that gives the brownies their signature fudgy texture. The cocoa butter in chocolate melts and coats the flour proteins, limiting gluten formation and preventing cakeyness.

Incorporating sugar into the warm chocolate mixture allows it to dissolve slightly, which contributes to the crackly, shiny top that we all love in brownies. Adding eggs one at a time and beating well introduces some air, but not enough to make the brownies fluffy — just enough to hold them together and give a slight lift.

Folding in the flour gently is crucial. Overmixing activates gluten, which leads to a tougher texture more like cake. The goal is a thick, glossy batter that bakes into a moist, fudgy center with a set but tender crumb.

Finally, baking time is everything. Pulling the brownies out when the edges are set but the center jiggles slightly ensures they remain gooey inside. Cooling allows the fats to solidify and the structure to stabilize, making cutting easier and preserving the texture.

  • Look for good-quality semi-sweet chocolate with at least 50-60% cocoa solids for a rich flavor. Avoid chocolate with vegetable oils or fillers, as they can affect melting and texture. Chips are convenient but bars chopped finely work great too. Melting chocolate gently prevents graininess and helps create that smooth, fudgy base.
  • Use unsalted butter so you can control the salt level in the recipe. Butter adds richness and moisture, and when melted with chocolate, it helps the batter emulsify. Room temperature butter melts evenly with chocolate, ensuring a silky texture without lumps.
  • Regular granulated sugar helps achieve the crackly, shiny brownie top by dissolving into the warm chocolate mixture. Using superfine sugar can speed dissolution but is not necessary. Avoid substituting with liquid sweeteners, which can alter texture and baking behavior.
  • Eggs provide structure and moisture. Adding them one at a time while beating ensures they emulsify properly into the chocolate mixture, preventing curdling. Using room temperature eggs helps them blend smoothly, ensuring a consistent batter.
  • All-purpose flour provides the necessary structure. Sifting it before folding prevents lumps and ensures even distribution. Overmixing activates gluten, so gentle folding is key to a fudgy, tender crumb. Substituting with cake flour will make brownies softer but less dense.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (170g) semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lining an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper or lightly greasing it. This prep step ensures your brownies won’t stick and the oven will be ready to bake evenly. It’s important to get this done first so the batter doesn’t sit too long, which can affect texture.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven preheated and pan fully prepared with parchment or grease
  2. Step 2

    In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the chocolate chips and butter. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring well after each, until completely melted and smooth. This gentle melting process prevents the chocolate from seizing or burning, which would ruin the texture. Stirring thoroughly after each interval ensures even melting.

    ⏱ 4-5 minutes✓ Smooth, glossy melted chocolate without lumps or graininess
  3. Step 3

    Add the sugar to the melted chocolate mixture and stir until fully combined. Mixing the sugar in while the chocolate is still warm helps it dissolve slightly, contributing to that shiny, crackly brownie top. Avoid adding sugar to cold chocolate as it won’t blend as well.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Mixture looks smooth and slightly thickened
  4. Step 4

    Beat in the eggs one at a time, stirring vigorously after each addition. This step is key for incorporating air gently, which helps the brownies hold structure without becoming cakey. If you add eggs too quickly, the batter can curdle or become too thin.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is shiny and slightly thickened, no streaks of egg visible
  5. Step 5

    Sift the flour over the batter to avoid lumps, then fold it in gently with a spatula. Overmixing here can develop gluten, leading to tougher brownies. Folding ensures the flour is just incorporated, preserving fudginess.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ No flour streaks remain, batter is thick and glossy
  6. Step 6

    Pour the batter into your prepared pan, spreading it evenly with the spatula. This helps the brownies bake uniformly. Uneven spreading can cause some edges to overcook while the center stays underdone.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Batter surface is level and smooth
  7. Step 7

    Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes. Keep a close eye starting at 20 minutes; you want a shiny, crackly top but a slightly soft center when you gently jiggle the pan. Overbaking dries them out, losing that fudgy texture.

    ⏱ 20-25 minutes✓ Top has shiny cracks, edges set but center jiggles slightly
  8. Step 8

    Remove the brownies from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling allows the brownies to set up and makes cutting cleaner. Cutting too soon can cause them to fall apart.

    ⏱ 30-45 minutes✓ Brownies are firm to the touch and cooled to room temperature
  9. Step 9

    Once cooled, lift the brownies out using the parchment paper and cut into squares with a sharp knife. For cleaner cuts, wipe the knife between slices. Serve immediately or store for later indulgence!

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Clean, even brownie squares ready to enjoy

Pro Tips

  • Melt chocolate and butter slowly over low heat or in short microwave bursts. — Chocolate is sensitive and can seize (turn grainy and clumpy) if overheated. Slow melting ensures a smooth, glossy mixture that emulsifies well with other ingredients, resulting in fudgy brownies.
  • Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. — This technique helps incorporate the eggs evenly, creating a stable batter that holds together without becoming cakey. Rushing this step can cause the batter to split or become too thin.
  • Don’t overmix once you add the flour. — Overmixing leads to gluten development, which makes brownies tougher and cakier. Gentle folding preserves a tender, fudgy texture by limiting gluten formation.
  • Use parchment paper to line your baking pan. — This prevents sticking and makes it easy to lift the whole batch out for clean cutting. It also helps the brownies bake evenly by providing a barrier between metal and batter.
  • Watch the baking time closely and test for doneness early. — Brownies go from perfectly fudgy to dry quickly. Pull them when the edges are set but the center still jiggles slightly for the best texture.
  • Let brownies cool completely before cutting. — Cooling allows fats to solidify and the structure to set, which prevents crumbling and messy cuts.
  • Use a sharp knife and wipe it clean between cuts. — A sharp knife makes clean slices, and wiping prevents crumbs from sticking and tearing the brownies apart.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overbaking the brownies
    Why: Many assume brownies are done when the top looks firm, but this can lead to dry, cakey results instead of fudgy ones.
    Fix: Start checking the brownies at 20 minutes and remove them when the edges are set but the center still jiggles slightly. This ensures a moist, gooey center.
  • Overmixing after adding flour
    Why: It's tempting to mix thoroughly to avoid lumps, but this activates gluten and toughens the brownies.
    Fix: Fold the flour in gently just until no streaks remain. Stop mixing right away to keep the texture tender.
  • Melting chocolate too quickly or at too high heat
    Why: Rushing the melting process can cause the chocolate to seize and become grainy.
    Fix: Melt chocolate and butter in short bursts in the microwave or over a double boiler, stirring often to keep it smooth.
  • Not lining the pan with parchment
    Why: Brownies can stick to the pan, making removal difficult and resulting in broken pieces.
    Fix: Always line your pan with parchment paper or grease thoroughly for easy removal and clean cuts.
  • Adding cold eggs directly
    Why: Cold eggs can cause the melted chocolate mixture to seize or curdle.
    Fix: Use room temperature eggs and add them slowly, beating well after each addition to keep the batter smooth.

Variations

  • Nutty Fudge Brownies: Stir in 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter for a crunchy texture contrast. The nuts add richness and a pleasant bite, balancing the fudgy softness.
    Add nuts right after folding in the flour to distribute evenly without overmixing.
  • Espresso-Infused Brownies: Add 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder to the melted chocolate mixture for a subtle coffee flavor that intensifies the chocolate notes without overpowering.
    Dissolve espresso powder in the warm butter-chocolate mix before adding sugar.
  • Salted Caramel Swirl Brownies: Drop spoonfuls of caramel sauce onto the batter and swirl gently with a knife before baking. The salty-sweet caramel pairs beautifully with the rich chocolate.
    Do not over-swirl; just enough to create marbled pockets to maintain texture contrast.
  • Vegan Fudgy Brownies: Replace eggs with 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce and use dairy-free chocolate and butter alternatives. Expect a slightly denser but still deliciously fudgy result.
    Baking time may need to be extended by 3-5 minutes; watch for doneness cues carefully.
  • Peanut Butter Swirl Brownies: Swirl in 1/3 cup creamy peanut butter into the batter before baking for a nutty richness that complements the chocolate beautifully.
    Warm the peanut butter slightly to make swirling easier, and avoid overmixing to keep distinct layers.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store brownies covered tightly at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them in an airtight container or wrapped in plastic wrap to prevent drying out. They stay fudgy and moist this way.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Brownies can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Note they may firm up and lose some softness. Let them come to room temperature before serving or warm briefly for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Wrap brownies tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to prevent freezer burn. Freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Reheat gently to restore softness.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm brownies in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Add a tiny splash of milk or butter if they feel dry. Avoid overheating which makes them tough.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it refrigerated in the baking pan, covered tightly with plastic wrap. Bake fresh the next day for best results. Alternatively, bake and freeze brownies, thawing and warming when ready to serve.

FAQ

Can I use cocoa powder instead of melted chocolate?
You can, but the texture will be less fudgy and more cakey. Cocoa powder lacks the cocoa butter that melted chocolate provides, which helps create that rich, gooey consistency. For best fudgy brownies, stick to melted chocolate.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Look for a shiny, crackly top and set edges. The center should still jiggle slightly when you gently shake the pan. This means the insides are moist and fudgy. Overbaking will dry them out, so start checking at 20 minutes.
Can I substitute butter with oil?
Butter adds flavor and helps with texture due to its milk solids and water content. Using oil can make brownies denser and less flavorful. If you must substitute, use a neutral oil like canola and expect a slightly different texture.
Why do my brownies come out cakey instead of fudgy?
This usually happens from overmixing after adding flour, overbaking, or using too much flour. Folding gently and watching baking time carefully helps keep them fudgy. Also, using melted chocolate rather than only cocoa powder affects texture.
Can I add mix-ins like nuts or chocolate chunks?
Absolutely! Stir in chopped nuts, chocolate chips, or chunks after folding in the flour. This adds texture and flavor without affecting the fudgy base.
How do I prevent the chocolate from seizing when melting?
Melt chocolate slowly over low heat or in short microwave bursts, stirring frequently. Avoid contact with water, as even a drop can cause chocolate to seize and become grainy.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, you can double ingredients and bake in a larger pan, but baking time will increase. Start checking for doneness at the original time and add extra minutes as needed.
What’s the best way to cut brownies cleanly?
Let brownies cool completely, then use a sharp knife wiped clean between cuts. This prevents sticking and crumbling for neat squares.
