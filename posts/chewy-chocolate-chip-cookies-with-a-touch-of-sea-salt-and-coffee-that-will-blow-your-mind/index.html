Classic Chewy Chocolate Chip Cookies with a Hint of Coffee and Sea Salt

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
12 min Time
24 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These cookies are chewy and packed with rich chocolate, subtly enhanced by a touch of coffee. The sea salt on top adds a surprising pop. Perfect for afternoon snacks or sharing with friends!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon adding a bit of coffee to my chocolate chip cookies when I was trying to deepen the flavor without making them taste like coffee. It was a happy accident that turned out to be a game changer! This version is my go-to because it strikes the perfect balance between chewy and rich, with just a subtle coffee undertone that intensifies the chocolate without overpowering it. I also sprinkle a little sea salt on top right before baking, which adds this amazing pop of flavor in every bite. What really sets this recipe apart is melting the butter first, which helps create that chewy texture I love, and using both an egg and an extra yolk for richness. It took a few tries to get the timing and salt just right, but now these cookies come out perfect every time. When they’re fresh from the oven, they’re soft and gooey, but after cooling slightly, they have that classic chewy bite with crispy edges. Honestly, it’s the kind of cookie that disappears quickly in my house, so I always double the batch when I make them!

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 cookies

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder
  • 1 large egg plus 1 egg yolk, at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line two baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats. This prevents sticking and helps the cookies bake evenly. It’s important to have the oven fully heated before baking so the cookies start to set quickly, which keeps them chewy inside. Avoid crowding the baking sheets; leave about 2 inches between each dough ball to allow spreading.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully preheated and baking sheets are ready with spaced dough balls.
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, baking soda, and 1/2 teaspoon sea salt. This evenly distributes the leavening agent and salt, which helps the cookies rise properly and enhances flavor. Make sure not to skip sifting if your flour is lumpy—it ensures a smooth dough and prevents dense cookies.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are combined with no lumps.
Step 3
In a large bowl, mix the melted butter with both sugars until smooth and shiny. The melted butter gives the cookies a soft, chewy texture while the sugars caramelize during baking, adding depth and chewiness. Use a whisk or hand mixer on low speed for about 1-2 minutes, making sure the mixture is well combined but not overworked.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugars form a glossy, homogeneous mixture.
Step 4
Add the instant espresso powder to the butter-sugar mixture and stir well. This is the secret step that enhances the chocolate flavor without making the cookies taste like coffee. It's subtle but powerful, so don’t skip it! The powder should dissolve completely for even flavor distribution.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Espresso powder is fully incorporated without grainy texture.
Step 5
Beat in the egg and extra yolk one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract. Eggs add moisture and richness, while the extra yolk contributes to a tender chewiness. Make sure each addition is fully mixed before adding the next to avoid curdling. The batter will become thick and creamy.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Smooth, thick batter with no streaks of egg visible.
Step 6
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, folding gently with a spatula until just combined. Overmixing develops gluten and makes cookies tough, so fold carefully until you don’t see any flour streaks. The dough will be thick and slightly sticky.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Dough is uniform in color and texture, slightly sticky but cohesive.
Step 7
Fold in the chocolate chips evenly throughout the dough. This step ensures every cookie has a good amount of chocolate. Use a spatula to gently distribute them without breaking the chips. If you want melty pockets of chocolate, use quality chips with good cocoa content.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are evenly spread and dough is uniform.
Step 8
Scoop dough onto the prepared baking sheets using a cookie scoop or tablespoon, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Before baking, sprinkle a tiny pinch of sea salt on each dough ball. This salty contrast enhances the chocolate and coffee flavors and adds a gourmet touch. Don’t overdo the salt—just a little goes a long way.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dough balls are spaced with a light sea salt sprinkle on top.
Step 9
Bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, rotating the pans halfway through. Look for edges to be golden but centers still soft and slightly underbaked—that’s the key to chewy cookies. Overbaking will dry them out. Let cookies cool on the sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to finish cooling.
⏱ Time: 12
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden and centers look set but soft.

Pro Tips

Use room temperature eggs for better mixing.
Room temperature eggs combine more evenly with melted butter and sugars, ensuring a smooth batter. Cold eggs can clump or cause the butter to seize, leading to uneven texture in the cookies.
Chill the dough for 30 minutes before baking.
Chilling solidifies the fats in the dough, which helps prevent excessive spreading during baking. It also allows the flavors to meld, resulting in more flavorful cookies with a better texture.
Don’t skip the sea salt sprinkle on top.
The contrast of salty and sweet enhances the chocolate and coffee flavors, making each bite more complex and satisfying. Without it, the cookies can taste one-dimensional.
Use a cookie scoop for uniform size.
Evenly sized cookies bake at the same rate, preventing some from burning while others are underbaked. It also gives a professional look and consistent chewiness.
Rotate baking sheets halfway through baking.
Ovens often have hot spots. Rotating ensures cookies bake evenly, avoiding burnt edges or uneven texture.
Let cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes.
This allows them to set while still warm, preventing them from breaking apart when transferring to cooling racks. It also helps achieve that chewy texture.
Store cookies in an airtight container with a slice of bread.
The bread releases moisture that keeps the cookies soft longer. Without it, cookies can harden and lose their chewy texture over time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold melted butter straight from the microwave

Why it happens People often melt butter quickly and add it immediately without letting it cool, which can cook the eggs or cause the dough texture to be off.

Fix Allow melted butter to cool to just warm before mixing with eggs. This prevents scrambling the eggs and ensures smooth, even dough.

Overmixing the dough after adding flour

Why it happens In a rush to combine ingredients, some mix vigorously, developing too much gluten which toughens cookies.

Fix Fold dry ingredients gently until just combined. Stop mixing as soon as no flour streaks remain for tender cookies.

Skipping the salt on top

Why it happens People may forget or think salt isn’t needed, missing out on flavor contrast.

Fix Sprinkle a light pinch of flaky sea salt on each cookie dough ball before baking to enhance flavor.

Not spacing cookies properly on the baking sheet

Why it happens Sometimes dough balls are placed too close, causing cookies to merge while baking.

Fix Leave at least 2 inches between dough balls to allow spreading and even baking.

Baking too long for crispy cookies

Why it happens People often bake until cookies look fully firm, which dries them out.

Fix Remove cookies when edges are golden but centers still look soft and slightly underbaked for perfect chewiness.

Variations

Nutty Chocolate Chip Cookies

Add 1 cup chopped toasted walnuts or pecans to the dough for extra crunch and flavor. Nuts complement the chocolate and coffee notes beautifully, making the cookies heartier.

Note: Fold nuts in gently with chocolate chips. Toasting nuts beforehand enhances their aroma and prevents them from going soggy in the dough.

Double Chocolate Cookies

Replace 1/4 cup of the flour with unsweetened cocoa powder for an intense chocolate base. The espresso powder intensifies the cocoa flavor even more, perfect for chocoholics.

Note: Sift cocoa powder with flour to avoid lumps and adjust baking time slightly as cocoa can dry the dough.

Vegan Chewy Chocolate Cookies

Use coconut oil instead of butter, flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg), and dairy-free chocolate chips. The cookies remain chewy and flavorful with a subtle coconut undertone.

Note: Chill the dough longer (about 1 hour) to help it firm up since coconut oil melts faster.

Spiced Chocolate Chip Cookies

Add 1 tsp ground cinnamon and 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper to the dry ingredients for a warm, spicy kick that pairs surprisingly well with coffee and chocolate flavors.

Note: Mix spices thoroughly with flour to ensure even distribution and balanced flavor.

Salted Caramel Chocolate Chip Cookies

Fold in 1/2 cup chopped soft caramel candies along with chocolate chips. The caramel melts slightly when baking, creating gooey pockets that contrast with the sea salt topping.

Note: Watch baking time carefully as caramel can burn; reduce by 1-2 minutes if needed.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep away from direct sunlight or heat sources to prevent melting chocolate and drying out. Covering tightly preserves moisture and chewiness.

Refrigerator

Cookies can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 7 days. The cooler temperature will firm them up, so it’s best to let them come to room temperature before serving for soft texture. Reheating slightly helps restore softness.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Wrap well to avoid freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature or warm gently in the oven to revive freshness.

Reheating

Warm cookies in a preheated oven at 300°F (150°C) for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 10-15 seconds. Adding a small piece of bread in the container during reheating can help keep them moist. Avoid overheating which can dry cookies out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough 1-2 days ahead and store tightly wrapped in the fridge. This resting time improves flavor and texture. Scoop and bake fresh on the day you want to serve for best results.

FAQ

Can I substitute regular brewed coffee for espresso powder?
Using brewed coffee adds liquid to the dough and can change texture, making cookies softer or cakier. Espresso powder is preferred because it dissolves evenly without affecting moisture. If you want coffee flavor, add a small amount of instant espresso powder instead.
Why are my cookies spreading too much?
This usually happens if the dough is too warm or if there’s too much butter. Chill the dough before baking and measure ingredients carefully. Also, avoid using low protein flour or substituting melted butter with oil, which can cause excessive spreading.
How do I get chewy cookies instead of crispy?
Chewiness comes from the right balance of sugars, melted butter, and baking time. Use both brown and granulated sugar, melt the butter, and bake until edges are set but centers are still soft. Overbaking leads to crisp cookies.
Can I use salted butter instead of unsalted?
You can, but reduce or omit the added salt in the recipe to avoid oversalting. Unsalted butter gives you better control over the final flavor, which is important with the sea salt topping.
What’s the best way to store leftover cookies?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature with a slice of bread to retain moisture. For longer storage, freeze them properly wrapped. Avoid refrigeration unless necessary, as it can dry cookies out.
Can I make the dough ahead and freeze it?
Yes! Scoop the dough into balls and freeze on a baking sheet, then transfer to a bag. Bake from frozen, adding a couple extra minutes to baking time. This is great for fresh cookies anytime.
Why do my cookies sometimes turn out cakey?
Cakey cookies often result from too much flour, too little fat, or overmixing. Measure flour carefully and avoid packing it. Melted butter helps chewiness, so stick to the recipe’s fat amounts and mix gently.
Is it okay to use chocolate chunks instead of chips?
Absolutely! Chunks create bigger pockets of melted chocolate and a different texture. Just make sure they’re evenly distributed so you get chocolate in every bite.
