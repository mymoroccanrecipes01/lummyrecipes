Crispy Air Fryer Veggie Pasta That’s Quick and Delicious

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 29, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This veggie-packed pasta gets perfectly crispy in the air fryer, giving it a fun twist on a classic. I love how the herbs sing through and it’s ready in under 45 minutes—great for busy weeknights or casual weekends!

Introduction

I first stumbled on this air fryer veggie pasta recipe when I was craving something comforting but lighter than my usual creamy sauces. What really hooked me was how the air fryer crisps up the pasta edges and veggies, giving this simple dish a delightful texture you don’t usually get with boiled pasta. I’ve tried baking and skillet methods before, but nothing quite matches the quick crispiness the air fryer delivers. Plus, it’s so easy to customize with whatever fresh veggies you have on hand. The trick is to toss the cooked pasta with olive oil, herbs, and garlic before air frying it, which locks in flavor and helps everything brown beautifully. When it’s done, the pasta has these little crunchy bits alongside tender veggies, and the herbs add a fresh pop that makes it feel homemade and special. It’s perfect for a weeknight dinner when you want something quick but satisfying, or even for a cozy weekend meal when you want to impress without fuss. I love serving it with a sprinkle of Parmesan and fresh basil on top, but it’s just as good dairy-free. Once you try this, you’ll find yourself reaching for the air fryer for pasta nights more often than not!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 8 oz dried penne pasta
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the penne pasta according to package instructions until al dente, usually about 9-11 minutes. Don’t overcook it here because the pasta will cook a bit more in the air fryer and you want it to stay firm to get that nice crispy texture. Drain the pasta and set aside.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be tender but still firm when bitten
Step 2
While the pasta cooks, prepare your veggies by halving the cherry tomatoes, dicing the zucchini and red bell pepper, and mincing the garlic. Fresh, evenly sized pieces help everything cook uniformly in the air fryer and prevent mushy spots.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Veggies should be cleanly cut and ready to toss
Step 3
In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked pasta, cherry tomatoes, zucchini, red bell pepper, and minced garlic. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle the dried oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Toss everything together gently but thoroughly so each piece is coated with oil and herbs. This step is key to infusing flavor and ensuring the pasta crisps up nicely.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Everything should be evenly coated and fragrant
Step 4
Preheat your air fryer to 375°F (190°C). Once ready, transfer the pasta and veggie mixture into the air fryer basket in an even layer. Avoid overcrowding to allow hot air to circulate and crisp up the pasta and veggies evenly.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Air fryer should be fully preheated before adding food
Step 5
Air fry the mixture for about 15 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through to move everything around. This helps prevent burning on one side and promotes even browning. Watch closely during the last few minutes to get the perfect golden crisp without drying out the veggies.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta edges will be golden and crispy; veggies slightly softened but not mushy
Step 6
Carefully remove the basket and transfer the crispy pasta and veggies to a serving dish. If you like, sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the top while still warm so it melts slightly and adds a savory depth to the dish.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheese should melt gently over warm pasta
Step 7
Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a bright, herbaceous finish that complements the crispy texture. The fresh basil adds a lovely aroma and a pop of color that makes this simple dish look extra special.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Fresh basil leaves look vibrant and fresh
Step 8
Serve immediately while warm and crispy. This dish is great on its own or paired with a simple green salad or crusty bread to round out the meal. Leftovers can be reheated but are best enjoyed fresh for maximum crispness.
⏱ Time: Serve now
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be warm and crispy when served
Step 9
Clean your air fryer basket promptly once cooled to avoid any stuck-on bits from the olive oil and cheese. Regular cleaning keeps your air fryer working efficiently and prevents flavors from carrying over to future recipes.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Basket is clean and ready for next use

Pro Tips

Cook pasta just until al dente before air frying.
Al dente pasta retains a slight firmness that prevents it from becoming mushy after air frying. Overcooked pasta will break down and become too soft, ruining the desired crispy texture. This also ensures the pasta finishes cooking perfectly in the air fryer while crisping up.
Toss pasta and veggies evenly with olive oil and herbs.
Even coating guarantees consistent browning and flavor infusion. Olive oil promotes the Maillard reaction, giving the pasta its golden crisp edges and enhancing the aroma. Uneven coating can lead to some pieces burning while others remain soggy.
Avoid overcrowding the air fryer basket.
Good air circulation is essential for crisping. Overcrowding traps steam and causes uneven cooking, resulting in soggy pasta and veggies. Spreading the mixture in a single layer lets hot air reach all surfaces evenly.
Shake the basket halfway through cooking.
Shaking moves the pasta and veggies around, preventing hot spots and ensuring all sides brown evenly. Neglecting this step often leads to uneven crispiness and burnt edges.
Add fresh basil only after air frying.
Fresh basil loses its bright flavor and color when exposed to high heat. Adding it at the end preserves its aroma and vibrant appearance, enhancing the dish’s freshness.
Use firm, fresh vegetables cut into similar sizes.
Uniform pieces cook evenly and maintain texture. Soft or oversized pieces can overcook or remain undercooked, disrupting the dish’s balance of crispy and tender bites.
Clean the air fryer basket immediately after it cools.
Olive oil and cheese residues can stick and burn if left to dry, affecting future cooking and flavors. Prompt cleaning maintains your air fryer’s performance and hygiene.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking pasta before air frying

Why it happens Many assume pasta should be fully cooked before any additional cooking steps, leading to mushy results after air frying.

Fix Cook pasta just until al dente so it retains firmness. This way, when it crisps in the air fryer, it maintains a pleasant texture instead of falling apart.

Using too little or no oil

Why it happens Some think air frying is healthier without oil, but oil is crucial for crisping and flavor development.

Fix Make sure to toss pasta and veggies with enough olive oil to coat evenly. This promotes browning and prevents dryness or burning.

Overcrowding the air fryer basket

Why it happens Trying to cook everything at once to save time can trap steam and stop crisping.

Fix Cook in batches if needed, spreading ingredients in a single layer for proper air circulation and even crisping.

Adding fresh herbs too early

Why it happens Fresh herbs lose flavor and color when exposed to high heat for too long.

Fix Use dried herbs during cooking and add fresh herbs as a garnish after air frying for best taste and appearance.

Not shaking the basket midway

Why it happens Without shaking, some pieces burn while others remain uncooked.

Fix Shake the basket halfway through cooking to redistribute the pasta and veggies, ensuring even crisping and browning.

Variations

Spicy Air Fryer Veggie Pasta

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes and a dash of smoked paprika to the herb mix for a smoky, spicy kick. Swap the Parmesan for a sprinkle of nutritional yeast to keep it vegan while adding umami flavor. Expect a bolder, warming flavor profile with the same crispy texture.

Note: Spices should be evenly mixed with the oil to avoid burning. The cooking time remains the same.

Creamy Air Fryer Veggie Pasta

After air frying, toss the pasta with a spoonful of cream cheese or ricotta and a splash of pasta water for a creamy finish. This variation softens the crispy edges slightly but adds a luscious, comforting texture perfect for cooler days.

Note: Add the creaminess off heat to maintain some crispness and avoid curdling.

Gluten-Free Air Fryer Pasta

Use your favorite gluten-free pasta and adjust cooking time slightly based on package instructions. This version keeps the crispy texture but caters to gluten sensitivities. Fresh veggies and herbs remain the same, keeping it fresh and tasty.

Note: Gluten-free pasta can be more delicate, so watch closely during air frying to prevent over-crisping.

Air Fryer Pesto Veggie Pasta

Toss the pasta and veggies with pesto instead of dried herbs before air frying. The basil and pine nut flavors caramelize beautifully, giving an aromatic, savory twist. Add fresh cherry tomatoes after air frying to keep their juiciness.

Note: Pesto’s oil content helps crisping but can brown faster; reduce air frying time by 2-3 minutes.

Mediterranean Air Fryer Veggie Pasta

Add chopped kalamata olives, sun-dried tomatoes, and crumbled feta after air frying for a Mediterranean flair. The salty, tangy additions complement the crispy pasta and fresh herbs wonderfully. Serve with a lemon wedge for brightness.

Note: Add olives and feta after cooking to preserve their texture and flavor.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This pasta can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate leftovers promptly. Cover with a clean cloth or lid to avoid drying out.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The crispiness will soften in the fridge, so reheat in the air fryer or oven to restore texture. Cold leftovers can be enjoyed but are less crispy.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 1 month. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain best texture.

Reheating

Reheat in the air fryer at 350°F for 5-7 minutes to restore crispness. Alternatively, warm in an oven at 375°F for 10 minutes. Avoid microwaving as it makes the pasta soggy. Add a splash of olive oil if it seems dry.

Make Ahead

Cook and toss the pasta and veggies with oil and herbs up to 1 day ahead. Store in the fridge uncovered to dry slightly, then air fry just before serving to get fresh crispiness.

FAQ

Can I use fresh herbs instead of dried?
Fresh herbs added before air frying tend to burn and lose their flavor due to the high heat. I recommend using dried herbs during cooking for even flavor distribution and adding fresh herbs as a garnish after air frying to keep their vibrant taste and color.
What if I don’t have an air fryer?
You can achieve a similar effect by spreading the pasta and veggies on a baking sheet and roasting in a preheated oven at 400°F for about 15-20 minutes, tossing halfway through. It won’t be quite as crispy, but still delicious.
Can I use other pasta shapes?
Yes! Shorter, sturdier shapes like rigatoni, farfalle, or fusilli work best because they crisp nicely and hold the veggies well. Thin or delicate pasta may overcook or break apart during air frying.
How do I avoid soggy pasta?
Make sure to cook the pasta al dente, use enough oil, and avoid overcrowding the air fryer basket. These steps allow the pasta to crisp up rather than steam and become soggy.
Can I add protein to this dish?
Absolutely! Cooked chickpeas, tofu cubes, or shredded mozzarella added after air frying can boost protein. If adding raw protein, cook it separately first to ensure it’s fully done.
Is this recipe vegan?
It’s naturally vegetarian. To make it vegan, skip the Parmesan or use a plant-based cheese alternative. The recipe works great without cheese and still tastes flavorful and satisfying.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Keep leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in the air fryer or oven to regain crispness. Avoid microwaving to prevent sogginess.
Can I make this gluten-free?
Yes, just use your favorite gluten-free pasta. Cooking times may vary slightly, so watch closely during air frying to avoid over-crisping or drying out.
