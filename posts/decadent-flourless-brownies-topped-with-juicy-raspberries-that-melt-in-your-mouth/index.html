Rich and Fudgy Flourless Brownies with Fresh Raspberries

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

These flourless brownies are insanely fudgy and rich, made with dark chocolate for depth. Fresh raspberries add a tart pop that balances the sweetness. Perfect for weekend dessert sharing or special occasions!

Introduction

I first tried flourless brownies years ago when a friend brought some over to a gathering. They were so rich and fudgy, unlike any brownie I'd had before. Since then, I've tweaked the recipe to get that perfect texture—really dense but still tender. The trick I learned is to melt the chocolate and butter gently together, then fold the dry ingredients carefully to keep that fudgy bite. Adding fresh raspberries on top is my favorite part; their tartness cuts through the richness beautifully. These brownies are my go-to when I want a dessert that feels indulgent but isn’t complicated. I’ve served them at weekend get-togethers, and they always disappear fast. If you love chocolate and want something gluten-free that doesn’t skimp on flavor, I think you’ll really enjoy these. They come out glossy on top with a soft, almost gooey center that’s incredibly satisfying. Plus, the fresh raspberries add a lovely pop of color and brightness. I promise, once you try this recipe, it’ll become a staple in your baking rotation too!

Why This Works

Flourless brownies rely heavily on the balance between eggs, chocolate, and fat to achieve their signature dense and fudgy texture. Unlike traditional brownies, there's no flour to provide structure via gluten, so the eggs act as a binding agent and contribute moisture and richness. The high-quality dark chocolate melts smoothly with butter, creating a luscious base that’s both flavorful and silky. Gently folding in cocoa powder instead of stirring aggressively helps maintain air pockets, preventing the brownies from becoming too dense or rubbery.

Temperature control during melting and baking is crucial. Melting chocolate over low heat prevents it from seizing, which can cause graininess and ruin the texture. Baking at a moderate temperature allows the edges to set while keeping the center slightly underbaked, resulting in that gooey, fudgy consistency. Cooling the brownies completely before slicing helps them firm up, making clean cuts easier and preserving the soft interior.

Fresh raspberries provide a natural acidity and juiciness that contrast with the chocolate’s sweetness and richness. This balance not only enhances flavor but also adds a refreshing element that prevents the dessert from feeling overly heavy. The raspberries also add visual appeal, making the brownies look as good as they taste.

Overall, this recipe succeeds because it carefully balances ingredient ratios, employs gentle mixing techniques, and controls baking time to produce a rich, moist, and deeply chocolatey treat that’s naturally gluten-free and perfectly complemented by fresh fruit.

  • Choose a high-quality dark chocolate with at least 60-70% cocoa content for a deep, rich flavor and smooth melting properties. Avoid chocolate chips as they contain stabilizers that can affect texture. Chop the chocolate finely for even melting and combine with butter gently to prevent overheating and seizing.
  • Use unsalted butter so you can control the salt content precisely. Butter adds richness and helps create a smooth, glossy batter. It also contributes to the fudgy texture by adding fat that coats the cocoa particles. Melt it slowly with the chocolate to avoid separating or burning.
  • Eggs are essential for structure and moisture in flourless brownies. Use large eggs at room temperature for better emulsification with the melted chocolate and butter. Adding them one at a time while whisking ensures a smooth, cohesive batter that traps air for lightness without compromising fudginess.
  • Use unsweetened, natural cocoa powder for intense chocolate flavor and to deepen the color. Sifting before adding prevents lumps and makes folding easier. Cocoa powder also absorbs some moisture, helping balance the batter's consistency to avoid being too wet or crumbly.
  • Choose ripe but firm raspberries to avoid excess juice that can make the brownies soggy. Their natural tartness cuts through the rich chocolate and adds a refreshing brightness. Add them just before serving to maintain their texture and vibrant appearance.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces high-quality dark chocolate, chopped
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, sifted
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries, for topping

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Line an 8-inch square baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides to lift the brownies out easily later. This step ensures even baking and easy removal, which is important because flourless brownies are delicate and fudgy. Avoid skipping the parchment or greasing only, as it can cause sticking and uneven edges.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven fully preheated and pan properly prepared with parchment paper lining
  2. Step 2

    Place the chopped dark chocolate and butter in a heatproof bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler). Stir occasionally until melted and smooth. This gentle melting prevents the chocolate from burning or seizing, which would ruin the texture. If you rush or use direct heat, the chocolate can become grainy.

    ⏱ 8 minutes✓ Chocolate and butter mixture is glossy, smooth, and fully melted without lumps
  3. Step 3

    Remove the melted chocolate and butter mixture from heat. Stir in the sugar while the mixture is still warm but not hot. This helps dissolve the sugar slightly, contributing to a smoother brownie texture. If the mixture is too hot, adding sugar can cause graininess, so let it cool a bit.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Sugar is well incorporated, mixture looks glossy and slightly thickened
  4. Step 4

    Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. This step is crucial for incorporating air and achieving the right consistency. If eggs are added too quickly or all at once, the batter may not emulsify properly, leading to a dense or uneven texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is smooth, thick, and slightly shiny with no visible egg streaks
  5. Step 5

    Mix in the vanilla extract and salt. These small additions enhance the chocolate flavor and balance the sweetness. Salt especially is key in chocolate baking to bring out depth and prevent flatness in taste.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Batter evenly combined with vanilla and salt, aroma deepens
  6. Step 6

    Sift the cocoa powder over the batter and gently fold it in with a spatula. Folding rather than stirring vigorously preserves the air bubbles and avoids overmixing, which can toughen the brownies. This step ensures your brownies stay tender and fudgy.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is uniform in color, thick but smooth without lumps
  7. Step 7

    Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. This even surface helps the brownies bake uniformly. Avoid tapping the pan too hard, which can cause air bubbles to escape and affect texture.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter evenly spread with a smooth, glossy surface
  8. Step 8

    Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes by gently shaking the pan; the edges should be set and the center just slightly jiggly but not wet. Overbaking dries them out, while underbaking leaves them too gooey to slice cleanly.

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ Edges firm and pulling away slightly from pan, center jiggles gently when shaken
  9. Step 9

    Let the brownies cool completely in the pan on a wire rack before lifting out and slicing. Cooling allows the fudgy center to set for neat slices. Cutting while warm will cause crumbling and sticky mess.

    ⏱ 60 minutes✓ Brownies are firm to touch, no longer warm, and hold shape when sliced
  10. Step 10

    Just before serving, scatter fresh raspberries over the brownies. The tartness of the raspberries complements the deep chocolate flavor perfectly, adding freshness and a pop of color. Serve as is or with a dusting of powdered sugar if you like.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Raspberries look fresh and vibrant atop the brownies, ready to enjoy

Pro Tips

  • Melt chocolate and butter slowly over simmering water to avoid burning. — Chocolate is very sensitive to heat; direct high heat can cause it to seize or become grainy. Using a double boiler ensures gentle, even melting, preserving the smooth texture essential for fudgy brownies.
  • Add eggs one at a time, whisking thoroughly after each addition. — This helps the batter emulsify properly, trapping air and creating a smooth, stable mixture. Rushing this step can cause the batter to curdle or separate, resulting in a less tender brownie.
  • Fold in cocoa powder gently after sifting to avoid overmixing. — Overmixing develops structure that flour typically provides, which flourless brownies lack. Gentle folding maintains air bubbles that keep the texture tender and prevents toughness.
  • Don’t overbake; remove brownies when the center is still slightly jiggly. — Overbaking dries out these brownies, which should be fudgy and moist inside. The residual heat finishes cooking as they cool, ensuring perfect gooeyness without being raw.
  • Let brownies cool completely before slicing for clean cuts. — Cutting warm brownies causes them to crumble and stick. Cooling allows the fudgy center to set, making slices neater and easier to serve.
  • Use parchment paper with overhang for easy removal from pan. — This prevents sticking and allows you to lift the brownies out intact, preserving edges and shape for better presentation.
  • Add fresh raspberries just before serving to keep them fresh. — Raspberries release juice quickly, which can make brownies soggy if added too early. Adding them at the end preserves their texture and bright flavor contrast.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overbaking the brownies
    Why: Many assume brownies should be fully set like cakes, but flourless brownies need to stay slightly underbaked to maintain their fudgy texture.
    Fix: Start checking the brownies a few minutes before the minimum bake time. Look for firm edges and a center that jiggles slightly. Remove immediately to prevent drying out.
  • Using low-quality chocolate or chocolate chips
    Why: Some think any chocolate will do, but lower-quality or chip chocolate contains stabilizers that affect melting and texture.
    Fix: Invest in good-quality baking chocolate or bars with high cocoa content. Chop finely and melt gently for a smooth, rich batter.
  • Skipping parchment paper or not greasing the pan
    Why: Assuming brownies won’t stick or forgetting to prep the pan can cause sticking and broken edges.
    Fix: Always line the pan with parchment paper and leave an overhang to lift brownies out easily. Lightly grease the paper if desired.
  • Adding eggs all at once
    Why: Rushing the mixing process can cause the batter to curdle or separate.
    Fix: Add eggs one at a time, whisking well after each to create a smooth, cohesive batter that traps air.
  • Mixing cocoa powder too vigorously
    Why: Some stir too aggressively, developing unwanted toughness in the batter.
    Fix: Sift cocoa powder first and fold gently with a spatula just until combined to keep the brownies tender.

Variations

  • Nutty Flourless Brownies: Add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans folded into the batter before baking for a crunchy texture and nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with the rich chocolate.
    Ensure nuts are evenly distributed but fold gently to maintain batter airiness; baking time remains the same.
  • Spiced Chocolate Brownies: Mix 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper into the cocoa powder for a warm, spicy twist that adds depth and a subtle kick to the brownies.
    Add spices with cocoa powder during folding; watch for flavor balance so spice doesn’t overpower chocolate.
  • Vegan Flourless Brownies: Replace eggs with flax eggs (3 tablespoons ground flaxseed mixed with 9 tablespoons water) and use dairy-free butter or coconut oil to make these brownies vegan-friendly without sacrificing fudginess.
    Flax eggs may slightly change texture and baking time; check doneness a bit earlier and expect a denser brownie.
  • Salted Caramel Brownies: Drizzle homemade or store-bought salted caramel sauce on top after baking and sprinkle with flaky sea salt for a decadent sweet-and-salty flavor combo.
    Add caramel once brownies have cooled; do not bake with caramel on top to prevent burning.
  • Raspberry Swirl Brownies: Swirl fresh raspberry puree into the brownie batter before baking to infuse the whole batch with tart fruit flavor and create a beautiful marbled effect.
    Gently swirl puree with a knife after pouring batter into pan; avoid overmixing to preserve texture and appearance.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store brownies covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them in an airtight container or wrapped tightly to prevent drying out. Avoid placing raspberries on top until serving to keep them fresh.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Brownies may firm up and become slightly denser when chilled. Bring to room temperature or warm gently before serving for best texture. Add fresh raspberries just before serving.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze brownies without raspberries for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge, then bring to room temperature before serving. Fresh raspberries should be added after thawing.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm brownies in a low oven (300°F) for 5-10 minutes or microwave briefly (15-20 seconds) to soften. Add a splash of milk or cream if desired to restore moisture. Avoid overheating, which can dry them out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can bake the brownies 1-2 days ahead and store them wrapped at room temperature or refrigerated. Add fresh raspberries just before serving to keep them bright and juicy. This makes entertaining easy and stress-free.

FAQ

Can I use milk chocolate instead of dark chocolate?
Milk chocolate has more sugar and milk solids, which can affect the texture and make the brownies sweeter and less fudgy. If you use milk chocolate, reduce added sugar slightly and expect a softer, less intense chocolate flavor.
Why do my flourless brownies crack on top?
A crackly top is normal and often desirable in brownies. It forms when the sugar and egg proteins set during baking. To avoid excessive cracking, don’t overbake and fold the batter gently to maintain moisture.
Can I substitute the raspberries with other fruits?
Absolutely! Fresh strawberries, blackberries, or even a drizzle of cherry sauce work well. Choose fruits with some tartness to balance the rich chocolate, and add them just before serving to keep freshness.
My brownies came out dry; what went wrong?
Dry brownies usually mean overbaking or too much cocoa powder. Make sure to check doneness early and use the exact measurements. Also, folding gently helps keep moisture locked in.
Can I make these brownies gluten-free safe?
Yes! Since this recipe contains no flour, it’s naturally gluten-free. Just ensure your cocoa powder and chocolate are certified gluten-free to avoid cross-contamination.
How do I store leftover brownies with raspberries?
If you’ve already added raspberries, store leftovers in the fridge and eat within 1-2 days since the fruit can get mushy. For longer storage, remove raspberries and add fresh ones when serving.
Can I double this recipe?
Yes, but use a larger pan or two pans to keep the same thickness. Baking time may increase slightly, so check for doneness carefully to avoid overbaking.
Is it okay to use salted butter?
You can, but reduce or omit the additional salt in the recipe to avoid over-salting. Unsalted butter gives you better control over the seasoning.
