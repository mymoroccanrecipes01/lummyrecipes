Quick and Easy 5-Ingredient Chocolate Cake for Cozy Weeknights

This chocolate cake is my go-to when I need a quick homemade dessert without fuss. Just five simple ingredients come together for a rich, moist treat that's ready in under an hour. Perfect for those cozy weeknights!

Pro Tips

Always sift your dry ingredients together before mixing.
Sifting aerates the flour and cocoa powder, removing lumps and ensuring a smooth batter. This step helps the cake rise evenly and prevents pockets of dry ingredients that can cause uneven texture.
Use room temperature milk for the best batter consistency.
Cold milk can cause the batter to seize or mix unevenly, which interferes with the baking powder’s leavening action. Room temperature ingredients combine more smoothly, producing a tender crumb.
Don’t overmix the batter once liquids are added.
Overmixing develops gluten strands in the flour, which makes the cake tough and dense instead of tender and moist. Stir just until ingredients are combined for the best texture.
Line your pan with parchment paper for easy removal.
Greasing alone sometimes isn’t enough to prevent sticking, especially with chocolate cakes. Parchment paper ensures the cake releases cleanly and keeps the sides intact.
Avoid opening the oven door during the first 20 minutes of baking.
Opening the door lets out heat and can cause the cake to sink or bake unevenly. Trust the timing and check only near the end for doneness.
Cool the cake in the pan before turning out.
Cooling helps the cake set and firm up, reducing the risk of breaking apart. Turning out too soon can cause the cake to crumble.
Use a toothpick test to check doneness.
Insert a toothpick into the center; if it comes out clean or with just moist crumbs, the cake is done. This prevents overbaking and drying out the cake.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Many cooks think mixing longer will make the batter smoother, but overmixing develops gluten and results in a tough cake.

Fix Mix just until the dry ingredients are moistened. Stop when you no longer see flour streaks. Use gentle folding motions rather than vigorous stirring.

Using cold milk directly from the fridge

Why it happens It’s easy to forget to warm milk, but cold milk shocks the batter and can cause uneven mixing and poor rise.

Fix Let milk sit out for 15-20 minutes before mixing or warm slightly (not hot) to room temperature.

Not greasing or lining the pan properly

Why it happens Skipping this step leads to sticking and broken cakes when removing from the pan.

Fix Grease the pan thoroughly and line the bottom with parchment paper for smooth release.

Opening the oven door too early

Why it happens Impatience can cause the cake to sink due to temperature fluctuations.

Fix Resist opening the door for at least the first 20 minutes. Use the oven light to peek if needed.

Using expired baking powder

Why it happens Old baking powder loses its leavening power, resulting in a flat cake.

Fix Test baking powder by mixing a teaspoon in hot water; it should fizz vigorously. Replace if no reaction.

Variations

Add Espresso for a Mocha Twist

Mix 1 teaspoon of instant espresso powder into the dry ingredients for a subtle coffee flavor that enhances the chocolate’s richness. This adds depth without overpowering the cake’s classic taste.

Note: Espresso powder dissolves easily and won’t affect batter texture; just fold it in with the dry ingredients.

Substitute Milk with Almond Milk for Dairy-Free

Replace regular milk with unsweetened almond milk to make this cake dairy-free. The texture remains moist, though the flavor will be slightly nuttier and lighter.

Note: Ensure the almond milk is at room temperature and unsweetened to avoid altering sweetness or baking chemistry.

Add Chocolate Chips for Extra Gooeyness

Fold in 1/2 cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips before baking for melty pockets of chocolate throughout the cake. This adds texture and an indulgent surprise.

Note: Coat chips lightly with flour before folding in to prevent sinking to the bottom during baking.

Use Greek Yogurt Instead of Milk for Tangy Moisture

Swap the milk for an equal amount of plain Greek yogurt to boost moisture and add a slight tang. This variation yields a denser but super moist cake.

Note: Mix yogurt with a splash of water to thin it slightly for easier blending with dry ingredients.

Top with Fresh Berries and Whipped Cream

Serve the cooled cake topped with fresh raspberries or strawberries and a dollop of whipped cream for a bright, fresh contrast to the rich chocolate.

Note: Add toppings just before serving to keep berries fresh and whipped cream fluffy.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This chocolate cake can be kept at room temperature, covered tightly with plastic wrap or in a cake dome, for up to 2 days. Keep it away from direct sunlight or heat to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The cake may firm up and lose some moisture when chilled, so bring to room temperature before serving or warm briefly in the microwave.

Freezer

Wrap the cooled cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or a few hours at room temperature. Reheat gently to restore softness.

Reheating

Warm slices in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk or covering with foil helps keep it moist. Avoid overheating to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a few hours ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake just before serving for freshest results. Alternatively, bake the cake a day ahead, store well, and add toppings right before serving.

FAQ

Can I substitute baking powder with baking soda?
Baking powder and baking soda aren’t interchangeable one-to-one. Baking soda needs an acidic ingredient to activate, which this recipe lacks, so using it alone would result in poor rise and a soapy taste. Stick to baking powder for best results.
What if I don’t have cocoa powder?
Cocoa powder is essential for the chocolate flavor and texture. You could try melted chocolate as a substitute, but it will alter the batter’s consistency and may require recipe adjustments. I recommend keeping cocoa powder on hand for this cake.
How do I know when the cake is fully baked?
The best test is the toothpick method: insert a toothpick in the center, and if it comes out clean or with moist crumbs (not wet batter), the cake is done. Also, the cake should pull slightly away from the pan edges and feel springy to touch.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes! Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend designed for baking. Make sure it contains xanthan gum or add it separately to help with structure. Baking time might vary slightly, so watch closely.
Why is my cake dense or dry?
Common causes include overmixing the batter, incorrect oven temperature, or expired baking powder. Overmixing develops gluten making it dense; too hot an oven dries it out; old leavening agents prevent proper rise. Follow the recipe closely and use fresh ingredients.
Can I add frosting or glaze?
Absolutely! This cake pairs beautifully with chocolate ganache, cream cheese frosting, or a simple powdered sugar glaze. Just make sure the cake is completely cooled before adding any topping to prevent melting.
Is this cake suitable for freezing?
Yes, it freezes well when wrapped tightly. Freeze after cooling completely, thaw in the fridge or at room temperature, then reheat gently if desired. Avoid freezing with frosting on, as it can affect texture.
Can I double the recipe?
You can double the ingredients and bake in a larger pan or two pans. Adjust baking time accordingly—larger pans may need longer baking. Check doneness with a toothpick frequently to avoid overbaking.

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup milk, room temperature

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8-inch round cake pan with butter or non-stick spray. This step ensures your cake doesn’t stick and helps it bake evenly. I like to line the bottom with parchment paper for an extra easy release. Getting the oven temperature right is crucial because too hot will dry out the cake, and too cool means it won’t rise properly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is well greased and parchment paper fits snugly
Step 2
In a large bowl, sift together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, and baking powder. Sifting helps break up any lumps, especially in the cocoa powder, giving you a smoother batter. Mix the dry ingredients gently so you don’t lose the lightness, which contributes to the cake’s tender crumb.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are combined evenly with no clumps
Step 3
Add the room temperature milk to the dry ingredients. Using room temperature milk helps everything combine smoothly without shocking the batter, which can affect texture. Stir gently with a spatula or wooden spoon until just combined—overmixing develops gluten and makes the cake dense.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no streaks of flour but not overly mixed
Step 4
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Smoothing ensures even cooking and a nice flat top for serving. Don’t tap the pan aggressively; a gentle shake to even out the batter is enough to release any large air bubbles.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread and surface smooth
Step 5
Bake the cake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes. Avoid opening the oven door early, which can cause the cake to sink. The cake is done when a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs attached.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick test passes with clean or slightly moist crumbs
Step 6
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan on a wire rack for 10-15 minutes. Cooling in the pan helps it set and prevents it from breaking apart when removed. After that, run a knife around the edges to loosen before turning it out onto the rack.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake feels firm to the touch and starts to pull away from pan sides
Step 7
Invert the cake onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing. This keeps the bottom from becoming soggy and helps the crumb finish setting. Patience here pays off with a better texture and easier slicing.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is completely cool to touch
Step 8
Once cooled, dust with powdered sugar or top with your favorite frosting or fresh berries if desired. This step is optional but adds a lovely finishing touch. If frosting, make sure the cake is fully cooled to prevent melting.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Decoration evenly applied with no melting or sliding
Step 9
Slice and serve with a glass of milk or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra cozy treat. The cake’s moist texture pairs wonderfully with creamy sides. Enjoy immediately or store properly for later.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Slices hold shape and cake remains moist
