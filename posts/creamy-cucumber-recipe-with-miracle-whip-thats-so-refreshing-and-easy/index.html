Creamy Cucumber Salad with a Tangy Miracle Whip Twist

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 28, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This cucumber salad is refreshingly creamy with a zesty Miracle Whip dressing that brightens up the summer table. Crunchy cucumbers tossed in a tangy, slightly sweet sauce make it a perfect side for any casual meal or potluck.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this creamy cucumber salad recipe during a summer potluck a few years back, and it instantly became a favorite in my family. What makes this version stand out is the use of Miracle Whip instead of traditional mayonnaise. It adds a tangy, slightly sweet kick that perfectly balances the crisp cucumbers. I love how easy it is to whip up in just minutes, yet it feels fresh and special enough to serve alongside everything from grilled chicken to burgers. One little trick I learned is to slice the cucumbers thinly and salt them first to draw out excess moisture, so the salad never turns watery. The finished dish is cool, creamy, and bright with just the right amount of zing. Honestly, it’s one of those recipes I go back to time and again when I want something simple but satisfying for summer dinners or a casual gathering.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 3 medium cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Miracle Whip
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and thinly slicing the cucumbers. You can use a mandoline slicer if you want really uniform thin slices, but a sharp knife works fine too. Once sliced, place the cucumbers in a colander and sprinkle with the salt. Toss gently and let them sit for about 10 minutes. This step is crucial because the salt draws out excess water from the cucumbers, preventing your salad from becoming watery and helping the flavors concentrate.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers will look slightly wilted and you’ll see water pooling in the colander.
Step 2
While the cucumbers are draining, prepare the dressing by combining Miracle Whip, white vinegar, sugar, black pepper, and fresh dill in a bowl. Whisk everything together until smooth and well blended. The vinegar adds brightness and a bit of acidity to cut through the creaminess of the Miracle Whip, while the sugar balances that tang with a subtle sweetness.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing should be smooth, creamy, and slightly glossy.
Step 3
After the cucumbers have drained, pat them dry with paper towels to remove any remaining moisture. This ensures your salad stays crisp and the dressing clings nicely. If you skip this drying step, your salad might turn watery quickly.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers feel dry to the touch but still crisp.
Step 4
Combine the cucumbers with the dressing in a large bowl. Toss gently but thoroughly so every slice is coated with that tangy creamy goodness. Be careful not to smash the cucumbers while mixing; you want to keep the texture light and fresh.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers are evenly coated with dressing without being mushy.
Step 5
Taste the salad and adjust seasoning if needed. Sometimes I add a little more salt or a splash more vinegar depending on how tangy I want it. This step lets you personalize the flavor before chilling.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with a mix of creamy, tangy, and slightly sweet.
Step 6
Cover the bowl and refrigerate the salad for at least 30 minutes before serving. This chilling time allows the flavors to marry and the cucumbers to soak up the dressing, making every bite deliciously flavorful.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad tastes harmonious and cool when served.
Step 7
Before serving, give the salad a gentle stir to redistribute any dressing that may have settled at the bottom. This ensures each bite is as good as the first.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dressing is evenly distributed and cucumbers look glossy.
Step 8
Serve the salad chilled as a refreshing side dish. It pairs wonderfully with grilled meats, sandwiches, or even as a light snack on its own during warm weather.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Salad remains crisp and creamy when served cold.
Step 9
If you want to prep ahead, you can slice and salt the cucumbers a day in advance, rinse and dry them, then store in an airtight container in the fridge. Mix with dressing just before serving to keep the best texture.
⏱ Time: Preparation varies
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers stay fresh and crisp when stored properly.

Pro Tips

Salt the cucumbers and let them sit for at least 10 minutes before draining and drying.
Salting draws out excess water from the cucumbers through osmosis, which prevents the salad from becoming watery and enhances the cucumber flavor. Skipping this step can leave you with a soggy, diluted salad.
Use thin slices of cucumber for the best texture and dressing coverage.
Thin slices allow the dressing to coat each piece evenly and help the cucumbers absorb flavors better. Thick slices might feel too bulky and don’t meld as well with the dressing.
Pat the cucumbers dry after salting to remove residual moisture.
Dry cucumbers help the dressing cling better and keep the salad from becoming watery. Any leftover moisture dilutes the dressing and can make the salad less creamy.
Chill the salad for at least 30 minutes before serving.
This resting period lets the flavors meld and the cucumbers soften slightly without losing their crunch, resulting in a more harmonious and refreshing dish.
Adjust the vinegar and sugar balance to suit your taste.
Everyone’s palate is different, so tasting and tweaking ensures the salad hits your preferred tangy-sweet spot. Too much vinegar can be overpowering, while too little makes it bland.
Add fresh dill for an authentic flavor boost.
Dill pairs beautifully with cucumbers and Miracle Whip, adding a fresh herbal note that elevates the salad from ordinary to memorable.
Use Miracle Whip instead of mayonnaise for a tangier dressing.
Miracle Whip contains vinegar and sugar, creating a uniquely tangy and slightly sweet profile that mayonnaise lacks. This difference is what gives the salad its signature zing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the salting step for cucumbers

Why it happens Many people want to save time and skip this step, assuming it’s unnecessary.

Fix Always salt and let cucumbers sit for at least 10 minutes to draw out moisture. Then rinse and dry thoroughly. This prevents a watery salad and intensifies flavor.

Using thick cucumber slices

Why it happens Sometimes people slice cucumbers too thickly for ease or preference.

Fix Slice cucumbers thinly to ensure they absorb dressing well and maintain a pleasant texture without overwhelming the bite.

Not drying cucumbers after salting

Why it happens It’s easy to forget or underestimate the importance of drying.

Fix Pat cucumbers dry with paper towels after salting to keep the salad from becoming watery and help dressing cling better.

Using mayonnaise instead of Miracle Whip

Why it happens Mayonnaise is a common substitute but changes the flavor profile.

Fix Use Miracle Whip for the signature tangy-sweet dressing. If you must use mayo, add a splash of vinegar and a pinch of sugar to mimic Miracle Whip’s flavor.

Serving salad immediately without chilling

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning can lead to skipping the chilling step.

Fix Refrigerate the salad for at least 30 minutes to allow flavors to meld and cucumbers to soften slightly, enhancing taste and texture.

Variations

Spicy Cucumber Salad

Add a dash of cayenne pepper or a few sliced jalapeños to the dressing for a spicy kick. This variation adds heat that pairs surprisingly well with the creamy tanginess of Miracle Whip and the cool cucumbers.

Note: Start with a small amount of spice and adjust to taste, as the heat can quickly overpower the delicate cucumber flavor.

Greek-Inspired Cucumber Salad

Swap Miracle Whip for Greek yogurt and add chopped red onions, olives, and feta cheese. This variation is tangy and creamy with Mediterranean flavors, making it a heartier side.

Note: Greek yogurt is thicker and tangier, so reduce vinegar slightly and adjust seasoning accordingly.

Herb Mix Salad

Mix fresh herbs like parsley, chives, and tarragon with dill for a more complex herbal flavor profile. This adds freshness and depth to the salad without changing the base dressing.

Note: Use finely chopped herbs and add them just before serving to keep flavors vibrant.

Vegan Version

Replace Miracle Whip with a vegan mayo alternative and use apple cider vinegar instead of white vinegar for a slightly fruitier tang. This keeps the salad creamy and flavorful while being plant-based.

Note: Check that the vegan mayo has a similar tang to Miracle Whip to maintain the characteristic flavor.

Add Crunch with Nuts

Toss in toasted sunflower seeds or chopped walnuts for added texture and a nutty note. This variation adds a satisfying crunch and a subtle flavor contrast.

Note: Add nuts just before serving to keep them crunchy and prevent sogginess.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the creamy dressing. Keep it covered and chilled until ready to serve.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. The cucumbers may release some water over time, so give the salad a gentle stir before serving. It’s best enjoyed cold and fresh.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as cucumbers become watery and lose their crisp texture upon thawing, and the dressing may separate.

Reheating

This salad is served cold and should not be reheated. If you want a warm cucumber dish, consider a different recipe.

Make Ahead

You can slice and salt cucumbers a day ahead, then rinse and dry them before storing in the fridge. Prepare the dressing separately and combine just before serving to keep the texture crisp and fresh.

FAQ

Can I use regular mayonnaise instead of Miracle Whip?
Yes, but the flavor will be milder and less tangy. To mimic Miracle Whip’s taste, add a teaspoon of vinegar and a pinch of sugar to the mayo. This helps replicate the sweet and tangy flavor profile that makes this salad stand out.
How thin should I slice the cucumbers?
Aim for slices about 1/8 inch thick. Thin slices absorb the dressing better and provide a crisp, delicate texture. Using a mandoline slicer can help achieve consistent thin slices, but a sharp knife works well too.
Can I make this salad gluten-free?
Absolutely! All the ingredients, including Miracle Whip, are gluten-free. Just double-check your brand of Miracle Whip to ensure no gluten-containing additives are included.
How long can I store leftovers?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. The cucumbers may soften and release water, so give the salad a gentle stir before serving. For best texture, consume within the first day.
Can I add other vegetables to this salad?
Yes, thinly sliced red onions or radishes work nicely for extra crunch and flavor. Add them with the cucumbers before salting to allow flavors to meld evenly.
What if I don’t have fresh dill?
You can also substitute with fresh parsley or chives for a different but still fresh flavor.
Why do I need to pat cucumbers dry after salting?
Patting dry removes excess moisture which prevents the salad from becoming watery and helps the dressing cling better to the cucumber slices. Skipping this can make the salad soggy and dilute the flavors.
Is there a vegan alternative for Miracle Whip?
Yes, many brands offer vegan mayonnaise alternatives that can substitute Miracle Whip. Look for one with a tangy flavor to keep the salad’s signature taste. You might need to adjust vinegar and sugar to taste.
