Cozy Loaded Baked Potato Casserole with Cheesy Sour Cream Goodness

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 25, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This baked potato casserole is like your favorite loaded potato, but easier and creamier. The sour cream adds a tangy richness, and sharp cheddar gives it that perfect cheesy punch. Ideal for family dinners or sharing at potlucks!

Pro Tips

Parboil potatoes just until tender but not falling apart.
Parboiling ensures the potatoes are cooked through but still firm enough to hold their shape during baking. Overcooking here leads to a mushy casserole, while undercooking results in hard potato chunks that take longer to bake.
Melt butter before mixing with milk and sour cream.
Melting butter first helps it incorporate evenly into the sauce, preventing greasy clumps and ensuring a smooth, creamy texture. Cold butter can cause separation and uneven richness.
Use freshly shredded sharp cheddar cheese.
Freshly shredded cheese melts more evenly and creates a better bubbly crust. Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can affect melting quality and texture.
Let casserole rest a few minutes after baking.
Resting allows the sauce to thicken and the casserole to set, making it easier to serve without it falling apart. It also helps flavors meld together for a better taste experience.
Add bacon and green onions just before baking.
This preserves their texture and flavor. Adding bacon too early can make it soggy, while green onions retain their fresh bite and color when baked briefly.
Season the potato mixture well with salt and pepper.
Potatoes can be bland without enough seasoning, so properly seasoning the sauce ensures every bite is flavorful and balanced, especially since sour cream and cheese add richness.
Avoid stirring the casserole too vigorously when mixing ingredients.
Gentle folding keeps the potato chunks intact, preserving texture. Overmixing breaks down the potatoes, turning the casserole into a mashed-potato texture which isn’t the intended outcome.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking potatoes in the boiling step.

Why it happens Many assume potatoes should be fully cooked before baking, but overcooking leads to mushy texture after baking.

Fix Parboil potatoes just until they’re fork-tender but still firm. This way, they finish cooking in the oven without losing their shape.

Using pre-shredded cheese instead of fresh.

Why it happens Pre-shredded cheese is convenient but contains anti-caking agents affecting melt quality.

Fix Grate your own cheese fresh for better melting and a smoother, creamier top crust.

Skipping the resting time after baking.

Why it happens Impatience to serve immediately leads to runny casserole and messy portions.

Fix Let the dish rest 5-10 minutes after baking to firm up and allow flavors to meld.

Not seasoning the potato mixture enough.

Why it happens Potatoes naturally taste bland, and relying on cheese alone isn’t enough.

Fix Add salt, pepper, and garlic powder to the sauce to ensure balanced, full flavor.

Mixing potatoes too vigorously.

Why it happens Trying to combine everything quickly can break potatoes down into mush.

Fix Fold ingredients gently to keep potato chunks intact for texture contrast.

Variations

Vegetarian Version Without Bacon

Simply omit the bacon and add extra green onions or sautéed mushrooms for a vegetarian-friendly casserole. You can also mix in some steamed broccoli for added veggies and texture. This version still delivers rich, cheesy comfort without the meat.

Note: Baking time remains the same; just ensure any added vegetables are pre-cooked to avoid sogginess.

Spicy Jalapeño Cheddar Potato Casserole

Add diced jalapeños or a dash of cayenne pepper to the sour cream mixture for a spicy kick. Swap regular cheddar for pepper jack cheese to enhance the heat and flavor. This variation is perfect if you like a little fire in your comfort food.

Note: Adjust spice level to your taste and consider reducing salt slightly if using spicy cheeses.

Loaded Potato Casserole with Chives and Sour Cream Topping

Top the casserole with extra sour cream and fresh chopped chives after baking for a fresh, tangy finish. This adds a classic loaded potato feel and a pop of green that brightens the dish visually and flavor-wise.

Note: Add toppings just before serving to maintain freshness and color.

Twice-Baked Potato Style Casserole

Bake whole potatoes first, scoop out the insides, then mix with sour cream, cheese, and toppings before baking again in a casserole dish. This method intensifies the baked potato flavor and gives a fluffier texture.

Note: Requires longer prep and bake time; watch closely to avoid drying out.

Cheesy Cauliflower Potato Casserole

Replace half the potatoes with steamed cauliflower florets for a lower-carb option that still feels indulgent. The cauliflower adds a subtle sweetness and creamy texture that pairs well with cheddar and sour cream.

Note: Steam cauliflower until just tender; baking time stays similar.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This casserole can safely sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Cover it loosely with foil or a clean towel to keep dust and insects away. Beyond 2 hours, refrigerate promptly to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The casserole may firm up and thicken in the fridge. Reheat gently in the microwave or oven to restore creaminess. Avoid reheating multiple times as texture will degrade.

Freezer

Freeze leftover casserole in a sealed freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating to maintain texture.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven covered with foil for about 20 minutes until warmed through, or microwave in short bursts stirring occasionally. Add a splash of milk or sour cream if it seems dry. Avoid overheating to prevent curdling or drying out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the potato mixture up to step 5 and refrigerate for up to 24 hours before baking. Add cheese and toppings just before baking to keep them fresh and crispy. This saves time on busy days without sacrificing flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen potatoes for this casserole?
Frozen diced potatoes aren’t ideal because they release extra water when thawed, which can make the casserole watery. If you must use frozen, thaw and drain them thoroughly, then reduce any added liquids slightly to compensate.
What cheese works best if I don’t have sharp cheddar?
You can substitute with other flavorful cheeses like gouda, Monterey Jack, or even a mild mozzarella. Just keep in mind that sharper cheeses give more punch, so milder cheeses may result in a less intense flavor.
How do I make this casserole ahead of time?
You can assemble the casserole up to the point of adding cheese and toppings, then cover and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. Add cheese and toppings fresh before baking to maintain texture and flavor.
Can I make this dairy-free or vegan?
For a dairy-free version, substitute sour cream with coconut or cashew-based sour cream alternatives and use vegan cheese. Keep in mind the flavor and texture will differ from the classic version.
Why does my casserole turn out watery sometimes?
Watery casserole often results from undercooked potatoes or too much liquid in the sauce. Make sure to parboil potatoes properly and measure liquids carefully. Also, avoid adding extra water-rich toppings before baking.
Can I add other vegetables to this casserole?
Absolutely! Steamed broccoli, sautéed mushrooms, or even corn can be stirred in for extra nutrition and texture. Just make sure any veggies are cooked or steamed beforehand to avoid excess moisture.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat leftovers covered in a 350°F oven for about 20 minutes or microwave in short intervals stirring occasionally. Adding a splash of milk or sour cream helps restore creaminess if the casserole seems dry.
Is it better to use salted or unsalted butter?
Unsalted butter is preferred so you can control the salt level precisely. If using salted butter, reduce added salt in the recipe to avoid over-seasoning.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 servings

  • 6 large russet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup sour cream, full fat
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup cooked bacon, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by peeling and dicing your russet potatoes into roughly 1-inch cubes. This size helps them cook evenly and absorb the creamy sauce without turning mushy. Place the diced potatoes in a large pot and cover with cold water to prevent them from browning. Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat and cook for about 10 minutes, or until the potatoes are just tender when pierced with a fork. Avoid overcooking here since the potatoes will continue to bake later, and overcooked potatoes can become too mushy and lose their structure.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Potatoes should be fork-tender but still hold their shape without falling apart
Step 2
While the potatoes cook, melt the butter in a small saucepan or microwave until fully liquid. Combine the melted butter with milk, sour cream, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Whisk these together until smooth and well blended. This mixture forms the creamy base of your casserole, and melting the butter first helps it incorporate evenly, preventing greasy spots. The garlic powder adds a subtle savory note that complements the potatoes perfectly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: The sauce should be smooth, creamy, and well mixed without lumps
Step 3
Drain the cooked potatoes thoroughly and add them to the bowl with the sour cream mixture. Gently fold the potatoes into the sauce to coat them evenly, taking care not to break them up too much. This gentle folding preserves the potato chunks for a nice texture contrast in the final dish. If you mix too vigorously, you’ll end up with a mashed-potato-like consistency, which isn’t the goal here.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Potatoes are fully coated in sauce but still hold their shape
Step 4
Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a 9x13-inch casserole dish with butter or non-stick spray to prevent sticking. Transfer the potato mixture into the prepared dish and spread it out evenly. This even layer ensures uniform baking and browning on top. If the casserole is too thick in some spots, those parts might cook unevenly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Mixture is evenly spread in the casserole dish without lumps or gaps
Step 5
Sprinkle the shredded sharp cheddar cheese evenly over the top of the casserole. Then add the crumbled cooked bacon and chopped green onions. These toppings add texture and bursts of flavor that elevate the dish from simple to spectacular. The sharpness of the cheddar also balances the creamy potatoes beneath, while the bacon adds a smoky crunch.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Toppings are evenly distributed across the casserole surface
Step 6
Place the casserole in the preheated oven and bake uncovered for 25 to 30 minutes. During this time, the cheese will melt and start to brown, creating a bubbly, golden crust. Baking also allows the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly. Avoid opening the oven door frequently as it can drop the temperature and affect the baking time.
⏱ Time: 25
✓ Doneness: Cheese is melted, bubbly, and golden brown on top
Step 7
Once baked, remove the casserole from the oven and let it rest for about 5 minutes. This resting time helps the sauce thicken further and makes serving easier. If you try to serve it immediately, the casserole might be too runny and hot, which can make plating messy and less enjoyable.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Casserole firms up slightly but remains creamy and warm
Step 8
Serve the casserole warm, optionally garnished with extra chopped green onions or a dollop of sour cream if you like. This adds freshness and a little zing to each bite. Pair it with a simple green salad or steamed veggies for a balanced meal.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Dish is hot but comfortably cool enough to eat
Step 9
Clean up any spills and store leftovers in an airtight container once cooled. This casserole reheats well and is perfect for next-day lunches or dinners. Just reheat gently in the oven or microwave to keep it creamy and delicious.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Leftovers are cooled to room temperature before storing
