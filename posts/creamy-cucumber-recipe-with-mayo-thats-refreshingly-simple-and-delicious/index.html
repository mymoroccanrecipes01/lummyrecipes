Creamy Cucumber Salad with Mayo: A Refreshing Summer Side Dish

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 28, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy cucumber salad is my quick fix for a cool, crunchy side. The mayo gives it a smooth richness that balances the crisp cucumbers perfectly. Ideal for hot summer dinners or casual potlucks.

Introduction

I first made this creamy cucumber salad on a sweltering summer afternoon when I needed something quick and refreshing to bring to a family BBQ. It was a last-minute throw-together, but it instantly became a favorite because of how effortlessly the mayo added a smooth, rich texture that complemented the crisp cucumbers. Unlike the usual vinegar-only cucumber salads, this one has that cozy creaminess that feels so satisfying without being heavy. One little trick I learned is to slice the cucumbers very thin — it helps the dressing cling better and makes every bite perfectly balanced. Plus, letting it chill for at least 30 minutes lets the flavors meld beautifully. When you serve it, expect a cool, tangy crunch with a gentle creaminess that feels like summer on a plate. Honestly, this salad works great whether you’re pairing it with grilled meats or just want a light snack. It’s simple, comforting, and always a hit with friends and family.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 cups, 4 servings

  • 3 medium cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and drying your cucumbers thoroughly. Using a sharp knife or mandoline, slice them as thinly as possible for the best texture and flavor absorption. Thin slices allow the dressing to coat each piece evenly and keep the salad from feeling chunky. Avoid thick slices which can be harder to eat and don’t soak up the dressing well. If you see any big seeds, you might want to scoop them out to prevent extra moisture making the salad watery.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers should look translucent and feel tender but still crisp to the touch.
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, white vinegar, sugar, salt, and pepper until smooth. The vinegar adds a bright tang that balances the mayo's richness, while the sugar softens the acidity. Whisking well ensures the sugar dissolves fully and the dressing is creamy without lumps. Skipping this step or rushing it can leave the dressing uneven and grainy.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: The dressing should be smooth, creamy, and well combined with no graininess.
Step 3
Add the sliced cucumbers to the dressing and gently fold them in with a spatula. Folding rather than stirring vigorously helps maintain the cucumber’s crunch and prevents bruising. Make sure every slice is coated, but don’t overmix or the salad will become soggy quickly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers should be evenly coated without any dry spots or clumps of dressing.
Step 4
Chop fresh dill finely and sprinkle it into the salad, then fold gently to combine. Dill adds a fresh, herbal note that brightens the creamy dressing. If you’re not a fan, fresh parsley or chives can work too, but dill is classic here. Don’t add too early or it can wilt.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Herbs should look fresh and evenly distributed without wilting.
Step 5
Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the flavors to meld and the cucumbers to soften slightly without losing their crunch. Skipping chilling means the salad won’t taste as harmonious or refreshing.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Salad should be cool and the flavors well blended when tasted.
Step 6
Before serving, give the salad a gentle stir and taste for seasoning. Sometimes the cucumbers absorb a bit of salt, so adjust salt and pepper if needed. This final seasoning step ensures the salad is flavorful and balanced.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Salad tastes balanced with a good mix of tang, creaminess, and seasoning.
Step 7
Transfer the salad to a serving bowl and garnish with a few sprigs of fresh dill or a light sprinkle of black pepper for presentation. This adds a nice visual touch and a hint of extra aroma.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Salad looks fresh, inviting, and well presented.
Step 8
Serve the salad chilled alongside grilled meats, sandwiches, or as a light snack. It pairs wonderfully with summer dishes and adds a cooling contrast to spicy or smoky flavors. If you’re packing it for a picnic, keep it cold until ready to eat.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Salad should be served cold and fresh.
Step 9
If making ahead, prepare the salad but hold off on adding herbs until just before serving to keep them vibrant. This little step helps maintain fresh flavor and appearance. Also, give the salad a quick stir before serving to redistribute any dressing that may have settled.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Herbs look fresh and salad maintains creamy, crunchy texture.

Pro Tips

Slice cucumbers as thinly as possible using a mandoline.
Thin slices maximize surface area for the dressing to cling to, ensuring every bite is flavorful. Thick slices can feel bulky and don’t absorb the dressing well, making the salad less cohesive.
Let the salad chill for at least 30 minutes before serving.
Chilling allows the flavors to meld and the cucumbers to soften slightly, which balances the texture and intensifies the taste. Serving immediately means the flavors might taste disjointed.
Add fresh herbs like dill just before serving.
Fresh herbs can wilt quickly in the dressing, losing their bright flavor and appealing look. Adding them last keeps their aroma fresh and improves presentation.
Use high-quality mayonnaise for best creaminess.
Creamier mayo provides a better texture and richer flavor, which is key for this salad. Low-fat or overly tangy mayos can throw off the balance and make the salad less satisfying.
Adjust salt and pepper after chilling.
Cucumbers absorb salt during resting, so seasoning again before serving ensures the salad has the right flavor punch without being under or overseasoned.
Avoid peeling cucumbers unless skin is thick or waxy.
The skin adds color, nutrients, and texture. Peeling removes these benefits and can leave the salad looking dull and less interesting.
Store leftover salad in an airtight container in the fridge.
Proper storage keeps the salad fresh and prevents it from drying out or absorbing fridge odors. It also slows down the cucumbers’ softening process.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using thick cucumber slices

Why it happens Many assume chunkier slices make the salad more substantial, but thick slices don’t absorb dressing well and can taste bland.

Fix Use a mandoline or sharp knife to slice cucumbers thinly and evenly. This ensures better dressing absorption and a lighter, more refreshing texture.

Skipping the chilling step

Why it happens In a rush, cooks often serve immediately, missing the flavor melding process.

Fix Plan ahead to chill the salad for at least 30 minutes. This step enhances flavor integration and softens cucumbers just enough for perfect texture.

Adding herbs too early

Why it happens Some add dill or parsley before chilling, thinking flavors blend better.

Fix Add fresh herbs just before serving to keep them vibrant and prevent wilting, preserving fresh aroma and appearance.

Using low-quality mayonnaise

Why it happens Trying to cut calories or save money leads to subpar mayo choices, affecting texture and flavor.

Fix Choose a creamy, good-quality mayo for richness and smoothness; this is key for the salad’s signature creaminess.

Not adjusting seasoning after resting

Why it happens Assuming initial seasoning is sufficient without tasting after chilling.

Fix Taste the salad after chilling and adjust salt and pepper as cucumbers absorb some seasoning, ensuring balanced flavor.

Variations

Greek Style with Feta and Red Onion

Add crumbled feta cheese and thinly sliced red onion to the creamy cucumber salad for a Mediterranean twist. The salty feta and sharp onion complement the creamy dressing beautifully, adding depth and a bit more texture.

Note: Add feta and onion just before serving to maintain freshness and prevent sogginess.

Spicy Kick with Jalapeño and Lime

Mix in finely chopped jalapeño and a squeeze of fresh lime juice to give the salad some heat and zest. This variation is great if you want to add a bold, spicy contrast to the creamy base.

Note: Adjust jalapeño quantity according to heat preference and add lime juice to the dressing for even flavor distribution.

Vegan Version with Vegan Mayo and Lemon

Swap regular mayonnaise for a vegan mayo and replace vinegar with fresh lemon juice for a plant-based version. The lemon juice adds brightness while keeping the salad creamy and satisfying.

Note: Choose a vegan mayo with a neutral flavor and creamy texture to mimic the original salad.

Herb Mix-Up with Mint and Parsley

Instead of dill, use a mix of fresh mint and parsley for a different herbal profile. Mint adds a cool freshness while parsley brings earthiness, creating a unique flavor combo that’s still light and refreshing.

Note: Add herbs just before serving to keep them vibrant and aromatic.

Add Crunch with Toasted Almonds

Sprinkle toasted sliced almonds on top of the salad before serving for a delightful crunch and nutty flavor. It adds texture contrast that makes the salad more interesting and satisfying.

Note: Toast almonds lightly in a dry pan until golden and cool before sprinkling.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the mayonnaise. Cover it tightly with plastic wrap or a lid if serving buffet style to keep it fresh and safe.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The cucumbers may soften over time and the salad can lose some crunch. It’s best served chilled and fresh but still tasty cold.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this salad because cucumbers become watery and mushy when thawed. The texture and creaminess will be compromised.

Reheating

No reheating is needed or recommended for this salad. Serve cold straight from the fridge for the best texture and flavor.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dressing and slice cucumbers 1 day ahead, storing them separately. Combine and add fresh herbs just before serving to keep the salad fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use regular vinegar instead of white vinegar?
Yes, but white vinegar is preferred for its clean, sharp flavor and clear color which keeps the salad visually appealing. Using stronger vinegars like apple cider or balsamic will alter the flavor and color, so use them cautiously.
How thin should I slice the cucumbers?
Aim for slices about 1/8 inch thick or thinner if possible. Thin slices absorb the dressing well and provide the best texture. Thick slices can feel watery and don’t blend as nicely with the creamy dressing.
Can I make this salad vegan?
Absolutely! Use a vegan mayonnaise substitute and replace white vinegar with fresh lemon juice if you prefer. This keeps the creamy texture and bright flavor while making it plant-based.
How long can I store this salad?
Store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The cucumbers will soften over time, so it’s best enjoyed fresh within the first day or two for optimal crunch.
Why does the salad sometimes get watery?
Cucumbers release water when sitting in the dressing, especially if sliced thick or not dried well. To reduce wateriness, slice thinly, pat dry, and avoid overmixing. Chilling also helps flavors meld without excess liquid.
Can I add other vegetables to this salad?
Yes! Thinly sliced red onions, radishes, or bell peppers can add crunch and flavor. Just be mindful of how these veggies affect moisture and balance the seasoning accordingly.
What’s the best way to prevent the salad from becoming soggy?
Use thin slices, pat cucumbers dry, fold gently when mixing, and add fresh herbs just before serving. Also, avoid making the salad too far in advance to keep it crisp.
Can I use dried dill instead of fresh?
Fresh dill is best for flavor and appearance. Dried dill tends to be less vibrant and can get lost in the creamy dressing. If using dried, add sparingly and earlier in the mixing process.
