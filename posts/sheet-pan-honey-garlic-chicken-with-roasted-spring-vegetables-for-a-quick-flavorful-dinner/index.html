Easy Honey Garlic Chicken Thighs with Roasted Spring Veggies

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 12, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This sheet pan dinner brings together juicy chicken thighs glazed in a sweet honey garlic sauce with crisp roasted spring vegetables. It’s a simple, tasty meal perfect for busy weeknights or laid-back dinners.

Introduction

I first made this honey garlic chicken thighs recipe on a hectic weeknight when I needed something quick but satisfying. What I love about this dish is how the honey glaze caramelizes beautifully on the chicken skin, giving it that irresistible sweet-savory crunch. Plus, roasting the veggies alongside the chicken means everything cooks evenly and you only have one pan to clean — a total win! I’ve tweaked the sauce a bit from other versions by adding a splash of apple cider vinegar to balance the sweetness, which really brightens up the flavor. The trick I learned is to pat the chicken dry before coating it with the glaze; this helps the skin crisp up perfectly. When it’s done, you get juicy, tender chicken with a sticky, flavorful coating, paired with crisp-tender spring vegetables that have absorbed those garlicky notes. It’s a simple meal but feels special enough for a casual weekend dinner or a quick weekday fix. I think you’ll love how easy it is to pull together yet so full of flavor.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs, cold
  • 3 tablespoons honey, liquid
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 cups asparagus, trimmed
  • 1 cup baby carrots, halved lengthwise
  • 1 cup snap peas, ends trimmed
  • Salt to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). This high temperature is key for crisping the chicken skin and roasting the veggies evenly. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper or foil for easy cleanup. Pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels — this step is crucial because moisture on the skin prevents it from getting crispy. Set the chicken aside while you prepare the glaze and veggies.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin feels dry to the touch before glazing
Step 2
In a small bowl, whisk together honey, minced garlic, soy sauce, olive oil, apple cider vinegar, black pepper, and smoked paprika. This glaze balances sweetness and acidity while the smoked paprika adds depth. Don’t rush this step — thoroughly combining these ingredients ensures even flavor distribution on the chicken.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze is smooth and well combined without separation
Step 3
Place the chicken thighs skin side up on the sheet pan, spacing them evenly. Use a brush or spoon to generously coat each piece with the honey garlic glaze, making sure to cover all the skin. The glaze will caramelize in the oven, creating a sticky, flavorful crust. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure proper roasting and browning.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin is fully coated, no dry spots
Step 4
Toss the asparagus, baby carrots, and snap peas in a bowl with a drizzle of olive oil and a pinch of salt. Spread the veggies around the chicken on the sheet pan in a single layer. This allows the vegetables to roast evenly and soak up some of the chicken’s savory juices during cooking. Don’t pile them on top of each other or the roasting will be uneven.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Veggies are evenly coated and spread out on the pan
Step 5
Place the sheet pan in the preheated oven and roast for 25-30 minutes. Halfway through, baste the chicken and veggies with any glaze or pan juices to keep them moist and flavorful. This also helps build layers of caramelized flavor. Avoid opening the oven too often as it lowers the temperature and affects cooking.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin is golden brown and veggies are tender-crisp
Step 6
Check the internal temperature of the chicken thighs with a meat thermometer; it should read 165°F (74°C) to ensure safety. If you don’t have a thermometer, cut into the thickest part — juices should run clear and meat should be opaque. If needed, roast an additional 5 minutes but watch carefully to avoid drying out the chicken.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken internal temperature reaches 165°F and juices run clear
Step 7
Remove the sheet pan from the oven and let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before serving. Resting allows the juices to redistribute, keeping the meat moist and tender. If you serve immediately, the juices will run out when cut, resulting in drier chicken.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels firm but juicy when gently pressed
Step 8
Serve the chicken thighs with the roasted vegetables spooned alongside. You can drizzle any remaining pan juices over the top for extra flavor. This meal pairs well with a simple side of rice or crusty bread to soak up the delicious glaze.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Plated dish looks vibrant with golden chicken and colorful veggies
Step 9
Optional: Garnish with fresh chopped parsley or a squeeze of lemon juice to brighten the dish just before serving. These fresh touches add a pop of color and freshness that complements the sweet and savory flavors.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Garnish evenly distributed and visually appealing

Pro Tips

Pat chicken skin dry before glazing.
Removing excess moisture from the chicken skin is essential for crispiness. Water on the surface creates steam in the oven, which prevents the skin from browning and getting crispy. Patting dry ensures the Maillard reaction can occur properly, resulting in that irresistible golden crust.
Use a meat thermometer to check doneness.
Checking the internal temperature ensures the chicken is cooked safely and perfectly juicy. Overcooking dries out the meat, while undercooking risks food safety. Aim for 165°F (74°C) for safe, tender chicken thighs. A thermometer takes the guesswork out of timing.
Don’t overcrowd the sheet pan.
Crowding the pan traps steam and prevents proper roasting. Spacing ingredients allows hot air to circulate, which crisps the chicken skin and evenly roasts vegetables. Overcrowding can lead to soggy veggies and less flavorful chicken.
Baste chicken halfway through cooking.
Basting with pan juices or glaze during roasting helps keep the chicken moist and builds layers of caramelized flavor. It also prevents the glaze from drying out and enhances the sticky texture on the skin.
Add a splash of acid like apple cider vinegar to the glaze.
Acid balances the sweetness of honey and the richness of chicken, brightening the overall flavor. It prevents the glaze from tasting too cloying and adds a subtle tang that enhances the dish’s complexity.
Let chicken rest after roasting.
Resting allows juices to redistribute throughout the meat instead of spilling out when cut. This results in moist, tender chicken rather than dry, tough slices.
Use fresh vegetables with similar roasting times.
Choosing veggies that roast at roughly the same rate ensures everything finishes cooking simultaneously. This prevents some veggies from burning while others remain undercooked, making for a balanced meal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the step to dry chicken skin.

Why it happens Many cooks overlook drying the chicken because it adds an extra step or they underestimate its importance. Moist skin seems harmless but seriously hinders crisping.

Fix Always pat the chicken skin dry with paper towels before applying the glaze. This removes surface moisture that causes steaming in the oven. It’s a quick step that makes a big difference in texture.

Overcrowding the pan with chicken and veggies.

Why it happens Trying to fit everything on one pan to save time or dishes leads to overcrowding. It’s tempting but affects cooking quality.

Fix Use a large enough sheet pan or roast in batches to leave space between pieces. Proper air circulation ensures even roasting and crisp skin.

Rushing the cooking time or opening the oven frequently.

Why it happens Impatience or checking too often disrupts oven temperature and cooking progress.

Fix Set a timer and resist opening the oven door repeatedly. Trust the process to allow the chicken skin to brown and veggies to roast evenly.

Not using a meat thermometer to check doneness.

Why it happens Some cooks rely on time alone or visual cues, which can be unreliable.

Fix Invest in an instant-read thermometer to ensure safe and perfect results every time. Insert into the thickest part of the thigh without touching bone.

Using frozen or wet vegetables without drying.

Why it happens Frozen or rinsed veggies often hold water, which can make roasting soggy.

Fix If using frozen, thaw and pat dry. For fresh veggies, ensure they’re dry before tossing with oil to achieve crisp roasting.

Variations

Spicy Honey Garlic Chicken

Add 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes or a dash of sriracha to the glaze for a spicy kick. This variation balances sweetness with heat, perfect if you like a little fire in your meal. The roasting time remains the same, but the flavor profile shifts to bold and exciting.

Note: Make sure to mix the spicy ingredient evenly into the glaze to avoid uneven heat spots. Adjust spice level to taste.

Lemon Herb Honey Garlic Chicken

Add fresh lemon zest and chopped thyme or rosemary to the glaze for a bright, herbal twist. This makes the dish more aromatic and refreshing, ideal for spring or summer dinners. The lemon juice can replace the apple cider vinegar for a citrusy tang.

Note: Add herbs finely chopped and lemon zest just before glazing to preserve freshness and aroma.

Honey Garlic Chicken with Roasted Potatoes

Swap the spring veggies for halved baby potatoes tossed in olive oil and rosemary. Roasting alongside chicken yields crispy, golden potatoes soaking up the savory glaze. The potatoes need about the same roasting time, making this a hearty variation.

Note: Cut potatoes into uniform pieces to ensure even cooking and crispness.

Gluten-Free Honey Garlic Chicken

Use tamari or coconut aminos instead of soy sauce to make this recipe gluten-free. The flavor remains rich and balanced, and it’s safe for those with gluten sensitivities. All other ingredients and steps stay the same.

Note: Check all ingredient labels for hidden gluten to maintain a fully gluten-free dish.

Honey Garlic Chicken with Broccoli and Cauliflower

Replace the asparagus and snap peas with broccoli and cauliflower florets for a different veggie mix. These cruciferous vegetables roast beautifully and add a nice texture contrast. Roast slightly longer if needed to get tender edges.

Note: Cut florets into similar sizes for even roasting and toss well with oil and glaze.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean cloth. Avoid leaving it out longer to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The chicken skin may soften when cold but reheats well in the oven or skillet to regain crispiness. Veggies can also be reheated or enjoyed cold in salads.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and vegetables separately in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a preheated 375°F oven for 10-15 minutes to restore crispiness. You can add a splash of water or broth to keep veggies moist. Avoid microwaving if you want to preserve the chicken skin’s texture, as it tends to get soggy.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the glaze and chop veggies 1-2 days ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Pat the chicken dry and keep it refrigerated until ready to assemble and roast. This saves time on busy days while ensuring fresh flavors and textures.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, but chicken breasts cook faster and tend to dry out more easily. Use boneless, skinless breasts and reduce roasting time to about 20-25 minutes. Pat them dry and consider adding a bit more glaze to keep them moist. The texture and flavor will be different since breasts are leaner.
What if I don’t have honey? Can I substitute it?
You can substitute honey with maple syrup or agave nectar for a similar sweetness and consistency. Both alternatives caramelize well and complement garlic nicely. Avoid using granulated sugar as it won’t melt evenly and can burn during roasting.
How do I keep the chicken skin crispy when reheating leftovers?
Reheat leftovers in a hot oven (around 375°F) on a wire rack placed over a baking sheet. This allows air circulation around the chicken, helping the skin crisp up instead of steaming. Avoid microwaving as it makes the skin soggy.
Can I add other vegetables to this sheet pan meal?
Absolutely! Root vegetables like sweet potatoes or parsnips, bell peppers, or Brussels sprouts work well. Just be mindful of roasting times; harder veggies may need to be cut smaller or added earlier to cook through evenly.
Is it necessary to baste the chicken during cooking?
Basting isn’t mandatory but it helps keep the chicken moist and builds extra layers of caramelized glaze. If you’re short on time, you can skip it, but the flavor and texture will be enhanced by basting halfway through roasting.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, simply swap regular soy sauce for tamari or coconut aminos, which are gluten-free alternatives. These maintain the savory umami flavor without gluten. Double-check all other ingredients to ensure they don’t contain hidden gluten.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Ensure leftovers cool to room temperature before refrigerating to prevent condensation, which can make the chicken skin soggy.
How can I make the dish spicier?
Add red pepper flakes, cayenne pepper, or a dash of hot sauce to the honey garlic glaze before roasting. Adjust the amount to your heat preference. This adds a nice contrast to the sweetness and enhances the overall flavor complexity.
