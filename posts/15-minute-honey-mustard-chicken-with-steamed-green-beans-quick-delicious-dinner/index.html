Easy Honey Mustard Chicken with Fresh Steamed Green Beans

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 29, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This honey mustard chicken is my go-to for busy weeknights—juicy chicken smothered in a tangy, sweet sauce paired with crisp steamed green beans. The honey adds just the right touch of natural sweetness. Super simple and satisfying!

Introduction

I first stumbled on this honey mustard chicken recipe when I needed a quick but tasty dinner on a hectic weeknight. My family loves dishes that have a balance of sweet and tangy flavors, and this one fits the bill perfectly. What sets my version apart is the simple honey mustard glaze that’s both sticky and flavorful without being overpowering. I also steam the green beans just right so they keep a fresh crunch rather than getting soggy. I’ve learned the hard way that overcooking green beans kills their bright color and snap, so timing is key here. When you plate this, the chicken should be juicy and tender with a glossy, golden sauce coating it. The green beans add a nice contrast, keeping the meal light and fresh. It’s a fuss-free recipe that feels special but comes together fast, perfect for those nights when you want comfort food without the extra work. I like to serve it with a side of rice or crusty bread to soak up the sauce. Trust me, once you try this, it’ll become a weeknight staple in your house too!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, patted dry
  • 1/3 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 1 tablespoon butter

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by prepping the chicken breasts: pat them dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture. This helps the mustard glaze stick better and promotes even cooking. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper. Don't skip drying the chicken; wet chicken won’t brown properly and the sauce won’t cling as well. This step ensures a nicely caramelized surface once cooked.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken surface looks dry and seasoned evenly, ready for the pan
Step 2
Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add chicken breasts and sear without moving them for about 5 minutes per side. This high heat sear locks in juices and creates a slight crust, which enhances flavor. Avoid flipping too early to prevent tearing the meat. The chicken should develop a golden-brown color on each side before lowering the heat.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown crust forms on both sides, chicken releases easily from pan
Step 3
While the chicken cooks, whisk together Dijon mustard, honey, minced garlic, and apple cider vinegar in a small bowl. This blend balances sweet, tangy, and savory notes perfectly. The vinegar adds brightness and helps tenderize the chicken slightly during cooking. Set aside the sauce for glazing later.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is smooth and well combined, ready to use
Step 4
Once both sides of the chicken are seared, reduce heat to medium-low and pour the honey mustard sauce over the chicken in the skillet. Spoon some sauce on top and let it simmer gently. This allows the sauce to thicken and glaze the chicken beautifully without burning. Cook for another 8-10 minutes, turning the chicken occasionally to coat evenly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce thickens slightly and clings to the chicken with a shiny glaze
Step 5
While the chicken simmers, bring a pot of salted water to a boil for the green beans. Add the trimmed green beans and blanch for 3-4 minutes until bright green and just tender. Blanching keeps the beans crisp and preserves their vibrant color. Drain and immediately plunge them into ice water to stop cooking and set the color.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Green beans are bright green, tender but still crisp when bitten
Step 6
In a separate pan, melt butter over medium heat and toss in the blanched green beans. Sauté for 2-3 minutes to warm through and add a touch of richness. This step enhances their flavor and gives a nice silky coating. Avoid overcooking here to keep the beans from becoming mushy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Green beans glisten with butter and are heated through without losing crunch
Step 7
Check the chicken’s internal temperature with a meat thermometer; it should reach 165°F (74°C) to be safely cooked through. If you don’t have a thermometer, cut into the thickest part to ensure juices run clear and meat is opaque. This guarantees juicy, safe-to-eat chicken without drying it out.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is opaque inside with clear juices, thermometer reads 165°F
Step 8
Once cooked, transfer the chicken breasts to a serving plate and spoon any remaining honey mustard sauce from the pan over the top. Let the chicken rest for 3-5 minutes before slicing to allow juices to redistribute, keeping it moist. Resting is a crucial but often skipped step that really improves texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken juices settle, and meat feels tender and moist when pressed
Step 9
Serve the honey mustard chicken alongside the buttery steamed green beans. Garnish with a sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper or chopped parsley if you like. This pairing is simple but satisfying, with the green beans providing a fresh, crunchy counterpoint to the rich, tangy chicken.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish looks vibrant and inviting with glossy chicken and bright green beans

Pro Tips

Pat chicken completely dry before seasoning and cooking.
Removing surface moisture prevents steaming and allows the chicken to brown properly via the Maillard reaction. This browning develops rich flavors and a satisfying texture. If the chicken is wet, it will release water into the pan, causing the meat to simmer rather than sear, resulting in a pale, rubbery texture.
Use medium-high heat to sear chicken before simmering in sauce.
Searing at medium-high heat creates a flavorful crust and locks in juices. Lower heat prevents browning, while too high heat can burn the sauce. After searing, reducing heat allows the honey mustard glaze to thicken gently and coat the chicken without burning, balancing texture and flavor.
Don’t skip resting the chicken after cooking.
Resting lets the juices redistribute inside the meat fibers. Cutting into the chicken immediately causes the juices to spill out, leading to dry meat. A short rest of 3-5 minutes ensures a moist, tender bite and improved overall eating experience.
Blanch green beans and shock in ice water immediately.
Blanching cooks the beans just enough to soften them while preserving their bright color and crunch. Shocking them in ice water stops the cooking process instantly, preventing overcooking and dull colors. This technique keeps the beans vibrant and fresh-tasting.
Use a meat thermometer to check chicken doneness.
Chicken should reach an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) to be safe to eat. Using a thermometer ensures perfectly cooked chicken—juicy and tender—without the guesswork. Overcooking dries out the meat; undercooking is unsafe.
Mix sauce ingredients just before cooking.
Preparing the honey mustard sauce fresh ensures the flavors are bright and the sauce has the right consistency. Letting it sit too long can cause separation or dullness in flavor. Freshly whisked sauce emulsifies better and cooks down nicely.
Trim green bean ends before cooking for better texture.
Removing tough stem ends prevents chewy bites and improves overall texture. It also helps the beans cook evenly and look more polished on the plate. This small prep step makes a big difference in the eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Cooking chicken straight from the fridge without drying

Why it happens Many cooks skip drying to save time, not realizing moisture prevents proper browning.

Fix Always pat chicken dry with paper towels before seasoning. This removes surface water, allowing the chicken to sear and caramelize correctly, creating better texture and flavor.

Overcooking green beans until mushy

Why it happens It’s easy to leave beans boiling too long, especially when multitasking.

Fix Set a timer for 3-4 minutes when blanching. Immediately transfer beans to ice water to stop cooking, preserving crunch and color.

Skipping resting time after cooking chicken

Why it happens Excitement to serve leads to slicing chicken too soon.

Fix Let chicken rest 3-5 minutes after cooking to let juices redistribute. This keeps meat moist and tender instead of dry.

Using too high heat when simmering sauce

Why it happens Rushing the sauce thickening process can cause burning or bitterness.

Fix Lower heat after searing and simmer sauce gently. This prevents burning and allows the glaze to thicken smoothly and coat the chicken.

Not seasoning the chicken enough

Why it happens Some assume the sauce adds all the flavor needed.

Fix Season chicken with salt and pepper before cooking. This enhances overall flavor and balances the sauce’s sweetness and tanginess.

Variations

Spicy Honey Mustard Chicken

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a drizzle of sriracha to the honey mustard sauce for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and depth without overwhelming the sweet-tangy balance. Serve with steamed green beans as usual for a fresh contrast.

Note: Adjust spice levels gradually to avoid overpowering the delicate honey flavor. The sauce may thicken slightly faster due to added ingredients, so watch closely.

Honey Mustard Chicken Thighs

Swap chicken breasts for bone-in, skin-on thighs for a richer, juicier result. The skin crisps up nicely during searing, adding texture and flavor. Cook slightly longer due to bone-in pieces, ensuring internal temperature reaches 165°F.

Note: Sear skin side down first to render fat and crisp skin. Adjust cooking time to accommodate thicker pieces.

Honey Mustard Chicken with Roasted Vegetables

Instead of steamed green beans, roast a mix of your favorite veggies like carrots, bell peppers, and sweet potatoes. Toss them in olive oil and roast until caramelized. This variation adds a heartier, earthy element to the meal.

Note: Roast vegetables at 425°F for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway. Timing differs from steaming, so prepare veggies ahead.

Honey Mustard Chicken Salad

Slice cooked honey mustard chicken and serve over mixed greens with cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, and red onion. Drizzle extra honey mustard sauce as dressing for a light, refreshing meal perfect for warmer days.

Note: Cool chicken completely before slicing to keep salad crisp and avoid wilting greens.

Gluten-Free Honey Mustard Chicken

Ensure all ingredients, particularly mustard and vinegar, are certified gluten-free. Use tamari instead of soy sauce if adding any for extra umami. This version is safe for gluten-sensitive diners without sacrificing flavor.

Note: Check labels carefully to avoid hidden gluten sources, especially in condiments.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so it’s best to refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may firm up when cold; reheat gently to restore moisture. Green beans are best eaten fresh but can be reheated briefly or served cold in salads.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and sauce separately from green beans for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly in freezer-safe containers or bags to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat chicken gently on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce, about 5-7 minutes. Avoid microwaving at high power which can dry out the meat. Warm green beans separately for 1-2 minutes or serve cold.

Make Ahead

Prepare the honey mustard sauce and blanch green beans 1-2 days ahead. Store sauce and beans separately in airtight containers. Cook chicken fresh or reheat gently before serving for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen chicken breasts for this recipe?
You can use frozen chicken breasts if you thaw them completely before cooking. Thaw in the fridge overnight to ensure even cooking. Cooking from frozen will result in uneven doneness and may cause the outside to overcook while the inside remains raw.
What can I substitute for Dijon mustard?
If you don’t have Dijon mustard, spicy brown mustard or yellow mustard can work but will change the flavor profile. Yellow mustard is milder and less tangy, while brown mustard adds more spice. Adjust quantities to taste and be mindful the sauce texture might differ slightly.
How do I know when the chicken is done without a thermometer?
Cut into the thickest part of the chicken breast; the meat should be opaque with no pink and the juices should run clear. If you see any pink or red juices, continue cooking a bit longer and check again. Using a thermometer is more reliable but this visual check works well too.
Can I make this recipe vegan or vegetarian?
To make a vegan version, substitute chicken with firm tofu or seitan and use maple syrup instead of honey. Steam or roast green beans as usual. Keep in mind the texture and flavor will change, but the honey mustard glaze concept still works well.
Is it better to bake or pan-fry the chicken?
Pan-frying allows you to get a nice sear and develop the glaze directly in the skillet, which enhances flavor and texture. Baking is possible but you’ll miss out on the caramelized crust and sauce reduction in the pan. For best results, stick to the sear-then-simmer method.
How can I make the sauce thicker?
To thicken the sauce, simmer it gently over low heat until it reduces and coats the back of a spoon. Avoid high heat which can burn the honey. You can also add a small slurry of cornstarch and water if you want a quicker thickening, but simmering is preferred for flavor.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
Yes, you can make the honey mustard sauce and blanch the green beans 1-2 days ahead. Store separately in airtight containers. Cook and reheat the chicken just before serving to keep it juicy and fresh. This approach saves time on busy nights.
What side dishes go well with this honey mustard chicken?
This chicken pairs beautifully with simple sides like steamed rice, quinoa, roasted potatoes, or a fresh green salad. The tangy-sweet flavors complement mild starches and fresh veggies well, making for a balanced and satisfying meal.
