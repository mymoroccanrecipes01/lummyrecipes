Rich and Creamy Vegan Chocolate Cheesecake for Hot Summer Days

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 12, 2026
15 min Prep Time
8 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This vegan chocolate cheesecake is a luscious, creamy delight made from cashews and coconut cream. It's perfect for warm days when you want something indulgent but refreshing. Serve chilled for a crowd-pleasing treat!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this vegan chocolate cheesecake recipe during a summer when I was desperate for a dessert that didn’t require turning on the oven. The weather was scorching, and the last thing I wanted was a hot kitchen. What I discovered was a creamy, indulgent cheesecake made entirely from cashews and coconut cream, which felt surprisingly light and refreshing. Over the years, I’ve tweaked the recipe to balance the richness of the chocolate with just the right amount of sweetness, and the crust made from vegan chocolate cookie crumbs adds a delightful crunch. One trick I learned early on was soaking the cashews overnight — it’s key to getting that silky smooth texture without any graininess. The finished cheesecake is velvety and dense but never heavy, with a subtle coconut undertone that pairs beautifully with the deep chocolate flavor. It’s perfect chilled and makes a stunning centerpiece for summer gatherings or anytime you want a cool, dairy-free treat. Honestly, it’s become my go-to dessert when friends come over, and everyone always asks for seconds!

What You'll Need

One 9-inch cheesecake, 8 servings

  • 1 ½ cups raw cashews, soaked overnight and drained
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut cream
  • ½ cup maple syrup
  • ¼ cup cocoa powder, unsweetened
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 ½ cups vegan chocolate cookie crumbs (for crust)
  • 3 tablespoons coconut oil, melted (for crust)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing the crust. In a bowl, combine the vegan chocolate cookie crumbs with 3 tablespoons of melted coconut oil. Stir until the crumbs are evenly coated and resemble wet sand. Press this mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, ensuring it's tightly packed and level. This creates a sturdy base that holds the creamy filling without crumbling. Avoid pressing too hard to prevent the crust from becoming too dense. Once pressed, place the crust in the fridge to chill while you make the filling.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Crust feels firm and compact when pressed with a finger
Step 2
Drain the soaked cashews and add them to a high-speed blender along with the coconut cream, maple syrup, cocoa powder, melted coconut oil, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Blend on high until completely smooth and creamy—this can take 2 to 3 minutes depending on your blender. The goal is a silky texture without any cashew bits. If the mixture is too thick, add a tablespoon or two of water to help it blend more smoothly. Don’t rush this step, as a well-blended filling is what makes the cheesecake luxuriously creamy.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth, glossy, and thick but pourable
Step 3
Pour the creamy chocolate filling over the chilled crust, smoothing the top with a spatula. Make sure to spread it evenly to avoid any lumps or air pockets. Cover the pan tightly with plastic wrap or a lid to prevent the cheesecake from absorbing fridge odors. Let it chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, though overnight is best. This chilling period allows the filling to firm up and the flavors to meld beautifully.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Filling is set and holds its shape when gently pressed
Step 4
Once fully chilled, carefully run a thin knife around the edge of the cheesecake to loosen it from the pan sides before releasing the springform. This prevents cracking or breaking. Transfer the cheesecake to a serving plate if desired. For a polished look, garnish with shaved vegan chocolate, fresh berries, or a sprinkle of cocoa powder. Serve chilled for the best texture and flavor experience.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake releases cleanly from the pan edges without sticking
Step 5
If you want to prepare ahead, you can freeze the cheesecake after chilling. Wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil to protect from freezer burn. Thaw it in the fridge overnight before serving. This makes it convenient for hosting or meal prepping. Just note that texture may be slightly denser after freezing but still delicious.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake is firm but creamy after thawing
Step 6
To enhance the chocolate flavor, you can gently melt some vegan chocolate chips and drizzle over the top before chilling. This adds a glossy finish and a slight crunch once set. Simply melt the chips in a double boiler or microwave in short bursts, stirring frequently to avoid burning.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chocolate drizzle is smooth and glossy, hardened after chilling
Step 7
For a lighter variation, swap half the coconut cream with unsweetened almond milk. This reduces fat slightly but keeps the cheesecake creamy. Be sure to soak cashews well to maintain a smooth texture. The flavor will be a bit less rich but still satisfyingly chocolatey.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Filling remains smooth and creamy despite lighter base
Step 8
If your blender struggles to get the filling smooth, blend the cashews and coconut cream first, then add the remaining ingredients and blend again. This staged blending helps break down the nuts more effectively. A powerful blender is ideal but this method works well with standard kitchen blenders too.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Filling is silky without grainy cashew bits
Step 9
When serving, let the cheesecake sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes to soften slightly. This brings out the flavors and makes slicing easier. Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water and wiped dry between cuts for clean slices without cracking.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Slices are smooth and hold shape without crumbling

Pro Tips

Soak cashews overnight for the creamiest filling.
Soaking softens the cashews, making them easier to blend into a smooth, creamy texture. Without soaking, the filling can be grainy or lumpy, which detracts from the cheesecake’s luxurious mouthfeel.
Use a high-speed blender or blend in stages.
A powerful blender ensures the cashews and coconut cream emulsify perfectly, giving a silky texture. If your blender is weaker, blend the cashews and coconut cream first, then add the rest to avoid gritty bits.
Chill the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight.
The chilling allows the fats to solidify and the flavors to meld, helping the cheesecake set firmly so it slices cleanly and tastes balanced.
Press the crust firmly but not too dense.
A well-pressed crust holds the filling without crumbling but pressing too hard can make it overly dense and hard to cut through.
Use melted coconut oil to bind the crust.
Coconut oil solidifies when chilled, which helps the crust hold together. It also adds a subtle coconut flavor that complements the filling.
Let cheesecake sit at room temperature before slicing.
Allowing it to soften slightly makes slicing easier and enhances flavor release, preventing cracks and crumbling.
Run a knife under hot water before each cut.
A warm knife glides through the firm cheesecake smoothly, giving clean, pretty slices without tearing the crust.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not soaking cashews long enough

Why it happens People often rush or forget to soak cashews, thinking a quick rinse is enough.

Fix Soak cashews for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight, to soften them properly. If short on time, soak in very hot water for 1-2 hours. This prevents grainy texture in the filling.

Skipping the chilling time

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning leads to serving the cheesecake too soon.

Fix Always chill the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This firms up the filling and improves flavor meld, making slicing and serving easier.

Using low-fat coconut milk instead of cream

Why it happens Some buy light coconut milk to reduce calories, not realizing the fat content affects texture.

Fix Use full-fat coconut cream or the thick cream layer from canned coconut milk. The fat is essential for creamy texture and proper setting.

Not pressing the crust firmly enough

Why it happens Sometimes the crust is just sprinkled in without packing it down.

Fix Press the crust firmly with the back of a spoon or your fingers to create a compact base that holds together when sliced.

Blending too quickly without scraping

Why it happens Rushing the blending or not stopping to scrape down sides results in uneven texture.

Fix Blend in bursts, stopping to scrape the blender sides. This ensures all cashews are evenly pureed and the filling is smooth.

Variations

Peanut Butter Chocolate Cheesecake

Add ¼ cup natural peanut butter to the filling for a rich, nutty twist. This pairs wonderfully with the chocolate and adds a creamy depth of flavor. Use crunchy peanut butter for texture or smooth for extra silkiness.

Note: Blend peanut butter with the cashews to ensure even distribution. The filling may be slightly thicker, so add a splash of almond milk if needed.

Berry Swirl Vegan Chocolate Cheesecake

Swirl in a berry puree (strawberry or raspberry) into the filling before chilling for a fresh, fruity contrast. This adds color and a tart brightness that balances the richness.

Note: Gently fold the puree into the filling after blending to create pretty swirls without fully mixing.

Mocha Vegan Chocolate Cheesecake

Mix in 1 tablespoon instant coffee granules dissolved in 1 tablespoon hot water to the filling for a mocha flavor. This enhances the chocolate notes and adds a subtle caffeine kick.

Note: Add coffee after blending the main filling ingredients and blend briefly to combine.

Gluten-Free Crust Cheesecake

Use gluten-free chocolate cookies or crushed nuts mixed with coconut oil for a gluten-free crust option. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and firm.

Note: Press the gluten-free crust firmly and chill well to prevent crumbling.

Tropical Vegan Chocolate Cheesecake

Add 2 tablespoons finely shredded unsweetened coconut to the filling for a tropical flair. Top with toasted coconut flakes before serving for extra texture and flavor.

Note: Fold shredded coconut into the filling after blending to maintain texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cheesecake can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered. Beyond that, refrigeration is necessary to maintain texture and prevent spoilage.

Refrigerator

Store the cheesecake in an airtight container or cover tightly with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 5 days. The texture will firm up more when cold; serve slightly softened for best flavor.

Freezer

Freeze the cheesecake wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, ensure no air exposure. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before serving.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Let the cheesecake sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before slicing to soften slightly. Avoid microwaving as it can change the texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the crust and filling 1-2 days ahead. Assemble the cheesecake and chill it overnight for best results. This makes it perfect for party prep.

FAQ

Can I use other nuts instead of cashews?
Cashews are preferred because of their creamy texture when soaked and blended. Almonds or macadamias can be used but may result in a grittier or less smooth filling. If using other nuts, soak them well and consider straining the mixture for smoothness.
What if I don’t have coconut cream?
If you only have canned coconut milk, refrigerate it overnight and scoop out the thick cream layer on top to use. Using light coconut milk will produce a thinner, less creamy cheesecake that may not set well.
How long does the cheesecake need to chill?
For best texture and flavor, chill the cheesecake at least 4 hours, but overnight chilling is ideal. This allows the fats to solidify and the flavors to meld, resulting in a firm yet creamy dessert.
Can I make this cheesecake nut-free?
This recipe relies heavily on cashews for creaminess, so nut-free substitutions are tricky. Some use silken tofu or soaked sunflower seeds, but the texture and flavor will differ significantly.
Why is my cheesecake grainy?
Graininess usually means the cashews weren’t soaked long enough or the blender isn’t powerful enough to fully puree them. Soak cashews overnight and blend thoroughly, scraping down the sides frequently.
Can I bake this cheesecake?
This recipe is designed as a no-bake cheesecake. Baking would change the texture and likely dry it out. If you want a baked vegan cheesecake, look for recipes specifically formulated for that method.
How do I store leftovers?
Store leftovers covered tightly in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. The cheesecake can also be frozen for up to 3 months. Always thaw in the fridge before serving.
Can I use a different sweetener?
Yes, you can substitute maple syrup with agave, brown rice syrup, or even coconut sugar dissolved in a bit of water. Adjust sweetness to taste, but maple syrup adds a nice depth of flavor.
