Decadent Flourless Chocolate Brownies with Fresh Raspberries

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These fudgy flourless brownies are pure chocolate bliss with a surprising zing from fresh raspberries. Super simple, gluten-free, and perfect for any time you crave a rich treat without the flour fuss.

Pro Tips

Use room temperature eggs for better emulsification.
Room temperature eggs blend more smoothly with melted chocolate, preventing the chocolate from seizing or clumping. This results in a silky batter that bakes evenly and produces a tender texture.
Fold the chocolate into the eggs gently to keep air in the batter.
Gentle folding preserves the air whipped into the eggs, which is essential for creating a light structure in flourless brownies. Overmixing deflates the batter, leading to dense, heavy brownies.
Line your pan with parchment paper and use the overhang for easy removal.
Parchment prevents sticking and allows you to lift the brownies out without breaking. This keeps edges intact and makes slicing neater.
Don't skip the cooling step before slicing.
Cooling lets the brownies set and firm up, preventing them from crumbling or sticking. Warm brownies are too soft and fragile to cut cleanly.
Watch your baking time carefully to avoid drying out.
Flourless brownies can quickly go from fudgy to dry if overbaked. Checking for moist crumbs on a toothpick helps you catch the perfect moment to pull them out.
Use a sharp knife wiped clean between cuts for tidy slices.
Chocolate can stick to knives, causing messy edges. Cleaning the blade between cuts keeps slices looking professional and appealing.
Add raspberries just before serving for freshness.
Adding fruit too early can cause moisture to seep into the brownies, making them soggy. Fresh raspberries add a pleasant tart contrast and vibrant color.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overbaking the brownies

Why it happens Many assume brownies need to be fully set like cake, so they bake them too long.

Fix Check for moist crumbs on a toothpick around 25 minutes. Pull the brownies out when the center is just set but still fudgy. This keeps them moist and rich.

Using cold eggs straight from the fridge

Why it happens It’s common to rush and not bring eggs to room temp, but cold eggs can shock the melted chocolate.

Fix Let eggs sit at room temperature for at least 20 minutes before mixing. This prevents the chocolate from seizing and ensures a smooth batter.

Skipping parchment paper in the pan

Why it happens Some think greasing the pan is enough for non-stick, but this recipe is sticky and delicate.

Fix Always line the pan with parchment paper, using an overhang to easily lift the brownies out intact after baking.

Overmixing the batter after adding chocolate

Why it happens Eager cooks often stir vigorously to combine ingredients quickly.

Fix Fold gently with a spatula until just combined to keep the air bubbles intact, which results in a lighter texture.

Adding raspberries before baking

Why it happens Some want the fruit baked in, but raspberries release moisture.

Fix Add fresh raspberries on top after baking and cooling to maintain their texture and prevent sogginess.

Variations

Nutty Flourless Brownies

Add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter for a crunchy contrast to the fudgy texture. Nuts bring earthiness and extra richness that pairs beautifully with dark chocolate.

Note: Fold nuts gently into the batter before pouring into the pan to avoid overmixing.

Spiced Chocolate Brownies

Mix in 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the batter for a subtle warmth that enhances the chocolate’s depth. This variation adds complexity and a little kick.

Note: Add spices with the sugar and salt during whisking to distribute evenly.

Vegan Flourless Brownies

Replace eggs with 3/4 cup unsweetened applesauce and use dairy-free dark chocolate. The texture will be a bit softer and less airy but still deliciously fudgy and rich.

Note: Baking time may increase by 5-7 minutes; check doneness carefully.

Salted Caramel Flourless Brownies

Drizzle salted caramel sauce over the batter before baking or swirl it in gently. The sweet-salty combo elevates these brownies to a decadent treat.

Note: Be careful not to overmix the caramel into the batter to keep distinct layers.

Orange Zest Brownies

Add 1 tablespoon finely grated orange zest to the batter for a fresh citrus note that brightens the chocolate flavor. Works wonderfully if you love a hint of fruitiness.

Note: Mix the zest with the sugar and salt to evenly infuse the flavor.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the brownies covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them in an airtight container to prevent drying out. They’re best eaten within this time for optimal fudginess and raspberry freshness.

Refrigerator

Refrigerate brownies in an airtight container for up to 5 days. The texture will firm up and become denser when cold. Bring to room temperature or warm gently before serving to soften and enhance flavor.

Freezer

Freeze brownies tightly wrapped in plastic wrap and foil for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. To restore softness, warm briefly in the microwave or oven before serving.

Reheating

Reheat brownies gently in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 15-20 seconds. Add a splash of water or cover loosely to retain moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

You can bake the brownies up to 2 days ahead and store them covered in the fridge. Add fresh raspberries just before serving to keep them bright and fresh. This makes it easy to prep dessert in advance without losing quality.

FAQ

Can I use milk chocolate instead of dark chocolate?
You can, but milk chocolate has more sugar and milk solids, which changes the texture and sweetness. The brownies will be softer and sweeter, and the deep chocolate flavor will be less intense. If you prefer milk chocolate, reduce added sugar slightly to balance sweetness.
What if my chocolate seizes when melting?
Chocolate seizes if it comes into contact with water or overheats. To fix it, add a small amount of warm cream or butter and stir gently until smooth. To prevent seizing, melt chocolate slowly over low heat or use a double boiler and keep utensils dry.
Can I substitute eggs for a vegan version?
Yes, but eggs provide structure and richness hard to replicate exactly. Use 3/4 cup unsweetened applesauce or a commercial egg replacer. Expect a softer, less airy brownie. Adjust baking time and check doneness carefully.
Why do my brownies turn out dry?
Dry brownies often result from overbaking or not enough fat. Check baking time closely and remove when a toothpick shows moist crumbs. Also, use good quality dark chocolate with enough cocoa butter for richness.
Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?
Absolutely! Chopped nuts, chocolate chips, or dried fruit can be folded in before baking. Just be gentle to keep the batter light. Mix-ins add texture and flavor variety.
How should I store leftover brownies?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature up to 2 days, or refrigerate up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze wrapped tightly for up to 3 months. Thaw before serving.
Why do my brownies crack on top?
A crackly top is a hallmark of good brownies caused by sugar crystallization during baking. It indicates the right balance of sugar and egg. Don’t worry—it’s a good thing!
Can I bake these brownies at higher altitude?
At high altitudes, reduce sugar slightly and increase baking time by a few minutes. The lower air pressure affects moisture evaporation, so watch closely for doneness.

What You'll Need

8 brownies

  • 8 ounces dark chocolate, chopped
  • 6 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving some overhang for easy removal. This ensures your brownies won’t stick and makes clean-up easier. It’s important to use parchment paper for this flourless recipe since the batter is quite delicate and sticky. Avoid greasing the pan as it can cause the edges to become oily and interfere with the crackly top texture.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Pan fully lined with parchment, oven preheated
Step 2
Melt the chopped dark chocolate gently using a double boiler or microwave in short bursts. Stir frequently to avoid burning. This slow melting preserves the chocolate’s smooth texture and prevents it from seizing. If the chocolate overheats, it can become grainy and ruin the batter’s consistency. Let it cool slightly before mixing with eggs.
⏱ Time: 8
✓ Doneness: Chocolate is smooth, glossy, and cooled to warm temperature
Step 3
In a large bowl, whisk the eggs, sugar, salt, and vanilla extract until pale and slightly thickened. This step incorporates air into the eggs which helps create a light structure in the otherwise dense brownie. Don’t rush here or skip whisking thoroughly, or the brownies could turn out too dense.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Egg mixture is pale yellow and slightly fluffy
Step 4
Slowly pour the melted chocolate into the egg mixture, folding gently with a spatula. Use a folding motion to combine without deflating the air you just incorporated. Overmixing can make brownies tough, so be gentle and stop once no streaks remain.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and even in color
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread evenly. Tap the pan lightly on the counter to release any air bubbles. This helps ensure even baking and a smooth top surface. Resist the urge to swirl or disturb the batter once poured, as this can break the delicate structure.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread with no large bubbles
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes. Start checking around 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. This ensures the brownies are fudgy inside but fully set on the outside. Overbaking will dry them out, so watch carefully.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out with moist crumbs, top is crackly
Step 7
Remove from oven and let the brownies cool completely in the pan. Cooling allows the brownies to set fully and makes them easier to cut. If you try to slice them warm, they’ll be too soft and may crumble or stick.
⏱ Time: 60
✓ Doneness: Brownies feel firm to the touch and cool to room temperature
Step 8
Once cooled, lift the brownies out using the parchment overhang and place on a cutting board. Slice into 8 squares with a sharp knife, wiping the blade clean between cuts to keep edges neat. The contrast between the fudgy interior and crackly top is best enjoyed with clean cuts.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cleanly cut brownies with intact edges
Step 9
Top each brownie with fresh raspberries just before serving. The tartness of the raspberries cuts through the richness of the chocolate beautifully, adding a fresh burst of flavor and a lovely pop of color. You can also serve with a dusting of powdered sugar or a dollop of whipped cream if you like.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Fresh raspberries arranged attractively on brownies
