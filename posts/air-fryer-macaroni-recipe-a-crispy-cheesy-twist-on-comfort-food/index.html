Crispy Air Fryer Macaroni and Cheese with Golden Cheesy Topper

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 29, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This air fryer macaroni and cheese nails that perfect balance: creamy pasta underneath with a crispy, golden cheese crust on top. The broil step in the air fryer is a game changer. Great for cozy dinners!

Introduction

I’ve always been a sucker for classic macaroni and cheese, but making it in the air fryer? That was a game changer I discovered by accident. One evening, I wanted that crispy, golden cheese topping you get from the oven broiler but faster and less messy. So, I tried finishing the mac and cheese in my air fryer, and wow — the crunch was incredible without drying out the creamy pasta beneath. What makes this version stand out is the quick broil step in the air fryer that crisps up the cheese and panko crumbs perfectly. Plus, I use a simple homemade cheese sauce with mustard and garlic powder to add depth without complexity. The trick I learned is to cook the pasta just shy of al dente before mixing it with the sauce, so it finishes cooking in the creamy mixture without turning mushy. The result is a comforting, cheesy dish with a delightful textural contrast — creamy, tender pasta underneath and a crisp, golden crust on top. It’s perfect for weeknight dinners or whenever you need a cozy meal that feels like a warm hug. I hope you love it as much as my family does!

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 8 ounces elbow macaroni, uncooked
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese, divided
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup panko breadcrumbs (optional for extra crunch)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the elbow macaroni and cook it for about 6 minutes, just until it's slightly undercooked (al dente). This is crucial because the pasta will finish cooking later in the sauce, and you don’t want it to get mushy. Be sure to stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Drain the pasta and set it aside.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be tender but still firm to the bite without being crunchy
Step 2
In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in the flour to create a roux, cooking for about 2 minutes until it turns a light golden color. This step is important because the roux thickens the cheese sauce and prevents it from tasting floury. Keep whisking to avoid lumps and ensure smoothness.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Roux should be smooth, slightly thickened, and have a nutty aroma
Step 3
Slowly pour in the milk while whisking constantly to avoid clumps. Keep whisking and cooking the mixture over medium heat until it thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon, around 5-7 minutes. Don’t rush this step; the sauce needs time to thicken properly for that creamy texture.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce coats the back of the spoon thickly but still pourable
Step 4
Remove the sauce from heat and stir in the Dijon mustard, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Then gradually add 1 1/2 cups of shredded cheddar and the mozzarella, stirring until melted and smooth. The mustard adds a subtle tang that lifts the cheese flavor, making it more complex and less one-note.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheese is fully melted into a glossy, smooth sauce
Step 5
Combine the cooked macaroni with the cheese sauce in a mixing bowl or directly in your baking dish. Stir gently to coat all the pasta evenly. This step ensures every bite is cheesy and creamy, not just the top layer.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta is fully coated and the mixture looks thick and creamy
Step 6
Transfer the mac and cheese mixture to a lightly greased, air fryer-safe baking dish. Sprinkle the remaining 1/2 cup cheddar cheese evenly on top. If you want extra crunch, add a thin layer of panko breadcrumbs over the cheese. This topping crisps beautifully in the air fryer and adds texture contrast.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Top is evenly covered with cheese and breadcrumbs (if using)
Step 7
Preheat your air fryer to 350°F (175°C). Place the baking dish inside and cook for 15 minutes. This heats the whole dish through and allows the pasta to finish cooking in the sauce without drying out. Keep an eye on it to prevent overbaking.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bubbles around the edges and cheese is melted and gooey
Step 8
Switch the air fryer to broil mode (or highest temperature) and cook for an additional 3-5 minutes until the top turns golden brown and crispy. Watch closely here—broiling too long can burn the cheese, so pull it out as soon as it reaches that perfect golden crust.
⏱ Time: 3-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Top is golden brown, bubbling, and crisp to the touch
Step 9
Let the mac and cheese cool for about 5 minutes before serving. This resting time helps the sauce thicken slightly and makes it easier to serve. Plus, it’s the perfect moment to grab your favorite sides or a fresh salad to balance the richness.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is warm but not scalding, sauce has thickened slightly

Pro Tips

Cook pasta just until slightly underdone to avoid mushy mac and cheese.
Pasta continues cooking when mixed with the hot cheese sauce and again in the air fryer. Starting with al dente pasta ensures it finishes perfectly tender without turning mushy, preserving texture and bite.
Use freshly shredded cheese instead of pre-shredded for smoother melting.
Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents that inhibit melting and can cause a grainy sauce. Freshly shredded cheese melts evenly, giving a creamier, silkier texture and better flavor.
Whisk constantly while making the roux and adding milk to prevent lumps.
Continuous whisking ensures the flour disperses evenly and the milk integrates smoothly. This prevents clumping and creates a silky sauce that clings well to the pasta.
Add a touch of Dijon mustard to the cheese sauce for depth.
Mustard enhances the cheese flavor by adding subtle tang and complexity. It cuts through the richness, preventing the sauce from tasting flat or overly heavy.
Broil the mac and cheese in the air fryer for a few minutes to crisp the top.
The broil function quickly melts and browns the cheese topping, creating a crunchy crust that contrasts with the creamy pasta. Watching carefully prevents burning and achieves the perfect golden finish.
Sprinkle panko breadcrumbs on top for extra crunch.
Panko’s coarse texture crisps beautifully in the air fryer, adding a light, crispy layer that complements the creamy macaroni beneath, enhancing overall mouthfeel.
Let the dish rest for 5 minutes before serving.
Resting allows the cheese sauce to thicken slightly and the flavors to meld, making it easier to serve and enhancing the overall eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the pasta before mixing with sauce

Why it happens Many cooks assume pasta should be fully cooked in boiling water, but since it cooks more in the sauce and air fryer, starting fully cooked leads to mushy results.

Fix Cook pasta for only about 6 minutes or until just under al dente. This way, it finishes cooking in the sauce during air frying, maintaining a tender but firm texture.

Skipping the roux or not cooking it long enough

Why it happens Some may rush through the roux step, leading to a floury taste or lumpy sauce.

Fix Cook the flour and butter together for at least 2 minutes until lightly golden to remove raw flour flavor. Whisk constantly when adding milk to prevent lumps.

Using pre-shredded cheese

Why it happens Pre-shredded cheese contains anti-caking agents that affect melting and texture.

Fix Grate your own cheese fresh for the smoothest melt and creamiest sauce.

Burning the cheese topping during air fryer broil

Why it happens Broiling too long or at too high a temperature causes the cheese to burn quickly.

Fix Watch closely during the last 3-5 minutes of broiling and remove as soon as the cheese is golden and bubbly.

Not letting the dish rest before serving

Why it happens Serving immediately can result in runny sauce and difficulty portioning.

Fix Allow the mac and cheese to rest for 5 minutes so the sauce thickens and flavors meld.

Variations

Spicy Jalapeño Mac and Cheese

Add finely diced jalapeños to the cheese sauce for a spicy kick that pairs beautifully with the creamy pasta. Top with pepper jack cheese instead of cheddar to enhance the heat. This twist adds a vibrant flavor and a little zing to your classic mac and cheese.

Note: Be cautious with jalapeño quantity to avoid overpowering the dish. Remove seeds for milder heat.

Bacon and Caramelized Onion Mac and Cheese

Mix in crispy bacon bits and sweet caramelized onions before air frying. These additions bring smoky and sweet layers to the dish, making it richer and more indulgent. Great for a special occasion or weekend treat.

Note: Cook bacon and onions separately and fully before adding to avoid excess moisture.

Gluten-Free Mac and Cheese

Use gluten-free pasta and substitute the all-purpose flour in the roux with a gluten-free blend or cornstarch. This keeps the recipe accessible without sacrificing creaminess or texture.

Note: Gluten-free flours may thicken differently; adjust quantities slightly for sauce consistency.

Vegan Mac and Cheese

Swap dairy milk for unsweetened plant-based milk, use vegan butter, and replace cheese with a cashew-based vegan cheese sauce. Nutritional yeast adds cheesy flavor. This version is creamy and satisfying without dairy.

Note: Cooking times remain similar, but watch sauce thickness carefully as plant milks behave differently.

Four-Cheese Mac and Cheese

Combine sharp cheddar, mozzarella, Gruyère, and Parmesan for a deeply flavorful, multi-dimensional cheese sauce. The mix of melting and aged cheeses creates a rich, nutty, and gooey mac and cheese experience.

Note: Grate all cheeses fresh and blend gradually for smooth melting.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Mac and cheese can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Cover tightly with foil or plastic wrap to prevent drying out or contamination. Beyond this, refrigeration is recommended to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and the texture slightly firm up when cold. Reheat gently to restore creaminess; avoid overheating to prevent drying.

Freezer

Freeze mac and cheese in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating for best texture.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of milk to loosen the sauce, stirring frequently. Alternatively, microwave in short bursts stirring between. Avoid high heat to prevent sauce separation or drying out.

Make Ahead

Make the cheese sauce and cook pasta up to 1 day ahead. Store separately in airtight containers. Assemble and air fry just before serving for optimal texture and freshness.

FAQ

Can I use frozen macaroni and cheese leftovers in the air fryer?
Yes, you can reheat frozen mac and cheese in the air fryer. Thaw it overnight in the fridge first for even heating. Then air fry at 350°F for about 8-10 minutes until heated through and crispy on top. Adding a splash of milk before reheating helps maintain creaminess.
What if I don’t have an air fryer? Can I make this in the oven?
Absolutely! You can bake the assembled mac and cheese in a preheated oven at 350°F for 20-25 minutes, then broil for a few minutes to brown the top. The air fryer just speeds up the process and crisps the topping beautifully.
How do I prevent the cheese sauce from becoming grainy?
Use freshly shredded cheese and melt it slowly off the heat to avoid overheating, which causes cheese to separate. Also, make sure your roux is smooth and sauce is thickened properly before adding cheese.
Can I substitute the cheddar with other cheeses?
Yes, cheeses like Gruyère, fontina, or Monterey Jack work well. Just keep in mind their meltability and flavor intensity. Mixing cheeses often gives the best flavor and texture.
Is it necessary to add panko breadcrumbs?
No, panko is optional but adds a lovely crunch. If you prefer a softer top, just use cheese alone or sprinkle extra cheese on top for a gooey crust.
How do I make this recipe spicier?
Try adding diced jalapeños or a pinch of cayenne pepper to the cheese sauce. Alternatively, sprinkle crushed red pepper flakes on top before air frying for a spicy finish.
Can I use low-fat milk or non-dairy milk?
Low-fat milk will work but may result in a thinner sauce. Non-dairy milks like almond or oat milk can be used but check for unsweetened versions and expect a slightly different flavor and texture.
How do I store leftover mac and cheese without it drying out?
Keep leftovers in an airtight container and if reheating, add a splash of milk to help restore creaminess. Cover the dish tightly when storing to prevent moisture loss.
