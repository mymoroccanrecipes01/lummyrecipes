Simple 5-Ingredient Chocolate Cake for Busy Weeknights

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This chocolate cake is the easiest fix for sudden sweet cravings. With just five ingredients and no fuss, it’s perfect for busy nights when you want homemade comfort fast.

Introduction

I stumbled on this chocolate cake recipe on a hectic weeknight when I craved something sweet but didn’t have the energy for a complicated bake. What I love about this cake is how minimal the ingredients are—just five pantry staples—and yet it turns out moist and rich every single time. I’ve tried fancy chocolate cakes before, but they usually require a dozen ingredients and tons of steps. This one is different because it’s so straightforward, and you don’t have to worry about complicated techniques. The trick that makes it so good is gently folding the dry ingredients into the wet without overmixing, which keeps the crumb tender and soft. When it comes out of the oven, it’s got a beautiful, slightly crackled top and a deep chocolate flavor that feels indulgent but not overwhelming. I usually make it for an easy dessert after dinner or whenever I just want a quick homemade treat without fuss. Plus, it’s a great base to dress up with whipped cream or fresh berries if you want to get fancy without extra work. I think you’ll love how simple and satisfying it is!

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, serves 6

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup milk, room temperature

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8-inch round cake pan with butter or non-stick spray. This step is crucial to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking. A well-prepared pan makes it easier to remove the cake without tearing the edges, preserving its shape and texture. If you skip greasing, your cake might stick and crumble when you try to take it out. While the oven heats, gather your ingredients to keep the process smooth.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven preheated and pan greased with no dry spots
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, and baking powder until evenly combined. This dry mix is the backbone of your cake's texture and chocolate flavor. Mixing these ingredients well ensures you won’t get any bitter clumps of cocoa or uneven rising from the baking powder. Take your time here to get a uniform powder; it really helps the cake bake evenly without dense pockets.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients form a smooth, uniform mixture with no lumps
Step 3
Add the milk to the dry ingredients and gently fold them together using a spatula or wooden spoon. It’s important not to overmix here; stir just until the flour disappears. Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make the cake tough instead of tender. The batter should be thick but smooth, with no visible streaks of flour. This gentle folding keeps the crumb soft and light.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth, thick, and free of dry flour patches
Step 4
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with the back of a spoon or spatula. This helps the cake bake evenly and gives it a nice flat surface for frosting or toppings. Don’t tap the pan hard or overwork the batter here, or you might lose the air bubbles needed for a light texture. Just a gentle smoothing is enough.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread and level in the pan
Step 5
Bake the cake in the preheated oven for about 30 minutes. The key here is to avoid opening the oven door too often as that lets heat escape and can cause the cake to sink. Around 25 minutes, start checking by inserting a toothpick in the center. If it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, the cake is done. If it’s wet batter, it needs more time.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean or with moist crumbs
Step 6
Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10-15 minutes. This resting time allows it to firm up so it doesn’t break apart when you remove it. Cooling also helps the flavors settle and makes it easier to frost or slice. Avoid cutting it too soon, or the texture might feel gummy and fragile.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake slightly cooled, pulling away from pan edges
Step 7
Run a knife around the edges to loosen the cake from the pan, then carefully invert it onto a wire rack to cool completely. This step prevents condensation from making the bottom soggy. Using a wire rack lets air circulate all around the cake, preserving the perfect texture. If you skip this, the cake might sweat and become mushy underneath.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake released cleanly and cooling evenly on rack
Step 8
Once fully cooled, slice and serve as is or top with your favorite frosting, whipped cream, or fresh berries. The cake’s rich chocolate flavor pairs beautifully with a simple vanilla or cream cheese frosting, but it’s also amazing on its own. Adding toppings is optional and lets you customize the dessert for any occasion.
⏱ Time: Variable
✓ Doneness: Cake cooled completely and ready to decorate or serve
Step 9
Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days. If refrigerated, bring to room temp before eating for best texture. You can also freeze slices to enjoy later, just wrap tightly to avoid freezer burn. Proper storage keeps the cake moist and delicious for several days.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cake stored properly with freshness maintained

Pro Tips

Sift your dry ingredients before mixing.
Sifting aerates the flour and cocoa powder, removing lumps and making it easier to incorporate air. This step helps the cake rise evenly and results in a lighter, fluffier texture. If you skip sifting, you might end up with dense patches or uneven crumb.
Use room temperature milk for best results.
Cold milk can cause the batter to seize or mix unevenly, which affects the cake’s texture and rise. Room temperature milk blends smoothly with dry ingredients and baking powder, ensuring a uniform batter and consistent baking outcome.
Avoid overmixing the batter once liquids are added.
Overmixing develops too much gluten, making the cake tough and dense rather than tender. Stir just until the flour disappears to keep the crumb soft and moist. This balance is key to a perfect homemade cake texture.
Don’t open the oven door during baking.
Opening the door lets heat escape and causes temperature fluctuations, which can make the cake collapse or bake unevenly. Wait until the last few minutes to check doneness to keep the rise steady and the texture perfect.
Cool the cake in the pan before removing.
Letting the cake cool slightly in the pan helps it firm up and prevents it from breaking apart when unmolded. This resting period also allows the crumb to set, making slicing and decorating easier.
Use a wire rack for cooling after removing from pan.
A wire rack allows air to circulate around the cake, preventing condensation which can make the bottom soggy. This keeps the crust crisp and the interior moist.
Measure ingredients precisely using the spoon-and-level method.
Scooping flour directly with a cup can pack it too densely, leading to a dry, heavy cake. Spoon the flour into the measuring cup and level it off with a knife for accuracy, ensuring the batter has the right balance of dry and wet ingredients.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter after adding milk

Why it happens Many cooks think more mixing makes a smoother batter, but it actually activates gluten excessively, resulting in a tough cake.

Fix Mix gently and only until the flour disappears. Use a spatula to fold ingredients instead of stirring vigorously. This keeps the cake tender and light.

Using expired baking powder

Why it happens Baking powder loses its effectiveness over time, but it’s easy to overlook the expiration date, assuming it’ll still work fine.

Fix Test baking powder by adding a teaspoon to warm water; it should bubble vigorously. Replace if it doesn’t. Fresh baking powder ensures proper rise and texture.

Not greasing the pan properly

Why it happens Skipping or unevenly greasing causes the cake to stick, making it hard to remove without breaking.

Fix Use butter or non-stick spray generously and consider dusting with flour. This creates a barrier so the cake releases cleanly.

Opening the oven door too early or too often

Why it happens Impatience or curiosity can cause cooks to peek frequently, causing temperature drops that affect rising.

Fix Set a timer and resist opening until the last 5 minutes. This steady heat keeps the cake fluffy and moist.

Using cold milk straight from the fridge

Why it happens Cold ingredients can shock the leavening agents, slowing reaction and causing uneven mixing.

Fix Bring milk to room temperature before mixing. This helps the batter come together smoothly and bake evenly.

Variations

Add Coffee for Deeper Chocolate Flavor

Swap half the milk with brewed coffee or espresso to amplify the chocolate notes. The coffee’s bitterness enhances the cocoa without adding a coffee taste. Expect a richer, more intense flavor with the same moist texture.

Note: Use cooled coffee to avoid affecting baking powder’s reaction. Adjust liquid ratios slightly if the coffee is very strong or concentrated.

Gluten-Free Version

Replace all-purpose flour with a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend. This lets those with gluten sensitivities enjoy the cake with minimal texture changes. The crumb might be slightly more crumbly but still deliciously moist.

Note: Choose a blend with xanthan gum included for best structure. Avoid overmixing to prevent toughness.

Vegan Chocolate Cake

Use plant-based milk like almond or oat and replace milk with the same amount of non-dairy milk. Add a tablespoon of apple cider vinegar to react with baking powder and help rise. This version is dairy-free and still incredibly moist.

Note: Mix the vinegar with plant milk and let it sit 5 minutes before adding to dry ingredients to create a buttermilk effect.

Add Chocolate Chips or Chunks

Fold in 1/2 cup of dark or semi-sweet chocolate chips into the batter for melty pockets of chocolate. This adds texture and an extra chocolate punch, making the cake more indulgent and fun.

Note: Coat chips lightly in flour before folding to prevent sinking to the bottom.

Frosted Layer Cake

Double the recipe and bake in two pans, then layer with your favorite frosting—cream cheese, buttercream, or ganache. This turns the simple cake into a showstopper perfect for celebrations.

Note: Make sure cakes are completely cooled before frosting to avoid melting.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cake covered at room temperature for up to 3 days. Use a cake dome or airtight container to prevent drying out. The texture stays soft and moist, perfect for quick enjoyment without refrigeration.

Refrigerator

Keep the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The cake may firm up and the crumb become denser when cold. Bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving to restore softness.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 2 months. Prevent freezer burn by removing as much air as possible. Thaw overnight in the fridge and then bring to room temperature before serving for best texture.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Add a small splash of milk or water if it feels dry. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out the crumb.

Make Ahead

You can mix the dry ingredients and store them in an airtight container 1-2 days ahead. Add milk and bake fresh when ready. Alternatively, bake the cake fully and store as described, frosting just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use baking soda instead of baking powder?
Baking soda requires an acidic ingredient to activate, which this recipe lacks. Using it alone will result in little rise and a soapy taste. If you want to substitute, add an acid like vinegar or lemon juice, but it’s easier to stick with baking powder for consistent results.
Why does my cake sometimes come out dry?
Dry cake often results from overbaking or too much flour. Make sure to measure flour correctly (spoon and level), and check doneness early with a toothpick. Also, avoid overmixing which can dry out the crumb.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes, swapping all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend with xanthan gum works well. The texture might be slightly different—more crumbly but still moist. Be gentle mixing to avoid toughness.
How do I store leftover cake?
Keep leftover cake in an airtight container at room temp for 3 days or refrigerated up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze well wrapped for up to 2 months. Bring to room temp before eating for best flavor and texture.
Can I add eggs to this recipe?
This recipe is designed without eggs for simplicity, but you can add one egg for extra richness and structure. You’d need to adjust the milk slightly to keep the batter balanced.
What frosting pairs best with this cake?
Cream cheese frosting, simple buttercream, or chocolate ganache all complement this cake beautifully. The cake’s rich chocolate base pairs well with both sweet and tangy frostings.
Why does my cake sink in the middle?
Sinking usually means the cake was underbaked or the oven temperature was too low. Make sure your oven is fully preheated and test doneness with a toothpick before removing. Avoid opening the oven door too early.
Can I make this cake vegan?
Yes! Substitute milk with plant-based milk and add a tablespoon of vinegar to mimic buttermilk’s acidity. This helps the baking powder work properly. The texture will be slightly different but still tasty.
