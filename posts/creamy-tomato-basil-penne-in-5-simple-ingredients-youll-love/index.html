Creamy Tomato Basil Penne That’s Ready in Under 30 Minutes

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 12, 2026
10 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy tomato basil penne is my go-to when I want something comforting but quick. The secret is a splash of cream that mellows the tangy tomato and fresh basil perfectly. Great for busy weeknights or when you need a cozy dinner fast!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this creamy tomato basil penne when I was craving something quick and comforting after a long day. I usually keep a few simple ingredients on hand, and this dish fits perfectly into that category. What makes my version stand out is the splash of cream I add to balance the acidity of the tomatoes and the fresh punch of basil. It’s a little trick I picked up after a few attempts where the sauce felt too sharp or watery. The cream gives it that velvety texture that feels indulgent, but it’s still light enough to enjoy any night of the week. Plus, it’s super easy to pull together, which is a lifesaver on busy evenings. When it’s done, you get a vibrant red sauce coating perfectly cooked penne, flecked with green basil and a hint of garlic, making every bite a cozy delight. I love serving it with a sprinkle of Parmesan and a side salad for a fuss-free meal that never disappoints. Honestly, this pasta has become a staple in my house because it’s simple, satisfying, and feels like a warm hug on a plate.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 12 ounces penne pasta, uncooked
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Adding enough salt is crucial because it seasons the pasta from the inside as it cooks, enhancing its flavor. Once boiling, add the penne pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente—usually around 10-12 minutes. Be sure not to overcook it, or the pasta will become mushy when combined with the sauce. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Drain the pasta but reserve about 1/2 cup of the pasta water to adjust the sauce consistency later if needed.
⏱ Time: 12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be tender but still firm to the bite (al dente) and not mushy.
Step 2
While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant but not browned. Browning garlic can make it bitter, so keep an eye on it and stir constantly. This step infuses the oil with garlic flavor, which forms the base of your sauce. Adding garlic first allows its aroma to develop fully and carry through the dish.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Garlic should be golden and fragrant, not dark or burnt.
Step 3
Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir to combine with the garlic oil. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and let it cook for about 8 minutes, stirring occasionally. This simmering reduces excess water from the tomatoes and concentrates the flavors, creating a richer sauce. Don’t rush this step; a slow simmer helps mellow the acidity and brings out the natural sweetness of the tomatoes.
⏱ Time: 8 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce should thicken slightly and have a deep, rich red color.
Step 4
Lower the heat and stir in the heavy cream. This addition is the magic touch that transforms the sauce from tangy to luxuriously creamy. Stir well until the cream is fully incorporated, creating a smooth, velvety texture. If the sauce feels too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up. The cream also balances the acidity of the tomatoes, making the sauce more mellow and comforting.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce should be smooth, creamy, and slightly lighter in color.
Step 5
Add the chopped fresh basil to the sauce, stirring it in just before combining with the pasta. Fresh basil adds bright herbal notes and a lovely aroma that elevates the dish. Adding it too early can cause the basil to lose its vibrant flavor and color, so wait until the sauce is nearly finished. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste, adjusting as needed.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Basil should look vibrant and fresh, not wilted or dark.
Step 6
Add the drained pasta directly into the skillet with the sauce. Toss gently but thoroughly to coat each piece of penne with the creamy tomato basil sauce. If the sauce needs loosening, add a little more reserved pasta water, a tablespoon at a time, until you reach your desired consistency. This ensures every bite is flavorful and the pasta isn’t dry or clumpy.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be evenly coated with sauce, glossy and smooth.
Step 7
Taste the pasta and adjust seasoning if needed. Sometimes a pinch more salt or pepper makes all the difference. If you want a little heat, a pinch of red pepper flakes works wonders here. Remember, seasoning is key to bringing out the full flavors of the dish.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with no overpowering acidity or blandness.
Step 8
Serve the creamy tomato basil penne immediately. Garnish with a generous sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese if you like, which adds a salty, nutty finish that pairs perfectly with the creamy sauce. Adding cheese at the end also melts slightly into the warm pasta, enhancing texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dish looks inviting with melted cheese and fresh basil on top.
Step 9
Optionally, serve with a simple side salad or crusty bread to make it a complete meal. The freshness of the salad or crunch of the bread complements the creamy pasta perfectly. Enjoy it right away for the best texture and flavor, as the sauce thickens if left to sit.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Complete meal balanced in texture and flavor.

Pro Tips

Reserve some pasta water before draining.
The starchy water helps loosen the sauce and binds it to the pasta for a creamy, cohesive texture. Without it, the sauce might be too thick or separate from the noodles.
Add fresh basil at the very end.
Basil's flavor and vibrant color fade quickly when cooked, so adding it last preserves its bright aroma and fresh taste, lifting the whole dish.
Don’t overcook the garlic.
Garlic burns easily and becomes bitter, which can ruin the sauce’s flavor. Sauté it just until fragrant and golden to keep a mellow, sweet garlic note.
Simmer the tomato sauce gently.
A slow simmer concentrates flavors and thickens the sauce without burning. Rushing this step can lead to watery sauce and less depth of flavor.
Use high-quality canned tomatoes.
Good tomatoes are the foundation of your sauce. Quality affects sweetness, acidity, and overall flavor, so invest in a reputable brand for best results.
Toss pasta and sauce off the heat.
Mixing off the heat prevents the cream from curdling and keeps the sauce smooth and silky, ensuring a perfect texture.
Season gradually and taste often.
Salt and pepper levels can change as ingredients combine and cook down. Tasting frequently helps you avoid over- or under-seasoning.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the pasta

Why it happens Many assume softer pasta is better, but overcooked pasta becomes mushy and doesn't hold sauce well.

Fix Cook pasta al dente by following package timing and tasting early. Drain promptly and toss with sauce to prevent sogginess.

Burning the garlic

Why it happens Garlic cooks quickly and can turn bitter if left too long or cooked at too high heat.

Fix Sauté garlic on medium heat just until fragrant and golden, stirring constantly to avoid dark spots.

Skipping the reserved pasta water

Why it happens People often discard pasta water without realizing its role in loosening sauce and helping it cling to pasta.

Fix Always save a small cup of pasta water before draining and add it gradually to adjust sauce consistency.

Adding basil too early

Why it happens Some add basil at the start, causing it to lose flavor and turn dull in color.

Fix Add fresh basil only at the end of cooking to preserve its bright flavor and color.

Using low-fat cream or milk substitutes

Why it happens Lower-fat options don't emulsify well and can cause the sauce to separate or be thin.

Fix Use heavy cream or full-fat alternatives to ensure a rich, stable sauce.

Variations

Spicy Creamy Tomato Basil Penne

Add a pinch or two of red pepper flakes when sautéing the garlic to give the sauce a subtle heat that balances the creamy richness. This variation adds a nice kick without overpowering the fresh tomato and basil flavors.

Note: Simmer the sauce gently to allow the heat to infuse evenly; avoid cooking too fast or the spice may become harsh.

Vegan Tomato Basil Penne

Swap out the heavy cream for coconut cream or a cashew cream sauce to keep it creamy without dairy. Use nutritional yeast instead of Parmesan for a cheesy flavor. It’s a great plant-based twist that’s just as comforting.

Note: Adjust seasoning carefully as coconut cream can add sweetness; balance with a splash of lemon juice if needed.

Add Roasted Vegetables

Roast bell peppers, zucchini, or eggplant and stir them into the sauce for extra texture and depth. This adds a hearty veggie boost and makes the dish more filling.

Note: Roast vegetables separately to avoid wateriness and add them at the end to maintain their texture.

Use Fresh Tomatoes

In season, swap canned tomatoes for peeled, chopped fresh tomatoes. Cook them down longer to develop flavor. This gives the sauce a fresher, lighter taste but requires more time.

Note: Simmer fresh tomatoes for 20-30 minutes to reduce excess water and concentrate flavors.

Pesto Tomato Basil Penne

Stir in a spoonful of basil pesto at the end for an herby, nutty layer of flavor that complements the creamy tomato sauce beautifully. It’s a quick way to elevate the dish.

Note: Add pesto off the heat to preserve its bright color and fresh flavor.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This pasta is best eaten fresh but can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce thickens in the fridge; reheat gently with a splash of water or cream to loosen it. Serving cold is possible but the texture and flavor are best warmed.

Freezer

Freeze the pasta in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating. The texture may soften slightly after freezing.

Reheating

Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat, stirring often and adding a splash of cream or water to restore creaminess. Microwave reheating works but may cause uneven heating and sauce separation if overheated.

Make Ahead

Prepare the sauce up to 2 days ahead and refrigerate. Cook pasta fresh on serving day, then toss with warmed sauce for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use milk instead of heavy cream?
Milk can be used but the sauce won’t be as rich or creamy and may separate when heated. Heavy cream’s higher fat content stabilizes the sauce and gives it that velvety texture, so I recommend sticking with cream for best results.
What if I don’t have fresh basil?
For best flavor, fresh basil is recommended. Dried basil can be used but add it earlier in cooking to infuse flavor.
How do I prevent the cream from curdling?
Add cream off the heat or on very low heat and stir constantly. High heat can cause the cream to separate. Also, avoid boiling the sauce once cream is added to keep it smooth and silky.
Can I make this gluten-free?
Absolutely! Just swap regular penne for your favorite gluten-free pasta and cook according to its package instructions. The sauce remains the same and works perfectly with gluten-free noodles.
Is this recipe freezer-friendly?
Yes, you can freeze leftovers, but the texture of the pasta may soften a bit after thawing. Freeze in an airtight container and thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating gently to restore creaminess.
What cheese works best as a topping?
Grated Parmesan is my favorite because it melts nicely and adds salty, nutty flavor. Pecorino Romano or Asiago also work well if you want something a bit tangier.
How can I make this recipe vegan?
Replace heavy cream with coconut cream or a cashew cream sauce, and skip the Parmesan or use nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor. Use olive oil as usual and fresh basil for the herbiness.
Can I add protein to this dish?
Definitely! Grilled chicken, sautéed shrimp, or crispy tofu are great additions. Add them just before serving to keep their texture and blend with the creamy sauce.
