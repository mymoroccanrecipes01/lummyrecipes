Garlicky Lemon Chicken with Crispy Roasted Brussels Sprouts That’s Ready in Under an Hour

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 4, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This garlicky lemon chicken is light and bursting with flavor, paired with crispy roasted Brussels sprouts for a healthy, satisfying meal. Perfect for weeknights when you want something nourishing and easy.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this garlicky lemon chicken recipe on a busy weeknight when I wanted something quick but still full of flavor. I’d tried lemon chicken before, but this version with its punchy garlic and bright lemon zest quickly became a staple in my kitchen. What makes it stand out is the balance between the juicy chicken and those crispy, caramelized Brussels sprouts roasted alongside it. I love how the lemon juice and zest brighten the dish without overpowering the savory garlic notes. One trick I learned is to give the chicken thighs a quick sear before roasting to lock in moisture and get a lovely golden crust. Also, roasting the Brussels sprouts with a little olive oil and finishing them with balsamic vinegar adds a subtle sweetness that pairs perfectly with the lemony chicken. The finished dish looks rustic but vibrant, with golden chicken and slightly charred, crispy Brussels sprouts. It’s light enough for a weeknight but comforting enough to feel like a real treat. I usually serve it with a side of rice or crusty bread to soak up the juices, and my family always asks for seconds. Honestly, it’s a recipe I keep coming back to because it’s simple, healthy, and just downright delicious.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken thighs, trimmed
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). This high temperature helps the chicken skin crisp up nicely while roasting the Brussels sprouts to a perfect caramelized finish. If the oven isn’t hot enough, you’ll miss that golden color and the sprouts might steam instead of roast, leaving them soggy.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully heated to 425°F, ready for roasting
Step 2
Pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture. This step is crucial because dry skin crisps up better in the oven. If you skip this, the chicken skin will steam, resulting in a less appetizing texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin feels dry to the touch, no visible moisture
Step 3
In a large bowl, combine 2 tablespoons olive oil, minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Toss the chicken thighs in this marinade, making sure each piece is well coated. The acid from the lemon juice tenderizes the meat while the garlic infuses deep flavor. Let it sit for 10 minutes if you have time, but you can cook immediately if pressed.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes (optional marinating)
✓ Doneness: Chicken evenly coated with marinade and slightly glossy
Step 4
While the chicken marinates, prep the Brussels sprouts by trimming their ends and slicing them in half. Toss them in 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper. The oil helps them roast evenly and develop that crispy exterior we all love.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brussels sprouts are evenly coated and shiny but not dripping
Step 5
Heat a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the chicken thighs skin-side down. Sear for 3-4 minutes without moving them so the skin crisps up and browns beautifully. This step locks in juices and adds flavor through the Maillard reaction.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin is golden brown and releases easily from the pan
Step 6
Flip the chicken thighs and scatter the prepared Brussels sprouts around them in the skillet. Make sure the sprouts are in a single layer for the best roasting. This arrangement allows everything to cook evenly and the chicken juices to mingle with the sprouts.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken flipped and Brussels sprouts arranged evenly in skillet
Step 7
Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Roast for about 20-25 minutes until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) and the Brussels sprouts are tender and caramelized. Don’t rush this step — undercooked chicken is unsafe, and the sprouts need time to get crispy edges.
⏱ Time: 20-25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is cooked through (no pink inside), sprouts are golden and crispy
Step 8
Optional: Drizzle the roasted Brussels sprouts with a tablespoon of balsamic vinegar once out of the oven and toss gently. This adds a lovely tangy sweetness that contrasts beautifully with the garlic and lemon flavors.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sprouts glistening with a light tangy coating
Step 9
Garnish the chicken and sprouts with freshly chopped parsley before serving. This adds a fresh herbal note and a pop of green color that makes the dish look inviting and finished.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dish looks vibrant and fresh with green parsley scattered on top

Pro Tips

Always pat your chicken dry before seasoning and cooking.
Removing surface moisture allows the skin to crisp properly during searing and roasting. If the chicken is wet, it will steam instead of brown, resulting in a soggy texture and less flavor development.
Let the chicken marinate for at least 10 minutes before cooking.
Allowing the lemon juice, garlic, and herbs to penetrate the meat tenderizes it and enhances flavor. Even a short marinating time makes a noticeable difference in juiciness and aroma.
Use an oven-safe skillet to sear and roast the chicken and vegetables together.
This saves time and minimizes cleanup. Plus, roasting everything in one pan lets the flavors meld as the chicken juices mingle with the Brussels sprouts, boosting overall taste.
Don’t overcrowd the Brussels sprouts on the pan.
Crowding traps steam and prevents proper caramelization, leaving the sprouts soggy. Give them space to brown evenly and get that crispy texture everyone loves.
Finish the Brussels sprouts with a splash of balsamic vinegar after roasting.
This adds a sweet acidity that complements the lemony chicken and balances the natural bitterness of the sprouts, elevating the flavor profile with minimal effort.
Use a meat thermometer to ensure chicken is perfectly cooked.
Chicken thighs can be tricky to judge by touch alone. Checking for 165°F internal temperature guarantees safety and juiciness without overcooking.
Let the chicken rest a few minutes after roasting before serving.
Resting allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, resulting in a moister bite. Cutting immediately can cause the juice to spill out, leaving the chicken dry.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sear and going straight to the oven.

Why it happens Many assume roasting alone is enough for crispy skin, but the sear creates the Maillard reaction that browns and crisps the chicken surface.

Fix Always preheat a skillet and sear the chicken skin-side down for several minutes before roasting. This locks in moisture and develops deep flavor and texture.

Overcrowding the Brussels sprouts on the pan.

Why it happens To fit everything in one pan, cooks sometimes pile the sprouts too closely, causing steaming instead of roasting.

Fix Arrange the sprouts in a single layer with space between pieces. If needed, roast in batches to ensure crispiness.

Using pre-minced garlic from a jar.

Why it happens Pre-minced garlic is convenient, but it can taste bitter or lack the fresh flavor of raw garlic when cooked at high heat.

Fix Always mince fresh garlic cloves yourself. This ensures a vibrant, mellow garlic flavor that complements the lemon perfectly.

Not drying the chicken before cooking.

Why it happens Some skip drying to save time, unaware it affects crispiness.

Fix Pat the chicken dry thoroughly with paper towels before seasoning. This step is essential for golden, crispy skin.

Skipping the balsamic vinegar finish on Brussels sprouts.

Why it happens People often overlook this step, missing the flavor boost it provides.

Fix Drizzle a little balsamic vinegar on the sprouts right after roasting and toss gently. It adds a subtle sweetness and acidity that brightens the dish.

Variations

Spicy Lemon Garlic Chicken

Add ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes to the marinade for a gentle heat that pairs beautifully with the lemon and garlic. This variation adds a kick without overpowering the fresh flavors and works great if you like a little spice.

Note: Since heat can intensify during cooking, adjust the amount of red pepper flakes to your taste and add them gradually.

Creamy Lemon Chicken with Brussels Sprouts

After roasting, stir in a splash of heavy cream or coconut milk to the pan juices to create a silky sauce. This adds richness and makes the dish feel more indulgent, perfect for a cozy night in.

Note: Add the cream after removing the pan from the oven and gently warm it on the stovetop to avoid curdling.

Herb-Infused Lemon Chicken

Include fresh thyme or rosemary sprigs in the marinade and toss some into the roasting pan. These herbs add an earthy aroma and deepen the flavor complexity, making the dish feel more gourmet.

Note: Use fresh herbs for best results and remove woody stems before serving.

Lemon Garlic Chicken with Sweet Potatoes

Swap Brussels sprouts for cubed sweet potatoes tossed in olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast alongside the chicken for a naturally sweet, colorful side that balances the lemony tang.

Note: Sweet potatoes may need slightly longer roasting time; cut into uniform cubes to ensure even cooking.

Grilled Lemon Garlic Chicken

Marinate the chicken as usual but cook it on a grill instead of the oven. This adds a smoky char and outdoor flavor, perfect for summer dinners or BBQs.

Note: Grill over medium-high heat for about 6-7 minutes per side, ensuring internal temperature reaches 165°F.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit out for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to avoid drying out. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken remains juicy, but Brussels sprouts may soften. Reheat gently to avoid drying out the meat.

Freezer

Freeze the chicken and Brussels sprouts separately in airtight containers or freezer bags for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible before sealing. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a skillet over medium-low heat, adding a splash of water or broth to keep the chicken moist. Avoid microwaving on high as it can dry out the meat and make sprouts soggy.

Make Ahead

You can marinate the chicken up to 24 hours ahead and store it in the fridge. Trim and prep the Brussels sprouts the day before as well. On the day of serving, sear and roast everything fresh for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs for this recipe?
Yes, chicken breasts can be used, but they cook faster and are leaner, so watch the cooking time carefully to avoid drying out. Consider pounding them to an even thickness for uniform cooking and reduce roasting time by 5-7 minutes.
What if I don’t have an oven-safe skillet?
If you don’t have an oven-safe skillet, you can sear the chicken in a regular pan and then transfer the chicken and Brussels sprouts to a baking dish for roasting. Just be careful transferring the hot chicken to avoid losing the seared crust.
Can I prepare this dish entirely on the stovetop?
While searing is done on the stove, roasting the Brussels sprouts in the oven develops better caramelization and crispiness. You could sauté sprouts on the stove, but they won’t get quite as crispy or evenly cooked.
How can I make this recipe spicier?
Add red pepper flakes or a dash of cayenne pepper to the marinade. You can also sprinkle some chili powder on the Brussels sprouts before roasting for a subtle heat that complements the lemon and garlic.
Is it okay to use frozen Brussels sprouts?
Frozen Brussels sprouts can be used but tend to release more moisture and won’t crisp up as well. If using frozen, pat them dry thoroughly and roast at a slightly higher temperature, but expect less caramelization.
What sides go well with this lemon garlic chicken?
This dish pairs beautifully with simple starches like rice, quinoa, or crusty bread to soak up the juices. A light green salad or steamed vegetables complement the bright flavors nicely.
How long can I marinate the chicken before cooking?
You can marinate the chicken for 10 minutes up to 24 hours. Longer marinating intensifies flavor and tenderizes the meat, but avoid going beyond 24 hours to prevent the lemon juice from breaking down the texture too much.
Can I use lemon juice from a bottle instead of fresh lemons?
Fresh lemon juice and zest provide a brighter, fresher flavor than bottled juice. If using bottled lemon juice, add extra lemon zest to compensate, but fresh is definitely preferred for best flavor.
