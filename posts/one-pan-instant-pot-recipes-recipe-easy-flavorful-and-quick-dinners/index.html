One-Pot Instant Pot Chicken and Rice for Busy Weeknights

Lummy Hayes Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This one-pot Instant Pot chicken and rice is a total lifesaver when you’re short on time but craving comfort. It’s all cooked in one pot, so cleanup’s a breeze. Great for busy weeknights or when you want something hearty fast!

Introduction

I stumbled on this one-pot Instant Pot chicken and rice recipe during a crazy busy week when I barely had time to cook, but I wanted something comforting and filling. What I love about this dish is how it combines simple ingredients into a cozy, flavorful meal without a ton of fuss. Plus, cooking everything together in the Instant Pot means the rice soaks up all those delicious chicken juices and spices, creating a perfect blend of textures and flavors. I’ve tried a bunch of similar recipes before, but this one stands out because of the smoky paprika and the timing that keeps the chicken tender and juicy without drying out the rice. One trick I learned is to rinse the rice really well before cooking — it prevents it from getting gummy and keeps the texture just right. When it’s done, the dish looks inviting with tender chicken pieces nestled in fluffy rice and bright green peas adding a pop of color. It’s such a satisfying meal that works great for weeknight dinners or even meal prep. Honestly, it’s become one of my favorite easy dinners to throw together when I want comfort food but don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen.

Why This Works

This recipe shines because it combines the magic of pressure cooking with simple, well-chosen ingredients. The Instant Pot cooks the chicken and rice simultaneously, which means the rice absorbs all the savory juices released by the chicken, resulting in deeply flavorful, moist grains rather than bland or dry rice. The sealed environment traps steam and heat, cooking the rice evenly and quickly without needing constant attention.

The sautéing steps before pressure cooking build layers of flavor through caramelization and the Maillard reaction. Softening the onions releases their natural sweetness, while browning the chicken adds complexity and richness. Deglazing the pot with broth ensures no burnt bits remain that could spoil the taste or trigger burn warnings on the Instant Pot.

Rinsing the rice is a small but crucial step: it removes excess surface starch that can cause clumping or gummy texture. By adding the rice on top without stirring, the grains cook evenly without sticking to the bottom, which can cause burning. The gentle natural pressure release allows the rice to finish absorbing liquid slowly, so it’s tender but not mushy.

Finally, adding peas at the end keeps them vibrant and fresh, contributing a pop of color and a slight sweetness that contrasts nicely with the smoky paprika and earthy thyme. This balance of technique and ingredients is what makes this recipe a dependable, crowd-pleasing weeknight meal.

  • Choose boneless skinless chicken thighs for their rich flavor and tenderness. Thighs stay juicy under pressure cooking and don’t dry out like breast meat can. If you prefer breast, reduce cooking time slightly to prevent dryness.
  • Rinse the rice thoroughly until water runs clear to remove surface starch. This prevents the rice from becoming sticky or mushy under pressure. Long-grain rice holds its shape well and cooks evenly in the Instant Pot.
  • Use a good-quality low-sodium chicken broth to control salt levels and add depth of flavor. The broth provides the cooking liquid that infuses the rice and chicken with savory notes.
  • This spice adds a subtle smoky warmth that elevates the dish beyond basic seasoning. Choose a fresh, vibrant smoked paprika for the best aroma and color.
  • Adding peas at the end keeps them tender-crisp and bright green. Frozen peas are convenient and maintain sweetness better than canned, which can be mushy and dull.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil, room temperature
  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas, thawed
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by setting your Instant Pot to the sauté function and heat the olive oil until shimmering. Add the diced onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until translucent and fragrant. This step is crucial because cooking the onion first builds a flavorful base for the dish. Avoid browning the onion too much as it can add bitterness; you just want it soft and sweet. Stir frequently to prevent sticking and burning.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Onions should be soft, translucent, and slightly glossy without browning
  2. Step 2

    Add the minced garlic to the onions and cook for another 30 seconds to 1 minute, stirring constantly. Garlic cooks fast and can burn easily, which would give a bitter taste, so keep the heat moderate and watch it closely. This step infuses the oil with garlic flavor, enhancing the overall depth of the dish.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Garlic should be fragrant but not browned or burnt
  3. Step 3

    Add the chicken chunks to the pot and season with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and dried thyme. Stir well to coat the chicken evenly with the spices. Sauté the chicken just until the pieces start to brown lightly on the edges, about 3-4 minutes. This browning step adds a nice layer of flavor through the Maillard reaction, which deepens the taste of the final dish.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Chicken edges should be lightly browned but not fully cooked through
  4. Step 4

    Pour in the chicken broth and scrape the pot bottom with a wooden spoon to release any browned bits stuck to the surface. These bits are flavor gold, so don’t skip this step! Adding the broth also deglazes the pot, preventing a burn warning during pressure cooking.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Broth is steaming and pot bottom is clear of stuck bits
  5. Step 5

    Add the rinsed rice evenly over the chicken and broth but do not stir it in. Cover the pot with the lid, seal the valve, and set to pressure cook on high for 10 minutes. Not stirring the rice keeps it from sticking to the bottom and helps it cook evenly with the chicken.

    ⏱ 10 minutes (pressure cook)✓ Pressure cooking completes with no burn alerts
  6. Step 6

    Once cooking is done, let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then carefully quick release any remaining pressure. Natural release lets the rice finish absorbing liquid gently, preventing it from being mushy or undercooked.

    ⏱ 10 minutes (natural release)✓ Pressure valve drops and lid unlocks easily
  7. Step 7

    Open the lid and gently fluff the rice and chicken with a fork. Stir in the thawed peas, then close the lid for 2 more minutes on the sauté function. This warms the peas without overcooking them, keeping their bright color and fresh taste.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Peas are heated through and rice is fluffy
  8. Step 8

    Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt or pepper if needed. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley for a burst of color and freshness. This finishing touch brightens the flavors and makes the dish look inviting.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Dish is seasoned to taste and visually appealing with parsley
  9. Step 9

    Serve the chicken and rice hot straight from the pot. This dish pairs well with a simple green salad or steamed veggies for a complete meal. Enjoy the cozy blend of tender chicken and perfectly cooked rice that’s full of smoky, herbaceous flavor.

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Dish is hot, flavorful, and ready to eat

Pro Tips

  • Rinse your rice before cooking. — Rinsing removes excess starch from the surface of the rice grains. This starch can cause the rice to stick together and turn gummy when pressure cooked. By rinsing, you ensure the rice cooks up fluffy and separate, which is exactly what you want in a one-pot dish.
  • Don’t stir the rice into the liquid before pressure cooking. — Leaving the rice on top of the broth and chicken rather than stirring helps prevent it from sticking to the pot’s bottom. This reduces the risk of a burn warning on the Instant Pot and ensures even cooking, producing tender grains rather than clumps.
  • Use natural pressure release instead of quick release. — Natural release allows the rice to finish absorbing liquid gently and prevents the grains from being undercooked or too firm. Quick release can cause the rice to be unevenly cooked or too wet, affecting texture negatively.
  • Brown the chicken before pressure cooking. — Sautéing the chicken first creates Maillard browning, which adds rich, savory flavors and a more appealing color. Skipping this step results in a blander dish with less depth.
  • Use boneless skinless thighs instead of breast. — Thighs stay moist and tender under pressure cooking because of their higher fat content. Breasts can dry out quickly, so if you use them, reduce cooking time accordingly.
  • Add peas at the end rather than cooking them under pressure. — Peas cook very quickly and can become mushy if pressure cooked. Stirring them in after cooking keeps their texture fresh and their color vibrant.
  • Taste and adjust seasoning after cooking. — Salt and spices can taste different after pressure cooking. Adjusting at the end ensures the dish is perfectly balanced and flavorful to your liking.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not rinsing rice before cooking
    Why: Many people skip rinsing rice because it seems like an extra step or they don’t realize it affects texture.
    Fix: Rinse the rice under cold running water until the water runs clear. This removes surface starch and prevents the rice from being gummy or sticking together when pressure cooked.
  • Stirring rice into the liquid before cooking
    Why: It feels natural to stir ingredients together, but this causes rice to stick to the pot bottom and triggers burn warnings.
    Fix: Add rinsed rice on top of the chicken and broth without stirring. This keeps the rice from clumping and ensures even cooking.
  • Using chicken breast without adjusting cooking time
    Why: Some swap thighs for breasts without realizing breasts cook faster and can dry out under long pressure cooking.
    Fix: If using breast, reduce pressure cook time by a few minutes and check for doneness early to keep meat juicy.
  • Quick releasing pressure immediately after cooking
    Why: Impatience or lack of knowledge about pressure release methods leads to quick release.
    Fix: Allow a natural pressure release for at least 10 minutes to let the rice finish cooking gently and avoid texture issues.
  • Adding peas during pressure cooking
    Why: Some add all ingredients at once for convenience but peas become mushy with pressure.
    Fix: Add peas after pressure cooking and warm them briefly on sauté mode to keep their texture and color.

Variations

  • Spicy Cajun Chicken and Rice: Add 1 tsp Cajun seasoning and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the chicken before cooking for a spicy kick. Swap peas for diced bell peppers and corn for a colorful twist. Expect a bolder flavor profile with a bit of heat that’s perfect for spice lovers.
    Spices with heat can intensify under pressure cooking, so adjust cayenne carefully to avoid overpowering the dish.
  • Creamy Mushroom Chicken and Rice: Stir in 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms with the onions and add 1/4 cup heavy cream after cooking for a creamy texture. This version is earthier and richer, perfect for cozy dinners with a touch of indulgence.
    Don’t add cream before pressure cooking as it can curdle; add it after and warm gently on sauté mode.
  • Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice: Add zest and juice of one lemon plus fresh chopped rosemary and thyme to brighten the dish. This variation tastes fresh and light with a zesty aroma, great for spring and summer meals.
    Add lemon juice after pressure cooking to preserve its bright flavor and prevent bitterness.
  • Vegetarian Instant Pot Rice Pilaf: Skip the chicken and use vegetable broth with sautéed mushrooms, carrots, and peas. Add toasted almonds and parsley for texture and flavor. This makes a hearty vegetarian main or side dish with a nutty, savory profile.
    Cooking time stays the same, but ensure vegetables are cut small to cook evenly.
  • Coconut Curry Chicken and Rice: Add 1 tbsp curry powder and swap chicken broth for coconut milk. Finish with chopped cilantro and lime juice for a creamy, aromatic dish with a tropical twist. Expect richer, spiced flavors and a creamier texture.
    Use light coconut milk to prevent curdling and add it before cooking for full flavor infusion.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered loosely with foil or a lid. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The rice may firm up when cold, so reheat with a splash of broth or water to restore moisture. Reheated chicken remains tender and flavorful.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in a sealed container or heavy-duty freezer bag for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible before sealing. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat, adding a little broth or water to loosen the rice. You can also microwave covered with a damp paper towel to retain moisture. Avoid high heat which can dry out the chicken.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep the chicken and sauté the onions and garlic 1-2 days ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Rinse the rice and measure spices in advance. Assemble and cook on the day you want to eat for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use brown rice instead of white rice in this recipe?
Brown rice requires longer cooking time and more liquid than white rice. If you swap, increase the pressure cook time to about 20-22 minutes and add extra broth (about 1 1/2 cups). Also, allow a longer natural pressure release. The texture will be chewier but still delicious.
What if I don’t have an Instant Pot? Can I make this on the stove?
Yes! Use a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven. Brown the chicken and onions first, then add rice and broth. Cover and simmer gently on low heat for about 20 minutes until rice is tender and chicken cooked through. Keep an eye on liquid levels to avoid drying out.
How do I prevent the rice from sticking and burning in the Instant Pot?
Make sure to deglaze the pot well after sautéing to lift any stuck bits. Rinse the rice well before adding it and do not stir it into the liquid. Also, ensure you add enough broth. These steps help prevent the burn notice and sticking.
Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
You can, but chicken breasts cook faster and can dry out. Reduce pressure cooking time by 2-3 minutes and consider quick releasing pressure early. Check for doneness to keep the chicken juicy.
Is it okay to add other vegetables like carrots or bell peppers?
Definitely! Add diced carrots or bell peppers during the sauté step to soften them. Hard vegetables may require a slightly longer cooking time. Softer veggies like spinach or zucchini are best added after pressure cooking to avoid overcooking.
How do I store leftovers and reheat without drying out the chicken?
Store in an airtight container in the fridge. When reheating, add a splash of broth or water and heat gently on the stove or microwave covered. This helps keep the chicken moist and the rice fluffy.
Can I double this recipe for a larger batch?
You can double the ingredients but be mindful of your Instant Pot’s max fill line. Also, cooking time remains the same, but natural pressure release might take longer. Avoid overfilling to ensure even cooking.
Why do you recommend natural pressure release instead of quick release?
Natural release lets the rice finish cooking gently and absorb liquid evenly. Quick release can cause unevenly cooked rice or undercooked grains because the pressure drops suddenly, interrupting the cooking process.
