Soft and Chewy Lavender Sugar Cookies with Brown Butter Magic

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 29, 2026
15 min Prep Time
12 min Time
24 Servings
Medium Difficulty

These lavender sugar cookies are delightfully soft and chewy, with a subtle floral hint that makes them extra special. Brown butter and extra egg yolk give them that perfect melt-in-your-mouth texture. Great for sharing with friends or a cozy afternoon treat!

Introduction

I first stumbled on this recipe during a lazy weekend when I wanted something a little different from the usual chocolate chip. The lavender sugar was a surprise gift from a friend, and I was curious how it would work in cookies. Turns out, it adds this subtle floral note that’s just so charming without being overpowering. What really makes these cookies special is the brown butter — it adds a deep, nutty flavor and a richness that you just can’t get with plain melted butter. I also add an extra egg yolk, which helps keep the cookies soft and chewy, rather than cakey or dry. I’ve tried making these a few times, and the trick I learned is to brown the butter just right, watching closely so it doesn’t burn but develops a toasty aroma. The final cookies come out golden, tender in the center, with a slight crisp edge and a delicate lavender hint that’s perfect with a cup of tea or coffee. Honestly, these cookies have become my go-to for gift boxes or when I want to impress friends without too much fuss. They’re easy to whip up but feel fancy enough for special occasions.

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 cookies

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, browned and cooled
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup lavender sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by browning the butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Stir frequently to prevent burning, and watch for the butter to foam and then turn a light amber color with a nutty aroma. This step is crucial because it develops deep, rich flavors that plain melted butter can’t match. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before using so it doesn’t cook the eggs in the next step.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter is golden brown with a nutty smell, no burnt bits
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, lavender sugar, and the slightly cooled brown butter until combined. Then add the egg and egg yolk along with the vanilla extract, mixing until smooth. The extra yolk helps keep the cookies soft and chewy by adding richness and moisture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth and slightly glossy, no lumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Sifting helps incorporate air and ensures even distribution of the leavening, which is key for the cookies’ texture. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, folding gently until just combined. Overmixing can develop gluten, making cookies tough, so stop as soon as you see no flour streaks.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is uniform with no pockets of dry flour
Step 4
Cover the dough and chill it for at least 30 minutes. Chilling firms up the dough, making it easier to scoop and preventing excessive spreading during baking. Plus, it allows the flavors to meld better, especially the lavender and brown butter notes.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough feels firm but pliable
Step 5
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper. Using a cookie scoop or spoon, portion dough balls about 1.5 tablespoons each, spacing them 2 inches apart. This spacing prevents cookies from merging as they spread.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough balls are evenly sized and spaced
Step 6
Bake the cookies for 10-12 minutes, until edges are lightly golden but centers still look soft. It’s tempting to bake until fully firm, but pulling them out while centers are slightly underbaked ensures a chewy texture as they continue to set on the hot tray.
⏱ Time: 12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges golden, centers slightly puffy and soft
Step 7
Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. This resting time lets the cookies set properly without breaking apart, and the residual heat finishes cooking them gently.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are firm enough to handle but still soft
Step 8
Once cooled, store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. They keep their softness for several days, but if you want that fresh-baked feel, reheat briefly in a warm oven or microwave.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies feel soft and warm after reheating
Step 9
Enjoy your lavender sugar cookies with tea or coffee, or package them as a thoughtful homemade gift. The delicate floral flavor and chewy texture make them stand out from typical cookies. Share and savor every bite!
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Cookies are tender, fragrant, and delicious

Pro Tips

Brown the butter slowly over medium heat to develop flavor without burning.
Slow, steady heating allows milk solids to toast evenly, creating a rich nutty aroma and flavor. Rushing or using high heat risks burning the butter, which imparts bitterness and ruins the cookie’s taste.
Chill the dough for at least 30 minutes before baking.
Chilling solidifies the butter, which controls cookie spread and improves texture. It also lets flavors meld, especially the lavender. Skipping this step often results in thin, crispy cookies instead of soft, chewy ones.
Use an extra egg yolk for added moisture and chewiness.
Egg yolks contain fats and emulsifiers that help retain moisture and tenderize the dough. This prevents the cookies from drying out and keeps them soft longer.
Avoid overmixing the dough once flour is added.
Overmixing develops gluten, making cookies tough and dense. Mix until just combined for a tender crumb and soft texture.
Bake until edges are golden but centers appear slightly undercooked.
Removing cookies while centers are still soft ensures a chewy texture as they finish cooking on the hot tray. Baking fully firm leads to dry, crumbly cookies.
Use parchment paper or silicone mats to prevent sticking and promote even baking.
These liners create a non-stick surface and help distribute heat evenly, preventing burnt bottoms or uneven browning.
Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature to keep them soft.
Exposure to air dries out cookies quickly. Airtight storage preserves moisture and freshness for several days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Burning the brown butter

Why it happens People often cook the butter on too high heat or leave it unattended, causing it to scorch quickly.

Fix Lower the heat to medium or medium-low and stir constantly. Watch for the color and smell closely, removing from heat as soon as it turns golden and nutty.

Skipping dough chilling

Why it happens Impatience or rushing to bake leads to skipping this important step.

Fix Plan ahead and chill dough at least 30 minutes. If short on time, pop it in the freezer for 15 minutes to firm up quickly.

Overmixing after adding flour

Why it happens Trying to fully incorporate flour leads to aggressive mixing.

Fix Fold gently and stop mixing as soon as flour disappears. This keeps gluten development minimal for soft cookies.

Baking cookies too long

Why it happens Waiting for cookies to look fully firm before removing.

Fix Pull cookies when edges are golden but centers still look soft; they’ll set as they cool for perfect chewiness.

Using too much lavender sugar

Why it happens Assuming more lavender equals better flavor.

Fix Stick to the recipe amount; too much lavender can taste soapy or bitter, overwhelming the cookie’s balance.

Variations

Lemon Lavender Sugar Cookies

Add 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon zest to the dough for a bright citrus twist that complements the floral lavender. These are refreshing and perfect for spring or summer gatherings.

Note: Zest should be finely grated and folded in with the dry ingredients to evenly distribute flavor.

Lavender White Chocolate Chip Cookies

Mix in 1 cup of white chocolate chips to the dough for a sweet, creamy contrast to the floral notes. The white chocolate melts beautifully, adding richness and texture.

Note: Fold chips in gently after combining dough to avoid breaking them up.

Gluten-Free Lavender Sugar Cookies

Swap all-purpose flour for a 1:1 gluten-free baking blend. These cookies remain soft and chewy with the same flavor, making them suitable for gluten-sensitive friends.

Note: Ensure the gluten-free blend contains xanthan gum or add 1/4 teaspoon to help with structure.

Lavender Honey Sugar Cookies

Replace half the granulated sugar with honey for a natural sweetness and a hint of floral depth that pairs beautifully with lavender. These cookies are extra moist and slightly sticky.

Note: Reduce oven temperature by 10°F and watch baking time closely, as honey browns faster.

Lavender Earl Grey Sugar Cookies

Infuse the dough with 1 tablespoon of finely ground Earl Grey tea leaves for an aromatic twist. The bergamot in the tea enhances the lavender’s floral notes for a sophisticated flavor.

Note: Strain tea leaves finely to avoid gritty texture; mix thoroughly into flour before combining with wet ingredients.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cookies can be kept at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Store away from direct sunlight and heat to preserve softness and flavor. Avoid stacking them tightly to prevent sticking.

Refrigerator

Store cookies in an airtight container for up to 7 days. Refrigeration can firm up the texture, so allow cookies to come to room temperature or warm briefly before serving for best softness.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet first, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature for a couple of hours before serving.

Reheating

Warm cookies in a preheated oven at 300°F (150°C) for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 10-15 seconds with a damp paper towel to restore softness. Avoid overheating as it can dry them out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough 1-2 days ahead and keep it tightly wrapped in the fridge. Scoop and bake fresh on the day you want to serve. Alternatively, bake cookies and freeze them for later, reheating as needed.

FAQ

Can I use dried lavender buds directly instead of lavender sugar?
Yes, but use finely ground culinary lavender buds sparingly to avoid bitterness. You can grind the buds in a spice grinder and mix about 1 to 1.5 teaspoons into the dry ingredients. Lavender sugar is milder and sweeter, so the flavor might be stronger if you use plain buds.
What if I don’t have brown butter? Can I use melted butter?
You can use melted butter, but you’ll miss out on the nutty depth brown butter provides. If you skip browning, consider adding a teaspoon of vanilla or a touch of toasted nuts to add richness. Brown butter adds flavor and affects texture, so it’s worth taking the extra step.
How do I prevent cookies from spreading too much?
Chilling the dough is key to controlling spread. Also, make sure your baking soda is fresh and don’t use too much butter. Using parchment paper or silicone mats helps cookies bake evenly without excessive spreading.
Can I make these cookies vegan?
To make a vegan version, substitute butter with vegan margarine or coconut oil and replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg). Note that texture and flavor will differ slightly, losing some chewiness and richness.
Why do my cookies turn out cakey instead of chewy?
Cakey cookies often result from too much flour, overmixing, or using whole eggs only. This recipe’s extra egg yolk and careful mixing keep cookies chewy. Also, don’t overbake; removing cookies while centers are soft helps retain chewiness.
Can I add other flavors to the dough?
Absolutely! Lemon zest, white chocolate chips, or finely ground Earl Grey tea are all great additions. Just fold them in gently after mixing the dough to maintain texture and flavor balance.
How do I store cookies to keep them soft longer?
Use an airtight container at room temperature, separated by parchment paper if stacking. Adding a slice of bread to the container can help maintain moisture. Avoid refrigeration if you want to keep them soft, as it tends to dry them out.
Can I freeze the dough instead of baked cookies?
Yes, cookie dough freezes well. Scoop dough balls onto a tray, freeze until solid, then transfer to a freezer bag. Bake from frozen, adding a couple extra minutes to baking time. This is handy for fresh cookies anytime.
