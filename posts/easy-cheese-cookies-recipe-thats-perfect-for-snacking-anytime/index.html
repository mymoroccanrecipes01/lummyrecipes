Crispy Cheddar Cheese Cookies That Are Perfect For Snacking

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 22, 2026
15 min Prep Time
20 min Time
24 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These cheddar cheese cookies are delightfully crispy with a rich, cheesy flavor in every bite. Super easy to make with simple ingredients, they’re my go-to for quick snacks or when guests drop by unexpectedly.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this cheese cookie recipe years ago when I needed a quick snack to bring to a friend’s casual get-together. What caught me was how simple the ingredients were, yet the result was so flavorful and crispy. I’ve tweaked it a bit over time—adding a hint of Dijon mustard and a touch of cayenne for a subtle kick—and now it’s a staple in my kitchen. The trick I learned early on is to chill the dough before baking; it helps the cookies hold their shape and develop those perfect crispy edges without spreading too much. When you bite into one, you get this lovely balance of sharp cheddar and nutty Parmesan with a crisp texture that feels so satisfying. They’re great for afternoon tea, as a party snack, or even paired with a glass of white wine for a casual evening. Honestly, these cheese cookies never last long in my house because everyone loves them so much!

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 cookies, serves 4-6

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, finely grated
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon cold water

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by sifting the flour, baking powder, salt, and cayenne pepper (if using) into a medium bowl. Sifting not only removes lumps but also aerates the flour, which helps achieve a lighter texture in the cookies. Mixing these dry ingredients thoroughly ensures the baking powder is evenly distributed, which is critical for proper rise and texture. Avoid skipping this step to prevent dense or unevenly baked cookies.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly combined with no clumps visible
Step 2
In a separate large bowl, cream the softened butter with the sharp cheddar and Parmesan cheeses using a hand mixer or wooden spoon. The goal is to combine them until smooth and well incorporated, which allows the fat in the butter to coat the cheese particles, releasing their flavors. Then mix in the Dijon mustard and cold water to help bind the dough. If you rush this step or don’t cream well, the dough may be crumbly or uneven.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, slightly fluffy, and uniformly cheesy
Step 3
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter-cheese mixture, folding gently with a spatula until just combined. Overmixing develops gluten which can make cookies tough, so stop as soon as you see no streaks of flour. The dough should come together but still feel slightly firm. If it feels too sticky, chill it before shaping.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dough is cohesive, smooth, and holds shape without sticking excessively
Step 4
Wrap the dough in plastic and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling firms the butter, which helps the cookies hold their shape during baking and prevents excessive spreading. It also lets the flavors meld, especially the sharpness of the cheeses. Skipping this step often results in flat, greasy cookies.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Dough is firm and cold to the touch
Step 5
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper to prevent sticking and promote even baking. Removing the dough from the fridge, roll it into small balls about 1 inch in diameter. Place them evenly spaced on the sheet to allow room for spreading. Press each ball lightly with a fork to create a crisscross pattern, which helps them bake evenly and adds a nice texture.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Cookies are spaced with visible fork marks pressed gently
Step 6
Bake the cookies for 15–20 minutes, rotating the pan halfway through for even heat distribution. Watch for golden brown edges and a slightly darker color on top. Underbaking leaves them soft and greasy, overbaking makes them too hard. The key is crisp edges with a tender center. Remove from oven once this balance is achieved.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden brown and centers look set but not burnt
Step 7
Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. This cooling period lets residual heat finish the cooking and helps them firm up to that perfect crispy texture. Moving them too soon might cause breakage, too late and they could become soggy from trapped steam.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cookies are firm enough to lift without breaking
Step 8
Once fully cooled, store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. This keeps them crisp and fresh for several days. If you prefer them warm, reheat briefly in a low oven to revive their crunch without drying them out.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Cookies are crisp and maintain texture after storage
Step 9
Enjoy your cheese cookies with a cup of tea or as part of a cheese board. They pair wonderfully with fresh fruit or a glass of white wine for an elevated snack. Don’t be surprised if these become your new favorite go-to treat!
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Cookies are served and enjoyed

Pro Tips

Always chill the dough before baking.
Chilling solidifies the fat in the dough, preventing excessive spreading during baking. This results in cookies with crisp edges and a tender center rather than flat, greasy discs. It also helps the flavors meld, enhancing the overall taste.
Use freshly grated cheese rather than pre-shredded.
Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can affect melting and texture. Freshly grated cheese melts more evenly and helps create a smoother dough and better texture in the final cookie.
Press cookies gently with a fork before baking.
This creates texture and helps the cookies bake evenly. The fork marks increase surface area, promoting crispiness and giving the cookie an appealing homemade look.
Rotate your baking sheet halfway through baking.
Ovens often have hot spots. Rotating ensures even browning and prevents some cookies from burning while others remain undercooked, resulting in consistent texture and flavor.
Use a sharp cheddar for bold flavor.
Milder cheeses won’t give the same punch. Sharp cheddar adds tang and depth, which when combined with Parmesan, provides a rich, balanced flavor that makes these cookies addictive.
Don’t overmix the dough once flour is added.
Overmixing develops gluten, which can make cookies tough and dense. Gently folding until just combined keeps the texture tender yet structured.
Store cookies in an airtight container once cooled.
Exposure to air can make the cookies lose their crispness. Airtight storage preserves their texture and flavor for several days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not chilling the dough before baking

Why it happens Many cooks rush to bake the dough immediately, thinking it saves time or that chilling isn’t necessary.

Fix Always refrigerate the dough for at least 30 minutes. This step firms up the butter, preventing the cookies from spreading too much and becoming greasy or flat. It also enhances flavor melding.

Using pre-shredded cheese

Why it happens Pre-shredded cheese is convenient and often the default choice for many home cooks.

Fix Grate your own cheese fresh. This improves melting and texture in the dough, resulting in a more cohesive and flavorful cookie.

Overmixing after adding flour

Why it happens It’s easy to keep stirring to ensure everything is combined, but this develops gluten.

Fix Mix flour in gently and stop as soon as it’s incorporated. This keeps the cookies tender instead of tough or chewy.

Baking at too high a temperature

Why it happens Some think higher heat means faster baking, but it can burn the edges before the center cooks.

Fix Bake at 350°F (175°C) for even cooking. Watch for golden edges as a cue to remove cookies to avoid bitterness.

Not spacing cookies properly on the baking sheet

Why it happens Crowding the pan is a common oversight, especially when baking multiple batches.

Fix Leave enough space (about 2 inches) between cookies to allow for spreading and proper air circulation, ensuring even baking.

Variations

Spicy Jalapeño Cheese Cookies

Add finely chopped jalapeños to the dough for a spicy kick that complements the cheese beautifully. These are perfect if you like a little heat in your snacks and want to impress guests with a twist.

Note: Chop jalapeños finely and reduce cayenne pepper slightly to balance spice levels. Watch baking times as the added moisture might lengthen baking slightly.

Herb and Garlic Cheese Cookies

Incorporate minced fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme and a teaspoon of garlic powder for an herby, savory variation. These cookies pair wonderfully with wine and cheese boards.

Note: Add herbs and garlic powder during the creaming of butter and cheese to evenly distribute flavors. No change in baking time needed.

Gluten-Free Cheese Cookies

Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend to make these suitable for gluten-sensitive friends. Texture might be slightly different but still crisp and tasty.

Note: Use a 1-to-1 gluten-free flour blend with xanthan gum. Chill dough thoroughly to help maintain structure during baking.

Extra Cheesy Parmesan Cookies

Increase Parmesan to 1/2 cup and reduce cheddar slightly for a nuttier, more intense cheese flavor. These are great for Parmesan lovers looking for a sharper taste.

Note: Adjust cheese ratios carefully to maintain dough consistency. Baking time remains the same but watch for quicker browning.

Sweet and Savory Cheese Cookies

Add a tablespoon of honey and a pinch of cinnamon for a subtle sweet-savory combo. This variation is unexpected and delicious, perfect for serving with tea or coffee.

Note: Incorporate honey with the butter and cheese. Cinnamon should be added with dry ingredients. Monitor dough moisture; chill longer if too soft.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cheese cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep away from direct sunlight and moisture to maintain crispness. If left uncovered, they can become soft and lose their texture.

Refrigerator

You can refrigerate the cookies in an airtight container for up to 5 days. However, refrigeration may cause them to lose some crispness. To enjoy the best texture, warm them briefly in a low oven before serving.

Freezer

Freeze the cookies in a sealed freezer-safe container or bag for up to 2 months. To avoid freezer burn, wrap them well in plastic wrap before placing in the container. Thaw at room temperature and reheat in the oven for a few minutes to restore crispiness.

Reheating

Reheat cookies in a preheated oven at 300°F (150°C) for 5–7 minutes to bring back their crunch. Avoid microwaving as it can make cookies chewy or soggy. A light spritz of water before reheating can help prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough 1–2 days ahead and keep it chilled. Shape and bake the cookies the day you plan to serve them to ensure maximum freshness and texture. Alternatively, bake and freeze the cookies, reheating as needed.

FAQ

Can I use shredded mozzarella instead of cheddar?
Mozzarella has a much milder flavor and higher moisture content than cheddar, so the cookies won’t have the same sharpness or crispiness. If you want a milder cheese cookie, you can try it, but expect a softer texture and less pronounced cheese flavor.
How do I prevent the cookies from spreading too much?
Chilling the dough for at least 30 minutes before baking is crucial. This firms up the fat, which controls spreading. Also, avoid using softened butter that’s too warm, as it causes the dough to be too loose and spread excessively.
Can I make the dough ahead and freeze it?
Yes, you can freeze the shaped dough balls on a baking sheet, then transfer them to a freezer bag. Bake straight from frozen, adding a couple extra minutes to the baking time to ensure they cook through properly.
What if my cookies come out too hard?
This usually means they were overbaked or the dough was overmixed. Try reducing baking time by a few minutes and mixing just until combined. Also, ensure you don’t use too much flour as it can dry out the cookies.
Can I use salted butter instead of unsalted?
You can, but reduce or omit added salt in the recipe to avoid overly salty cookies. Unsalted butter gives you better control over the final seasoning.
Why do my cookies sometimes turn out greasy?
Greasy cookies often result from using butter that’s too warm or overmixing the dough. Make sure to chill the dough and use properly softened, not melted, butter to maintain the right fat distribution.
Is it okay to add herbs or spices?
Absolutely! Fresh herbs like rosemary or spices like smoked paprika can add a lovely twist. Just add them in small amounts during the creaming stage to balance flavors without overpowering the cheese.
How should I store leftover cookies?
Store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, freeze them in a sealed container. Reheat before serving to bring back crispness.
