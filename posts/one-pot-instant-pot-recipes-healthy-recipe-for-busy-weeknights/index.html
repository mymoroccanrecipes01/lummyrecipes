One-Pot Instant Pot Chicken and Veggie Dinner for Busy Weeknights

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This one-pot Instant Pot chicken dinner is a total game-changer for busy nights. Loaded with fresh veggies and lean chicken, it’s quick, healthy, and bursting with flavor. Perfect when you want a wholesome meal without fuss!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this one-pot Instant Pot chicken and veggie dinner during a particularly hectic week when I barely had time to think about what to cook. Honestly, I was skeptical that something this quick could taste good, but this recipe completely won me over. What makes it stand out is the way the Instant Pot locks in all those fresh veggie flavors alongside tender chicken, making every bite comforting yet light. I love how all the ingredients cook together, which means fewer dishes and less stress — a total win in my book. One trick I picked up is sautéing the onions and garlic right in the pot before adding everything else; it gives the dish a deeper flavor that really sets it apart from your typical dump-and-go meal. The finished dish has this lovely balance of tender chicken, slightly crisp veggies, and a savory broth that’s just perfect for a quick family dinner. If you’re juggling work, kids, or just want something nourishing without fuss, this recipe will quickly become a go-to.

Why This Works

This recipe works because it leverages the Instant Pot’s pressure cooking technology to quickly tenderize chicken and cook vegetables perfectly without turning them mushy. The sealed environment traps steam and heat, which speeds up cooking and intensifies flavors by allowing the ingredients to mingle deeply.

Sautéing onions, garlic, and chicken before pressure cooking unlocks complex flavors through caramelization and the Maillard reaction — browning proteins and sugars creates rich, savory notes that a quick dump-and-go recipe might miss. This initial step is critical for adding depth and aroma.

The balance of vegetables chosen offers a mix of textures and nutrients. Carrots and bell peppers provide sweetness and color, while zucchini and green beans add a fresh crunch. Cooking them together under pressure softens them just enough while retaining their natural vibrancy.

Finally, the broth deglazes the pot bottom, capturing those caramelized bits and preventing burning. It also creates a flavorful cooking liquid that infuses the chicken and vegetables, resulting in a cohesive, comforting dish that’s both healthy and satisfying.

  • Look for fresh, boneless, skinless chicken breasts that are firm and pinkish. They cook quickly and evenly in the Instant Pot, making them ideal for this recipe. Avoid frozen chicken unless you adjust cooking times, as it can affect texture. Cutting them into uniform pieces ensures even cooking and tender bites.
  • Extra virgin olive oil is best for sautéing here as it adds a subtle fruitiness and helps develop flavor without overpowering the dish. It also has a relatively high smoke point, making it suitable for the sauté step. Avoid overheating to prevent bitterness.
  • Yellow or white onions work well for their natural sweetness that deepens when sautéed. Fresh onions should be firm and dry. Chopping them evenly helps them cook uniformly, which is important for flavor balance.
  • Fresh garlic cloves provide a pungent aroma and flavor that infuses the oil during sautéing. Mince finely for even distribution. Avoid pre-minced garlic from jars as it can be less vibrant and may alter the flavor profile.
  • Use low-sodium broth to control salt levels and avoid overpowering the dish. A good-quality broth adds savory depth and moisture necessary for pressure cooking. If unavailable, water with bouillon cubes can be a substitute but flavor may be less rich.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Set your Instant Pot to the sauté function and heat the olive oil. Add the chopped onion and cook for about 3-4 minutes until it softens and starts to turn translucent. This step is important because it builds a flavorful base for the dish. Avoid rushing it or cooking on too high heat, as burnt onions can add bitterness. Stir frequently to ensure even cooking and to release the natural sweetness of the onions.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Onions should be soft, translucent, and slightly golden at the edges.
  2. Step 2

    Add the minced garlic to the pot and sauté for another 30 seconds to 1 minute until fragrant. Garlic cooks quickly and can burn easily, so keep a close eye on it. This step infuses the oil with garlicky aroma, enhancing the overall flavor depth. If you skip this, the dish might lack that subtle background warmth garlic provides.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Garlic should smell aromatic but not browned.
  3. Step 3

    Add the chicken pieces to the pot and season with salt, pepper, dried thyme, and smoked paprika. Stir to coat the chicken evenly with the spices and sauté for about 5 minutes until the chicken starts to brown slightly. Browning the chicken adds a rich layer of flavor through the Maillard reaction, so take your time here. Avoid overcrowding the pot to ensure even browning.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Chicken pieces should have a light golden crust on the outside.
  4. Step 4

    Add the chopped carrots, bell pepper, zucchini, and green beans to the pot. Stir everything gently to combine. The variety of textures and colors makes this dish visually appealing and nutritionally balanced. Don’t over-stir to avoid bruising the vegetables, which can lead to mushiness later.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Vegetables are evenly distributed and coated with seasoning.
  5. Step 5

    Pour in the chicken broth carefully, scraping the bottom of the pot with a wooden spoon to loosen any browned bits. These bits add deep flavor and prevent the burn warning on your Instant Pot. Ensuring the broth reaches the bottom is crucial for proper pressure cooking.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Broth covers the ingredients and bottom of pot is deglazed.
  6. Step 6

    Secure the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 8 minutes. This timing allows the chicken to cook through while keeping the veggies tender but not mushy. If you rush this step or set a shorter cooking time, the chicken might be undercooked; too long and the veggies turn to mush.

    ⏱ 8 minutes✓ Instant Pot timer counts down, and pressure builds without error.
  7. Step 7

    Once cooking is complete, perform a quick release by carefully turning the valve to venting to release pressure. This stops the cooking immediately, preserving the texture of the veggies. Be cautious of the steam to avoid burns.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Steam escapes and the float valve drops, allowing the lid to open safely.
  8. Step 8

    Open the lid and gently stir the contents. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional salt and pepper if needed. This final seasoning step ensures a balanced flavor since broth and veggies sometimes absorb salt differently during cooking.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Dish is evenly mixed and seasoning is balanced to taste.
  9. Step 9

    Serve the chicken and veggies hot, garnished with fresh chopped parsley if you like. The fresh herb adds a bright note and makes the dish look inviting. Pair with a side of rice or crusty bread for a complete meal.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Dish is steaming hot and visually vibrant with fresh garnish.

Pro Tips

  • Brown the chicken before pressure cooking. — Browning the chicken creates the Maillard reaction, which develops rich, savory flavors and a pleasant texture. Skipping this step can result in a flatter taste and pale-looking chicken.
  • Don’t overfill the Instant Pot. — Overfilling can block the steam release valve and cause safety issues or uneven cooking. Keeping the pot no more than two-thirds full ensures proper pressure build-up and cooking performance.
  • Use fresh herbs for garnish. — Fresh herbs add a bright, fresh contrast to the cooked ingredients, enhancing visual appeal and flavor complexity. Dried herbs used at the end won’t have the same fresh impact.
  • Perform a quick release after cooking. — Quick releasing pressure stops cooking immediately, preserving the texture of delicate vegetables. Natural release can overcook veggies, making them mushy.
  • Cut vegetables uniformly. — Uniform pieces cook evenly under pressure, preventing some from being overdone while others remain undercooked, which improves overall texture and presentation.
  • Deglaze the pot to avoid burn errors. — Scraping browned bits off the bottom with broth prevents the Instant Pot from triggering a burn warning, which can interrupt cooking and affect final flavor.
  • Adjust seasoning after cooking. — Flavors concentrate during pressure cooking, so seasoning before can lead to over-salting. Tasting and adjusting at the end ensures perfect balance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the sauté step.
    Why: Some cooks assume they can just dump all ingredients and pressure cook, thinking it saves time.
    Fix: Always sauté onions, garlic, and chicken first. This step builds flavor through caramelization and prevents a bland final dish. Take your time here for best results.
  • Overcooking vegetables.
    Why: Pressure cooking times can be intimidating, and many leave the food in too long.
    Fix: Follow the recommended 8-minute cook time and use quick pressure release to avoid mushy veggies. Overcooked vegetables lose texture and nutrients.
  • Not deglazing the pot.
    Why: Rushing through or forgetting to scrape the bottom after sautéing can cause burnt bits to stick.
    Fix: Pour chicken broth in and scrape the bottom thoroughly before pressure cooking. This prevents burn errors and adds flavor.
  • Using frozen chicken without adjusting time.
    Why: Some use frozen chicken to save prep time but don’t modify cooking settings.
    Fix: Use fresh chicken or increase pressure cooking time if using frozen. Undercooked chicken is unsafe and unpleasant.
  • Overfilling the Instant Pot.
    Why: Trying to cook a large batch at once leads to ignoring max fill lines.
    Fix: Cook in batches if needed. Overfilling can cause safety issues and uneven cooking.

Variations

  • Spicy Southwest Chicken: Add 1 diced jalapeño and swap smoked paprika for chili powder and cumin for a smoky, spicy twist. Top with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime for brightness. Expect a bolder, zestier flavor profile with a hint of heat.
    Use caution with jalapeño seeds for milder heat; cooking time remains the same.
  • Creamy Coconut Chicken and Veggies: Replace chicken broth with 1 cup coconut milk and add a teaspoon of curry powder. This variation brings a rich, creamy texture and a tropical flavor that pairs beautifully with the veggies. Serve with jasmine rice for a comforting meal.
    Use full-fat coconut milk for best creaminess; stir gently to avoid curdling.
  • Mediterranean Herb Chicken: Use fresh oregano, basil, and rosemary instead of thyme. Add diced tomatoes and olives for a Mediterranean vibe. This version is aromatic, slightly tangy, and perfect with crusty bread.
    Add tomatoes with the broth; olives go in after cooking to keep their texture.
  • Vegetarian Version with Chickpeas: Swap chicken for 2 cups cooked chickpeas and use vegetable broth. This makes a protein-packed, plant-based meal that’s hearty and flavorful. Add extra spices like smoked paprika and cumin for depth.
    Cook time stays the same, but sauté veggies a bit longer for flavor.
  • Asian-Inspired Chicken and Veggies: Replace chicken broth with low-sodium soy sauce mixed with water, add grated ginger and a dash of sesame oil. Finish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds for an umami-rich flavor profile.
    Use caution with soy sauce saltiness; reduce added salt accordingly.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, refrigerate promptly to avoid bacterial growth. Cover with a lid or foil to keep it from drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The dish may thicken or lose some crispness of the veggies when cold. Reheat gently and add a splash of broth or water if it seems dry.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze leftovers in a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, press plastic wrap directly on the surface before sealing. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over medium heat with a splash of broth to loosen the sauce, or microwave in short intervals stirring in between. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out the chicken and veggies.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can chop all vegetables and cut the chicken 1-2 days in advance, storing them separately in airtight containers. Sauté onions and garlic ahead and refrigerate. Assemble and pressure cook on the day of serving for maximum freshness.

FAQ

Can I use frozen chicken in this recipe?
Yes, but you’ll need to increase the pressure cooking time to about 12 minutes to ensure the chicken cooks through. Also, avoid sautéing frozen chicken as it won’t brown properly. Using fresh chicken gives the best texture and flavor.
What if I don’t have an Instant Pot?
You can make this recipe on the stovetop in a large pot or Dutch oven. Sauté the ingredients as directed, then simmer with broth covered for about 25-30 minutes until chicken is cooked and vegetables are tender. Adjust liquid as needed.
Can I add rice or potatoes to this dish?
Yes! For rice, add 1 cup rinsed rice with 1 1/4 cups broth and cook under pressure for 10 minutes. For potatoes, add diced potatoes with the vegetables. Adjust cooking times slightly to ensure everything cooks evenly.
How can I make this dish spicier?
Add diced jalapeños or a pinch of red chili flakes during the sauté step. Alternatively, drizzle hot sauce on top when serving. Be mindful of spice levels to suit your taste and those eating with you.
Is it okay to use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth?
Absolutely! Vegetable broth works well and makes the dish vegetarian-friendly if you swap out the chicken. Just choose a good-quality broth to keep the flavor rich.
Why did my Instant Pot show a burn warning?
This usually happens if there’s not enough liquid or if food sticks to the bottom. Make sure to deglaze the pot thoroughly after sautéing to loosen any browned bits. Also, check that you have enough broth to generate steam.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
Yes, you can chop vegetables and marinate chicken a day ahead. Sauté onions and garlic in advance and refrigerate. Assemble and cook on the day you plan to serve for best texture and flavor.
How do I keep the vegetables from getting mushy?
Cut vegetables into uniform, bite-sized pieces and follow the cooking times closely. Use quick pressure release to stop cooking immediately once time is up. Avoid overfilling the pot to allow even heat distribution.
