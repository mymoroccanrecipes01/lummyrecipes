Light and Fresh Lemon Blueberry Cake Perfect for Summer Gatherings

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 3, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This lemon blueberry cake is so fresh and light, perfect for warm days. It uses just five simple ingredients and bursts with juicy blueberries and zesty lemon flavor. Great for casual get-togethers or backyard parties!

Introduction

I first tried a lemon blueberry cake years ago at a summer picnic, and I was instantly hooked on that fresh, vibrant flavor combo. Since then, I’ve experimented with several recipes, but this one stands out because it’s incredibly simple and uses just a handful of ingredients you probably already have. What makes this cake different? The secret is gently folding in the blueberries so they don’t burst and turn the batter blue, and using both lemon zest and juice to layer the citrus flavor. I’ve learned from past attempts that overmixing can make the cake dense, so I always mix just until combined. The finished cake is light, moist, and bursting with juicy blueberries and bright lemon zing. It’s perfect served slightly warm or at room temperature, ideal for summer get-togethers or a casual afternoon treat. I love how easy it is to whip up but still feels special enough to impress guests or just enjoy with a cup of tea on the porch.

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, approximately 8 servings

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries, washed and dried
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Optional: powdered sugar for dusting

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch round cake pan with butter or non-stick spray. This step is crucial because a well-greased pan ensures your cake releases easily after baking, preventing any frustrating sticking. You can also line the bottom with parchment paper for extra insurance. Proper oven preheating guarantees even cooking right from the start, so don’t skip this part.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully preheated and cake pan is evenly greased without excess grease pooling
Step 2
In a mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar using an electric mixer on medium speed until light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. This aerates the butter, creating a tender crumb in your cake. Rushing this step or skipping it will result in a denser texture, so be patient and let the mixture turn pale and creamy.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugar mixture looks pale, fluffy, and has increased in volume
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition to fully incorporate them. This helps create a smooth batter and prevents curdling. If you add both eggs at once or don’t beat enough, your batter might separate or become lumpy.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and homogeneous with no streaks of egg visible
Step 4
Mix in the lemon zest and lemon juice. The zest adds bright citrus oils, while the juice brings tang and moisture. Stir gently to combine, making sure not to overwork the batter, which can toughen the cake.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Even distribution of lemon zest and juice throughout the batter
Step 5
In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. This ensures even distribution of the leavening agent and salt, which are critical for rise and flavor balance.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are thoroughly combined with no lumps
Step 6
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, folding gently with a spatula. Mixing slowly prevents over-developing gluten, which keeps the cake tender. Stop mixing as soon as you see no more streaks of flour.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth without visible flour pockets but not overmixed
Step 7
Toss the blueberries with a teaspoon of flour to coat them lightly; this helps prevent them from sinking to the bottom of the cake during baking. Then fold the blueberries gently into the batter, taking care not to crush them and turn the batter blue.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Blueberries are evenly distributed without bursting or color bleeding
Step 8
Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any air bubbles trapped inside, which helps create an even crumb.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread and surface is smooth with no large air pockets
Step 9
Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs. Avoid opening the oven door too early or too often, as this can cause the cake to collapse. The cake should be golden on top and spring back slightly when pressed.
⏱ Time: 30-35 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown top with toothpick clean and slight springiness to touch

Pro Tips

Always use room-temperature eggs and butter for best mixing.
Room-temperature eggs and butter combine more easily, creating a smooth, emulsified batter. Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly, which affects texture and rise.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, resulting in a tough, dense cake. Mixing just until the flour disappears keeps the crumb tender and soft.
Coat blueberries in flour before folding them in.
This prevents the berries from sinking to the bottom during baking and stops their juice from bleeding into the batter, preserving the cake’s pretty appearance.
Use a microplane to zest lemons finely.
Fine zest releases more aromatic oils and blends better into the batter, giving a brighter, more intense lemon flavor without bitter pith bits.
Preheat your oven fully before baking.
An evenly hot oven ensures proper rise and even cooking. Baking in a too-cool oven can cause a dense texture and uneven crumb.
Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before removing.
This allows the cake to firm up slightly, reducing the chance it breaks apart when you turn it out.
Serve the cake slightly warm or at room temperature.
Warm cake enhances the juicy blueberry bursts and lemon aroma, while room temperature keeps it moist and tender.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter after adding flour

Why it happens Many cooks want to ensure everything is well combined and mix too vigorously or for too long.

Fix Mix gently and stop as soon as the flour streaks disappear. Use a folding motion rather than stirring to avoid gluten development.

Using cold butter or eggs

Why it happens For convenience, sometimes ingredients are used straight from the fridge.

Fix Take butter and eggs out 30 minutes before baking to reach room temperature, which helps them combine smoothly and trap air for a light texture.

Not coating blueberries with flour

Why it happens This step is often overlooked because it seems unnecessary or time-consuming.

Fix Toss blueberries gently with a teaspoon of flour before folding in to keep them suspended and prevent color bleeding.

Opening the oven door too early

Why it happens Curiosity or impatience leads to frequent peeks.

Fix Avoid opening the oven for at least 20 minutes to prevent the cake from collapsing due to temperature fluctuations.

Using bottled lemon juice or dried zest

Why it happens Convenience or lack of fresh lemons.

Fix Use fresh lemon zest and juice for the best flavor. Bottled juice lacks brightness and dried zest can taste bitter or dull.

Variations

Gluten-Free Lemon Blueberry Cake

Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend suitable for baking. Expect a slightly different crumb texture but still moist and delicious. Add a teaspoon of xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t include it for better structure.

Note: Gluten-free flours absorb liquids differently; you may need to adjust baking time and check doneness carefully.

Lemon Blueberry Bundt Cake with Glaze

Bake the batter in a bundt pan and drizzle a simple lemon glaze made from powdered sugar and lemon juice over the cooled cake. This adds extra sweetness and a pretty finish.

Note: Bundt pans require thorough greasing to prevent sticking and may need 5-10 minutes extra baking time.

Vegan Lemon Blueberry Cake

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg) and use vegan butter or coconut oil. The texture will be slightly denser but still flavorful and moist.

Note: Flax eggs add moisture and binding but don’t aerate like eggs, so mix gently and don’t expect as much rise.

Lemon Blueberry Muffins

Divide the batter into muffin tins for portable, single-serve treats. Baking time reduces to about 18-22 minutes. Perfect for breakfast or snack on the go.

Note: Watch closely to avoid overbaking, which can dry out the muffins.

Add a Cream Cheese Frosting

Top the cooled cake with a tangy cream cheese frosting for extra richness and balance to the citrus. This turns it into a more decadent dessert for special occasions.

Note: Ensure the cake is fully cooled before frosting to prevent melting.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cake can be kept covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Use a cake dome or airtight container to prevent it from drying out. It’s best enjoyed within this time for optimal freshness.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The cake may firm up when chilled but will retain flavor. Bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving to soften.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil, then freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and then bring to room temperature. Freezing may slightly affect texture but flavor remains good.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Avoid overheating which dries out the cake. Adding a small dab of butter can help restore moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a few hours ahead and keep it covered in the fridge before baking. Alternatively, bake the cake a day in advance and store it wrapped; this actually improves flavor as it allows the lemon and blueberry flavors to meld.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries instead of fresh?
Yes, you can use frozen blueberries, but it's best to thaw and drain them well before folding into the batter to avoid excess moisture. Also, toss them in a bit of flour to prevent sinking and color bleeding. Using frozen berries straight from the freezer may make the batter watery and affect the texture.
What can I substitute for lemon zest if I don’t have fresh lemons?
Fresh lemon zest is best for flavor and aroma, but if unavailable, you can use a high-quality lemon extract. Use about 1/2 teaspoon extract in place of zest. Avoid dried lemon peel as it can taste bitter and lacks the fresh oils that give the bright lemon flavor.
Why is my cake dense and not fluffy?
Dense cake often results from overmixing the batter after adding flour or using cold ingredients that don’t cream well. Make sure to cream butter and sugar thoroughly, use room temperature eggs, and mix flour in gently just until combined. Also, check that your baking powder is fresh and the oven temperature is correct.
Can I make this cake dairy-free?
Yes, substitute butter with a dairy-free margarine or coconut oil. The texture may be slightly different, but the cake will still be moist and flavorful. Ensure your margarine is suitable for baking and avoid oils that are too liquid at room temperature.
How do I prevent blueberries from sinking?
Coating the blueberries lightly with flour before folding them into the batter helps suspend them evenly. Also, fold them in gently to avoid breaking the berries and releasing juice that can weigh them down.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes, use a gluten-free baking blend that includes xanthan gum for structure. Baking times may vary slightly, so check doneness with a toothpick. The texture will be a bit different but still delicious.
How should I store leftover cake?
Store leftover cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. Bring to room temperature before serving for the best texture and flavor.
Can I freeze the cake?
Absolutely! Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and foil, then freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and bring to room temperature. Freezing might slightly change the texture but maintains flavor well.
