Smoky and Tender Pellet Grilled Chicken with Wood Pellets

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 14, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty
4.7 (20 ratings)✓ Thanks for rating!

This pellet grilled chicken is juicy and smoky without any hassle. Using wood pellets gives it that rich, deep flavor you just can't replicate. Perfect for weekend cookouts or relaxed family dinners!

Introduction

I first tried pellet grilling a few summers ago and honestly, it changed how I think about backyard cooking. The magic of wood pellets is that they add this irresistible smoky flavor without any complicated steps or constant attention. This recipe is my go-to when I want juicy chicken with that perfect smoky kiss. I've learned that seasoning the chicken simply with smoked paprika and garlic powder lets the pellet grill do all the heavy lifting flavor-wise. One trick I picked up is to preheat the grill well and keep the temperature steady around 225°F to 250°F; this slow cooking locks in moisture and creates that tender texture everyone loves. The finished chicken is juicy, lightly charred, and infused with subtle smoke — perfect for casual dinners or impressing friends at weekend cookouts. Plus, it’s quick enough that you’re not stuck outside all day. If you’ve never tried pellet grilling, this recipe is a friendly introduction, and I think you’ll love how hands-off yet delicious it is!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Wood pellets (hickory or apple) for the pellet grill

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your pellet grill to about 225°F. This lower temperature lets the chicken cook slowly and soak up the wood smoke. Make sure your grill is clean and the wood pellets are fresh for the best flavor. Avoid rushing this step because consistent heat is key for tender, juicy chicken instead of drying it out.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Grill temperature steady at 225°F and smoke steadily coming from the grill
Step 2
While the grill warms, pat the chicken breasts dry with paper towels. This helps the seasoning stick and promotes better browning. Rub each breast with olive oil evenly to keep moisture locked in during cooking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken breasts feel slightly tacky from the olive oil, ready for seasoning
Step 3
Mix the smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper in a small bowl. Sprinkle this seasoning blend evenly over both sides of each chicken breast. The smoked paprika is key here for that subtle smoky depth that complements the pellet grill’s flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken breasts are evenly coated with a reddish seasoning layer
Step 4
Place the chicken breasts on the grill grates, spacing them out to ensure even smoke exposure. Close the lid and let them cook undisturbed at 225°F. Resist the urge to flip too often; slow and steady is the way to go for tender results.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken surface looks cooked and slightly firm when pressed gently
Step 5
After about 20 minutes, check the internal temperature with a meat thermometer. Aim for 160°F before the final sear. If it hasn’t reached that, close the lid and let it cook a bit longer. This step ensures the chicken is safe to eat but still juicy.
⏱ Time: 5-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Internal temperature reads 160°F
Step 6
Increase the grill temperature to 400°F for a quick sear. Flip the chicken breasts and sear each side for 2-3 minutes to develop a nice caramelized crust. This final step adds texture and enhances flavor complexity.
⏱ Time: 6 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken has golden-brown grill marks and a crisp crust
Step 7
Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for 5 minutes. Resting lets the juices redistribute inside the meat, so it stays moist when you cut into it. Don’t skip this or you’ll lose all that delicious moisture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels firm but springs back slightly when pressed
Step 8
Slice the chicken against the grain for the best tenderness. Serve it with your favorite sides like grilled veggies or a fresh salad. The smoky flavor pairs beautifully with simple, bright sides.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Slices reveal juicy interior with slight pinkish tint but fully cooked
Step 9
Clean your grill grates while still warm to prevent residue buildup and keep your pellet grill ready for next time. This quick maintenance step helps maintain flavor purity and extends grill life.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Grill grates are free of stuck-on bits and ready for next use

Pro Tips

Preheat your pellet grill thoroughly before cooking.
A properly preheated grill ensures stable temperature and consistent smoke output. If the grill isn’t hot enough, the chicken won’t cook evenly and may absorb too much smoke, resulting in a bitter taste.
Pat chicken dry before applying oil and seasoning.
Removing surface moisture helps the oil and spices stick better and promotes the Maillard reaction, which creates that delicious browned crust during searing.
Use a meat thermometer to check doneness.
Chicken breast can dry out quickly if overcooked. Monitoring internal temperature precisely ensures juicy, safe-to-eat chicken every time without guesswork.
Let the chicken rest after grilling before slicing.
Resting allows juices to redistribute throughout the meat. Cutting immediately causes juices to run out, resulting in dry chicken.
Choose wood pellets based on desired smoke intensity.
Fruit woods like apple provide mild, sweet smoke, while hickory offers a stronger, traditional BBQ flavor. Matching wood choice with your seasoning creates balanced flavor.
Clean grill grates after each use.
Removing residue prevents flare-ups and preserves the pure wood smoke flavor for future grilling sessions.
Sear chicken at high heat at the end.
The quick sear caramelizes the spices and creates a tasty crust without drying out the interior.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Cooking chicken at too high a temperature from the start

Why it happens Many cooks want to speed up grilling, assuming higher heat means faster cooking, but this dries out the chicken and prevents smoke from penetrating.

Fix Start at a low temperature (225°F) to allow slow cooking and smoke absorption, then finish with a quick high-heat sear for crust.

Not using a meat thermometer

Why it happens Guessing doneness can lead to overcooked or undercooked chicken since visual cues can be misleading.

Fix Use an instant-read thermometer to check for 160°F internal temperature before searing, ensuring juicy and safe chicken.

Skipping the resting step

Why it happens Impatience often causes cooks to slice immediately, losing precious juices.

Fix Allow chicken to rest 5 minutes after grilling. This keeps it moist and tender.

Applying seasoning on wet chicken

Why it happens Moisture prevents spices from sticking evenly, causing patchy flavor and less browning.

Fix Pat chicken dry thoroughly before oil and seasoning application.

Using old or poor quality wood pellets

Why it happens Old pellets can produce weak or unpleasant smoke, affecting flavor negatively.

Fix Buy fresh, 100% hardwood pellets and store them in a dry place to maintain quality and flavor.

Variations

Spicy Pellet Grilled Chicken

Add 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper and 1 teaspoon chili powder to the seasoning mix for a nice kick. This variation adds heat but keeps the smoky flavor balanced. Great if you like a little spice in your BBQ.

Note: Be careful not to overdo the cayenne; too much can overpower the smoke and dry out the chicken.

Herb-Infused Pellet Grilled Chicken

Mix in 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh rosemary and thyme with the dry spices. The herbs add an earthy aroma that pairs beautifully with the wood smoke, creating a more complex flavor profile.

Note: Add herbs just before grilling to preserve their aroma and avoid burning.

Citrus-Marinated Pellet Grilled Chicken

Marinate the chicken in lemon juice, olive oil, and garlic for 2 hours before seasoning and grilling. The acid tenderizes the meat and adds brightness that contrasts nicely with the smoky notes.

Note: Don’t marinate longer than 3 hours or the acid may start to 'cook' the chicken, altering its texture.

Pellet Grilled Chicken Thighs

Swap breasts for bone-in, skin-on thighs for more juicy and flavorful meat. Thighs hold up well to smoking and develop a crispy skin when seared.

Note: Increase cooking time by 10-15 minutes and monitor internal temperature carefully.

Honey-Glazed Pellet Grilled Chicken

Brush chicken with honey mixed with a splash of apple cider vinegar during the last 5 minutes of grilling for a sweet, tangy glaze that caramelizes beautifully.

Note: Apply glaze towards the end to prevent burning the sugars.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cooked chicken can safely be left out for up to 2 hours covered loosely with foil or a clean cloth to prevent drying out. Beyond this, refrigerate promptly to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The smoky flavor intensifies overnight but the texture may firm up slightly. Reheat gently to avoid drying.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken in airtight freezer bags or containers for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator for best results.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat or in the microwave with a splash of water or broth to keep moist. Avoid high heat which can dry the chicken out quickly.

Make Ahead

Season the chicken and store it covered in the fridge for up to 24 hours before grilling. This allows flavors to develop deeper. Grill and sear just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use bone-in chicken instead of boneless breasts?
Yes! Bone-in chicken thighs or breasts work well on the pellet grill and stay juicy. Just increase cooking time by 10-15 minutes and always check internal temperature to ensure doneness.
What wood pellets are best for chicken?
Fruit woods like apple or cherry give mild, sweet smoke that pairs nicely with chicken. Hickory is stronger and traditional but can be overpowering if overused. Use fresh, pure hardwood pellets for best results.
How do I avoid dry chicken on the pellet grill?
Cook at a lower temperature (225°F–250°F) and use a meat thermometer to remove chicken at 160°F internal temperature before searing. Rest the meat before slicing to retain juices.
Can I marinate the chicken before grilling?
Absolutely! A citrus or herb marinade adds extra flavor and tenderness. Just don’t marinate too long (max 3 hours) or the acid may change the texture.
How long does it take to cook chicken on a pellet grill?
Typically 25-30 minutes at 225°F, plus about 5-6 minutes for the final sear at higher heat. Time varies slightly based on thickness and grill model.
Do I need to flip the chicken during cooking?
Flipping once halfway through cooking is ideal to ensure even smoke exposure and cooking. Flipping too often can interrupt smoke absorption and cause uneven cooking.
Can I use this recipe on a charcoal or gas grill?
You can, but you won’t get the same smoky flavor from wood pellets. On charcoal, add wood chips for smoke; on gas, consider using a smoker box with wood chips.
How do I clean my pellet grill after cooking?
Once cooled slightly, scrape grill grates with a grill brush to remove residue. Empty the pellet hopper regularly and wipe down surfaces to maintain performance and flavor.
