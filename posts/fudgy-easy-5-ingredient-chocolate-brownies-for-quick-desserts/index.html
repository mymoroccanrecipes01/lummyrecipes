Fudgy Five-Ingredient Brownies for Quick Chocolate Comfort

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 16, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These brownies are my go-to when I need something chocolaty and quick. Only five ingredients but super fudgy and rich. Great for an easy weekend treat or last-minute dessert fix!

Pro Tips

Use a glass or metal baking pan for even heat distribution.
Glass and metal pans conduct heat differently; metal pans heat faster and create crisper edges, while glass heats more slowly and can cause underbaking if not monitored. Choosing the right pan type affects texture and baking time, so adjust accordingly.
Don’t overmix once flour is added to avoid tough brownies.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which toughens the texture and makes brownies cakier rather than fudgy. Mixing just until combined keeps the crumb tender and dense.
Let the brownies cool completely before slicing.
Cooling allows the brownies to firm up and prevents crumbling or falling apart when cut. Cutting them too soon results in a gooey mess and uneven pieces.
Use a sharp knife wiped clean between cuts for neat squares.
A sharp, clean knife prevents dragging and tearing the brownies, giving you smooth edges and professional-looking pieces. Wiping the knife removes sticky residue that can cause ragged cuts.
Sift cocoa powder to avoid lumps in the batter.
Cocoa powder often clumps, which can cause uneven chocolate flavor and texture. Sifting ensures a smooth, uniform batter and a consistent fudgy texture throughout.
Add a pinch of salt even if using salted butter for balance.
Salt enhances the chocolate flavor and balances sweetness. Even if your butter is salted, a small pinch of extra salt can brighten the overall taste.
Use room temperature eggs for best emulsification.
Cold eggs can cause the batter to seize or curdle when mixed with melted butter. Room temperature eggs blend more smoothly, resulting in a uniform batter and better texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overbaking the brownies.

Why it happens Many people leave the brownies in the oven too long thinking they need to be fully set. The desire for a fully cooked center leads to dry, cakey brownies.

Fix Start checking the brownies around 25 minutes. Look for set edges with a slightly jiggly center. Pull them out to avoid drying and allow residual heat to finish cooking during cooling.

Using cold eggs straight from the fridge.

Why it happens It’s easy to overlook this step and add cold eggs directly, which can cause the batter to seize or curdle when mixed with warm butter.

Fix Let eggs sit at room temperature for 20-30 minutes before using. This helps them blend smoothly, preventing separation and ensuring a uniform batter.

Overmixing after adding flour.

Why it happens In a rush, people tend to mix vigorously to combine ingredients quickly, which develops gluten and toughens the brownies.

Fix Fold flour gently with a spatula just until no streaks remain. Stop mixing immediately to keep the texture fudgy and tender.

Not greasing or lining the pan properly.

Why it happens Skipping this step often leads to brownies sticking to the pan and breaking apart when removed.

Fix Grease the pan thoroughly with butter or nonstick spray, or line it with parchment paper extending over the edges to lift brownies out easily.

Using low-quality cocoa powder.

Why it happens Choosing weak or old cocoa powder results in lackluster chocolate flavor and dull color.

Fix Buy fresh, high-quality natural unsweetened cocoa powder. Store it in a cool, dry place to maintain potency.

Variations

Nutty Chocolate Brownies

Add 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans into the batter before baking for extra crunch and flavor. Nuts add texture contrast and a toasty note that complements the fudgy brownies perfectly.

Note: Fold nuts gently to avoid overmixing and distribute evenly. Baking time remains the same.

Chocolate Chip Brownies

Stir in 1 cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips for pockets of melty chocolate throughout. This variation ups the chocolate factor and adds gooey bites that everyone loves.

Note: Add chips last and fold in carefully. Use good quality chips for best melting and flavor.

Espresso-Infused Brownies

Mix 1 tablespoon of instant espresso powder into the cocoa before combining. The espresso intensifies the chocolate flavor and adds a subtle mocha twist.

Note: Dissolve espresso powder fully for even flavor. No change in baking time needed.

Gluten-Free Brownies

Replace all-purpose flour with a 1:1 gluten-free baking blend. These brownies stay fudgy and rich with a slightly different texture but just as satisfying.

Note: Check blend ingredients and add xanthan gum if not included to maintain structure.

Salted Caramel Brownies

Drizzle homemade or store-bought salted caramel sauce over the batter before baking and swirl lightly. This adds a sweet-salty surprise and an extra layer of decadence.

Note: Swirl carefully to avoid deflating the batter. Baking time may increase by a few minutes due to added moisture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store brownies covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Use an airtight container or wrap tightly with plastic wrap to prevent drying out. They remain fudgy and moist when stored properly.

Refrigerator

Keep brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Chilling firms them up and enhances flavor, but they may become slightly denser. Bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving for best texture.

Freezer

Wrap brownies tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave at room temperature for a few hours or warm gently in the oven. Freezing may slightly change texture but they remain delicious.

Reheating

Warm brownies in a low oven (300°F) for 5-10 minutes or microwave briefly (15-20 seconds) with a damp paper towel to prevent drying. Avoid overheating, which can toughen them. Add a scoop of ice cream for extra indulgence.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and refrigerate it covered. Bring to room temperature and stir gently before baking. Alternatively, bake the brownies and store them refrigerated or frozen to serve later.

FAQ

Can I substitute oil for butter in this recipe?
Yes, you can use vegetable oil instead of melted butter for a slightly different texture. Oil makes brownies moist but less rich and dense. Butter adds flavor and helps create the crackly top, so the taste might be less buttery with oil.
Why are my brownies cakey instead of fudgy?
Overmixing after adding flour or baking too long are common reasons. Mix the flour gently and bake until the center jiggles slightly. Using too much flour or substituting with self-rising flour can also cause cakey texture.
Can I add chocolate chips or nuts?
Absolutely! Fold in 1 cup of chocolate chips or chopped nuts before baking. They add texture and extra flavor. Just be careful not to overmix when adding to keep the batter tender.
How do I store leftover brownies?
Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze tightly wrapped for up to 3 months. Thaw before serving for best texture.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, swap the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum for structure. The texture might be slightly different but still fudgy and delicious.
Why is my brownie batter grainy?
This usually happens if the sugar hasn’t dissolved well in the melted butter. Stir thoroughly until the mixture looks smooth and glossy to avoid graininess.
Can I use Dutch-processed cocoa powder?
You can, but it will affect the pH and slightly change the texture and flavor. Dutch-processed cocoa is less acidic and produces a milder chocolate flavor. Adjust leavening if you add baking powder or soda.
How to tell when brownies are done?
Look for set edges and a shiny, crackly top. The center should still jiggle slightly when you gently shake the pan. This ensures fudgy, not overbaked brownies.

What You'll Need

8 large brownies

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking pan. This ensures your brownies don’t stick and bake evenly. Getting the oven temperature right is crucial because too hot will dry them out, and too cool will make them dense. Greasing the pan thoroughly or lining it with parchment paper helps you lift the brownies out easily once baked — trust me, it saves a lot of frustration!
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased or parchment paper is snugly fitted without wrinkles
Step 2
In a large bowl, pour 1 cup of melted unsalted butter and immediately add 2 cups of granulated sugar. Stir vigorously until combined. This step is key because the melted butter coats the sugar, helping to dissolve it slightly, which contributes to the fudgy texture of the brownies. If you skip this or mix it cold, your brownies might turn out grainy or cakey.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks smooth and glossy with no visible sugar granules
Step 3
Add 3/4 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder to the wet mixture and whisk until fully incorporated. The cocoa powder is the chocolate heart of these brownies, so make sure to sift it beforehand if it’s clumpy. Mixing it in well avoids streaks and ensures the brownies have a uniform chocolate flavor. Using good quality cocoa really makes a difference here.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is rich brown and smooth, without lumps
Step 4
Beat in 4 large eggs one at a time, mixing thoroughly after each addition. The eggs provide structure but also moisture, helping the brownies stay dense yet soft. Adding them slowly ensures the batter emulsifies properly, which is why your brownies won’t crumble or dry out. If you add all eggs at once, the batter might separate or become runny.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter thickens slightly and looks shiny
Step 5
Gently fold in 1 cup of all-purpose flour using a spatula. Be careful not to overmix here — you want just enough to combine the flour without developing gluten, which would make the brownies tough. Stop mixing as soon as you don’t see dry flour. This step balances the fudgy texture with a little bit of structure.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter looks thick and uniform, no flour streaks
Step 6
Pour the batter evenly into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Spreading it evenly ensures uniform baking and that you don’t get dry edges or underbaked centers. Don’t tap the pan too aggressively to avoid air bubbles, which can create holes in your brownies.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Surface is smooth and level
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Keep a close eye starting at 25 minutes; overbaking is the biggest pitfall here. The brownies are done when the edges look set and shiny but the center still has a slight jiggle when you gently shake the pan. This ensures fudginess without being raw.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are firm, center jiggles slightly
Step 8
Remove the brownies from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling is essential because the brownies continue to set as they cool, making them easier to cut without crumbling. Cutting too hot can lead to a mess and loss of that dense, fudgy texture.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownies are cool to the touch and hold their shape
Step 9
Once cooled, cut into squares with a sharp knife. For clean edges, wipe the knife between cuts. Serve as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgence. These brownies keep their fudgy texture for days, making them perfect for making ahead or sharing with friends.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Squares hold shape with clean edges when sliced
