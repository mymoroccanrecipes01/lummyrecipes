No-Bake Mango Cheesecake Cups That Are Summer’s Best Refreshing Treat

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 17, 2026
15 min Prep Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These no-bake mango cheesecake cups are an easy, fresh dessert perfect for hot days. The creamy filling pairs beautifully with sweet mango puree, no oven needed. Great for casual gatherings or a quick summer fix!

Pro Tips

Chill your mixing bowls and beaters before whipping cream.
Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold stiff peaks longer. Warm bowls or beaters can cause the cream to be runny or not whip at all, making it harder to achieve the light, airy texture needed for the filling.
Press the graham cracker crust firmly into the cups.
Firm pressing compacts the crumbs and butter, preventing a crumbly crust that falls apart when eating. This ensures each bite has a satisfying crunch and stable base to support the creamy filling.
Fold whipped cream into cream cheese gently using a spatula.
Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring or beating aggressively would deflate the mixture, resulting in a dense, heavy cheesecake layer.
Use ripe mangos for the puree and strain excess juice.
Ripe mangos provide natural sweetness and smooth texture. Straining removes extra liquid that could make the filling watery or cause layers to blend, ensuring clean separation and better mouthfeel.
Chill the assembled cups for at least 2 hours before serving.
Chilling firms up the filling and crust, allowing flavors to meld and layers to set. Serving too soon results in runny texture and less satisfying mouthfeel.
Use individual serving cups for easy portion control and presentation.
Serving in cups makes this dessert perfect for parties and casual gatherings. It also keeps layers intact and makes it easy to garnish beautifully with mango chunks or mint.
Avoid adding extra sugar to the mango puree.
The natural sweetness of ripe mango balances the cream cheese filling. Adding sugar can make the topping overly sweet and mask the fresh mango flavor, disrupting the dessert’s balance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not softening the cream cheese enough

Why it happens People often try to mix cream cheese straight from the fridge, thinking it saves time.

Fix Allow cream cheese to sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes before mixing. This ensures it blends smoothly without lumps, giving a creamy texture rather than a grainy or chunky filling.

Overwhipping the heavy cream

Why it happens Some think whipping cream longer will make it fluffier, but it can quickly turn to butter.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. Watch carefully and test by lifting the beaters to see if peaks hold shape without drooping. Overwhipped cream becomes grainy and separates, ruining the filling texture.

Skipping the chilling time

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning leads to serving immediately after assembly.

Fix Plan ahead to chill the cheesecake cups at least 2 hours in the fridge. This sets the layers and improves texture. If rushed, the dessert will be runny and less enjoyable.

Using underripe or fibrous mangoes

Why it happens Sometimes only unripe mangos are available, or fibrous parts aren’t removed.

Fix Choose sweet, ripe mangos with smooth flesh. Blend thoroughly and strain to remove fibers. This ensures a silky puree that complements the cheesecake filling without gritty texture.

Pressing the crust too lightly

Why it happens It’s tempting to just sprinkle crumbs into cups without firm pressing.

Fix Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to firmly press the crumb mixture into the bottom of each cup. This creates a stable crust that won’t crumble apart when eating.

Variations

Tropical Twist with Coconut

Add 1/4 cup shredded coconut to the graham cracker crust for a tropical flair. You can also fold a tablespoon of coconut cream into the filling for extra richness and subtle coconut flavor. Expect a slightly nuttier crust texture and creamier filling that pairs beautifully with mango.

Note: Incorporate coconut gently into the crust mixture before pressing. Adjust butter slightly if the mixture feels dry. Coconut cream should be well chilled and folded carefully to maintain filling texture.

Berry Mango Cheesecake Cups

Layer fresh berries like raspberries or blueberries on top of the mango puree for a colorful, tangy contrast. This adds freshness and a pop of flavor that balances the sweet mango. Perfect for berry season or when you want more fruit variety.

Note: Add berries just before serving to keep them fresh and prevent them from bleeding into the mango layer. Use firm, ripe berries for best texture.

Vegan Mango Cheesecake Cups

Swap cream cheese with vegan cream cheese and use coconut cream instead of heavy cream. Use a dairy-free butter substitute for the crust. This version is creamy and delicious while being dairy-free, perfect for vegan guests or those with lactose intolerance.

Note: Whip coconut cream well and chill thoroughly before folding. Vegan cream cheese should be softened to blend smoothly. The crust may require a bit more pressing to hold together.

Spiced Mango Cheesecake Cups

Add a pinch of ground cardamom or cinnamon to the mango puree for warm spice notes. This subtle addition adds depth and complexity, making the dessert feel more special and aromatic.

Note: Mix spices into the mango puree thoroughly and taste to avoid overpowering. Keep spice addition light to complement mango’s natural sweetness.

Chocolate Mango Cheesecake Cups

Add a thin layer of melted dark chocolate between the crust and cheesecake filling. The bittersweet chocolate pairs wonderfully with the sweet mango and cream cheese, creating a decadent, multi-layered dessert experience.

Note: Allow the chocolate layer to set slightly before adding the cheesecake filling to prevent mixing. Use good-quality chocolate for best flavor.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These cheesecake cups should not be kept at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the dairy content. Cover them with plastic wrap or a lid to protect from dust and odors if left out briefly.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The texture remains creamy and fresh, though the mango layer may soften slightly. Serve chilled for best taste and texture.

Freezer

Freeze the cheesecake cups in airtight containers for up to 1 month. Wrap cups individually with plastic wrap and then place in a freezer bag to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and stir gently before serving to restore texture.

Reheating

This dessert is best served cold and should not be reheated. If the filling feels too firm after chilling, let it sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes to soften slightly before serving.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the crust and mango puree up to 2 days in advance, storing them separately in airtight containers. Assemble the cheesecake cups on the day of serving for the freshest texture and best layer definition.

FAQ

Can I use canned mango puree instead of fresh mango?
While canned mango puree can be used in a pinch, fresh mango puree is preferred for its bright, natural flavor and smooth texture. Canned versions sometimes contain added sugars or preservatives that can alter the taste and consistency of the dessert.
How long can I store these cheesecake cups in the fridge?
Store them in an airtight container for up to 3 days. After that, the texture and flavor may start to degrade, especially the mango layer, which can become watery. For best results, enjoy within the first two days.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! Simply substitute the graham cracker crumbs with gluten-free cookie crumbs or crushed gluten-free crackers. Make sure your other ingredients, like cream cheese and butter, don’t contain gluten additives.
What if I don’t have heavy cream? Can I use milk or half-and-half?
Heavy cream is necessary because it whips into stiff peaks and provides the light, airy texture. Milk or half-and-half won’t whip properly and will result in a dense, runny filling.
How can I make the mango puree if I don’t have a blender?
You can mash ripe mango flesh with a fork or potato masher until smooth, then press through a fine sieve to remove fibers. This manual method takes a bit more effort but works well for small quantities.
Can I use flavored cream cheese like strawberry or honey?
It’s best to use plain cream cheese to keep the mango flavor front and center. Flavored cream cheeses can clash with the mango and change the dessert’s balance.
Why is my cheesecake filling grainy?
This usually happens if the cream cheese wasn’t softened enough or was overmixed. Make sure to bring cream cheese to room temperature before beating and mix just until smooth.
Can I add gelatin to help the filling set?
This recipe is designed to set without gelatin thanks to the whipped cream and chilling. Adding gelatin could change the texture, making it firmer and less creamy, so it’s not necessary.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 individual servings

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs, finely crushed
  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 3/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh mango puree (about 2 ripe mangos)
  • Fresh mango chunks for garnish (optional)
  • Mint leaves for garnish (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the crust: combine the finely crushed graham cracker crumbs with the melted butter in a medium bowl. Mix well until the crumbs are evenly coated and the mixture holds together when pressed. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs, creating that perfect crumbly yet firm base. Avoid using too little butter, or the crust will be dry and crumbly. Press the mixture evenly into the bottom of your serving cups or glasses, creating a solid layer. Chill in the fridge for at least 10 minutes to set while you prepare the filling.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Crust feels firm and holds shape when lightly pressed
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with a hand mixer on medium speed until smooth and creamy, about 2-3 minutes. This ensures there are no lumps and the texture will be silky. Gradually add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, mixing until fully incorporated. The sugar sweetens the cream cheese just enough without overpowering the mango flavor. Take care not to overmix at this stage to avoid making the mixture too runny.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, slightly glossy, and thick
Step 3
In a separate chilled bowl, whip the cold heavy cream using clean beaters until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the beaters, the peaks hold their shape without drooping. The cold temperature helps the cream whip faster and hold better, which is essential for a fluffy, airy filling. Be careful not to overwhip, or the cream will turn grainy and start to separate into butter.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Whipped cream forms stiff, glossy peaks
Step 4
Gently fold about one-third of the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture to lighten it. Use a spatula and fold carefully to avoid deflating the air you just whipped in. Then fold in the remaining whipped cream until the mixture is uniform and fluffy. This folding technique keeps the cheesecake filling light and creamy instead of dense. Avoid stirring aggressively, which would make the filling heavy and lose volume.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Filling is light, smooth, and consistent in texture
Step 5
Spoon or pipe a generous layer of the cheesecake filling over the chilled graham cracker crust in each cup. Smooth the top gently with the back of a spoon or spatula. This layering creates the creamy base that contrasts with the crunchy crust and fruity topping. Work carefully to keep the layers neat and even for a beautiful presentation.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake layer is smooth and evenly spread
Step 6
Next, spoon the fresh mango puree over the cheesecake layer, spreading it evenly to cover the filling. The mango puree adds a bright, natural sweetness and vibrant color that makes these cups irresistible. Use ripe, fragrant mangos for the best flavor. If your puree is too runny, strain it through a fine sieve to remove excess juice so it sets nicely without mixing into the filling.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Mango layer is smooth, glossy, and evenly distributed
Step 7
Place the assembled cups in the refrigerator to chill for at least 2 hours, or until the cheesecake filling firms up and the flavors meld. Chilling is important to set the layers so they hold their shape when served. Resist the temptation to rush this step, as serving too soon will result in a runny texture and less defined layers.
⏱ Time: 120
✓ Doneness: Filling is firm to the touch and chilled through
Step 8
Just before serving, garnish each mango cheesecake cup with fresh mango chunks and a small mint leaf if desired. This adds a fresh, decorative touch and a pop of color that makes the dessert look even more inviting. Plus, the mint adds a subtle herbal contrast to the sweet mango and creamy filling.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Garnishes are fresh and vibrant, enhancing presentation
Step 9
Serve chilled and enjoy immediately for the best texture and flavor. These cups are perfect for summer parties or a quick treat when you want something cool and fruity without the fuss of baking. Leftovers can be stored in the fridge but are best eaten within a couple of days for freshness.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Dessert is cold, creamy, and refreshing
