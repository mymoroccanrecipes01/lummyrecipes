Easy One-Pot Instant Pot Chicken and Rice Dinner for Busy Nights

Lummy Hayes Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This one-pot Instant Pot chicken and rice dinner is a total lifesaver when you're short on time. Everything cooks together, locking in flavor and making cleanup a breeze. Fresh herbs at the end add a lovely pop of brightness—great for weeknight meals!

Introduction

I first tried this Instant Pot chicken and rice recipe when I was desperate for something quick and satisfying after a long day. It quickly became a favorite because it’s just so easy—everything cooks together in one pot, which means less cleanup and more time to relax. What makes my version different is the mix of spices I use, especially smoked paprika, which gives it a subtle smoky depth that really elevates the dish. Plus, I always add fresh parsley right at the end for a bright, fresh touch that balances the rich flavors. I’ve had my share of rice disasters in the past—either mushy or undercooked—but the Instant Pot timing here is spot on, making the rice perfectly fluffy every time. The chicken stays juicy, and the peas add just the right pop of sweetness. I think this recipe works great for weeknights when you want comfort food without spending hours in the kitchen.

Why This Works

This recipe succeeds because it balances moisture, flavor development, and timing perfectly within the Instant Pot’s pressure cooking environment. First, sautéing the onions and garlic builds a rich aromatic foundation through gentle caramelization and flavor release. Browning the chicken triggers the Maillard reaction, which creates complex savory notes that elevate the dish beyond simple boiled chicken and rice.

Toasting the rice before pressure cooking is a clever step that coats the grains with oil, helping them stay separate and preventing a mushy texture. This is essential in pressure cooking where the high heat and steam can otherwise cause clumping. The deglazing step with broth prevents burning by releasing flavorful browned bits from the pot’s surface, while also enriching the cooking liquid with umami.

Pressure cooking locks moisture inside, ensuring the chicken stays juicy and the rice cooks evenly. The natural pressure release lets the steam finish cooking the rice gently, avoiding overcooking. Adding frozen peas at the end uses residual heat to warm them just right, preserving their bright color and texture. Finally, fresh parsley adds a fresh herbal note that cuts through the richness and rounds out the flavors beautifully.

  • Look for boneless, skinless chicken thighs with a fresh pink color and firm texture. Thighs are preferred here because their higher fat content keeps the meat tender and juicy during pressure cooking, unlike leaner breasts that can dry out. If you need to substitute, chicken breasts are possible but watch cooking times closely to avoid toughness.
  • Choose long grain white rice for its fluffy texture and ability to stay separate when cooked. Rinsing the rice removes excess surface starch, which helps prevent clumping. Avoid instant or parboiled rice here, as their cooking times and water absorption differ, which can throw off the recipe.
  • Use a good-quality low-sodium chicken broth to control salt levels and add depth. Broth provides the necessary liquid for pressure cooking and infuses the rice and chicken with savory flavor. Homemade broth works wonderfully here, but store-bought is fine as long as it’s not too salty.
  • Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky warmth that enhances the chicken’s flavor without overpowering it. It’s a key ingredient that differentiates this recipe from basic chicken and rice dishes. If you don’t have smoked paprika, regular paprika or a mild chili powder can be used, but the smoky nuance will be missing.
  • Fresh parsley is best added at the end to preserve its bright color and fresh, slightly peppery flavor. It adds a lovely contrast to the rich, savory notes of the cooked dish. Flat-leaf parsley is preferred for its robust flavor, but curly parsley works fine too. Avoid dried parsley here, as it won’t provide the same freshness.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs, trimmed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups long grain white rice, rinsed
  • 2 1/4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Set your Instant Pot to the sauté function and heat the olive oil until shimmering. Add the diced onion and cook for about 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is soft and translucent. This step is key because sautéing the onion first builds a flavorful base for the dish. Avoid rushing it or the onions won’t develop their natural sweetness, which helps balance the spices later.

    ⏱ 5✓ Onions should be soft and translucent without any browning.
  2. Step 2

    Add the minced garlic and dried thyme to the pot and sauté for another 30 seconds to 1 minute until fragrant. Garlic burns quickly, so keep an eye on it and stir constantly. This quick step releases the garlic’s essential oils and infuses the oil with flavor, which will permeate the whole dish.

    ⏱ 1✓ You should smell a fresh, garlicky aroma but see no browning.
  3. Step 3

    Season the chicken thighs with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika. Place them in the pot on top of the sautéed onions and garlic. Sauté for 2-3 minutes per side, just until they get a light golden color. This browning step adds a rich, caramelized flavor through the Maillard reaction, which deepens the overall taste of the dish. Don’t overcook here since the chicken will finish cooking under pressure.

    ⏱ 6✓ Chicken should have a light golden crust but not be fully cooked through.
  4. Step 4

    Remove the chicken temporarily and add the rinsed rice to the pot. Stir it into the onion and garlic mixture for about 1-2 minutes to toast the rice lightly. This technique helps prevent mushy rice by coating each grain with oil, which keeps them separate during cooking. Toasting also adds a subtle nutty flavor.

    ⏱ 2✓ Rice grains appear slightly translucent and lightly coated with oil.
  5. Step 5

    Pour in the chicken broth, scraping the bottom of the pot with a wooden spoon to release any browned bits. This deglazing step is crucial to prevent the Instant Pot from triggering a burn warning and to incorporate all that flavor into the liquid. Make sure you really loosen those bits—they’re packed with umami.

    ⏱ 2✓ The bottom of the pot is clean, and broth is evenly mixed.
  6. Step 6

    Nestle the browned chicken thighs back into the pot on top of the rice. Seal the Instant Pot lid and set it to cook on high pressure for 10 minutes. This pressure cooking perfectly cooks both the rice and chicken simultaneously, locking in moisture and flavor. Don’t rush the natural pressure release afterward; letting it sit for 10 minutes helps the rice finish steaming gently.

    ⏱ 10✓ Instant Pot completes cooking cycle and natural release finishes.
  7. Step 7

    After the natural release, open the lid and quickly stir in the frozen peas. The residual heat will warm the peas without overcooking them, preserving their bright color and fresh flavor. Stirring them in at the end also keeps their texture pleasant and slightly crisp.

    ⏱ 2✓ Peas are heated through but still vibrant green and not mushy.
  8. Step 8

    Taste the dish and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Adding seasoning at the end lets you control the saltiness since broth and chicken already contribute salt. This step ensures balanced flavor without overdoing it.

    ⏱ 1✓ Balanced, well-rounded seasoning that enhances all ingredients.
  9. Step 9

    Sprinkle fresh chopped parsley over the top before serving. The fresh herbs brighten the dish and add a lovely contrast in texture and color. It’s a simple touch that transforms the meal from everyday to special in seconds.

    ⏱ 1✓ Fresh, vibrant green herbs scattered evenly on top.

Pro Tips

  • Always rinse your rice before cooking. — Rinsing removes excess surface starch from the rice grains, which can cause the rice to become gummy or clump together during cooking. By washing it until the water runs clear, you ensure fluffier, separated grains in the final dish.
  • Use natural pressure release instead of quick release. — Allowing the Instant Pot to release pressure naturally lets the rice finish cooking gently with the residual steam. Quick release can shock the rice, resulting in uneven texture or undercooked grains.
  • Don’t skip the deglazing step after sautéing. — Deglazing loosens the flavorful browned bits stuck to the pot’s bottom, preventing burn errors during pressure cooking and adding deep umami flavor to the dish.
  • Add frozen peas after pressure cooking. — Adding peas at the end preserves their bright green color and fresh texture, preventing them from turning mushy or losing flavor.
  • Trim excess fat from chicken thighs before cooking. — Removing excess fat helps prevent greasy texture in the final dish while still keeping the chicken juicy due to its naturally higher fat content.
  • Use a wooden or silicone spatula to scrape the pot bottom. — Using metal utensils can scratch the Instant Pot’s inner surface and damage the non-stick coating, so gentle tools prolong your pot’s lifespan.
  • Season at the end to taste. — Broth and chicken already contain salt, so waiting until the dish is finished lets you adjust seasoning without over-salting.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the rice rinse step
    Why: Many cooks overlook rinsing rice because it seems like an extra step or they assume it’s unnecessary.
    Fix: Always rinse the rice under cold water until it runs clear to remove starch. This simple step prevents clumping and yields fluffy rice instead of a sticky mess.
  • Not deglazing the pot after sautéing
    Why: Some assume sautéing is just to soften veggies and skip scraping the pot, ignoring the flavor in browned bits.
    Fix: Pour in broth and use a wooden spoon to scrape the bottom thoroughly before pressure cooking. This prevents burn warnings and adds rich flavor.
  • Overcooking chicken during sauté
    Why: Trying to fully cook chicken in the sauté step wastes time and results in dry meat after pressure cooking.
    Fix: Just brown chicken lightly for flavor; the pressure cooking will finish cooking it perfectly while keeping it moist.
  • Releasing pressure too quickly
    Why: Impatience leads some to quick release immediately after cooking, which can shock the rice.
    Fix: Use natural pressure release for about 10 minutes to let the rice steam gently and finish cooking evenly.
  • Adding frozen peas too early
    Why: Some add peas before pressure cooking, causing them to become mushy and lose color.
    Fix: Stir peas in after cooking while the pot is still warm to heat them through without overcooking.

Variations

  • Spicy Cajun Chicken and Rice: Add 1 teaspoon Cajun seasoning and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the chicken before sautéing. Use diced bell peppers and swap peas for corn for a Southern twist. Expect a bolder, spicier flavor with a slightly different veggie texture.
    Watch the spice level and adjust broth saltiness accordingly, as Cajun seasoning can be salty.
  • Creamy Mushroom Chicken and Rice: Sauté 8 ounces sliced mushrooms with the onions, then add 1/2 cup heavy cream after pressure cooking. This adds a rich, velvety texture and earthy depth to the dish, making it feel more indulgent.
    Add cream only after pressure cooking to avoid curdling.
  • Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice: Add zest and juice of one lemon along with fresh thyme and rosemary. This variation brightens the dish with citrus notes and fragrant herbs, perfect for spring and summer dinners.
    Add lemon juice after cooking to preserve freshness and avoid bitterness.
  • Vegetable-Packed Chicken and Rice: Stir in diced carrots, celery, and bell peppers with the onions. Use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth for a lighter flavor. This makes the dish heartier and more colorful with extra nutrients.
    Increase broth by 1/4 cup to accommodate extra veggies.
  • Brown Rice and Chicken Instant Pot Dinner: Swap white rice for brown rice, increasing the cooking time to 22 minutes under high pressure. This variation adds nuttier flavor and more fiber but requires a longer cook time.
    Use 2 1/2 cups broth with brown rice to ensure proper cooking.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This chicken and rice dish can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Cover tightly with foil or a lid to avoid drying out or contamination.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The rice may firm up when cold but reheats well in the microwave with a splash of broth or water to regain moisture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, press out as much air as possible before sealing. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat gently in the microwave or on the stovetop over low heat with a small splash of broth to prevent dryness. Avoid overheating, which can make the chicken tough and the rice mushy.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep the sauté steps and add the rice and broth a day ahead, storing them covered in the fridge. When ready, add the chicken and cook in the Instant Pot as directed for fresh, fast dinner.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, you can substitute chicken breasts, but they cook faster and can dry out more easily. Reduce pressure cooking time by 2-3 minutes and use natural release to keep them juicy.
What if I don’t have smoked paprika?
You can use regular paprika or a mild chili powder. The smoky flavor will be missing, but the dish will still be delicious. Adding a small pinch of cumin can help add warmth.
Can I make this recipe in a slow cooker instead?
Yes, but cooking times and liquid amounts will differ. Slow cook on low for 4-6 hours or high for 2-3 hours, adding broth gradually to avoid soggy rice.
How do I prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom?
Make sure to rinse the rice and deglaze the pot well after sautéing. Also, avoid using the sauté function after adding the rice to prevent burning.
Can I add other vegetables?
Absolutely! Diced carrots, bell peppers, or mushrooms work well. Add them during the sauté step or stir frozen veggies in after cooking.
Is this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, as long as you use gluten-free chicken broth and check your spices for additives, this recipe is naturally gluten-free.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat gently with a little broth in the microwave or on the stovetop over low heat to keep the rice moist and chicken tender.
Can I double this recipe?
You can double it, but make sure not to fill your Instant Pot beyond the max fill line. You may need to cook in batches or use a larger pressure cooker.
