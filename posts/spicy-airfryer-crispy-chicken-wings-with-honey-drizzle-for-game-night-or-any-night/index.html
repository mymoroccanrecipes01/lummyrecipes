Crispy Air Fryer Wings with a Sweet and Spicy Twist

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These wings come out perfectly crispy thanks to the air fryer, with a spicy kick mellowed by a drizzle of honey. Super easy and great for casual dinners or game nights with friends.

Pro Tips

Make sure to pat the wings completely dry before seasoning.
Dry skin crisps up much better in the air fryer. Moisture causes steaming, which prevents the skin from getting that desirable crunch. This simple step makes the biggest difference in texture.
Don’t overcrowd the air fryer basket when cooking wings.
Crowding traps steam and blocks airflow, which leads to soggy wings. Cooking in a single layer with space allows air to circulate and crisp all sides evenly.
Flip or shake the wings every 8-10 minutes during air frying.
Turning the wings ensures they cook evenly and prevents burning on one side. It also helps develop uniform browning and crispness.
Add the honey glaze only after the wings are cooked.
Honey can burn easily at high heat, turning bitter. Applying it after cooking gives a sweet, sticky coating without compromising flavor or texture.
Adjust cayenne pepper amount to your preferred spice level.
Everyone’s heat tolerance is different. Starting with less cayenne allows you to control the spice without overwhelming the wings.
Preheat your air fryer before cooking.
A hot start promotes the Maillard reaction, browning the skin quickly and locking in juices. Skipping preheating can cause uneven cooking and less crispiness.
Use a meat thermometer to check doneness at 165°F.
Ensures wings are safely cooked through without drying out. Overcooking can make wings tough, so this helps get perfect juicy results every time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Cooking wings straight from the fridge without drying

Why it happens Many people skip drying since it seems like an extra step or they’re in a hurry.

Fix Always pat wings dry with paper towels before seasoning. This removes surface moisture that would steam the skin and prevent crispiness.

Overcrowding the air fryer basket

Why it happens Trying to cook all wings at once to save time or effort.

Fix Cook wings in batches if needed. Leave space between pieces so hot air can circulate and crisp the skin evenly.

Skipping the flipping or shaking during cooking

Why it happens Assuming the air fryer cooks evenly without intervention.

Fix Shake or flip wings every 8-10 minutes to ensure all sides brown and cook evenly, avoiding burnt spots or undercooked areas.

Applying honey glaze before cooking

Why it happens Thinking the glaze will caramelize better if cooked.

Fix Add honey glaze after cooking to avoid burning and bitterness. The residual heat will warm the glaze without compromising flavor.

Not preheating the air fryer

Why it happens Skipping preheating to save time or unaware it’s needed.

Fix Preheat the air fryer to 400°F before adding wings. This jumpstarts the crisping process and helps achieve golden skin.

Variations

Buffalo Style Wings

Swap the honey glaze for a classic buffalo sauce made with hot sauce and melted butter. This variation gives you that iconic tangy, spicy flavor while still keeping the wings crispy from the air fryer.

Note: Apply buffalo sauce after air frying just like the honey glaze to prevent burning. Serve with celery and blue cheese.

Lemon Pepper Wings

Use lemon zest, black pepper, and garlic powder in place of smoked paprika and cayenne. Toss wings with fresh lemon juice after cooking for a bright, zesty flavor that’s refreshing and light.

Note: Keep the same cooking time and temperature; just adjust seasoning before air frying.

Asian-Inspired Honey Soy Wings

Mix honey with soy sauce, ginger, and garlic for a savory-sweet glaze. The soy adds umami depth while the ginger gives a nice aromatic kick. Perfect for an Asian twist on crispy wings.

Note: Brush glaze on after cooking and serve with sesame seeds and green onions.

Dry Rub BBQ Wings

Skip the honey glaze and coat wings with a smoky BBQ dry rub before air frying. This gives intense flavor with a crispy, spice-coated skin. Great for BBQ lovers who want less sweetness.

Note: Add a light spray of oil before air frying to help the dry rub stick and crisp.

Garlic Parmesan Wings

After air frying, toss wings with melted butter, minced garlic, and grated Parmesan cheese. This rich, savory coating pairs perfectly with the crispy skin for a deliciously indulgent snack.

Note: Toss wings immediately after cooking while still hot to help the cheese and butter stick.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Wings can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean cloth. Beyond this, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store cooled wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Flavor and crispness will slightly diminish when cold, so reheat before serving to restore texture.

Freezer

Freeze wings in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat wings in the air fryer at 350°F for 5-7 minutes to regain crispness. Alternatively, use an oven at 375°F for 10 minutes. Avoid microwaving as it makes skin soggy. Add a splash of water if dry.

Make Ahead

You can season and air fry wings a day ahead, then store them refrigerated. Reheat just before serving and add fresh honey glaze. This saves time on busy days without sacrificing flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but make sure to fully thaw and pat them dry before seasoning. Cooking from frozen will result in soggy skin because excess moisture prevents crisping. Thawing ensures even cooking and crispy texture.
How spicy are these wings?
The spice level is moderate thanks to cayenne pepper, but you can adjust it by reducing or increasing the cayenne and hot sauce in the glaze. If you prefer mild, just skip or reduce the cayenne.
Can I bake these wings instead of using an air fryer?
Absolutely! Bake at 425°F on a wire rack over a baking sheet for 35-40 minutes, flipping halfway. The wire rack helps air circulate for crispier skin, but the air fryer generally crisps faster and more evenly.
Why are my wings not crispy?
Most likely because the wings weren’t dried well, the basket was overcrowded, or the air fryer wasn’t preheated. Moisture and crowding trap steam which prevents crisping. Follow the drying and spacing tips for best results.
Can I use a different sweetener instead of honey?
Yes, maple syrup or agave work well as substitutes. They provide similar sweetness and stickiness but may alter the flavor slightly. Avoid thick syrups that can burn easily.
How do I store leftover wings?
Avoid microwaving as it will make the skin soggy.
Can I double the recipe?
You can double, but cook wings in batches to avoid overcrowding the air fryer basket. Crowding reduces airflow and crisping. Alternatively, cook in two rounds for best texture.
What dipping sauces go well with these wings?
Ranch, blue cheese, or a simple yogurt dip complement the spicy-sweet wings nicely. They add a cooling contrast that balances the heat and sweetness.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 2 pounds chicken wings, separated into flats and drumettes, patted dry
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (adjust to taste)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by patting your chicken wings completely dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture on the skin will steam the wings instead of crisping them. Once dry, toss the wings in a large bowl with olive oil, making sure each piece is lightly coated. The oil helps the skin crisp up and promotes even browning in the air fryer. Avoid skipping the drying step, or your wings will come out soggy. Getting the wings as dry as possible is the key to that coveted crispy texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings feel dry to the touch before oiling, no visible moisture on the skin
Step 2
Next, add the garlic powder, smoked paprika, cayenne, salt, and black pepper to the bowl. Toss everything thoroughly so the wings are evenly coated with the spices. The smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky depth, while the cayenne gives it a spicy kick without overpowering. Be sure to mix well so every bite has balanced flavor. If you rush this step or don’t mix enough, you’ll end up with uneven seasoning and less flavor impact.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings are evenly coated with a reddish spice blend, no clumps or bare spots
Step 3
Preheat your air fryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 3-5 minutes. Preheating ensures the wings start cooking immediately at high heat, which helps crisp the skin. If you skip preheating, the wings will cook unevenly and may not get as crispy. A hot air fryer basket is key to that golden, crunchy finish everyone loves.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Air fryer basket is hot to the touch but not smoking, temperature reaches 400°F
Step 4
Arrange the wings in a single layer in the air fryer basket. Crowding the basket will trap steam and prevent crisping, so work in batches if needed. The hot circulating air crisps the skin on all sides, so spacing is important. If the wings overlap or pile up, they’ll steam instead of roast, leaving you with soggy skin. This step makes all the difference for texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings lie flat without touching, basket airflow isn’t blocked
Step 5
Air fry the wings at 400°F for 25-30 minutes, shaking the basket or flipping the wings every 8-10 minutes. This keeps them cooking evenly and ensures all sides get crispy. Don’t skip the shaking—otherwise, the wings on the bottom might burn while the top ones remain undercooked. The skin should turn golden brown and crackly by the end of cooking.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings are deep golden brown, skin is crispy and crackly, internal temp 165°F
Step 6
While the wings cook, mix the honey and hot sauce in a small bowl. I like to adjust the hot sauce amount depending on how spicy I want it. The honey adds a beautiful sweetness that balances the heat perfectly. This simple combo is what sets this recipe apart from your typical spicy wings.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Honey and hot sauce are fully combined into a smooth glaze
Step 7
Once the wings are done, transfer them to a serving plate and drizzle the honey-hot sauce glaze over the top. Toss gently if you like, or just let the glaze sit on top for a pretty presentation. The warm wings will absorb some of the glaze, creating a sticky, sweet-spicy coating that’s irresistible. Don’t glaze too early or cook again after glazing, or the honey may burn and turn bitter.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings glisten with a sticky honey coating without pooling or burning
Step 8
Serve the wings immediately while hot and crispy. They pair perfectly with celery sticks and a cooling dip like ranch or blue cheese if you want to round out the flavors. Waiting too long makes the skin lose its crispness, so enjoy them fresh out of the air fryer for the best experience.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings are hot, crispy, and juicy when served
Step 9
If you have leftovers, cool them completely before storing in an airtight container in the fridge. To reheat, warm them in the air fryer or oven to bring back some crispness. Avoid microwaving if possible, as that makes the skin soggy. Proper reheating keeps the wings tasty and close to freshly made.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes reheating
✓ Doneness: Wings are heated through and skin feels crisp again
