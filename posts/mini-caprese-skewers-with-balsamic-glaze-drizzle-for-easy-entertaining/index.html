Fresh and Easy Caprese Skewers with Sweet Balsamic Glaze

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These bite-sized Caprese skewers are a fresh twist on the classic salad, combining juicy cherry tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil. The balsamic glaze adds a sweet tang that makes them irresistible. Perfect for summer parties or quick snacks!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon Caprese skewers at a summer BBQ a few years back, and since then, they've become my go-to quick appetizer when friends come over. What I love most about this version is how easy it is to customize — you can switch up the mozzarella size or even add a touch of olive oil for extra richness. The balsamic glaze is my secret weapon here; it adds that perfect sweet tang that ties everything together beautifully. I've tried just tossing the ingredients in a bowl before, but threading them on skewers makes the presentation so much more fun and inviting. Plus, it's super easy to eat standing up at parties! When finished, these skewers look vibrant and colorful, with the bright reds, greens, and creamy whites popping on the plate. They taste fresh, light, and just a little bit indulgent thanks to the glaze. If you want a fuss-free snack that feels special, this is it!

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 skewers, serves 6

  • 24 cherry tomatoes, washed
  • 24 small fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • 24 fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup balsamic glaze
  • 24 small wooden skewers or toothpicks
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing the cherry tomatoes thoroughly under cool running water. This step is crucial because tomatoes can carry dirt or pesticides on their skin. After washing, gently dry them with a kitchen towel to prevent excess moisture, which can dilute the flavors and make the skewers soggy. Using ripe but firm tomatoes ensures they hold up well on the skewers without bursting. Avoid overly soft or green tomatoes as they won't have the best texture or taste.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Tomatoes are clean, dry, and firm to the touch without any bruises.
Step 2
Drain the fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini) and pat them lightly with paper towels to remove excess moisture. This helps prevent sogginess and keeps the skewers fresh. If the mozzarella balls are too large, you can cut them in half to match the size of the tomatoes for better balance. Handling the cheese gently preserves its creamy texture, so avoid squeezing or pressing too hard.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Mozzarella balls are dry on the surface but still soft and creamy inside.
Step 3
Lay out your basil leaves and pick the freshest, most vibrant ones. Tear any large leaves in half to fit nicely on the skewers. Basil is delicate, so handle it gently to avoid bruising, which can cause discoloration and bitterness. Using fresh basil rather than dried makes a noticeable difference in flavor, adding that bright, herbal note that balances the cheese and tomato.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Basil leaves look fresh, bright green, and intact without tears.
Step 4
Begin assembling the skewers by threading one cherry tomato onto each wooden skewer, followed by a basil leaf, and then a mozzarella ball. Repeat the pattern if you want longer skewers or just keep it simple with one of each. The order helps keep the flavors balanced in every bite. Be careful not to push too hard, which can bruise the ingredients or split the tomato skin.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Each skewer has a neat, balanced arrangement of tomato, basil, and mozzarella.
Step 5
Once all the skewers are assembled, arrange them on a serving platter. Lightly drizzle the balsamic glaze over the skewers just before serving. The glaze adds a glossy finish and a sweet, tangy flavor that complements the fresh ingredients perfectly. Avoid drizzling too early, as the glaze can make the skewers soggy if left sitting too long.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Skewers glisten with a thin, even coating of balsamic glaze.
Step 6
Optionally, you can sprinkle a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper over the skewers to enhance the flavors. A light drizzle of extra virgin olive oil can also add richness and a silky mouthfeel. These small touches elevate the simple ingredients and make the dish taste more sophisticated.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Seasoning is evenly distributed, enhancing aroma and flavor.
Step 7
Serve the Caprese skewers immediately for the freshest taste and best texture. If you need to prepare them ahead, keep them covered loosely in the refrigerator and add the balsamic glaze just before serving. This ensures the basil stays bright and the mozzarella remains firm.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Skewers look fresh with vibrant colors and no wilting basil.
Step 8
If you want to make these skewers more festive, consider adding extras like marinated artichoke hearts or olives to the skewers for variety. Just be sure to balance flavors so the classic Caprese taste still shines. Experimenting with different balsamic glazes can also add unique flavor notes.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Additional ingredients complement but don’t overpower the core flavors.
Step 9
For transporting to a party, place the assembled skewers in a single layer in a shallow container lined with parchment paper. Cover loosely with plastic wrap to prevent bruising. Keep chilled until ready to serve to maintain freshness and prevent the mozzarella from sweating.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Skewers remain intact, fresh, and visually appealing after transport.

Pro Tips

Use room temperature ingredients for best flavor.
Cold mozzarella and tomatoes from the fridge can mute their natural flavors. Letting them sit at room temperature for 15-20 minutes before assembling allows their aromas and tastes to shine. This simple step makes a noticeable difference in the finished dish.
Pat mozzarella dry to avoid soggy skewers.
Fresh mozzarella usually comes packed in water or brine. Excess moisture can make the skewers wet and cause the balsamic glaze to slide off. Gently drying the cheese ensures better texture and helps the glaze stick, maintaining visual appeal.
Drizzle balsamic glaze just before serving.
Balsamic glaze is sticky and can break down the basil leaves or make the mozzarella soggy if applied too early. Adding it last preserves the fresh look of the skewers and keeps the basil vibrant, ensuring the dish looks as good as it tastes.
Handle basil leaves gently to prevent bruising.
Basil bruises easily, which can cause discoloration and bitterness. Using clean hands and minimal handling keeps the leaves intact and fresh-looking, enhancing both the flavor and presentation of the skewers.
Use wooden or bamboo skewers for easy serving.
Small wooden skewers make the Caprese bite-sized and easy to eat without utensils. They also add a rustic charm and help keep the ingredients neatly stacked. Avoid metal skewers which can be slippery and less visually appealing.
Choose ripe but firm cherry tomatoes.
Overripe tomatoes can burst and release too much juice, making the skewers soggy. Firm tomatoes hold their shape and provide a satisfying burst of flavor without compromising texture.
Experiment with flavored balsamic glazes.
Adding glazes infused with fig, raspberry, or honey can provide unique flavor twists while maintaining the classic Caprese balance. This keeps the dish exciting and customizable for different occasions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using overly soft or underripe tomatoes

Why it happens People often grab tomatoes that look ripe but are either too soft or not ripe enough, thinking all cherry tomatoes are equal. This happens because tomatoes vary widely in texture and ripeness at the store.

Fix Pick tomatoes that are firm but yield slightly to gentle pressure. Avoid any with bruises or cracks. If tomatoes are underripe, let them ripen at room temperature for a day before using to improve flavor and texture.

Skipping drying the mozzarella

Why it happens Many assume fresh mozzarella is ready to use straight from the package, but the excess liquid can make the skewers soggy and dilute flavors.

Fix Drain mozzarella balls well and gently pat them dry with paper towels before assembling. This step reduces moisture and helps the glaze adhere better.

Applying balsamic glaze too early

Why it happens It's tempting to dress the dish ahead of time, but balsamic glaze can cause basil leaves to wilt and mozzarella to soften too much.

Fix Add the glaze just before serving to keep the ingredients fresh and visually appealing.

Bruising basil leaves during assembly

Why it happens Rushing or rough handling can tear or bruise basil leaves, which causes bitterness and discoloration.

Fix Handle basil gently with clean hands, tearing large leaves instead of forcing them on skewers whole.

Overcrowding skewers with too many ingredients

Why it happens Trying to cram too many items on a skewer can make it hard to eat and cause ingredients to fall apart.

Fix Stick to one tomato, one basil leaf, and one mozzarella ball per skewer for balanced flavor and easy eating.

Variations

Add Prosciutto for a Savory Twist

Wrap a small piece of thinly sliced prosciutto around the mozzarella before skewering. This adds a salty, savory depth that pairs beautifully with the fresh ingredients. The texture contrast also makes the skewers more filling and elegant.

Note: Be careful not to overpower the delicate flavors; use just a thin slice and assemble gently to keep the skewer balanced.

Use Heirloom Cherry Tomatoes for Color Variety

Swap regular cherry tomatoes for colorful heirloom varieties in yellow, orange, and green. This adds visual interest and subtle flavor differences, making the skewers even more appealing at parties.

Note: Heirloom tomatoes can be softer, so handle carefully and assemble shortly before serving.

Make Vegan Skewers with Tofu and Pesto

Replace mozzarella with firm tofu cubes marinated in basil pesto. This vegan alternative mimics the creamy texture and adds herbaceous flavor, perfect for plant-based guests.

Note: Press tofu to remove excess moisture and marinate for at least 30 minutes to infuse flavor.

Add a Drizzle of Spicy Honey for Heat

Mix honey with a pinch of chili flakes and drizzle over the skewers for a sweet-spicy kick. This unexpected twist adds complexity and pairs surprisingly well with the balsamic glaze.

Note: Use sparingly to avoid overpowering the fresh ingredients.

Serve with Pesto Dipping Sauce

Instead of drizzling balsamic glaze, serve the skewers alongside a small bowl of homemade basil pesto for dipping. This allows guests to customize each bite and adds a fresh, herby flavor boost.

Note: Keep pesto chilled and serve immediately to maintain vibrant color and taste.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Caprese skewers can safely sit at room temperature for up to 1 hour when covered loosely to prevent drying out. Avoid leaving them out longer to keep the mozzarella fresh and basil vibrant.

Refrigerator

Store assembled skewers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. Add the balsamic glaze just before serving to prevent sogginess. Flavors mellow slightly when chilled; serve at room temperature for best taste.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as fresh mozzarella and basil lose texture and flavor. If necessary, freeze components separately but expect quality loss upon thawing.

Reheating

This dish is best served cold or at room temperature. Avoid reheating as it alters texture and flavor. If desired, let refrigerated skewers sit out for 20 minutes before serving.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the skewers a few hours ahead and keep them refrigerated without the balsamic glaze. Drizzle the glaze and add fresh basil leaves just before serving to keep everything fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use dried basil instead of fresh basil?
Dried basil won't provide the same fresh aroma or texture and can taste quite different. For best results, use fresh basil leaves. If you must substitute, add dried basil sparingly and consider mixing it into the balsamic glaze or olive oil to infuse flavor.
How do I make my own balsamic glaze?
To make balsamic glaze, simmer 1 cup of balsamic vinegar with 2 tablespoons of sugar over medium heat until reduced by half and syrupy. Stir occasionally to prevent burning. Let cool before drizzling. This homemade glaze tastes fresher and less sweet than store-bought versions.
Can I prepare these skewers a day in advance?
You can assemble the skewers a day ahead but keep them refrigerated without the balsamic glaze. Add the glaze and any delicate herbs like basil just before serving to keep flavors fresh and prevent sogginess.
What can I substitute for mozzarella if I’m lactose intolerant?
Try using a dairy-free mozzarella alternative made from cashews or almonds, available at many stores. Tofu marinated in herbs and olive oil can also work as a creamy substitute, though the flavor will differ slightly.
Why are my tomatoes soggy on the skewers?
This usually happens if the tomatoes are overripe or if the mozzarella wasn't dried properly, causing excess moisture. Use firm tomatoes and pat the cheese dry before assembling. Also, add the balsamic glaze just before serving to avoid sogginess.
Can I use toothpicks instead of skewers?
Yes, toothpicks work well for bite-sized portions and are great for smaller gatherings. Just be mindful that they are shorter, so the ingredient sizes should be balanced accordingly.
How long does balsamic glaze keep once opened?
Store-bought balsamic glaze usually keeps for several months when sealed and refrigerated. Always check the label for specifics. Homemade glaze should be refrigerated and used within two weeks for best flavor.
Can I add other ingredients to the skewers?
Absolutely! Olives, marinated artichokes, or even small pieces of roasted red pepper can add variety. Just keep the flavors balanced so the classic Caprese taste isn’t overshadowed.
