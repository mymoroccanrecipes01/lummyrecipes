Creamy Cucumber Salad with Tangy Sour Cream Dressing

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 25, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy cucumber salad is a refreshing side with a tangy sour cream dressing that’s light but flavorful. Perfect for summer barbecues or a quick, cool snack on warm days. Super simple and fresh!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this creamy cucumber salad a few summers ago when I needed a quick, refreshing side for a backyard barbecue. Honestly, I wasn’t expecting much, but it quickly became a favorite in my family. The magic is in the simple sour cream dressing that’s slightly tangy and just creamy enough to coat the crisp cucumbers without weighing them down. What sets this version apart is how lightly it’s seasoned — just a touch of sugar and vinegar to brighten up the flavors, plus a sprinkle of fresh dill if you have it on hand. I love that it’s ready in minutes and doesn’t require any cooking, which means it’s perfect for those hot days when you want something cool and easy. Every time I make it, it feels like a little summer celebration on the plate. The cucumbers stay crisp, and the dressing adds that perfect balance of creaminess and tang that keeps you coming back for more. It’s also incredibly versatile — a great companion to grilled meats or just as a light snack. If you’re like me and sometimes overthink salads, this one’s a no-brainer that never disappoints.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 3 medium cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing your cucumbers thoroughly. Then, using a sharp knife or mandoline, slice them thinly and evenly. Thin slices are key here because they allow the dressing to cling nicely without making the salad soggy. If your cucumbers are particularly watery, you can sprinkle a little salt over the slices and let them sit in a colander for 10 minutes to draw out excess moisture — just be sure to pat them dry afterward. This step helps keep the salad crisp and prevents it from becoming watery when dressed.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cucumber slices should be thin, even, and slightly flexible but not mushy.
Step 2
In a mixing bowl, combine the sour cream, white vinegar, sugar, salt, and freshly ground black pepper. Whisk these together until you have a smooth, creamy dressing. The vinegar adds a subtle tang that balances the creaminess, while the sugar softens the acidity. Make sure the sugar dissolves fully so you don’t get any grainy bites, and adjust seasoning to taste. If you want an herbal note, stir in fresh chopped dill here for a bright flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing should be smooth, creamy, and well-seasoned with no graininess.
Step 3
Add the sliced cucumbers to the bowl with your dressing. Toss everything gently but thoroughly so every slice is coated evenly. Be careful not to mash the cucumbers — a light hand keeps the salad crisp and fresh. If you tossed salted cucumbers earlier, give them a quick taste before adding more salt; the dressing should have enough seasoning to bring out the best flavors without overpowering the cucumber’s natural freshness.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: All cucumber slices are evenly coated with dressing, looking glossy but not drenched.
Step 4
Cover the salad and refrigerate for at least 15 minutes before serving. This resting time lets the flavors meld together and the cucumbers soak up the dressing’s tangy creaminess. If you serve it immediately, the flavors won’t be as balanced, and the cucumbers might feel a bit plain. I find that chilling it for about 30 minutes gives the best taste and texture.
⏱ Time: 15-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad is chilled, and flavors taste blended but cucumbers remain crisp.
Step 5
Before serving, give the salad a gentle stir to redistribute any dressing that may have settled at the bottom. Taste once more and adjust seasoning with a pinch more salt or pepper if needed. Sometimes a quick squeeze of fresh lemon juice right before serving can lift the flavors even more, adding a fresh brightness that complements the creamy dressing.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad tastes balanced with fresh, tangy, and creamy notes.
Step 6
For an optional crunchy texture, sprinkle toasted sunflower seeds or sliced almonds on top just before serving. This adds a nice contrast to the creamy and crisp components. If you want an extra herbaceous kick, garnish with a bit more fresh dill or even chopped chives. These little touches can personalize the salad and make it feel more special.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad has a fresh herbal aroma and a light crunch from nuts if added.
Step 7
Serve your creamy cucumber salad chilled as a side dish with grilled chicken, fish, or your favorite summer BBQ fare. It’s also a lovely light snack on its own or alongside sandwiches. Because it’s so simple and fresh, it pairs well with a wide range of dishes without overpowering them.
⏱ Time: Immediate serving
✓ Doneness: Salad is cold, fresh, and vibrant on the plate.
Step 8
If you want to prepare this salad ahead, keep the cucumbers and dressing separate until just before serving. Cucumbers dressed too early tend to get soggy. Store each component in airtight containers in the fridge, then toss together right before mealtime for the freshest bite.
⏱ Time: Preparation tip
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers remain crisp and salad tastes freshly mixed.
Step 9
Leftovers can be stored in the fridge for up to 2 days, but the cucumbers will soften over time. To refresh, drain excess liquid and toss with a bit more sour cream or fresh herbs. This helps revive the texture and flavor for another quick serving.
⏱ Time: Storage tip
✓ Doneness: Salad retains as much crispness and flavor as possible after storage.

Pro Tips

Salt cucumbers lightly and let them drain before dressing.
Salting draws out excess moisture from cucumbers, preventing your salad from becoming watery. This step also firms up the slices, keeping the texture crisp. If you skip this, the salad may turn soggy quickly as cucumbers release water when mixed with dressing.
Use a mandoline or very sharp knife for thin, even slices.
Evenly thin slices ensure the dressing coats each piece uniformly and that every bite has consistent texture. Uneven slices can result in some pieces being soggy while others remain dry, affecting the overall balance of the salad.
Chill the salad for at least 15 minutes before serving.
This resting time allows flavors to meld and the cucumbers to absorb the tangy dressing. Serving immediately means the salad tastes less integrated and can feel bland. The chill also enhances the refreshing quality, perfect for hot weather.
Adjust seasoning after chilling, not before.
Flavors develop and intensify during chilling, so seasoning too early can lead to over-salting. Tasting the salad after it’s rested lets you add the right amount of salt or pepper for balanced flavor without overpowering the fresh cucumbers.
Add fresh herbs like dill or chives for brightness.
Herbs add an aromatic lift that complements the creamy, tangy dressing and fresh cucumbers. Without herbs, the salad can feel one-dimensional. Fresh herbs also enhance the visual appeal and make the dish feel more vibrant and summery.
Serve immediately after tossing with dressing for the best texture.
While chilling is important, tossing cucumbers too far in advance can lead to sogginess. If preparing ahead, store cucumbers and dressing separately and combine just before serving to retain crispness.
Use full-fat sour cream for richer flavor and better texture.
Full-fat sour cream emulsifies better and tastes creamier, which balances the acidity nicely. Low-fat options may make the dressing runnier and less satisfying, affecting the mouthfeel and overall enjoyment of the salad.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the salting and draining step

Why it happens Many cooks want to skip salting because it adds time or they aren’t aware it helps reduce wateriness.

Fix Take 10 minutes to salt and drain the cucumbers before mixing with dressing. This draws out excess moisture, keeping the salad crisp and preventing it from becoming watery and diluted.

Using thick or uneven cucumber slices

Why it happens Without proper tools, slices can be uneven or too thick, leading to inconsistent texture and flavor absorption.

Fix Use a mandoline or sharp knife for thin, uniform slices. This ensures even coating of dressing and a pleasant crisp bite throughout the salad.

Adding too much vinegar or skipping sugar

Why it happens Some assume more vinegar means more flavor, or forget sugar’s role in balancing acidity.

Fix Stick to the recommended vinegar amount and add sugar to soften the sharpness. Taste as you go to maintain a balanced tangy but smooth dressing.

Serving the salad immediately without chilling

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning leads to serving too soon, resulting in underdeveloped flavors.

Fix Chill the salad for at least 15 minutes so the flavors meld and cucumbers soak up the dressing. This step is key for a bright, balanced taste.

Overmixing and breaking cucumbers

Why it happens Trying too hard to coat every piece can crush slices, making the salad mushy.

Fix Toss gently with a light hand to keep cucumber slices intact and crisp while ensuring an even dressing coat.

Variations

Greek Yogurt Dressing

Swap sour cream for Greek yogurt for a lighter, tangier dressing with added protein. This version tastes a bit fresher and less rich but still creamy and satisfying. It’s great if you want a healthier twist without sacrificing flavor.

Note: Greek yogurt can be more watery, so strain it before use to avoid thinning the dressing too much.

Add Red Onion and Cherry Tomatoes

For a more colorful and flavorful salad, toss in thinly sliced red onion and halved cherry tomatoes. The onion adds a mild sharpness, while tomatoes bring juiciness and sweetness, making the salad more complex and vibrant.

Note: Add these ingredients just before serving to keep the onions from overpowering and tomatoes from getting mushy.

Spicy Kick with Chili Flakes

If you like a bit of heat, sprinkle in some red chili flakes or finely chopped fresh chili peppers. The spice contrasts nicely with the cool creaminess and adds a surprising depth of flavor that’s perfect for adventurous palates.

Note: Start with a small amount and adjust to taste, as too much can overwhelm the delicate cucumber flavor.

Vegan Version with Cashew Cream

Replace sour cream with a smooth cashew cream for a dairy-free, vegan-friendly salad. This alternative keeps the creamy texture and tangy flavor by adding lemon juice and apple cider vinegar to the cashew base, making it just as delicious.

Note: Soak cashews overnight and blend with lemon juice and vinegar for best results.

Add Fresh Mint and Lime Juice

For a refreshing twist, add chopped fresh mint leaves and swap the vinegar for lime juice. This variation gives the salad a bright, citrusy flavor perfect for summer picnics or pairing with grilled seafood.

Note: Use fresh mint sparingly at first to avoid overpowering other flavors.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cucumber salad should not be left at room temperature for more than 1-2 hours, especially in warm weather, due to the dairy content in the dressing. Always cover it with plastic wrap or store in an airtight container if left out briefly.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Note that the cucumbers will gradually soften and release water, making the salad less crisp. It’s best served chilled but fresh. Stir before serving to redistribute dressing.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this salad because cucumbers become mushy and watery when thawed, and the sour cream dressing may separate. Enjoy fresh for best texture and flavor.

Reheating

This salad is served cold and should not be reheated. If needed, let it sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving to take the chill off, but avoid warming it.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dressing up to 2 days ahead and keep refrigerated. Slice cucumbers and store separately in an airtight container. Combine just before serving to maintain crispness and freshness.

FAQ

Can I use regular mayonnaise instead of sour cream?
You can substitute mayonnaise for sour cream, but the flavor and texture will change. Mayonnaise is richer and less tangy, making the salad heavier. If you do swap, consider adding a splash of vinegar or lemon juice to introduce acidity and balance the creaminess.
How do I keep the cucumbers from getting soggy?
Salting the cucumber slices and letting them drain before adding dressing is the best way to prevent sogginess. This draws out excess water. Also, toss cucumbers with dressing just before serving rather than too far in advance to maintain crispness.
Can I make this salad vegan?
Yes! Replace sour cream with vegan alternatives like cashew cream or coconut yogurt. Cashew cream works best because it’s creamy and mild. Add a bit of lemon juice or vinegar to keep the tanginess in the dressing.
What’s the best way to slice cucumbers for this salad?
Thin, even slices are ideal. Use a mandoline slicer if you have one for consistent thickness, which helps the dressing coat evenly and keeps texture pleasant. If using a knife, try to slice as thinly and uniformly as possible.
Is fresh dill necessary?
Fresh dill adds a lovely herbal brightness but isn’t mandatory. You can omit it or substitute with other fresh herbs like parsley or chives. Dried dill can be used but add less since it’s more concentrated and less fresh tasting.
Can I add other vegetables to this salad?
Absolutely! Cherry tomatoes, thinly sliced red onion, or bell peppers can add color and flavor. Add these just before serving to keep them fresh and crunchy, avoiding sogginess.
How long can I store leftovers?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. The cucumbers will soften over time, so it’s best eaten fresh. Stir before serving to redistribute dressing and drain any excess liquid.
Can I prepare this salad ahead of time?
Yes, but keep cucumbers and dressing separate until just before serving to avoid soggy cucumbers. Prepare the dressing a day ahead and refrigerate. Slice cucumbers and store separately to maintain their crispness.
