Soft Italian Easter Cookies Bursting with Almond and Anise Flavors

Lummy Hayes Published on May 22, 2026
20 min prep 20 min cook 24 servings

These Italian Easter cookies are soft and subtly sweet with a lovely almond and anise kick. A sprinkle of colorful sugar adds a festive touch. Perfect for sharing during spring celebrations or any joyful moment!

Introduction

I first discovered these Italian Easter cookies when my grandmother handed me a plate full of them one spring morning. They immediately became a tradition in our family because of their soft texture and the unique almond and anise flavor combo. What sets this recipe apart is the gentle touch of anise extract, which adds that subtle licorice note without overpowering the sweet almond base. Plus, sprinkling colorful sanding sugar on top before baking gives them a festive look that’s perfect for spring holidays. I’ve tried a few variations over the years, but this version keeps the cookies tender and just sweet enough, making them irresistible. The trick I learned is to mix the almond flour and sugar thoroughly before adding eggs so the dough comes together smoothly. When these cookies come out of the oven, they’re soft on the inside with a slight crisp on the edges, and that little pop of color on top just makes them feel special. I love sharing them with friends and family during Easter, but honestly, they’re great any time you want a sweet, nutty treat.

Why This Works

The key to these Italian Easter cookies' tender texture lies in the almond flour, which provides moisture and a delicate crumb unlike traditional wheat flour. Almond flour contains natural oils that keep the cookies soft and moist even after baking, which is why this recipe skips regular flour altogether.

Anise extract adds a subtle licorice flavor that complements the nuttiness of the almonds without overpowering the palate. The use of extract rather than seeds ensures an even distribution of flavor throughout the dough. This ingredient is essential for the traditional taste that makes these cookies special.

Baking powder is included to give a slight lift, but because the dough is dense, it only puffs the cookies gently, maintaining their soft structure. Overusing leavening agents here would cause the cookies to spread too thin and become crispy, which isn’t the goal.

The colored sanding sugar on top not only adds a festive visual appeal but also introduces a textural contrast—a tiny crunch that enhances the soft cookie base. The sugar melts slightly during baking, creating a delicate crust that’s pleasing to bite into.

  • Look for finely ground almond flour without skins for a smooth texture and sweet flavor. Blanched almond flour works best because it blends seamlessly into the dough, providing moisture and tenderness. Avoid almond meal with skins as it can make cookies gritty and darker in color.
  • Choose pure anise extract rather than artificial flavoring for an authentic licorice note. It’s potent, so a little goes a long way. Anise extract infuses the dough evenly and adds a distinctive aroma and flavor that’s signature to Italian Easter cookies.
  • Standard granulated sugar works well, but make sure to mix it thoroughly with the almond flour to avoid grainy spots. The sugar sweetens the dough evenly and contributes to the slight crispness on the edges during baking.
  • Use fresh, double-acting baking powder to ensure a gentle rise. It creates the perfect balance by lifting the cookies just enough to keep them light without making them cakey or crunchy. Old baking powder can lead to flat, dense cookies.
  • This coarse sugar adds festive color and crunch. Use it sparingly on top before baking to avoid overwhelming sweetness. It melts slightly during baking, creating a pretty, sparkling crust that’s a hallmark of these cookies.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups almond flour, finely ground
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon anise extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Colored sanding sugar for topping

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line two baking sheets with parchment paper. This ensures the cookies bake evenly and don't stick. Preparing the oven and pans ahead is important so you can move quickly once the dough is ready, preventing the eggs from warming too much and changing the dough texture.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven fully preheated and parchment paper smooth on baking sheets
  2. Step 2

    In a large bowl, whisk together almond flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt until well combined. This dry mix ensures even distribution of leavening agents and sweetness, which helps the cookies rise evenly and taste balanced. Make sure there are no lumps for a smooth dough.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients fully blended with no clumps
  3. Step 3

    Add eggs and anise extract to the dry ingredients. Stir gently until a sticky dough forms. Avoid overmixing here; just combine until no dry flour remains. Overmixing can make the cookies dense and tough, so a gentle fold is key to keep them tender.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Sticky dough that holds together but is not overly wet
  4. Step 4

    Using a tablespoon or cookie scoop, drop dough balls onto the prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart. This spacing allows the cookies to spread slightly without merging. If the dough is too sticky, lightly wet your hands to scoop and shape the balls for easier handling.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Uniform dough balls spaced evenly on sheets
  5. Step 5

    Sprinkle each dough ball generously with colored sanding sugar. This adds a festive look and a slight crunch on top. Don’t press the sugar into the dough; letting it sit on top ensures the sugar stays visible after baking.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Each cookie ball topped with a colorful, even sugar coating
  6. Step 6

    Bake the cookies for 18-20 minutes, rotating the pans halfway through to promote even browning. The cookies should puff slightly and develop a light golden edge. Avoid overbaking as they can dry out; the centers should remain soft and tender.

    ⏱ 20 minutes✓ Light golden edges and no wet dough on top
  7. Step 7

    Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheets for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Cooling on the sheet allows them to firm up slightly, making them easier to move without breaking. Transferring too soon can cause them to fall apart.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Cookies slightly firm but still soft in the center
  8. Step 8

    Once completely cooled, store the cookies in an airtight container to keep them soft. If you want them a bit crisper, leave the container open for a few hours. These cookies actually improve in flavor if left overnight as the anise intensifies.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Cookies fully cooled and stored properly
  9. Step 9

    Enjoy these cookies with a cup of tea or coffee, ideally within 3 days for best freshness. They’re perfect for sharing at holiday gatherings or as a sweet treat anytime. If you want to refresh them after storage, briefly warm in a low oven for a few minutes to soften.

    ⏱ Varies✓ Cookies warm, soft, and ready to enjoy

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature eggs for easier mixing and better dough consistency. — Room temperature eggs emulsify better with dry ingredients, resulting in a smoother, more uniform dough. Cold eggs can cause the dough to clump and resist combining, leading to uneven texture in the cookies.
  • Don’t overmix the dough once eggs are added to keep cookies tender. — Overmixing develops gluten and can make cookies tough. Since almond flour lacks gluten, the risk is less, but stirring too much still compresses the dough, resulting in denser cookies rather than soft, melt-in-your-mouth ones.
  • Chill the dough briefly if it feels too sticky to handle. — Chilling firms up the dough, making it easier to scoop and shape. This prevents the cookies from spreading too much during baking and helps maintain their soft, rounded shape with a nice sugary crust.
  • Rotate baking sheets halfway through baking for even color and texture. — Ovens often have hot spots. Rotating the trays ensures cookies bake evenly, avoiding burnt edges or undercooked centers. This simple step guarantees consistent texture and appearance across the batch.
  • Use parchment paper or silicone mats to prevent sticking and promote even baking. — These liners create a non-stick surface that protects the delicate cookies and allows heat to circulate evenly underneath. It also makes cleanup easier and preserves the cookies’ shape and texture.
  • Let cookies cool on the baking sheet before transferring to racks. — Cooling on the sheet helps cookies set and prevents them from breaking apart. Moving them too soon can cause the soft cookies to crumble, so patience here preserves their perfect texture.
  • Store cookies in an airtight container to maintain softness. — Because almond flour cookies tend to dry out quickly, airtight storage locks in moisture. Leaving them exposed to air causes them to harden and lose their tender crumb.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using almond meal instead of almond flour
    Why: Some might assume any ground almonds will work, but almond meal often includes skins and is coarser.
    Fix: Use blanched almond flour for a finer texture and sweeter flavor. This prevents gritty cookies and ensures a tender crumb.
  • Overbaking the cookies until they’re hard
    Why: It’s tempting to bake until golden brown, but these cookies are meant to be soft.
    Fix: Remove them as soon as edges turn lightly golden and centers look set but still soft. They’ll firm up more as they cool.
  • Skipping the anise extract or substituting with a different flavor
    Why: Not everyone has anise extract on hand or understands its role.
    Fix: Use anise extract for authentic flavor; if unavailable, try a small amount of fennel seed powder but expect a different taste.
  • Not spacing cookies enough on the baking sheet
    Why: Crowding leads to merged cookies.
    Fix: Leave about 2 inches between dough balls to allow gentle spreading without sticking.
  • Using regular sugar instead of colored sanding sugar on top
    Why: Colored sanding sugar may not be in every pantry.
    Fix: You can use plain sugar, but colored sanding sugar adds festive texture and appearance that’s worth the extra step.

Variations

  • Lemon Zest Almond Cookies: Add 1 tablespoon of finely grated lemon zest to the dough for a bright citrus twist. The lemon complements the almond base beautifully, creating a refreshing spring flavor. These cookies are perfect if you want a lighter, zestier version.
    Incorporate zest with dry ingredients and watch for slightly faster browning due to citrus oils.
  • Chocolate-Dipped Almond Easter Cookies: After baking and cooling, dip half of each cookie in melted dark chocolate and let set. This adds a rich, decadent layer that balances the almond and anise flavors. Great for gifting or special occasions.
    Allow cookies to cool completely before dipping to prevent melting or sogginess.
  • Gluten-Free Version with Coconut Flour: Replace 1/4 cup of almond flour with coconut flour to add a subtle coconut flavor and adjust texture. Coconut flour absorbs more moisture, so add an extra egg white to keep dough moist and soft.
    Monitor dough consistency closely; coconut flour thickens dough more than almond flour.
  • Spiced Italian Easter Cookies: Add 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon and 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves to the dry ingredients for a cozy, spiced version. These warm spices deepen the flavor and complement the anise beautifully, perfect for cooler spring evenings.
    Spices may slightly darken dough and cookies; bake carefully to avoid overbaking.
  • Vegan Almond Easter Cookies: Use flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and substitute granulated sugar with coconut sugar. These swaps create a vegan-friendly cookie with a slightly nutty, caramel flavor that still holds the soft texture.
    Flax eggs may make dough denser; chill dough before shaping for better handling.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These cookies can be safely kept at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat sources to avoid drying out or melting the sugar topping.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container for up to 7 days. Refrigeration may firm up the cookies, so bring them to room temperature or warm slightly before serving to restore softness.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature to prevent condensation and sogginess.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm cookies in a 300°F (150°C) oven for 5-7 minutes to refresh softness and enhance aroma. Avoid microwaving as it can make the cookies rubbery or dry.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the dough up to 24 hours in advance, wrapped tightly and refrigerated. Shape and bake the day of serving for the freshest texture and best flavor.

FAQ

Can I substitute almond flour with regular flour?
Substituting with regular flour will change the texture and flavor significantly. Almond flour provides moisture and a soft crumb, while wheat flour creates a firmer cookie. If you must substitute, use a blend of all-purpose flour and a bit of extra fat, but expect a different result.
What if I don’t have anise extract?
Anise extract gives these cookies their signature flavor, but if you don’t have it, you can try fennel seed powder or a small amount of star anise steeped in warm water. The flavor will be less intense but still pleasant.
Why are my cookies too dry or crumbly?
Dry or crumbly cookies can happen if the dough is overbaked or if the almond flour is old and dried out. Also, overmixing can cause the dough to lose moisture. Try reducing baking time slightly and ensure fresh almond flour is used.
Can I make these cookies gluten-free?
Yes! This recipe is naturally gluten-free since it uses almond flour. Just make sure your baking powder is gluten-free and avoid cross-contamination.
How should I store leftover cookies?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or refrigerate for up to a week. To keep them soft, add a slice of bread in the container to maintain moisture.
Can I freeze these cookies?
Absolutely! Freeze baked cookies in a single layer first, then transfer to a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving to keep texture intact.
Why do my cookies spread too much?
If cookies spread too much, the dough might be too warm or too wet. Try chilling the dough before baking and be sure not to add extra liquid. Also, check your baking powder freshness.
What’s the best way to get colorful sugar on top?
Use coarse colored sanding sugar and sprinkle it generously just before baking. Don’t press it into the dough so it stays visible and sparkly after baking.
