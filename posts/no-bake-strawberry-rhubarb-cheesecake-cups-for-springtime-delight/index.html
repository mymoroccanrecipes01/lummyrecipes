No-Bake Rhubarb Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Fresh & Easy Spring Dessert

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

These no-bake cheesecake cups mix tangy rhubarb and sweet strawberries for a light, fresh dessert. The creamy filling sets without oven time, making it a breeze to prepare for spring gatherings or a quick treat after dinner.

Introduction

I stumbled upon this no-bake cheesecake cups recipe last spring when I wanted a fresh dessert that didn’t tie me to the oven. It’s perfect for those days when you crave something creamy and fruity but don’t want to fuss with baking or long prep times. What makes this version special is the rhubarb-strawberry combo—it’s tangy and sweet, brightening up the classic cheesecake flavor without being too heavy. I love how the creamy filling sets just right in the fridge, giving it a smooth texture that melts in your mouth. Plus, using graham cracker crumbs with melted butter for the base adds that perfect buttery crunch without any baking. One trick I learned is to cook the rhubarb and strawberries gently with just a bit of sugar to soften them and bring out their natural sweetness without turning them mushy. The result is a beautiful layered dessert that’s as pretty to look at as it is delicious to eat. It’s become my go-to for spring get-togethers or when I want a quick, light dessert after dinner. Honestly, it’s so easy and fresh that my family keeps asking for it, and I think you’ll love it too!

Why This Works

This no-bake cheesecake recipe succeeds because it balances texture and flavor perfectly without the need for baking. The graham cracker crust sets firmly thanks to the melted butter binding the crumbs, which creates a buttery, crunchy base that contrasts beautifully with the creamy filling. The key is pressing the crumbs tightly so the crust holds when scooped.

The cream cheese base is lightened by folding in whipped cream, which introduces air and prevents the filling from becoming too dense or heavy. Whipping the cream to stiff peaks before folding ensures the filling will set with a fluffy, mousse-like texture that still holds its shape when chilled.

Cooking the rhubarb and strawberries gently softens the fruit and dissolves some of the sugar, releasing juices that create a natural compote. This adds a bright tartness and sweetness that cuts through the richness of the cream cheese. Low heat prevents the fruit from breaking down too much, preserving some texture for contrast.

Finally, chilling the assembled cups allows the filling to firm up as the fats in the cream cheese and butter solidify. This step is crucial because it sets the structure without baking, resulting in a smooth, creamy dessert with distinct layers of flavor and texture.

  • Look for firm, bright red stalks without blemishes or softness. Rhubarb provides a unique tartness that balances the sweet creaminess of the filling. Since rhubarb is quite fibrous, cooking it gently helps soften the texture without losing its fresh flavor. If unavailable, you can substitute with tart green apples or cranberries, but the flavor profile will change.
  • Choose ripe, fragrant strawberries with a deep red color and no mushy spots. Strawberries add natural sweetness and juiciness that complements the tangy rhubarb. Fresh berries work best here; frozen can be used but may release extra water, which affects texture.
  • Use full-fat cream cheese softened to room temperature for the creamiest texture and best flavor. Low-fat or whipped varieties won’t set as well and can make the filling watery or less stable. Properly softened cream cheese blends smoothly with powdered sugar and vanilla to create a luscious base.
  • Chilled heavy cream whips up to stiff peaks, adding lightness and air to the dense cream cheese mixture. Avoid overwhipping as it can turn grainy or start to separate. Using cream with at least 36% fat content ensures stable peaks and a fluffy filling.
  • Finely crushed graham crackers create the classic cheesecake crust texture. Mixing with melted butter binds the crumbs together, forming a sturdy base that doesn’t crumble. Using finely ground crumbs helps the crust pack tightly for better structure.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 3/4 cup fresh strawberries, chopped
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar, divided
  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, chilled

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by preparing the fruit compote. In a small saucepan, combine the chopped rhubarb, strawberries, and half of the granulated sugar. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, until the fruit softens and releases juices, about 8-10 minutes. This slow cooking softens the rhubarb’s tartness and sweetens the strawberries without breaking them down completely, giving a nice texture contrast in the cups. Avoid high heat to prevent the fruit from turning mushy or caramelizing too much.

    ⏱ 10✓ Fruit is softened but still holds some shape; juices are slightly thickened
  2. Step 2

    While the fruit cooks, prepare the crust by mixing graham cracker crumbs with melted butter and a tablespoon of sugar. Stir until the crumbs are evenly coated and feel like damp sand. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs, creating a firm base that won’t crumble when you serve. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of your serving cups or jars using the back of a spoon. Don’t skip pressing it down well, or the crust will fall apart when eating.

    ⏱ 5✓ Crust is compact and holds shape when pressed
  3. Step 3

    In a mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with the powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. This ensures no lumps remain and the filling is silky. Take your time here; rushing can leave lumps that affect the texture.

    ⏱ 5✓ Filling is smooth, creamy, and homogenous
  4. Step 4

    In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Stiff peaks mean the cream holds its shape firmly when you lift the whisk. This whipped cream lightens the cream cheese mixture, giving the filling a fluffy, airy texture that's not too dense.

    ⏱ 5✓ Whipped cream forms stiff peaks that hold shape
  5. Step 5

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Folding preserves the airiness you just whipped into the cream. Mix just until combined—overmixing will deflate the cream and make the filling dense.

    ⏱ 3✓ Filling is light and airy without visible streaks of cream
  6. Step 6

    Layer the filling over the pressed crust in each cup, filling about halfway. Then spoon a layer of the cooled rhubarb-strawberry compote on top. Layering keeps the flavors distinct and lets each bite have a balance of creamy and fruity.

    ⏱ 5✓ Cups have distinct layers of crust, filling, and fruit compote
  7. Step 7

    Add another layer of the cream cheese filling over the fruit compote until cups are nearly full. Smooth the top with a spatula or back of a spoon for a clean finish. This double layer of filling keeps the dessert creamy throughout.

    ⏱ 3✓ Top layer is smooth and even
  8. Step 8

    Cover each cup with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. The chilling time allows the filling to firm up nicely without baking. Don’t rush this step or the filling will be too soft to hold its shape when served.

    ⏱ 240✓ Filling is set and firm to the touch but still creamy
  9. Step 9

    Before serving, optionally garnish with a few fresh strawberry slices or a small sprig of mint for a fresh pop of color and flavor. This final touch makes the dessert look inviting and adds a fresh aroma.

    ⏱ 2✓ Garnish looks fresh and vibrant

Pro Tips

  • Press the graham cracker crust firmly into the cups. — Firmly pressing the crust ensures it holds together when you scoop into the cheesecake. If it’s too loose, the crust will fall apart, making the dessert messy to eat. The butter coats the crumbs, but compacting them tightly is what provides structural integrity.
  • Use room temperature cream cheese for a smooth filling. — Cold cream cheese is stiff and can cause lumps when mixing. Letting it soften makes beating easier and results in a smoother, creamier filling. This step is crucial for achieving that silky texture you want in a no-bake cheesecake.
  • Whip the heavy cream to stiff peaks before folding it in. — Stiff peaks mean the cream is aerated enough to lighten the dense cream cheese mixture. Folding rather than mixing preserves this air, giving the filling a fluffy, mousse-like texture instead of a heavy cream cheese block.
  • Cook the rhubarb and strawberries on low heat slowly. — Slow cooking softens the rhubarb’s fibrous stalks and sweetens the fruit gently without turning it mushy. High heat can break down the fruit too much, losing texture and making the compote watery instead of pleasantly chunky.
  • Chill the assembled cups for at least 4 hours or overnight. — Chilling lets the fats in the cream cheese and butter solidify, setting the filling and crust firmly. Without enough chill time, the dessert will be too soft and won’t hold its shape when served.
  • Use powdered sugar instead of granulated in the filling. — Powdered sugar dissolves completely in the cream cheese, preventing any gritty texture. Granulated sugar can leave a grainy mouthfeel, which is unpleasant in creamy desserts.
  • Fold gently to combine whipped cream with cream cheese. — Folding preserves the air bubbles whipped into the cream. Stirring or beating aggressively will deflate the mixture, resulting in a denser, less airy filling.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not softening the cream cheese enough
    Why: Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but its stiffness causes lumps and uneven texture.
    Fix: Let the cream cheese sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing. If short on time, cut into small cubes to soften faster and beat thoroughly to remove lumps.
  • Overcooking the fruit compote
    Why: Rushing the cooking process by using high heat breaks down fruit too much, making it watery and mushy.
    Fix: Cook the rhubarb and strawberries gently on low heat, stirring occasionally. Stop cooking as soon as the fruit softens but still holds shape to maintain texture.
  • Not pressing the crust firmly
    Why: Some skip firmly pressing the crumbs, thinking it's not necessary, but this leads to a loose base that crumbles when served.
    Fix: Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to press the crust tightly into the bottom of the cups. A compact crust holds together better, especially without baking.
  • Overwhipping the cream
    Why: Trying to get fluffy cream but going too far causes it to turn grainy or start to separate into butter and liquid.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. The cream should hold its shape but still look smooth and glossy.
  • Skipping the chilling time
    Why: Impatience leads to serving the dessert too soon, resulting in a runny filling and crumbly crust.
    Fix: Plan ahead and chill the cups for at least 4 hours or overnight. This step is essential for the filling to set and the crust to firm up.

Variations

  • Lemon Rhubarb No-Bake Cheesecake Cups: Add a tablespoon of fresh lemon juice and a teaspoon of lemon zest to the cream cheese filling for a bright citrus twist. The lemon complements the rhubarb’s tartness beautifully and adds a refreshing zing.
    Incorporate lemon juice and zest after beating the cream cheese for even flavor distribution. Be careful not to add too much liquid to keep the filling stable.
  • Ginger-Spiced Rhubarb and Strawberry Cups: Stir a teaspoon of ground ginger or freshly grated ginger into the fruit compote. This adds a warm spice note that balances the sweetness and tartness, giving the dessert a cozy depth perfect for cooler spring evenings.
    Add ginger early in the fruit cooking stage to let the flavors meld well without overpowering the delicate fruit.
  • Vegan No-Bake Rhubarb Strawberry Cups: Replace cream cheese with vegan cream cheese and use coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks instead of heavy cream. Use vegan butter for the crust. The texture will be slightly different but still creamy and delicious.
    Chill the coconut cream overnight before whipping to ensure it whips properly. Vegan crust may be softer, so press extra firmly.
  • Nutty Graham Cracker Crust: Mix finely chopped toasted almonds or pecans into the graham cracker crust for added crunch and nutty flavor. This variation adds texture and richness to the base layer.
    Toast nuts lightly before chopping to enhance flavor. Adjust butter quantity slightly if the mixture feels too dry.
  • Layered Berry Medley Cheesecake Cups: Add layers of blueberries or raspberries along with the rhubarb and strawberries for a colorful, mixed berry dessert. This also adds varied sweetness and texture with each bite.
    Cook all berries together gently or layer fresh berries between filling layers for a fresher bite.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These cheesecake cups should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours as the dairy filling can spoil. Keep them covered with plastic wrap or lids if sitting out briefly during serving.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store the cheesecake cups in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The filling will firm up more over time, and the crust may soften slightly but remains tasty. Serve chilled for best texture and flavor.
  • 🧊 Freezer: You can freeze the assembled cups for up to 1 month. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving. The texture may be slightly less creamy after freezing but still delicious.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating is needed or recommended for this dessert. Serve chilled straight from the fridge. If you want to soften slightly, let sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before eating.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the crust and fruit compote up to 2 days in advance and store separately in the fridge. Assemble the filling and layer everything the day of serving for the freshest texture. Alternatively, fully assemble and chill overnight.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries and rhubarb for this recipe?
Yes, you can use frozen fruit if fresh isn’t available. Just thaw and drain excess liquid before cooking the compote to avoid a watery filling. Frozen fruit may release more water, so cooking gently and reducing the compote slightly helps keep the texture right.
What can I substitute for cream cheese if I’m allergic or vegan?
For a vegan or dairy-free version, use vegan cream cheese alternatives made from nuts or soy, and replace heavy cream with coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks. These substitutions work well but may slightly alter the texture and flavor.
Why isn’t my filling setting properly?
Common reasons include not whipping the cream enough, overfolding and deflating the mixture, or insufficient chilling time. Make sure the cream is whipped to stiff peaks and folded gently into softened cream cheese. Chill for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the filling to firm up.
Can I make this dessert gluten-free?
Absolutely! Substitute graham cracker crumbs with gluten-free graham crackers or crushed gluten-free cookies. The rest of the recipe is naturally gluten-free, so it’s easy to adapt for dietary needs.
How long can I store leftover cheesecake cups?
Store leftovers in the refrigerator in airtight containers for up to 3 days. The texture remains best when served chilled but within this timeframe. Avoid keeping them out at room temperature for extended periods to maintain freshness.
Is it possible to make this recipe in a large dish instead of cups?
Yes, you can prepare it in a small trifle dish or baking pan. Layer the crust, filling, and fruit compote as directed, then chill to set. Serving will be a bit different, requiring scooping instead of individual portions, but the flavor and texture stay the same.
What’s the best way to crush graham crackers for the crust?
Place graham crackers in a sealed plastic bag and crush them with a rolling pin until fine crumbs form. Alternatively, pulse in a food processor for even crumbs. Avoid large chunks to ensure a compact crust that holds well.
Can I add gelatin to help the filling set?
This recipe doesn’t require gelatin because the whipped cream and cream cheese set firmly when chilled. Adding gelatin is optional but not necessary. If used, it can make the filling firmer but may alter the creamy texture.
