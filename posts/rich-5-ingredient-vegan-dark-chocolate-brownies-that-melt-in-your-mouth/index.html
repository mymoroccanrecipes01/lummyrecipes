Insanely Fudgy Vegan Brownies with Just 5 Simple Ingredients

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 13, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These vegan brownies are unbelievably fudgy and rich, made with just five easy ingredients. Dark cocoa powder and almond milk give them that melt-in-your-mouth softness. Perfect for a cozy afternoon snack or sharing with friends!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this vegan brownie recipe one rainy afternoon when I was craving something chocolatey but didn’t want to deal with eggs or dairy. After a few trials, I found this simple 5-ingredient version that’s insanely fudgy and rich — and it’s become my go-to treat! What I love about these brownies is how the dark cocoa powder really deepens the chocolate flavor, while the splash of almond milk keeps them moist without weighing them down. Plus, the texture melts in your mouth, which is pretty rare for vegan brownies I've tried before. I remember once I rushed the mixing and ended up with a crumbly batch, so now I make sure to gently fold the wet and dry ingredients just until combined. The result? A perfectly soft, chewy brownie that’s great for an afternoon pick-me-up or sharing with friends when you want to impress without the fuss. Honestly, these brownies feel indulgent but are so simple to whip up that I always keep this recipe handy.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 brownies, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 3/4 cup dark cocoa powder, unsweetened
  • 1 cup sugar, organic or cane
  • 1/2 cup almond milk, unsweetened
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil (neutral flavor)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease or line an 8-inch square baking pan with parchment paper. This step ensures your brownies don’t stick and come out cleanly. Using parchment also helps you lift the whole batch out easily once baked. Avoid skipping this or using too little oil, which can cause sticking and breakage when cutting.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is fully greased or parchment paper is neatly fitted without wrinkles
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour and dark cocoa powder. Sifting removes lumps and aerates the dry ingredients, which helps the brownies bake evenly and develop a tender crumb. Be sure to sift thoroughly; clumps of cocoa powder can cause uneven flavor and texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry mixture is smooth, lump-free, and uniform in color
Step 3
Add the sugar to the sifted dry ingredients and whisk briefly to combine. This distributes the sugar evenly throughout the flour and cocoa mixture, which is important for consistent sweetness and texture throughout the brownies.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly mixed with no visible clumps of sugar
Step 4
Pour in the almond milk and vegetable oil into the dry mixture. Use a spatula to gently fold the wet ingredients into the dry, mixing just until combined. Over-mixing can develop gluten and make the brownies tough rather than soft and fudgy, so be gentle and stop as soon as the batter looks smooth and uniform.
⏱ Time: 3-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no visible streaks of flour or cocoa
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly using a spatula. Smooth the top so the brownies bake uniformly. Uneven batter can cause some brownies to be overcooked while others remain underdone.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter surface is flat and even across the pan
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Check for doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. This ensures the brownies are set but still fudgy inside. Baking too long will dry them out, so watch carefully starting at 25 minutes.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out with moist crumbs, edges pull away slightly from pan
Step 7
Remove the brownies from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling allows the brownies to set fully and makes cutting easier without crumbling. Cutting too soon while warm can cause the brownies to fall apart.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownies feel firm to the touch and have cooled to room temperature
Step 8
Once cooled, lift the brownies out of the pan using the parchment paper edges. Transfer to a cutting board and slice into 8 equal squares with a sharp knife. Wiping the knife between cuts helps keep edges clean and prevent crumbling.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean, sharp edges on brownies without excessive crumbs
Step 9
Serve immediately or store in an airtight container. These brownies are best enjoyed within a few days to maintain their fudgy texture. You can also warm them slightly before serving for a gooey treat. Enjoy with a glass of almond milk or your favorite vegan ice cream!
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownies are moist, soft, and delicious when eaten

Pro Tips

Sift dry ingredients thoroughly before mixing.
Sifting removes lumps from cocoa powder and flour, ensuring even distribution and a smooth batter. This aerates the mixture, helping the brownies bake uniformly and preventing dense or uneven texture.
Fold wet and dry ingredients gently until just combined.
Over-mixing develops gluten in the flour, leading to tough brownies. Gentle folding keeps the batter tender, resulting in a soft, fudgy texture that melts in your mouth rather than a cakey crumb.
Use a sharp knife wiped clean between cuts for neat slices.
Brownies can crumble easily if cut with a dull or sticky knife. Cleaning the blade between cuts helps maintain clean edges and prevents dragging crumbs, making your presentation much nicer.
Let brownies cool completely before slicing.
Cooling allows the brownies to set and firm up. Cutting while warm causes crumbling and messy slices. Patience here results in perfect squares with a satisfying bite.
Use unsweetened almond milk to control moisture and flavor.
Sweetened or flavored milks can add unexpected sweetness or alter texture. Unsweetened almond milk keeps the balance right for fudgy brownies with a clean chocolate taste.
Don’t overbake; check with a toothpick at 25 minutes.
Baking too long dries out brownies, losing the fudgy texture. Pulling them out when a toothpick shows moist crumbs ensures the perfect gooey center.
Line your pan with parchment paper for easy removal.
Parchment paper prevents sticking and lets you lift the entire batch out for cutting. This avoids breaking or crumbling that happens when trying to scoop brownies directly from the pan.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Many cooks think more mixing will make the batter smoother, but overmixing activates gluten in the flour, making brownies tough instead of soft.

Fix Mix the wet and dry ingredients just until there are no flour streaks visible. Stop immediately to preserve the tender, fudgy texture.

Using sweetened almond milk or flavored varieties

Why it happens Sometimes people use what’s on hand without checking, but sweetened or flavored almond milk can throw off the sweetness and moisture balance.

Fix Always use unsweetened almond milk to keep flavors balanced and texture right.

Skipping parchment paper or greasing the pan inadequately

Why it happens Some cooks underestimate how sticky the batter can be, leading to brownies sticking and breaking when removing.

Fix Line the pan with parchment paper and lightly grease it to ensure easy removal and clean cuts.

Baking too long

Why it happens Waiting for the toothpick to come out completely clean can lead to overbaking and dry brownies.

Fix Check at 25 minutes and remove when toothpick shows moist crumbs to keep brownies fudgy and moist.

Cutting brownies while still warm

Why it happens Impatience causes brownies to crumble because they haven’t set properly.

Fix Wait at least 30 minutes after baking for the brownies to cool completely before slicing.

Variations

Nutty Vegan Brownies

Add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter for a crunchy texture and nutty flavor. This addition complements the rich cocoa and adds a bit of complexity to each bite.

Note: Fold nuts gently into the batter right before pouring into the pan to avoid overmixing.

Spiced Vegan Brownies

Mix in 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper for a warm, spicy twist. These spices enhance the chocolate flavor and add a subtle kick that’s surprisingly addictive.

Note: Add spices to the dry ingredients during sifting to distribute evenly.

Vegan Brownies with Chocolate Chips

Fold 1/3 cup vegan dark chocolate chips into the batter before baking for pockets of gooey melted chocolate. This makes the brownies extra indulgent and perfect for chocoholics.

Note: Gently incorporate chips last to prevent them from melting too early in the batter.

Gluten-Free Vegan Brownies

Substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend that includes xanthan gum. Texture may be slightly more crumbly but still fudgy and delicious for gluten-sensitive eaters.

Note: Adjust baking time slightly if needed, as gluten-free flours can bake differently.

Mocha Vegan Brownies

Add 1 tablespoon instant coffee granules dissolved in the almond milk to infuse a subtle coffee flavor that enhances the chocolate notes beautifully.

Note: Dissolve coffee fully in almond milk to avoid grainy texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat sources to maintain moisture and prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

You can refrigerate brownies in an airtight container for up to 5 days. They may firm up and taste less fudgy when cold, so warming slightly before serving helps restore softness.

Freezer

Freeze brownies wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave at room temperature for a few hours or warm gently in the oven to regain softness. Proper wrapping prevents freezer burn and texture loss.

Reheating

Warm brownies in the oven at 300°F (150°C) for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 15-20 seconds. Adding a small splash of almond milk before warming can help restore moisture if they’ve dried out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the batter 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the refrigerator. Bake fresh on the day of serving for best texture and flavor. Alternatively, bake brownies in advance and freeze for later enjoyment.

FAQ

Can I substitute almond milk with another plant milk?
Absolutely! You can use soy, oat, or coconut milk instead. Just make sure it’s unsweetened to avoid altering sweetness levels. Different plant milks vary in fat content and flavor, which may slightly affect texture and taste, but the recipe will still work well.
Why are my brownies cakey instead of fudgy?
This usually happens if you overmix the batter or bake too long. Overmixing develops gluten, making the texture more cake-like. Also, baking past the moist crumb stage dries the brownies out. Gently fold ingredients and remove from oven when a toothpick shows moist crumbs.
Can I use natural cocoa powder instead of Dutch-processed?
You can, but natural cocoa is more acidic and can affect the flavor and texture. Since this recipe doesn’t use baking soda or powder, Dutch-processed cocoa is preferred for its mellow taste and color. Using natural cocoa might make brownies slightly more bitter and lighter in color.
How do I know when the brownies are done without overbaking?
Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center. Pull it out—if it has moist crumbs but no raw batter, the brownies are done. This ensures a fudgy center rather than dry or cakey brownies.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum for structure. Baking time might vary slightly, and texture may be a bit more crumbly but still delicious and fudgy.
Why are my brownies dry after baking?
Dry brownies often result from overbaking or using too much flour. Make sure to measure ingredients accurately and check doneness early. Also, avoid overmixing, which can lead to tough texture.
Can I add nuts or chocolate chips?
Definitely! Folding in nuts like walnuts or pecans adds crunch, while vegan chocolate chips create gooey pockets of chocolate. Add these at the end of mixing to avoid overworking the batter.
How should I store leftover brownies?
Keep them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. To restore softness, warm slightly before serving. For longer storage, freeze wrapped tightly for up to 3 months.
