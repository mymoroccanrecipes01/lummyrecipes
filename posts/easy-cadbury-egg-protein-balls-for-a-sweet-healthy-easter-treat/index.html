Homemade Cadbury Egg Inspired Protein Balls Recipe

Lummy Hayes
Published on March 26, 2026
15 min prep
0 min cook
15 min total
12 servings
Easy

This recipe recreates the familiar flavors of Cadbury eggs in a wholesome protein ball form, combining creamy, sweet, and slightly tangy notes with a dense, chewy texture. Ready in under 30 minutes, these protein balls are ideal for Easter snacking or as a convenient post-workout treat. The balance of natural sweeteners and protein powder makes them satisfying without being overly sweet.

Pro Tips

Use natural peanut butter without added oils or sugars.
Added oils can make the mixture too greasy and difficult to bind, while sugars can alter sweetness and texture unexpectedly.
Chill the dough before shaping to reduce stickiness.
Cold dough is easier to handle and less likely to stick to your hands, making the shaping process smoother.
Use powdered sugar in the filling rather than granulated sugar.
Powdered sugar dissolves easily, creating a smooth creamy texture without gritty bits.
If coating with chocolate, temper it slightly or use high-quality melting chocolate.
Properly tempered chocolate sets with a glossy finish and pleasant snap, enhancing texture and appearance.
Adjust the amount of protein powder to achieve desired firmness.
More protein powder thickens the mixture; less yields a softer ball. Balance to maintain chewiness without crumbling.
Add a pinch of salt to the base mixture.
Salt balances sweetness and enhances the overall flavor complexity.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using wet or oily peanut butter.

Why it happens Some peanut butters have added oils that separate and make the mixture too loose.

Fix Stir the peanut butter well before measuring or use brands labeled 'natural' and 'no added oils.' Refrigerate if necessary.

Skipping the chilling step before shaping.

Why it happens Warm dough sticks to hands and is hard to form into balls.

Fix Refrigerate the dough for at least 10 minutes to firm it up for easier handling.

Filling leaks out during shaping.

Why it happens Dough may be too soft or filling too loose, causing balls to break or leak.

Fix Ensure dough is firm and handle gently when enclosing filling; chill filling slightly if too soft.

Chocolate coating melts or doesn't harden properly.

Why it happens Chocolate overheated or not tempered correctly can remain sticky or dull.

Fix Melt chocolate slowly in short bursts; allow to cool slightly before dipping; refrigerate to set quickly.

Variations

Vegan Version

Replace cream cheese with coconut cream or a plant-based cream cheese alternative. Use maple syrup instead of honey and a vegan protein powder.

Note: Coconut cream has higher fat content and different consistency, so chilling is essential to firm the filling.

Peanut-Free

Use sunflower seed butter or tahini instead of peanut butter to accommodate allergies.

Note: Sunflower seed butter has a thinner consistency; you may need to add an extra 1-2 tablespoons of oats to maintain firmness.

No-Coating Option

Skip the chocolate coating and roll the finished balls in finely shredded coconut or crushed nuts for texture.

Note: This reduces sweetness and adds a contrasting texture but omits the chocolate snap layer.

Flavor Boost with Spices

Add 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon or pumpkin pie spice to the base mixture for a warm, seasonal twist.

Note: Spices complement the vanilla and nutty flavors without overpowering the creamy center.

Protein Powder-Free

Omit protein powder and increase oats by 1/4 cup. Add 1 tablespoon of chia seeds for extra protein and binding.

Note: Texture will be softer and less dense; chia seeds help absorb moisture and hold shape.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Store protein balls in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 24 hours. Beyond this, they may become too soft.

Refrigerator

Keep refrigerated in a sealed container for up to 5 days. Chilling firms the structure and preserves the cream cheese filling.

Freezer

Freeze protein balls in a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before serving.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Allow refrigerated or thawed balls to come to room temperature for 10 minutes for best flavor and texture.

Make Ahead

Prepare the base mixture and filling separately and refrigerate up to 24 hours before assembling. This shortens active preparation time when ready to serve.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make these protein balls ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare them up to 24 hours ahead. Store them refrigerated in an airtight container to maintain freshness and texture.
Why are my protein balls too sticky to handle?
The mixture may be too warm or wet. Chill the dough for at least 10 minutes and ensure peanut butter is not overly oily.
Can I use a different nut butter instead of peanut butter?
Yes, almond or cashew butter works well, though it will slightly change the flavor and texture.
How long do protein balls last in the fridge?
They keep well for up to 5 days when stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator.
Can I skip the chocolate coating?
Absolutely. The coating is optional and can be replaced with a dusting of shredded coconut or nuts if preferred.
Is it necessary to use protein powder?
Protein powder adds structure and boosts protein content, but you can omit it and add more oats or chia seeds, though texture will differ.

Instructions

Step 1
In a mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, protein powder, and a pinch of salt. Stir to evenly distribute dry ingredients.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are uniformly mixed without visible clumps.
Step 2
Add peanut butter, honey (or maple syrup), and vanilla extract to the dry mixture. Use a sturdy spoon or your hands to mix until a sticky, cohesive dough forms. It should hold together when pressed but not be overly wet.
⏱ Time: 3-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture clumps into a dough that holds shape when pressed between fingers.
Step 3
Place the dough in the refrigerator for 10 minutes. This firms the mixture, making it easier to shape without sticking excessively to hands.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough feels cooler and slightly firmer to touch.
Step 4
Meanwhile, prepare the filling by mixing softened cream cheese, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and a few drops of yellow food coloring if using. Whip with a spoon or electric mixer until smooth and creamy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth, fluffy, and holds together without lumps.
Step 5
Remove the dough from the fridge. Pinch off a golf ball-sized portion, flatten it in your palm, then place about 1 teaspoon of filling in the center. Carefully fold the dough around the filling, rolling it into a smooth ball to enclose the cream cheese completely.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls are smooth and no filling is visible on the outside.
Step 6
Optional: Melt chocolate chips in a microwave-safe bowl in 20-second increments, stirring in between, until fully melted and smooth. Dip each protein ball into the chocolate, using a fork or skewer to coat evenly, then place on parchment paper to set.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes plus setting time
✓ Doneness: Chocolate coating hardens at room temperature or in fridge, creating a slight snap when touched.
Step 7
Refrigerate the protein balls for at least 15 minutes before serving to allow the filling to firm up and flavors to meld.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls feel firm but not hard, with distinct creamy center.
