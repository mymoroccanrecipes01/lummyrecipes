Moist No Sugar Cake Made with Natural Sweeteners for Guilt-Free Treats

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 6, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This no sugar cake is a delightful way to enjoy dessert without the guilt. Using wholesome ingredients and natural sweeteners, it stays moist and flavorful. Perfect for afternoon tea or a healthy celebration!

Pro Tips

Don’t over-mix the batter once you combine wet and dry ingredients.
Over-mixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes the cake tough and dense instead of tender and soft. Folding gently ensures a light crumb and better texture.
Use room temperature eggs to help ingredients blend smoothly.
Cold eggs can cause the melted coconut oil to solidify and the batter to clump, leading to uneven texture. Room temperature eggs emulsify better, creating a consistent batter.
Sift the whole wheat flour to avoid clumps and lighten the batter.
Sifting aerates the flour and breaks up clumps, which helps produce a finer crumb and prevents dense patches in the cake.
Let the cake cool completely before slicing.
Cutting too soon can cause the cake to crumble or be gummy because the structure hasn’t set. Cooling lets the crumb firm up for clean slices.
Use fresh baking powder and baking soda for best rise.
Old or expired leavening agents lose potency, resulting in a flat or dense cake. Always check dates to ensure proper leavening.
Grease and flour your pan well to prevent sticking.
Whole wheat flour batter can stick more than white flour cakes. Properly preparing the pan ensures the cake releases easily without breaking.
Adjust sweetness by varying the amount of honey or maple syrup.
Natural sweeteners vary in intensity and moisture content. Tasting the batter (safe with raw eggs omitted) or adjusting the quantity helps you get the perfect balance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Over-mixing the batter

Why it happens Many cooks think mixing thoroughly is better, but overdoing it activates gluten, making the cake tough.

Fix Mix just until the flour disappears. Use a spatula to fold gently and stop as soon as the batter looks uniform.

Using cold ingredients

Why it happens Some people forget to bring eggs or yogurt to room temperature, leading to uneven mixing and texture.

Fix Plan ahead to let eggs and yogurt sit out for 30 minutes before baking. This ensures smooth emulsification and consistent batter.

Not checking oven temperature

Why it happens Ovens can run hot or cold, causing under- or over-baking.

Fix Use an oven thermometer to verify temperature and adjust baking time accordingly for even results.

Skipping greasing the pan

Why it happens Assuming non-stick pans are enough can lead to sticking, especially with whole wheat batters.

Fix Always grease and lightly flour the pan or use parchment paper to ensure easy removal.

Overbaking the cake

Why it happens Leaving the cake too long dries it out.

Fix Start checking doneness a few minutes before the recommended time using a toothpick test.

Variations

Add Warm Spices

Mix in 1 tsp cinnamon and 1/2 tsp nutmeg to the dry ingredients for a cozy, spiced variation. This adds warmth and complexity, perfect for fall or winter desserts.

Note: Spices blend best when added with the dry ingredients to distribute evenly without clumping.

Use Almond Flour

Replace half the whole wheat flour with almond flour for a nuttier flavor and more tender crumb. This also adds healthy fats and reduces carbs slightly.

Note: Almond flour absorbs less moisture, so you may need to add a tablespoon more milk for a balanced batter.

Include Fresh Berries

Fold in 1 cup fresh blueberries or raspberries gently into the batter before baking. This adds bursts of juicy sweetness and a pretty look.

Note: Coat berries lightly in flour before adding to prevent them from sinking to the bottom.

Top with Greek Yogurt Frosting

Mix Greek yogurt with a little honey and vanilla to spread on top once the cake cools. It adds tang and moisture without refined sugar.

Note: Apply frosting just before serving to avoid sogginess.

Make It Vegan

Swap eggs for flax eggs (1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water per egg) and use plant-based yogurt and milk. This keeps the cake moist and flavorful while vegan-friendly.

Note: Flax eggs need 5 minutes to gel before mixing into batter.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cake can safely keep at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight container to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days. The cake may firm up in the fridge and lose some moisture, so bring to room temperature before serving or warm slightly in the microwave.

Freezer

Freeze the cake wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then foil for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave in the fridge overnight and warm gently in the oven or microwave to restore softness.

Reheating

Reheat slices in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk or yogurt before warming can help keep it moist. Avoid overheating to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter up to a day before baking; store covered in the fridge and bake fresh for best texture. Alternatively, bake the cake and freeze it; thaw the day before serving.

FAQ

Can I substitute the whole wheat flour with all-purpose flour?
Yes, you can use all-purpose flour instead, which will make the cake lighter and less dense. However, you’ll lose some of the nutty flavor and fiber. Adjust baking time slightly as the texture may change.
What if I don’t have Greek yogurt? Can I use regular yogurt?
Regular yogurt can work but is usually thinner, which might affect the cake’s moisture and texture. Strain it through a cheesecloth to thicken or reduce some of the milk to compensate.
Is it okay to use a different natural sweetener?
Absolutely! Maple syrup, agave nectar, or even mashed bananas can substitute honey. Just keep in mind moisture levels and sweetness intensity might require minor adjustments.
How do I make this cake vegan?
Replace eggs with flax or chia eggs (1 tbsp seed + 3 tbsp water per egg), use plant-based yogurt and milk, and coconut oil instead of butter. This keeps the texture moist and delicious.
Why is my cake dense or heavy?
Over-mixing the batter or using expired leavening agents can cause density. Mix gently and use fresh baking powder and soda to get a light, airy texture.
Can I add nuts or fruit to this cake?
Yes! Fold in chopped nuts or fresh berries gently before baking. Coat berries with a little flour to prevent sinking. This adds texture and flavor variety.
How do I store leftovers to keep them fresh?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. To refresh, warm slices gently before serving.
Can I make cupcakes with this recipe?
Definitely! Bake the batter in cupcake liners for 18-22 minutes at 350°F. Keep an eye on them to avoid drying out.

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour, sifted
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/3 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk (dairy or plant-based)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and greasing a 9-inch round cake pan. This ensures your cake won’t stick and will come out cleanly. Greasing and lightly flouring the pan helps create a slight barrier, so the edges don’t dry out or break apart when you remove it. Also, make sure the oven is fully heated before baking to get an even rise.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully preheated and pan is evenly greased with no dry spots
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mixing these dry ingredients first helps distribute the leavening agents evenly throughout the batter. If you skip this, the cake might rise unevenly or develop dense spots. Sifting is optional but recommended for a lighter texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined, free of lumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, combine the applesauce, honey (or maple syrup), Greek yogurt, melted coconut oil, eggs, vanilla extract, and milk. Whisk gently until smooth and fully incorporated. This wet mixture provides moisture, natural sweetness, and richness. Avoid over-beating here to keep the batter tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wet ingredients form a smooth, homogenous mixture without streaks
Step 4
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and fold them together gently using a spatula. Mix just until you no longer see flour patches. Over-mixing at this stage can develop gluten and make the cake tough instead of soft and tender. The batter should be slightly thick but pourable.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is uniform with no dry flour, slightly thick but smooth
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan, smoothing the top with the spatula for even baking. Tap the pan lightly on the counter to release any large air bubbles. This step helps the cake bake evenly without big holes or tunnels inside.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread and surface smooth with no large bubbles
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes. Start checking at 28 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. Baking too long can dry the cake out, so keep an eye on it towards the end.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean or with moist crumbs
Step 7
Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Cooling slightly in the pan allows the cake to set and makes it easier to remove without breaking. Avoid trying to unmold it while hot, or it might crumble.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake edges pull slightly away from pan sides and surface feels firm
Step 8
Run a knife around the edges and invert the cake onto a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling fully ensures the texture firms up and flavors develop. If you cut it too soon, the cake might be gummy or fall apart.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is fully cooled and holds shape when handled
Step 9
Serve plain or with a light dusting of cinnamon or a dollop of Greek yogurt on top. This cake pairs beautifully with tea or coffee and keeps well for several days when stored properly. Enjoy the natural sweetness and moist texture without any refined sugar!
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake slice holds together with moist crumb and subtle sweetness
