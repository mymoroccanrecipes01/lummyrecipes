Moist No Sugar Cake Made with Natural Sweeteners for Guilt-Free Treats

Lummy Hayes Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

This no sugar cake is a delightful way to enjoy dessert without the guilt. Using wholesome ingredients and natural sweeteners, it stays moist and flavorful. Perfect for afternoon tea or a healthy celebration!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this no sugar cake recipe when I wanted to make a dessert for my family that wouldn’t spike anyone’s sugar levels but still felt indulgent. After a few experiments, I found that using unsweetened applesauce paired with a touch of honey or maple syrup gave the perfect natural sweetness without the heaviness of refined sugar. What makes this cake stand out is how moist it stays thanks to the Greek yogurt and coconut oil — they keep it tender and rich without being greasy. I also love that it’s made with whole wheat flour, which adds a lovely nutty depth and makes it a bit more wholesome. The trick I learned early on was not to over-mix the batter; just enough to combine the ingredients gently, so the cake stays light and airy. When it’s done, the cake has a subtle sweetness and a comforting, homey flavor that works beautifully for afternoon tea or a casual celebration. Honestly, it’s become a favorite in my house because it feels like a treat but without the guilt. Give it a go — I think you’ll be surprised how satisfying a no sugar cake can be!

Why This Works

What makes this no sugar cake work so well is the balance of natural sweeteners and moistening agents. The combination of unsweetened applesauce and a touch of honey or maple syrup provides subtle sweetness without overwhelming the palate or adding refined sugar. Applesauce also contributes moisture and helps bind the batter, replacing some of the fat and sugar traditionally used.

Using whole wheat flour is another key element. It adds a nutty depth and more fiber, making the cake feel wholesome and satisfying. However, whole wheat flour can sometimes make baked goods dense, so the addition of Greek yogurt and coconut oil helps keep the crumb tender and moist. Greek yogurt adds protein and acidity that tenderizes the gluten, while coconut oil adds richness without heaviness.

The leavening agents—baking powder and baking soda—work together to give the cake a gentle rise. The baking soda reacts with the acidity in the yogurt to produce carbon dioxide bubbles that lighten the batter. Proper mixing technique is crucial here; over-mixing develops gluten and creates a tough texture, so folding gently ensures a soft crumb.

Finally, the baking time and temperature are calibrated to produce a golden, tender cake. Baking at 350°F allows the cake to cook evenly without drying out. Checking doneness with a toothpick ensures it’s fully baked but still moist inside. All these elements combine to create a naturally sweet, moist, and flavorful cake that feels indulgent but is actually quite wholesome.

  • Choose finely milled whole wheat flour for a smoother texture; it provides nuttiness and fiber but can make baked goods dense if overused. Sifting helps lighten it, and combining with moist ingredients like yogurt balances the texture.
  • Acts as a natural sweetener and moisture booster while reducing the need for added fats. Look for pure applesauce without added sugar or preservatives to keep the cake wholesome and naturally sweet.
  • Adds protein and acidity which tenderizes gluten and keeps the crumb moist. Use plain, full-fat Greek yogurt for richness. Avoid flavored varieties that add unwanted sweetness or thickeners.
  • Gives richness and moisture without the heaviness of butter. Melted coconut oil blends easily into the batter and contributes subtle coconut flavor. Use virgin or refined depending on your flavor preference.
  • Natural sweeteners that add depth of flavor and moisture. They’re less processed than white sugar and provide antioxidants. Adjust quantity to taste, but too much can make the cake overly moist and dense.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour, sifted
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/3 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk (dairy or plant-based)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and greasing a 9-inch round cake pan. This ensures your cake won’t stick and will come out cleanly. Greasing and lightly flouring the pan helps create a slight barrier, so the edges don’t dry out or break apart when you remove it. Also, make sure the oven is fully heated before baking to get an even rise.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven is fully preheated and pan is evenly greased with no dry spots
  2. Step 2

    In a large bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mixing these dry ingredients first helps distribute the leavening agents evenly throughout the batter. If you skip this, the cake might rise unevenly or develop dense spots. Sifting is optional but recommended for a lighter texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are well combined, free of lumps
  3. Step 3

    In a separate bowl, combine the applesauce, honey (or maple syrup), Greek yogurt, melted coconut oil, eggs, vanilla extract, and milk. Whisk gently until smooth and fully incorporated. This wet mixture provides moisture, natural sweetness, and richness. Avoid over-beating here to keep the batter tender.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wet ingredients form a smooth, homogenous mixture without streaks
  4. Step 4

    Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and fold them together gently using a spatula. Mix just until you no longer see flour patches. Over-mixing at this stage can develop gluten and make the cake tough instead of soft and tender. The batter should be slightly thick but pourable.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is uniform with no dry flour, slightly thick but smooth
  5. Step 5

    Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan, smoothing the top with the spatula for even baking. Tap the pan lightly on the counter to release any large air bubbles. This step helps the cake bake evenly without big holes or tunnels inside.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Batter evenly spread and surface smooth with no large bubbles
  6. Step 6

    Bake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes. Start checking at 28 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. Baking too long can dry the cake out, so keep an eye on it towards the end.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean or with moist crumbs
  7. Step 7

    Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Cooling slightly in the pan allows the cake to set and makes it easier to remove without breaking. Avoid trying to unmold it while hot, or it might crumble.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Cake edges pull slightly away from pan sides and surface feels firm
  8. Step 8

    Run a knife around the edges and invert the cake onto a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling fully ensures the texture firms up and flavors develop. If you cut it too soon, the cake might be gummy or fall apart.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Cake is fully cooled and holds shape when handled
  9. Step 9

    Serve plain or with a light dusting of cinnamon or a dollop of Greek yogurt on top. This cake pairs beautifully with tea or coffee and keeps well for several days when stored properly. Enjoy the natural sweetness and moist texture without any refined sugar!

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Cake slice holds together with moist crumb and subtle sweetness

Pro Tips

  • Don’t over-mix the batter once you combine wet and dry ingredients. — Over-mixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes the cake tough and dense instead of tender and soft. Folding gently ensures a light crumb and better texture.
  • Use room temperature eggs to help ingredients blend smoothly. — Cold eggs can cause the melted coconut oil to solidify and the batter to clump, leading to uneven texture. Room temperature eggs emulsify better, creating a consistent batter.
  • Sift the whole wheat flour to avoid clumps and lighten the batter. — Sifting aerates the flour and breaks up clumps, which helps produce a finer crumb and prevents dense patches in the cake.
  • Let the cake cool completely before slicing. — Cutting too soon can cause the cake to crumble or be gummy because the structure hasn’t set. Cooling lets the crumb firm up for clean slices.
  • Use fresh baking powder and baking soda for best rise. — Old or expired leavening agents lose potency, resulting in a flat or dense cake. Always check dates to ensure proper leavening.
  • Grease and flour your pan well to prevent sticking. — Whole wheat flour batter can stick more than white flour cakes. Properly preparing the pan ensures the cake releases easily without breaking.
  • Adjust sweetness by varying the amount of honey or maple syrup. — Natural sweeteners vary in intensity and moisture content. Tasting the batter (safe with raw eggs omitted) or adjusting the quantity helps you get the perfect balance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-mixing the batter
    Why: Many cooks think mixing thoroughly is better, but overdoing it activates gluten, making the cake tough.
    Fix: Mix just until the flour disappears. Use a spatula to fold gently and stop as soon as the batter looks uniform.
  • Using cold ingredients
    Why: Some people forget to bring eggs or yogurt to room temperature, leading to uneven mixing and texture.
    Fix: Plan ahead to let eggs and yogurt sit out for 30 minutes before baking. This ensures smooth emulsification and consistent batter.
  • Not checking oven temperature
    Why: Ovens can run hot or cold, causing under- or over-baking.
    Fix: Use an oven thermometer to verify temperature and adjust baking time accordingly for even results.
  • Skipping greasing the pan
    Why: Assuming non-stick pans are enough can lead to sticking, especially with whole wheat batters.
    Fix: Always grease and lightly flour the pan or use parchment paper to ensure easy removal.
  • Overbaking the cake
    Why: Leaving the cake too long dries it out.
    Fix: Start checking doneness a few minutes before the recommended time using a toothpick test.

Variations

  • Add Warm Spices: Mix in 1 tsp cinnamon and 1/2 tsp nutmeg to the dry ingredients for a cozy, spiced variation. This adds warmth and complexity, perfect for fall or winter desserts.
    Spices blend best when added with the dry ingredients to distribute evenly without clumping.
  • Use Almond Flour: Replace half the whole wheat flour with almond flour for a nuttier flavor and more tender crumb. This also adds healthy fats and reduces carbs slightly.
    Almond flour absorbs less moisture, so you may need to add a tablespoon more milk for a balanced batter.
  • Include Fresh Berries: Fold in 1 cup fresh blueberries or raspberries gently into the batter before baking. This adds bursts of juicy sweetness and a pretty look.
    Coat berries lightly in flour before adding to prevent them from sinking to the bottom.
  • Top with Greek Yogurt Frosting: Mix Greek yogurt with a little honey and vanilla to spread on top once the cake cools. It adds tang and moisture without refined sugar.
    Apply frosting just before serving to avoid sogginess.
  • Make It Vegan: Swap eggs for flax eggs (1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water per egg) and use plant-based yogurt and milk. This keeps the cake moist and flavorful while vegan-friendly.
    Flax eggs need 5 minutes to gel before mixing into batter.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: The cake can safely keep at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight container to prevent drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days. The cake may firm up in the fridge and lose some moisture, so bring to room temperature before serving or warm slightly in the microwave.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze the cake wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then foil for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave in the fridge overnight and warm gently in the oven or microwave to restore softness.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat slices in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk or yogurt before warming can help keep it moist. Avoid overheating to prevent drying.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter up to a day before baking; store covered in the fridge and bake fresh for best texture. Alternatively, bake the cake and freeze it; thaw the day before serving.

FAQ

Can I substitute the whole wheat flour with all-purpose flour?
Yes, you can use all-purpose flour instead, which will make the cake lighter and less dense. However, you’ll lose some of the nutty flavor and fiber. Adjust baking time slightly as the texture may change.
What if I don’t have Greek yogurt? Can I use regular yogurt?
Regular yogurt can work but is usually thinner, which might affect the cake’s moisture and texture. Strain it through a cheesecloth to thicken or reduce some of the milk to compensate.
Is it okay to use a different natural sweetener?
Absolutely! Maple syrup, agave nectar, or even mashed bananas can substitute honey. Just keep in mind moisture levels and sweetness intensity might require minor adjustments.
How do I make this cake vegan?
Replace eggs with flax or chia eggs (1 tbsp seed + 3 tbsp water per egg), use plant-based yogurt and milk, and coconut oil instead of butter. This keeps the texture moist and delicious.
Why is my cake dense or heavy?
Over-mixing the batter or using expired leavening agents can cause density. Mix gently and use fresh baking powder and soda to get a light, airy texture.
Can I add nuts or fruit to this cake?
Yes! Fold in chopped nuts or fresh berries gently before baking. Coat berries with a little flour to prevent sinking. This adds texture and flavor variety.
How do I store leftovers to keep them fresh?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. To refresh, warm slices gently before serving.
Can I make cupcakes with this recipe?
Definitely! Bake the batter in cupcake liners for 18-22 minutes at 350°F. Keep an eye on them to avoid drying out.
