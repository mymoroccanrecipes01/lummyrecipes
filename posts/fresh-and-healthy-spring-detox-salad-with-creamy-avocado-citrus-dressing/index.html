Spring Salad with Avocado and Citrus Dressing

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 1, 2026
15 min Prep Time
15 min Total Time
2 Servings
Easy Difficulty

A fresh spring salad combining crisp greens, creamy avocado, and a lively citrus dressing that balances acidity and richness. This recipe highlights the importance of ingredient freshness and dressing balance to create a salad that is satisfying yet light, ideal for warm weather meals.

Pro Tips

Dry the salad greens thoroughly before dressing.
Excess water dilutes the dressing and causes the leaves to wilt quickly, affecting texture and flavor.
Add avocado last and serve immediately.
Avocado oxidizes quickly; adding it just before serving keeps it fresh and visually appealing.
Use a gentle tossing motion when mixing the salad.
Vigorous tossing can bruise delicate greens, causing them to discolor and become mushy.
Balance acidity and sweetness carefully in the dressing.
Too much acid can overpower the salad, while insufficient acidity results in a flat taste. A slight sweetness helps round the flavors.
Prepare the salad components ahead but dress just before serving.
Prepping in advance saves time, but dressing too early leads to soggy greens.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using overripe avocado that is mushy or browned.

Why it happens Overripe avocado loses structure and can turn brown quickly when exposed to air.

Fix Select avocado that is ripe but still firm, and add it to the salad just before serving.

Not drying salad greens properly after washing.

Why it happens Water clings to leaves, diluting the dressing and causing limpness.

Fix Use a salad spinner or pat dry thoroughly with kitchen towels.

Making the dressing with bottled citrus juice.

Why it happens Bottled juices often lack freshness and contain preservatives, altering flavor.

Fix Always use freshly squeezed lemon and orange juice for best brightness.

Tossing the salad too vigorously.

Why it happens Rough handling damages delicate greens, causing bruising and discoloration.

Fix Use gentle folding motions to combine ingredients evenly.

Variations

Vegan Variation

Omit honey in the dressing and substitute with maple syrup or agave nectar to keep the dressing plant-based.

Note: Maple syrup provides similar sweetness and viscosity without altering the balance of acidity.

Add Crunch with Nuts

Sprinkle toasted walnuts or almonds on top for added texture and nutty flavor.

Note: Toasting nuts enhances their aroma and prevents them from becoming soggy when combined with dressing.

Herb-Forward Salad

Increase parsley quantity and add fresh mint or basil for a more aromatic and layered herb flavor.

Note: Fresh herbs should be added just before serving to preserve their brightness and prevent wilting.

Seasonal Berry Addition

In late spring, include fresh strawberries or blueberries to add a subtle sweetness and color contrast.

Note: Berries release juice quickly; add them last and serve immediately to avoid sogginess.

Low-Oil Dressing

Reduce olive oil to 1 teaspoon and increase citrus juice for a lighter dressing with more pronounced acidity.

Note: Lower oil content reduces mouthfeel richness but increases brightness; balance with a touch of sweetener if desired.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad is best served fresh and should not be left at room temperature for more than 1 hour to maintain freshness.

Refrigerator

Store salad components separately (greens, sliced avocado, and dressing) in airtight containers for up to 1 day. Avoid dressing the salad until ready to serve to prevent wilting.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this salad due to the high water content of the greens and avocado, which will degrade texture upon thawing.

Reheating

Not applicable for this salad as it is served cold and fresh.

Make Ahead

Prepare and store the dressing and chopped vegetables in advance. Combine and dress the salad just before serving to preserve texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I make this spring salad ahead of time?
You can prepare the dressing and chop vegetables ahead, but dress the salad just before serving to keep the greens crisp and avocado fresh.
Why does my avocado turn brown quickly in the salad?
Avocado browns due to oxidation when exposed to air. Adding it last and serving immediately helps maintain its color.
Can I substitute lemon juice with lime juice in the dressing?
Yes, lime juice can replace lemon juice and will add a slightly different citrus note but similar acidity.
How long does the salad last in the fridge after dressing?
Once dressed, the salad is best eaten within a few hours as the greens will wilt and the avocado may brown.
Can I use bottled orange juice instead of fresh?
Freshly squeezed juice is recommended for best flavor, but if using bottled juice, choose one without added sugars or preservatives.

What You'll Need

2 servings

  • 4 cups (120g) mixed salad greens (such as baby spinach, arugula, and butter lettuce)
  • 1 ripe avocado (about 150g), peeled, pitted, and sliced
  • 1 small cucumber (100g), thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup (75g) cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup (10g) fresh parsley, chopped
  • For the citrus dressing:
  • 3 tbsp (45ml) fresh orange juice
  • 2 tbsp (30ml) fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp (15ml) extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp (5ml) honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp (1g) fine sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Rinse the mixed salad greens under cold water and spin or pat dry thoroughly. Excess moisture can dilute the dressing and cause the salad to become soggy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Greens are crisp and free of water droplets.
Step 2
Prepare the avocado by cutting it in half, removing the pit, and slicing the flesh into even slices. Handle gently to keep the slices intact and avoid bruising.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Avocado slices are smooth, creamy, and hold their shape without browning.
Step 3
Thinly slice the cucumber and halve the cherry tomatoes. Chop the parsley finely to release its aroma.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are evenly cut to ensure uniform texture and flavor distribution.
Step 4
In a small bowl, whisk together the fresh orange juice, lemon juice, olive oil, honey (if using), salt, and pepper until the dressing emulsifies and slightly thickens.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing is homogenous with a glossy appearance and balanced acidity.
Step 5
In a large bowl, combine the salad greens, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and parsley. Drizzle the citrus dressing over and toss gently to coat evenly without bruising the leaves.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad components are evenly coated with dressing; leaves remain crisp.
Step 6
Arrange the avocado slices on top of the tossed salad just before serving to maintain their texture and color.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Avocado is fresh and vibrant with no browning.
