Easter Nest Cookies: A Springtime Treat with Toasted Coconut and Mini Chocolate Eggs

Lummy Hayes
Published on March 21, 2026
15 min prep
10 min cook
25 min total
24 servings
Easy

These Easter nest cookies combine the crisp texture of toasted coconut and crunchy chow mein noodles with the festive touch of mini chocolate eggs. Ideal for spring gatherings, their light sweetness and delicate crunch offer a nostalgic yet fresh treat for all ages.

Pro Tips

Toast the coconut evenly and watch it carefully during baking to avoid burning.
Coconut burns quickly and uneven toasting can create bitter spots. Stirring halfway ensures consistent color and flavor development.
Dry the chow mein noodles gently with a paper towel if they feel greasy before mixing.
This prevents excess oil from making the mixture too heavy or greasy, ensuring a crisp final texture.
Work quickly when shaping nests as the syrup mixture hardens when cools.
The mixture stiffens as it cools, making it harder to shape. Preparing all ingredients ahead helps speed up the process.
Press the mini chocolate eggs into the nests right out of the oven while warm.
Warm nests are pliable and allow the eggs to stick securely. Adding eggs after cooling can cause them to fall off.
Use unsweetened shredded coconut to control the overall sweetness of the cookie.
Sweetened coconut can make the cookies overly sugary and mask the delicate flavors of butter and syrup.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not toasting the coconut or toasting it unevenly.

Why it happens Overlooking this step or leaving the coconut unattended can cause burning or raw flavors.

Fix Toast in a single layer, stir halfway, and watch closely during the last few minutes.

Adding chocolate eggs before baking.

Why it happens Placing eggs in the nests too early causes them to melt or burn in the oven.

Fix Always add eggs immediately after baking when nests are warm but out of the oven.

Overcrowding the baking sheet leading to uneven baking.

Why it happens Placing nests too close causes steam buildup and soggy texture.

Fix Leave enough space between nests to allow airflow and even heat distribution.

Using wet or oily chow mein noodles.

Why it happens Noodles from the package may have excess oil or moisture that affects texture.

Fix Pat noodles dry with paper towels before mixing to maintain crispness.

Variations

Gluten-Free Version

Replace chow mein noodles with gluten-free crispy rice cereal or puffed rice for similar crunch without gluten.

Note: Ensure the cereal is plain and unsweetened to maintain flavor balance; texture will be slightly different but still crisp.

Vegan Option

Use coconut oil instead of butter and maple syrup in place of golden syrup. Confirm mini chocolate eggs are dairy-free or use vegan chocolate chips.

Note: Coconut oil solidifies differently than butter; cookies may be slightly less rich but still hold shape well.

Nutty Twist

Add 1/2 cup chopped toasted almonds or hazelnuts to the mixture for extra crunch and flavor depth.

Note: Nuts increase texture complexity and richness; adjust syrup quantity slightly if mixture feels dry.

Citrus Zest Infusion

Add 1 teaspoon finely grated orange or lemon zest to the butter and syrup mixture for a bright, fresh note.

Note: Citrus oils enhance aroma and balance sweetness; add zest after removing from heat to preserve flavor.

Chocolate Drizzle Decoration

Once nests are cooled, drizzle melted dark or white chocolate over the top for added flavor and visual appeal.

Note: Chocolate sets quickly on cooled cookies and adds a contrasting texture; use a piping bag or spoon for control.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat to prevent melting of the chocolate eggs.

Refrigerator

If you prefer, cookies can be refrigerated for up to 1 week. Bring to room temperature before serving for best texture.

Freezer

Cookies freeze well for up to 2 months when placed in a single layer between parchment sheets inside an airtight container. Thaw at room temperature before serving.

Reheating

No reheating is necessary. If desired, allow refrigerated or frozen cookies to come to room temperature to regain crispness.

Make Ahead

Prepare the coconut and syrup mixture a day in advance and refrigerate. Shape nests and bake just before serving to retain freshness and texture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make Easter nest cookies ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the mixture a day ahead and keep it refrigerated. Shape and bake the nests shortly before serving to preserve freshness.
Why are my nests falling apart after baking?
This usually happens if the syrup mixture wasn’t hot enough to bind the ingredients or if nests were not pressed firmly. Ensure the butter and syrup are well melted and coat all ingredients evenly.
Can I substitute golden syrup with honey or maple syrup?
Golden syrup works best for its mild flavor and sticky texture. Honey or maple syrup can be used but may alter flavor and texture slightly, making nests softer or stickier.
How long do these cookies last stored at room temperature?
Stored in an airtight container, they remain fresh for up to 3 days at room temperature without losing crunch.
Can I use regular shredded coconut instead of toasted?
Toasting is important for flavor and texture. Using untoasted coconut will result in a milder taste and softer texture that may not hold up as well.

Ingredients

Approximately 24 cookies

  • 3 cups (75g) chow mein noodles
  • 2 cups (160g) shredded unsweetened coconut
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) golden syrup (or light corn syrup as substitute)
  • 1 tsp (5ml) vanilla extract
  • Mini chocolate eggs (about 48 pieces)

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Spread the shredded coconut evenly over a baking sheet and toast for about 5-7 minutes, stirring once halfway through. Watch closely—the coconut should turn golden brown and emit a warm, nutty aroma without burning.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Coconut is evenly golden brown with a fragrant, toasted smell
Step 2
In a medium saucepan over low heat, melt the butter together with the granulated sugar and golden syrup. Stir gently until the mixture is smooth and glossy. Avoid boiling; you want the sugar to dissolve without caramelizing further.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, slightly thickened, and shiny with no sugar granules visible
Step 3
Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Add the toasted coconut and chow mein noodles, folding gently to coat all pieces evenly with the buttery syrup mixture.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are fully coated and slightly glossy, no dry patches remain
Step 4
Line two baking sheets with parchment paper. Using a tablespoon or small ice cream scoop, portion out the mixture and gently shape it into nest-like cups with an indentation in the center for the eggs. Work quickly before the mixture cools and stiffens.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Each nest holds its shape with a visible well in the center, nests are evenly sized
Step 5
Bake the nests in the preheated oven for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are lightly browned and the nests feel firm to the touch but not brittle. Baking sets the shape and intensifies the flavor of the coconut.
⏱ Time: 8-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden, nests are firm but still slightly pliable
Step 6
Remove the nests from the oven and immediately press 2 mini chocolate eggs into the indentation of each nest. Let the cookies cool completely on the baking sheets; the nests will harden as they cool, securing the eggs in place.
⏱ Time: 15-20 minutes cooling
✓ Doneness: Nests are fully cooled and firm, chocolate eggs are set without sinking

Final Thoughts
