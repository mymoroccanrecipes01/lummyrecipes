Crispy Roasted Cabbage Steaks with Garlic Butter Glaze

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
10 min prep 25 min cook 4 servings

This recipe transforms simple cabbage into a side dish that’s crispy on the edges and tender in the center. Roasting with a garlic butter glaze adds rich aroma and a caramelized finish that complements both casual dinners and special occasions. It’s a straightforward technique that highlights how roasting can elevate humble vegetables.

Introduction

I first started roasting cabbage steaks on a chilly autumn evening when I wanted something simple yet satisfying to accompany a roast chicken. The smell of caramelizing edges and garlic filling the kitchen was a clear sign I’d found a reliable side. This version stands out because it balances the natural sweetness of roasted cabbage with a buttery, garlicky glaze that crisps beautifully. It’s a dish I return to during colder months when hearty vegetables are especially welcome, and it works well for both weeknight dinners and informal gatherings. As someone who’s tested this recipe multiple times, I appreciate how straightforward it is to get satisfying texture and flavor with minimal fuss. Let me walk you through what makes this approach effective and how you can get those rich, golden edges every time.

Why This Works

Roasting cabbage in thick slices creates two contrasting textures: a tender, almost creamy core and crispy, caramelized edges. This texture contrast comes from the cabbage’s structure and how heat affects it. The outer leaves have more exposure to dry heat, which encourages Maillard browning and sugar caramelization, while the inner layers retain moisture, softening gently.

The garlic butter glaze adds flavor and promotes browning. Butter contains milk solids that brown easily, contributing to the rich aroma and deeper color on the surface. Meanwhile, the garlic infuses its pungent aroma without burning due to the moderate oven temperature, releasing savory compounds that enhance the natural sweetness of the cabbage.

Seasoning with salt before roasting helps draw out moisture from the cabbage’s surface, which then evaporates during roasting, concentrating flavors. The optional acid like lemon juice or vinegar brightens the dish by balancing richness with a subtle tang.

  • Choosing a firm, fresh green cabbage is key. Look for tight, dense leaves without discoloration or wilting. The thickness of the cabbage steaks should be about 1.5 to 2 cm (around ¾ inch) to allow the outer layers to crisp while the interior remains tender without drying out.
  • Unsalted butter works best as it allows precise control over salt levels. Butter’s milk solids brown quickly, which helps develop the golden crust and nutty aroma on the cabbage edges. Clarified butter could be used to avoid burning but results in less flavor complexity.
  • Freshly minced garlic releases sweet, aromatic compounds when roasted. Adding it in the glaze rather than whole cloves ensures even distribution and prevents burnt bits, which can taste bitter.
  • Olive oil raises the smoke point slightly and helps the butter spread evenly over the cabbage. It also adds a subtle fruity note that complements the butter’s richness.
  • Using kosher salt evenly seasons the cabbage and aids in moisture extraction, improving texture and flavor concentration during roasting.

Ingredients

  • 1 large green cabbage (about 1.5 kg / 3.3 lbs)
  • 4 tbsp (60 g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp (15 ml) olive oil
  • 1 tsp (5 g) kosher salt, divided
  • 1/2 tsp (1 g) freshly ground black pepper
  • Optional: 1 tbsp (15 ml) lemon juice or apple cider vinegar for brightness
  • Optional garnish: fresh parsley or chives, finely chopped

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it to prevent sticking.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ The oven reaches stable temperature; baking sheet prepared
  2. Step 2

    Remove the outer leaves of the cabbage if wilted or damaged. Cut the cabbage into 1.5 to 2 cm (¾ inch) thick slices, aiming for about 4 'steaks' from the center. Smaller inner slices can be roasted separately or saved for another use.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Cabbage steaks are uniform thickness and intact
  3. Step 3

    In a small bowl, combine melted butter, minced garlic, olive oil, half of the salt, and black pepper. Stir well to mix.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Glaze is smooth and evenly mixed
  4. Step 4

    Place cabbage steaks on the prepared baking sheet in a single layer. Brush both sides generously with the garlic butter glaze. Sprinkle the remaining salt evenly over the steaks.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Steaks are evenly coated and lightly seasoned
  5. Step 5

    Roast in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, flipping the steaks halfway through (around 10-12 minutes). Look for edges turning golden brown and caramelized while the center softens but holds shape.

    ⏱ 20-25 minutes✓ Edges are crisp and browned, center is tender and slightly translucent
  6. Step 6

    Remove from oven and, if using, drizzle with lemon juice or vinegar to add brightness. Garnish with chopped fresh herbs before serving.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Steaks glistening with glaze and vibrant herb garnish

Pro Tips

  • Use a sharp knife to cut cabbage steaks cleanly and evenly. — Even thickness ensures uniform cooking and consistent texture between the crispy edges and tender core.
  • Dry the cabbage slices thoroughly with a kitchen towel before glazing. — Removing surface moisture helps the glaze adhere better and encourages browning rather than steaming.
  • Flip the cabbage halfway through roasting for balanced color and texture on both sides. — Roasting only one side can result in uneven cooking and less even caramelization.
  • Avoid overcrowding the baking sheet. — Good air circulation around each steak ensures crisp edges and prevents steaming.
  • Add the garlic to melted butter off the heat to prevent it from burning. — Burnt garlic tastes bitter and can spoil the flavor of the dish. Mixing garlic into warm butter preserves its aroma and sweetness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Cutting cabbage steaks too thin.
    Why: Thin slices cook too quickly and dry out, losing the creamy interior texture.
    Fix: Aim for 1.5 to 2 cm thickness to balance tenderness and crispiness.
  • Not drying cabbage before applying the glaze.
    Why: Wet surfaces cause the cabbage to steam instead of roast, preventing browning.
    Fix: Pat dry each steak thoroughly with paper towels before adding butter and oil.
  • Roasting at too low a temperature.
    Why: Lower temperatures don’t promote Maillard browning or caramelization effectively.
    Fix: Maintain oven temperature at 200°C (400°F) for optimal browning.
  • Adding garlic directly to hot butter and cooking it too long before mixing.
    Why: Garlic burns quickly in hot fat, producing bitterness.
    Fix: Melt butter first, remove from heat, then stir in garlic before brushing onto cabbage.

Variations

  • Vegan Roasted Cabbage Steaks: Replace butter with a mixture of olive oil and vegan margarine or coconut oil. Use garlic powder if fresh garlic is avoided.
    Oils with higher smoke points prevent burning during roasting and maintain flavor without dairy.
  • Spiced Roasted Cabbage: Add 1 tsp smoked paprika and ½ tsp ground cumin to the garlic butter glaze for a smoky, warm flavor.
    Spices should be mixed evenly into the glaze to avoid burning and ensure balanced flavor.
  • Parmesan Garlic Cabbage Steaks: Sprinkle 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese over the steaks during the last 5 minutes of roasting for a savory crust.
    Cheese browns quickly, so add near the end to prevent burning.
  • Asian-Inspired Roasted Cabbage: Add 1 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tsp sesame oil to the glaze, and sprinkle toasted sesame seeds before serving.
    Soy sauce adds salt and umami, while sesame oil adds a nutty aroma complementary to roasted cabbage.
  • Winter Root Vegetable Mix: Roast alongside thick slices of parsnip and turnip, brushing the same garlic butter glaze over all vegetables.
    Root vegetables require similar roasting times; cut sizes should be consistent for even cooking.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Serve immediately for best texture. If left at room temperature, consume within 2 hours to avoid spoilage.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store cooled cabbage steaks in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheating will soften the crisp edges.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freezing is not recommended as the texture degrades significantly after thawing.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat in a 180°C (350°F) oven for 8-10 minutes to restore some crispness. Avoid microwave reheating as it softens the texture.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the garlic butter glaze and cut cabbage steaks in advance. Store separately and assemble just before roasting to maintain freshness and texture.

FAQ

Can I make roasted cabbage steaks ahead of time?
You can prepare the cabbage slices and garlic butter glaze ahead but roast just before serving for best texture and flavor.
Why are my cabbage steaks soggy instead of crispy?
This usually happens if the cabbage wasn’t dried well or the oven temperature was too low, causing steaming rather than roasting.
Can I use red cabbage instead of green cabbage?
Yes, red cabbage works well and adds a vibrant color, but it may take slightly longer to roast due to its density.
How long do roasted cabbage steaks last in the fridge?
Stored properly, they keep up to 3 days but are best eaten within 24 hours for optimal texture.
Is it possible to roast cabbage steaks without butter?
Yes, you can use olive oil alone or a plant-based oil for a dairy-free option, though the flavor and browning will be less rich.
Can I add other seasonings to the glaze?
Absolutely. Herbs like thyme or spices like smoked paprika complement the cabbage well and can be mixed into the glaze before roasting.

Final Thoughts

Roasted cabbage steaks offer a simple way to transform an often overlooked vegetable into a satisfying side. The combination of crisp edges and tender centers, enhanced by garlic butter, makes this dish a reliable addition to many meals.
