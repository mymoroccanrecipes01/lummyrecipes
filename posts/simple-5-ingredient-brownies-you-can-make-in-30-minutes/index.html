Easy Five-Ingredient Fudgy Brownies You Can Whip Up Fast

Published on April 16, 2026
10 min Prep Time
20 min Time
9 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These brownies are the perfect quick fix when you need a chocolate hit. Made with just five simple ingredients, they’re rich, fudgy, and come together in no time. Great for last-minute dessert cravings!

Pro Tips

Use room temperature eggs for better mixing.
Room temperature eggs blend more easily into the batter, preventing lumps and ensuring a smooth texture. Cold eggs can cause the butter to seize or the batter to curdle, which affects the final crumb of the brownies.
Don’t overmix after adding flour.
Overmixing flour activates gluten, which can make brownies tough and cakey instead of fudgy. Gently folding flour until just combined keeps the texture tender and moist.
Line your baking pan with parchment paper for easy removal.
Parchment paper prevents sticking and allows you to lift the entire batch out for cleaner cuts. It also protects the brownies’ edges from overbaking.
Check brownies early to avoid overbaking.
Ovens vary, so start checking at 20 minutes. Overbaking dries out brownies, losing that fudgy center. Look for moist crumbs on a toothpick, not wet batter.
Cool brownies completely before slicing.
Cooling lets the brownies set and firm up. Cutting too soon causes them to crumble or fall apart, while proper cooling yields clean, neat squares.
Use a sharp knife and wipe between cuts.
A sharp knife slices cleanly through the fudgy texture, and wiping between cuts prevents dragging batter and messy edges.
Store brownies in an airtight container to keep moist.
Exposure to air dries out brownies quickly. Airtight storage preserves moisture and flavor for several days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overbaking the brownies

Why it happens Many assume brownies should be fully set and dry before removing from oven, leading to overbaking.

Fix Check the brownies at the minimum baking time with a toothpick. Remove when it has moist crumbs, not wet batter. This keeps the center fudgy and prevents dryness.

Using salted butter

Why it happens Some cooks use salted butter by default, thinking it won’t affect the recipe.

Fix Use unsalted butter to control salt levels. Salted butter can alter the flavor balance and texture, sometimes making brownies too salty or changing the way sugar dissolves.

Overmixing after adding flour

Why it happens Eager cooks often mix vigorously to ensure no lumps, but this activates gluten.

Fix Fold flour in gently until just combined. This keeps the brownies tender and prevents toughness.

Skipping the parchment paper or greasing

Why it happens To save time, some skip this step and risk sticking.

Fix Always grease or line your pan. It ensures easy removal and clean edges.

Cutting brownies before cooling

Why it happens Impatience leads to cutting warm brownies that fall apart.

Fix Let brownies cool completely in the pan to set the structure before slicing for neat squares.

Variations

Nutty Fudge Brownies

Add 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter for a crunchy texture and nutty flavor. This adds a nice contrast to the fudgy base and makes the brownies more satisfying.

Note: Fold nuts in with the flour to distribute evenly and avoid sinking during baking.

Espresso-Infused Brownies

Mix 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder into the cocoa powder for a subtle coffee kick that intensifies the chocolate flavor. Perfect for coffee lovers!

Note: Dissolve espresso powder in melted butter before mixing for even flavor distribution.

Peanut Butter Swirl Brownies

Drop spoonfuls of peanut butter over the batter and swirl gently with a knife before baking. This creates pockets of creamy peanut flavor throughout the brownies.

Note: Swirl just enough to create marbled effect; overmixing will blend flavors and lose the swirl look.

Gluten-Free Brownies

Substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend. These brownies remain fudgy and delicious without gluten, great for those with sensitivities.

Note: Ensure the gluten-free blend contains xanthan gum for structure and check baking time as it might vary slightly.

Vegan Brownies

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon flaxseed meal + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use coconut oil instead of butter. These brownies still come out moist and rich, perfect for a vegan diet.

Note: Mix flax eggs well and let them thicken before adding to batter to mimic egg binding properties.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Brownies can safely be kept at room temperature for up to 2 days if stored in an airtight container. Keep them covered to prevent drying out and maintain their fudgy texture.

Refrigerator

Store brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Refrigeration can make them firmer; let them come to room temperature before serving or warm briefly for that fresh-baked feel.

Freezer

Freeze brownies for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe bag to avoid freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature before serving.

Reheating

Warm brownies in a microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes to revive their fudgy softness. Adding a small splash of milk or a scoop of ice cream enhances the experience. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it refrigerated in the pan, covered tightly with plastic wrap. Bake fresh the next day for best texture and flavor. Alternatively, bake and freeze brownies in advance, then thaw and warm when ready to serve.

FAQ

Can I use brown sugar instead of granulated sugar?
Brown sugar adds moisture and a slight caramel flavor, which can make brownies more cakey and less fudgy. If you prefer a chewier texture, you can substitute half the granulated sugar with brown sugar, but for classic fudgy brownies, stick to granulated sugar.
Why are my brownies cakey instead of fudgy?
Cakey brownies often result from overmixing, too much flour, or overbaking. Make sure to fold the flour gently and bake just until the center has moist crumbs. Using melted butter and avoiding extra leavening agents also helps keep them dense and fudgy.
Can I make these brownies gluten-free?
Yes! Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend that contains xanthan gum for structure. Baking time might vary slightly, so start checking a few minutes earlier to avoid overbaking.
How do I store leftover brownies?
Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze them wrapped tightly for up to 3 months. Always bring refrigerated or frozen brownies back to room temperature before serving for best texture.
Can I add nuts or chocolate chips?
Absolutely! Fold in about 1 cup of chopped nuts or chocolate chips with the flour for added texture and flavor. They add a nice crunch and bursts of extra chocolate without changing the baking time much.
Why do my brownies stick to the pan?
Not greasing or lining the pan properly is the usual culprit. Use parchment paper or generously grease the pan to ensure easy release. Letting the brownies cool fully before removing also helps prevent sticking.
Can I use salted butter?
You can, but it may affect the flavor balance by adding extra saltiness. If you use salted butter, omit any additional salt in the recipe and be cautious about adding salty mix-ins.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Insert a toothpick at the 20-minute mark; it should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter. The edges will look set and may slightly pull away from the pan. Overbaking leads to dry brownies, so err on the side of slightly underbaking.

What You'll Need

Approximately 9 large brownies, 9 servings

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper. This step ensures your brownies don’t stick and come out cleanly. Don’t skip greasing or lining, or you’ll struggle to remove the brownies without breaking them apart.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased or parchment paper is flat with no wrinkles
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, combine the melted butter, granulated sugar, and unsweetened cocoa powder. Stir until smooth and glossy. This blend is the foundation for fudgy brownies because the melted butter coats the sugar and cocoa, helping create a dense, moist texture once baked.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is glossy and uniform in color without lumps
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. This helps incorporate air into the batter, balancing the fudginess with a slight lift. Be careful not to overbeat here; just mix until combined.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and slightly thickened, no egg streaks
Step 4
Sift the all-purpose flour over the batter, then gently fold it in using a spatula. Folding preserves the air you just incorporated and prevents overworking the gluten, which can make brownies tough. Stop mixing once no flour streaks remain.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is thick but smooth, no visible flour patches
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly with a spatula. Make sure to reach the edges so the brownies bake uniformly. Uneven batter can cause some parts to overcook while others stay underdone.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter layer is even and smooth across the pan
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes. The trick is not to overbake; you want a slightly soft center that sets as it cools. Check at 20 minutes by inserting a toothpick — it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter.
⏱ Time: 20-25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges pull slightly away from pan; toothpick with moist crumbs
Step 7
Remove the pan from the oven and let the brownies cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling allows the fudgy center to firm up, making cutting cleaner and the texture perfect. Cutting too soon causes crumbling.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownies are firm to the touch but still moist inside
Step 8
Once cooled, use a sharp knife to cut into squares. Wipe the knife clean between cuts for neat edges. If you want gooier brownies, cut slightly larger pieces; for more balanced bites, smaller squares work well.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean, straight cuts without crumbling
Step 9
Serve as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat. These brownies also freeze well if you want to save some for later. Just thaw at room temperature and enjoy!
⏱ Time: Optional serving time
✓ Doneness: Brownies maintain fudgy texture when served or reheated
