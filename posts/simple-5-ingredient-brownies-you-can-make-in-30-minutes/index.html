Easy Five-Ingredient Fudgy Brownies You Can Whip Up Fast

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
10 min prep 20 min cook 9 servings

These brownies are the perfect quick fix when you need a chocolate hit. Made with just five simple ingredients, they’re rich, fudgy, and come together in no time. Great for last-minute dessert cravings!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this brownie recipe on a hectic weeknight when all I wanted was a quick chocolate fix without a complicated list of ingredients. What I love most is how it requires just five common pantry staples but delivers that rich, fudgy texture that usually takes way more fuss. I’ve tried countless brownie recipes before, but many ended up cakey or dry, so I was thrilled when this one came out perfectly gooey every time. The secret? Melting the butter first and mixing it with sugar and cocoa powder before adding eggs — it creates a smooth, glossy batter that bakes into fudgy perfection. These brownies have become my go-to when I want something fast yet indulgent. Plus, they’re so simple kids can help make them! When they come out of the oven, they have that crackly top and moist center that just melts in your mouth. Honestly, it’s like magic in a pan, and I can’t wait for you to try it yourself.

Why This Works

The magic of these brownies lies in balancing fat, sugar, and flour to achieve that perfect fudgy texture. Melting the butter first allows it to fully coat the sugar and cocoa powder, which helps dissolve the sugar and create a smooth, glossy batter. This step is crucial because it influences the crumb of the finished brownies, making them dense and moist rather than cakey.

Adding eggs one at a time and beating gently introduces just enough air to lighten the batter slightly without making it fluffy. Overmixing at this stage can develop gluten in the flour and lead to tougher brownies, so folding in the flour carefully maintains tenderness. The all-purpose flour provides structure but not so much as to dry the brownies out.

Baking time is another key factor. Baking just until the edges set and the center still has moist crumbs ensures the brownies remain fudgy. The Maillard reaction during baking creates the crackly top that adds a delightful texture contrast. Cooling fully before slicing allows the brownies to finish setting, preventing crumbling and ensuring clean cuts.

Overall, this recipe is a perfect example of how simple ingredient interactions and careful technique combine to create a rich, indulgent dessert that feels homemade yet effortlessly delicious.

  • Choose good quality unsalted butter for richer flavor and better control over salt content. Melting it before mixing helps incorporate fat evenly, resulting in moist, fudgy brownies. Avoid using margarine or salted butter, as they can affect texture and taste.
  • Regular white sugar dissolves well when mixed with melted butter, aiding in the glossy batter texture. It also contributes to the crackly top crust through caramelization during baking. Avoid substituting with brown sugar here, as it can make the texture cakier.
  • Use natural unsweetened cocoa powder for a deep chocolate flavor and proper acidity balance. Dutch-processed cocoa changes pH and can affect the batter’s rise and texture, so stick to natural for best results.
  • Eggs provide moisture, structure, and richness. Adding them one at a time and mixing gently helps maintain a tender crumb. Using room temperature eggs is best to ensure even mixing and avoid seizing the batter.
  • All-purpose flour offers the right protein content to give structure without making the brownies tough. Sifting or folding it in gently helps prevent lumps and overdevelopment of gluten, which keeps the brownies tender and fudgy.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper. This step ensures your brownies don’t stick and come out cleanly. Don’t skip greasing or lining, or you’ll struggle to remove the brownies without breaking them apart.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Pan is evenly greased or parchment paper is flat with no wrinkles
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the melted butter, granulated sugar, and unsweetened cocoa powder. Stir until smooth and glossy. This blend is the foundation for fudgy brownies because the melted butter coats the sugar and cocoa, helping create a dense, moist texture once baked.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is glossy and uniform in color without lumps
  3. Step 3

    Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. This helps incorporate air into the batter, balancing the fudginess with a slight lift. Be careful not to overbeat here; just mix until combined.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is smooth and slightly thickened, no egg streaks
  4. Step 4

    Sift the all-purpose flour over the batter, then gently fold it in using a spatula. Folding preserves the air you just incorporated and prevents overworking the gluten, which can make brownies tough. Stop mixing once no flour streaks remain.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is thick but smooth, no visible flour patches
  5. Step 5

    Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly with a spatula. Make sure to reach the edges so the brownies bake uniformly. Uneven batter can cause some parts to overcook while others stay underdone.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter layer is even and smooth across the pan
  6. Step 6

    Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes. The trick is not to overbake; you want a slightly soft center that sets as it cools. Check at 20 minutes by inserting a toothpick — it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter.

    ⏱ 20-25 minutes✓ Edges pull slightly away from pan; toothpick with moist crumbs
  7. Step 7

    Remove the pan from the oven and let the brownies cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling allows the fudgy center to firm up, making cutting cleaner and the texture perfect. Cutting too soon causes crumbling.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Brownies are firm to the touch but still moist inside
  8. Step 8

    Once cooled, use a sharp knife to cut into squares. Wipe the knife clean between cuts for neat edges. If you want gooier brownies, cut slightly larger pieces; for more balanced bites, smaller squares work well.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Clean, straight cuts without crumbling
  9. Step 9

    Serve as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat. These brownies also freeze well if you want to save some for later. Just thaw at room temperature and enjoy!

    ⏱ Optional serving time✓ Brownies maintain fudgy texture when served or reheated

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature eggs for better mixing. — Room temperature eggs blend more easily into the batter, preventing lumps and ensuring a smooth texture. Cold eggs can cause the butter to seize or the batter to curdle, which affects the final crumb of the brownies.
  • Don’t overmix after adding flour. — Overmixing flour activates gluten, which can make brownies tough and cakey instead of fudgy. Gently folding flour until just combined keeps the texture tender and moist.
  • Line your baking pan with parchment paper for easy removal. — Parchment paper prevents sticking and allows you to lift the entire batch out for cleaner cuts. It also protects the brownies’ edges from overbaking.
  • Check brownies early to avoid overbaking. — Ovens vary, so start checking at 20 minutes. Overbaking dries out brownies, losing that fudgy center. Look for moist crumbs on a toothpick, not wet batter.
  • Cool brownies completely before slicing. — Cooling lets the brownies set and firm up. Cutting too soon causes them to crumble or fall apart, while proper cooling yields clean, neat squares.
  • Use a sharp knife and wipe between cuts. — A sharp knife slices cleanly through the fudgy texture, and wiping between cuts prevents dragging batter and messy edges.
  • Store brownies in an airtight container to keep moist. — Exposure to air dries out brownies quickly. Airtight storage preserves moisture and flavor for several days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overbaking the brownies
    Why: Many assume brownies should be fully set and dry before removing from oven, leading to overbaking.
    Fix: Check the brownies at the minimum baking time with a toothpick. Remove when it has moist crumbs, not wet batter. This keeps the center fudgy and prevents dryness.
  • Using salted butter
    Why: Some cooks use salted butter by default, thinking it won’t affect the recipe.
    Fix: Use unsalted butter to control salt levels. Salted butter can alter the flavor balance and texture, sometimes making brownies too salty or changing the way sugar dissolves.
  • Overmixing after adding flour
    Why: Eager cooks often mix vigorously to ensure no lumps, but this activates gluten.
    Fix: Fold flour in gently until just combined. This keeps the brownies tender and prevents toughness.
  • Skipping the parchment paper or greasing
    Why: To save time, some skip this step and risk sticking.
    Fix: Always grease or line your pan. It ensures easy removal and clean edges.
  • Cutting brownies before cooling
    Why: Impatience leads to cutting warm brownies that fall apart.
    Fix: Let brownies cool completely in the pan to set the structure before slicing for neat squares.

Variations

  • Nutty Fudge Brownies: Add 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter for a crunchy texture and nutty flavor. This adds a nice contrast to the fudgy base and makes the brownies more satisfying.
    Fold nuts in with the flour to distribute evenly and avoid sinking during baking.
  • Espresso-Infused Brownies: Mix 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder into the cocoa powder for a subtle coffee kick that intensifies the chocolate flavor. Perfect for coffee lovers!
    Dissolve espresso powder in melted butter before mixing for even flavor distribution.
  • Peanut Butter Swirl Brownies: Drop spoonfuls of peanut butter over the batter and swirl gently with a knife before baking. This creates pockets of creamy peanut flavor throughout the brownies.
    Swirl just enough to create marbled effect; overmixing will blend flavors and lose the swirl look.
  • Gluten-Free Brownies: Substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend. These brownies remain fudgy and delicious without gluten, great for those with sensitivities.
    Ensure the gluten-free blend contains xanthan gum for structure and check baking time as it might vary slightly.
  • Vegan Brownies: Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon flaxseed meal + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use coconut oil instead of butter. These brownies still come out moist and rich, perfect for a vegan diet.
    Mix flax eggs well and let them thicken before adding to batter to mimic egg binding properties.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Brownies can safely be kept at room temperature for up to 2 days if stored in an airtight container. Keep them covered to prevent drying out and maintain their fudgy texture.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Refrigeration can make them firmer; let them come to room temperature before serving or warm briefly for that fresh-baked feel.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze brownies for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe bag to avoid freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature before serving.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm brownies in a microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes to revive their fudgy softness. Adding a small splash of milk or a scoop of ice cream enhances the experience. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it refrigerated in the pan, covered tightly with plastic wrap. Bake fresh the next day for best texture and flavor. Alternatively, bake and freeze brownies in advance, then thaw and warm when ready to serve.

FAQ

Can I use brown sugar instead of granulated sugar?
Brown sugar adds moisture and a slight caramel flavor, which can make brownies more cakey and less fudgy. If you prefer a chewier texture, you can substitute half the granulated sugar with brown sugar, but for classic fudgy brownies, stick to granulated sugar.
Why are my brownies cakey instead of fudgy?
Cakey brownies often result from overmixing, too much flour, or overbaking. Make sure to fold the flour gently and bake just until the center has moist crumbs. Using melted butter and avoiding extra leavening agents also helps keep them dense and fudgy.
Can I make these brownies gluten-free?
Yes! Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend that contains xanthan gum for structure. Baking time might vary slightly, so start checking a few minutes earlier to avoid overbaking.
How do I store leftover brownies?
Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze them wrapped tightly for up to 3 months. Always bring refrigerated or frozen brownies back to room temperature before serving for best texture.
Can I add nuts or chocolate chips?
Absolutely! Fold in about 1 cup of chopped nuts or chocolate chips with the flour for added texture and flavor. They add a nice crunch and bursts of extra chocolate without changing the baking time much.
Why do my brownies stick to the pan?
Not greasing or lining the pan properly is the usual culprit. Use parchment paper or generously grease the pan to ensure easy release. Letting the brownies cool fully before removing also helps prevent sticking.
Can I use salted butter?
You can, but it may affect the flavor balance by adding extra saltiness. If you use salted butter, omit any additional salt in the recipe and be cautious about adding salty mix-ins.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Insert a toothpick at the 20-minute mark; it should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter. The edges will look set and may slightly pull away from the pan. Overbaking leads to dry brownies, so err on the side of slightly underbaking.
