Creamy Garlic Chicken That’s Perfect for Busy Weeknights

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 16, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy garlic chicken is a lifesaver on hectic evenings. The rich garlic sauce thickens quickly, giving you comfort food without the wait. I love serving it over fluffy rice or pasta for an easy dinner.

Pro Tips

Always pound chicken breasts to an even thickness before cooking.
This ensures the chicken cooks evenly, preventing dry edges and undercooked centers. Uneven thickness often causes uneven cooking times, which can ruin the texture and juiciness of the meat.
Don’t rush the searing step; let the chicken develop a golden crust.
The Maillard reaction creates deep, savory flavors and appealing texture. Moving the chicken too soon or over-crowding the pan causes steaming instead of searing, resulting in bland, rubbery meat.
Use fresh garlic and add it at the right time to avoid bitterness.
Fresh garlic provides vibrant flavor, but it burns quickly. Adding it after the chicken is cooked and stirring constantly prevents it from burning and turning bitter, which can spoil the sauce.
Simmer the sauce gently; avoid boiling cream to prevent curdling.
High heat breaks the emulsion of cream and cheese, causing the sauce to separate. A gentle simmer thickens the sauce smoothly while preserving its creamy texture.
Deglaze the pan with broth to capture all the browned flavor bits.
Browned bits stuck to the pan contain concentrated flavor. Deglazing dissolves them into the sauce, adding depth and richness you wouldn’t get otherwise.
Let the chicken rest briefly after cooking before adding to sauce.
Resting allows juices to redistribute inside the meat, keeping it juicy. Adding hot chicken immediately to sauce without resting can cause it to dry out or lose texture.
Use freshly grated Parmesan for best flavor and melting quality.
Pre-grated Parmesan often contains anti-caking agents that prevent smooth melting and can alter flavor. Fresh cheese melts better and adds a more authentic savory note to the sauce.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the pounding step leading to uneven cooking

Why it happens Many cooks assume chicken breasts cook evenly as is, but natural thickness variation causes uneven cooking times.

Fix Use a meat mallet or rolling pin to gently pound breasts to uniform thickness about 1/2 inch. This ensures faster, even cooking and juicy results.

Burning the garlic during sauce preparation

Why it happens Garlic cooks very fast and is easy to overcook if not watched closely.

Fix Add garlic after chicken is removed, cook on medium heat stirring constantly for 1-2 minutes until fragrant but not browned.

Boiling the cream, causing sauce to break

Why it happens High heat causes dairy proteins to separate and curdle.

Fix Simmer sauce gently on medium-low heat, stirring occasionally to keep it smooth and creamy.

Overcrowding the pan during searing

Why it happens Too much chicken in the pan lowers temperature, causing steaming instead of browning.

Fix Cook chicken in batches if needed, allowing space for proper searing and crust development.

Not letting chicken rest after cooking

Why it happens Cooked meat releases juices if cut or moved too early, drying out the meat.

Fix Rest chicken 5 minutes off heat before slicing or adding to sauce to retain moisture.

Variations

Spicy Creamy Garlic Chicken

Add 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes with the garlic for a spicy kick. You can also stir in a teaspoon of smoked paprika for smoky depth. This variation adds warmth without overpowering the creamy sauce.

Note: Spices should be added early with the garlic to bloom their flavors in the oil before adding liquids.

Mushroom Creamy Garlic Chicken

Sauté 1 cup sliced mushrooms after removing the chicken, then proceed with garlic and sauce. Mushrooms add earthiness and texture, making the dish heartier and perfect for cooler nights.

Note: Cook mushrooms until browned and their moisture evaporates to avoid watery sauce.

Lemon Herb Creamy Garlic Chicken

Add a tablespoon of fresh lemon juice and zest to the sauce for brightness. Finish with fresh thyme or rosemary instead of dried herbs for a fresh herbal note that complements garlic beautifully.

Note: Add lemon juice off heat to preserve its fresh flavor and prevent bitterness.

Dairy-Free Creamy Garlic Chicken

Use coconut cream instead of heavy cream and nutritional yeast in place of Parmesan. This keeps the sauce creamy and flavorful while accommodating dairy-free diets.

Note: Coconut cream has a different melting point and flavor; simmer gently and adjust seasoning accordingly.

Creamy Garlic Chicken with Spinach

Stir in 2 cups fresh spinach leaves during the last 2 minutes of simmering. The spinach wilts into the sauce, adding color, nutrients, and a slight earthiness that balances the richness.

Note: Add spinach at the end to keep it tender and vibrant rather than overcooked.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This creamy garlic chicken can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to keep it safe.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The sauce may thicken and chicken may firm up slightly when cold; reheat gently to restore creaminess.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap tightly with plastic wrap then foil. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat over low heat in a skillet or microwave in short bursts. Add a splash of broth or cream to loosen sauce if it thickens too much. Avoid high heat which can cause the cream to separate.

Make Ahead

You can cook the chicken breasts and prepare the sauce separately up to 1-2 days ahead. Store chicken and sauce in separate airtight containers. Reheat sauce gently and combine with warmed chicken right before serving.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. Adjust cooking time slightly—thighs may need a bit longer to cook through. Pounding isn’t necessary but you can flatten them for even cooking.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
For a lighter option, use half-and-half or whole milk with a tablespoon of flour to thicken, but the sauce won’t be as rich or thick. Coconut cream works for dairy-free but changes the flavor profile.
How do I prevent the sauce from separating?
Simmer the sauce gently over medium-low heat and avoid boiling. Stir frequently to keep the cream and cheese emulsified. Adding cheese slowly and off high heat helps maintain a smooth texture.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
Yes! Cook the chicken and sauce separately, store them chilled, and combine when reheating. This helps maintain texture and flavor without the sauce becoming too thick or the chicken drying out.
Is it okay to use pre-minced garlic?
Fresh minced garlic always tastes better, but if you use pre-minced, add it later in the cooking process and watch closely to avoid bitterness. The flavor can be milder and less vibrant.
What sides go best with creamy garlic chicken?
Rice, pasta, mashed potatoes, or crusty bread are perfect for soaking up the sauce. Steamed veggies or a fresh salad add balance and freshness to the meal.
How do I know when the chicken is cooked without a thermometer?
Cut into the thickest part; the meat should be white with clear juices. If it’s pink or juices are red, it needs more time. The chicken should feel firm but springy when pressed.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! This recipe is naturally gluten-free if you use gluten-free chicken broth. Avoid any added thickeners containing gluten and check labels on Parmesan cheese.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by patting the chicken breasts dry and pounding them to an even thickness of about 1/2 inch. This step ensures they cook evenly and stay juicy. Season both sides with salt and black pepper. Getting the chicken dry before seasoning helps the olive oil crisp the surface instead of steaming it, which is key for good texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken breasts should be evenly flattened and seasoned well
Step 2
Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add the chicken breasts and cook without moving them for about 5-6 minutes on one side until golden brown. This sear locks in moisture and creates flavor through the Maillard reaction. Avoid flipping too early or you’ll tear the meat and lose that crust.
⏱ Time: 6 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown crust forms on the cooked side, chicken releases easily from pan
Step 3
Flip the chicken and cook the other side for 4-5 minutes until cooked through. The internal temperature should reach 165°F. If you rush this, the chicken might be undercooked or rubbery, so patience is key. Once cooked, remove chicken from the pan and set aside to rest — this keeps juices locked in.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is cooked through, firm but not tough when pressed
Step 4
Reduce heat to medium and add minced garlic to the same pan. Stir frequently for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant but not browned. Garlic burns quickly and turns bitter, so keep an eye on it and stir constantly to release its sweet aroma.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Garlic is golden and fragrant, not dark or burnt
Step 5
Pour in the chicken broth and stir to deglaze the pan, scraping up all those tasty browned bits. This step lifts all the flavor stuck to the bottom and incorporates it into the sauce, giving it depth and richness.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Broth loosens pan residue, sauce begins to simmer gently
Step 6
Lower heat to medium-low and stir in the heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, and dried Italian herbs. Let the sauce simmer gently for about 5 minutes so it thickens slightly. Don’t boil vigorously or the cream might separate. Stir occasionally to keep it smooth.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce thickens, coats the back of a spoon, and is creamy
Step 7
Return the chicken breasts to the pan, spooning sauce over them. Let them heat in the sauce for 2-3 minutes so the flavors meld and the chicken stays warm. This final step ensures every bite is juicy and saucy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is warmed through and coated in creamy sauce
Step 8
Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning with extra salt or pepper if needed. A quick taste test is important because the broth and cheese add saltiness, so you want a balanced flavor without overdoing it.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Sauce is well-seasoned, flavorful but not overpowering
Step 9
Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and fresh flavor. Serve immediately over rice, pasta, or your favorite side. This final touch brightens the dish and adds a subtle herbal note.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dish looks inviting with fresh green garnish
