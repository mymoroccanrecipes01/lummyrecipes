Classic Easter Lamb Cake with Tender Sponge and Buttercream Frosting

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
30 min prep 35 min cook 8 servings

This Easter lamb cake recipe offers a tender, moist sponge shaped into a charming lamb and generously frosted with creamy buttercream. It captures the nostalgic feel of spring gatherings while balancing light sweetness and a soft texture that’s satisfying without being heavy. The cake is ideal for family celebrations and seasonal brunches.

Introduction

Whenever spring approaches, I find myself drawn to this traditional Easter lamb cake that my grandmother used to prepare. The process of baking the sponge in a lamb-shaped mold always brings a sense of nostalgia, and the soft, warm aroma of vanilla and butter filling the kitchen is a comforting reminder of family gatherings. What distinguishes this version is the careful attention to the sponge texture—light yet moist—and the smooth buttercream frosting that isn’t overly sweet, allowing the cake’s subtle flavors to shine. It’s a recipe I’ve tested numerous times, adjusting ingredient proportions to achieve the ideal crumb and frosting consistency. This cake works well for Easter brunches or any springtime occasion where a tender, lightly sweetened dessert feels just right. Below, I’ll share the step-by-step method that consistently delivers a tender crumb and well-balanced frosting.

Why This Works

The success of this Easter lamb cake lies in the balance between the sponge's structure and the buttercream's creaminess. The sponge relies on whipped eggs and sugar to incorporate air, which acts as a natural leavening agent, creating a light crumb. Sifting the flour and baking powder ensures even distribution, preventing dense pockets. Adding warm milk and melted butter at the right temperature helps maintain moisture without deflating the batter.

Buttercream frosting complements the cake by adding richness and a smooth texture. Creaming the butter thoroughly before adding powdered sugar creates tiny air pockets, resulting in a light frosting. The milk adjusts the consistency for spreading, while the vanilla extract enhances the aroma and flavor depth. Careful temperature control throughout ensures the cake bakes evenly, and the frosting holds shape without becoming greasy or runny.

  • Eggs provide structure and leavening by trapping air during beating. Using eggs at room temperature allows them to whip more effectively, leading to a finer crumb and better rise. Cold eggs do not incorporate air as well, resulting in denser cake.
  • All-purpose flour provides the necessary gluten structure to hold the cake together. Sifting it with baking powder prevents clumps and ensures even rising. Overmixing after adding flour can develop too much gluten, making the cake tough, so gentle folding is key.
  • Butter adds flavor and tenderness. Melting it and adding it warm but not hot helps blend it smoothly into the batter without cooking the eggs prematurely. In the frosting, softened butter whipped fully traps air, giving a light texture rather than a greasy mouthfeel.
  • Using sifted powdered sugar in the frosting prevents graininess and helps achieve a silky smooth texture. It also dissolves easily when mixed with butter and milk, creating a consistent spread.
  • Warm milk in the batter helps dissolve sugar and flour evenly, contributing to a moist crumb. In the frosting, milk thins the mixture to a spreadable consistency and balances sweetness.

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease the lamb-shaped cake mold thoroughly with butter, then dust lightly with flour to prevent sticking. Set aside.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Mold is evenly greased and floured with no excess powder remaining
  2. Step 2

    In a large bowl, beat the eggs and granulated sugar together using an electric mixer on high speed until the mixture is pale, thick, and triples in volume—about 5-7 minutes. The batter should form ribbons when the beaters are lifted.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Mixture is pale yellow, thick, and holds its shape briefly when lifted
  3. Step 3

    In a separate bowl, sift together flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the egg mixture in three additions using a spatula, employing gentle folding motions to preserve air. Avoid overmixing.

    ⏱ 3-5 minutes✓ Batter is smooth with no visible flour pockets, light and airy texture maintained
  4. Step 4

    Warm the milk gently and stir in the melted butter along with the vanilla extract. Slowly pour this liquid mixture into the batter, folding carefully until just combined. The batter should remain light and slightly glossy.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes✓ Batter is homogeneous with a slightly fluid consistency but still aerated
  5. Step 5

    Pour the batter into the prepared lamb mold, smoothing the surface evenly with a spatula. Tap the mold lightly on the counter to release any large air bubbles.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter fills mold evenly without large bubbles or gaps
  6. Step 6

    Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick into the thickest part of the cake; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. The cake surface should be lightly golden and spring back gently when touched.

    ⏱ 30-35 minutes✓ Toothpick inserted comes out clean, cake edges lightly golden, surface springs back to touch
  7. Step 7

    Allow the cake to cool in the mold for 10-15 minutes before gently unmolding onto a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling completely prevents the buttercream from melting when applied.

    ⏱ 30-45 minutes cooling✓ Cake is fully cooled to room temperature, firm but tender to touch
  8. Step 8

    To prepare the buttercream, beat the softened butter on medium speed until creamy and pale, about 3-5 minutes. Gradually add sifted powdered sugar in batches, mixing well after each addition. Add vanilla extract and milk, adjusting for desired consistency. Beat on high for a final 2-3 minutes to incorporate air.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Buttercream is smooth, fluffy, and spreads easily without being runny
  9. Step 9

    Frost the cooled lamb cake evenly using a spatula or piping bag to create the desired texture and detail. Add any optional decorations or coloring at this point. Chill briefly to set the frosting if needed.

    ⏱ 15-20 minutes✓ Frosting holds shape, surface is matte and smooth without melting or separation

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature eggs for batter preparation. — Room temperature eggs whip up more easily and trap more air, creating a lighter, more aerated batter which translates into a tender crumb.
  • Sift dry ingredients before adding to the batter. — Sifting eliminates lumps and evenly distributes baking powder, ensuring an even rise and a fine crumb texture without dense spots.
  • Avoid overmixing once flour is added. — Overmixing develops gluten, which makes the cake tough rather than soft. Gentle folding preserves air bubbles essential for a light texture.
  • Cool the cake completely before frosting. — Applying buttercream on a warm cake causes the frosting to melt and become runny, affecting the appearance and texture.
  • Use a light hand when folding liquid ingredients into the batter. — Incorporating melted butter and milk gently prevents deflating the whipped eggs, maintaining the batter’s volume and resulting in a moist, tender cake.
  • Grease and flour the mold thoroughly. — This prevents sticking and allows for a clean release that preserves the lamb’s intricate shape and detail.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold eggs straight from the fridge.
    Why: Cold eggs do not whip as efficiently, resulting in less volume and a denser, heavier cake.
    Fix: Let eggs sit at room temperature for 30 minutes before beating.
  • Overmixing batter after adding flour.
    Why: Mixing too vigorously develops gluten, making the cake tough and chewy instead of tender.
    Fix: Fold dry ingredients gently with a spatula just until combined.
  • Applying frosting on a warm cake.
    Why: Warm cake melts the buttercream, causing it to become runny and lose structure.
    Fix: Ensure the cake is completely cool before frosting.
  • Not greasing and flouring the mold properly.
    Why: Insufficient preparation causes the cake to stick, damaging the shape when unmolding.
    Fix: Use softened butter and a light dusting of flour, tapping out excess before pouring batter.

Variations

  • Gluten-Free Lamb Cake: Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that contains xanthan gum. Baking powder should be gluten-free as well.
    Gluten-free flours behave differently and often require extra moisture; adding 1-2 tablespoons of yogurt or applesauce can help maintain tenderness.
  • Vegan Version: Use flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water per egg) and substitute dairy milk and butter with plant-based alternatives.
    Vegan butters and milks can alter texture slightly; careful mixing and slightly longer baking times may be necessary.
  • Lemon-Infused Buttercream: Add 1-2 teaspoons of freshly grated lemon zest and 1 tablespoon lemon juice to the buttercream for a fresh citrus note.
    Adding lemon juice thins the frosting; adjust powdered sugar quantity to maintain spreadable consistency.
  • Chocolate Buttercream Frosting: Stir in 2-3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder to the buttercream while beating for a rich chocolate flavor.
    Cocoa powder absorbs moisture; increase milk slightly to retain smooth texture.
  • Mini Lamb Cupcakes: Use a mini lamb mold or silicone cupcake liners and reduce baking time to 15-20 minutes. Frost individually for easier serving.
    Smaller portions bake faster; test doneness early to avoid overbaking and drying out.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store the cake covered at room temperature away from direct sunlight for up to 1 day. Exposure to air can dry the sponge and cause the frosting to harden.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Keep the cake in an airtight container or covered loosely with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 3 days. Cold storage firms up the buttercream, so allow the cake to come to room temperature before serving for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Wrap the unfrosted cake tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil, then freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight and frost once fully thawed. Freezing after frosting is not recommended as the buttercream texture may degrade.
  • ♨️ Reheating: This cake is best served at room temperature and is not typically reheated. If desired, gently warm a slice in the microwave for 10-15 seconds to soften.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Bake the sponge cake a day in advance and store wrapped at room temperature. Prepare the buttercream separately, then frost the cake on the day of serving to ensure the freshest texture and appearance.

FAQ

Can I make the Easter lamb cake ahead of time?
Yes, the sponge can be baked a day ahead and stored wrapped tightly. Frosting is best applied on the day you plan to serve to keep it fresh.
Why is my cake dense instead of light and fluffy?
This usually happens if the eggs were too cold or if the batter was overmixed after adding flour. Use room temperature eggs and fold the dry ingredients gently.
Can I substitute butter with oil in the sponge?
Butter is preferred for flavor and structure, but you can substitute with a neutral oil. The crumb will be more moist but less rich in flavor.
How long does the frosted cake last in the fridge?
When stored in an airtight container, the frosted cake keeps well for up to 3 days. Bring to room temperature before serving for best texture.
Can I use a different shape mold instead of lamb?
Absolutely. This recipe works well in most shaped cake molds, just adjust baking time depending on size and depth.
How can I prevent the frosting from becoming too soft?
Make sure the butter is properly softened but not melted before whipping. Refrigerate the frosted cake if your kitchen is warm to help the frosting set.
