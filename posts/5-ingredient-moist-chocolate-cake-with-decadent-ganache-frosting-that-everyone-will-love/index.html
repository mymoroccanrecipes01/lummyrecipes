Simple 5-Ingredient Chocolate Cake with Silky Ganache Frosting

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This chocolate cake is super easy but tastes like you spent hours on it. Just five ingredients and a luscious ganache make it moist and rich. Great for birthdays or any time you want a special treat without the fuss!

Introduction

I first made this chocolate cake on a whim when I needed a quick dessert for a last-minute gathering. I was honestly surprised how rich and moist it turned out with just five main ingredients. The real magic is in the ganache frosting—it’s so silky and smooth, it feels like a fancy bakery cake but without all the fuss. Over time, I tweaked the recipe to get the crumb tender but still sturdy enough to hold that luscious ganache. I’ve found melting the butter first and letting it cool before mixing makes the batter silky, which helps the cake stay moist. Plus, folding in the dry ingredients gently keeps it light. When it’s done, you get a shiny, rich chocolate cake that’s perfect for birthdays or any time you want a no-fail, impressive dessert. It’s become a go-to when I want something that looks special but is super easy to make. Honestly, I think this cake tastes better than some of the complicated ones I’ve tried. If you love chocolate and simplicity, you’ll enjoy making this as much as eating it!

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 2 large eggs
  • Ganache:
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 ounces bittersweet chocolate, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch round cake pan with butter or non-stick spray. Line the bottom with parchment paper to ensure the cake releases easily. This step is crucial because it prevents sticking, which can ruin the cake’s appearance and texture. Preparing the pan properly means your cake will come out clean and smooth, giving you that perfect presentation without tears or crumbling.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased with parchment paper securely fitted
Step 2
In a large bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, and sugar. Sifting removes lumps and blends the dry ingredients evenly, which helps the cake rise uniformly and results in a tender crumb. If you skip sifting, you might get small pockets of cocoa or flour that don’t mix well, leading to uneven texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are fully combined with no lumps
Step 3
In another bowl, whisk the eggs lightly, then add the melted butter once it has cooled to room temperature. The cooling is important because hot butter can cook the eggs prematurely, causing the batter to curdle. Combining eggs and butter creates a rich, smooth base that helps the cake stay moist and tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Eggs and butter mixture is smooth and slightly thickened
Step 4
Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, folding gently with a spatula until just combined. Overmixing can develop gluten in the flour, making the cake tough. Folding ensures you keep the batter light while evenly incorporating everything. Stop mixing as soon as you don’t see dry flour patches.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth, slightly thick, and uniform in color
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter a couple of times to release any air bubbles trapped inside. This helps the cake bake evenly without large holes or tunnels and ensures a consistent texture throughout.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread with a smooth surface
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking will dry the cake, while underbaking leaves it gooey in the middle. The right bake time creates a tender crumb that holds together well.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out with moist crumbs
Step 7
While the cake cools, prepare the ganache. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan over medium heat until it just begins to simmer—don’t let it boil or it might scorch. Pour the hot cream over chopped bittersweet chocolate in a heatproof bowl. Let it sit for a minute to soften the chocolate before stirring gently until smooth and glossy. This technique ensures a silky, shiny ganache.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ganache is smooth, glossy, and pourable
Step 8
Once the cake is completely cool, remove it from the pan and place it on a serving plate. Pour the ganache over the top, letting it drip down the sides naturally. Use a spatula to gently spread if needed, but keep it loose for that beautiful shine. Cooling the cake fully prevents the ganache from melting and sliding off.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ganache coats the cake evenly with a shiny finish
Step 9
Allow the ganache to set for at least 30 minutes at room temperature before slicing. This resting time lets the ganache firm up slightly while staying soft and creamy. Cutting the cake too soon can cause the ganache to smudge and the slices to look messy. When ready, use a sharp knife wiped clean between cuts for perfect slices.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ganache is set but still soft and shiny

Pro Tips

Always let melted butter cool before adding to eggs.
Hot butter can scramble the eggs, causing lumps and uneven batter texture. Cooling the butter prevents this and helps create a smooth, silky batter that bakes evenly with a tender crumb.
Sift dry ingredients together before mixing.
Sifting aerates the flour and cocoa powder, removing lumps and ensuring even distribution. This step helps the cake rise uniformly and produces a finer crumb structure without dense pockets.
Fold the batter gently instead of stirring vigorously.
Overmixing activates gluten, making the cake tough and dense. Folding preserves the air bubbles incorporated during mixing, resulting in a lighter, fluffier cake with tender texture.
Use room temperature eggs for better mixing.
Cold eggs don’t combine well with melted butter and dry ingredients, which can cause curdling or uneven texture. Room temperature eggs emulsify better, giving a smooth batter and consistent crumb.
Don’t rush cooling before frosting the cake.
Applying ganache to a warm cake causes it to melt and slide off, ruining the finish. Cool the cake completely so the ganache sets properly with a shiny, smooth surface.
Heat cream just to simmer, not boil, for ganache.
Boiling cream can scorch and change flavor, also causing the ganache to seize or become grainy. Gentle heating melts chocolate evenly for a glossy, silky finish.
Let ganache rest before slicing the cake.
Ganache firms slightly as it cools, making it easier to cut clean slices without smearing. This also enhances texture, keeping the frosting creamy but stable.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using hot melted butter directly on eggs

Why it happens Many assume melted butter can be added immediately, but hot butter cooks the eggs, causing lumps.

Fix Always let the melted butter cool to room temperature before mixing with eggs to ensure a smooth batter.

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens It’s tempting to stir vigorously to combine everything quickly, but this develops gluten.

Fix Fold the dry ingredients into wet gently until just combined to keep the cake tender and light.

Not sifting cocoa and flour

Why it happens Skipping sifting saves time but leads to lumps and uneven texture.

Fix Take the extra minute to sift dry ingredients together for a smoother, more uniform crumb.

Applying ganache on a warm cake

Why it happens Rushing to frost before cooling causes ganache to melt and slide off.

Fix Allow the cake to cool completely to room temperature before pouring ganache for a neat finish.

Boiling cream for ganache

Why it happens Overheating cream scorches it and can cause a grainy texture.

Fix Heat cream just until it simmers, then pour over chocolate and stir gently for a silky ganache.

Variations

Mocha Chocolate Cake

Add 1 tablespoon of instant espresso powder to the dry ingredients to enhance the chocolate flavor with a subtle coffee note. This gives the cake a deeper, more complex taste without overpowering the chocolate. The texture remains moist and tender.

Note: Be sure to dissolve espresso powder evenly to avoid gritty spots and maintain smooth batter consistency.

Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake

Swap all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend designed for baking. This makes the cake suitable for gluten-sensitive eaters while keeping the crumb tender. You might notice a slightly different texture but still rich and moist.

Note: Choose a blend with xanthan gum included for structure; otherwise, add about 1/4 teaspoon xanthan gum to the mix.

Vegan Chocolate Cake

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use a plant-based butter substitute. Use dairy-free chocolate and coconut cream for the ganache to keep it vegan-friendly. Expect a slightly denser but still delicious cake.

Note: Let flax eggs thicken for 5 minutes before mixing to ensure proper binding.

Chocolate Raspberry Cake

Fold fresh or frozen raspberries into the batter before baking or add a layer of raspberry jam between cake layers before ganache frosting. This adds a bright, fruity contrast to the rich chocolate and moist crumb.

Note: If using frozen raspberries, thaw and drain excess liquid to avoid soggy batter.

Nutty Chocolate Cake

Stir in 1/2 cup chopped toasted walnuts or pecans into the batter for added crunch and flavor complexity. Nuts complement the chocolate and add texture, making the cake more interesting and satisfying.

Note: Toast nuts lightly to enhance their flavor and reduce moisture before mixing.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cake can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered loosely with a cake dome or foil to prevent drying out. Avoid leaving it uncovered as the ganache can harden and the cake crumb can become stale.

Refrigerator

Store the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The ganache will firm up more when cold, so let the cake sit at room temperature for 20-30 minutes before serving for best texture and flavor.

Freezer

Freeze the cake tightly wrapped in plastic wrap and then foil for up to 2 months. To thaw, place it in the fridge overnight and bring to room temperature before serving to restore moistness and ganache softness.

Reheating

If desired, warm individual slices gently in the microwave for 10-15 seconds to soften the ganache and bring out flavor. Avoid overheating to prevent drying the cake or melting the ganache too much.

Make Ahead

Bake the cake layers up to 2 days ahead and keep wrapped tightly at room temperature. Prepare the ganache the day of serving for best shine and texture, then frost the cooled cake just before guests arrive.

FAQ

Can I use Dutch-processed cocoa instead of natural cocoa?
You can, but Dutch-processed cocoa is less acidic and may affect the cake’s rise slightly. Since this recipe doesn’t use baking soda or powder, the difference is minimal, but the flavor might be a bit milder. For best results, stick to natural unsweetened cocoa.
What if my ganache is too thick or too thin?
If ganache is too thick, gently warm it over a double boiler or microwave in short bursts and stir to loosen. If it’s too thin, let it cool longer at room temperature or refrigerate briefly; it will thicken as it cools. Adjust cream or chocolate ratio in future batches if needed.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes, substitute with a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend that includes xanthan gum. This helps maintain structure. The texture might be slightly different but still moist and delicious. Avoid almond or coconut flour alone as they behave differently in baking.
How do I know when the cake is fully baked?
Insert a toothpick into the center around 25 minutes. It should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter. If it’s still wet, bake a few minutes longer and check frequently to avoid overbaking and drying out the cake.
Can I use salted butter instead of unsalted?
Yes, but reduce or omit any added salt in the recipe. Unsalted butter allows better control over salt levels. Using salted butter may slightly alter the flavor balance but won’t ruin the cake.
What’s the best way to store leftover cake?
Keep leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerated up to 5 days. Let refrigerated cake warm to room temp before serving for best taste and texture.
Can I double the recipe to make a larger cake?
Yes, doubling works well. Use two 9-inch pans or a larger pan, but adjust baking time accordingly. Start checking doneness a few minutes earlier to avoid overbaking.
Is there a way to make this cake vegan?
You can replace eggs with flax eggs and use plant-based butter and dairy-free chocolate and cream for the ganache. The texture will be slightly different—denser but still tasty. Let flax eggs thicken before mixing to ensure proper binding.
