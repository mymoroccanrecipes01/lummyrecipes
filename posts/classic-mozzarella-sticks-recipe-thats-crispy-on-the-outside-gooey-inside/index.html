Crunchy Double-Breaded Mozzarella Sticks That Melt in Your Mouth

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 25, 2026
15 min Prep Time
10 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These mozzarella sticks are my favorite snack when I want gooey cheese with a crispy crust. The double breading trick makes all the difference. Great for movie nights or casual hangouts!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this double-breaded mozzarella sticks recipe years ago when I was craving a snack that was crunchy on the outside but delightfully melty on the inside. My first try was a total flop—the cheese leaked everywhere and the crust was soggy. But after experimenting with a double breading method, I finally nailed that perfect crispiness I’d been chasing. What makes this version special is that extra breading layer, which locks in the cheese and creates a golden, crunchy shell that holds up well to frying. I love serving these at casual get-togethers or movie nights because they’re easy to make but always impress. The smell of garlic and oregano in the breadcrumb mix adds a subtle flavor boost that keeps you coming back for more. When you bite into one, you get that satisfying crunch followed by a warm, gooey center — it’s pure comfort food magic. Honestly, once you try this, it’s hard to go back to the single-breading method. Give it a shot, and you’ll see why this recipe has become a family favorite in my house.

What You'll Need

Approximately 16 mozzarella sticks, 4 servings

  • 16 ounces mozzarella cheese sticks, cut in half
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups Italian seasoned breadcrumbs
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by cutting the mozzarella sticks in half if you want smaller bites. This helps them cook evenly and makes snacking easier. Set up a breading station with three shallow bowls: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with the seasoned breadcrumbs mixed with garlic powder and oregano. The flour helps the egg stick, and the egg helps the breadcrumbs adhere, so each layer is crucial for the final crunch.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Flour-coated cheese sticks look evenly dusted with no wet spots
Step 2
Dip each mozzarella stick first into the flour, shaking off excess, then into the eggs, making sure it’s fully coated. Next, roll it in the breadcrumbs until completely covered. For the double breading, repeat the egg and breadcrumb steps once more. This extra layer is what creates the thick, crispy crust that keeps the cheese from leaking out during frying.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sticks feel evenly coated with a thick breadcrumb layer, no bare spots
Step 3
Place the breaded sticks on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freeze them for at least 30 minutes. Freezing firms up the coating and the cheese, which helps prevent the cheese from melting too fast and leaking out during frying. Don’t skip this step—it’s key for that perfect gooey interior with a crispy exterior.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mozzarella sticks are firm to the touch and not sticky
Step 4
Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or fryer to 350°F (175°C). Use enough oil to fully submerge the sticks, so they cook evenly. If the oil is too cool, the sticks will absorb oil and become greasy; too hot, and the coating will burn before the cheese melts.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oil shimmers and a small breadcrumb sizzles immediately when dropped
Step 5
Carefully lower the frozen mozzarella sticks into the hot oil in batches, avoiding overcrowding which lowers the oil temperature. Fry for 1-2 minutes until golden brown and crispy. Keep a close eye because the cheese melts fast and can leak if overcooked.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sticks are golden brown and float to the surface
Step 6
Use a slotted spoon to remove the sticks from the oil and drain them on paper towels. This step removes excess oil, keeping your sticks crisp instead of greasy. Serve immediately while the cheese is still melty inside.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oil stops dripping and sticks feel crisp when touched
Step 7
For an extra touch, sprinkle some flaky sea salt or fresh herbs like parsley on top while still hot. This adds a nice flavor contrast and makes them look more inviting. It’s a simple step but makes a big difference.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salt or herbs stick to the warm crust
Step 8
Serve with your favorite marinara sauce or a spicy ranch dip. The acidity or creaminess of dips balances the rich, cheesy sticks perfectly. I like to offer a couple of options so everyone can choose their favorite.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dips are ready alongside the sticks for serving
Step 9
If you want to keep leftovers, cool the sticks completely before storing them in an airtight container in the fridge. To reheat, bake in a 375°F oven for 8-10 minutes to bring back the crisp. Avoid microwaving unless you want a soggy crust and rubbery cheese.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sticks are crisp and warm after reheating

Pro Tips

Freeze the breaded sticks before frying.
Freezing firms up the coating and the cheese inside, preventing the cheese from melting too quickly and leaking out during frying. It also helps the breading stay intact, resulting in a crispier final product.
Don’t overcrowd the fryer or pan.
Adding too many sticks at once lowers the oil temperature, causing greasy, soggy crusts. Fry in small batches so the oil stays hot and the sticks cook evenly and crisp up properly.
Use a thermometer to monitor oil temperature.
Maintaining the oil around 350°F ensures the crust crisps quickly while the cheese melts inside. Too low, and sticks absorb oil; too high, and the crust burns before the cheese melts.
Use Italian seasoned breadcrumbs for extra flavor.
The herbs and garlic in seasoned breadcrumbs add a subtle but delicious boost that plain crumbs lack. It complements the mozzarella’s mild flavor perfectly.
Serve immediately after frying.
Mozzarella sticks are best enjoyed hot so the cheese is gooey and the crust is crisp. Letting them sit too long causes the crust to soften and cheese to harden.
Add a pinch of flaky sea salt right after frying.
Salt enhances the flavor and balances the richness of the cheese. Adding it while still hot helps it stick to the crust.
Use low-moisture mozzarella sticks.
Low-moisture cheese melts well without releasing too much liquid, which keeps the crust from getting soggy or falling apart.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the freezing step.

Why it happens Some cooks rush to fry right after breading because they’re eager to eat, not realizing the importance of freezing.

Fix Always freeze breaded sticks for at least 30 minutes. This step solidifies the coating and cheese, preventing cheese from leaking out and ensuring a crispy crust.

Using fresh mozzarella instead of low-moisture.

Why it happens Fresh mozzarella is often preferred for its creaminess without knowing it’s too wet for frying.

Fix Choose low-moisture mozzarella sticks to avoid soggy breading and leaking cheese. It holds shape better and melts perfectly inside.

Overcrowding the frying pan.

Why it happens Trying to cook all sticks at once to save time lowers oil temperature.

Fix Fry in small batches so the oil stays hot. This gives an even, crispy crust and prevents greasy, soggy sticks.

Not using enough breading layers.

Why it happens Some think one coating is enough and skip the double breading.

Fix Do the double egg and breadcrumb coating for a thick, crunchy crust that seals in the cheese.

Microwaving leftovers to reheat.

Why it happens Microwaving is quick but often used out of convenience.

Fix Reheat in the oven at 375°F for 8-10 minutes to restore crispiness. Microwaving makes the crust soggy and cheese rubbery.

Variations

Spicy Mozzarella Sticks

Add cayenne pepper or chili powder to the breadcrumb mix for a spicy kick. Serve with a creamy ranch dip to balance the heat. This variation adds a fun twist for those who love a bit of spice.

Note: Adjust seasoning carefully; too much spice can overpower the cheese flavor.

Herb-Crusted Mozzarella Sticks

Mix fresh chopped herbs like parsley, basil, and thyme into the breadcrumbs for a fresh, fragrant crust. This adds brightness and elevates the flavor profile, making the sticks taste more sophisticated.

Note: Fresh herbs can add moisture; pat breadcrumbs dry before use to maintain crispiness.

Gluten-Free Mozzarella Sticks

Use gluten-free flour and gluten-free seasoned breadcrumbs to make this snack safe for gluten-sensitive eaters. The double breading method still works well, producing a crispy crust without gluten.

Note: Choose fine gluten-free flour and breadcrumbs for best texture.

Baked Mozzarella Sticks

For a lighter option, bake the breaded sticks at 400°F for 10-12 minutes until golden and crispy. They won’t be quite as crunchy as fried but still delicious and gooey inside.

Note: Spray sticks lightly with oil before baking to help browning.

Cheese Variety Mozzarella Sticks

Try mixing mozzarella with provolone or cheddar sticks for a different cheese flavor. The mix adds complexity and a sharper taste while keeping the melty texture.

Note: Cheddar melts differently; monitor frying time to prevent leaking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Mozzarella sticks should not be left out more than 2 hours at room temperature. Cover loosely with foil if serving at parties to keep them warm but avoid sogginess.

Refrigerator

Store cooled sticks in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The crust will soften when cold, so reheat in the oven to regain crispiness before serving.

Freezer

Freeze the breaded, un-fried sticks in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag for up to 2 months. This prevents freezer burn and preserves texture.

Reheating

Reheat in a preheated 375°F oven for 8-10 minutes. Avoid microwaving as it makes the crust soggy and cheese rubbery. Adding a splash of water to the baking tray can help keep cheese melty.

Make Ahead

Prepare and bread the sticks up to 1 day ahead, then freeze. Fry or bake on the day you plan to serve for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use fresh mozzarella instead of low-moisture mozzarella?
Fresh mozzarella has higher moisture content and is softer, which can cause the breading to slip off and the cheese to leak during frying. Low-moisture mozzarella sticks are firmer and hold their shape better, giving you that perfect melty center without a mess.
How do I prevent cheese from leaking out while frying?
The biggest help is double breading and freezing the sticks before frying. The extra coating creates a stronger barrier, and freezing firms the cheese, so it melts slowly and stays inside the crust.
Can I bake these instead of frying?
Yes! Baking is a healthier option. Just spray the breaded sticks lightly with oil and bake at 400°F for 10-12 minutes until golden. They won’t be as crispy as fried, but still tasty and melty inside.
What oil is best for frying mozzarella sticks?
Use a neutral oil with a high smoke point like vegetable, canola, or peanut oil. Olive oil has a lower smoke point and can burn, affecting flavor and crispiness.
How long can I store leftover mozzarella sticks?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven to restore crispiness. Avoid microwaving as it makes them soggy.
Can I make these gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use gluten-free flour and gluten-free breadcrumbs. The double breading method still works well to create a crispy crust.
What dipping sauces go well with mozzarella sticks?
Classic marinara is a favorite, but ranch, spicy mayo, or garlic aioli are great too. Offering a few options lets everyone enjoy their favorite flavor combo.
Why is my breading falling off?
This usually happens if you skip the flour or egg layers or don’t let the breaded sticks freeze before frying. Each layer helps the next stick properly, and freezing firms everything for frying.
