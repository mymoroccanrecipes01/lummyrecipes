No-Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Jars: Creamy, Fresh & Easy Dessert

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

This no-bake strawberry cheesecake in a jar is ultra creamy and fresh, with a delightful graham cracker crunch. It’s super easy to make and perfect for summer gatherings or a quick sweet treat anytime!

Introduction

I first made this no-bake strawberry cheesecake in a jar a couple of summers ago when I wanted something sweet but fuss-free for a picnic. It quickly became a favorite because it’s creamy, fresh, and you don’t need an oven. What sets this version apart is the crushed graham cracker base that adds a lovely crunchy texture to contrast the silky cream cheese filling. Plus, using jars makes it easy to serve and perfect for entertaining. One trick I learned is to chill the cream cheese and whip the cream separately before folding them together gently — this keeps the filling light and airy, not dense. The final result is a dessert that feels indulgent but fresh, with juicy strawberry bits adding a bright pop. It’s great for hot days when you want something cold and satisfying without turning on the oven. Honestly, the first time I tried to skip chilling the cream cheese, the filling was too stiff and not as smooth, so don't skip that step! Overall, this recipe is a keeper because it’s simple, looks cute in jars, and tastes like a little bite of summer magic.

Why This Works

This no-bake strawberry cheesecake succeeds because it balances texture, flavor, and temperature perfectly. The graham cracker crust provides a buttery, crunchy foundation that contrasts the creamy filling beautifully. Melting the butter and combining it well with the crumbs lets the crust hold together without baking, relying on the butter’s solidifying fats when chilled.

The filling’s smoothness comes from properly softened cream cheese that’s whipped to remove lumps and create a silky base. Adding powdered sugar sweetens without graininess, while vanilla deepens the flavor. Separately whipping the cream to stiff peaks introduces air, lightening the dense cream cheese and preventing a heavy, overly rich dessert. Folding the cream gently preserves this airy texture.

Fresh strawberries add natural sweetness and moisture, brightening the flavor and adding a juicy pop. Folding them in gently avoids breaking the fruit down too much, which would release excess liquid and risk a watery filling. Chilling the assembled jars allows the fats in butter and cream cheese to firm up, setting the layers without baking. This refrigeration step is crucial for texture and flavor melding, making the dessert refreshing and satisfying.

Overall, this recipe leverages simple ingredient chemistry and careful technique to create a no-bake dessert that feels indulgent yet fresh, easy yet impressive.

  • Look for finely crushed graham cracker crumbs for the best crust texture. Coarser crumbs won’t pack as well, and too fine may become mushy. The crumbs provide structure and a buttery, slightly sweet flavor that complements the creamy filling. Store crumbs in an airtight container to keep them crisp. You can substitute digestive biscuits or vanilla wafer crumbs if preferred, but graham crackers give the classic cheesecake flavor.
  • Using unsalted butter lets you control the salt level in the crust. Melted butter binds the crumbs together, and as it cools in the fridge, it solidifies to hold the crust in place without baking. Avoid using margarine or low-fat butter substitutes, as they won’t firm up properly and can make the crust soggy or crumbly.
  • Choose full-fat cream cheese for a rich, creamy filling. It should be softened to room temperature for smooth mixing and to prevent lumps. Low-fat or whipped cream cheese won’t provide the same texture or flavor. Properly softened cream cheese emulsifies with sugar and vanilla to create a silky base for the filling.
  • Chilled heavy cream whips into stiff peaks, adding lightness and volume to the dense cream cheese mixture. Don’t substitute with half-and-half or milk, as they lack fat content needed for whipping. Overwhipping can cause the cream to separate, so watch closely during this step.
  • Use ripe, fresh strawberries for the best flavor and juiciness. They provide natural sweetness and a fresh contrast to the rich filling. Avoid frozen or underripe berries, which can be watery or bland. Chop the strawberries evenly to distribute flavor and texture throughout the filling.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs, crushed
  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, chopped
  • Optional: fresh whole strawberries for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by preparing the crust. Combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and granulated sugar in a bowl. Mix well until the crumbs are evenly coated and sticky. This mixture forms the base of your cheesecake jars, providing that essential crunch and buttery flavor. Press about 2-3 tablespoons of the crust mixture firmly into the bottom of each jar to create a compact layer. Pressing firmly is key here; if it’s too loose, the crust won’t hold together when you eat it. Avoid overmixing the crumbs with butter or they can get soggy.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Crust layer is compacted and holds shape when pressed
  2. Step 2

    In a separate large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with an electric mixer until smooth and creamy, about 2-3 minutes. Make sure the cream cheese is at room temperature before starting to avoid lumps. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, then beat again until fully combined and silky. This step is important because it creates a smooth, sweet base for the cheesecake filling. Rushing this or using cold cream cheese will result in a lumpy texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling is smooth, creamy, and free of lumps
  3. Step 3

    In a chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the beaters, the cream holds a firm peak without collapsing. Whipping the cream separately creates a light and airy texture that will make the cheesecake filling fluffy and not dense. Be careful not to overwhip or it may turn grainy and start to separate.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Cream forms stiff peaks that hold shape
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use gentle folding motions to combine without deflating the whipped cream. This technique keeps the filling light and fluffy. Overmixing will cause the filling to become dense and heavy. Take your time and fold until no white streaks remain.

    ⏱ 3-5 minutes✓ Filling is homogenous, smooth, and airy
  5. Step 5

    Fold the chopped fresh strawberries into the cheesecake filling, reserving a few pieces for garnish. The strawberries add freshness and a juicy burst of flavor. Be careful to fold gently so the fruit doesn't break down too much and turn the filling watery.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Strawberries are evenly distributed without crushing
  6. Step 6

    Spoon the cheesecake filling evenly over the graham cracker crust layer in each jar. Use a spatula to smooth the top gently. Filling the jars carefully ensures neat layers and an attractive presentation. Avoid overfilling to prevent spilling when serving.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Jars are filled with distinct crust and creamy layers
  7. Step 7

    Cover the jars with lids or plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight. Chilling allows the filling to set and the flavors to meld. If rushed, the texture will be softer and less firm. Overnight chilling also enhances the cheesecake flavor.

    ⏱ 2+ hours✓ Filling is chilled and set but still creamy
  8. Step 8

    Before serving, garnish each jar with reserved fresh strawberries or a small mint leaf for a pop of color and freshness. This finishing touch makes the dessert look inviting and adds a little extra flavor.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Jars are visually appealing with fresh garnish
  9. Step 9

    Serve chilled directly from the jars. Use a small spoon to enjoy the layered textures of crunchy crust, creamy cheesecake, and juicy strawberries. These jars are perfect for individual portions, making cleanup easy and presentation charming.

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Dessert is cold, creamy, and layered distinctly

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature cream cheese for smooth mixing. — Cold cream cheese is stiff and difficult to blend, often causing lumps in the filling. Letting it soften ensures it mixes smoothly with sugar and vanilla, creating a creamy, lump-free base essential for a perfect cheesecake texture.
  • Chill the mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream. — Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold its shape better. Warm bowls or beaters can cause the cream to take longer to whip or fail to reach stiff peaks, resulting in a denser filling.
  • Press the crust firmly into jars to prevent crumbling. — A compact crust layer holds together better when eating and prevents the dessert from falling apart. If the crust is loose, it can become messy and less enjoyable, so take time to press it well.
  • Fold whipped cream gently into cream cheese mixture. — Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring vigorously deflates the mixture, leading to a dense, heavy texture rather than a creamy, airy one.
  • Chill the assembled jars overnight for best results. — Refrigeration firms up the crust and filling, allowing flavors to meld and textures to set. Serving too soon can result in a softer, less cohesive dessert that lacks the classic cheesecake mouthfeel.
  • Use fresh strawberries instead of frozen for better texture. — Frozen strawberries release excess liquid when thawed, which can make the filling watery. Fresh berries maintain their shape and provide a burst of natural sweetness without diluting the texture.
  • Avoid overwhipping the cream to prevent graininess. — Overwhipping causes the cream to separate into butter and liquid, ruining the smooth texture needed for the filling. Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form to maintain the ideal consistency.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold cream cheese directly from the fridge
    Why: Many assume cream cheese can be mixed immediately, but cold cream cheese is too firm and lumps easily when beaten.
    Fix: Let cream cheese sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing. This softens it enough to beat smoothly, avoiding lumps and ensuring a creamy filling.
  • Skipping chilling time after assembly
    Why: Impatience or underestimating how much chilling firms the dessert leads to serving a runny or loose cheesecake.
    Fix: Refrigerate the jars for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight. This allows the crust to set and the filling to firm up, giving the cheesecake its ideal texture.
  • Overmixing whipped cream into filling
    Why: Inexperience folding can cause cooks to stir vigorously, deflating the whipped cream.
    Fix: Use gentle folding motions with a spatula, turning the bowl slowly and lifting the mixture from underneath to keep the airy texture intact.
  • Not pressing the crust firmly enough
    Why: Rushing this step or using loose crumbs causes a crumbly crust that falls apart.
    Fix: Press the crust firmly into the bottom of the jars with the back of a spoon or your fingers until compact and even.
  • Using frozen strawberries without draining
    Why: Frozen berries release water when thawed, making the filling watery and less flavorful.
    Fix: Use fresh strawberries or if using frozen, thaw completely and drain excess liquid before folding into the filling.

Variations

  • Blueberry No-Bake Cheesecake Jars: Swap strawberries for fresh or frozen blueberries for a slightly tart and sweet variation. Blueberries add a lovely purple hue and pair beautifully with the creamy filling. You can also swirl in blueberry jam for extra flavor and a pretty marbled effect.
    If using frozen blueberries, thaw and drain well to avoid excess moisture. The texture will be similar but with a different fruit flavor profile.
  • Chocolate No-Bake Cheesecake Jars: Add 1/4 cup cocoa powder to the cream cheese mixture for a rich chocolate twist. Top with mini chocolate chips or shaved chocolate for garnish. This version is perfect for chocolate lovers craving a quick, no-bake treat.
    Sift cocoa powder before adding to avoid lumps. Mixing cocoa reduces sweetness slightly, so taste and adjust powdered sugar as needed.
  • Lemon No-Bake Cheesecake Jars: Brighten up the filling with 2 tablespoons of fresh lemon juice and 1 teaspoon lemon zest. This adds a refreshing citrus zing that pairs wonderfully with the creamy texture and crumbly crust.
    Add lemon juice gradually to avoid curdling the cream cheese. The tartness balances the richness and makes the dessert feel lighter.
  • Peach and Raspberry No-Bake Cheesecake: Layer chopped fresh peaches and raspberries into the filling or on top for a summery fruit medley. The peaches add sweetness and the raspberries bring a tart contrast, making the jars vibrant and flavorful.
    Use ripe fruit and fold gently to keep fruit pieces intact. This variation adds texture and color complexity.
  • Salted Caramel No-Bake Cheesecake: Drizzle homemade or store-bought salted caramel sauce over the top of the cheesecake jars before serving. The sweet-salty flavor combo adds depth and decadence to the creamy dessert.
    Add caramel just before serving to keep crust crisp and filling from becoming too sweet or sticky.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This no-bake cheesecake should not be kept at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the dairy content. Keep jars covered to avoid contamination if left out briefly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store assembled jars in an airtight container or covered with lids/plastic wrap for up to 3 days. The crust may soften slightly over time, but the flavor stays fresh. Serve chilled for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freezing is not recommended as the texture of the cream cheese and whipped cream filling can become grainy and separate upon thawing.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating is needed or recommended. Serve straight from the fridge for the best creamy, refreshing texture.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the crust and filling up to 2 days ahead. Assemble jars and store covered in the fridge overnight to let flavors meld and filling set perfectly before serving.

FAQ

Can I use low-fat cream cheese for this recipe?
Low-fat cream cheese is not ideal because it lacks the fat content that gives the filling its creamy texture and rich flavor. Using it may result in a thinner, less smooth filling that doesn’t set well. For best results, use full-fat cream cheese.
How long can I store these cheesecake jars in the fridge?
You can safely store the assembled cheesecake jars in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Keep them covered to prevent them from absorbing other odors. The crust might soften a bit over time, but the flavor will remain delicious.
Can I skip the graham cracker crust?
While you can skip the crust, it’s an important textural contrast that balances the creamy filling. Without it, the jars might feel too soft and less satisfying. If you want a crustless version, consider adding crushed nuts or a cookie crumble topping instead.
What if I don’t have fresh strawberries?
Fresh strawberries are best for texture and flavor, but you can substitute frozen strawberries if thawed and drained well. Avoid using frozen berries straight from the freezer as they can release too much liquid and make the filling watery.
How do I prevent the filling from being too dense?
Whip the heavy cream to stiff peaks separately and fold it in gently to incorporate air. Also, make sure the cream cheese is fully softened and beaten smooth before adding cream. Overmixing or skipping the whipped cream step leads to a dense filling.
Can I use a food processor to crush the graham crackers?
Yes, a food processor works great for crushing graham crackers evenly and quickly. Just pulse until you get fine crumbs. Avoid over-processing into powder, which can affect crust texture.
Is it okay to use plastic jars instead of glass?
Plastic jars can be used, but glass jars are preferable because they look nicer for presentation and don’t retain odors or stains. Glass also chills evenly and is more environmentally friendly.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
This recipe relies on cream cheese and heavy cream, so it’s not vegan as is. However, you could try vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks as substitutes. Keep in mind texture and flavor will differ.
