Classic Southern Tomato Pie with Flaky Crust and Creamy Cheese Filling

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 18, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
6 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This Southern tomato pie feels like a warm hug from grandma’s kitchen. The flaky crust pairs perfectly with juicy ripe tomatoes and a creamy, cheesy filling boosted by a little mayo. Great for a sunny lunch or summer potluck!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon tomato pie during a summer visit to my aunt in Georgia. It was unlike any tomato dish I'd ever had — a perfect balance of flaky crust, juicy tomatoes, and a creamy cheese filling that felt both comforting and fresh. I’ve been making my own version ever since, tweaking it to get just the right amount of richness and tang. What I love most about this pie is the subtle use of mayonnaise mixed with cheese, which adds moisture and a silky texture without overpowering the tomatoes. The trick I learned is to salt the tomatoes ahead of time to draw out excess water, so the crust doesn’t get soggy. When baked, the crust turns golden and crisp, while the filling bubbles with cheesy goodness. Every bite is like a little celebration of summer’s best flavors. It’s perfect for a casual lunch, a picnic, or a potluck where you want to impress without too much fuss. Plus, it comes together pretty quickly, which is always a win in my book.

What You'll Need

One 9-inch pie, 6 servings

  • 1 pre-made pie crust, thawed
  • 4 large ripe tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). While it warms up, roll out the pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish. Press it gently to avoid tearing and trim any excess dough along the edges. Prick the bottom a few times with a fork to prevent bubbling during baking. This step ensures a flaky, evenly cooked crust that stays crisp beneath the juicy filling.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Pie crust is evenly spread and edges are neatly trimmed without tears.
Step 2
Slice the tomatoes about 1/4 inch thick and lay them on a paper towel-lined tray. Sprinkle them evenly with 1 teaspoon of salt and let them sit for 15 minutes. This draws out moisture, preventing a soggy pie. Afterward, gently pat the slices dry with more paper towels to remove the excess liquid.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Tomato slices feel less wet and have released noticeable moisture on the towels.
Step 3
In a medium bowl, combine the mayonnaise, shredded cheddar, mozzarella, Parmesan, chopped basil, black pepper, and garlic powder. Mix everything well until you have a creamy, cheesy filling. This mixture will bind the tomatoes and add rich flavor and texture to the pie.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cheese mixture is smooth, well combined, and fragrant with herbs and spices.
Step 4
Layer half of the tomato slices evenly over the pie crust. Spread half of the cheese mixture over the tomatoes, gently smoothing it out but not pressing down too hard. Repeat with the remaining tomatoes and cheese mixture. This layering helps distribute flavors and keeps the pie moist but structured.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Layers are evenly distributed and the pie surface looks creamy and inviting.
Step 5
Place the pie on the center rack of the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes. Keep an eye on the crust edges; if they brown too quickly, cover them loosely with foil to prevent burning. The pie is done when the crust is golden and the cheese filling is bubbly and slightly browned on top.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Crust is golden brown and cheese filling bubbles with light browning spots.
Step 6
Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for at least 15 minutes before slicing. This rest time allows the filling to set, making it easier to cut clean slices without the filling oozing out. Serve warm or at room temperature for best flavor.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Filling has firmed up slightly and pie slices hold their shape when cut.
Step 7
Optional but recommended: garnish with a few fresh basil leaves or a light drizzle of olive oil before serving. This adds a fresh, herbal brightness that complements the richness beautifully.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Fresh basil looks vibrant and the pie has an inviting glossy finish.
Step 8
If you want to add a little extra texture, sprinkle some toasted breadcrumbs or crushed crackers on top during the last 5 minutes of baking. This adds a delightful crunch that contrasts nicely with the creamy filling.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Top is golden and crunchy without burning.
Step 9
Serve slices with a simple green salad or fresh fruit for a light summer meal. Leftovers keep well in the fridge and reheat nicely in a low oven or microwave with a splash of water to keep moist.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Pie tastes fresh and balanced when served with light side dishes.

Pro Tips

Salt the tomato slices and let them rest on paper towels before assembling.
This step is key to drawing out excess moisture from the tomatoes, which helps prevent the crust from getting soggy. The salt also concentrates the tomato flavor, making each bite more intense and delicious.
Use a mix of cheeses for depth of flavor and texture.
Combining sharp cheddar, mozzarella, and Parmesan balances meltiness with sharpness and nuttiness. Each cheese plays a role: cheddar adds tang, mozzarella provides creaminess, and Parmesan brings umami and a slight crunch when baked.
Don’t skip the resting time after baking before slicing.
Letting the pie cool allows the filling to set and firm up, which makes slicing cleaner and prevents the filling from spilling out. It also helps flavors meld, so the pie tastes even better.
Cover crust edges with foil if they brown too fast during baking.
Pie crust edges tend to brown quicker than the center. Covering them protects from burning while the filling finishes cooking, ensuring the whole pie bakes evenly.
Use a fork to prick the pie crust before filling.
Pricking vents the crust to release steam during baking, preventing air bubbles and uneven rising. This keeps the crust flat and flaky instead of puffing up.
Choose tomatoes that are ripe but firm to avoid watery filling.
Overripe tomatoes release more liquid, which can make the pie soggy. Firm tomatoes hold their shape better and provide a fresh, juicy texture without excess moisture.
Add fresh herbs like basil just before serving for brightness.
Herbs lose their vibrant flavor and color if baked too long. Adding them fresh preserves their aroma and adds a fresh contrast to the rich, baked filling.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using un-salted, wet tomatoes directly in the pie

Why it happens Many cooks skip the salting step because it feels like an extra hassle or they don’t realize its importance.

Fix Always salt and rest tomato slices on paper towels to draw out moisture. This prevents soggy crust and concentrates flavor. If you skip this, the crust will be mushy and the pie less enjoyable.

Overfilling the pie with tomatoes or cheese

Why it happens It’s tempting to pile on ingredients, thinking more is better, but it can overwhelm the crust and make baking uneven.

Fix Stick to the recipe quantities and layer the filling evenly. This balance ensures the pie cooks through properly and the crust stays crisp.

Not allowing the pie to cool before slicing

Why it happens Impatience leads to cutting too soon, causing the filling to spill out.

Fix Let the pie rest at least 15 minutes after baking. This helps the filling set and makes slicing neater and easier.

Ignoring crust edge browning

Why it happens Some think a browned crust is always good, but burnt edges taste bitter and ruin the dish.

Fix Cover edges with foil halfway through baking if they brown too quickly. This protects from burning and keeps crust perfect.

Using low-fat mayonnaise or pre-shredded cheese

Why it happens Low-fat or pre-shredded products may seem healthier or convenient but don’t melt or bind as well.

Fix Use full-fat mayo and freshly shredded cheese for best texture and flavor. They melt smoothly and create a creamy filling.

Variations

Add Crispy Bacon

Mix in cooked, crumbled bacon into the cheese filling for a smoky, savory twist. The salty bacon contrasts beautifully with the sweet tomatoes and creamy cheese, adding extra texture and flavor complexity.

Note: Cook bacon until crisp and drain well to avoid adding excess grease to the filling.

Use a Buttery Pesto Crust

Swap the plain pie crust for a pesto-infused dough or spread a thin layer of pesto on the crust before adding filling. This adds a fresh, herby dimension that pairs wonderfully with tomatoes and cheese.

Note: Be careful not to add too much pesto to keep the crust from getting soggy.

Make It Vegan

Replace cheeses with vegan cheese alternatives and use vegan mayo. Choose firm tomatoes and add nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor boost. The texture will be different but still satisfying and delicious.

Note: Vegan cheeses melt differently, so monitor baking time to avoid drying out.

Add Fresh Herbs and Spices

Incorporate fresh thyme, oregano, or a pinch of red pepper flakes into the cheese mixture for a more aromatic and slightly spicy pie. This variation enhances the flavor complexity and adds a subtle kick.

Note: Add herbs fresh or dried but adjust quantity to avoid overpowering the tomatoes.

Use a Cornmeal Crust

Substitute regular pie crust with a cornmeal crust for a nuttier, grainier texture that complements the Southern vibe. This crust adds a lovely crunch and slightly sweet flavor that pairs well with the filling.

Note: Cornmeal crusts can be more fragile; handle gently when assembling.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This tomato pie can safely sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean towel. Beyond that, bacteria growth can increase, so refrigerate leftovers promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The pie can be eaten cold but tastes best reheated gently to bring back the creamy texture and warm the crust.

Freezer

Freeze the pie wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating. Freezing may soften the crust slightly, so reheat in the oven to restore crispness.

Reheating

Reheat slices in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes until warmed through. A quick microwave reheat works but may soften the crust. Add a splash of water or cover loosely to keep filling moist.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the crust and cheese filling up to a day ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Slice and salt the tomatoes just before assembling and baking to keep them fresh and juicy.

FAQ

Can I make this tomato pie ahead of time?
Yes! You can prepare the crust and cheese mixture a day ahead and keep them refrigerated separately. Slice and salt the tomatoes just before assembling to keep them fresh. Bake the pie the same day you plan to serve it for best texture and flavor.
What if I don’t have mayonnaise? Can I substitute it?
Mayonnaise adds creaminess and helps bind the cheese, but you can substitute with Greek yogurt or sour cream for a tangy alternative. Keep in mind the texture and flavor will shift slightly, but it still works well.
How do I prevent a soggy crust?
Salting the tomatoes and letting them rest on paper towels is the best way to reduce moisture. Also, pricking the pie crust before filling and possibly blind baking it for 5-7 minutes helps keep it crisp.
Can I use fresh herbs other than basil?
Absolutely! Fresh thyme, oregano, or parsley work nicely. Just be mindful of strong flavors; add herbs in moderation so they complement rather than overpower the tomatoes.
Is it okay to use a store-bought pie crust?
Definitely! Store-bought crusts save time and work well here. Just make sure to thaw it properly and prick it before filling to avoid bubbling.
Can I add other vegetables to the pie?
You can add thinly sliced zucchini or bell peppers for extra flavor and texture. Just keep the slices thin and dry them well to avoid adding too much moisture.
How long does the pie keep in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, the pie stays good for about 3 days. Reheat in the oven to bring back the crust’s crispness and warm the filling.
What is the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheating in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes is best to warm thoroughly and crisp the crust. Microwaving is faster but can make the crust soggy.
