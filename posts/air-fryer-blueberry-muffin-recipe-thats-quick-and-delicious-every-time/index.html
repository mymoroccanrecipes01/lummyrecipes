Perfectly Moist Air Fryer Blueberry Muffins with Golden Tops

Lummy Hayes
Published on June 29, 2026
15 min Prep Time
15 min Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These blueberry muffins are a game-changer—super moist and golden thanks to the air fryer’s magic. I love how quick they come together, making them ideal for busy mornings or a cozy snack anytime.

Introduction

I first tried making blueberry muffins in my air fryer on a busy weekend morning when I didn’t feel like heating up the whole kitchen. Honestly, I wasn’t sure if the texture would be right, but to my surprise, they came out beautifully moist with a golden top that looked like they’d been baked in a traditional oven. What makes this version stand out is the quick, simple mix method combined with the air fryer’s even heat distribution, which helps the muffins cook evenly and stay tender inside. I love that you don’t have to fuss with complicated steps or wait forever. The trick I learned was to not overmix the batter — just enough to combine the ingredients gently so the muffins stay light and fluffy. When they come out, they have this inviting aroma and a slightly crisp top that’s just perfect alongside a cup of coffee or tea. I’ve made these several times now, and they’re always a hit with my family, especially for quick breakfasts or afternoon snacks. It’s such a cozy, effortless treat that you can whip up anytime.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 muffins

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1/3 - 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by mixing the dry ingredients: sift together the flour, sugar, salt, and baking powder in a large bowl. Sifting helps remove lumps and aerates the flour, which contributes to a lighter muffin texture. Make sure everything is evenly combined, but don’t overwork it. Overmixing flour can develop gluten and make muffins tough, so keep your stirring gentle and just enough to blend.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients should be evenly combined with no visible clumps.
Step 2
In a separate bowl, whisk together the wet ingredients: vegetable oil, beaten egg, milk (start with 1/3 cup and add more if needed), and vanilla extract. The oil keeps the muffins moist, and the vanilla adds subtle flavor depth. Make sure the egg is fully beaten to incorporate air, which helps the muffins rise. Adding milk gradually allows you to control the batter consistency — it should be thick but pourable.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Wet ingredients are smooth and fully blended, no streaks of egg or oil.
Step 3
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry mixture and fold gently with a spatula. The key here is to combine just until the flour disappears. Overmixing at this stage can make the muffins dense and chewy. The batter should be lumpy but uniform. If it feels too thick, add a splash more milk to loosen it slightly; if too runny, a little extra flour can help.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Batter is combined with no dry flour visible, still slightly lumpy.
Step 4
Carefully fold in the fresh blueberries, using a spatula to avoid crushing them. Blueberries release moisture as they cook, so folding gently prevents the batter from turning blue or watery. Fresh berries work best here; frozen berries can be added but may require a little extra baking time and can produce more juice.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Blueberries are evenly distributed without breaking.
Step 5
Line your air fryer basket with silicone muffin cups or lightly grease a small baking pan that fits inside. Spoon the batter evenly into 6 muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full to allow room for rising. Avoid overfilling as muffins can spill over during cooking. Getting the right portion size helps ensure even cooking and beautiful muffin tops.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Muffin cups are filled uniformly and ready for air frying.
Step 6
Preheat your air fryer to 320°F (160°C) for about 3 minutes. Preheating is crucial as it helps the muffins start cooking immediately, promoting a nice rise and even browning. Skipping this step can cause uneven cooking and a denser texture.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Air fryer reaches stable temperature before placing muffins inside.
Step 7
Place the muffin cups in the air fryer basket in a single layer, making sure there’s space around each for proper air circulation. Cook at 320°F for 12-15 minutes. The lower temperature and steady airflow help the muffins bake evenly without drying out. Resist the urge to open the basket too often, as this lets heat escape and interrupts cooking.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Muffins are golden on top and spring back lightly when touched.
Step 8
Check doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center of a muffin — it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs, but no wet batter. If not done, cook in 2-minute increments. Avoid overbaking, which dries out the muffins and diminishes their tender crumb.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean or with moist crumbs.
Step 9
Let the muffins cool in the air fryer basket or on a wire rack for about 5 minutes before serving. Cooling slightly allows the crumb to set and makes them easier to remove without breaking. They’re best enjoyed warm but also keep well if cooled completely and stored properly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Muffins are firm enough to handle but still warm and soft inside.

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix the batter; fold just until combined.
Overmixing develops gluten strands in the flour, which leads to dense, chewy muffins instead of soft and tender ones. Keeping lumps in the batter is okay and actually desirable for a light texture.
Use fresh blueberries and fold them in gently.
Fresh berries maintain their shape and release less juice, preventing the batter from becoming watery and blue-stained. Gentle folding avoids bursting the berries and keeps the muffins looking pretty.
Preheat your air fryer before baking.
Preheating ensures immediate and even heat, which helps muffins rise properly and develop a golden top. Skipping this step can cause uneven cooking and a dense crumb.
Fill muffin cups only about 3/4 full.
This gives the batter space to rise without spilling over, ensuring pretty, well-shaped muffins with domed tops.
Use silicone or well-greased muffin cups for easy release.
Air fryer baskets can be sticky, so lining with silicone cups or greasing prevents muffins from sticking and breaking apart when removing.
Test doneness with a toothpick inserted into the center.
A clean toothpick or one with moist crumbs means the muffins are cooked through but still moist inside. Avoid overbaking to prevent dryness.
Let muffins cool slightly before removing from the air fryer.
Cooling allows the crumb to set and makes the muffins easier to handle without breaking.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Many cooks think mixing longer makes a smoother batter, but it actually develops gluten, making muffins tough.

Fix Mix ingredients just until combined, leaving some lumps in the batter. Use a gentle folding motion rather than vigorous stirring.

Using frozen blueberries without adjustment

Why it happens Frozen berries release extra juice as they thaw, which can make the batter watery and muffins soggy.

Fix Add frozen berries directly to the batter without thawing to minimize juice release, or reduce liquid slightly in the recipe.

Not preheating the air fryer

Why it happens Skipping preheat leads to uneven temperature and poor muffin rise.

Fix Always preheat your air fryer for a few minutes before baking to ensure even heat distribution.

Overfilling muffin cups

Why it happens Filling too full causes batter to spill over and uneven cooking.

Fix Fill cups only about 3/4 full to allow space for rising and avoid mess.

Removing muffins too soon

Why it happens Taking muffins out while too hot can cause them to break apart.

Fix Let muffins cool for 5 minutes before removing to let crumb set properly.

Variations

Lemon Blueberry Muffins

Add 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon zest and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice to the wet ingredients for a fresh, zesty twist that complements the blueberries beautifully.

Note: The acid in lemon juice reacts slightly with baking powder, so reduce milk by a tablespoon to maintain batter consistency.

Gluten-Free Blueberry Muffins

Substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture will be a bit denser but still moist and delicious.

Note: Gluten-free flours absorb liquid differently; you may need to add a splash more milk to get the right batter thickness.

Vegan Blueberry Muffins

Replace the egg with a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water) and use plant-based milk to make these muffins vegan-friendly without sacrificing moisture.

Note: Flax eggs add binding but not as much leavening, so add 1/4 teaspoon extra baking powder for lift.

Blueberry Oat Muffins

Add 1/2 cup rolled oats to the dry ingredients for a heartier texture and nuttier flavor. These are great for a more filling breakfast option.

Note: Oats absorb more moisture; increase milk by 2 tablespoons to keep batter moist.

Blueberry Streusel Muffins

Top muffins with a simple streusel made from butter, brown sugar, and flour before air frying for a crunchy, sweet topping that contrasts the soft crumb.

Note: Add streusel topping just before cooking; it crisps nicely in the air fryer without burning at the moderate temperature used.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Muffins can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 days if stored in an airtight container or covered with plastic wrap. They’ll stay soft but are best eaten within this time to avoid drying out.

Refrigerator

Store muffins in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Refrigeration may firm the crumb slightly; warm briefly before serving to refresh softness.

Freezer

Freeze muffins individually wrapped in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer-safe bag for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature or warm in the microwave for 20-30 seconds to restore texture.

Reheating

Reheat muffins in the microwave for 20-30 seconds or in a 350°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small dab of butter or a splash of milk before warming can help maintain moisture.

Make Ahead

Prepare the batter up to folding in the blueberries 1 day ahead and store covered in the fridge. Add the berries and bake fresh in the air fryer the next morning for best results.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries instead of fresh?
Yes, you can use frozen blueberries but add them directly to the batter without thawing to prevent excess moisture. The muffins might take a couple extra minutes to cook and could be slightly more moist inside.
What if my muffins come out dense or heavy?
Dense muffins usually result from overmixing the batter or using old baking powder. Be sure to fold ingredients gently and check that your leavening agent is fresh for a light, fluffy texture.
Can I make these muffins without an air fryer?
Absolutely! You can bake them in a conventional oven at 350°F for about 20-25 minutes. Keep an eye on the muffins and test doneness with a toothpick.
How do I prevent blueberries from sinking to the bottom?
Toss the blueberries lightly in a tablespoon of flour before folding them into the batter. This helps suspend them evenly throughout the muffins.
Can I substitute vegetable oil with butter?
You can melt butter and use it as a substitute, but it may make the muffins slightly denser. Oil tends to keep them moister and softer for longer.
Is it necessary to preheat the air fryer?
Preheating is recommended because it helps the muffins rise evenly and develop a nice crust. Skipping this step can lead to uneven cooking.
How long will these muffins keep fresh?
Stored properly, they stay fresh at room temperature for up to 2 days and refrigerated for up to 5 days. Freezing extends their life to about 3 months.
Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?
Yes! Chopped nuts, chocolate chips, or dried fruit can be folded into the batter. Just reduce the blueberries slightly to keep the balance of moisture.
