No Bake Chocolate Peanut Butter Cheesecake Cups That Melt in Your Mouth

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These no bake cheesecake cups blend silky peanut butter with rich chocolate for a creamy, indulgent treat. No oven needed, so they’re perfect for quick dessert cravings or casual get-togethers.

Introduction

I first made these no bake cheesecake cups on a hot summer evening when I didn’t want to turn on the oven but still craved something indulgent. The combo of peanut butter and chocolate is a classic, but blending them into a smooth, creamy cheesecake filling felt like a fun twist. What I love most is how simple this recipe is—no baking, no fuss—just stirring and chilling. The secret is using cold heavy cream whipped to stiff peaks, which gives the filling that light yet rich texture without heaviness. Plus, the buttery graham cracker crust adds the perfect crunch. Every bite is silky and melts right in your mouth. It’s perfect for casual weekends, last-minute guests, or anytime you want a decadent treat without the wait or mess. I’ve tried other no bake cheesecakes before, but this one stands out because it balances sweetness and richness so well without being overpowering. If you love peanut butter and chocolate, you’ll definitely want to keep this recipe handy!

What You'll Need

6 individual cheesecake cups

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter, room temperature
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream, cold
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips, melted and slightly cooled

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by mixing the graham cracker crumbs with melted butter until the mixture feels like wet sand. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs together, forming the base of your cheesecake cups. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of your serving cups or ramekins to create an even, compact crust. Avoid pressing too hard or the crust will be too dense; just firm enough to hold its shape. Chill the crust in the fridge while you prepare the filling to help it set up nicely.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust is firm and holds together when pressed lightly
Step 2
In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and peanut butter together until smooth and creamy. This blending step ensures no lumps and a silky texture. Add powdered sugar and vanilla extract, mixing on low speed to combine evenly without overbeating, which can make the filling grainy. The cream cheese provides tanginess while the peanut butter adds richness and depth. Be sure to use room temperature ingredients here to avoid lumps and to get a smooth, velvety filling.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, glossy, and lump-free
Step 3
Whip the cold heavy cream in a separate bowl until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the peaks stand straight without folding over. Whipping the cream correctly is essential because it lightens the dense cream cheese mixture, giving the cheesecake cups a fluffy yet creamy texture. Overwhipping can turn it grainy or even butter-like, so watch closely and stop as soon as stiff peaks develop.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream holds stiff peaks and looks smooth and glossy
Step 4
Gently fold the whipped cream into the peanut butter and cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Folding means carefully incorporating the cream without deflating it, which keeps the filling light and airy. Use a gentle motion: cut through the center, scoop under, and turn the bowl. Avoid stirring vigorously, or you'll lose the fluffiness that makes this cheesecake so dreamy.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is uniform in color and texture without streaks
Step 5
Pour half of the peanut butter cheesecake filling over the chilled graham cracker crust in each cup. Smooth the tops with a spatula or the back of a spoon to create an even layer. This layering helps keep the crust and filling distinct and makes for a nice presentation. Don’t rush this step; a smooth surface looks more polished and inviting.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling layer is even and level in each cup
Step 6
Drizzle or spoon the melted chocolate over the cheesecake filling. You can swirl it gently with a toothpick or knife to create a marbled effect if you like. The warm chocolate adds a rich, glossy finish that hardens slightly as it cools, giving a delightful texture contrast. Make sure the chocolate is not too hot or it will melt the cheesecake layer beneath.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate is glossy and slightly set on top without mixing into filling
Step 7
Refrigerate the assembled cups for at least 4 hours, or overnight for best results. Chilling allows the filling to firm up and the flavors to meld beautifully. Resist the temptation to eat them too soon as the texture won’t be as creamy and the crust might be soggy. Patience here really pays off!
⏱ Time: 4 hours or overnight
✓ Doneness: Filling is set and holds its shape when spooned
Step 8
Before serving, you can add a light sprinkle of chopped peanuts or a small dollop of whipped cream for extra texture and flair. This step is optional but adds a nice crunch and visual appeal. If you like it sweeter, a drizzle of honey or caramel can also complement the peanut butter and chocolate flavors.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toppings look fresh and add texture contrast
Step 9
Serve the cheesecake cups chilled straight from the fridge. Use a small spoon to enjoy the layers of crunchy crust, creamy peanut butter filling, and silky chocolate topping. These cups are perfect for casual entertaining or a cozy night in when you want a little indulgence without fuss. Leftovers keep well covered for a couple of days.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake is cold and firm yet creamy when eaten

Pro Tips

Make sure your cream cheese is fully softened before mixing.
Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with peanut butter and sugar, preventing lumps and ensuring a silky filling. Cold cream cheese won’t combine well and can leave gritty bits, spoiling the texture.
Whip the heavy cream just until stiff peaks form—don’t overbeat.
Overwhipping can cause the cream to break down into butter and buttermilk, ruining the light texture we want. Stiff peaks hold the air that lightens the cheesecake filling.
Press the graham cracker crust firmly but don’t compact it too much.
Pressing binds the crust so it holds shape but too much pressure makes it hard and dense rather than crumbly and tender, which is less pleasant to eat.
Cool the melted chocolate slightly before pouring over the filling.
If the chocolate is too hot, it will melt into the cheesecake layer and lose that shiny, firm topping effect. Letting it cool ensures a nice glossy finish that adds texture contrast.
Chill the cheesecake cups for at least 4 hours or overnight.
The filling needs time to set and flavors to meld. Rushing this leads to a softer, less structured dessert that won’t slice or spoon as nicely.
Use a gentle folding motion to combine whipped cream with the peanut butter mixture.
Folding preserves the air in whipped cream, keeping the filling light. Stirring or beating at this stage deflates the mixture, resulting in a dense cheesecake.
Serve chilled straight from the fridge for best texture and flavor.
Warm cheesecake cups become too soft and lose their structure. Chilling keeps the filling firm and the chocolate topping slightly crisp.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese directly from the fridge

Why it happens Many people forget to soften cream cheese, assuming it will blend easily. This leads to lumps and a grainy texture.

Fix Take cream cheese out at least 30 minutes before starting or warm it gently in the microwave in short bursts, stirring between, until smooth but not melted.

Overwhipping the heavy cream

Why it happens It’s easy to keep whipping past stiff peaks, especially if you’re trying to get it really thick.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form—lift the whisk and the cream should hold a firm peak without falling over. If you overwhip, start over with fresh cream.

Pressing the crust too hard

Why it happens Thinking a hard press makes a better crust, some press aggressively, making it dense and tough.

Fix Press just firmly enough to hold crumbs together; test by lightly pressing with your finger—it should hold but still feel crumbly.

Adding hot melted chocolate directly onto the filling

Why it happens When in a hurry, some pour chocolate right after melting, which melts into the filling.

Fix Let the melted chocolate cool for 5-10 minutes until warm but not hot before drizzling to keep a firm, glossy layer.

Not chilling the cheesecake long enough

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning leads to serving too soon when the filling hasn’t set.

Fix Plan ahead and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to let the filling firm up and flavors develop.

Variations

Salted Caramel No Bake Cheesecake Cups

Add a swirl of salted caramel sauce between the peanut butter filling and chocolate topping for a sweet-salty twist. The caramel adds a rich, buttery flavor that complements the peanut butter beautifully.

Note: Drizzle caramel before adding chocolate and chill well to prevent mixing into the filling.

Crunchy Peanut Butter Cheesecake Cups

Mix in some chopped roasted peanuts into the filling or sprinkle on top for added crunch and texture. This variation adds a nutty bite that contrasts nicely with the creamy layers.

Note: Fold peanuts gently into filling to maintain lightness.

Vegan No Bake Cheesecake Cups

Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks instead of heavy cream. Swap butter for coconut oil and use dairy-free chocolate for a plant-based delight.

Note: Chilling time may vary with coconut cream; ensure it’s well chilled before whipping.

Mocha Infused Cheesecake Cups

Add 1 teaspoon instant espresso powder to the peanut butter mixture for a subtle coffee flavor that pairs wonderfully with chocolate. This adds depth and a slight bitterness to balance sweetness.

Note: Dissolve espresso in a teaspoon of hot water before mixing in.

Berry Topped Cheesecake Cups

Top with fresh raspberries or sliced strawberries for a pop of color and tartness. The fresh fruit cuts through the richness and adds freshness to each bite.

Note: Add berries just before serving to keep them fresh and vibrant.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These cheesecake cups should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the dairy content. Keep them covered or refrigerated until serving to maintain freshness and safety.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture remains creamy but the crust may soften slightly. Best served chilled but can be left out 10-15 minutes before eating for softer texture.

Freezer

Freeze cheesecake cups in airtight containers or wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 1 month. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving. The texture may be slightly less creamy after freezing but still delicious.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled. If you prefer a softer texture, let sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before serving. Avoid microwaving as it can separate the filling.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the crust and filling up to 2 days ahead, store separately, and assemble just before chilling. Alternatively, assemble fully and refrigerate overnight for best flavor and texture.

FAQ

Can I use crunchy peanut butter instead of creamy?
Yes, you can use crunchy peanut butter if you want some added texture in the filling. Keep in mind that it will change the smoothness slightly, making the cheesecake a bit chunkier. It’s a tasty variation if you like a bit of crunch.
What if I don’t have graham crackers for the crust?
You can substitute with digestive biscuits, vanilla wafers, or even crushed pretzels for a salty twist. Just crush finely and mix with melted butter as usual. Each option will give a different flavor but works well as a crust.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use gluten-free graham cracker crumbs or gluten-free cookies for the crust. Ensure all other ingredients like chocolate are certified gluten-free. The rest of the recipe remains the same.
How long does the cheesecake need to chill?
At least 4 hours is needed to let the filling firm up properly, but overnight chilling is best for optimal texture and flavor melding. Rushing this step results in a softer, less stable dessert.
Can I prepare these cheesecake cups in advance for a party?
Yes, they’re perfect for make-ahead desserts. Assemble them a day or two in advance and keep refrigerated. Add any fresh toppings like nuts or berries just before serving to keep them fresh.
Why does the filling sometimes turn out grainy?
Graininess usually happens if cream cheese isn’t softened properly or if powdered sugar isn’t sifted before mixing. Also, overmixing can break down the texture. Use room temperature ingredients and mix gently.
Can I double the recipe?
Definitely! Just double all ingredients and use larger containers or more cups. The chilling time remains the same, but make sure your fridge has enough space to chill everything evenly.
Is it possible to use a different nut butter?
Yes, almond or cashew butter can be used for a different flavor profile. The texture might vary slightly depending on the nut butter’s consistency, so choose a creamy type for best results.
