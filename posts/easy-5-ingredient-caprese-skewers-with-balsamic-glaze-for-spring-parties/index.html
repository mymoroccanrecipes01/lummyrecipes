Fresh and Easy Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Glaze for Spring

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 9, 2026
15 min Prep Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These Caprese skewers are a fresh, simple appetizer bursting with juicy cherry tomatoes and creamy mozzarella, drizzled with tangy balsamic glaze. Perfect for spring parties or quick snacks!

Introduction

I first made these Caprese skewers for a spring garden party a few years ago, and they instantly became a hit with everyone. What I love about this version is how the balsamic glaze adds a sweet tang that perfectly balances the creamy mozzarella and juicy tomatoes. It’s such a simple recipe, but the trick I learned was to simmer the balsamic vinegar slowly with a bit of honey until it thickens into a syrupy glaze—that step really elevates the whole dish. Plus, threading the ingredients onto skewers makes them super easy to serve and eat at any casual gathering. The finished skewers are colorful and fresh, with a lovely contrast of textures and flavors that make you want to keep popping them. I think they’re great for spring and summer when tomatoes and basil are at their best, but honestly, I make them year-round because they’re so quick and delicious. Just a little drizzle of that balsamic glaze right before serving, and you’ve got a crowd-pleaser that’s as pretty as it is tasty.

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 skewers, serves 6

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, washed
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • Fresh basil leaves, about 24 leaves
  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey or brown sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers or toothpicks

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the balsamic glaze. Pour the balsamic vinegar into a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the honey or brown sugar and stir to combine. Let it simmer gently, stirring occasionally, until it reduces by about half and becomes syrupy. This slow reduction concentrates the flavors and adds a sweet tang that balances the acidity of the vinegar. Be careful not to let it burn; watch for a glossy texture and slightly thickened consistency. Once done, remove from heat and let it cool while you prepare the skewers.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon and have a shiny, syrupy appearance
Step 2
While the glaze is reducing, wash and dry the cherry tomatoes and basil leaves thoroughly. Drain the mozzarella balls on paper towels to remove excess moisture. This helps prevent the skewers from getting soggy and keeps the flavors fresh. You want each bite to have a nice balance of juicy tomato, creamy cheese, and fragrant basil.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ingredients should be dry to the touch and ready for assembling without dripping
Step 3
Begin assembling your skewers by threading one cherry tomato onto a wooden skewer or toothpick, followed by a folded basil leaf, then a mozzarella ball. Repeat this pattern until you have about 24 skewers. Folding the basil leaf helps it fit nicely and releases its aroma. This order ensures each bite has all three flavors evenly distributed.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers should be neatly assembled with ingredients holding firmly in place
Step 4
Once all skewers are assembled, arrange them on a serving platter. Drizzle the cooled balsamic glaze evenly over the skewers just before serving. The glaze adds a glossy finish and an irresistible tangy sweetness that ties all the ingredients together. Avoid drizzling too early, or the basil leaves may wilt.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers glisten with a thin layer of balsamic glaze without sogginess
Step 5
Season lightly with salt and freshly cracked black pepper to enhance the flavors. The salt brings out the sweetness of the tomatoes and the creaminess of the mozzarella, while the pepper adds a subtle kick. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed, but keep it light to let the fresh ingredients shine.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Seasoning is balanced and complements the fresh ingredients without overpowering
Step 6
If not serving immediately, cover the platter loosely with plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to an hour. This keeps the ingredients fresh but avoid making too far ahead or the basil may turn dark and limp. For best texture and flavor, serve within an hour of assembling.
⏱ Time: Up to 1 hour
✓ Doneness: Skewers remain fresh-looking and basil stays vibrant without wilting
Step 7
For a fun twist, try adding a small piece of prosciutto or a green olive between the mozzarella and basil on some skewers. This adds a salty or briny element that pairs beautifully with the other flavors. It’s an easy way to customize the recipe for different tastes or occasions.
⏱ Time: Optional, 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Additional ingredients are evenly spaced and secure on skewers
Step 8
Serve these skewers chilled or at room temperature. They’re perfect as finger food for parties, picnics, or casual snacks. The contrast of cold mozzarella, sweet tomatoes, and warm balsamic glaze is so refreshing and satisfying. Plus, they’re super easy to eat without utensils!
⏱ Time: Serve immediately
✓ Doneness: Skewers are cool but not cold, flavors are vibrant and fresh
Step 9
Clean up by rinsing skewers if reusable or disposing of toothpicks properly. Store any leftover glaze in a sealed container in the fridge for up to a week; just warm it slightly before using again. These little details help keep your kitchen tidy and your ingredients fresh for next time.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Kitchen is clean and glaze stored properly without crystallizing

Pro Tips

Simmer the balsamic glaze slowly over low heat.
Slow simmering allows the sugars in the balsamic vinegar and honey to caramelize gently without burning, resulting in a rich, syrupy glaze. Rushing this step or using high heat can cause bitterness or a burnt flavor, ruining the balance of sweetness and acidity.
Dry the mozzarella and basil well before assembling.
Removing excess moisture prevents the skewers from becoming soggy and helps the balsamic glaze stick better. Wet ingredients can dilute flavors and make the presentation less appealing as the glaze slides off.
Fold basil leaves before threading them onto skewers.
Folding basil releases its essential oils, enhancing aroma and flavor in every bite. It also helps the leaves fit neatly on the skewer without tearing or slipping off, improving both taste and presentation.
Use wooden skewers or sturdy toothpicks for assembly.
Wooden skewers provide a good grip and support for the ingredients, preventing them from sliding around. Thin or flimsy picks might not hold the mozzarella and tomatoes securely, making serving and eating messy.
Add salt and pepper just before serving.
Seasoning at the last moment ensures the salt doesn’t draw out too much moisture from the tomatoes or basil, which could make the skewers watery. It also preserves the fresh texture and vibrant colors of the ingredients.
Serve the skewers at room temperature for best flavor.
Serving chilled mozzarella and tomatoes can mute their flavors. Letting them sit out for 10-15 minutes before serving allows the flavors to open up and meld with the balsamic glaze, creating a more satisfying taste experience.
Make the balsamic glaze a day ahead if needed.
Preparing the glaze in advance saves time on party day and lets the flavors deepen overnight. Just rewarm gently before drizzling to restore its syrupy consistency. This tip helps streamline prep without sacrificing quality.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Rushing the balsamic reduction.

Why it happens Many cooks want to save time and turn up the heat to speed up the glaze, but this causes burning or uneven caramelization.

Fix Simmer the vinegar and honey mixture on low heat, stirring occasionally, and be patient until it reduces by half. Watch closely near the end to avoid burning and remove from heat as soon as it thickens.

Using wet mozzarella or basil.

Why it happens Skipping the drying step or using packaged mozzarella without draining leads to soggy skewers.

Fix Drain mozzarella balls on paper towels and gently pat dry basil leaves before assembling. This keeps the skewers fresh and prevents the glaze from sliding off.

Overcrowding the skewers.

Why it happens Trying to fit too many ingredients on one skewer can cause them to fall apart or not cook evenly.

Fix Stick to the recommended pattern: one tomato, one basil leaf, one mozzarella ball per skewer. This balance ensures each bite is perfect and the skewers hold together.

Drizzling balsamic glaze too early.

Why it happens Applying the glaze too far ahead causes basil to wilt and ingredients to get soggy.

Fix Wait to drizzle the glaze until right before serving. This preserves the fresh look and texture of the skewers.

Not seasoning the skewers.

Why it happens Skipping salt and pepper leaves the flavors flat and less vibrant.

Fix Lightly season each skewer with salt and freshly cracked black pepper just before serving to enhance all the natural flavors.

Variations

Prosciutto Caprese Skewers

Add a thin slice of prosciutto between the mozzarella and basil for a salty, savory twist. This variation adds richness and a delightful contrast to the fresh ingredients, making it perfect for more indulgent occasions.

Note: Use thinly sliced prosciutto and fold it to fit neatly on the skewer without overpowering the other ingredients.

Pesto Drizzled Caprese Skewers

Swap the balsamic glaze for a drizzle of fresh basil pesto. This adds a vibrant herbal flavor and a creamy texture that complements the mozzarella and tomatoes beautifully, offering a fresh, aromatic alternative.

Note: Use a thin pesto drizzle to avoid overpowering the skewers and maintain balance in flavors.

Caprese Skewers with Avocado

Add small cubes of ripe avocado to the skewers for extra creaminess and a subtle buttery flavor. This variation adds richness and makes the skewers more filling, great for a light lunch or snack.

Note: Add avocado last to prevent browning and serve skewers soon after assembling.

Spicy Caprese Skewers

Sprinkle red pepper flakes on the assembled skewers or add a slice of jalapeño for a spicy kick. This is perfect if you like a little heat balancing the sweetness of the glaze and freshness of the basil.

Note: Adjust spice level carefully to avoid overpowering the delicate flavors.

Vegan Caprese Skewers

Replace mozzarella with firm tofu cubes marinated in lemon juice and herbs for a vegan-friendly version. This keeps the fresh vibe while offering a plant-based protein alternative.

Note: Press tofu well to remove excess water and marinate for at least 30 minutes before assembling.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These skewers can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with plastic wrap or foil to prevent drying out. Beyond that, freshness and safety decline, especially in warm weather.

Refrigerator

Store leftover skewers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 day. Note that basil leaves may darken and mozzarella can firm up when cold. Best served shortly after removing from fridge or at room temperature.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as fresh tomatoes and basil lose texture and flavor when thawed, and mozzarella becomes rubbery. The balsamic glaze can be frozen separately for up to 2 months in a sealed container.

Reheating

No reheating needed for these skewers. Serve chilled or at room temperature. If glaze thickens in fridge, warm slightly in a microwave-safe container for 10-15 seconds and stir before drizzling.

Make Ahead

Prepare the balsamic glaze up to 2 days ahead and store in the fridge. Wash and dry tomatoes and basil leaves in advance. Assemble skewers no more than 1 hour before serving and drizzle glaze just before presenting.

FAQ

Can I use regular mozzarella instead of fresh mozzarella balls?
Fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini) are best because they’re soft, creamy, and hold their shape well on skewers. Regular shredded mozzarella lacks the same texture and moisture balance, which might make the skewers less appealing. If you must substitute, cut a block of fresh mozzarella into bite-sized cubes and drain well.
How long does the balsamic glaze keep in the fridge?
The balsamic glaze can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. Before using, warm it slightly to restore its syrupy consistency. Avoid freezing it directly on the skewers as it may crystallize and lose flavor.
Can I make these skewers vegan?
Absolutely! Replace mozzarella with firm tofu cubes marinated in lemon juice and herbs or use vegan cheese alternatives. Keep the rest of the ingredients the same. The balsamic glaze and fresh basil still provide great flavor and freshness.
What’s the best way to prevent basil from wilting?
Keep basil leaves dry and add the balsamic glaze just before serving. Also, assemble the skewers no more than an hour before eating and store them covered in the fridge if needed. Avoid prolonged refrigeration after glazing, as moisture and acidity can cause wilting.
Can I use a store-bought balsamic glaze?
Yes, store-bought glaze works fine and saves time. Just check the ingredients for added sugars or preservatives if you prefer a natural version. Homemade glaze tends to have a fresher flavor and better thickness but both are delicious.
How do I keep the skewers from falling apart?
Use sturdy wooden skewers or toothpicks and thread the ingredients firmly but gently. Folding basil leaves before threading helps them stay put. Avoid overcrowding the skewers to maintain balance and stability.
Can I add other ingredients to the skewers?
Definitely! You can add olives, small pieces of prosciutto, or even avocado cubes. Just keep the flavors balanced and be mindful of texture differences. For example, add avocado last to prevent browning.
What’s a good substitute for honey in the glaze?
Maple syrup or agave nectar are great vegan-friendly substitutes for honey in the balsamic glaze. They offer similar sweetness and help with caramelization during reduction without altering the flavor too much.
