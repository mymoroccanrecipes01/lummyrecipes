Fudgy Avocado Brownies with a Tangy Raspberry Swirl for Cozy Weekend Treats

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 17, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Medium Difficulty

These rich brownies swap butter for ripe avocado, giving them a creamy texture and deep chocolate flavor. The raspberry swirl adds a fresh, tangy zing that balances the sweetness. Perfect for sharing on a relaxed weekend!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this avocado brownie recipe when I wanted a healthier twist on a classic treat. Using ripe avocado instead of butter sounded unusual, but the creamy texture it adds is incredible. My first batch was a bit too dense, but after tweaking the amount of flour and adding a raspberry swirl, it turned out perfectly fudgy and balanced. The raspberry swirl isn’t just for looks—it cuts through the chocolate richness with a bright, tangy note that makes every bite exciting. I love making these on weekends when I have some extra time, and they’re always a hit with friends. What makes this version special is how the avocado keeps the brownies moist without any greasy feel, plus the chocolate stays rich and deep thanks to melted dark chocolate. When you bake these, expect a dense, chewy texture with a glossy, crackly top and that lovely hint of fruitiness from the raspberry swirl. It’s a real crowd-pleaser and a fun way to sneak a superfood into dessert!

What You'll Need

8 fudgy brownies

  • 1 cup ripe avocado, mashed
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 ounces dark chocolate, melted
  • 1/2 cup fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving some overhang for easy removal. This step ensures even baking and prevents sticking, which is crucial for getting those perfect fudgy edges without burning. Be sure to smooth the parchment into the corners so the batter spreads evenly, and avoid using a pan that's too large or small to maintain the right thickness.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Oven preheated to 350°F and pan fully lined, ready for batter
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the mashed avocado and sugar until smooth and creamy. The avocado should be ripe and soft to blend well, providing moisture and fat without heaviness. Mixing thoroughly at this stage ensures the sugar dissolves evenly, which helps the brownies bake up with a tender crumb rather than grainy texture.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks smooth with no visible lumps of avocado
Step 3
Add the eggs and vanilla extract to the avocado mixture, whisking until fully combined. The eggs act as a binder and give structure, while the vanilla enhances the chocolate flavor. Whisk just until mixed to avoid overworking, which can toughen the brownies.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Mixture is glossy and homogenous with no streaks
Step 4
Sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt into a separate bowl. Sifting removes lumps and aerates the dry ingredients, which helps create a lighter texture and prevents dense spots in the brownies. Take your time here because clumps can cause uneven baking.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are light, fine, and lump-free
Step 5
Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet avocado mixture using a spatula. Mixing gently preserves the air you incorporated, which is key for a tender, fudgy crumb. Overmixing can develop gluten and make the brownies cakey, so stop as soon as the flour disappears.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Batter looks thick, smooth, and uniform with no flour streaks
Step 6
Stir in the melted dark chocolate until fully combined. Adding melted chocolate enriches the flavor and improves the fudgy texture by increasing fat content. Make sure the chocolate isn’t too hot or it might scramble the eggs; it should be warm but pourable.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Batter is glossy and deep chocolate brown
Step 7
Pour half the batter into the prepared pan and smooth it out evenly. Then mix the fresh raspberries with powdered sugar in a small bowl and gently swirl this mixture over the batter using a knife or skewer. Finally, spoon the remaining batter on top and swirl again lightly. This layering and swirling technique creates beautiful raspberry ribbons that add a fresh tartness to every bite.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Pan shows visible swirls of pink raspberry within the chocolate batter
Step 8
Bake the brownies for 25-30 minutes, checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center. The toothpick should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Baking too long dries out the brownies; too short and they’ll be undercooked and gummy. Keep an eye on the edges—they should be firm and slightly pulled away from the pan.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Edges are set and toothpick has moist crumbs, not wet batter
Step 9
Allow the brownies to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack before slicing. Cooling lets the structure set and flavors meld, preventing them from falling apart when cut. Use the parchment overhang to lift the brownies out gently, then slice with a sharp knife for clean edges. Serve at room temperature to enjoy the full fudgy texture and raspberry brightness.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Brownies are firm to touch and cool to room temperature

Pro Tips

Use very ripe avocados for the creamiest texture.
Ripe avocados mash smoothly and integrate well without lumps, which is essential for a uniform batter and fudgy brownies. Unripe avocados are firmer and can create uneven texture and bitterness, negatively impacting the final result.
Melt chocolate gently over a double boiler or low heat.
Chocolate can seize or burn if overheated, ruining its smoothness and flavor. Melting it slowly preserves the texture and ensures it blends seamlessly into the batter, contributing to the fudgy richness.
Don’t overmix the batter once flour is added.
Overmixing activates gluten, which can make brownies cakey and tough instead of tender and fudgy. Folding just until combined keeps the crumb delicate and moist.
Line the pan with parchment paper for easy removal.
Parchment prevents sticking and allows you to lift the brownies out cleanly without breaking, which is especially important for dense, fudgy brownies that can stick to the pan.
Use fresh raspberries for the swirl, not frozen.
Frozen raspberries release more water, which can thin the swirl mixture and affect the batter’s consistency, resulting in uneven baking and less distinct raspberry flavor.
Let brownies cool completely before slicing.
Cooling allows the structure to set and prevents crumbling. Warm brownies are more fragile and can fall apart when cut.
Test doneness with a toothpick for moist crumbs.
Pulling brownies out too early means raw batter; too late dries them out. A toothpick with moist crumbs ensures the perfect fudgy texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe avocado

Why it happens Sometimes people grab any avocado without checking ripeness, assuming it will mash fine.

Fix Always use soft, ripe avocados that yield easily to pressure. If unsure, let them ripen a day or two more. This ensures smooth texture and avoids bitterness.

Overbaking brownies

Why it happens It’s tempting to bake until fully dry to avoid soggy centers, but brownies need some moistness.

Fix Check at 25 minutes and remove when a toothpick comes out with moist crumbs. This keeps brownies fudgy and tender, not dry or crumbly.

Skipping sifting of dry ingredients

Why it happens It’s easy to skip sifting for speed, but lumps can remain unnoticed.

Fix Always sift cocoa powder and flour to avoid clumps that cause uneven texture and baking spots.

Overmixing after adding flour

Why it happens Some think thorough mixing makes batter smoother, but it develops gluten.

Fix Fold flour in gently just until no streaks remain to keep brownies tender and fudgy.

Using frozen raspberries for the swirl

Why it happens Frozen berries are convenient but can release too much water.

Fix Use fresh raspberries for a thicker, more vibrant swirl and better flavor contrast.

Variations

Nutty Avocado Brownies

Add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans for crunch and a toasty flavor contrast. The nuts add texture and richness without overpowering the chocolate or raspberry notes. Great for nut lovers seeking extra bite.

Note: Fold nuts into batter gently before pouring into the pan to distribute evenly without breaking the fragile batter.

Vegan Avocado Brownies

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flax + 3 tbsp water per egg) and use dairy-free dark chocolate. This keeps the brownies moist and fudgy while making them fully plant-based. Expect a slightly denser texture.

Note: Allow flax eggs to gel for 5 minutes before mixing and bake a few minutes longer if needed to ensure doneness.

Spiced Raspberry Swirl Brownies

Add 1/2 tsp cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne to the batter for a warm, spicy kick that complements the raspberry swirl beautifully. This variation offers a festive twist perfect for holidays.

Note: Mix spices with dry ingredients to ensure even flavor distribution.

White Chocolate Raspberry Swirl Brownies

Swap dark chocolate for melted white chocolate in the batter and use the raspberry swirl as usual. This creates a sweeter, creamier brownie with a bright fruit contrast, ideal for those who prefer milder chocolate.

Note: White chocolate melts differently; melt gently and cool slightly before mixing to avoid curdling.

Gluten-Free Avocado Brownies

Use a gluten-free flour blend instead of all-purpose flour to make these brownies safe for gluten-sensitive folks. The fudgy texture remains intact, though the crumb might be slightly more tender.

Note: Ensure the flour blend contains xanthan gum or add 1/4 tsp for structure.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These brownies can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 days if stored in an airtight container away from direct sunlight. Covering well prevents them from drying out and keeps the raspberry swirl fresh.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The brownies will firm up and may lose some fudginess but the flavor stays vibrant. Let them come to room temperature before serving or warm briefly for softness.

Freezer

Wrap brownies tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm slightly to restore texture. Proper wrapping prevents freezer burn and preserves flavor.

Reheating

Reheat brownies gently in a microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Add a small splash of milk or cream if they feel dry. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out or toughening the texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bring to room temperature and give a gentle stir before baking. The raspberry swirl is best added fresh right before baking to maintain its bright flavor and appearance.

FAQ

Can I substitute avocado with butter in this recipe?
Yes, you can substitute butter for avocado using a 1:1 ratio, but the texture and flavor will change. Butter yields a more traditional brownie texture and richer taste, while avocado gives a creamier, slightly lighter result with added nutrients.
What if I don't have fresh raspberries for the swirl?
You can use frozen raspberries, but thaw and drain excess liquid first to avoid thinning the batter. Alternatively, raspberry jam or preserves can work, but use less sugar in the batter to prevent over-sweetness.
How do I know when the brownies are done baking?
Insert a toothpick in the center after 25 minutes. It should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter. This ensures fudgy brownies without being undercooked or dry.
Can I make these brownies gluten-free?
Absolutely! Replace the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that contains xanthan gum for structure. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and fudgy.
Why do my brownies sometimes turn out dry?
Dry brownies usually result from overbaking or using too much flour. Make sure to measure flour correctly and bake just until a toothpick shows moist crumbs. Also, ripe avocado helps keep moisture locked in.
Can I store these brownies in the freezer?
Yes, brownies freeze well. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and place in an airtight container or freezer bag. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm slightly before serving for best texture.
Is the avocado flavor noticeable in the brownies?
When ripe and properly mashed, avocado flavor is very subtle and mostly adds creaminess. The strong chocolate and raspberry flavors dominate, so you won’t really taste the avocado itself.
Can I add nuts to the batter?
Definitely! Chopped walnuts or pecans add a lovely crunch and depth of flavor. Fold them in gently before baking to avoid breaking the batter’s delicate structure.
