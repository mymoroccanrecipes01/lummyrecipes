One Pot Party Pasta: Easy, Flavorful, and Perfect for Big Groups

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This one pot pasta is a total game changer—everything cooks together, meaning less mess and maximum flavor. I love how the tomatoes and herbs meld for a fresh, vibrant taste. Great for casual dinners or feeding a crowd!

Pro Tips

Don’t rush sautéing the onions; cook them low and slow until translucent.
Slow cooking onions releases their natural sugars, creating a sweeter, more complex flavor base. Rushing or cooking on too high heat risks browning or burning, which adds bitterness and harshness that can overpower the dish.
Stir the pasta regularly while it simmers to prevent sticking.
Pasta releases starch as it cooks, which can cause it to stick together or to the pot. Frequent gentle stirring ensures even cooking and prevents clumps, resulting in a creamy, cohesive sauce rather than a sticky mess.
Add fresh basil only after removing the pot from heat.
Basil’s aromatic oils are heat sensitive and evaporate quickly. Adding it off heat preserves its vibrant flavor and bright color, enhancing the freshness of the finished dish.
Let the pasta rest covered for a few minutes after cooking.
Resting allows the pasta to absorb remaining liquid gently, finishing the cooking process without becoming mushy. This step thickens the sauce naturally and improves texture.
Use low-sodium broth to control the saltiness of the dish.
Broth brands vary widely in salt content. Using low-sodium lets you season gradually and avoid an overly salty final dish, especially since canned tomatoes and cheese also add salt.
Grate Parmesan fresh instead of using pre-grated cheese.
Freshly grated Parmesan melts better and has a richer, fresher flavor. Pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents that prevent smooth melting and can affect texture.
Press the pasta down gently into the liquid when first added.
Ensuring the pasta is submerged helps it cook evenly. Pasta exposed above the liquid cooks unevenly and can dry out or stay hard.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding basil too early in the cooking process.

Why it happens Many cooks add herbs at the start out of habit, not realizing fresh basil loses its flavor and color when cooked too long.

Fix Wait until the pasta is fully cooked and the pot is off heat before stirring in chopped basil. This preserves its bright aroma and fresh taste.

Cooking pasta uncovered for the entire time without stirring.

Why it happens People often underestimate how much starch pasta releases and forget to stir, leading to clumping.

Fix Stir every few minutes during simmering to prevent sticking and promote even cooking.

Using water instead of broth.

Why it happens Sometimes cooks use what’s on hand and skip broth, not realizing how much flavor it adds.

Fix Use vegetable or chicken broth to add savory depth; if you must use water, boost flavor with extra herbs or seasoning.

Overcooking the pasta until mushy.

Why it happens Some think longer cooking makes pasta softer, but it ruins texture and turns the dish gluey.

Fix Check pasta a couple minutes before package instructions and remove from heat once al dente; let it rest to finish cooking gently.

Not tasting and adjusting seasoning at the end.

Why it happens It’s easy to assume the dish is seasoned properly, but canned tomatoes and broth vary widely in saltiness.

Fix Always taste before serving and add salt, pepper, or a squeeze of lemon juice to balance flavors perfectly.

Variations

Spicy One Pot Party Pasta

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes during the onion sauté step to give the dish a nice kick. You can also toss in some sliced spicy sausage or chorizo for extra heat and protein. The spicy notes complement the fresh basil and tomatoes beautifully, making it a crowd-pleaser for those who like a bit of fire.

Note: Monitor the heat carefully when adding red pepper flakes to avoid bitterness; add sausage early so it cooks through.

Creamy One Pot Pasta

Stir in 1/2 cup of heavy cream or coconut milk after the pasta has cooked and before adding basil. This gives the sauce a rich, silky texture that’s comforting and indulgent. It’s perfect if you want a creamier, less tomato-forward dish without losing the fresh herb flavor.

Note: Add cream off heat to prevent curdling and stir gently to combine.

Vegan One Pot Pasta

Simply omit the Parmesan cheese or replace it with a vegan cheese alternative or nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor. Use vegetable broth and skip any animal products to keep it fully plant-based. This version still delivers on flavor and is great for vegan guests or anyone avoiding dairy.

Note: Nutritional yeast should be added off heat to preserve its flavor and avoid bitterness.

Loaded Veggie One Pot Pasta

Add extra diced vegetables like zucchini, mushrooms, or spinach along with the bell pepper to boost nutrition and texture variety. This makes the dish heartier and colorful, great for sneaking in veggies for picky eaters. The extra moisture from veggies may require adding a bit more broth.

Note: Add softer veggies like spinach at the end to avoid overcooking.

Cheesy Baked One Pot Pasta

After cooking, transfer the pasta to a baking dish, top with shredded mozzarella and Parmesan, then bake at 375°F for 10-15 minutes until bubbly and golden. This adds a crispy, cheesy crust that transforms the dish into a comforting casserole perfect for cooler nights.

Note: Make sure pasta isn’t too dry before baking; add a splash of broth if needed.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

You can keep the pasta at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered with foil or a lid. Leaving it out longer risks bacterial growth, so refrigerate leftovers promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The pasta tends to thicken and firm up in the fridge; reheat gently with a splash of broth or water to loosen the sauce and restore creaminess.

Freezer

Freeze the pasta in a freezer-safe container or heavy-duty bag for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible before sealing. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of broth or water, stirring frequently until heated through. Alternatively, microwave covered in 1-minute bursts, stirring in between. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the entire dish up to the resting step a day ahead. Store covered in the fridge, then reheat gently and stir in fresh basil and cheese just before serving to keep flavors bright.

FAQ

Can I use a different type of pasta for this recipe?
Absolutely! While penne works great because it holds sauce well, you can use rigatoni, ziti, or even fusilli. Just keep in mind that cooking times may vary slightly depending on pasta shape and thickness, so watch it closely to avoid overcooking.
What if I don’t have vegetable broth on hand?
If you don’t have vegetable broth, you can substitute with chicken broth or even water. Using broth adds more flavor, but if you use water, consider boosting the dish with extra herbs, garlic, or a splash of soy sauce to add depth.
How do I prevent the pasta from sticking to the pot?
Stirring regularly during cooking is key to prevent sticking. Also, make sure the pasta is fully submerged in liquid and maintain a gentle simmer rather than a rolling boil to avoid evaporation and clumping.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! Use your favorite gluten-free pasta and ensure the broth and canned tomatoes are gluten-free as well. Cooking times might be a bit shorter, so keep an eye on the pasta texture to avoid mushiness.
Why does the pasta sometimes turn mushy?
Overcooking pasta or cooking at too high a temperature can cause mushiness. To avoid this, simmer gently, check for doneness early, and remove from heat when pasta is just al dente. Letting it rest off heat also helps finish cooking without getting soggy.
Can I add protein to this dish?
Definitely! You can stir in cooked chicken, sausage, or beans for extra protein. Add cooked proteins toward the end to warm through without drying out. For a vegetarian option, chickpeas or tofu work well.
Is this recipe suitable for meal prep?
Yes, it stores well in the fridge and can be reheated easily. Keep fresh basil and cheese separate until serving to maintain freshness. Portion into containers for quick lunches or dinners throughout the week.
How can I make this recipe spicier?
Add red pepper flakes or a diced jalapeño during the sauté step for heat. You can also drizzle hot sauce over the finished dish. Adjust the amount to your taste, starting small to avoid overpowering the flavors.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 servings

  • 1 pound dry penne pasta
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes with juice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by heating the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté for about 4-5 minutes until it becomes soft and translucent. This step is crucial because it builds the base flavor for the whole dish. Cooking the onion gently releases its sweetness, which balances the acidity of the tomatoes later on. Avoid rushing this step or turning the heat too high — you want the onion to soften, not brown or burn, which can add bitterness.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Onions are soft, translucent, and slightly fragrant without any browning
Step 2
Add the minced garlic and diced red bell pepper to the pot. Stir them around for about 1-2 minutes until the garlic is fragrant but not browned. Garlic cooks quickly and can turn bitter if overcooked, so keep a close eye and stir constantly. The bell pepper adds a subtle sweetness and texture contrast that makes this pasta more interesting. If you skip this, the dish could taste a bit flat, so don’t rush it!
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Garlic is fragrant, and bell pepper is slightly softened but still bright in color
Step 3
Pour in the can of diced tomatoes with their juice, then add the vegetable broth. Stir everything together to combine. The broth is what will cook the pasta, so it’s important to use enough liquid for even cooking. Using vegetable broth instead of water adds depth and a richer flavor. If you use water, the dish will still work but might lack that savory punch. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium-high heat.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Mixture is bubbling gently with no large boiling splashes
Step 4
Once boiling, add the dry penne pasta, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir well to make sure the pasta is submerged in the liquid. This step is key because the pasta needs to cook evenly. If it’s not fully covered, some pieces will be undercooked. You can press the pasta down gently with a spoon to help it soak up the broth. Reduce the heat to medium-low to maintain a gentle simmer.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Pasta is mostly covered by liquid and simmering gently
Step 5
Let the pasta cook uncovered for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Keep the heat at a low simmer — too high and the liquid will evaporate before the pasta cooks through, too low and the pasta may stick to the bottom. Stirring helps distribute heat evenly and stops the noodles from clumping. Check the pasta texture around 12 minutes to avoid overcooking. You want it tender but with a bit of bite (al dente).
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Pasta is tender with a slight firmness in the center, liquid mostly absorbed
Step 6
Turn off the heat and stir in the fresh chopped basil. Cover the pot with a lid and let it sit for 3-5 minutes. This resting period allows the pasta to finish absorbing the sauce and lets the basil infuse its flavor without cooking it, which keeps it bright and fresh. Skipping this step means you miss out on that aromatic punch that makes this dish so special.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Basil looks vibrant and the sauce has thickened slightly
Step 7
If you like, stir in grated Parmesan cheese just before serving. The cheese adds a creamy, salty finish that complements the tomato base beautifully. Make sure to add it off the heat to prevent the cheese from clumping or becoming stringy. If you’re dairy-free, try a sprinkle of nutritional yeast or leave it out entirely — the pasta is delicious either way.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Cheese is melted smoothly into the pasta, enhancing creaminess
Step 8
Taste and adjust seasoning with extra salt and pepper if needed. Because the broth and tomatoes vary in saltiness, this final step helps balance flavors perfectly. Don’t skip tasting! It’s the best way to make sure your dish is just right. If it tastes a bit flat, a squeeze of lemon juice can brighten it up.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with no overpowering salt or blandness
Step 9
Serve the pasta hot, garnished with extra basil or Parmesan if you like. This dish pairs wonderfully with a simple green salad or crusty bread. Since everything cooks in one pot, cleanup is quick, leaving you more time to enjoy your company. Leftovers reheat well and taste even better the next day!
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Pasta plated attractively with fresh garnish, steaming hot
