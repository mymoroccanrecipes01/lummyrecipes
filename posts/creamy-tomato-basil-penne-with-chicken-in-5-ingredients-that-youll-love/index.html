Creamy Tomato Basil Penne with Chicken: A Cozy Weeknight Dinner

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This creamy tomato basil penne with chicken is a total lifesaver on busy nights. With just five ingredients, it’s packed with fresh basil and a velvety sauce that feels like a warm hug. Ideal for casual dinners!

Introduction

I first stumbled on this creamy tomato basil penne with chicken during a hectic week when I needed dinner on the table fast but still wanted something comforting. What I love about this recipe is how it uses just a handful of ingredients but delivers big on flavor — especially thanks to the fresh basil that brightens up the rich tomato cream sauce. One trick I learned early on is to cook the chicken pieces just until golden so they stay juicy and tender rather than drying out. Also, stirring in the Parmesan at the end helps thicken the sauce and adds a subtle nuttiness that makes the dish feel extra special. The final dish is creamy, cozy, and has just the right balance of tangy tomato and fragrant basil. It’s become my go-to for busy weeknights or relaxed dinners with friends, and I think you’ll love how simple yet satisfying it is. Plus, it reheats beautifully, so leftovers are a bonus!

Why This Works

This recipe succeeds because of the harmony between its simple yet carefully balanced ingredients and techniques. The fresh basil is key — its bright, aromatic oils contrast beautifully with the richness of the cream and the acidity of the tomato sauce, preventing the dish from feeling heavy or one-dimensional. Adding basil at the end preserves its flavor and color, which would otherwise fade with prolonged cooking.

The chicken is cooked just right to develop a golden crust through the Maillard reaction, which adds complex, savory notes beyond plain boiled meat. Cooking it in batches avoids steaming and ensures each piece caramelizes properly, locking in juices and texture.

The cream is gently folded into the tomato sauce without boiling, which prevents curdling and creates that velvety mouthfeel. This emulsification of cream and tomato sauce balances acidity and fat perfectly. Reserving pasta water to adjust sauce consistency is a classic technique that uses the starch in the water to help the sauce cling to the pasta, creating a cohesive dish instead of a dry mix.

Finally, stirring in Parmesan cheese adds umami and helps thicken the sauce further, enhancing the luxurious texture. This layering of flavors and textures — fresh herbs, creamy sauce, well-cooked chicken, and perfectly al dente pasta — is what makes this recipe so comforting and satisfying every time.

  • Choose a good-quality dried penne pasta with ridges to help the sauce cling better. Look for pasta made from 100% durum wheat semolina for firm texture and bite. Avoid overcooking to prevent mushiness; al dente pasta absorbs sauce well without becoming soggy.
  • Fresh, boneless skinless chicken breasts work best here because they cook quickly and evenly. Cut into bite-sized pieces for even cooking and easy eating. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure proper browning and juicy texture rather than steaming.
  • Fresh basil leaves add essential brightness and aroma to the dish. Pick vibrant green leaves without blemishes or wilting. Chop just before adding to preserve flavor. Dried basil is not a good substitute as it lacks the fresh herbal notes and can taste flat.
  • Use heavy cream with at least 36% fat content for the richest, creamiest sauce. Lower-fat creams or milk won’t emulsify the sauce well and may curdle when heated. Add cream slowly over low heat to keep the sauce smooth and prevent separation.
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese provides salty, nutty umami that deepens the sauce’s flavor. Pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can affect melting and texture. Stir in at the end to melt gently without clumping.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces penne pasta, uncooked
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/3 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil to cook the penne pasta. Using plenty of water helps prevent the pasta from sticking and ensures even cooking. Add the penne and cook according to package instructions until al dente — usually around 10-12 minutes. Avoid overcooking because the pasta will continue to soften slightly when combined with the sauce later. Drain the pasta, reserving about half a cup of the pasta water, which you’ll use to loosen the sauce if needed.

    ⏱ 12 minutes✓ Pasta should be tender but still firm to the bite with no mushiness.
  2. Step 2

    While the pasta cooks, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the bite-sized chicken pieces, seasoning them with salt and pepper. Cook without moving them too much so they develop a nice golden crust, about 4-5 minutes per side. This browning adds flavor through the Maillard reaction. Be careful not to overcrowd the pan; if needed, cook the chicken in batches to maintain heat and avoid steaming. Once cooked through and golden, remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Chicken should be golden brown on the outside and cooked through with no pink inside.
  3. Step 3

    In the same skillet, reduce heat to medium and pour in the tomato sauce. Use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits left from the chicken — these add extra depth to the sauce. Let the sauce simmer gently for 3-4 minutes to thicken slightly and develop flavor. If the sauce feels too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it. This step is important because simmering melds the flavors and helps the sauce coat the pasta nicely.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Sauce should be slightly thickened and aromatic with no raw tomato smell.
  4. Step 4

    Pour the heavy cream into the tomato sauce and stir well to combine. The cream softens the acidity of the tomato and creates that luscious, velvety texture that makes this dish so comforting. Keep the sauce over low heat — avoid boiling to prevent the cream from curdling. Stir gently and let it warm through for about 3 minutes. This slow incorporation ensures the sauce stays smooth and silky.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Sauce appears creamy and smooth with a rich pinkish color.
  5. Step 5

    Return the cooked chicken pieces to the skillet and stir them into the creamy tomato sauce. Let everything heat together for 2-3 minutes so the chicken absorbs some of the sauce flavors. This melding step makes the dish taste cohesive rather than like separate components thrown together. Keep the heat low to avoid drying out the chicken.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Chicken is hot and well coated with the creamy sauce.
  6. Step 6

    Add the drained penne pasta to the skillet with the sauce and chicken. Toss everything gently but thoroughly to combine. If the sauce feels too thick or dry, splash in a bit more reserved pasta water to reach your desired consistency. This technique helps the sauce cling to the pasta perfectly, making each bite flavorful and moist.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Pasta is evenly coated and glossy with sauce, no dry spots.
  7. Step 7

    Stir in the chopped fresh basil, folding it into the pasta just before serving. The basil adds a vibrant, herbal brightness that cuts through the richness of the sauce and lifts the whole dish. Adding it last preserves the fresh flavor and bright green color. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Basil is fragrant and vivid green, evenly distributed without wilting.
  8. Step 8

    Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the pasta and give it one final gentle toss. The cheese adds a nutty, salty finish and helps thicken the sauce a bit more. Don’t skip this step — it really rounds out the flavors and makes the dish feel more indulgent. Serve immediately while warm.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Cheese is melted slightly and incorporated into the sauce.
  9. Step 9

    Plate the creamy tomato basil penne with chicken and garnish with a few extra basil leaves if you like. This finishing touch adds a pretty pop of color and freshness. Serve with a simple green salad or garlic bread for a complete meal. Enjoy the comforting, creamy flavors with every forkful!

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Dish looks vibrant and inviting with fresh basil garnish.

Pro Tips

  • Reserve pasta water before draining to adjust sauce consistency. — The starchy pasta water acts as a natural thickener and helps the sauce cling to the pasta. Without it, the sauce might be too thick or dry. Adding small amounts gradually allows you to reach the perfect creamy texture.
  • Don’t overcrowd the skillet when cooking chicken. — Crowding traps steam and prevents browning, resulting in less flavor and rubbery texture. Cooking in batches allows each piece to sear properly, locking in juices and developing a golden crust that adds depth.
  • Add fresh basil at the very end of cooking. — Basil’s delicate oils and color degrade quickly with heat. Adding it last preserves its bright flavor and vibrant green hue, giving the dish fresh herbal notes that balance the creamy tomato sauce.
  • Use medium-low heat when combining cream and tomato sauce. — High heat can cause the cream to curdle or separate. Gently warming the mixture allows the cream to emulsify smoothly with the acidic tomato, creating a rich, velvety sauce without lumps.
  • Grate Parmesan cheese fresh for best melting and flavor. — Pre-grated cheese often contains additives that prevent smooth melting and can alter texture. Freshly grated cheese melts uniformly and adds a clean, nutty umami that enhances the sauce’s richness.
  • Cook pasta just until al dente and avoid rinsing it. — Rinsing removes surface starch that helps sauce stick. Al dente pasta has a firm bite that holds up well when tossed with sauce, preventing mushiness and blending flavors perfectly.
  • Scrape browned bits from the skillet after cooking chicken. — These browned bits contain concentrated flavor compounds from the Maillard reaction. Incorporating them into the sauce adds depth and richness, elevating the overall taste.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overcooking the chicken until dry.
    Why: In a rush or due to high heat, cooks may leave chicken on the pan too long, thinking it needs thorough cooking.
    Fix: Cook chicken over medium-high heat in batches, flipping once to get a golden crust. Remove as soon as it’s opaque and juices run clear to keep it juicy and tender.
  • Boiling the cream-tomato mixture causing curdling.
    Why: High heat can destabilize the cream proteins when mixed with acidic tomato sauce.
    Fix: Lower the heat to medium-low and stir gently as you add cream. Warm slowly without boiling to maintain a smooth, silky sauce.
  • Adding basil too early, losing fresh flavor.
    Why: Basil is added during cooking instead of at the end out of habit or oversight.
    Fix: Chop and stir fresh basil into the pasta right before serving to keep its bright color and aroma intact.
  • Overcooking pasta past al dente.
    Why: Following package times blindly or letting pasta sit too long after draining.
    Fix: Test pasta a minute or two before package time; it should be firm but not hard. Drain promptly and toss with sauce to finish cooking gently.
  • Not reserving pasta water before draining.
    Why: Many forget this step and miss out on a natural sauce thickener.
    Fix: Always scoop out a cup of pasta water before draining. Use it to adjust sauce texture and help it coat the pasta perfectly.

Variations

  • Spicy Creamy Tomato Basil Penne: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes when cooking the tomato sauce for a subtle heat that complements the creamy richness. This variation adds a nice kick without overpowering the fresh basil flavor, making it perfect for spice lovers.
    Simmer the sauce gently with the red pepper flakes to infuse the heat evenly; adjust quantity to taste.
  • Vegetarian Creamy Tomato Basil Penne: Skip the chicken and toss in sautéed mushrooms or roasted vegetables like zucchini and bell peppers. The cream sauce remains the star, and the veggies add texture and earthiness for a hearty meatless meal.
    Cook vegetables until tender and caramelized to maximize flavor before adding the sauce.
  • Penne with Pesto and Cherry Tomatoes: Swap out the tomato sauce for a fresh basil pesto and toss with halved cherry tomatoes and grilled chicken. This version is lighter but still bursting with basil flavor and makes a colorful, fresh alternative.
    Add pesto off heat to preserve its bright, fresh taste and vibrant green color.
  • Gluten-Free Creamy Tomato Basil Penne: Use gluten-free penne pasta to make this dish suitable for gluten-sensitive diets. The sauce and chicken stay the same, so you get all the same creamy comfort without gluten worries.
    Cook gluten-free pasta carefully as it can overcook quickly; watch timing closely.
  • Creamy Tomato Basil Penne with Bacon: Add crispy bacon bits to the dish for a smoky, salty twist. Cook bacon first, then use the rendered fat to sauté chicken for extra flavor. This variation adds a delicious savory depth to the creamy sauce.
    Drain excess bacon fat if too much to avoid greasiness; add bacon at the end to keep crisp.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered to prevent drying out. Beyond that, bacterial growth increases, so refrigerate leftovers promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce thickens in the fridge; reheat gently on the stove with a splash of water or cream to loosen it. Avoid overheating to maintain creamy texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap container with plastic wrap or foil. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating. Sauce texture may change slightly but still tasty.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat over low heat on the stovetop, stirring occasionally, and add a splash of cream or water if the sauce is too thick. Microwave reheating can be uneven; stir halfway through to heat uniformly.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Cook the chicken and pasta separately up to 1 day ahead and store in the fridge. On serving day, warm the sauce and combine everything fresh to keep textures and flavors vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are juicier and add more flavor. Just cut them into similar-sized pieces and cook until golden and cooked through. They might take a couple minutes longer to cook than breasts, so watch closely to avoid overcooking.
What if I don’t have heavy cream?
You can substitute with half-and-half or whole milk, but the sauce won’t be quite as rich or creamy. To prevent curdling, add a teaspoon of flour or cornstarch mixed with a little water to help thicken the sauce when using lighter dairy.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
To make it vegan, swap the chicken for tofu or mushrooms, use coconut cream or cashew cream instead of heavy cream, and replace Parmesan with a vegan cheese or nutritional yeast. Keep the fresh basil for brightness!
How do I store leftovers to keep the pasta from getting mushy?
Store pasta and sauce separately if possible, or toss the pasta with a little olive oil before refrigerating to prevent sticking. Reheat gently with a splash of liquid to restore creaminess without overcooking.
Can I prepare this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! Use your favorite gluten-free penne pasta and follow cooking instructions carefully as gluten-free pasta can cook faster and become mushy if overdone.
What’s the best way to reheat this dish without drying it out?
Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring often and adding a splash of cream, milk, or water to loosen the sauce. Avoid high heat and microwaving without stirring to prevent drying or uneven heating.
Can I add other herbs besides basil?
Sure! Thyme or oregano can add nice complementary flavors, but basil’s fresh, sweet aroma is key here. Add other herbs sparingly so they don’t overpower the basil’s brightness.
How can I make this recipe spicier?
Add crushed red pepper flakes when simmering the tomato sauce or mix in a dash of hot sauce. Start with a small amount and adjust to your heat preference to keep the balance with the creamy sauce.
