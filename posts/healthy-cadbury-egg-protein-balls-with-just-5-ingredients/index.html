Protein Balls Inspired by Cadbury Eggs: A Simple High-Protein Snack

Published on March 26, 2026

0 min cook

These protein balls offer a straightforward, wholesome twist on the classic Cadbury egg. Made with just five ingredients, they provide a satisfying texture with a creamy center and a subtle, chocolatey exterior. Ideal for quick snacks or post-workout refueling, this recipe is approachable for any home cook seeking a protein boost.

Pro Tips

Dry ingredients like protein powder vary in absorbency; adjust the amount of coconut cream or peanut butter slightly to achieve a pliable dough.
Protein powders from different brands or types (whey vs plant-based) absorb moisture differently. Adding too much liquid makes the dough sticky and hard to shape; too little makes it crumbly.
Chill the balls before dipping in chocolate to prevent melting and ensure a smooth coating.
If the balls are too warm, the chocolate will melt unevenly or slide off, resulting in a messy finish. Cooling firms the balls to withstand the dipping process.
Use a fork to lift balls out of melted chocolate and tap gently to remove excess coating.
This prevents thick, uneven chocolate layers which can be clumpy or take longer to set. A thin coating provides a pleasant snap without overpowering the texture.
Store the finished protein balls in a single layer or separated by parchment paper to avoid sticking together.
The chocolate coating can become sticky at warmer temperatures and cause balls to clump. Proper storage preserves their shape and texture.
If the chocolate begins to harden while dipping, gently reheat it in short bursts to maintain a smooth consistency.
Chocolate can seize or thicken if left at room temperature too long. Regular stirring and gentle reheating ensure an even coating.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using protein powder that’s too coarse or grainy without mixing well.

Why it happens Some protein powders don’t dissolve or blend easily, leading to a gritty texture.

Fix Mix thoroughly and consider sifting protein powder before combining. Using a spoon or spatula with some pressing helps integrate ingredients better.

Skipping the chilling step before dipping in chocolate.

Why it happens Rushing to coat warm balls causes chocolate to melt unevenly and drip off.

Fix Chill balls firmly in the refrigerator for at least 15 minutes before coating to ensure they hold their shape.

Adding too much liquid coconut cream or syrup, resulting in sticky, unshapable dough.

Why it happens Liquid amounts vary; too much moisture loosens the dough, preventing it from rolling into balls.

Fix Add liquids gradually and stop when dough holds together but isn’t sticky. If too wet, add small amounts of protein powder or peanut butter to firm up.

Not tapping off excess chocolate after dipping.

Why it happens Thick chocolate coats take longer to set and can become clumpy or uneven.

Fix Use a fork and tap lightly to remove excess chocolate, ensuring a thin, smooth coating.

Variations

Vegan Protein Balls

Use plant-based vanilla protein powder, replace honey with maple syrup, and ensure peanut butter is natural and unsweetened.

Note: Replacing honey with maple syrup slightly reduces binding strength, so chill the dough longer before shaping to maintain firmness.

Chocolate Peanut Butter Protein Balls

Add 2 tablespoons (12g) unsweetened cocoa powder to the dough for a richer chocolate flavor inside the balls.

Note: Adding cocoa powder increases dryness; compensate with a slight increase in coconut cream or peanut butter to keep dough pliable.

Nut-Free Version

Replace peanut butter with sunflower seed butter and ensure protein powder is nut-free.

Note: Sunflower seed butter has a similar fat content and moisture but may impart a slightly different flavor and color.

Spiced Protein Balls

Add 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon and a pinch of nutmeg to the dough for a warm, aromatic twist.

Note: Spices do not affect texture but enhance flavor complexity, especially good for autumn or winter snacks.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Protein balls can be kept at room temperature for up to 4 hours in a cool place but are best refrigerated to maintain shape and freshness.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 7 days. The chocolate coating remains firm and the interior stays moist and fresh.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container or bag for up to 1 month. Thaw in the refrigerator for 1-2 hours before serving to avoid condensation affecting the chocolate coating.

Reheating

No reheating necessary. Serve chilled or at room temperature. If preferred, allow to sit out for 10 minutes to soften slightly.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough and shape balls up to 2 days ahead, keep refrigerated without coating. Dip in chocolate just before serving or storing to maintain freshness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make these protein balls ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the dough and shape balls a day or two ahead. Keep them refrigerated and coat with chocolate shortly before serving or storing.
Why are my protein balls crumbly and falling apart?
This usually happens if there's too little moisture or binding ingredients. Adjust by adding a bit more peanut butter or coconut cream to achieve a cohesive dough.
Can I use almond butter instead of peanut butter?
Yes, almond butter works well but may result in a slightly softer texture and milder flavor. Adjust moisture if needed.
How long do protein balls last in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, they keep fresh for up to 7 days with their texture and flavor intact.
Can I skip the chocolate coating?
Absolutely. The dough itself is flavorful and holds well without coating, but the chocolate adds texture contrast and depth.

Ingredients

About 12 protein balls

  • 1 cup (90g) vanilla protein powder (whey or plant-based)
  • 1/2 cup (120g) natural peanut butter (or almond butter as substitute)
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup (85g) dark chocolate chips or chopped dark chocolate (70% cocoa or higher)
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) coconut cream or full-fat coconut milk

Instructions

Step 1
In a mixing bowl, combine the vanilla protein powder, natural peanut butter, honey, and coconut cream. Stir thoroughly until the mixture forms a consistent, sticky dough. The texture should be firm enough to hold shape but pliable.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture clumps together without excess dryness or stickiness; smooth and uniform texture
Step 2
Using a tablespoon or small cookie scoop, portion the dough and roll into smooth balls with your hands. Aim for even sizes to ensure consistent chilling and coating.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls are well-formed, smooth on the surface, and hold shape without crumbling
Step 3
Place the protein balls on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate for 15 minutes to firm up slightly before coating.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls feel cool and slightly firmer to the touch, ready for chocolate coating
Step 4
While the balls chill, melt the dark chocolate gently using a double boiler or microwave in 20-second intervals, stirring between each until smooth and glossy.
⏱ Time: 3-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate is fully melted with no lumps or graininess
Step 5
Dip each chilled protein ball into the melted chocolate, coating evenly. Use a fork to lift and gently tap off excess chocolate before placing the ball back on the parchment paper.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls are fully coated with a thin, even layer of chocolate
Step 6
Return the coated balls to the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the chocolate to set completely.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate coating is firm and snaps gently when touched
Step 7
Serve chilled or store in an airtight container in the refrigerator until ready to eat.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls maintain shape and texture, with firm chocolate exterior and creamy interior
