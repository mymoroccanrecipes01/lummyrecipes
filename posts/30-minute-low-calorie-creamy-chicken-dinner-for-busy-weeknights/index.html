Easy Creamy Greek Yogurt Chicken Dinner Ready in 30 Minutes

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy chicken dinner uses Greek yogurt to keep things light yet satisfying. It’s perfect for busy weeknights when you crave comfort without heaviness. Plus, it’s done in just half an hour!

Pro Tips

Always temper the Greek yogurt before adding it to the hot pan.
Gradually warming the yogurt with warm broth prevents it from curdling when mixed with the hot sauce. Adding cold yogurt directly to the pan causes the proteins to clump, ruining the creamy texture.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when browning chicken.
Overcrowding traps steam and prevents the chicken from browning properly. Browning is essential for flavor development via the Maillard reaction, which adds complexity and texture.
Cook onions slowly until translucent before adding garlic.
Slow cooking onions releases their natural sweetness, balancing the tanginess of the yogurt. Adding garlic later prevents it from burning, which would create a bitter taste.
Use low heat when simmering the sauce after adding yogurt.
High heat breaks the emulsion in the yogurt sauce, causing it to separate. Gentle simmering keeps the sauce smooth and creamy.
Finish with fresh parsley just before serving.
Adding parsley at the end preserves its vibrant color and fresh herbal flavor, brightening the dish visually and taste-wise.
Let the dish rest a few minutes off heat before serving.
Resting allows flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly, improving texture and taste.
Use full-fat Greek yogurt for best creaminess.
Full-fat yogurt has a richer texture and is less likely to curdle compared to low-fat versions, resulting in a more luscious sauce.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding cold yogurt directly to the hot pan

Why it happens Many people skip tempering because they’re in a hurry or unaware of the curdling risk.

Fix Always mix the yogurt with some warm broth first to gently raise its temperature before stirring it into the hot pan. This prevents separation and keeps the sauce creamy.

Overcrowding the skillet when cooking chicken

Why it happens Trying to cook all chicken pieces at once to save time.

Fix Cook in batches or use a larger pan so each piece has room to brown. This develops better flavor and texture.

Cooking the sauce at too high heat after adding yogurt

Why it happens Impatience or misunderstanding that yogurt sauces need gentle heat.

Fix Reduce heat to low and simmer gently. High heat causes yogurt to curdle and separate.

Burning garlic by adding it too early

Why it happens Adding garlic with onions at the start without monitoring the heat.

Fix Add garlic after onions are softened and cook only briefly to release aroma without burning.

Not seasoning properly

Why it happens Underestimating the need for salt and spices to balance flavors.

Fix Taste the sauce and adjust salt, pepper, and herbs before serving to enhance the overall flavor.

Variations

Spicy Creamy Chicken

Add 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper or some red chili flakes to the sauce for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and balances the tangy creaminess with heat, making it perfect for spice lovers.

Note: Add spices when simmering the sauce to infuse the heat evenly without overpowering.

Creamy Mushroom Chicken

Sauté 1 cup of sliced mushrooms with the onions for an earthy addition. Mushrooms add umami depth and a meaty texture that complements the creamy sauce beautifully.

Note: Cook mushrooms until browned and moisture evaporates to avoid a watery sauce.

Lemon Herb Creamy Chicken

Add a tablespoon of fresh lemon juice and 1 teaspoon of chopped fresh thyme or rosemary at the end. The citrus brightens the dish and the herbs add a fragrant note.

Note: Add lemon juice off heat to preserve its fresh flavor.

Creamy Chicken with Spinach

Stir in 2 cups fresh baby spinach during the last few minutes of cooking. The spinach wilts quickly and adds color, texture, and nutrition without changing the creamy profile.

Note: Add spinach last to avoid overcooking and maintain its vibrant green color.

Dairy-Free Version

Replace Greek yogurt with coconut cream or cashew cream for a dairy-free alternative. This changes the flavor slightly but keeps the sauce rich and creamy.

Note: Use gentle heat and stir carefully to prevent separation with dairy-free creams.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This creamy chicken can safely sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases. Always cover with a lid or foil to avoid drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken when chilled; reheat gently and add a splash of broth or water to loosen it. It can be eaten cold but is best warmed.

Freezer

Freeze in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap before placing in a container to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently to restore texture.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring frequently. Add a little chicken broth or water if the sauce is too thick. Avoid microwaving on high heat as it can cause the yogurt sauce to separate.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the chicken and sauce base up to 2 days in advance, storing separately if preferred. Combine and warm gently just before serving to maintain creaminess.

FAQ

Can I use sour cream instead of Greek yogurt?
Sour cream can be used but it has a thinner consistency and less protein, which may result in a less thick sauce. Also, sour cream is more prone to curdling if overheated. If using sour cream, temper it carefully and avoid boiling.
How do I prevent the yogurt sauce from curdling?
The key is tempering the yogurt by mixing it with warm broth before adding it to the hot pan, and then cooking the sauce gently over low heat. Avoid boiling the sauce after adding yogurt, as high heat causes curdling.
Can I make this recipe with chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes! Boneless skinless chicken thighs add extra juiciness and flavor. Adjust cooking time slightly as thighs may take a bit longer to cook through, and ensure they are browned well.
Is this recipe suitable for meal prep?
Absolutely. It reheats well and can be stored in the fridge for a few days. Just reheat gently and add a splash of broth to refresh the sauce. It’s a great option for quick lunches or dinners.
Can I add vegetables to this dish?
Yes, adding vegetables like spinach, peas, or mushrooms works well. Add them towards the end of cooking to keep their texture and color intact.
What can I serve this creamy chicken with?
It pairs beautifully with rice, pasta, quinoa, or steamed vegetables. The sauce is versatile and complements many sides.
How spicy is this dish?
The base recipe is mild and creamy. You can easily add spices like paprika or chili flakes to increase heat according to your taste.
Can I freeze leftovers?
Yes, freeze in an airtight container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently to avoid sauce separation.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt (full-fat or low-fat)
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the chicken pieces in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Cooking the chicken this way ensures it browns nicely, developing flavor through the Maillard reaction rather than steaming. Let the chicken cook without stirring for 3-4 minutes until the underside is golden, then flip and cook another 3 minutes until cooked through. Avoid stirring too often or moving the chicken too soon, as this prevents a good sear and results in less flavorful meat.
⏱ Time: 8 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is golden brown on both sides and cooked through with no pink inside.
Step 2
Remove chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same pan, add the chopped onion and cook over medium heat until softened and translucent, about 4-5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds to release its aroma, being careful not to burn it. This step builds the flavor base for the sauce, and cooking the onions slowly allows their natural sweetness to come out, balancing the tang of the yogurt later.
⏱ Time: 6 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions are soft and translucent; garlic is fragrant but not browned.
Step 3
Pour in the chicken broth and stir to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits stuck to the bottom. These bits are packed with flavor and will enrich the sauce. Let the broth simmer for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly, concentrating its flavor and warming it up so it won’t curdle the yogurt when added.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Broth has reduced slightly and the pan is deglazed with no stuck bits.
Step 4
In a small bowl, mix the Greek yogurt with a few tablespoons of the warm broth to temper it. This gradual warming prevents the yogurt from curdling when added to the hot pan. Slowly stir the tempered yogurt into the skillet, combining well to create a creamy sauce. Cooking the yogurt gently and avoiding boiling keeps the sauce smooth and prevents separation.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is smooth, creamy, and well combined with no lumps.
Step 5
Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and stir to coat it evenly with the sauce. Add the oregano, paprika, salt, and pepper, then reduce the heat to low and let everything simmer gently for 5-7 minutes. This allows the chicken to absorb the flavors and the sauce to thicken slightly. Avoid high heat here to keep the yogurt from breaking down.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce has thickened slightly and chicken is coated and heated through.
Step 6
Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of chicken broth to loosen it. The balance of seasoning is key here — the oregano adds earthiness while paprika gives a subtle smoky note, complementing the tangy yogurt beautifully.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Flavor is balanced with a creamy yet tangy sauce consistency.
Step 7
Turn off the heat and sprinkle freshly chopped parsley over the dish for a bright, fresh finish. The parsley adds a pop of color and a slight herbal note that lifts the richness of the sauce. Serve immediately over rice, pasta, or steamed veggies for a complete meal.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Parsley is vibrant and evenly scattered, dish looks appetizing.
Step 8
If you want to add an extra veggie boost, stir in some fresh spinach or peas during the last 2 minutes of simmering. This adds color, texture, and nutrients without overpowering the creamy sauce. Just be gentle so the greens wilt but don’t overcook.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Greens are bright and tender, integrated into the sauce.
Step 9
Let the dish rest for a few minutes off the heat before serving. This brief rest allows the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly, giving a richer mouthfeel. Don’t cover tightly as it might cause the sauce to become watery from trapped steam.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is slightly thicker and flavors have mellowed together.
