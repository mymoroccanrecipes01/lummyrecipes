Creamy Baked Corn Casserole with a Golden, Buttery Crust

Lummy Hayes Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 45 min cook 6 servings

This baked corn casserole is my go-to comfort side dish—creamy, slightly sweet, and perfectly golden on top. The secret? A touch of sour cream adds richness that makes every bite cozy. Great for holiday dinners or family nights!

Introduction

I first made this baked corn casserole years ago during a holiday dinner when I wanted something comforting but easy to throw together. It quickly became a family favorite, especially because of the creamy, slightly sweet texture that perfectly balances savory flavors. What sets this recipe apart is the addition of sour cream, which adds a velvety richness and keeps the casserole moist without being heavy. I’ve tried versions without it, and honestly, they just don’t compare. Also, using a bit of butter melted right into the batter, plus a sprinkle of sharp cheddar cheese, creates that irresistible golden crust on top that everyone fights over. The trick I learned is not to overmix the batter; just combine until everything is moistened to keep the texture light and tender. When it comes out of the oven, it’s bubbly and golden, with a soft, creamy middle that’s the ultimate comfort food. It's perfect for holiday dinners but honestly, it’s such a crowd-pleaser that I make it year-round for family meals. If you like a cozy, easy side that feels homemade and special, you’ll love this one.

Why This Works

This corn casserole works because of the perfect balance of moisture, texture, and flavor. The combination of whole kernel corn and cream-style corn gives it a unique texture that’s both creamy and slightly chunky, preventing it from being a one-note mush. The sour cream is key—it adds richness and moisture that keeps the casserole from drying out during baking. This dairy fat also helps create that luscious mouthfeel that makes it so comforting.

The use of Jiffy corn muffin mix is a clever shortcut that provides just the right amount of structure and crumb. It contains cornmeal, flour, leavening agents, and a bit of sugar, which all contribute to the casserole’s rise and tenderness. The eggs act as a binder, giving the casserole a custard-like consistency while helping it hold together once baked.

Melting butter into the batter adds flavor and promotes browning through the Maillard reaction, which gives the casserole its golden crust. The shredded sharp cheddar cheese sprinkled in creates pockets of savory goodness and enhances the crust’s complexity. Baking uncovered allows moisture to evaporate and the top to crisp up nicely, balancing the creamy interior.

Finally, letting the casserole rest after baking is crucial. It allows the structure to set, so when you cut into it, it holds its shape but remains soft and creamy. All these elements combined create a dish that feels indulgent yet simple, perfect for family gatherings or cozy dinners.

  • When buying whole kernel corn, look for cans with no added salt or sugar to control the flavor balance. The kernels provide texture contrast to the creamy elements. Fresh or frozen corn can be substituted if drained well, but canned is convenient and consistent for this recipe.
  • Cream-style corn adds smoothness and moisture to the casserole. Choose a brand with minimal additives and natural sweetness. It’s essential for the creamy texture and helps bind the dish together without needing extra liquid.
  • Full-fat sour cream is best as it adds richness and moisture, preventing dryness. It also contributes a slight tang that balances the sweetness of the corn. Light or non-dairy substitutes may alter texture and flavor, so use cautiously.
  • This mix is a convenient base that combines cornmeal, flour, and leavening. It gives structure and a tender crumb. If unavailable, you can substitute with a homemade blend of cornmeal, flour, baking powder, and a pinch of sugar, but results may vary slightly.
  • Sharp cheddar adds a punch of savory flavor and helps form the crust. Use freshly shredded cheese for better melting and texture. Pre-shredded cheeses often contain anti-caking agents that can affect melting quality.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) whole kernel corn, drained
  • 1 can (15 oz) cream-style corn
  • 1 cup sour cream, full fat
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 box (8.5 oz) Jiffy corn muffin mix
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish well with butter or nonstick spray to prevent sticking and help the casserole brown evenly. This step ensures the edges won’t burn and the casserole comes out cleanly for serving. It’s important to prepare the dish properly so your casserole bakes with a nice crust but doesn’t stick or break apart.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven warmed to 350°F and baking dish fully greased and ready
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the drained whole kernel corn and cream-style corn. The mix of textures from the kernels and creamy corn adds a nice contrast in the final dish. Stir gently to mix without breaking up the kernels too much, preserving that fresh corn bite.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Corn mixture looks evenly combined with some creamy texture
  3. Step 3

    Add the sour cream, melted butter, and granulated sugar to the corn mixture. The sour cream adds richness and moisture, while the butter deepens the flavor and helps create a golden crust. Stir just until combined — overmixing can make the casserole dense instead of light and tender.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth and creamy, without lumps or dry spots
  4. Step 4

    Next, add the eggs one at a time, beating lightly after each addition to incorporate. Eggs provide structure and bind the ingredients. Be careful not to overbeat here — you want just enough to combine everything smoothly. Overbeating can cause a tough texture.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is smooth and slightly thickened, no streaks of egg visible
  5. Step 5

    Sprinkle in the Jiffy corn muffin mix, salt, and black pepper. Fold these dry ingredients gently into the wet mixture. The corn muffin mix acts as the base, giving the casserole body and that classic corn flavor. Mixing gently prevents the batter from becoming gummy or tough.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is thick but smooth, no large lumps of dry mix
  6. Step 6

    Fold in the shredded sharp cheddar cheese last. This adds a savory bite and helps form a golden crust on top. Don’t overmix here either—just fold until evenly distributed. Too much stirring will break down the cheese and affect texture.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Cheese is evenly scattered through the batter without clumps
  7. Step 7

    Pour the batter into your prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly with a spatula. Leveling the batter helps it bake uniformly and develop that beautiful golden crust. If you leave it uneven, some parts might cook faster or burn.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is evenly spread in the dish, smooth surface
  8. Step 8

    Bake uncovered in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes. The key here is to let the top turn golden brown and for the casserole to be set in the center. Avoid opening the oven too often as it can cause temperature drops, affecting the bake.

    ⏱ 40-45 minutes✓ Top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs
  9. Step 9

    Let the casserole rest for 10 minutes before serving. This resting time allows it to firm up and makes it easier to slice and serve. Plus, it helps all the flavors meld together beautifully. If you cut into it too soon, it may be too soft and fall apart.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Casserole is slightly cooled, firm but still creamy inside

Pro Tips

  • Don’t overmix the batter once the dry ingredients are added. — Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make the casserole tough and dense rather than tender and creamy. Stir gently until just combined to keep the texture light.
  • Use full-fat sour cream for the best richness and moisture. — The fat in sour cream adds creaminess and prevents the casserole from drying out. Lower-fat versions can make the dish too dry and less flavorful.
  • Let the casserole rest before slicing. — Resting allows the custard-like mixture to set, making it easier to serve clean slices without the casserole falling apart.
  • Grease the baking dish thoroughly. — A well-greased dish prevents sticking, helps with even browning on the edges, and makes cleanup easier.
  • Use freshly shredded cheese instead of pre-shredded. — Freshly shredded cheese melts better and creates a smoother, more flavorful crust. Pre-shredded cheese often contains additives that can alter melting.
  • Bake uncovered to get a golden crust. — Covering traps moisture and prevents browning. Baking uncovered encourages evaporation, resulting in a crispy, golden top.
  • Use a toothpick to test doneness. — A toothpick inserted in the center should come out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs. This ensures the casserole is cooked through but still moist inside.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overmixing the batter
    Why: Many cooks think mixing longer will improve texture, but it actually activates gluten in the flour, making the casserole dense and rubbery.
    Fix: Mix ingredients just until combined. Stop as soon as there are no dry spots visible to keep the texture tender and light.
  • Using low-fat or non-dairy sour cream substitutes
    Why: Some try to lighten the dish or use alternative ingredients without realizing the impact on texture and moisture.
    Fix: Stick to full-fat sour cream or a high-quality dairy substitute with similar fat content. This keeps the casserole moist and rich.
  • Not greasing the baking dish well
    Why: Rushing or forgetting this step leads to sticking and uneven browning.
    Fix: Use softened butter or nonstick spray generously on all sides of the dish before adding batter.
  • Opening the oven too often during baking
    Why: Curious cooks check progress too frequently, causing heat loss and uneven baking.
    Fix: Trust the timing and visual cues. Only check near the end to avoid temperature fluctuations.
  • Cutting into the casserole too soon
    Why: Impatience leads to slicing before the casserole has set, causing it to fall apart.
    Fix: Let it rest 10-15 minutes after baking to firm up for clean slices.

Variations

  • Spicy Jalapeño Corn Casserole: Add diced jalapeños and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the batter for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and a nice contrast to the sweet corn, perfect for spice lovers.
    Adjust baking time slightly if adding extra moisture from jalapeños; watch for golden top.
  • Bacon and Cheddar Corn Casserole: Fold in crispy cooked bacon bits and extra sharp cheddar cheese for a smoky, savory twist. The bacon fat also enhances richness and flavor complexity.
    Drain bacon well to avoid excess grease, which can affect texture.
  • Herbed Corn Casserole: Stir in fresh chopped herbs like thyme, parsley, or chives to brighten the flavor profile. This makes the casserole feel fresher and less heavy.
    Add herbs at the end of mixing to preserve their fresh taste.
  • Vegan Corn Casserole: Use vegan sour cream, flax eggs, and dairy-free butter and cheese substitutes. The texture will be slightly different but still creamy and delicious.
    Flax eggs need extra binding time; bake a bit longer and check doneness carefully.
  • Sweet Corn and Jalapeño Casserole: Combine sweet corn kernels with diced jalapeños and a touch of honey for a sweet-heat combo. It’s a fun twist that balances sweetness and spice.
    Reduce sugar slightly to compensate for honey’s sweetness.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This casserole can safely sit out for up to 2 hours covered. After that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly. Cover with foil or plastic wrap to prevent drying.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The casserole may firm up and lose some crispness on top. Reheat gently in the oven or microwave with a splash of milk or butter to restore creaminess.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers or wrapped tightly in foil for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, double-wrap and avoid temperature fluctuations. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes until warmed through. Cover loosely with foil to prevent over-browning. You can also microwave individual portions, adding a splash of milk or butter to keep it moist.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the batter up to 24 hours ahead and refrigerate in the baking dish covered tightly. Bake fresh when ready to serve for best texture. Alternatively, fully bake and reheat just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use fresh corn instead of canned corn?
Yes! Fresh corn can be used and will add a wonderful sweetness and texture. Use about 2 cups of fresh kernels, cooked briefly or blanched. Make sure to drain any excess moisture well to avoid a soggy casserole.
What if I don’t have Jiffy mix? Can I make my own?
You can substitute with a mix of 1 cup cornmeal, 1/2 cup all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon baking powder, and 1 tablespoon sugar. This homemade blend will mimic Jiffy’s texture but may require slight adjustments in liquid.
How do I know when the casserole is done baking?
The top should be golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the center should come out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs. The casserole should be set and not jiggly in the middle.
Can I make this casserole ahead of time?
Absolutely! You can prepare the batter and refrigerate it up to 24 hours before baking. Bake fresh when ready for best results. Alternatively, bake fully and reheat gently before serving.
Is it okay to freeze leftovers?
Yes, freeze leftovers in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat in the oven. Texture may be slightly softer but still tasty.
Can I add other vegetables to this casserole?
Sure! Diced bell peppers, jalapeños, or green onions can add flavor and color. Just be mindful of moisture content to avoid sogginess and adjust baking time if needed.
What cheese works best in this recipe?
Sharp cheddar is preferred for its flavor and melting quality, but you can experiment with Monterey Jack or a mild Colby. Avoid pre-shredded for best melting results.
How can I make this recipe dairy-free?
Use dairy-free sour cream, vegan butter, and plant-based cheese alternatives. Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon flaxseed meal + 3 tablespoons water per egg). Texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
