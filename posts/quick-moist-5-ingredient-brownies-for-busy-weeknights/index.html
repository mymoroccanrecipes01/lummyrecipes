Fudgy Five-Ingredient Brownies That Save the Day

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 15, 2026
10 min Prep Time
20 min Time
9 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These brownies are incredibly fudgy and moist with just five simple ingredients. I love how quick and fuss-free they are—perfect for when a chocolate craving hits on a busy evening or last-minute dessert plans.

Introduction

I stumbled upon this brownie recipe on a hectic weeknight when all I wanted was something chocolatey without a ton of fuss. It quickly became a lifesaver in my kitchen because it uses just five simple ingredients and comes together in under 30 minutes. What sets this version apart from other brownie recipes is its incredible fudgy texture—thanks to the melted butter and minimal flour. I’ve tried adding nuts or chocolate chips, but honestly, the classic simplicity is what keeps me coming back. The trick I learned is not to overmix the batter; just combine until everything’s barely blended to keep the brownies tender and dense. When baked right, you get a shiny cracked top with a rich, moist interior that melts in your mouth. These brownies are perfect for busy evenings, last-minute dessert plans, or just a treat when you need a little chocolate boost. Plus, since they don’t require any fancy ingredients or equipment, it’s a recipe I always keep on hand.

What You'll Need

9 fudgy brownies, about 2-inch squares

  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup (40g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8x8-inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper. This step ensures your brownies won’t stick and makes cleanup easier. Properly preheating the oven is crucial so the brownies cook evenly and develop that signature crackly top without drying out.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated, pan ready with parchment or grease
Step 2
In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the melted butter and sugar until smooth. The butter should be warm but not hot to avoid cooking the eggs later. This mixture forms the base of your brownie and helps create a rich, moist texture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth, glossy mixture with no sugar clumps
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. This helps to incorporate air and create a better structure for the brownies. If you add both eggs at once, the batter might look curdled and result in a denser texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter looks homogeneous and slightly thickened
Step 4
Sift the cocoa powder and flour directly into the wet ingredients to avoid lumps. Gently fold them in with a spatula until just combined. Overmixing here can develop gluten and make the brownies cakey instead of fudgy, so stop as soon as no streaks remain.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly mixed batter with a rich chocolate color, no flour streaks
Step 5
Pour the batter into your prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. This ensures even cooking and a neat presentation. Avoid tapping the pan too much as it can create air bubbles that affect the texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread with a smooth surface
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 18-22 minutes. Start checking around 18 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. This timing keeps the brownies fudgy instead of dry or cakey.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out with moist crumbs, top is set with slight cracks
Step 7
Remove the pan from the oven and let the brownies cool completely in the pan. Cooling allows the brownies to set and makes cutting easier without crumbling. If you slice them too soon, they might fall apart or be gooey.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownies firm to the touch and edges slightly pulling away from pan
Step 8
Once cooled, use a sharp knife to cut into 9 squares. Wiping the knife clean between cuts helps keep edges neat. Serve as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean, even brownie squares with intact edges
Step 9
Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. If you want to keep them longer, refrigeration or freezing works well (see storage tips). Reheat gently to bring back that soft, fudgy texture.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Brownies remain moist and fresh after storage

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix the batter once you add the flour and cocoa.
Overmixing develops gluten, which makes brownies cakey and tough instead of fudgy. Mixing just until the flour disappears keeps the texture dense and moist.
Use melted butter instead of softened butter.
Melted butter coats the flour better and contributes to a fudgy texture. Softened butter whipped with sugar traps air and creates a lighter crumb, which isn’t what you want here.
Check the brownies a few minutes before the timer.
Ovens vary, and overbaking is the biggest culprit for dry brownies. Start testing early with a toothpick so you catch the perfect moist crumb stage.
Line your pan with parchment paper with an overhang.
This makes removing the brownies super easy without breaking them. It also helps avoid greasing, which can sometimes make edges too soft.
Use room temperature eggs for best mixing.
Cold eggs can cause the butter to seize or the batter to curdle, affecting texture. Room temperature eggs blend more smoothly and help the batter emulsify properly.
Don’t skip sifting the cocoa powder and flour.
Sifting prevents lumps and ensures even distribution throughout the batter, leading to consistent texture and flavor.
Let the brownies cool completely before cutting.
Warm brownies are fragile and crumbly. Cooling lets them set and results in cleaner cuts and a better eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overbaking the brownies

Why it happens Many assume brownies are done when the toothpick comes out clean, but this often means they’ve baked too long and dried out.

Fix Check for moist crumbs on the toothpick instead of a clean one. Remove the brownies from the oven as soon as you see moist crumbs to keep them fudgy.

Using too much flour

Why it happens Some measure flour by scooping directly with the cup, which packs too much flour into the measurement.

Fix Spoon flour into the measuring cup and level off with a knife to avoid packing. This keeps the flour amount accurate and the brownies moist.

Overmixing after adding dry ingredients

Why it happens In a rush, some mix until perfectly smooth, which activates gluten and toughens brownies.

Fix Fold the dry ingredients in gently and stop mixing as soon as no streaks remain.

Not preheating the oven fully

Why it happens Starting to bake in a cold or warming oven causes uneven rise and texture problems.

Fix Always preheat the oven to the correct temperature before putting in the brownies.

Skipping parchment paper or greasing

Why it happens Without proper preparation, brownies stick to the pan and break apart when removed.

Fix Line the pan with parchment paper or grease it well to ensure easy removal.

Variations

Nutty Fudgy Brownies

Add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter before baking for a delightful crunch and nutty flavor. This adds texture and a toasty note that pairs beautifully with the rich chocolate.

Note: Gently fold nuts in last to avoid overmixing. Baking time remains the same.

Chocolate Chip Brownies

Stir in 1/2 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips to add pockets of melty chocolate throughout. This makes the brownies extra decadent and crowd-pleasing.

Note: Add chips with the flour and cocoa, fold gently to distribute evenly.

Espresso-Infused Brownies

Mix 1 teaspoon instant espresso powder with the melted butter to boost chocolate flavor with a subtle coffee kick. It deepens the richness without overpowering.

Note: Dissolve espresso powder fully in warm butter before combining with sugar.

Gluten-Free Brownies

Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free flour blend with xanthan gum to keep the fudgy texture. These brownies stay moist and rich while accommodating dietary needs.

Note: Check your blend’s instructions; baking time might need slight adjustment.

Vegan Brownies

Replace eggs with 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce or mashed banana and use vegan butter. These brownies are still fudgy and delicious with a subtle fruity undertone.

Note: Mix wet ingredients thoroughly to ensure proper binding with egg substitutes.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Brownies can be kept at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Covering them well prevents them from drying out and keeps them soft and fudgy.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Refrigeration firms them up and can make edges chewier. To enjoy at room temperature, take them out 30 minutes before serving or warm gently.

Freezer

Wrap brownies tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container or bag. Freeze up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature to maintain texture.

Reheating

Warm brownies gently in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) wrapped in foil for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk before reheating can restore moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it refrigerated, but bake fresh for best texture. Alternatively, bake brownies and freeze, then thaw and warm when ready to serve.

FAQ

Can I use brown sugar instead of granulated sugar?
Yes, you can substitute brown sugar for granulated sugar. It will add a slight molasses flavor and make the brownies a bit more moist and chewy. Just use the same amount and mix as usual.
What if I don’t have unsweetened cocoa powder?
If you only have sweetened cocoa or chocolate powder, the brownies will be sweeter and may alter the texture. It’s best to use unsweetened cocoa to control sweetness and get that rich chocolate flavor.
Can I double this recipe?
Absolutely! Use a 9x13-inch pan for doubled batter and adjust baking time to about 25-30 minutes. Keep checking with a toothpick for moist crumbs to avoid overbaking.
Why are my brownies cakey instead of fudgy?
Cakey brownies usually result from overmixing the batter or using too much flour. Make sure to fold the dry ingredients gently and measure flour correctly by spooning and leveling.
Can I add chocolate chips or nuts?
Yes! Adding 1/2 cup of either chocolate chips or chopped nuts enhances texture and flavor. Fold them in gently after mixing the dry ingredients.
How do I know when brownies are done?
Insert a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. The top should look set with slight cracks forming.
Can I make these brownies vegan?
You can substitute eggs with 1/4 cup applesauce or mashed banana and use vegan butter. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and fudgy.
What’s the best way to store leftover brownies?
Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or freeze for longer storage. Reheat gently to restore softness.
