Easy Springtime Lemon Blueberry Tart That Everyone Will Love

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 8, 2026
20 min Prep Time
35 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This lemon blueberry tart is a fresh and simple dessert bursting with bright citrus and juicy berries. The buttery crust and creamy filling make it perfect for spring gatherings or a sunny weekend treat.

Pro Tips

Chill the dough thoroughly before rolling out.
Chilling firms up the butter and relaxes gluten, preventing shrinkage and ensuring a flaky, tender crust. Skipping this step often results in a tough or misshapen tart shell.
Blind bake the crust with weights to keep it flat.
Using pie weights prevents the crust from puffing or shrinking during baking, which keeps the base even and crisp, essential for holding the wet custard filling without sogginess.
Whisk eggs and sugar slowly to dissolve sugar fully.
This technique aerates the eggs and prevents grainy texture in the custard, giving the filling a smooth, creamy consistency after baking.
Use fresh lemon juice and zest for best flavor.
Fresh citrus provides natural brightness and aromatic oils that bottled juice can't replicate, making the tart taste vibrant and fresh.
Bake the filled tart until the center is just set but still slightly wobbly.
Overbaking causes the custard to crack and become dry. A slight wobble ensures a creamy texture once cooled.
Let the tart cool completely before slicing.
Cooling allows the filling to firm up for clean slices. Cutting warm custard results in messy, runny pieces.
Use a sharp, serrated knife for slicing.
A serrated knife cuts through the crust and delicate filling cleanly without crushing or tearing the tart.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overworking the dough

Why it happens Many home cooks try to mix the dough too much to bring it together quickly, not realizing this develops gluten, which makes the crust tough.

Fix Mix the dough just until it holds together and avoid kneading. Chill the dough before rolling to relax gluten and keep the butter cold for flakiness.

Skipping blind baking

Why it happens People often pour filling directly into raw dough to save time, unaware this leads to a soggy bottom.

Fix Always blind bake the crust with weights to set the shell and prevent sogginess when adding custard.

Using bottled lemon juice

Why it happens Convenience leads cooks to use bottled juice, which lacks fresh flavor and can alter the custard's taste.

Fix Use fresh lemons for juice and zest to achieve bright, natural citrus flavor.

Overbaking the filling

Why it happens Waiting for the filling to look completely firm can cause it to dry out and crack.

Fix Remove the tart when the center still jiggles slightly; it will firm up as it cools.

Cutting tart too soon

Why it happens Impatience leads to slicing while the filling is still warm and unset, causing a runny mess.

Fix Allow the tart to cool completely to room temperature before slicing for neat, clean pieces.

Variations

Gluten-Free Almond Crust

Swap the all-purpose flour for almond flour and a bit of tapioca starch to create a nutty, gluten-free crust. The texture will be slightly denser but still deliciously buttery and pairs beautifully with the lemon filling.

Note: Chill the almond-based dough longer as it’s softer, and handle gently to prevent crumbling.

Add Fresh Mint to the Filling

Infuse the lemon custard with finely chopped fresh mint leaves for a refreshing herbal twist. The mint complements the citrus and blueberry flavors, adding complexity without overpowering.

Note: Strain the filling after whisking to remove mint bits for a smooth texture.

Use Mixed Berries Instead of Blueberries

Replace blueberries with a combination of raspberries, blackberries, and strawberries for a colorful berry medley. This adds varied sweetness and texture for a more complex berry flavor profile.

Note: Be mindful of berry moisture; pat dry to avoid soggy crust.

Make Mini Tarts

Divide the dough and filling into mini tart pans for individual servings. These are perfect for parties or elegant dessert plates and can speed up baking time slightly.

Note: Adjust baking time to 20-25 minutes, watching for a slight wobble in filling.

Add a Streusel Topping

Top the tart with a simple streusel made from butter, sugar, and flour before baking for a crunchy, sweet contrast to the smooth filling and tender crust.

Note: Apply streusel after blind baking the crust, then add filling on top and bake.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This tart can safely sit at room temperature for up to 4 hours if covered loosely with a cake dome or foil to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store the tart in an airtight container or covered with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 3 days. The filling firms up when chilled, so it tastes great cold but can also be served at room temperature by letting it sit out for 20 minutes.

Freezer

Freeze the tart wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then foil for up to 1 month. Thaw overnight in the fridge. The crust may lose some crispness but the filling stays creamy.

Reheating

Reheat slices gently in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes to warm without melting the custard. Avoid microwave reheating as it can make the filling rubbery or watery.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the crust and blind bake it up to 1 day ahead, then refrigerate. Make the filling and assemble the tart the day of serving for the freshest flavor and best texture.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries for this tart?
You can use frozen blueberries, but thaw and drain them thoroughly to avoid excess moisture that could make the crust soggy. Pat them dry with paper towels before adding to the tart shell.
What if my lemon filling cracks after baking?
Cracks usually happen if the custard is overbaked or cooled too quickly. Remove the tart from the oven when the center still jiggles slightly and cool it gradually at room temperature before refrigerating.
Can I make the crust without a food processor?
Absolutely! Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the cold butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs. The texture is key, so work quickly to keep the butter cold.
How do I prevent the crust from shrinking during baking?
Chill the dough well before rolling and after fitting it in the pan. Avoid stretching the dough when placing it in the tart pan. Also, blind bake with weights to keep the crust in place.
Is there a substitute for heavy cream in the filling?
You can use full-fat coconut milk or half-and-half, but the texture will be slightly less rich and creamy. Heavy cream helps create a velvety custard and contributes to the filling’s stability.
Can I make this tart vegan?
This recipe relies on eggs and dairy for texture and flavor, so vegan substitutions require significant changes. You could try a vegan pastry crust and a lemon filling made from coconut cream and cornstarch, but the texture will differ.
Why do I need to prick the crust before blind baking?
Pricking the crust with a fork (docking) lets steam escape during baking, preventing air bubbles and ensuring the crust stays flat and even.
How do I store leftover tart slices?
Store leftover slices in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Bring to room temperature before serving or enjoy chilled for a refreshing treat.

What You'll Need

One 9-inch tart, serves 8

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon ice water
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice (about 2 lemons)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the tart crust. In a large bowl, combine the sifted flour and powdered sugar. Cut in the cold, cubed butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. This step is crucial because the cold butter creates layers in the crust, giving it a tender, flaky texture. Avoid overmixing or warming the butter, which can make the crust dense instead of light and crumbly.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: The mixture looks sandy with pea-sized butter chunks evenly distributed.
Step 2
Add the egg yolk and ice water to the flour mixture. Use a fork or your hands to gently bring the dough together, just until it forms a ball. The dough will be slightly sticky but should hold together without crumbling. Don’t overwork it — that can develop gluten and toughen the crust. Once combined, flatten into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes to relax the gluten and firm up the butter.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Dough holds together smoothly and feels firm but pliable after chilling.
Step 3
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Press the dough into the pan, trimming any excess from the edges. Prick the base all over with a fork to prevent bubbles during baking. This docking step ensures the crust stays flat and bakes evenly.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Dough fits snugly in the pan with an even thickness and fork holes visible.
Step 4
Line the tart shell with parchment paper or foil and fill with pie weights or dried beans. Blind bake the crust for 15 minutes to set the base. This prevents a soggy bottom when adding the wet filling later. Remove the weights and parchment and bake for an additional 5 minutes until the crust is lightly golden.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Crust is dry to the touch, with a pale golden color and firm edges.
Step 5
While the crust bakes, prepare the lemon filling. In a medium bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and eggs until pale and slightly thickened. This step helps dissolve the sugar and aerate the eggs for a smooth custard. Then stir in fresh lemon juice, lemon zest, and heavy cream. Mixing gently but thoroughly ensures a silky filling that will set perfectly in the oven.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth, glossy, and well combined without lumps.
Step 6
Arrange fresh blueberries evenly over the prebaked tart shell. Pour the lemon custard filling carefully over the berries, allowing them to float naturally. The berries add bursts of sweetness and a lovely contrast to the tart lemon flavor. Be gentle pouring so the crust doesn’t get disturbed.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Blueberries are evenly distributed and filling covers them without spilling over.
Step 7
Bake the filled tart at 350°F (175°C) for 25-30 minutes. The filling should be just set but still slightly wobbly in the center when you gently shake the pan. Overbaking can cause cracks or a dry custard, so keep an eye on it near the end. The crust edges should deepen to a golden brown for the perfect balance of texture.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Filling is mostly firm with a slight jiggle in the center and golden crust edges.
Step 8
Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack. Cooling allows the filling to fully set and develop its creamy texture. If you try to slice it warm, the custard may be too soft and runny. Patience here pays off with neat slices and a luscious mouthfeel.
⏱ Time: 60
✓ Doneness: Tart feels firm and cool to the touch, with no liquid wobbling.
Step 9
Once cooled, carefully remove the tart from the pan and transfer to a serving plate. Garnish with a few extra fresh blueberries or lemon zest for a pretty finish. Serve chilled or at room temperature. This tart pairs beautifully with a cup of tea or a light dessert wine for spring entertaining.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Tart slices cleanly without cracking or crumbling.
