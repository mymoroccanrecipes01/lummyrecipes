Easy One-Pot Instant Pot Chicken and Rice That Feels Like Home

Lummy Hayes Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This one-pot chicken and rice is the ultimate comfort food that’s ready in under an hour. The secret? Cooking everything together in the Instant Pot to lock in flavors and keep it moist. Perfect for a cozy weeknight dinner!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this one-pot Instant Pot chicken and rice recipe during one of those chaotic weeknights when I wanted something comforting but didn’t have the energy to slave away in the kitchen. What I love about this version is how everything cooks together, so the chicken stays juicy and the rice soaks up all those wonderful savory flavors. I’ve tried making chicken and rice separately before, but it just never had the same cozy feel or depth of flavor. The trick I learned was to brown the chicken first in the Instant Pot’s sauté mode to get a nice caramelized edge before adding the rice and broth. This step adds a subtle smoky richness that you wouldn’t get otherwise. Plus, I toss in a bit of smoked paprika and thyme for an earthy warmth that’s just irresistible. The finished dish is comforting, hearty, and perfect for a no-fuss dinner. It’s one of those meals that feels like a warm hug after a long day. I’m sure you’ll find yourself making this again and again!

Why This Works

One-pot meals like this Instant Pot chicken and rice succeed because they allow flavors to meld deeply while minimizing moisture loss. Browning the chicken first initiates the Maillard reaction, a chemical process that creates complex, savory flavors and an appealing golden crust. This step is crucial to building a rich base for the dish.

Next, toasting the rice briefly before pressure cooking helps improve its texture by slightly drying the surface and adding a subtle nuttiness. This prevents the rice from becoming overly sticky or mushy during the pressure cooking phase. The sealed environment of the Instant Pot traps steam and heat, cooking the rice and chicken evenly and efficiently.

The choice of seasonings, like smoked paprika and thyme, contributes aromatic depth without overpowering the dish. The paprika adds a smoky warmth, while thyme brings a fresh, earthy note. Adding frozen peas at the end preserves their bright color and sweet flavor, contrasting nicely with the savory components.

Allowing the pressure to release naturally after cooking is another key technique. This gradual release lets the rice absorb any remaining liquid gently, ensuring a tender, fluffy texture rather than a dry or sticky one. Altogether, these steps combine to create a comforting, well-balanced meal that feels homemade and satisfying.

  • Chicken thighs are ideal for this recipe because they stay moist and tender even under pressure cooking. Look for fresh thighs with a uniform pink color and minimal excess fat. Thighs have more connective tissue and fat than breasts, which breaks down during cooking to enrich the dish’s flavor and texture. If you prefer breasts, reduce cooking time slightly to prevent drying out.
  • Choosing long grain white rice is important because it cooks up fluffy and separate, not sticky or gummy. Rinse the rice before cooking to remove excess starch that can cause clumping. Avoid using short grain or sticky rice varieties here, as their texture won’t complement the chicken and broth as well.
  • Low sodium chicken broth is best so you can control the salt level in the dish. The broth adds depth and moisture, infusing the rice and chicken with savory flavor. Homemade broth is ideal but store-bought works well too. Avoid broths with strong artificial flavors or excess salt that can overpower the delicate balance.
  • Smoked paprika lends a subtle smoky flavor and vibrant red color that elevates this simple dish. Opt for Spanish smoked paprika if you can find it, as it has a rich, deep smokiness. It’s a key ingredient here that adds warmth without heat. Regular paprika or sweet paprika can be used but won’t provide the same complexity.
  • Frozen peas are a convenient way to add sweetness and color without extra prep. They retain their bright green color and fresh flavor when stirred in at the end, avoiding overcooking. Fresh peas can be used seasonally, but frozen peas are more accessible and consistent in quality year-round.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, room temperature
  • 1 pound boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup long grain white rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth, low sodium
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas, thawed
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by setting your Instant Pot to sauté mode and heat the olive oil until shimmering but not smoking. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer, making sure not to crowd the pot, and let them brown without moving them for about 4 minutes. This caramelization is key because it develops deep flavor through the Maillard reaction, which you don’t get if you just throw everything in raw. Once browned on one side, stir and brown the other sides for another 3-4 minutes. Watch carefully to avoid burning, as burnt bits can turn bitter and ruin the dish.

    ⏱ 8✓ Chicken pieces are golden brown on all sides with a slight crust
  2. Step 2

    Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pot with the browned chicken. Sauté everything together for about 3 minutes, stirring often until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant. This step softens the onion and releases its natural sweetness, balancing the savory chicken. Don’t rush this part; undercooked onion can taste sharp and harsh. If you notice any sticking, scrape the bottom gently to deglaze and incorporate those flavorful browned bits into the mix.

    ⏱ 3✓ Onions are soft and translucent; garlic is aromatic but not burnt
  3. Step 3

    Pour in the rinsed rice and stir well to coat every grain with the oil and chicken juices. This helps the rice toast slightly, which improves texture and adds a nutty flavor. Toasting rice also helps prevent it from becoming mushy later. Mix in the smoked paprika, dried thyme, salt, and pepper evenly to distribute the seasoning throughout. Taking the time to stir at this stage ensures every bite is flavorful.

    ⏱ 2✓ Rice grains look glossy and lightly coated; spices evenly mixed
  4. Step 4

    Slowly pour the chicken broth over the rice and chicken mixture, then give it a gentle stir to combine. Make sure the rice is submerged to cook evenly. Seal the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to sealing. Select the manual or pressure cook mode and set the timer for 8 minutes at high pressure. This timing is ideal for tender chicken and perfectly cooked rice without being mushy. If you rush or shorten the cooking time, the rice may be undercooked; too long and it turns gummy.

    ⏱ 1✓ Instant Pot starts building pressure; liquid covers rice completely
  5. Step 5

    Once the cooking cycle completes, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes before carefully switching the valve to venting to release any remaining steam. This natural release lets the rice finish absorbing liquid gently and prevents it from drying out or sticking to the bottom. Quick-releasing pressure here can cause the rice texture to be uneven or the chicken to toughen up, so patience is key.

    ⏱ 10✓ Pressure valve drops, and no hissing steam remains
  6. Step 6

    Open the lid and fluff the rice gently with a fork to separate the grains and mix the chicken pieces. Stir in the thawed peas, which will warm through with the residual heat. This adds a pop of color and a subtle sweetness that contrasts nicely with the smoky paprika. Avoid stirring vigorously to keep the rice fluffy and prevent it from becoming mushy.

    ⏱ 2✓ Rice is fluffy and separated; peas are bright green and warmed
  7. Step 7

    Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. Adding seasoning at the end ensures the flavors are balanced and not too salty from the broth. If it tastes a bit flat, a squeeze of fresh lemon juice or a sprinkle of chopped parsley can brighten the whole dish beautifully. I always keep some fresh herbs on hand to lift flavors at the last minute.

    ⏱ 2✓ Balanced savory taste with fresh herb aroma
  8. Step 8

    Serve your chicken and rice hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley for a burst of color and freshness. This simple garnish makes the dish feel special and inviting. Pair it with a crisp green salad or steamed veggies for a complete meal. Enjoy the comforting blend of tender chicken, savory broth, and fluffy rice that’s so satisfying after a busy day.

    ⏱ 1✓ Dish looks vibrant and inviting with fresh herb garnish
  9. Step 9

    Clean your Instant Pot promptly after use to prevent any stuck-on bits from hardening. A quick soak and wipe while still warm makes cleanup easier and keeps your pot in great shape for the next meal. Trust me, a clean pot always means better tasting meals next time!

    ⏱ 5✓ Pot is clean, no residue or burnt bits remain

Pro Tips

  • Brown the chicken first on sauté mode before pressure cooking. — Browning creates a flavorful crust through the Maillard reaction, which adds depth and complexity to the dish. Skipping this step results in less savory flavor and a paler, less appetizing appearance.
  • Rinse the rice thoroughly before adding it to the pot. — Rinsing removes surface starch that can cause the rice to clump or become gummy when cooked under pressure. Clean rice grains cook up fluffy and separate, improving texture significantly.
  • Let the pressure release naturally for at least 10 minutes. — Natural release allows the rice to finish absorbing liquid gently, preventing it from drying out or sticking to the bottom. Quick release can shock the grains, leading to uneven texture.
  • Use low sodium broth to control seasoning better. — Broths vary widely in salt content. Starting with low sodium lets you add salt gradually, avoiding an overly salty final dish that masks other flavors.
  • Add frozen peas at the end rather than cooking them under pressure. — Peas cook very quickly and can become mushy or lose their vibrant color if overcooked. Stirring them in at the end preserves their texture and freshness.
  • Fluff the rice gently with a fork after cooking. — Using a fork rather than a spoon separates the grains without crushing them, maintaining a light, fluffy texture that’s more appealing.
  • Serve immediately for best texture and flavor. — Rice dishes tend to firm up as they cool. Serving right away ensures the chicken stays juicy and rice stays fluffy, giving you the best eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the chicken browning step.
    Why: Many cooks want to save time and skip browning, assuming pressure cooking will develop flavor sufficiently.
    Fix: Take the extra 8 minutes to brown the chicken on sauté mode first. This step creates the foundation of flavor and improves texture, making a big difference in the final dish.
  • Not rinsing the rice before cooking.
    Why: Rinsing rice is sometimes overlooked because it feels like an unnecessary step or extra cleanup.
    Fix: Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear to remove extra starch. This prevents clumping and results in fluffier rice.
  • Releasing pressure too quickly after cooking.
    Why: Impatience or unfamiliarity with natural release leads some to quick-release immediately.
    Fix: Allow the pressure to release naturally for at least 10 minutes to let the rice finish cooking evenly and avoid texture problems.
  • Adding peas too early in the cooking process.
    Why: Assuming all ingredients should cook together under pressure for convenience.
    Fix: Add peas after cooking to preserve their color and texture. Stir them in just before serving.
  • Using chicken breasts without adjusting cook time.
    Why: Using thighs or breasts interchangeably without changing cooking parameters.
    Fix: If using breasts, reduce pressure cooking time by 2-3 minutes to prevent drying out and maintain tenderness.

Variations

  • Spicy One-Pot Chicken and Rice: Add 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper or a diced jalapeño when sautéing the onions for a spicy kick. You can also sprinkle chopped cilantro on top before serving for a fresh contrast. This variation brings warmth and depth with a little heat.
    Adjust spice level carefully to avoid overpowering the dish; monitor jalapeño seeds for milder flavor.
  • Creamy Mushroom Chicken and Rice: Sauté 1 cup sliced mushrooms along with the onions, then stir in 1/4 cup heavy cream after cooking. This adds an earthy richness and creamy texture that makes the dish feel more indulgent and cozy.
    Add cream off heat to prevent curdling and stir gently.
  • Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice: Add zest and juice of one lemon along with fresh thyme and rosemary to brighten the flavors. Finish with a drizzle of olive oil and extra fresh herbs for a light, aromatic twist that’s perfect for spring or summer meals.
    Add lemon juice after cooking to keep its brightness intact.
  • Vegetable-Packed Chicken and Rice: Mix in diced carrots, bell peppers, and zucchini with the onions for a colorful, nutrient-rich meal. This boosts the veggie content and adds texture variety without complicating the cooking process.
    Cut vegetables uniformly to ensure even cooking under pressure.
  • Brown Rice and Chicken Instant Pot Meal: Swap white rice for brown rice and increase cooking time to 22 minutes under pressure. This variation offers a nuttier flavor and more fiber, perfect if you want a heartier, healthier version.
    Ensure enough liquid is added as brown rice absorbs more and requires longer cooking.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: The dish can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered to prevent contamination. Beyond this, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The rice may firm up and chicken might dry slightly when cold, so reheat gently with a splash of broth or water to restore moisture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight, freezer-safe containers for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating for best texture retention.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over low heat or in the microwave with a splash of broth or water to keep the rice moist. Avoid overheating, which can make chicken tough or rice dry.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can brown the chicken and sauté the aromatics up to 1 day ahead and refrigerate. Add rice, broth, and seasonings on the day of cooking for freshest flavor and texture.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, you can substitute chicken breasts, but reduce the pressure cooking time by 2-3 minutes to prevent them from drying out. Chicken breasts cook faster and can become tough if overcooked under pressure.
What if I don’t have smoked paprika?
If you don’t have smoked paprika, you can use regular paprika or a pinch of chili powder for some warmth. The dish won’t have the same smoky depth, but it will still be flavorful and delicious.
Can I use brown rice in this recipe?
Brown rice cooks differently and requires longer to become tender, so adjust liquid and time carefully to avoid undercooked or dry rice.
Is it necessary to rinse the rice?
Rinsing the rice removes excess surface starch that can cause the grains to stick together and become gummy. It’s a simple step that helps achieve fluffy, separate rice grains in the finished dish.
Can I add other vegetables to this dish?
Absolutely! Diced carrots, bell peppers, or zucchini work well. Add them during the sauté step with the onions to soften, or stir in quick-cooking vegetables like peas at the end to maintain texture and color.
How do I prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom?
Make sure to brown the chicken first and deglaze the pot well by stirring after adding broth. Also, avoid stirring the rice once you start pressure cooking, as this can activate starch and cause sticking.
Can I double this recipe?
You can double the ingredients, but be mindful of your Instant Pot’s max fill line. Cook time stays the same, but make sure not to overfill the pot to allow proper pressure build-up.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat gently on the stovetop or microwave with a splash of broth or water to prevent the rice from drying out. Avoid high heat or overcooking, which can toughen the chicken and make rice mushy.
