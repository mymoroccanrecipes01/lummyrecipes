Pro Tips

Bring all refrigerated ingredients to room temperature before mixing.
Room temperature ingredients emulsify better, preventing curdling and ensuring an even batter that bakes uniformly.
Do not overmix the batter once the flour is added.
Overmixing develops gluten, resulting in a tougher, denser cake instead of a tender crumb.
Use fresh baking powder and baking soda for reliable rise.
Old leavening agents lose potency, causing flat or dense cakes.
Check the cake a few minutes before the minimum baking time to avoid overbaking.
Ovens vary; overbaking dries out the cake and reduces tenderness.
Cool cake completely before frosting to prevent melting and sliding.
Frosting applied on warm cake will melt and not hold its shape.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold ingredients straight from the fridge.

Why it happens Cold butter, eggs, or dairy don’t combine smoothly, leading to curdled batter or uneven texture.

Fix Plan ahead and remove ingredients from the fridge 30-60 minutes before starting.

Overmixing after adding flour.

Why it happens Mixing too long develops gluten, making the cake tough and chewy.

Fix Mix only until dry ingredients are incorporated; stop as soon as no flour streaks remain.

Skipping the sifting of dry ingredients.

Why it happens Lumps in flour or leavening agents can cause uneven rising and dense spots.

Fix Always sift or whisk dry ingredients together before adding to the batter.

Baking at too high or too low temperature.

Why it happens Incorrect oven temperature causes uneven cooking — too high burns edges before center cooks; too low results in dense texture.

Fix Use an oven thermometer to verify temperature; adjust racks to the center of the oven.

Variations

Gluten-Free Version

Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free 1:1 baking flour blend that contains xanthan gum. Proceed with the recipe as usual.

Note: Gluten-free blends vary; ensure the blend includes binding agents to mimic gluten’s structure for proper rise and texture.

Vegan Adaptation

Replace butter with vegan margarine, eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg), and use plant-based buttermilk (soy or almond milk with 1 tbsp vinegar). Use a vegan sour cream alternative or full-fat coconut yogurt.

Note: Vegan substitutes alter texture slightly; the cake will be tender but may be less rich. Baking time may need slight adjustment.

Citrus-Infused Cake

Add 1 tbsp finely grated lemon or orange zest to the batter along with the vanilla extract to introduce a bright aroma and subtle flavor.

Note: Citrus zest oils enhance flavor without affecting the structure; avoid adding juice to keep batter consistency.

Chocolate Birthday Cake

Replace 1/2 cup (60g) of the flour with unsweetened cocoa powder. Increase sugar by 2 tbsp to balance bitterness. Add 1 tsp instant espresso powder to deepen chocolate flavor.

Note: Cocoa powder absorbs more moisture; this substitution slightly dries batter so the sour cream is important to maintain moisture.

Low-Sodium Option

Omit salt and reduce baking soda by half, adjusting baking powder to 2 tsp to maintain rise without increasing sodium content.

Note: Reducing sodium requires balancing leavening agents carefully to avoid off textures or flavors.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Store the cooled cake covered tightly at room temperature for up to 2 days. Use a cake dome or airtight container to prevent drying.

Refrigerator

Keep the cake wrapped or in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Be aware refrigeration can slightly dry the cake; bring to room temperature before serving.

Freezer

Wrap the cooled cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil. Freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator or at room temperature before serving.

Reheating

If desired, warm individual slices in a microwave for 10-15 seconds to soften the crumb before eating. Avoid reheating the whole cake as it may dry out.

Make Ahead

Bake the cake layers a day ahead and wrap tightly. Store at room temperature or refrigerate. Assemble and frost the next day for best texture and flavor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make this birthday cake ahead of time?
Yes, you can bake the layers up to a day in advance and store them wrapped at room temperature or refrigerated. Assemble and frost on the day of serving.
Why is my cake dense instead of light and fluffy?
This often happens due to overmixing after adding flour or using expired leavening agents. Also, cold ingredients can inhibit proper rise.
Can I use regular milk instead of buttermilk?
You can make a buttermilk substitute by adding 1 tablespoon of vinegar or lemon juice to 1 cup of milk and letting it sit for 5 minutes before using.
How long does this cake last in the fridge?
Stored properly in an airtight container, the cake will keep in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
Can I use cake flour instead of all-purpose flour?
Yes, cake flour will produce a softer crumb, but you may need to adjust the flour amount slightly since cake flour is lighter.
Why does my cake crack on top?
Cracking can be caused by oven temperature being too high or batter being overmixed. Use an oven thermometer and mix just until combined.

Ingredients

One 9-inch (23cm) round cake, 12 servings

  • 2 1/2 cups (310g) all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 1/2 tsp (6g) baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp (3g) baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp (3g) fine sea salt
  • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1 3/4 cups (350g) granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tsp (5ml) pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (240ml) buttermilk, well shaken
  • 1/2 cup (120g) sour cream, full fat and room temperature

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch (23cm) round cake pan or line it with parchment paper. This preparation ensures the cake releases easily after baking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly coated with butter and flour or parchment paper is securely fitted
Step 2
In a medium bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside. Sifting incorporates air and breaks up any lumps, leading to a smoother batter.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly combined without clumps
Step 3
In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar on medium speed until the mixture becomes pale and fluffy, about 4-5 minutes. This creaming step traps air, which contributes to a tender crumb and volume.
⏱ Time: 4-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is lighter in color, fluffy in texture, and sugar granules are partially dissolved
Step 4
Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Then mix in the vanilla extract. Incorporating eggs gradually helps maintain emulsion and prevents the batter from curdling.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and homogenous without separated liquid
Step 5
With the mixer on low speed, alternately add the dry ingredient mixture and the buttermilk-sour cream mixture (combine buttermilk and sour cream before adding). Start and end with the dry ingredients. Mix just until combined after each addition to avoid over-developing gluten.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and thick, no streaks of flour or liquid remain, but not overmixed
Step 6
Pour the batter evenly into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any large air bubbles.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Surface is level and smooth, no large air pockets visible
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes. Begin checking at 28 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center. The cake is done when the toothpick comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter.
⏱ Time: 28-32 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are lightly golden and start pulling away from the pan, toothpick test passes
Step 8
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan on a wire rack for 10-15 minutes. Then run a knife around the edges, invert onto the rack, and peel off the parchment paper to cool completely before frosting.
⏱ Time: 15-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is warm but firm, releases cleanly from pan, surface is dry and slightly springy
