Crispy Garlic Parmesan Chicken Wings Ready in 20 Minutes

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 17, 2026
10 min Prep Time
10 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These garlic parmesan chicken wings are perfectly crispy and loaded with flavor thanks to a light parmesan coating. I love how quick they come together in the air fryer—ideal for busy nights or game day snacks!

Pro Tips

Always pat wings dry before seasoning.
Removing moisture ensures the skin crisps up rather than steams. Moisture creates steam that prevents the Maillard reaction, which is why dry wings are key to achieving that perfect crunch.
Don’t overcrowd the air fryer basket.
Crowding traps steam and blocks airflow, leading to uneven cooking and soggy spots. Leaving space around each wing lets hot air circulate for consistent crispiness.
Flip wings halfway through cooking.
Turning the wings exposes all sides to direct heat, promoting even browning and preventing one side from overcooking or burning.
Use freshly grated parmesan cheese.
Freshly grated cheese melts and crisps better than pre-shredded, which often contains additives that inhibit melting and texture.
Preheat your air fryer before cooking.
Starting with a hot air fryer jumpstarts the cooking process, helping to crisp the skin quickly and evenly.
Rest wings briefly after cooking.
Allowing wings to rest sets the cheese crust and lets juices redistribute, resulting in juicier, more flavorful bites.
Add fresh parsley just before serving.
Parsley adds a fresh, vibrant note that balances the richness of garlic and parmesan without overpowering the flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the drying step.

Why it happens People often rush and season wings straight from the package or fridge, forgetting that wet skin won’t crisp properly.

Fix Always pat wings dry thoroughly with paper towels before seasoning. This simple step prevents sogginess and ensures the skin crisps up nicely in the air fryer.

Using pre-shredded parmesan cheese.

Why it happens Pre-shredded cheese is convenient but contains anti-caking agents that prevent proper melting and crisping.

Fix Grate your own parmesan fresh for best texture and flavor. It melts evenly and creates the signature crispy crust that defines this recipe.

Overcrowding the air fryer basket.

Why it happens Trying to cook too many wings at once can seem efficient, but it traps steam and reduces airflow.

Fix Cook wings in batches if needed, leaving space between them so hot air circulates for even crisping.

Not flipping wings halfway through.

Why it happens Some skip flipping to save time, but this leads to uneven browning and soggy spots.

Fix Set a timer to flip wings halfway through cooking to ensure all sides get crispy and golden.

Cooking at too low a temperature.

Why it happens Lower temps might seem safer, but they cause wings to dry out or cook unevenly without crisping.

Fix Stick to 400°F for air frying to get that perfect balance of crispy skin and juicy meat.

Variations

Spicy Garlic Parmesan Wings

Add 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the seasoning mix for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and depth without overpowering the parmesan flavor, perfect if you like a little heat.

Note: The extra spices won’t affect cooking time, but watch the wings closely near the end to avoid burning the paprika.

Lemon Herb Garlic Parmesan Wings

Mix 1 tablespoon of lemon zest and 1 teaspoon of fresh thyme with the garlic and parmesan for a bright, fresh twist. The citrus cuts through the richness and adds a lovely aromatic note.

Note: Add lemon zest after tossing with parmesan to prevent it from drying out during cooking.

Honey Garlic Parmesan Wings

Brush wings with a mixture of honey and garlic before air frying for a sticky, sweet glaze. This creates a delicious contrast with the salty parmesan crust, making the wings extra addictive.

Note: Reduce air fryer temperature to 375°F to avoid burning the honey glaze.

Gluten-Free Garlic Parmesan Wings

Ensure all seasonings and parmesan are gluten-free, then follow the recipe as is. This version is perfect for those with gluten sensitivities without compromising on flavor or texture.

Note: No changes in technique required; just confirm ingredient labels carefully.

Baked Garlic Parmesan Wings

If you don’t have an air fryer, bake wings at 425°F on a wire rack for 35-40 minutes, flipping halfway. This method yields crispy wings with a golden parmesan crust, though the texture is slightly different from air frying.

Note: Use a wire rack to allow air circulation around wings for maximum crispiness in the oven.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cooked wings can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria growth risk increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. They may lose some crispness but reheat well in the air fryer or oven to restore texture. Avoid microwaving as it makes the skin soggy.

Freezer

Freeze wings in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag for up to 3 months. To thaw, refrigerate overnight and reheat in the air fryer or oven to regain crispiness.

Reheating

Reheat wings in the air fryer at 375°F for 5-7 minutes until warmed through and crispy. Alternatively, use a preheated oven at 400°F for 10 minutes. Avoid microwaving as it softens the parmesan crust.

Make Ahead

You can prep and season wings a day ahead, storing them in the fridge covered. When ready, add parmesan just before cooking to keep the crust crisp and fresh.

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but thaw them completely and pat dry before seasoning. Cooking frozen wings directly can cause uneven cooking and soggy skin due to excess moisture.
What if I don’t have an air fryer?
You can bake the wings at 425°F on a wire rack for 35-40 minutes, flipping halfway. This yields crispy wings but the texture differs slightly from air frying.
Can I substitute parmesan with another cheese?
Parmesan’s unique melting and crisping properties are key here. Hard cheeses like Pecorino Romano can work, but softer cheeses won’t crisp properly.
How do I keep the wings crispy after cooking?
Serve immediately or reheat in the air fryer or oven. Avoid microwaving as it softens the crust. Resting briefly after cooking helps set the crust.
Can I make these wings spicy?
Absolutely! Add cayenne pepper, chili powder, or hot sauce to the seasoning mix for a spicy kick without losing the parmesan flavor.
Why is it important to flip the wings halfway?
Flipping ensures even exposure to hot air, creating uniform browning and crispiness on all sides.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Keep wings in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in the air fryer to restore crispiness.
Can I add fresh herbs to this recipe?
Yes! Add fresh parsley or thyme after cooking for a fresh flavor boost and pretty garnish.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1.5 pounds chicken wings, whole or separated
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by patting your chicken wings dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture on the skin prevents crispiness. If the wings are damp, the parmesan and oil won’t stick well, and you’ll miss out on that perfect crunchy texture. Dry wings help the seasoning adhere better, ensuring every bite is flavorful. Take your time here—blot thoroughly but gently to avoid tearing the skin.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Wings feel dry to the touch with no visible moisture
Step 2
In a large bowl, toss the wings with olive oil, minced garlic, salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning. The oil helps the garlic and spices cling to the wings and promotes browning in the air fryer. Make sure each wing is evenly coated by mixing well with your hands or tongs. This even coating is key to consistent flavor and crispiness across all the wings.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Wings are evenly coated with oil and seasoning, shiny but not dripping
Step 3
Sprinkle the grated parmesan cheese over the wings and toss again to distribute it evenly. The parmesan creates a crispy crust as it cooks, locking in flavor and moisture. Be gentle when mixing so the cheese coats the wings without clumping too much in one spot. This step is what makes these wings stand out from your usual garlic wing recipe.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Wings are lightly but evenly coated with parmesan, no large clumps
Step 4
Preheat your air fryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 3 minutes. Preheating ensures the wings start cooking immediately, which helps develop that crispy exterior. Skipping this step can lead to uneven cooking and soggy spots. While it heats up, arrange the wings in a single layer in the air fryer basket to allow hot air to circulate around them.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Air fryer reaches 400°F and basket is ready with wings spaced out
Step 5
Cook the wings in the air fryer at 400°F for 10 minutes. Halfway through, flip the wings to promote even browning on all sides. This flipping is essential because it exposes all surfaces to hot air, helping the parmesan crisp up perfectly. Avoid overcrowding the basket; if you have too many wings, cook in batches.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Wings are golden brown, crisp on all sides, and cooked through
Step 6
Once cooked, transfer the wings to a serving plate and sprinkle with fresh chopped parsley if you like. The fresh herb adds a nice pop of color and a subtle freshness that balances the rich garlic and parmesan flavors. Serve immediately for the best crunch.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Wings plated, garnished, and ready to serve hot
Step 7
If you want extra crispy wings, you can air fry them for an additional 2-3 minutes, but watch carefully to avoid burning the parmesan crust. The cheese can brown quickly, so this step is about finding the perfect golden color without going too far. This little extra crispiness is perfect if you like your wings extra crunchy.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Parmesan coating is golden and crunchy but not burnt
Step 8
For a twist, try tossing the wings in a little melted butter mixed with extra garlic after cooking. This adds richness and a glossy finish, enhancing the flavor even more. However, don’t skip the initial parmesan coating—this final step is just a flavor boost, not a replacement.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Wings are glossy with melted butter and extra garlic aroma
Step 9
Let the wings rest for 2 minutes before digging in. This short resting time allows the juices to redistribute and the parmesan crust to set perfectly. Eating right away might cause the cheese to flake off and the wings to be too hot to enjoy. Patience here really pays off with the best texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Wings slightly cooled but still warm and crispy

Final Thoughts
