Creamy Spinach and Artichoke Dip: A Balanced and Flavorful Classic

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

This recipe walks you through crafting a creamy spinach and artichoke dip that balances rich textures and fresh flavors. Detailed steps show how to prepare the ingredients for the creamiest results and why slow baking creates a golden, slightly crispy crust that contrasts beautifully with the soft interior.

Introduction

One of my favorite appetizers to prepare during chilly evenings or casual gatherings is spinach and artichoke dip. Over the years, I’ve refined my approach to ensure the dip is both creamy and fresh, avoiding the pitfalls of overly dense or watery textures. What sets this version apart is the careful preparation of the spinach and artichokes to maintain their structure and a slow, gentle bake that develops a golden crust without drying out the interior. I often serve it during winter get-togethers or game nights when everyone wants something comforting but shareable. The balance of tangy cream cheese, bright spinach, and tender artichokes makes it a dish that appeals to many palates. Below, I’ll guide you through each step with insights on why they matter.

Why This Works

The success of this dip hinges on balancing moisture and texture. Fresh or properly drained frozen spinach provides a vibrant green color and a slight bite, preventing the dip from becoming mushy. Similarly, draining and chopping the artichokes preserves their tenderness and prevents excess liquid from diluting the creamy base.

Combining cream cheese, sour cream, and mayonnaise creates a rich but smooth texture. Cream cheese offers body and a slight tang, sour cream adds moisture and acidity, while mayonnaise contributes creaminess and richness without overpowering.

Slow baking allows moisture to gently evaporate, concentrating flavors and forming a golden crust without drying the dip. This crust adds a pleasant contrast to the creamy interior, enhancing the overall mouthfeel.

  • Using fresh spinach ensures a bright color and slightly firm texture after cooking. If you choose frozen, it must be fully thawed and squeezed to remove all water, as excess moisture can make the dip runny and dilute flavors.
  • Canned artichoke hearts offer a tender yet firm texture that holds up in the dip. Chop them into small but distinct pieces to distribute flavor evenly while maintaining some bite. Avoid marinated versions as they can alter the dip’s flavor balance.
  • Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with other ingredients, creating the dip's creamy base. Room temperature cream cheese integrates easier and prevents lumps, ensuring a uniform texture.
  • Parmesan adds a salty, nutty flavor and promotes browning on the crust. Using freshly grated Parmesan rather than pre-grated ensures better melting and flavor.
  • A light drizzle of olive oil helps sauté the garlic and spinach, enhancing their aromas without adding heaviness. It also contributes subtle fruity notes that complement the dip.

Ingredients

  • 225g (8 oz) fresh spinach, roughly chopped (or substitute 150g/5 oz frozen spinach, thoroughly drained)
  • 240g (8 oz) canned artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
  • 170g (6 oz) cream cheese, softened
  • 120ml (1/2 cup) sour cream
  • 120ml (1/2 cup) mayonnaise
  • 100g (1 cup) grated mozzarella cheese
  • 50g (1/2 cup) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 tsp (1g) salt, adjust to taste
  • 1/4 tsp (0.5g) black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp (0.5g) crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 tbsp (15ml) olive oil

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F). Lightly grease a medium-sized baking dish (around 1 quart or 1 liter) with oil or butter to prevent sticking.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dish surface is evenly coated with oil, slightly glossy but not pooling
  2. Step 2

    If using fresh spinach, heat olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant, stirring constantly to avoid burning. Add the spinach and cook, stirring occasionally, until wilted and bright green, about 3-4 minutes. Remove from heat and transfer to a colander to drain excess liquid. Press gently with a spoon to remove more moisture. Let cool slightly.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Spinach appears wilted but still bright green. Garlic aroma is noticeable but not burnt.
  3. Step 3

    If using frozen spinach, ensure it is fully thawed and squeezed dry. Skip the sauté step and proceed directly to chopping if needed.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes for draining and chopping✓ Spinach is dry to the touch, no visible liquid when pressed
  4. Step 4

    In a mixing bowl, combine softened cream cheese, sour cream, and mayonnaise. Mix vigorously with a spatula or whisk until smooth and uniform in texture. This step ensures there are no lumps and the base is creamy.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth, glossy, and homogenous with no visible lumps
  5. Step 5

    Fold in the drained spinach, chopped artichokes, mozzarella, Parmesan, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if using. Stir gently but thoroughly to evenly distribute all ingredients without breaking down the vegetables.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes✓ Mixture is thick and creamy, with visible pieces of spinach and artichokes evenly distributed
  6. Step 6

    Transfer the mixture into the prepared baking dish and spread evenly. Smooth the surface with the back of a spoon or spatula to create an even layer for uniform baking.

    ⏱ 1-2 minutes✓ Surface is level, mixture fills dish without gaps or peaks
  7. Step 7

    Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake uncovered for 25-30 minutes. Watch for the edges bubbling gently and the top turning a light golden brown. This indicates the moisture is reducing and the crust is forming.

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ Edges are bubbling, top is lightly golden with some browning spots, dip feels set but still creamy inside
  8. Step 8

    Remove from oven and let the dip rest for 5 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the dip to thicken slightly and cool enough to handle while maintaining warmth.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dip surface firms slightly, aroma of garlic and cheese is strong and inviting

Pro Tips

  • Always squeeze spinach thoroughly to remove excess moisture. — Excess water from spinach can make the dip runny and prevent proper browning during baking. Removing moisture concentrates the spinach flavor and improves texture.
  • Use room temperature cream cheese for easier blending. — Cold cream cheese is difficult to mix smoothly and can create lumps, which affects the dip's overall texture.
  • Cook garlic gently to avoid bitterness. — Garlic burns quickly and turns bitter if overheated. Cooking it briefly over medium heat until fragrant preserves its aromatic quality without harshness.
  • Do not cover the dip while baking. — Leaving the dish uncovered allows moisture to evaporate, which helps form the characteristic golden crust and prevents a soggy top.
  • Let the dip rest after baking before serving. — Resting helps the dip set and thickens the texture slightly, making it easier to scoop and enhancing the eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using frozen spinach without fully thawing and draining.
    Why: Frozen spinach often retains excess water after thawing, which can make the dip watery if not drained properly.
    Fix: Thaw spinach completely and press firmly with paper towels or a clean kitchen cloth to remove all moisture.
  • Adding uncooked garlic directly into the mix.
    Why: Raw garlic has a sharp, sometimes harsh flavor that can overpower the dip and does not mellow during baking as well.
    Fix: Sauté garlic briefly before adding to the spinach to develop a mellow, aromatic flavor.
  • Overbaking the dip, leading to dryness.
    Why: Baking too long or at too high a temperature evaporates too much moisture, turning the dip dry and losing creaminess.
    Fix: Watch for bubbling edges and golden top around 25 minutes, then remove promptly.
  • Skipping the resting period after baking.
    Why: Serving immediately means the dip is too hot and runny, making it difficult to serve and eat.
    Fix: Allow the dip to rest for 5 minutes to thicken and cool slightly.

Variations

  • Vegan Spinach and Artichoke Dip: Substitute cream cheese, sour cream, and mayonnaise with plant-based alternatives such as vegan cream cheese and vegan mayo. Use nutritional yeast instead of Parmesan for a cheesy flavor.
    Ensure plant-based cheeses are soft and blend well to maintain creaminess. Baking time and texture may vary slightly due to different moisture content.
  • Gluten-Free Version: This recipe is naturally gluten-free, but double-check labels on mayonnaise and other processed ingredients to avoid hidden gluten.
    No changes needed in preparation or baking.
  • Spicy Jalapeño Spinach and Artichoke Dip: Add 1 finely diced jalapeño pepper (seeds removed for less heat) during the sauté step with spinach and garlic for added warmth and complexity.
    Adjust red pepper flakes accordingly to balance heat levels.
  • Cheesy Three-Cheese Variation: Increase mozzarella to 150g (1.5 cups) and add 50g (1/2 cup) shredded sharp cheddar cheese for a richer, cheesier flavor.
    Cheddar melts differently and may increase oiliness slightly; ensure thorough mixing for even distribution.
  • Seasonal Herb Infusion: Add 1 tablespoon of finely chopped fresh herbs such as thyme, parsley, or chives into the mixture before baking to brighten flavor and add freshness.
    Fresh herbs add aroma and complexity without affecting texture.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: After serving, do not leave the dip out longer than 2 hours to prevent bacterial growth.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover dip in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The dip will thicken as it chills.
  • 🧊 Freezer: This dip does not freeze well because the texture of the cream cheese base changes upon thawing, becoming grainy.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat gently in a 175°C (350°F) oven for 10-15 minutes until warmed through. Avoid microwaving at high power, which can cause uneven heating and separation.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the mixture up to step 5, cover tightly, and refrigerate up to 24 hours before baking. This allows flavors to meld and reduces prep time on the day of serving.

FAQ

Can I make spinach and artichoke dip ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the mixture a day in advance and refrigerate it before baking. This helps the flavors develop and saves time on serving day.
Why is my dip watery after baking?
This often happens if the spinach or artichokes were not drained properly. Make sure to squeeze out all excess moisture before mixing.
Can I use frozen spinach instead of fresh?
Yes, but it must be fully thawed and squeezed dry to avoid excess liquid that can make the dip runny.
How long does spinach and artichoke dip last in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, it lasts up to 3 days. Consume within this time for best flavor and texture.
Can I freeze leftover dip?
Freezing is not recommended as the creamy texture can separate and become grainy upon thawing.
How do I achieve a crispy top on my dip?
Bake uncovered at 175°C (350°F) until the edges bubble and the top is lightly golden, usually 25-30 minutes. Avoid covering the dish to allow moisture to evaporate.

Final Thoughts
