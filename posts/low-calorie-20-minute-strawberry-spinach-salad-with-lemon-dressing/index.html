Fresh Strawberry Spinach Salad with Zesty Lemon Dressing for Quick Lunches

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 10, 2026
15 min Prep Time
5 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This strawberry spinach salad is my go-to when I want something fresh and light. The lemon dressing adds a bright zing that really wakes up the flavors. Perfect for a quick lunch or a colorful side at dinner!

Pro Tips

Always dry your greens thoroughly before dressing.
Water clinging to leaves dilutes the dressing and makes your salad soggy quickly. Using a salad spinner or patting dry ensures the dressing sticks to the leaves instead of sliding off, preserving crispness and flavor.
Toast nuts just before assembling the salad.
Toasted nuts lose their crunch and aroma over time, so toasting them fresh maximizes their nutty flavor and textural contrast. This simple step elevates the salad from ordinary to memorable.
Whisk the dressing until it emulsifies fully.
Mustard acts as an emulsifier, binding oil and lemon juice into a creamy dressing. Whisking thoroughly prevents separation, ensuring each bite is evenly coated and flavorful.
Add dressing just before serving.
Dressing greens too early causes wilting and sogginess. Adding it last preserves the salad’s fresh texture and vibrant colors, making it more appealing and enjoyable.
Use ripe, fresh strawberries for best flavor.
Ripe strawberries bring natural sweetness and juiciness, balancing the tangy dressing. Less ripe berries can taste tart or bland, which throws off the flavor harmony.
Gently toss the salad to avoid bruising berries.
Strawberries are delicate and can become mushy if handled roughly. A gentle toss keeps the salad visually appealing and preserves texture.
Experiment with adding fresh herbs like mint or basil.
Herbs add an extra layer of freshness and complexity. Mint pairs beautifully with strawberries, enhancing the overall flavor without overpowering the salad.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding dressing too early

Why it happens People often dress salads right after assembling, not realizing the greens will wilt quickly.

Fix Keep the dressing separate and add right before serving. This keeps spinach crisp and strawberries fresh, preventing a soggy salad.

Not drying spinach properly

Why it happens Rushing prep leads to wet greens, which dilute dressing and cause sogginess.

Fix Use a salad spinner or pat spinach dry thoroughly before dressing to maintain crisp texture.

Skipping toasting almonds

Why it happens Some skip this step for convenience, unaware it impacts flavor and texture.

Fix Toast almonds in a dry pan until golden and fragrant to add crunch and nuttiness.

Using underripe strawberries

Why it happens Sometimes berries are chosen for appearance rather than ripeness.

Fix Select ripe, fragrant strawberries to ensure natural sweetness that balances the dressing.

Over-tossing the salad

Why it happens Trying to mix thoroughly can bruise delicate strawberries and wilt spinach.

Fix Toss gently with salad tongs to combine ingredients without damaging them.

Variations

Add Avocado for Creaminess

Slice in fresh avocado for a creamy texture that pairs well with the tangy dressing. It also boosts healthy fats and makes the salad more filling. The avocado adds a buttery richness that complements the sweet strawberries.

Note: Add avocado slices just before serving to prevent browning and gently fold them in to avoid mashing.

Use Baby Kale Instead of Spinach

Swap baby spinach for baby kale for a heartier green with a slight bitterness. This variation adds more chew and pairs nicely with the sweet and tangy flavors. It’s great if you want a more robust salad.

Note: Massage baby kale with a little olive oil before assembling to soften the leaves and improve texture.

Swap Feta for Goat Cheese

Use crumbly goat cheese instead of feta for a milder, creamier tang. Goat cheese melts slightly into the salad, creating a luscious mouthfeel and subtle flavor difference that’s lovely with the lemon dressing.

Note: Add goat cheese last to maintain its texture and avoid breaking it up too much.

Add Red Onion for a Sharp Bite

Thinly sliced red onion adds a sharp, pungent contrast that cuts through the sweetness of the strawberries. It gives the salad a bit more complexity and a subtle kick.

Note: Soak sliced onions in cold water for 10 minutes before adding to mellow their sharpness if preferred.

Make it Vegan by Omitting Cheese

Simply leave out the feta cheese to make this salad vegan-friendly without losing the fresh, vibrant flavors. You can add toasted pumpkin seeds for extra crunch instead.

Note: Add pumpkin seeds last and toast lightly for best texture and flavor.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad is best served fresh and should not be kept at room temperature for more than 1 hour. Cover loosely if waiting to serve to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store undressed salad components separately in airtight containers for up to 2 days. Once dressed, consume immediately or within a few hours as spinach wilts quickly. Dressing can be stored in the fridge for up to 5 days.

Freezer

Freezing this salad is not recommended as fresh greens and strawberries lose texture and flavor. However, you can freeze the dressing separately for up to 1 month in a sealed container.

Reheating

This salad is served cold and should not be reheated. If you want a warm version, try sautéing the spinach and serving with warm grilled strawberries separately.

Make Ahead

Toast almonds and prepare the dressing 1-2 days ahead and store separately. Wash and dry greens, hull and slice strawberries, and store in airtight containers in the fridge. Assemble and dress just before serving to maintain freshness.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries for this salad?
Frozen strawberries are not ideal because they release excess water when thawed, making the salad soggy. Fresh, ripe strawberries work best to maintain texture and flavor.
How long can I store the salad after adding dressing?
Once dressed, the salad is best eaten immediately or within a couple hours. Spinach wilts quickly when dressed, so for best texture, add dressing just before serving.
Can I substitute spinach with other greens?
Yes, baby kale, arugula, or mixed salad greens can be used. Keep in mind that kale is tougher and may need massaging, while arugula adds peppery notes.
Is there a good substitute for feta cheese?
Goat cheese is a great substitute for a milder, creamier tang. For a vegan option, omit cheese and add toasted nuts or seeds for texture.
What can I do if the dressing separates?
Whisk the dressing vigorously before use to re-emulsify it. Mustard helps bind the oil and lemon juice, but it can separate if left standing.
Can I make the dressing without honey?
Yes, you can use maple syrup or agave nectar as a vegan alternative. The sweetener balances the lemon’s acidity and helps emulsify the dressing.
How do I prevent strawberries from bruising while tossing?
Toss the salad gently with tongs or your hands, folding ingredients rather than stirring vigorously to keep the berries intact.
Can I add protein to make this a full meal?
Absolutely! Grilled chicken, shrimp, or chickpeas pair wonderfully with this salad to make it more filling and balanced.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 6 cups fresh baby spinach leaves, washed and dried
  • 1 pint fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • For the dressing:
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and thoroughly drying the baby spinach leaves. Excess water can make the salad soggy and dilute the dressing, so using a salad spinner or patting dry with paper towels is key. Once dry, place the spinach in a large salad bowl. This forms the fresh, green base that’ll hold all the other flavors and textures nicely.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Spinach leaves should be dry and vibrant green without any wilting.
Step 2
Hull and slice the strawberries evenly, about 1/4 inch thick. Try to keep the slices uniform so they distribute evenly throughout the salad and each bite has a balanced sweetness. Add the sliced strawberries directly on top of the spinach to keep them fresh and prevent bruising.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries should look fresh, juicy, and not mushy or overly soft.
Step 3
Toast the sliced almonds in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently. This step is crucial because toasting brings out their nutty aroma and adds a wonderful crunch to contrast the soft spinach and juicy berries. Watch closely to avoid burning — once they turn golden brown and smell fragrant, remove from heat and let cool.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Almonds should be golden brown and emit a warm, nutty fragrance.
Step 4
In a small bowl, whisk together fresh lemon juice, olive oil, honey, and Dijon mustard until fully combined and slightly thickened. The honey balances the acidity of the lemon while the mustard helps emulsify the dressing, giving it a silky texture that clings well to the salad ingredients. Season with salt and pepper to taste and adjust sweetness or tanginess as you prefer.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing should be smooth, slightly thick, and well-blended without oil separation.
Step 5
Pour the dressing over the spinach and strawberries, then gently toss everything together using salad tongs or clean hands. Tossing gently is important to avoid bruising the strawberries or wilting the spinach too much. The goal is to coat all ingredients evenly so every bite has a hint of that bright lemon flavor.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad ingredients should look evenly coated with dressing without sogginess.
Step 6
Sprinkle the toasted almonds and crumbled feta cheese over the top of the salad. The feta adds a creamy, tangy contrast that complements the sweetness of the berries and the crunch of the almonds. Adding these toppings last keeps their texture intact and makes the salad visually appealing.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Feta and almonds should be evenly distributed and remain intact.
Step 7
Give the salad one last gentle toss to incorporate the toppings slightly without breaking them up. This final step helps marry the flavors just before serving and ensures a balanced mix in every forkful. Serve immediately for the freshest taste and best texture.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Salad looks fresh, colorful, and evenly mixed without wilting.
Step 8
If you’re prepping ahead, keep the dressing separate and add it just before serving. This prevents the spinach from getting soggy. Store the salad components individually in airtight containers in the fridge and assemble when ready to eat.
⏱ Time: No cook time
✓ Doneness: Salad ingredients stay crisp and fresh when stored separately.
Step 9
Enjoy this salad as a light lunch or pair it with grilled chicken or fish for a satisfying dinner. The balance of sweet, tangy, creamy, and crunchy makes it a crowd-pleaser any time of day. Don’t be afraid to experiment with adding avocado, red onion, or fresh herbs for your own twist!
⏱ Time: No cook time
✓ Doneness: Salad tastes fresh, balanced, and vibrant.
