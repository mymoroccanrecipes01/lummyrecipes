No Bake Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Bars That Are Summer’s Best Treat

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 13, 2026
15 min Prep Time
9 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These no bake cheesecake bars are bursting with fresh lemon zing and juicy blueberries, making them a perfect quick dessert for warm days. The creamy texture with a crunchy crust is just irresistible!

Introduction

I first stumbled on this no bake lemon blueberry cheesecake bar recipe a few summers ago when I wanted a dessert that was bright, fresh, and didn’t require turning on the oven in the heat. My first attempt was a bit too tart, but after dialing back the lemon juice and adding a touch more sugar, it became an instant favorite at family BBQs. What I love about this version is how the lemon zest and juice really brighten the creamy cheesecake filling, while the fresh blueberries add juicy pops of sweetness. The crust is buttery and crumbly, providing the perfect contrast to the smooth, tangy filling. The trick I learned is whipping the cream separately and folding it in gently — it keeps the bars light and airy instead of dense. When these bars set in the fridge, they firm up just enough to slice neatly but still feel creamy and luscious in every bite. They’re perfect for summer gatherings, potlucks, or whenever you want a quick, fuss-free dessert that feels special. Plus, no oven means less heat in the kitchen and more time enjoying the sunshine!

What You'll Need

Approximately 9 bars

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs, finely crushed
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 2/3 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest, finely grated
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream, cold
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries, plus extra for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing the crust. Combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and granulated sugar in a bowl. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and the mixture holds together when pressed. This step is crucial to create a sturdy yet crumbly base that will support the creamy filling without becoming soggy. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a lined 8x8 inch pan, making sure to distribute it evenly and pack it down tightly using the back of a spoon or a flat-bottomed glass. This compacts the crumbs and prevents the crust from crumbling when you slice the bars later.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Crust feels firmly packed and there are no loose crumbs when you tap the pan gently
Step 2
Chill the crust in the refrigerator for about 10 minutes while you prepare the filling. This step helps the crust set and prevents it from mixing with the filling when poured on top. Skipping this can result in a soggy crust or uneven layers. Cold crust also makes it easier to get clean slices once the bars are ready.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Crust feels cool and firm to the touch
Step 3
In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar, lemon zest, lemon juice, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. The key here is to ensure the cream cheese is truly softened; otherwise, you’ll end up with lumps. Use room temperature cream cheese and beat thoroughly to get a silky texture. The lemon zest provides aromatic oils that boost the fresh lemon flavor without overpowering the filling. Don’t rush this step — well-blended filling means a smooth cheesecake texture.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, creamy, and free of lumps
Step 4
In a separate bowl, whip the cold heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the cream holds its shape without collapsing. Whipping the cream separately is important to create a light and airy filling. Overwhipping can cause the cream to turn grainy or start turning into butter, so watch carefully and stop as soon as stiff peaks appear.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Whipped cream forms stiff, stable peaks that hold shape
Step 5
Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use a light hand and fold slowly to keep as much air in the mixture as possible; this keeps the filling fluffy rather than dense. Overmixing here will deflate the cream and make the bars heavy. Fold until no streaks remain and the mixture is uniform in color and texture.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth, airy, and homogenous without streaks
Step 6
Carefully fold in the fresh blueberries, reserving a small handful for garnish. Folding them gently prevents bursting the berries and turning the filling purple. The blueberries add natural sweetness and a juicy texture contrast that complements the tanginess of the lemon. If you prefer, you can lightly crush some berries for a marbled effect, but I like them whole for the best texture.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Blueberries are evenly distributed without breaking apart
Step 7
Pour the filling over the chilled crust and spread it evenly with a spatula. Smooth the top for a clean finish. This step matters because an even layer ensures the bars set uniformly and slice neatly. Uneven filling can cause cracking or uneven texture when cut.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Filling surface is level and smooth
Step 8
Refrigerate the assembled bars for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling time allows the filling to set firmly so the bars hold their shape when sliced. Rushing this step can cause the bars to be too soft and messy to serve. Overnight chilling also helps flavors meld and intensify.
⏱ Time: 240
✓ Doneness: Filling is firm to the touch and bars slice cleanly
Step 9
Before serving, garnish the bars with the reserved fresh blueberries for a pretty, fresh touch. Use a sharp knife to cut into squares, wiping the knife clean between cuts for neat slices. Serve chilled for the best texture and refreshing flavor. These bars keep well in the fridge and are even better after a day as the flavors develop.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Bars cut cleanly with fresh blueberry garnish on top

Pro Tips

Chill the crust before adding the filling
Chilling the crust firms it up and prevents it from mixing into the filling when poured on top. This keeps layers distinct and the crust crisp rather than soggy, ensuring neat slices and a great texture contrast.
Use room temperature cream cheese
Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with other ingredients, avoiding lumps. If it’s too cold, you’ll get clumps that ruin the texture of the filling. Bringing it to room temp ensures a silky, creamy base.
Whip heavy cream separately until stiff peaks form
Whipping cream separately incorporates air, making the filling light and airy. Folding it in gently preserves this fluffiness. Skipping this step results in a dense, heavy texture that’s less enjoyable.
Fold blueberries in last and gently
Blueberries are delicate and can burst, bleeding color into the filling. Folding them in carefully keeps the filling visually appealing and preserves the texture contrast of juicy bursts.
Use fresh lemon juice and zest
Fresh lemon juice provides bright acidity that balances the richness of cream cheese. Zest adds aromatic oils that boost lemon flavor without adding liquid, which helps maintain the filling’s texture.
Cover and refrigerate bars for at least 4 hours
Extended chilling lets the filling set firmly through fat crystallization and protein structure development. This makes slicing easier and enhances flavor melding, giving a better-tasting dessert.
Press crust firmly and evenly
A well-packed crust holds together when cutting bars and prevents crumbling. Uneven or loose crust leads to a crumbly base that falls apart, ruining the eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese without softening

Why it happens Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but it’s too firm and lumps easily.

Fix Take the cream cheese out 30-60 minutes before starting to allow it to soften. Beat it thoroughly until smooth before adding other ingredients.

Skipping chilling time for the crust

Why it happens In a rush, some omit this step thinking it’s unnecessary.

Fix Always chill the crust 10-15 minutes before filling. This sets the butter and binds crumbs, preventing sogginess.

Overwhipping the cream

Why it happens It’s easy to keep whipping past stiff peaks, turning cream grainy or buttery.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. Watch carefully and test often to avoid overwhipping.

Mixing blueberries too vigorously

Why it happens Trying to fold quickly or using a spoon instead of spatula often bursts berries.

Fix Fold blueberries gently with a spatula just until evenly distributed to keep them intact.

Not zesting lemons properly

Why it happens Some include the white pith which is bitter, or skip zesting altogether.

Fix Use a fine grater or zester to remove only the yellow outer peel. Avoid the white pith to keep flavor bright and fresh.

Variations

Strawberry Lemon Cheesecake Bars

Swap blueberries for chopped fresh strawberries for a sweeter, softer berry flavor. Strawberries add a lovely red hue and pair beautifully with lemon’s brightness. These bars will be slightly juicier, so ensure the crust is well packed to hold up.

Note: Chop strawberries into small pieces and fold gently to prevent excess moisture from softening the filling too much.

Lemon Raspberry No Bake Bars

Use fresh raspberries instead of blueberries for a tart twist. Raspberries bring a delicate texture and a bit more acidity, enhancing the lemon’s zing. The color contrast is stunning, making these bars a pretty dessert option.

Note: Raspberries are softer so fold in carefully to avoid breaking them apart and coloring the filling.

Gluten-Free Crust Option

Replace graham crackers with gluten-free almond flour or gluten-free cookie crumbs. The almond flour adds a subtle nuttiness and keeps the crust firm. This variation is perfect for those avoiding gluten without sacrificing texture or flavor.

Note: Add an extra tablespoon of melted butter to help bind almond flour crust properly.

Coconut Lemon Blueberry Bars

Add 1/4 cup shredded unsweetened coconut to the crust or sprinkle on top for a tropical flair. The coconut adds texture and a hint of sweetness that complements the lemon and blueberry perfectly.

Note: Toast the coconut lightly before adding for deeper flavor and crunch.

Vegan No Bake Lemon Blueberry Bars

Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped in place of heavy cream. Swap butter with coconut oil and use vegan graham crackers. These bars keep the same tangy, creamy flavor with a plant-based twist.

Note: Chill coconut cream overnight and whip cold for best texture similar to heavy cream.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These bars can safely sit out for up to 2 hours at room temperature if covered to protect from insects and drying. For longer periods, refrigeration is recommended to maintain texture and freshness.

Refrigerator

Store bars in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. They may firm up more when cold but will still be creamy. For best texture, let sit at room temperature 10-15 minutes before serving.

Freezer

Freeze bars for up to 2 months by wrapping tightly in plastic wrap and placing in a freezer-safe container. Thaw overnight in the fridge. Texture may soften slightly but flavor remains great.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled or at room temperature. If desired, let bars warm slightly on the counter 10-15 minutes for softer texture.

Make Ahead

Make the crust and filling a day ahead, assemble and chill overnight. This allows flavors to meld and saves time on serving day. Garnish with fresh blueberries just before serving for best appearance.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries in this recipe?
Yes, you can use frozen blueberries but make sure to thaw and drain them completely before folding into the filling to avoid excess moisture. Frozen berries can release juice that might make the filling runny or change its color.
What can I substitute for cream cheese?
You can use mascarpone or ricotta cheese as alternatives, but mascarpone will yield a richer, creamier texture while ricotta will be grainier. For a vegan option, use plant-based cream cheese. Keep in mind substitutions can slightly alter flavor and texture.
How long do these bars last in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, these cheesecake bars last about 4 days in the fridge. Beyond that, the texture may start to degrade and the crust can become soggy. For best taste, consume within this timeframe.
Can I make these bars without whipping cream?
Whipping cream adds lightness and fluffiness to the filling. Without it, the bars will be denser and more like a traditional cheesecake texture. You could substitute with whipped coconut cream for a dairy-free option.
Why did my crust become soggy?
A soggy crust usually means the crust wasn’t packed firmly enough or the filling was too wet. Make sure to press the crust tightly and chill it before adding the filling. Also, avoid adding too much liquid to the filling.
Can I use lemon extract instead of fresh lemon juice and zest?
Lemon extract is much more concentrated and can taste artificial if overused. Fresh lemon juice and zest provide natural brightness and aromatic oils that extracts can’t replicate. It’s best to use fresh for authentic flavor.
How do I get clean slices when cutting the bars?
Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water and wiped dry before slicing. Clean the knife between cuts to prevent dragging filling. Let the bars chill thoroughly so they’re firm enough to cut neatly.
Can I add other fruits besides blueberries?
Absolutely! Strawberries, raspberries, or blackberries work well. Just fold them in gently and be mindful of moisture content, as some fruits release more juice which can affect texture.
