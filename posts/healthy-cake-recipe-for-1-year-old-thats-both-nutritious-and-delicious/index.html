Soft and Natural First Birthday Cake with No Added Sugar

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 6, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This gentle cake is perfect for your little one's first birthday—soft, naturally sweetened, and easy on tiny tummies. Using wholesome ingredients makes it a guilt-free treat to celebrate milestones.

Pro Tips

Always sift or thoroughly mix your dry ingredients before combining.
Sifting or mixing helps distribute leavening agents evenly, preventing uneven rising or bitter spots from clumped baking soda or powder. It also aerates the flour, contributing to a lighter texture.
Use ripe bananas and unsweetened applesauce for natural sweetness.
Ripe bananas have more sugars and a more intense flavor, which sweetens the cake naturally. Unsweetened applesauce adds moisture without extra sugar, helping keep the cake tender and baby-friendly.
Don’t overmix the batter once you combine wet and dry ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the whole wheat flour, resulting in a tough, dense cake. Gentle folding preserves a tender crumb and light texture essential for a baby’s cake.
Preheat your oven fully before baking.
Starting with a hot oven activates the leavening agents immediately, helping the cake rise properly. A cold oven can cause the cake to be dense and unevenly cooked.
Let the cake cool in the pan before removing.
Cooling allows the cake to set and reduces the risk of breaking apart. Removing it too soon can cause it to crumble, especially since it’s a soft cake.
Use full-fat yogurt for best moisture and texture.
Full-fat yogurt adds richness and moisture, improving the cake’s tenderness. Low-fat or non-fat versions can dry out the cake and make it less flavorful.
Use a gentle folding technique when mixing batter.
Folding helps combine ingredients without overworking the flour, which keeps the cake light and fluffy. Stirring vigorously can lead to a dense texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the preheating of the oven

Why it happens Sometimes home cooks start mixing and baking without waiting for the oven to reach temperature, assuming it will heat quickly enough.

Fix Always preheat your oven fully before placing the cake inside. This ensures the leavening agents activate properly and the cake rises evenly. Use an oven thermometer if needed to confirm accurate temperature.

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Eager to combine ingredients quickly, people often stir the batter too much, thinking it will improve texture.

Fix Fold dry ingredients into wet gently and only until just combined. A few streaks of flour are okay. This prevents gluten overdevelopment and keeps the cake tender.

Using unripe bananas

Why it happens Sometimes bananas are added before they’re ripe enough for sweetness and moisture.

Fix Use bananas with brown spots for natural sweetness and soft texture. Unripe bananas can make the cake less sweet and drier.

Substituting low-fat yogurt

Why it happens Trying to reduce fat, some choose non-fat yogurt, not realizing its impact on moisture.

Fix Stick with full-fat yogurt to ensure richness and tenderness. Low-fat versions can dry out the cake and affect flavor.

Not greasing the pan properly

Why it happens Rushing or skipping greasing the pan leads to sticking and broken cake when removing.

Fix Always grease the pan well with coconut oil or line with parchment paper to ensure smooth removal.

Variations

Banana and Blueberry First Birthday Cake

Add 1/2 cup fresh or frozen blueberries to the batter for a fruity twist. The blueberries add natural sweetness and a pop of color, making the cake even more appealing to little ones. Expect a moist crumb with bursts of juicy flavor.

Note: Fold blueberries gently into the batter at the end to avoid breaking them and staining the batter too much.

Carrot and Cinnamon Baby Cake

Grate 1/2 cup finely shredded carrot into the batter for added texture and natural sweetness. The cinnamon in the recipe complements the carrots beautifully for a cozy flavor perfect for fall birthdays.

Note: Squeeze excess moisture from grated carrots if very wet to prevent sogginess in the cake.

Pumpkin Spice First Birthday Cake

Replace the mashed banana with 1/2 cup pure pumpkin puree and add a pinch of nutmeg and cloves alongside the cinnamon. This variation is perfect for autumn and adds a lovely depth of flavor with natural sweetness.

Note: Pumpkin puree is wetter than banana, so reduce applesauce slightly to keep batter consistency balanced.

Apple and Oat Soft Baby Cake

Add 1/4 cup finely ground oats to the dry ingredients and fold in 1/2 cup finely chopped apples for extra fiber and a gentle texture. This gives the cake a subtle chewiness and natural sweetness from the apples.

Note: Use quick oats ground in a blender or oat flour for best texture.

Dairy-Free Coconut Baby Cake

Swap yogurt with coconut milk yogurt and use avocado oil instead of coconut oil for a dairy-free version. This keeps the cake moist and tender while accommodating dietary restrictions without sacrificing flavor.

Note: Check that coconut milk yogurt is plain and unsweetened to avoid extra sugars.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cake can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 24 hours if covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight container. Keeping it covered prevents drying out and contamination.

Refrigerator

Store the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Refrigeration may firm the cake slightly; bring it to room temperature before serving to restore softness. Avoid leaving it uncovered to prevent drying.

Freezer

Freeze the cake wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then in foil or a freezer bag for up to 2 months. To thaw, leave it overnight in the fridge and then bring to room temperature. Freezing may slightly change texture but it remains tasty.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Adding a tiny splash of water or covering loosely helps retain moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh on the day of serving for best texture. Alternatively, bake the cake a day before and store it wrapped at room temperature or refrigerated.

FAQ

Can I substitute all-purpose flour for whole wheat flour?
Yes, you can use all-purpose flour if you prefer a lighter texture. However, whole wheat flour adds fiber and a slight nuttiness. If substituting, expect a less dense cake and adjust baking time slightly if needed.
Is this cake suitable for babies with egg allergies?
This recipe contains egg for structure and moisture. For egg allergies, you can try replacing the egg with a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water) but texture may be slightly different. Always test cautiously with allergies.
Can I use a different oil instead of coconut oil?
Yes, mild-flavored oils like avocado or light olive oil work well. Coconut oil adds a subtle richness and helps with texture, but alternatives won’t drastically change the cake if you prefer.
How do I know when the cake is fully baked?
Insert a toothpick or cake tester into the center—if it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs (not wet batter), it’s done. The top should be lightly golden and spring back when touched gently.
Can I make this cake vegan?
To make it vegan, substitute the egg with a flax or chia egg and use a plant-based yogurt. Replace coconut oil with a vegan-friendly oil. The texture may vary slightly but still delicious.
What if my cake turns out dense or dry?
Dense cake usually results from overmixing or too much flour. Dryness can come from overbaking. Measure ingredients carefully, fold gently, and check doneness early to avoid these issues.
Can I freeze leftover cake?
Yes, wrap leftover cake tightly and freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight and warm gently before serving for best texture.
Is it okay to add frosting for a baby’s first birthday?
Absolutely! Use gentle, natural options like yogurt-based frosting or fruit purees. Avoid sugary or heavy frostings to keep it baby-friendly.

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/4 cup mashed ripe banana
  • 1/3 cup plain full-fat yogurt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons melted coconut oil

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8-inch round cake pan with a little coconut oil or line it with parchment paper. This step ensures your cake doesn’t stick and comes out cleanly. It’s important to prepare the pan before mixing because once the batter is ready, you want to bake it immediately to keep the leavening agents active for a nice rise.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased and parchment paper fits snugly
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, and ground cinnamon. Make sure to sift or at least thoroughly mix these dry ingredients to evenly distribute the baking soda and powder. This helps the cake rise uniformly and avoids any bitter pockets from the leavening agents.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no lumps
Step 3
In a separate large bowl, combine the unsweetened applesauce, mashed banana, yogurt, egg, vanilla extract, and melted coconut oil. Whisk until smooth and creamy. The bananas and applesauce provide natural sweetness and moisture, while the yogurt adds tenderness. Make sure the coconut oil is fully melted but not hot to avoid cooking the egg.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wet ingredients have a smooth, lump-free consistency
Step 4
Slowly add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, folding gently with a spatula. Mix just until combined — overmixing can develop gluten and make the cake tough. It’s okay if there are a few small streaks of flour; they will bake out. The goal is a tender crumb, so gentle folding is key.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no large flour pockets but not overmixed
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any air bubbles trapped inside. This helps the cake bake evenly without large holes or tunnels.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread and surface slightly smoothed
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Avoid opening the oven door too early because the cake needs steady heat to rise properly. Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center — it should come out clean or with just a few moist crumbs.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden top and toothpick inserted comes out clean
Step 7
Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. This resting time helps the cake set and makes it easier to remove from the pan without breaking. After that, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before serving or decorating.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake slightly shrinks from pan edges and is cool to touch
Step 8
If you want, you can frost the cake with a simple yogurt-based frosting or serve it plain for a natural, wholesome treat. For frosting, mix plain yogurt with a little mashed banana or pureed fruit for sweetness. Keep it light and fresh to complement the cake’s soft texture.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Frosting is smooth and spreadable without being runny
Step 9
Serve the cake at room temperature for the best texture. It’s perfect for little hands or spoon-feeding. This cake keeps well for a day or two if covered, but I usually recommend enjoying it fresh to preserve its softness and natural flavors.
⏱ Time: Immediate serving
✓ Doneness: Cake is tender, moist, and lightly sweetened
