Easy Sheet Pan Peach Cobbler That’s Perfect for Cozy Nights In

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 28, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This peach cobbler is my go-to when I want something sweet but simple. It bakes right on one pan for easy cleanup, with juicy peaches and a buttery crust. Great for a relaxed weekend dessert!

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix the batter once you add wet ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make the crust tough and chewy instead of tender and flaky. Stir just until combined to keep the texture light and soft.
Use ripe but firm peaches for best texture.
Overripe peaches tend to break down too much during baking, making the filling runny. Firm peaches hold their shape and provide that perfect bite and juiciness.
Let the cobbler rest before serving.
Resting allows the juices to thicken and the crust to set, preventing the filling from being too runny and making it easier to slice and serve.
Line your sheet pan with parchment paper for easier cleanup.
The sugary juices can caramelize and stick to the pan. Parchment paper prevents sticking and makes washing up quicker, so you spend more time enjoying dessert.
Add a sprinkle of coarse sugar on top before baking.
Coarse sugar adds a pleasant crunch and sparkle to the crust, enhancing texture and visual appeal.
Use fresh lemon juice, not bottled.
Fresh lemon juice provides bright acidity that balances sweetness and prevents the peaches from browning, preserving their natural color and flavor.
If peaches aren’t in season, try substituting nectarines.
Nectarines have a similar texture and sweetness but a slightly firmer skin and a bit more tartness, which complements the buttery crust nicely.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using overripe peaches that turn mushy.

Why it happens People often pick very soft peaches thinking they’ll be sweeter, but they break down too much during baking.

Fix Choose peaches that yield slightly when pressed but still hold their shape. If peaches are very ripe, reduce baking time slightly to prevent them from turning to mush.

Overmixing the batter leading to a tough crust.

Why it happens It’s tempting to stir thoroughly to avoid lumps, but this develops gluten in the flour.

Fix Mix wet and dry ingredients just until combined. A few lumps are okay and won’t affect the final texture.

Skipping the lemon juice and getting dull peaches.

Why it happens Lemon juice prevents oxidation, but some skip it to avoid adding acidity.

Fix Always toss peaches with lemon juice right before baking to keep them bright and flavorful.

Baking at too high a temperature causing burnt edges.

Why it happens Higher temps brown the crust quickly but don’t allow the filling to bubble and cook through.

Fix Maintain 375°F and tent with foil if crust browns too fast before filling is bubbly.

Not letting cobbler rest before serving.

Why it happens Impatience leads to cutting into it hot, which makes the filling run everywhere.

Fix Allow 10-15 minutes for the filling to thicken and the crust to set for neat slices.

Variations

Mixed Berry Sheet Pan Cobbler

Swap out peaches for a mix of fresh berries like blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries. The tartness of berries pairs beautifully with the buttery crust and adds a burst of vibrant color.

Note: Berries release more juice than peaches, so reduce sugar slightly and bake a few minutes longer to prevent sogginess.

Gluten-Free Peach Cobbler

Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend instead of regular flour to make this cobbler suitable for gluten-sensitive eaters. The texture remains tender with the right blend.

Note: Add a teaspoon of xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t contain it to help bind the batter and improve structure.

Vegan Peach Cobbler

Replace butter with coconut oil and use a plant-based milk like almond or oat milk. This keeps the cobbler dairy-free but still rich and flavorful.

Note: Coconut oil solidifies when cold, so melt it before mixing to incorporate evenly.

Spiced Maple Peach Cobbler

Add a tablespoon of maple syrup to the batter and increase cinnamon to 1.5 teaspoons for a deeper, autumnal flavor. Great for cooler months or holiday gatherings.

Note: Maple syrup adds moisture, so reduce milk by a couple of tablespoons to keep batter consistency.

Peach Cobbler with Oat Topping

Replace half the flour in the batter with rolled oats and add a tablespoon of brown sugar for a crispier, more textured topping that’s a bit heartier.

Note: Oats absorb more moisture, so add an extra tablespoon of milk to maintain batter consistency.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

You can keep the cobbler covered at room temperature for up to 4 hours. Beyond this, the fruit may ferment or spoil, so refrigeration is best for longer storage.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The crust may soften but reheating in the oven will help restore some crispness. It can also be enjoyed cold, though texture will differ.

Freezer

Freeze cobbler in a tightly wrapped container for up to 2 months. To freeze, wrap tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes until warmed through. Adding a splash of milk or cream before heating can help refresh the crust. Avoid microwaving to keep texture intact.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the peach mixture and batter separately 1 day ahead and keep refrigerated. Assemble and bake on the day you plan to serve for freshest results.

FAQ

Can I use canned peaches instead of fresh?
You can, but canned peaches are softer and more watery, which may result in a soggier crust. Drain them well and reduce added sugar since canned peaches are often sweetened. Fresh peaches give the best texture and flavor.
What if I don’t have a sheet pan? Can I use a different dish?
Absolutely! A 9x13 inch baking dish works perfectly. Just keep an eye on baking time since deeper dishes might require a few extra minutes to bake through.
How do I know when the cobbler is done baking?
Look for a golden crust and bubbling fruit juices around the edges. The crust should be firm to the touch but not burnt. If juices aren’t bubbling yet, it needs more time.
Can I make this gluten-free?
Yes, use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend and add xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t have it. This helps maintain the structure and texture similar to regular flour.
Is it okay to substitute almond milk or other non-dairy milk?
Definitely! Non-dairy milks like almond, oat, or soy milk work well and won’t drastically change the flavor. Just use unsweetened versions to control sweetness.
Can I add other fruits to the peaches?
Sure! Blueberries, raspberries, or sliced nectarines complement the peaches nicely. Just adjust sugar depending on the sweetness of the fruit mix.
Why is my crust sometimes soggy?
Sogginess often comes from too much liquid or underbaking. Make sure peaches aren’t overly juicy and bake until juices bubble and crust is golden. Resting before serving also helps.
How do I store leftovers to keep the crust crisp?
Store leftovers loosely covered to avoid trapping moisture. Reheat in the oven rather than microwave to help crisp the crust back up.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 servings

  • 6 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). While it’s warming up, prepare the peaches by peeling and slicing them into even pieces. Toss them in a mixing bowl with lemon juice, sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg. The lemon juice brightens the fruit and prevents browning, while the spices add warmth and depth. Make sure the peaches are evenly coated to ensure consistent flavor throughout the cobbler.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peaches look glossy and evenly coated with spices
Step 2
In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. This dry mix forms the base of your crust, so it’s important to sift or whisk well to avoid lumps. A light, even mix will help the crust rise properly and have a tender crumb once baked.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are uniformly combined with no clumps
Step 3
Add the melted butter, milk, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Stir gently just until combined — don’t overmix, or the crust can become tough. The butter adds richness and moisture, while the milk helps create a soft texture. The vanilla enhances the flavor subtly but noticeably.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth but still slightly thick, no dry flour spots
Step 4
Lightly grease a large sheet pan or 9x13 baking dish. Spread the peach mixture evenly across the pan. Pour the batter over the peaches in dollops, then gently spread it out with a spatula, leaving some gaps so the peaches peek through. This creates that rustic cobbler look and allows steam to escape during baking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peaches are spread evenly, batter covers with some peach edges visible
Step 5
Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for about 30 minutes. During baking, the batter will puff up and turn golden brown while the peaches bubble underneath, releasing their juices. Keep an eye on the crust’s color — you want it golden but not burnt. If it browns too quickly, tent with foil.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust is golden brown and peaches are bubbling at edges
Step 6
Remove the cobbler from the oven and let it cool for at least 10 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the juices to thicken slightly and the crust to set. It also makes it easier to cut and serve without the filling running all over the place.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cobbler feels set and warm but not too runny when touched
Step 7
Serve warm, ideally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. The cold creaminess contrasts beautifully with the warm, spiced peaches and buttery crust. This combo is what turns a simple dessert into something truly memorable.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ice cream starts to melt slightly on warm cobbler
Step 8
If you have leftovers, cover the pan loosely with foil and store in the fridge. Reheat gently in the oven to keep the crust crisp and the filling warm. Avoid microwaving if possible, since it can make the crust soggy.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Leftovers warmed through with crust still slightly crisp
Step 9
For extra flair, sprinkle a handful of chopped pecans or sliced almonds over the top before baking. This adds a lovely crunch and nutty flavor that pairs perfectly with the peaches. Just be sure to watch the baking time as nuts can toast quickly.
⏱ Time: Optional, 1 minute prep
✓ Doneness: Nuts are golden and fragrant after baking

Final Thoughts

Give this easy sheet pan peach cobbler a try—you’ll love how simple it is and how much flavor it packs. Don’t forget to share your cozy dessert moments!
