Cozy Mexican Cinnamon-Anise Cookies with a Zesty Orange Twist

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 22, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
24 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These Mexican cookies blend warm cinnamon and subtle anise with a surprising pop of orange zest that livens up each bite. Perfect for sharing with friends or enjoying with a cup of tea on a lazy afternoon.

Pro Tips

Always sift your dry ingredients together before adding to the wet mixture.
Sifting evenly distributes leavening agents and spices, preventing clumps and ensuring uniform flavor and rise. It aerates the flour, which helps produce a lighter, more tender cookie. Skipping this can cause uneven texture and flavor pockets.
Chill the dough for at least an hour before baking.
Chilling solidifies the butter, slowing spread in the oven, which results in thicker, chewier cookies. It also lets the flour hydrate and flavors meld, giving a more complex taste. Baking warm dough often leads to thin, crispy cookies that lack that tender center.
Use freshly grated orange zest, not bottled extract.
Fresh zest contains natural oils that provide vibrant citrus flavor and aroma, which bottled extracts lack. These oils volatilize during baking, lifting the cookie's flavor profile. Using bottled extract can give an artificial taste and less brightness.
Cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
This step incorporates air bubbles that expand during baking, creating a soft, tender texture. Rushing or under-beating yields dense cookies with poor rise. Proper creaming also dissolves sugar into butter, improving texture and sweetness distribution.
Don’t overmix the dough once the flour is added.
Overmixing develops gluten, which makes cookies tough and chewy in an unwanted way. Folding just until combined keeps the texture tender. Overworked dough also can cause cookies to spread unevenly during baking.
Use parchment paper or silicone mats on baking sheets.
This prevents sticking and promotes even baking by insulating the bottom of the cookies. It also makes cleanup easier and helps maintain cookie shape. Using unlined sheets can cause burning or uneven browning.
Let cookies cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring.
This resting lets the cookies firm up slightly, preventing breakage when moved. The residual heat finishes baking the centers gently, ensuring a chewy texture. Immediate removal can cause cookies to fall apart or be doughy inside.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the dough chilling step

Why it happens Often people want to bake immediately and think chilling is unnecessary or time-consuming.

Fix Plan ahead and chill the dough for at least an hour. This solidifies the butter and hydrates flour, preventing excessive spreading and producing thicker, chewier cookies with better flavor. If short on time, chill at least 30 minutes.

Overmixing after adding flour

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix thoroughly to combine ingredients, but this can develop gluten.

Fix Fold dry ingredients gently just until no streaks remain. Overmixing toughens cookies and causes uneven texture. Use a spatula to fold instead of a mixer at this stage.

Using old or stale spices

Why it happens Spices lose potency over time and are often forgotten in the pantry.

Fix Check spice freshness before baking. Fresh cinnamon and anise provide vibrant flavor. Replace spices yearly or grind fresh seeds for best results.

Adding too much orange zest or including pith

Why it happens Sometimes zest is scraped too deeply or overused, causing bitterness.

Fix Use only the thin colored outer layer of the orange peel. Avoid white pith beneath, which is bitter. One orange’s zest is enough to add brightness without overpowering.

Not creaming butter and sugar long enough

Why it happens People rush this step to save time or use cold butter.

Fix Use softened, room-temperature butter and beat sugars and butter for 3-4 minutes until pale and fluffy. This traps air, essential for tender cookies. Rushing results in dense, heavy texture.

Variations

Chocolate-Dipped Cinnamon-Anise Cookies

After baking and cooling, dip half of each cookie in melted dark chocolate and let set. The rich chocolate pairs beautifully with the warm spices and adds a touch of indulgence. Perfect for gifting or special occasions.

Note: Use good-quality chocolate and temper it if possible for a shiny finish. Let cookies cool completely before dipping to prevent melting.

Gluten-Free Mexican Spice Cookies

Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum. This keeps the texture tender while maintaining the spice flavor. Ideal for gluten-sensitive friends or family members.

Note: Gluten-free dough may be slightly more fragile; chill well before baking and handle gently.

Spiced Orange Iced Cookies

Once cooled, drizzle a simple icing made from powdered sugar and fresh orange juice over the cookies. This adds sweetness and a glossy finish, enhancing the citrus notes and making them extra festive.

Note: Apply icing when cookies are fully cool to avoid melting. Allow icing to set before storing.

Anise-Free Cinnamon Orange Cookies

Omit the anise seed and add an extra teaspoon of cinnamon plus a pinch of nutmeg for a more classic cinnamon cookie. This version is great for those who prefer less licorice flavor but still want warm spices.

Note: Adjust spices carefully to keep balance; nutmeg is potent and should be added sparingly.

Vegan Mexican Spice Cookies

Replace butter with vegan margarine or coconut oil and use a flax egg (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water) instead of a chicken egg. These swaps keep the cookies tender and flavorful without animal products.

Note: Chill the dough well to compensate for the softer fat and moisture from flax egg.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. Keep away from direct sunlight and heat to maintain freshness and prevent them from becoming stale or soft.

Refrigerator

Cookies can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 10 days. Refrigeration may firm them up and slightly dull the citrus aroma, so allow to come to room temperature before serving for best flavor.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a sealed freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Separate layers with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Thaw at room temperature before serving to restore texture and flavor.

Reheating

Warm cookies briefly in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes to refresh softness and revive the orange zest aroma. Avoid microwaving as it can make them rubbery. Adding a slice of bread in the container helps keep cookies moist.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough 1-2 days ahead and keep chilled tightly wrapped. Scoop and roll dough balls, then freeze on a tray. When ready to bake, place frozen dough on baking sheets and bake an extra 2-3 minutes.

FAQ

Can I substitute anise seed with another spice?
Yes, if you don’t like anise, you can substitute with fennel seed or simply increase cinnamon slightly. Both fennel and anise have similar licorice-like notes but fennel is milder. Avoid overpowering the dough, so add spices sparingly to keep balance.
What if I don’t have orange zest? Can I use extract?
While fresh orange zest is best for vibrant citrus oils, you can substitute with 1/2 teaspoon of orange extract. Be cautious as extracts can be strong and sometimes artificial tasting. Add gradually and taste the dough to avoid overpowering the spices.
Why do my cookies spread too much and get flat?
Flat cookies usually result from dough that’s too warm or not chilled long enough, or from over-softened butter. Chilling the dough solidifies fat, preventing excessive spread. Also, don’t overmix after adding flour as it can affect texture.
Can I make these cookies gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use a gluten-free flour blend that includes xanthan gum to mimic gluten’s binding properties. The dough may be more delicate, so chill it well and handle gently. Baking times might vary slightly, so watch closely.
How long do these cookies keep their freshness?
Stored properly in an airtight container at room temperature, these cookies stay fresh for about 5 days. After that, they may start to lose crispness or become stale. Refrigeration extends freshness but can dull flavors slightly.
Can I freeze the dough instead of the baked cookies?
Yes! Freeze the dough balls on a tray until firm, then transfer to a freezer bag. When ready, bake from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the baking time. This is a great way to have fresh cookies on demand.
Why are my cookies dry or crumbly?
Dry cookies often result from overbaking or too much flour. Make sure to measure flour properly by spooning it in and leveling off. Also, follow baking times closely and remove cookies when centers are just set but not browned.
Can I add nuts or dried fruit to this recipe?
Yes! Chopped pecans or walnuts add a nice crunch, while dried cranberries or raisins bring sweetness and chew. Fold in about 1/2 cup during the final mixing stage. Adjust baking time slightly if adding moist ingredients.

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 cookies

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground anise seed
  • Zest of 1 large orange
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: extra sugar and cinnamon for rolling

How To Do It

Step 1
In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, ground cinnamon, and ground anise seed. This helps evenly distribute the leavening agents and spices, ensuring every cookie has consistent flavor and texture. Sifting the flour also aerates it, which can result in a lighter cookie. Avoid skipping this step because uneven mixing could cause some cookies to rise too much or taste too spicy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are uniformly combined with no clumps of spices or flour visible
Step 2
In a large bowl, beat the softened butter with both sugars until creamy and light in color, about 3-4 minutes. This creaming step is crucial because it incorporates air into the dough, helping the cookies to rise and develop a tender crumb. If you rush it or don’t beat long enough, your cookies might turn out dense or greasy.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugars form a pale, fluffy mixture that holds soft peaks
Step 3
Add the egg and vanilla extract to the butter-sugar mixture, beating well to combine. Then stir in the orange zest. The egg adds moisture and structure, while vanilla and orange zest give depth and brightness to the flavor. Make sure to fully incorporate these ingredients so the dough is smooth and fragrant.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is smooth, slightly sticky, and evenly scented with orange and vanilla
Step 4
Gradually add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, folding gently with a spatula. Don’t overmix here — just combine until no streaks of flour remain. Overmixing develops gluten which can make your cookies tough instead of tender. The dough will be soft but manageable.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough forms a cohesive ball without excess flour visible
Step 5
Cover the dough with plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour. Chilling firms up the butter and helps the flavors meld. It also prevents the cookies from spreading too much while baking, leading to a thicker, chewier texture. Don’t skip this step or your cookies may bake too thin and crisp.
⏱ Time: 60 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is firm to the touch and easy to scoop
Step 6
Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper. Scoop tablespoon-sized balls of dough, then roll them in a mixture of sugar and cinnamon if desired for an extra crunchy, spiced crust. Place them about 2 inches apart to allow room for spreading.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are evenly spaced and coated with sugar mixture, ready to bake
Step 7
Bake the cookies for 12-15 minutes until the edges are golden and the centers look set but still soft. The cookies will continue to firm up as they cool, so avoid overbaking or they’ll be dry. Watch closely starting at 12 minutes to catch the perfect balance of chewy and crisp.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are lightly browned and centers no longer look wet
Step 8
Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. This resting time allows the cookies to firm up gently and prevents them from breaking apart when moved. Cooling on a rack ensures air circulation so they don’t get soggy underneath.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are cool enough to handle without breaking
Step 9
Store the cooled cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. They taste best the day they’re made but keep well and maintain their texture. If you want to refresh them, warming briefly in a low oven helps regain that fresh-baked softness.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cookies remain fresh and flavorful when stored properly
