Easy 30-Minute Vegan Tofu Stir-Fry with Fresh Greens

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 2, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This vibrant vegan stir-fry is a lifesaver for busy nights. Crispy tofu soaked in a quick marinade pairs beautifully with fresh greens in a savory sauce. Ready in just 30 minutes—perfect for a healthy dinner!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this vegan tofu stir-fry when I was craving something quick yet packed with fresh flavors. What hooked me was how the tofu gets a quick marinade that really amps up its flavor before it crisps up in the pan. I’ve tried lots of tofu recipes, but this one stands out because it’s both easy and satisfying — no long marinating or complicated steps. The secret? Pressing the tofu well and letting it soak in a simple soy-ginger marinade while you prep the veggies. That way, you get crispy edges and a deeply flavored interior. Plus, the fresh greens tossed in at the end add a lovely pop of color and nutrients. It’s become my go-to for weeknights when I want a healthy meal without fuss. The finished dish is vibrant, with a balance of savory, sweet, and tangy notes, and the tofu has just the right texture. I think you’ll love how fast it comes together and how fresh it tastes!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 14 oz firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 cups baby spinach or kale
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onions
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds
  • Cooked jasmine or brown rice, to serve

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by pressing your tofu to remove excess moisture. Wrap it in a clean kitchen towel and place a heavy pan on top for about 15 minutes. This step is crucial because it helps the tofu crisp up nicely instead of steaming in the pan. Once pressed, cut the tofu into even cubes around 1-inch in size to ensure they cook evenly and get a good sear. Avoid skipping this step or your tofu might come out soggy.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu feels firm and dry to the touch before cooking
Step 2
While the tofu presses, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup, minced garlic, and grated ginger in a bowl. This marinade balances salty, sweet, and tangy flavors that will soak into the tofu, making it flavorful throughout. Pour the marinade over the tofu cubes and gently toss to coat. Let it sit for at least 10 minutes — the longer, the better — so the tofu absorbs those flavors deeply. Rushing this step means less flavor inside the tofu.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu cubes are evenly coated and have a slight sheen from marinade
Step 3
Heat the sesame oil in a large non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. When hot, carefully add the tofu cubes in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. This allows each piece to crisp up properly. Cook without moving them for about 4-5 minutes until the bottoms are golden brown and crispy. Then flip and repeat on all sides. The key here is patience—flipping too soon will cause the tofu to stick or break apart.
⏱ Time: 12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu cubes have a golden-brown crust on all sides
Step 4
Once the tofu is crispy and cooked through, transfer it to a plate and set aside. In the same pan, add the sliced red bell pepper and broccoli florets. Stir-fry over medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp. This quick cooking preserves their vibrant color and crunch, adding freshness to the dish. Stir often to prevent burning, but don’t overcook or the veggies will turn mushy.
⏱ Time: 6 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables look bright and are tender but still firm
Step 5
Add the baby spinach or kale to the pan with the vegetables and toss for another 2 minutes until the greens have just wilted. This step adds a beautiful pop of color and boosts the nutrient content without overwhelming the dish. Overcooking greens can make them slimy, so keep an eye on them and remove from heat as soon as they soften.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Greens have softened but retain vibrant green color
Step 6
Return the crispy tofu to the pan with the vegetables and gently toss everything to combine. Pour in any remaining marinade left from earlier to add extra flavor and moisture, stirring carefully so the tofu doesn’t break. Warm everything together for 1-2 minutes just to meld the flavors. Avoid cooking longer or tofu might lose its crispiness.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is heated through with a glossy sauce coating tofu and veggies
Step 7
Remove the stir-fry from heat and sprinkle with sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds. These final touches add a nice crunch and fresh herbal note that brighten the dish. Don’t skip the sesame seeds—they add a subtle nuttiness that complements the soy-ginger flavors perfectly.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Garnishes evenly distributed and dish looks vibrant
Step 8
Serve the stir-fry hot over cooked jasmine or brown rice. The rice soaks up the delicious sauce and balances the textures of crispy tofu and tender veggies. If you prefer, you can also serve this with noodles or quinoa for a different twist. Make sure your rice is warm and fluffy before plating to keep the dish comforting and satisfying.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Rice is hot and fluffy, ready to complement the stir-fry
Step 9
Finally, enjoy your vibrant vegan tofu stir-fry fresh! Leftovers can be refrigerated but the tofu crisps best when freshly cooked. Pair this meal with a simple side salad or steamed dumplings for a complete vegan feast. The balance of textures and flavors makes it a crowd-pleaser every time.
⏱ Time: Immediate serving
✓ Doneness: Dish is hot, colorful, and smells fragrant with garlic and ginger

Pro Tips

Press your tofu for at least 15 minutes before cooking.
Removing excess water from tofu is critical for achieving a crispy texture. Water creates steam during cooking, which prevents browning. Pressing ensures the tofu sears properly and absorbs the marinade better, resulting in a more flavorful, satisfying bite.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when frying tofu cubes.
Crowding traps steam and lowers the pan’s temperature, causing tofu to steam rather than crisp. Fry in batches if necessary to maintain high heat and get a golden crust on all sides, which adds texture and flavor.
Marinate tofu for at least 10 minutes, preferably longer.
The longer tofu soaks in the marinade, the more flavor it absorbs. Since tofu is porous, quick marination imparts surface flavor, but giving it time allows the taste to penetrate inside, enhancing every bite.
Use high heat for stir-frying vegetables.
High heat cooks vegetables quickly, preserving their color, texture, and nutrients. Slow cooking breaks down cell walls, leading to mushy veggies. Stir constantly to avoid burning while keeping veggies crisp-tender.
Add leafy greens last to the stir-fry.
Greens wilt quickly and can become slimy if overcooked. Adding them at the end retains their vibrant color and fresh flavor, enhancing both the dish’s appearance and nutrition.
Toast sesame seeds before garnishing.
Toasting sesame seeds unlocks their nutty aroma and flavor, adding a delightful crunch that complements the stir-fry’s textures. Untoasted seeds lack this depth and can taste bland.
Serve immediately after cooking for best texture.
Tofu crisps and vegetables taste best fresh. Leftover stir-fry can become soggy as moisture redistributes. Reheating gently helps, but fresh serving ensures optimal texture contrast.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the tofu pressing step

Why it happens Many cooks underestimate how much water tofu contains and skip pressing to save time.

Fix Set aside 15 minutes to press tofu properly using a towel and weight. This step is essential to ensure the tofu crisps up rather than steaming. If short on time, press as long as you can — even 10 minutes helps.

Overcrowding the pan when frying tofu

Why it happens Trying to cook all tofu at once to save time leads to steaming, not frying.

Fix Cook tofu in batches to maintain pan temperature and get a golden crust. Use a large skillet or wok and leave space between pieces.

Overcooking vegetables until mushy

Why it happens Leaving veggies on heat too long or cooking on low heat causes loss of crunch and nutrients.

Fix Use high heat and stir often. Remove veggies as soon as they are tender-crisp and vibrant in color.

Not marinating tofu long enough

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning leads to a quick toss in marinade, resulting in bland tofu.

Fix Plan ahead to marinate tofu for at least 10 minutes. Longer marination allows flavors to penetrate deeply.

Adding greens too early

Why it happens Greens cook quickly and are sometimes added with other vegetables, causing them to overcook.

Fix Add leafy greens at the very end, tossing briefly to wilt just until tender.

Variations

Spicy Sriracha Tofu Stir-Fry

Add 1-2 tablespoons of sriracha sauce to the marinade for a spicy kick. You can also toss in some chili flakes while stir-frying veggies. Expect a vibrant heat that pairs well with the sweet and savory sauce.

Note: Monitor spice levels carefully and add sriracha gradually to avoid overpowering the tofu’s delicate flavor.

Peanut Sauce Vegan Stir-Fry

Swap the soy-vinegar marinade for a creamy peanut sauce made with peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, and a touch of maple syrup. This adds richness and a nutty depth to the dish, perfect for peanut lovers.

Note: Peanut sauce thickens quickly; add a splash of water or broth to thin before tossing with tofu and veggies.

Tofu Stir-Fry with Cashews and Snap Peas

Add roasted cashews and fresh snap peas for extra crunch and sweetness. This variation brings a nice texture contrast and a slightly nutty flavor to the stir-fry.

Note: Toast cashews separately to maintain crunch and add snap peas towards the end of cooking to keep them crisp.

Gluten-Free Tamari Stir-Fry

Use tamari instead of soy sauce to make this dish gluten-free without sacrificing flavor. This swap works well and keeps the marinade just as tasty and balanced.

Note: Ensure all other ingredients like rice vinegar and any sauces are certified gluten-free to avoid contamination.

Coconut Ginger Tofu Stir-Fry

Replace the soy sauce with coconut aminos and add a splash of coconut milk for a sweeter, creamier twist. This variation has a tropical flair and a milder, fragrant taste.

Note: Coconut milk can thin the sauce, so reduce other liquids slightly or cook a bit longer to thicken.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This stir-fry can be left out at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to keep it safe.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The tofu may lose some crispness but reheating gently can restore texture. Flavors often deepen when chilled, but serve warm for best experience.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible before sealing. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a skillet over medium heat with a splash of water or soy sauce to loosen the sauce and prevent drying. Avoid microwaving at high power which can make tofu rubbery. Warm just until heated through.

Make Ahead

You can press and marinate the tofu 1 day ahead and store it covered in the fridge. Chop vegetables in advance but keep them separate. On the day, stir-fry veggies fresh and combine with tofu for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use soft or silken tofu instead of firm?
Soft or silken tofu is not recommended for stir-frying as it breaks apart easily and won’t crisp up. Firm or extra-firm tofu holds its shape and crisps nicely. If you only have soft tofu, consider using it in soups or smoothies instead.
How do I make this recipe gluten-free?
Simply swap regular soy sauce for gluten-free tamari. Make sure any other sauces or vinegars you use are gluten-free certified. This substitution preserves the flavor while keeping the dish safe for gluten-sensitive diets.
What can I serve with this tofu stir-fry?
This dish pairs wonderfully with steamed jasmine or brown rice, quinoa, or noodles. You can also add a simple side salad or steamed dumplings for a more complete meal. The rice soaks up the sauce and balances the textures.
How do I get tofu crispy without oil splatter?
Use a non-stick or well-seasoned pan and medium-high heat. Make sure tofu is well pressed and dry before adding it to the hot oil. Don’t overcrowd the pan and flip carefully to avoid splashes. Using less oil and controlling heat helps reduce splatter.
Can I add other vegetables to this stir-fry?
Absolutely! Feel free to add mushrooms, carrots, snap peas, or baby corn. Just adjust the cooking time so each vegetable is cooked to your liking. Add denser veggies earlier and delicate ones like greens last.
What if my tofu doesn’t absorb the marinade well?
Make sure to press tofu thoroughly to remove moisture, which helps it soak up marinade. Also, marinate for at least 10 minutes, preferably longer. If pressed and marinated properly, tofu should absorb the flavors nicely.
Is it okay to skip marinating the tofu?
You can skip marinating in a pinch, but the tofu will be less flavorful inside. Marinating adds depth and balances the dish’s savory-sweet notes, so it’s worth the extra step for best results.
How do I store leftovers to keep tofu crispy?
Tofu crisps best when freshly cooked. For leftovers, store in an airtight container in the fridge. Reheat gently in a skillet to help restore some crispness. Avoid microwaving as it often makes tofu rubbery.
