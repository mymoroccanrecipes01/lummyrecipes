Fresh and Easy Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Glaze for Summer Parties

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 14, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These Caprese skewers are a fresh, bite-sized twist on the classic salad. Juicy cherry tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil come alive with a sweet balsamic glaze—perfect for summer get-togethers or quick snacks.

Pro Tips

Make the balsamic glaze ahead of time and store it in the fridge.
Preparing the glaze in advance lets you focus on assembling the skewers when guests arrive. The glaze thickens and flavors deepen as it cools, making it even more luscious. If you skip this step, you risk serving a runnier glaze that won't cling well to the skewers.
Use wooden skewers soaked in water for 10 minutes before assembling.
Soaking prevents the skewers from burning or splintering if you decide to grill or broil the skewers later. Even though this recipe is served fresh, this simple step ensures the skewers stay sturdy and safe for handling.
Thread the ingredients gently to avoid bruising basil and squishing tomatoes.
Applying too much pressure can tear delicate basil leaves and burst tomatoes, which affects both appearance and texture. Gentle handling preserves the visual appeal and ensures each bite is perfectly balanced.
Serve the skewers at room temperature rather than chilled.
Chilling dulls the flavors of mozzarella and basil and can cause the olive oil to solidify. Bringing skewers to room temp enhances the aroma and flavor, making them taste fresher and more vibrant.
Add a pinch of flaky sea salt right before serving.
A final sprinkle of flaky salt adds a delightful crunch and bursts of salty flavor that elevate the fresh ingredients. Regular table salt can dissolve too quickly and won’t provide the same texture contrast.
For a colorful presentation, mix red and yellow cherry tomatoes.
Visual appeal is important for appetizers. Using a variety of tomato colors makes the skewers look festive and inviting, which encourages guests to dig in.
Use fresh herbs other than basil for a flavor twist.
Adding herbs like oregano or mint can introduce new flavor layers and keep the recipe exciting. However, basil’s aroma is classic and pairs best with balsamic, so swap sparingly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using low-quality or pre-shredded mozzarella

Why it happens People often choose convenience over quality, assuming shredded cheese works just as well. This leads to a loss of the creamy texture and mild flavor that fresh mozzarella provides.

Fix Always use fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini). Drain them well and handle gently to retain their softness and moisture, which is essential for the perfect Caprese experience.

Burning the balsamic glaze while reducing

Why it happens Rushing the reduction or using too high heat causes the sugars to caramelize too quickly and burn, resulting in a bitter taste.

Fix Simmer the balsamic vinegar over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat as soon as it thickens to a syrupy consistency to keep the glaze sweet and balanced.

Piercing basil leaves instead of folding them

Why it happens Many assume threading leaves is straightforward, but piercing tears the delicate leaves, causing them to wilt and look unappetizing.

Fix Fold basil leaves in half before threading to keep them intact and fresh-looking. This technique preserves flavor and visual appeal.

Assembling skewers too far ahead of serving

Why it happens Making skewers hours in advance causes basil to wilt and mozzarella to release moisture, making the dish soggy.

Fix Prepare ingredients ahead but assemble and dress the skewers just before serving to maintain freshness and texture.

Skipping seasoning with salt and pepper

Why it happens People think fresh ingredients don’t need seasoning, but this dulls the overall flavor profile.

Fix Always add a pinch of salt and freshly ground pepper after assembling to enhance and balance the natural flavors.

Variations

Grilled Caprese Skewers

For a smoky twist, grill the assembled skewers for 1-2 minutes per side until the tomatoes blister slightly and the mozzarella softens. This adds a warm, smoky flavor and changes the texture to be more rustic and hearty.

Note: Watch the grill closely to avoid burning the basil or cheese. Use soaked skewers to prevent charring.

Caprese Skewers with Pesto Drizzle

Swap the balsamic glaze for a drizzle of homemade or store-bought basil pesto. This variation adds a rich, herbaceous punch and a creamy texture that complements the mozzarella beautifully.

Note: Pesto is thicker than glaze, so use sparingly to avoid overpowering the fresh ingredients.

Caprese Skewers with Prosciutto

Add thin slices of prosciutto between the tomato and mozzarella for a salty, savory layer. This makes the skewers more filling and adds a delightful contrast of textures and flavors.

Note: Fold prosciutto slices neatly to fit on the skewer without overpowering the other ingredients.

Vegan Caprese Skewers

Replace mozzarella with firm tofu cubes or vegan cheese alternatives, and use a balsamic glaze made without honey. This keeps the fresh flavors while making the dish suitable for vegan diets.

Note: Choose tofu varieties that hold shape well and marinate briefly in olive oil and herbs for extra flavor.

Caprese Skewers with Avocado

Add small avocado chunks to the skewers for creamy richness and a buttery texture. Avocado pairs well with the tomato and basil, creating a more substantial bite.

Note: Add avocado last and serve immediately to prevent browning.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Caprese skewers can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered to avoid drying out. Beyond that, the mozzarella may soften excessively and basil can wilt, so it’s best to serve fresh.

Refrigerator

Store assembled skewers in an airtight container for up to 1 day. Keep the balsamic glaze separate if possible to prevent sogginess. Let skewers come to room temperature before serving for best flavor.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as fresh mozzarella and basil lose texture and flavor when thawed. Prepare fresh for best results.

Reheating

These skewers are best served fresh or at room temperature. If you want a warm version, try grilling briefly instead of reheating in the microwave or oven, which can ruin texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the balsamic glaze and wash/dry tomatoes and basil a day ahead. Assemble and dress the skewers just before serving to keep them fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use regular vinegar instead of balsamic for the glaze?
Regular vinegar won’t provide the same sweetness or depth as balsamic vinegar, and the glaze may turn out too sharp or sour. If you don’t have balsamic, you can try mixing vinegar with a sweetener like honey, but the flavor won’t be quite the same.
How long can I store the balsamic glaze?
Stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator, balsamic glaze keeps well for up to a week. Make sure it’s cooled completely before sealing. Reheat gently if it thickens too much before using again.
What’s the best way to keep basil from wilting on skewers?
Handle basil leaves gently and fold them instead of piercing directly. Assemble skewers close to serving time and avoid refrigerating assembled skewers for long periods, as cold temperatures cause wilting.
Can I substitute fresh mozzarella with shredded mozzarella?
Shredded mozzarella won’t work well here because it lacks moisture and won’t hold its shape on the skewer. Fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini) are essential for the creamy texture and bite-sized presentation.
Is it okay to make these skewers a day ahead?
I recommend prepping ingredients ahead but assembling and dressing the skewers the same day. Assembled skewers can become soggy and basil will wilt if stored overnight with the glaze and oil.
Can I add other ingredients to these skewers?
Absolutely! You can add prosciutto, olives, or even small cucumber slices for variety. Just be mindful of balancing flavors so the fresh Caprese elements remain the star.
How do I prevent the mozzarella from being watery?
Drain fresh mozzarella balls well and pat them dry with paper towels before assembling. Excess moisture can make the skewers soggy and dilute the flavors.
What if I don’t have wooden skewers?
You can use toothpicks or small metal skewers as alternatives. Just make sure they’re sturdy enough to hold the ingredients without bending or breaking.

What You'll Need

Approximately 20 skewers, serving 4

  • 20 cherry tomatoes, washed and dried
  • 20 small fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • 20 fresh basil leaves, whole
  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar
  • Wooden skewers, about 6 inches long
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the balsamic glaze. Pour the 1/2 cup of balsamic vinegar and 1 tablespoon of honey into a small saucepan. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture reduces by half and thickens to a syrupy consistency. This usually takes about 10-12 minutes. Watch closely to avoid burning, as the sugars can caramelize quickly. Once thickened, remove from heat and let cool completely. This glaze is key for adding sweetness and tang that elevates the skewers.
⏱ Time: 12 minutes
✓ Doneness: The glaze should coat the back of a spoon and have a shiny, syrupy texture without being too runny or burnt.
Step 2
While the glaze cools, prepare the skewers. Thread one cherry tomato onto each wooden skewer, followed by a fresh basil leaf, then a mozzarella ball, and finally another basil leaf or tomato if you prefer a longer skewer. This layering ensures every bite has a balance of flavors. If your mozzarella balls are large, you can halve them to fit better on the skewer. Arrange the finished skewers on a serving platter.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers are neatly assembled with alternating colors and textures, looking vibrant and fresh.
Step 3
Drizzle the cooled balsamic glaze evenly over the skewers. Then, sprinkle a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Finish by lightly brushing or drizzling extra virgin olive oil over the top. This adds richness and a lovely sheen. The salt enhances the natural sweetness of the tomatoes and cheese, while the pepper adds a subtle kick.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers glisten with glaze and oil, and the seasoning is evenly distributed.
Step 4
Serve the Caprese skewers immediately at room temperature. If you need to prepare them ahead, keep them covered in the fridge but add the glaze and olive oil just before serving to keep the fresh basil from wilting and the mozzarella from becoming soggy.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers are fresh, colorful, and ready to enjoy with no sogginess or wilted leaves.
Step 5
If you want to add a little extra flair, sprinkle some finely chopped fresh herbs like oregano or a pinch of red pepper flakes for a mild heat. This step is optional but adds a nice depth of flavor if you like experimenting.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Herbs or spices are evenly sprinkled without overpowering the fresh ingredients.
Step 6
For a fun twist, you can swap cherry tomatoes for small heirloom varieties or grape tomatoes for different colors and sweetness levels. Just make sure they’re firm enough to hold on the skewer without falling apart.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tomatoes are firm and vibrant, maintaining shape on the skewer.
Step 7
When assembling, be gentle with the basil leaves so they don't tear. Folding them in half before threading can help keep them intact and looking pretty on the skewer.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Basil leaves are whole or neatly folded, adding fresh aroma and color.
Step 8
If you want to serve these at a party, arrange the skewers on a large platter with some extra balsamic glaze on the side for dipping. This encourages guests to add more if they like and keeps the presentation neat.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Platter looks inviting and well-organized with glaze available.
Step 9
Clean up is a breeze since there’s no cooking beyond the glaze. Just wash the skewers and utensils, and store any leftover glaze in a sealed container in the fridge for up to a week. It’s great on salads or grilled veggies too!
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Kitchen remains tidy and glaze is stored properly for reuse.
