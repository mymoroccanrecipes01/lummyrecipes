Fresh and Easy Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Glaze for Summer Parties

Lummy Hayes Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

These Caprese skewers are a fresh, bite-sized twist on the classic salad. Juicy cherry tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil come alive with a sweet balsamic glaze—perfect for summer get-togethers or quick snacks.

Introduction

I first made these Caprese skewers at a summer potluck a few years ago, and they were an instant hit! What I love about this recipe is how it transforms the classic Caprese salad into a fun, finger-food format that's perfect for parties or casual snacking. Unlike the usual salad plated on a dish, these skewers are all about bite-sized ease and vibrant presentation. One trick I learned early on is to use fresh, high-quality mozzarella and ripe cherry tomatoes—this makes all the difference in flavor. Also, I like to make a quick balsamic glaze by simmering balsamic vinegar with a bit of honey until it thickens; it adds a sweet tang that perfectly balances the creamy cheese and fresh basil. This glaze is the secret ingredient that ties everything together beautifully. Whenever I serve these skewers, they disappear quickly because they’re light but satisfying, and the combo of textures and flavors is just so refreshing. Whether it’s a backyard barbecue, picnic, or simple weeknight appetizer, these skewers always hit the spot. They’re fresh, colorful, and totally fuss-free, which is exactly what I want in a summer recipe.

Why This Works

The success of these Caprese skewers lies in the balance and interaction of fresh, simple ingredients combined with the transformative effect of the balsamic glaze. Cherry tomatoes bring juicy acidity and sweetness, which contrasts beautifully with the creamy mozzarella. Basil adds a fragrant herbal note that ties the flavors together. The glaze isn't just a drizzle; its reduction concentrates the vinegar’s tartness while the honey adds a mellow sweetness, creating a perfect harmony that elevates the traditional salad into a bite-sized delight.

Texturally, the mozzarella balls offer a soft, smooth mouthfeel that contrasts with the slight snap of the cherry tomatoes' skin. Threading the ingredients onto skewers creates a layered experience in each bite, making the flavors pop more than when simply tossed together. The olive oil adds richness and helps carry the flavors, while the seasoning enhances the natural sweetness and freshness of the produce.

Technique-wise, simmering the balsamic vinegar slowly is crucial to avoid burning and to achieve the right glaze thickness. If too thin, it won't cling to the skewers; too thick or burnt, it becomes bitter and unpalatable. The fresh basil must be handled gently to maintain its vibrant aroma and appearance, which is why folding rather than piercing it is recommended. This attention to detail in assembly ensures each skewer looks appetizing and tastes balanced.

In sum, the recipe works because it respects the integrity of each ingredient while using simple cooking science—like reduction and seasoning—to bring out deeper flavors. The result is a fresh, elegant appetizer that’s easy to make but impressive to serve.

  • Choose firm, ripe cherry tomatoes with smooth, shiny skins for the best flavor and texture. They should feel heavy for their size, indicating juiciness. Avoid overly soft or wrinkled tomatoes, as they won't hold up well on skewers and can become mushy. The natural sweetness and acidity of fresh tomatoes are key to balancing the creamy mozzarella and tangy glaze.
  • Select fresh mozzarella balls packed in water or whey for maximum creaminess and mild flavor. Avoid pre-shredded or low-moisture varieties as they lack the soft texture essential for this recipe. Drain well before assembling to prevent excess moisture from making the skewers soggy. The mozzarella’s delicate texture is a cornerstone of the dish’s mouthfeel.
  • Use vibrant green, unblemished basil leaves with a strong aroma. Freshness is crucial because basil loses flavor and wilts quickly. When threading, folding the leaves helps maintain their structure and preserves their bright flavor. Avoid bruised or yellowing leaves, as they can impart bitterness and detract from the skewer’s visual appeal.
  • Opt for a good-quality balsamic vinegar with a balanced acidity and sweetness. Traditional aged balsamic vinegars work best for making glaze as they reduce nicely without becoming overly sharp or bitter. The vinegar’s sugars caramelize during reduction, creating the syrupy texture and complex flavor that ties the ingredients together.
  • Use a fresh, fruity extra virgin olive oil to add richness and depth. It acts as a flavor carrier, enhancing the herbs and balancing the acidity of the glaze. Avoid oils with strong peppery notes that might overpower the delicate flavors of mozzarella and basil. The oil also gives the skewers a beautiful sheen and smooth mouthfeel.

Ingredients

  • 20 cherry tomatoes, washed and dried
  • 20 small fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • 20 fresh basil leaves, whole
  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar
  • Wooden skewers, about 6 inches long
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by making the balsamic glaze. Pour the 1/2 cup of balsamic vinegar and 1 tablespoon of honey into a small saucepan. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture reduces by half and thickens to a syrupy consistency. This usually takes about 10-12 minutes. Watch closely to avoid burning, as the sugars can caramelize quickly. Once thickened, remove from heat and let cool completely. This glaze is key for adding sweetness and tang that elevates the skewers.

    ⏱ 12 minutes✓ The glaze should coat the back of a spoon and have a shiny, syrupy texture without being too runny or burnt.
  2. Step 2

    While the glaze cools, prepare the skewers. Thread one cherry tomato onto each wooden skewer, followed by a fresh basil leaf, then a mozzarella ball, and finally another basil leaf or tomato if you prefer a longer skewer. This layering ensures every bite has a balance of flavors. If your mozzarella balls are large, you can halve them to fit better on the skewer. Arrange the finished skewers on a serving platter.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Skewers are neatly assembled with alternating colors and textures, looking vibrant and fresh.
  3. Step 3

    Drizzle the cooled balsamic glaze evenly over the skewers. Then, sprinkle a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Finish by lightly brushing or drizzling extra virgin olive oil over the top. This adds richness and a lovely sheen. The salt enhances the natural sweetness of the tomatoes and cheese, while the pepper adds a subtle kick.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Skewers glisten with glaze and oil, and the seasoning is evenly distributed.
  4. Step 4

    Serve the Caprese skewers immediately at room temperature. If you need to prepare them ahead, keep them covered in the fridge but add the glaze and olive oil just before serving to keep the fresh basil from wilting and the mozzarella from becoming soggy.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Skewers are fresh, colorful, and ready to enjoy with no sogginess or wilted leaves.
  5. Step 5

    If you want to add a little extra flair, sprinkle some finely chopped fresh herbs like oregano or a pinch of red pepper flakes for a mild heat. This step is optional but adds a nice depth of flavor if you like experimenting.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Herbs or spices are evenly sprinkled without overpowering the fresh ingredients.
  6. Step 6

    For a fun twist, you can swap cherry tomatoes for small heirloom varieties or grape tomatoes for different colors and sweetness levels. Just make sure they’re firm enough to hold on the skewer without falling apart.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Tomatoes are firm and vibrant, maintaining shape on the skewer.
  7. Step 7

    When assembling, be gentle with the basil leaves so they don't tear. Folding them in half before threading can help keep them intact and looking pretty on the skewer.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Basil leaves are whole or neatly folded, adding fresh aroma and color.
  8. Step 8

    If you want to serve these at a party, arrange the skewers on a large platter with some extra balsamic glaze on the side for dipping. This encourages guests to add more if they like and keeps the presentation neat.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Platter looks inviting and well-organized with glaze available.
  9. Step 9

    Clean up is a breeze since there’s no cooking beyond the glaze. Just wash the skewers and utensils, and store any leftover glaze in a sealed container in the fridge for up to a week. It’s great on salads or grilled veggies too!

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Kitchen remains tidy and glaze is stored properly for reuse.

Pro Tips

  • Make the balsamic glaze ahead of time and store it in the fridge. — Preparing the glaze in advance lets you focus on assembling the skewers when guests arrive. The glaze thickens and flavors deepen as it cools, making it even more luscious. If you skip this step, you risk serving a runnier glaze that won't cling well to the skewers.
  • Use wooden skewers soaked in water for 10 minutes before assembling. — Soaking prevents the skewers from burning or splintering if you decide to grill or broil the skewers later. Even though this recipe is served fresh, this simple step ensures the skewers stay sturdy and safe for handling.
  • Thread the ingredients gently to avoid bruising basil and squishing tomatoes. — Applying too much pressure can tear delicate basil leaves and burst tomatoes, which affects both appearance and texture. Gentle handling preserves the visual appeal and ensures each bite is perfectly balanced.
  • Serve the skewers at room temperature rather than chilled. — Chilling dulls the flavors of mozzarella and basil and can cause the olive oil to solidify. Bringing skewers to room temp enhances the aroma and flavor, making them taste fresher and more vibrant.
  • Add a pinch of flaky sea salt right before serving. — A final sprinkle of flaky salt adds a delightful crunch and bursts of salty flavor that elevate the fresh ingredients. Regular table salt can dissolve too quickly and won’t provide the same texture contrast.
  • For a colorful presentation, mix red and yellow cherry tomatoes. — Visual appeal is important for appetizers. Using a variety of tomato colors makes the skewers look festive and inviting, which encourages guests to dig in.
  • Use fresh herbs other than basil for a flavor twist. — Adding herbs like oregano or mint can introduce new flavor layers and keep the recipe exciting. However, basil’s aroma is classic and pairs best with balsamic, so swap sparingly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using low-quality or pre-shredded mozzarella
    Why: People often choose convenience over quality, assuming shredded cheese works just as well. This leads to a loss of the creamy texture and mild flavor that fresh mozzarella provides.
    Fix: Always use fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini). Drain them well and handle gently to retain their softness and moisture, which is essential for the perfect Caprese experience.
  • Burning the balsamic glaze while reducing
    Why: Rushing the reduction or using too high heat causes the sugars to caramelize too quickly and burn, resulting in a bitter taste.
    Fix: Simmer the balsamic vinegar over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat as soon as it thickens to a syrupy consistency to keep the glaze sweet and balanced.
  • Piercing basil leaves instead of folding them
    Why: Many assume threading leaves is straightforward, but piercing tears the delicate leaves, causing them to wilt and look unappetizing.
    Fix: Fold basil leaves in half before threading to keep them intact and fresh-looking. This technique preserves flavor and visual appeal.
  • Assembling skewers too far ahead of serving
    Why: Making skewers hours in advance causes basil to wilt and mozzarella to release moisture, making the dish soggy.
    Fix: Prepare ingredients ahead but assemble and dress the skewers just before serving to maintain freshness and texture.
  • Skipping seasoning with salt and pepper
    Why: People think fresh ingredients don’t need seasoning, but this dulls the overall flavor profile.
    Fix: Always add a pinch of salt and freshly ground pepper after assembling to enhance and balance the natural flavors.

Variations

  • Grilled Caprese Skewers: For a smoky twist, grill the assembled skewers for 1-2 minutes per side until the tomatoes blister slightly and the mozzarella softens. This adds a warm, smoky flavor and changes the texture to be more rustic and hearty.
    Watch the grill closely to avoid burning the basil or cheese. Use soaked skewers to prevent charring.
  • Caprese Skewers with Pesto Drizzle: Swap the balsamic glaze for a drizzle of homemade or store-bought basil pesto. This variation adds a rich, herbaceous punch and a creamy texture that complements the mozzarella beautifully.
    Pesto is thicker than glaze, so use sparingly to avoid overpowering the fresh ingredients.
  • Caprese Skewers with Prosciutto: Add thin slices of prosciutto between the tomato and mozzarella for a salty, savory layer. This makes the skewers more filling and adds a delightful contrast of textures and flavors.
    Fold prosciutto slices neatly to fit on the skewer without overpowering the other ingredients.
  • Vegan Caprese Skewers: Replace mozzarella with firm tofu cubes or vegan cheese alternatives, and use a balsamic glaze made without honey. This keeps the fresh flavors while making the dish suitable for vegan diets.
    Choose tofu varieties that hold shape well and marinate briefly in olive oil and herbs for extra flavor.
  • Caprese Skewers with Avocado: Add small avocado chunks to the skewers for creamy richness and a buttery texture. Avocado pairs well with the tomato and basil, creating a more substantial bite.
    Add avocado last and serve immediately to prevent browning.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Caprese skewers can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered to avoid drying out. Beyond that, the mozzarella may soften excessively and basil can wilt, so it’s best to serve fresh.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store assembled skewers in an airtight container for up to 1 day. Keep the balsamic glaze separate if possible to prevent sogginess. Let skewers come to room temperature before serving for best flavor.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freezing is not recommended as fresh mozzarella and basil lose texture and flavor when thawed. Prepare fresh for best results.
  • ♨️ Reheating: These skewers are best served fresh or at room temperature. If you want a warm version, try grilling briefly instead of reheating in the microwave or oven, which can ruin texture.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the balsamic glaze and wash/dry tomatoes and basil a day ahead. Assemble and dress the skewers just before serving to keep them fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use regular vinegar instead of balsamic for the glaze?
Regular vinegar won’t provide the same sweetness or depth as balsamic vinegar, and the glaze may turn out too sharp or sour. If you don’t have balsamic, you can try mixing vinegar with a sweetener like honey, but the flavor won’t be quite the same.
How long can I store the balsamic glaze?
Stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator, balsamic glaze keeps well for up to a week. Make sure it’s cooled completely before sealing. Reheat gently if it thickens too much before using again.
What’s the best way to keep basil from wilting on skewers?
Handle basil leaves gently and fold them instead of piercing directly. Assemble skewers close to serving time and avoid refrigerating assembled skewers for long periods, as cold temperatures cause wilting.
Can I substitute fresh mozzarella with shredded mozzarella?
Shredded mozzarella won’t work well here because it lacks moisture and won’t hold its shape on the skewer. Fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini) are essential for the creamy texture and bite-sized presentation.
Is it okay to make these skewers a day ahead?
I recommend prepping ingredients ahead but assembling and dressing the skewers the same day. Assembled skewers can become soggy and basil will wilt if stored overnight with the glaze and oil.
Can I add other ingredients to these skewers?
Absolutely! You can add prosciutto, olives, or even small cucumber slices for variety. Just be mindful of balancing flavors so the fresh Caprese elements remain the star.
How do I prevent the mozzarella from being watery?
Drain fresh mozzarella balls well and pat them dry with paper towels before assembling. Excess moisture can make the skewers soggy and dilute the flavors.
What if I don’t have wooden skewers?
You can use toothpicks or small metal skewers as alternatives. Just make sure they’re sturdy enough to hold the ingredients without bending or breaking.
