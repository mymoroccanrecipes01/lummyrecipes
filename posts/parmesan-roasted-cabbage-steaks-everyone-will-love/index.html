Roasted Cabbage Steaks with Crispy Parmesan Crust

Lummy Hayes
Published on March 23, 2026
10 min prep
30 min cook
40 min total
4 servings
Easy

This recipe for roasted cabbage steaks highlights the vegetable’s natural sweetness balanced with a crisp, nutty Parmesan crust. The roasting process softens the cabbage’s interior while creating a satisfying crunch on the edges. It’s a straightforward dish that works well as a vegetarian main or a flavorful side, especially in cooler months when cabbage is at its best.

Pro Tips

Use a sharp knife to slice the cabbage in thick, even steaks.
A clean cut helps the layers stay intact during roasting, ensuring even cooking and a better presentation.
Apply the Parmesan mixture just before roasting.
Adding cheese early helps it melt and brown properly, forming a crisp crust rather than becoming soggy.
Rotate the baking sheet halfway through cooking.
Ovens often have hot spots; rotating ensures even caramelization and browning on all steaks.
Don’t overcrowd the pan.
Leaving space allows hot air to circulate around each steak, helping the edges crisp up instead of steaming.
Reserve any loose cabbage leaves for roasting separately with oil and seasoning.
These smaller pieces roast quickly and can be added to salads or as snacks, reducing waste.
Adjust roasting time based on your oven’s heat and cabbage size.
Some ovens may cook faster or slower; watch for visual cues rather than relying solely on time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Slicing cabbage steaks too thinly.

Why it happens Thin slices dry out quickly or burn before the interior softens.

Fix Keep slices at least 1.5 cm thick for balanced texture.

Skipping oil or applying too little.

Why it happens Oil promotes browning and prevents sticking; without enough, cabbage can dry and burn.

Fix Brush both sides with oil evenly before roasting.

Using pre-grated Parmesan cheese.

Why it happens Pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents that prevent proper melting and browning.

Fix Grate fresh Parmesan for best crust texture.

Overcrowding the baking sheet.

Why it happens Crowding traps steam and prevents crisping.

Fix Arrange steaks spaced apart to allow airflow.

Variations

Vegan Roasted Cabbage Steaks

Replace Parmesan with a blend of nutritional yeast, ground almonds, garlic powder, and smoked paprika for a similar savory crust without dairy.

Note: Nutritional yeast adds umami flavor, while ground almonds provide texture to mimic the crust.

Spicy Roasted Cabbage Steaks

Add 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper or crushed red chili flakes to the Parmesan mixture for a subtle heat.

Note: Spices should be balanced to not overpower the cabbage's natural flavor.

Herbed Parmesan Cabbage Steaks

Mix finely chopped fresh herbs such as thyme, rosemary, or oregano into the Parmesan before applying.

Note: Fresh herbs add aromatic notes that complement the roasted flavors.

Balsamic Glazed Cabbage Steaks

Drizzle a tablespoon of balsamic reduction over the finished steaks just before serving for a sweet-tart contrast.

Note: The acidity of balsamic cuts through the richness of the cheese crust and roasted cabbage.

Cheesy Cauliflower Cabbage Steaks

Add a tablespoon of finely grated aged cheddar to the Parmesan mixture for extra sharpness and depth.

Note: Combining cheeses changes melting behavior; be sure to watch closely to avoid burning.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Do not leave roasted cabbage steaks at room temperature for more than 2 hours to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store cooled cabbage steaks in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Reheating may slightly soften the crisp crust.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as the texture of roasted cabbage degrades significantly upon thawing.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a preheated oven at 175°C (350°F) for 8-10 minutes to help restore crispness. Avoid microwaving as it softens the crust and leaves cabbage soggy.

Make Ahead

Prepare the Parmesan topping in advance and store covered in the refrigerator. Slice and oil the cabbage just before roasting to maintain freshness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make roasted cabbage steaks ahead of time?
You can prepare the cabbage and Parmesan mixture ahead and store separately. Roast just before serving for best texture.
Why are my cabbage steaks soggy instead of crispy?
Likely due to overcrowding the pan or insufficient oil. Ensure steaks are spaced out and coated evenly with oil.
Can I use red cabbage for this recipe?
Yes, red cabbage works well and adds a vibrant color. Cooking times remain similar.
Is it necessary to use Parmesan cheese?
Parmesan provides a distinctive nutty crust, but you can substitute with other hard cheeses or vegan alternatives.
How long does roasted cabbage last in the fridge?
Stored properly in an airtight container, it lasts 3 days. Reheat gently to maintain texture.
Can I add other seasonings or herbs?
Absolutely. Garlic powder, smoked paprika, and fresh herbs complement cabbage well and can be adjusted to taste.

Ingredients

4 cabbage steaks

  • 1 medium green cabbage (about 1.5 kg / 3.3 lbs), trimmed
  • 3 tbsp (45 ml) olive oil, divided
  • 1/2 cup (50 g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp (5 g) garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp (3 g) smoked paprika (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp (3 g) fine sea salt, divided
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it to prevent sticking.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes for preheating
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches and maintains consistent 220°C (425°F) temperature.
Step 2
Remove the outer loose leaves of the cabbage and trim the base slightly to create a flat bottom. Slice the cabbage into 4 thick steaks approximately 1.5–2 cm (about 2/3 inch) thick. Some leaves may fall apart; set those aside for roasting separately or discard.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Steaks are uniform thickness, with intact layers holding together.
Step 3
Brush each cabbage steak on both sides with 2 tablespoons (30 ml) of olive oil in total, ensuring even coverage. Sprinkle about 1/4 teaspoon (1.5 g) of salt evenly over both sides of each steak.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Steaks appear glossy and lightly coated with oil and salt.
Step 4
In a small bowl, combine the grated Parmesan, garlic powder, smoked paprika, a pinch of salt, and freshly ground black pepper. Sprinkle this mixture evenly over the top side of each cabbage steak, pressing lightly so the cheese adheres.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheese mixture forms a uniform layer on the steak surfaces.
Step 5
Place the cabbage steaks on the prepared baking sheet with the cheese-coated side facing up. Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, rotating the pan halfway through. The steaks are done when the edges are golden brown and crisp, the cheese is bubbling and browned, and the cabbage interior is tender when pierced with a fork.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges caramelized to golden brown, cheese crust crisp and browned, interior soft but not mushy.
Step 6
Remove the steaks from the oven and sprinkle with a small pinch of salt and freshly chopped parsley if desired. Serve warm.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Steaks steaming hot with visible crisp edges and browned cheese crust.
