No Bake Raspberry Swirl Cheesecake That’s Creamy & Refreshing

Lummy Hayes
Published on April 13, 2026
15 min Prep Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This no bake cheesecake is a total game-changer—super creamy with a fresh raspberry swirl that brightens every bite. It’s perfect for when you want a fuss-free dessert that still wows. Great for summer get-togethers or last-minute treats!

Pro Tips

Always use room temperature cream cheese for smooth mixing.
Cold cream cheese lumps easily and is hard to blend evenly, which can ruin the filling’s texture. Letting it soften at room temperature for about 30 minutes makes it easier to whip into a silky, smooth base.
Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture.
Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring or beating aggressively deflates the mixture, resulting in a dense, heavy cheesecake.
Chill the crust before adding the filling.
Chilling firms up the crust, helping it hold shape and preventing it from becoming soggy once the filling is added. It also makes slicing cleaner and easier later.
Use a fine mesh sieve to strain the raspberry sauce.
Removing seeds creates a smooth swirl in the filling, avoiding gritty texture that can distract from the creamy cheesecake experience.
Refrigerate the cheesecake overnight for best results.
The filling needs time to set and firm up. Chilling overnight ensures the cheesecake slices cleanly and the flavors meld beautifully.
Dip your knife in hot water before slicing.
Wiping the knife clean between cuts prevents drag and keeps slices neat and attractive, making serving easier and more elegant.
Use fresh raspberries for the brightest flavor in the swirl.
Fresh berries have a natural brightness and texture that frozen berries can’t match, elevating the overall taste and appearance of the cheesecake.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese straight from the fridge

Why it happens Many home cooks forget to soften cream cheese, assuming mixing will smooth it out.

Fix Always take cream cheese out 30-60 minutes before starting. If in a rush, microwave in short bursts (5 seconds) to soften but not melt.

Overwhipping the heavy cream

Why it happens It’s easy to get carried away trying to reach stiff peaks or forget to stop on time.

Fix Watch carefully for soft peaks where cream holds shape but tips fold over. Stop immediately to avoid grainy texture or butter formation.

Not chilling the cheesecake long enough

Why it happens Impatience or time constraints lead to slicing too soon.

Fix Plan ahead and refrigerate at least 6 hours or overnight so filling sets properly and slices cleanly.

Skipping the straining of raspberry sauce

Why it happens Some think straining is unnecessary or too much work.

Fix Strain to remove seeds for smooth texture. Use a fine mesh sieve and press gently with a spoon.

Pressing the crust too lightly

Why it happens Not realizing crust needs to be compact to hold together.

Fix Use the bottom of a measuring cup or glass to firmly press crust evenly and tightly before chilling.

Variations

Mixed Berry Swirl Cheesecake

Swap out raspberries for a mix of blueberries, blackberries, and strawberries. Cook them down with sugar and lemon juice the same way. This variation adds complexity and a deeper berry flavor profile, perfect for berry lovers.

Note: Berry mixtures may release more juice; cook longer to reduce and strain well to avoid runny filling.

Chocolate Swirl No Bake Cheesecake

Add melted semi-sweet chocolate to half the filling and swirl it with the plain cream cheese mixture. This creates a rich chocolate-vanilla marbled cheesecake that’s indulgent yet simple.

Note: Cool melted chocolate before folding in to prevent melting the whipped cream.

Lemon Raspberry No Bake Cheesecake

Add fresh lemon zest and a tablespoon of lemon juice to the cream cheese mixture for a bright citrus kick that complements the raspberry swirl beautifully.

Note: Adjust sugar slightly to balance the added acidity from lemon juice.

No Bake Cheesecake with Oreo Crust

Replace graham cracker crust with crushed Oreo cookies mixed with melted butter for a chocolatey base that contrasts nicely with the creamy filling and raspberry swirl.

Note: Press Oreo crust firmly and chill well to prevent crumbling.

Vegan No Bake Raspberry Cheesecake

Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to soft peaks. Sweeten with powdered sugar and use a nut-based crust. This dairy-free version is just as creamy and delicious with the same fresh raspberry swirl.

Note: Coconut cream needs to be well chilled and whipped carefully to achieve the right texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cheesecake should not be kept at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to its dairy content. Always cover it with plastic wrap or a cake dome if left out briefly.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container or covered tightly with plastic wrap for up to 4 days. The texture remains creamy but may firm up slightly. Serve chilled or let sit a few minutes at room temperature for softer slices.

Freezer

Freeze cheesecake slices individually wrapped in plastic wrap and foil for up to 2 months. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight. Texture might be slightly less creamy but still delicious.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled. If too firm, let sit at room temperature for 15 minutes before serving to soften.

Make Ahead

Make the crust and raspberry sauce up to 2 days ahead and store separately. Prepare filling and assemble 1 day before serving. Chill overnight for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use frozen raspberries for the swirl?
Yes, but frozen raspberries release more liquid, so cook the sauce longer to reduce excess moisture. Also, strain well to avoid a watery filling.
How long does the cheesecake need to chill before serving?
At least 6 hours is necessary for the filling to set properly, but overnight chilling is best for clean slices and optimal texture.
What if I don’t have a springform pan?
You can use a regular pie dish or cake pan lined with parchment paper for easier removal. Just be gentle when slicing and serving.
Can I make this cheesecake without gelatin?
This recipe doesn’t use gelatin; the whipped cream provides enough structure when chilled properly.
How do I prevent the crust from getting soggy?
Press the crust firmly and chill it before adding the filling. This helps it set and creates a barrier to keep it crisp.
Can I substitute cream cheese with mascarpone?
Yes, mascarpone can be used for a slightly sweeter and silkier texture. You may want to reduce the powdered sugar slightly.
Is it possible to make this cheesecake dairy-free?
Absolutely! Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to soft peaks. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
How do I get those pretty raspberry swirls?
Drizzle the raspberry sauce onto the filling and use a skewer or knife to gently swirl it through without overmixing to maintain a marbled effect.

What You'll Need

8 servings

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs, finely crushed
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for raspberry swirl)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the crust. Combine the finely crushed graham cracker crumbs with the melted butter and granulated sugar in a mixing bowl. Stir until the mixture resembles wet sand. Press this mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan or pie dish, making sure it’s even and compact. Pressing firmly is important because a loosely packed crust will crumble when you slice the cheesecake later. Chill the crust in the fridge while you prepare the filling so it firms up nicely.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust is evenly packed and feels firm to the touch after chilling
Step 2
Prepare the raspberry swirl by placing fresh raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice into a small saucepan over medium heat. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the berries break down and the mixture thickens slightly, about 5-7 minutes. Then strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve to remove seeds. Let the raspberry sauce cool completely before folding it into the cheesecake filling. This step is crucial because warm sauce can melt your whipped cream and ruin the texture.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Raspberry sauce is thickened, smooth, and cooled to room temperature
Step 3
In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. This ensures the filling has no lumps and tastes evenly sweetened. Make sure the cream cheese is at room temperature to avoid lumps and to get a silky texture. Scrape down the sides of the bowl as needed to incorporate everything evenly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream cheese mixture is smooth, fluffy, and free of lumps
Step 4
In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream until it forms soft peaks. This means when you lift the whisk, the cream holds its shape but the tip slightly folds over. Whipping to soft peaks is key because over whipping can make the cream grainy and under whipping won’t provide enough structure. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium-high speed for best results.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Whipped cream holds soft peaks that gently fold over
Step 5
Gently fold about two-thirds of the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use a light hand to keep the mixture airy and fluffy. Folding instead of stirring prevents deflating the whipped cream, which keeps the cheesecake light instead of dense. Once combined, fold in half of the cooled raspberry sauce, swirling it gently through the filling for a marbled effect.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth, fluffy with visible swirls of raspberry sauce
Step 6
Spread half of the cheesecake filling evenly over the chilled crust. Drizzle half of the remaining raspberry sauce on top and use a skewer or knife to create swirls by gently dragging it through the sauce and filling. Repeat layering with the remaining filling and sauce, swirling again. This layering technique creates a beautiful marbled look that makes the cheesecake visually stunning.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling layers are even with decorative raspberry swirls on top
Step 7
Cover the cheesecake loosely with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling time lets the filling set properly, so the cheesecake slices cleanly and holds its shape. Rushing this step can result in a runny filling and messy slices.
⏱ Time: 6+ hours
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake is firm to the touch and slices cleanly
Step 8
When ready to serve, remove the cheesecake from the fridge and carefully release it from the springform pan. Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water and wiped dry to slice the cheesecake cleanly. Wiping the knife between cuts helps maintain neat slices without dragging the filling.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Slices have clean edges and hold their shape
Step 9
Garnish with fresh raspberries or mint leaves if desired for an extra pop of color and freshness. Serve chilled and enjoy the creamy, tangy, and sweet flavors all in one luscious bite. This dessert pairs well with a cup of coffee or a light dessert wine.
⏱ Time: Optional garnish time 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake is attractively garnished and ready to serve
