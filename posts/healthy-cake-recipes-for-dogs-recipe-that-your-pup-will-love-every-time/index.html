Wholesome Pumpkin Oat Cake Your Dog Will Adore

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 6, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This pumpkin oat cake is a simple, healthy treat for your pup packed with wholesome ingredients like oats and pumpkin. It’s perfect for birthdays or just spoiling your furry buddy with something tasty and nutritious!

Pro Tips

Use canned pumpkin puree instead of fresh pumpkin for consistent moisture.
Canned pumpkin puree is cooked and pureed, providing a smooth texture and reliable moisture content. Fresh pumpkin varies in water content and may require additional cooking and blending to achieve the same consistency. Using canned pumpkin ensures your cake stays moist and tender.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add dry ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make the cake tough and dense. Since this recipe uses whole wheat flour and oats, mixing just until combined keeps the texture soft and tender, perfect for dogs to chew easily.
Let the cake cool completely before slicing.
Cutting a warm cake can cause it to crumble or fall apart because the structure hasn’t fully set yet. Cooling allows the proteins and starches to firm up, resulting in cleaner slices that hold their shape well for serving.
Avoid sweetened or flavored peanut butter.
Many commercial peanut butters contain added sugar or xylitol, which is toxic to dogs. Using natural, unsweetened peanut butter keeps the treat safe and healthy while still providing that irresistible nutty flavor.
Use a toothpick to check doneness instead of relying solely on timer.
Oven temperatures can vary, so checking with a toothpick ensures your cake is perfectly baked. A clean or lightly crumbed toothpick means the cake is done, preventing under or overbaking.
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge.
Proper storage keeps the cake fresh and prevents it from drying out or absorbing fridge odors. Dogs appreciate fresh-tasting treats, and storing correctly extends the cake’s shelf life up to 5 days.
Freeze extra slices for longer storage.
Freezing preserves the cake’s flavor and texture for up to 3 months. Wrap slices tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn. Thaw in the fridge overnight before serving.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using sweetened or flavored peanut butter

Why it happens Many people assume any peanut butter is safe for dogs, but some contain harmful additives like xylitol or excess sugar.

Fix Always check ingredient labels and use only natural, unsweetened, and xylitol-free peanut butter. This keeps the treat safe and healthy for your dog.

Overbaking the cake

Why it happens Following the timer exactly without checking doneness can dry out the cake, making it hard and crumbly.

Fix Start checking with a toothpick a few minutes before the timer ends. Remove the cake once the toothpick comes out clean or with moist crumbs for a tender texture.

Skipping the cooling step before slicing

Why it happens Impatience or not realizing that warm cake is fragile leads to messy, crumbled slices.

Fix Allow the cake to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. This firms up the cake and makes slicing easier and neater.

Adding too much flour

Why it happens Trying to make the batter thicker can lead to a dry, dense cake that dogs might find hard to chew.

Fix Stick to the recipe measurements. The batter should be thick but moist, resulting in a soft, tender cake.

Not greasing the pan properly

Why it happens Omitting this step causes the cake to stick, making it difficult to remove.

Fix Lightly grease the pan with coconut oil or non-stick spray before pouring the batter to ensure easy release.

Variations

Sweet Potato and Apple Dog Cake

Swap the pumpkin puree for cooked sweet potato and add 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce. This variation adds a natural sweetness and extra fiber. The cake will be slightly denser but still moist and delicious for your dog.

Note: Adjust moisture by reducing water slightly if the batter seems too wet due to applesauce.

Banana and Peanut Butter Dog Cake

Replace pumpkin with one ripe mashed banana. This gives the cake a sweeter flavor and softer texture. It’s great for dogs who love bananas and peanut butter together.

Note: Banana adds extra moisture; reduce water by 2 tablespoons to maintain proper batter consistency.

Carrot and Oat Dog Cake

Add 1/2 cup finely grated carrots to the batter along with the pumpkin. Carrots add crunch and vitamins, making the cake even more nutritious. Expect a slightly chunkier texture from the grated veggies.

Note: Fold carrots in gently to avoid overmixing and keep the batter light.

Gluten-Free Pumpkin Oat Dog Cake

Use oat flour instead of whole wheat flour to make this cake gluten-free. It’s perfect for dogs sensitive to wheat. The texture will be a bit softer and more crumbly but still tasty and moist.

Note: Oat flour absorbs less liquid; reduce water by about 2 tablespoons to keep batter consistency right.

No-Peanut Butter Pumpkin Dog Cake

If your dog is allergic to peanuts, simply omit the peanut butter and increase the water by 2 tablespoons. You can also substitute with sunflower seed butter if safe for your dog. The cake will still be moist and flavorful.

Note: Sunflower seed butter can be a bit oilier; adjust water accordingly to maintain batter thickness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cake can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 8 hours if covered well to prevent drying out or contamination. Use a cake dome or airtight container to keep it fresh during this time.

Refrigerator

Store the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The cake will firm up and may dry slightly but remains tasty. Let it come to room temperature before serving for the best texture and flavor.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container or bag. Freeze for up to 3 months. To thaw, move the cake to the fridge overnight and bring to room temperature before serving to restore softness.

Reheating

Avoid reheating in the microwave or oven as this can dry out the cake. Instead, let it come to room temperature naturally. If desired, a few seconds in the microwave on low power can soften the cake, but watch carefully to prevent overheating.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake the next day for maximum freshness. Alternatively, bake the cake and freeze slices, then thaw as needed for quick treats.

FAQ

Can I use fresh pumpkin instead of canned puree?
Yes, but you’ll need to cook and puree the fresh pumpkin first until smooth. Fresh pumpkin can have more water content, so you might need to adjust the water amount in the recipe to keep the batter consistency right. Using canned pumpkin puree is easier and more consistent.
Is this cake safe for puppies?
This cake uses gentle, wholesome ingredients safe for most puppies. However, always check with your vet before introducing new treats, especially if your puppy is very young or has health concerns. Start with a small piece to watch for any reactions.
Can I substitute peanut butter with something else?
Absolutely! If your dog is allergic to peanuts, try sunflower seed butter or tahini, but make sure they don’t contain added salt or sugar. You can also omit the peanut butter and slightly increase the water to maintain moisture.
How long does the cake last once baked?
Stored in an airtight container in the fridge, the cake stays fresh for about 5 days. For longer storage, freeze it in portions for up to 3 months. Always check for mold or off smells before feeding leftovers.
Can I add frosting or toppings?
Yes! A simple dog-safe frosting made with plain Greek yogurt or cream cheese mixed with a bit of peanut butter or honey makes a tasty topping. Just avoid sweeteners like xylitol or artificial ingredients.
What if my dog has a wheat allergy?
You can substitute the whole wheat flour with oat flour or rice flour to make the cake wheat-free. The texture might be slightly different — softer or more crumbly — but still delicious and safe.
Is it okay to give my dog a lot of this cake?
This cake is meant as an occasional treat, not a meal replacement. Too much can cause digestive upset or weight gain. Offer small portions and balance with your dog’s regular diet.
Can I make this cake in muffin tins?
Definitely! Using muffin tins cuts baking time down to about 15-20 minutes. The texture will be similar, and muffins are great for portion control or travel treats.

What You'll Need

Makes about 8 small slices, perfect for 8 treats

  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree, unsweetened
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup natural peanut butter, unsalted and unsweetened
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 large egg

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly greasing your cake pan with a bit of coconut oil or non-stick spray. This step is important because it prevents the cake from sticking and helps it bake evenly. If you skip this, you might have trouble getting the cake out in one piece, which can be frustrating. While the oven heats up, gather your dry ingredients — oats, whole wheat flour, and baking powder — and mix them together in a bowl. Mixing these dry ingredients first ensures they’re evenly distributed, which helps the cake rise nicely and have a consistent texture.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Oven preheated and pan greased without residue
Step 2
In a separate large bowl, combine the pumpkin puree, peanut butter, water, and egg. Whisk these together until smooth. The peanut butter should be well incorporated to add flavor and moisture. If you rush this step or don’t mix well enough, you might get pockets of peanut butter or an uneven texture in the cake. Once smooth, gradually add the dry oat mixture to the wet ingredients, folding gently with a spatula until just combined. Overmixing can make the cake tough, so stop as soon as there are no dry streaks.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth, thick, and uniform without lumps
Step 3
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and gently smooth the top with a spatula. You want an even surface so the cake bakes uniformly. If the batter is uneven, some spots might cook faster and dry out. Pop it into the oven and bake for about 25-30 minutes. Around the 25-minute mark, start checking with a toothpick inserted in the center. When it comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs, the cake is done. Baking too long will dry it out, so keep an eye on it towards the end.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Golden edges and toothpick comes out clean or with moist crumbs
Step 4
Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling is crucial because cutting into a warm cake can cause it to crumble or be too soft to slice neatly. Plus, your dog will appreciate the cake being at room temperature rather than hot. If you want to serve immediately, make sure to let it cool at least 20 minutes.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Cake is firm to the touch and cool throughout
Step 5
After cooling, carefully run a butter knife around the edges to loosen the cake, then invert it onto a plate. Slice into 8 equal pieces or smaller treats depending on your dog’s size. Smaller bites are easier for little pups and help with portion control. You can store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days or freeze for longer storage.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Slices hold their shape and have a tender crumb
Step 6
If you want to get fancy, you can frost the cake with a dog-safe yogurt or cream cheese frosting. Just mix plain Greek yogurt or softened cream cheese with a little honey or peanut butter for sweetness. Spread a thin layer on top once the cake is completely cool. This adds extra flavor and makes the cake look festive for special occasions.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Frosting is smooth and evenly spread without melting
Step 7
Always introduce any new treat gradually to your dog’s diet to avoid tummy upset. Start with a small piece and watch for any reactions. This pumpkin oat cake uses gentle ingredients, but every dog’s digestion is different. If your dog enjoys it, you can safely include it as an occasional treat.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dog happily eats treat with no adverse reactions
Step 8
Clean up your prep area promptly to avoid peanut butter residue attracting ants or pests. Wash pans and utensils with warm soapy water. Keeping your kitchen tidy makes the next baking session more enjoyable and hygienic.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: All equipment clean and dry
Step 9
Store any leftover cake slices in an airtight container in the fridge and use within 5 days. For longer storage, wrap tightly and freeze up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight before serving again. Proper storage preserves freshness and prevents spoilage, so you always have a safe treat ready.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cake stored properly without drying out or mold
