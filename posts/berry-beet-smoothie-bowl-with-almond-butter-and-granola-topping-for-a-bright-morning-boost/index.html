Bright Beet & Berry Smoothie Bowl with Almond Butter Swirl

Lummy Hayes
Published on May 9, 2026
15 min Prep Time
2 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This smoothie bowl combines earthy beets and sweet berries with a creamy almond butter swirl for a fresh, energizing start. Topped with crunchy granola, it’s perfect for a colorful breakfast or snack that feels nourishing and vibrant.

Pro Tips

Always cook and peel beets before blending.
Raw beets are tough and have a strong earthy flavor that can overpower the smoothie bowl. Cooking softens their texture and brings out natural sweetness, making blending easier and the flavor more balanced.
Use frozen fruit to keep the smoothie bowl thick without watering it down.
Frozen berries and banana chunks chill and thicken the bowl naturally. Using fresh fruit would require ice or extra liquid, which can make the texture runny and less satisfying.
Swirl almond butter on top instead of blending it in completely.
Swirling creates a visual contrast and distinct nutty pockets of flavor. Blending it fully can mute the almond butter’s taste and make the texture too uniform.
Add crunchy granola just before serving.
Granola softens quickly when exposed to moisture. Adding it last preserves its crisp texture, which contrasts beautifully with the smooth base.
Adjust almond milk quantity carefully to get the right thickness.
Too much liquid makes the bowl runny and harder to eat with a spoon. Too little makes blending difficult and the texture tough. Add milk slowly for best control.
Use a high-powered blender for the smoothest texture.
A good blender breaks down fibrous beet and frozen fruit efficiently, creating a creamy, lump-free smoothie bowl.
Store cooked beets separately if making ahead.
Prepping beets ahead saves time, but storing them separately prevents the smoothie base from becoming too watery or mushy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using raw beet in the smoothie

Why it happens People sometimes assume all ingredients can go in raw, but raw beet is hard and bitter, which can ruin the flavor and texture.

Fix Cook and peel the beet before blending to soften it and bring out natural sweetness. This ensures a smooth, balanced bowl.

Adding too much almond milk

Why it happens In a rush, it’s tempting to add more liquid to blend faster, but this dilutes the texture.

Fix Add almond milk gradually and blend in short bursts. Stop once you reach a thick, spoonable texture.

Mixing almond butter fully into the smoothie

Why it happens Some blend it all together for convenience, losing the visual and textural swirl.

Fix Add almond butter on top and swirl gently with a spoon to keep distinct nutty pockets.

Adding granola too early

Why it happens Adding granola before serving causes it to become soggy quickly.

Fix Add granola right before eating to maintain crunch.

Using unripe banana

Why it happens Unripe bananas are less sweet and can taste starchy, affecting flavor and texture.

Fix Use ripe bananas with brown spots for natural sweetness and creaminess.

Variations

Tropical Twist Smoothie Bowl

Swap mixed berries for mango and pineapple, and use coconut milk instead of almond milk. Top with shredded coconut and chopped macadamia nuts for a tropical vibe. This changes the flavor profile to bright and sunny with a creamy tropical texture.

Note: Coconut milk is richer and thicker, so you may need less liquid overall to maintain the right texture.

Green Beet Smoothie Bowl

Add a handful of spinach or kale to the original blend for extra greens. Use a green granola topping or pumpkin seeds for crunch. Expect a more earthy, nutrient-dense bowl with a mild green flavor balancing the beet’s sweetness.

Note: Blend greens well to avoid fibrous texture; add more almond milk if needed.

Chocolate Beet Smoothie Bowl

Add a tablespoon of cocoa powder or cacao nibs to the smoothie for a chocolatey twist. Top with dark chocolate shavings and sliced almonds. This creates a rich, indulgent flavor with the earthiness of beet paired with deep chocolate notes.

Note: Cocoa powder can dry out the mixture slightly, so add a splash more almond milk.

Protein-Packed Beet Smoothie Bowl

Add a scoop of vanilla or unflavored plant-based protein powder to the blend. Top with extra nuts and seeds for a filling, muscle-friendly meal. This version is great post-workout or when you need sustained energy.

Note: Protein powder can thicken the bowl; increase almond milk slightly for balance.

Spiced Beet Smoothie Bowl

Add a pinch of cinnamon and ginger to the blend for a warming flavor profile. Top with chopped walnuts and a drizzle of maple syrup. This adds a cozy spice note that’s perfect for cooler mornings.

Note: Spices intensify quickly; start with small amounts and adjust to taste.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The smoothie bowl is best eaten immediately and should not be left at room temperature for more than 1 hour to avoid spoilage and loss of freshness. Cover it loosely if needed during this time.

Refrigerator

Store leftover smoothie base (without granola) in an airtight container for up to 2 days. The texture may thicken and colors may dull slightly. Add a splash of almond milk and stir well before serving cold or reheating gently.

Freezer

You can freeze the blended smoothie base in a sealed container for up to 1 month. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and stir well. Texture may be slightly grainy but still tasty. Avoid freezing granola toppings.

Reheating

If you prefer a warmer bowl, gently warm the smoothie base on low heat on the stovetop or microwave in short bursts, stirring often. Avoid overheating to preserve nutrients and texture. Add a little almond milk to loosen if needed.

Make Ahead

Cook and peel beets 1-2 days ahead and store refrigerated. Blend the smoothie base the night before and keep chilled. Store granola and toppings separately and add just before serving for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use raw beets instead of cooked in this smoothie bowl?
Using raw beets is not recommended because they are tough and have a strong earthy flavor that doesn’t blend smoothly. Cooking softens the beet, reduces bitterness, and enhances natural sweetness, resulting in a creamier, tastier bowl.
What can I substitute for almond butter if I have a nut allergy?
Sunflower seed butter or tahini can be great substitutes. They provide a similar creamy texture and healthy fats but have a different flavor profile. Start with a smaller amount and adjust to taste to avoid overpowering the smoothie.
How do I keep granola crunchy when adding it to the smoothie bowl?
Add granola immediately before serving to prevent it from absorbing moisture and becoming soggy. Store granola separately in an airtight container if prepping the smoothie base ahead of time.
Can I make this smoothie bowl vegan?
Absolutely! Use maple syrup or agave instead of honey for sweetness, and ensure your granola is free from honey or dairy ingredients. The rest of the recipe is naturally plant-based.
Is it okay to use fresh berries instead of frozen?
Fresh berries work but will result in a thinner, less chilled bowl. You might want to add ice cubes or freeze the fresh berries beforehand to maintain the thick texture typical of smoothie bowls.
How do I store leftovers if I have any?
Store the smoothie base without toppings in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Add fresh granola and toppings only when ready to eat to maintain crunch and freshness.
Can I add protein powder to this recipe?
Yes, adding a scoop of your favorite protein powder can boost nutrition and make the bowl more filling. You might need to add a bit more almond milk to maintain the right consistency.
What blender works best for this smoothie bowl?
A high-powered blender like a Vitamix or Blendtec handles frozen fruit and fibrous beets best, producing a creamy, smooth texture. Lower-powered blenders may struggle and leave chunks.

What You'll Need

2 smoothie bowls

  • 1 small cooked beet, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup frozen mixed berries
  • 1 ripe banana, peeled
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 2 tablespoons almond butter
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/4 cup granola for topping
  • Fresh berries and seeds for garnish (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by prepping your beet if it’s not already cooked—roasting or boiling until tender works best. This step is crucial because raw beets are too hard and earthy for a smoothie bowl. Once cooked and peeled, chop it into smaller pieces to help your blender work smoothly. Using cooked beet enhances the natural sweetness and gives you that vibrant color without any bitterness. Avoid skipping this step or using raw beet, as it will affect both texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Beet pieces should be soft enough to easily pierce with a fork and bright in color
Step 2
Add the cooked beet, frozen berries, banana, and almond milk into your blender. Blend on high until smooth and creamy. The frozen berries help thicken the mixture, while the banana adds natural sweetness and creaminess. If the blend feels too thick, add a splash more almond milk, but be careful not to make it too runny—you want a thick texture you can eat with a spoon. Rushing this step or using too much liquid will cause a watery bowl that lacks that satisfying mouthfeel.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smoothie should be thick, velvety, and homogenous with no large chunks
Step 3
Next, swirl in the almond butter by adding it on top of your blended smoothie bowl and using a spoon or knife to gently mix it in. This step adds richness and a subtle nutty flavor that complements the earthiness of the beet and tart berries. If you blend the almond butter in completely, you lose that lovely marbled effect and some texture. Taking a moment to swirl it creates a beautiful presentation and a nice contrast in taste.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Visible swirls of almond butter on the surface, not fully blended
Step 4
Top your smoothie bowl with granola for crunch. This adds texture contrast and a touch of sweetness, making each bite more interesting. You can also sprinkle fresh berries or seeds like chia or pumpkin for extra nutrients and color. Don’t skip the toppings—they transform the bowl from just a smoothie to a satisfying meal. Using stale or soggy granola will ruin this effect, so always use fresh granola.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Granola and toppings are evenly scattered and look fresh and crisp
Step 5
If you like it a bit sweeter, drizzle a little honey or maple syrup over the top. This balances the earthiness of the beet and adds a gentle sweetness without overpowering the natural flavors. Adding sweetener is optional but recommended if your berries aren’t very ripe. Avoid pouring too much syrup, as it can make the bowl overly sweet and mask the vibrant flavors.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Light glossy drizzle over the toppings, enhancing appearance and taste
Step 6
Serve immediately for the best texture and freshness. The smoothie bowl is best eaten right away because the granola stays crunchy and the colors look vibrant. Letting it sit too long causes the granola to soften and the smoothie to separate, losing that appealing texture and look. If you need to prepare ahead, keep the granola separate and add it just before serving.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smoothie bowl looks fresh, toppings crunchy, no liquid pooling
Step 7
For an extra nutrient boost, sprinkle some chia seeds or hemp hearts on top. These add fiber, protein, and healthy fats without changing the flavor much. Tossing them on just before serving keeps them crunchy and visually appealing. Adding them too early can make the texture soggy, so timing matters here.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Evenly distributed seeds with a fresh look
Step 8
If you want to prep beets ahead, store cooked peeled beets in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. This saves time on busy mornings. Just be sure to chop them fresh before blending to avoid a mushy texture. Prepping this way helps streamline your morning routine without sacrificing flavor or quality.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Beets stay firm and bright after refrigeration
Step 9
Experiment with toppings like coconut flakes, sliced almonds, or fresh mint leaves to add variety. These small touches can elevate the bowl and keep it exciting if you make it regularly. Just remember to keep toppings fresh and add them right before eating to maintain their texture and flavor impact.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toppings look fresh and vibrant, enhancing the bowl’s appeal
